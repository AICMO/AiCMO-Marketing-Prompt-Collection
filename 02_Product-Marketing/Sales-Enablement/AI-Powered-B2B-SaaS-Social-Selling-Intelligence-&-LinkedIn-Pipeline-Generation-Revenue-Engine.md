**LinkedIn Sales Navigator:**
- Set up automated saved searches with weekly alerts: new hires in ICP roles, funding announcements, headcount growth events

- Enable "Buyer Intent" signals (Advanced/Advanced Plus) to surface accounts researching your category before outreach

- Custom field: "Social Selling Touch" checkbox on Contact/Lead record
- Custom activity type: "LinkedIn Message," "LinkedIn Comment," "LinkedIn InMail"
- Report: "Opportunities with ≥1 Social Touch" vs "No Social Touch" — compare win rates quarterly

- Contact property: "LinkedIn Profile URL" (required on all contact records)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Reps say they don't have time for social selling**
Solution: The 30-minutes-per-day constraint is non-negotiable. If reps say it takes longer, they are over-personalizing every message, scrolling passively instead of executing a checklist, or creating original content every day. Start with 3 reps who are already somewhat LinkedIn-active. Let the pilot's pipeline results create peer pressure. Frame social selling as replacing lower-ROI activities (manually building prospect lists, sending unmeasured cold emails) — not as additional work on top of them.

**Problem: Connection acceptance rates are below 15%**
Solution: Reps are connecting cold before establishing familiarity. Enforce the "engage first" rule: minimum 2 meaningful comments on a prospect's posts before sending a connection request. Use Sales Navigator's "TeamLink" feature to identify mutual connections for warm introduction paths. If a rep has a job-title-as-headline LinkedIn profile, no message will compensate — invest 2 hours in profile optimization before any outreach starts.

**Problem: Content engagement isn't translating to booked meetings**
Solution: Check whether reps are following up privately with users who engage on their posts within 2–4 hours while interest is highest. A buyer who comments "this is exactly what we're dealing with" and receives no personal follow-up within a few hours loses interest. Also review post formats: posts that end with a reflective question ("what are you seeing in your org?") generate comments that open the DM door far better than posts that end with a CTA to book a demo.
