## 4. Partner Objection Handling Matrix

For each objection, provide:
- **Exact objection language** (verbatim as a skeptical partner would phrase it)
- **Root cause** (what's really behind the resistance — often not what's stated)
- **LAER Response** structured as:
  - L (Listen signal): Acknowledge with 1 sentence that shows you heard them
  - A (Acknowledge): Validate the concern as reasonable, not a problem to dismiss
  - E (Explore): Ask 1 question that surfaces the real issue
  - R (Respond): Provide a factual, specific, non-defensive counterargument
- **Proof point to deploy** (stat, customer quote, or competitive data point)
- **Pivot question** (a question that advances the conversation after the response)

Objections to address:

## 6. Partner Rep Elevator Pitch (5-Minute Verbatim Script)

Write a complete, memorizable script with timing cues. Requirements:
- Written entirely in the partner rep's voice — never says "our vendor" or "the company we partner with"
- Opens with a customer pain point the prospect will immediately recognize (no product mention for first 60 seconds)
- Uses specific numbers at least 4 times (benchmark data, customer outcomes, timeframes, ROI figures)
- Handles the most common in-conversation objection inline (preemptive objection neutralization)
- Closes with a single, low-friction ask that moves the conversation forward

Script structure:
- **Hook** (0:00-0:30): Open with a question or observation about a pain the prospect experiences every quarter
- **Problem Amplification** (0:30-1:30): Make the status quo cost concrete and urgent — quantify the pain
- **Solution Introduction** (1:30-2:30): Introduce the capability without feature-dumping — focus on what changes for the customer
- **Proof Delivery** (2:30-3:30): Tell one specific customer story with a real outcome and timeframe
- **Business Case** (3:30-4:00): Frame ROI in terms of the customer's metrics, not vendor metrics
- **The Ask** (4:00-5:00): One clear next step — a 30-minute call, a shared case study, or a POC conversation

## Troubleshooting

**Issue 1: Generated partner messaging sounds like vendor marketing copy, not a third-party voice**

*Root cause:* Product description in the prompt uses superlative vendor language ("industry-leading," "AI-powered revolution") instead of outcome-anchored language partners can claim credibly as advisors.

*Fix:* Rewrite product description as customer outcome statements before pasting into the prompt. Instead of "our AI-powered platform transforms supply chain operations," use "manufacturers using this tool eliminate stockouts in 30 days and reduce excess inventory by an average of 23% in the first 90 days." Also add this explicit constraint to your prompt: "Write all messaging from the perspective of a trusted advisor speaking to a peer customer — the partner is NOT endorsing a vendor; they are solving a customer problem using a tool they've evaluated and recommend."

---

**Issue 2: Objection responses feel dismissive or skip the acknowledgment step, making the partner sound defensive**

*Root cause:* The prompt didn't specify a tone constraint for objection handling, so the model defaults to salesy rebuttals rather than consultative responses.

*Fix:* Add this constraint to your prompt: "For each objection response, the first sentence must validate the partner's concern as legitimate and understandable. The response should make a skeptical partner feel heard — not sold to. Use the LAER framework strictly: the Acknowledge and Explore steps must come before the Respond step, and the Respond step should include a question, not just a statement."

---

**Issue 3: Partner revenue math doesn't resonate because different partner types have completely different economics**

*Root cause:* The prompt used a single margin figure without clarifying that referral partners, SIs, and VARs have entirely different economic models. A referral partner earns a commission for no implementation work; an SI earns margin plus 60+ hours of billable services. Treating them identically undermines credibility.

*Fix:* Add a separate economic model section per partner type in your prompt. Example additions: "For referral partners: frame economics as passive income — no implementation, no support, just a check for connecting the right people. For SIs: calculate total economics as margin PLUS billable implementation hours at the partner's rate. For VARs: show first-year margin plus 3-year renewal economics to illustrate compounding value. For MSPs: frame as MRR addition to their existing recurring revenue stack with minimal incremental support burden."
