**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** competitive-intelligence, sales-enablement, battlecards, messaging, positioning, b2b-saas, automation

## Overview
This prompt system continuously monitors competitor moves, synthesizes win/loss signals, and generates updated competitive battlecards that sales reps can deploy in real-time conversations. Use it when you need a self-updating competitive intelligence layer that translates market signals directly into objection-handling scripts, differentiation narratives, and deal-winning positioning — no analyst middleman required.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior competitive intelligence strategist for [Your Company], a B2B SaaS platform competing against [Competitor 1], [Competitor 2], and [Competitor 3].

Build a complete competitive battlecard for [Primary Competitor] using this input data:
- Our product: [Your Product] — key capabilities: [List 3-5 features]
- Competitor's known weaknesses (from G2/Capterra/Reddit/sales calls): [Paste raw feedback]
- Our recent wins against them: [Describe 2-3 win scenarios]
- Our recent losses to them: [Describe 1-2 loss scenarios]
- Their recent pricing/product changes: [Any known changes]

Produce:

1. KILLER OPENING SLIDE (3 bullets): Why we win vs. [Competitor] — written for a sales rep to say out loud in 30 seconds.

2. OBJECTION KILL SHEET: For each of these objections, write a 2-sentence response a rep can memorize:
   - "We're already using [Competitor]"
   - "[Competitor] is cheaper"
   - "[Competitor] has [specific feature we lack]"
   - "We know [Competitor] — we don't know you"

3. TRAP QUESTIONS (5 questions): Questions a rep should ask that expose competitor weaknesses without naming them directly.

4. PROOF POINT ARSENAL: 3 customer quotes (realistic, industry-specific), 2 metrics comparisons, 1 analyst citation format.

5. WHEN WE LOSE: Honest scenarios where [Competitor] actually wins, and how to qualify out early.

6. SLACK-READY DEAL ALERT: A template a rep pastes into Slack when they spot [Competitor] in a deal — triggers the team.

Format each section as copy-paste ready text. No bullet-point jargon — write in the voice of a confident enterprise sales rep.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Objection responses feel generic and reps aren't using them**
*Fix:* Feed real Gong transcripts where these objections appeared into the prompt. Replace the "realistic example" instruction with actual (anonymized) call language. Reps trust language that sounds like real conversations, not marketing copy.

**Problem: Battlecard becomes stale after 30 days and competitor launches a new feature**
*Fix:* Add a "Signal Trigger" system — set Google Alerts for "{{COMPETITOR_NAME}} new feature", monitor their changelog page, and set a Slack workflow that pings the PMM to run a battlecard refresh when a trigger fires. The prompt's Section 9d debrief questions feed directly back in.

**Problem: Trap questions are too obvious and prospects recognize them as sales tactics**
*Fix:* Have 3 AEs review the questions and mark any that feel "salesy." Replace flagged questions with versions that start with genuine curiosity phrases ("I'm trying to understand how most teams in your space handle...") and remove any question that could only be asked because you know the competitor's weakness.
