SECTION 1: SKEPTIC PROFILE INTELLIGENCE

**BURNED-BEFORE BUYER**
Core fear: Career risk from repeating a public failure

Core fear: Regulatory exposure and audit liability

What they need: Explainability, audit trails, human-in-the-loop architecture, data governance documentation
Messaging principle: "AI isn't making the decision — it's giving your team better information to make the decision. Here's the paper trail your auditors will love."

**ROI SKEPTIC**
Core fear: Spending budget on productivity theater that doesn't show in the numbers
Hidden message: "I've approved AI tools that generated dashboards nobody used"
What they need: Time-to-measurable-outcome data, use-case-specific ROI (not generic), cohort comparison of users vs non-users

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: The buyer agrees with the framework but still won't commit to a pilot.
**Solution**: The real objection isn't the AI — it's internal budget authority. Ask: "If we could make the pilot zero-cost and zero-commitment for the first 30 days, what would stop you from starting?" The answer reveals the true barrier. Often it's: (a) procurement process too slow, (b) IT security review required first, (c) internal champion doesn't have sign-off authority. Address the operational barrier, not the skepticism.

**Problem**: The champion converts but the economic buyer kills the deal in legal/procurement.
**Solution**: Economic buyer skepticism is usually risk-averse, not product-skeptical. Provide: a redlined contract with vendor liability for accuracy SLA misses, a one-page "What Happens If This Fails" document with exit terms, and a reference call with another CFO/CRO who was in the same position. The economic buyer needs to explain the decision to their board — give them the language.

**Problem**: Competitor is positioned as "more enterprise-proven" and buyer uses this to justify inaction.
**Solution**: Reframe: "More installed base" means "higher risk of being a maintenance customer, not an innovation customer." Use the Hype-Fatigue Buyer inoculation content (#2: "What to Ask Any AI Vendor") to force competitor into accuracy/transparency comparison. If you win the methodology transparency comparison, you win the credibility battle — even if competitor has more logos.
