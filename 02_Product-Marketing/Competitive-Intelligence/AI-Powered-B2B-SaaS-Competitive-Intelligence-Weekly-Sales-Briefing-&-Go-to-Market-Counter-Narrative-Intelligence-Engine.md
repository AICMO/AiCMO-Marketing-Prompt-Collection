## Integration Tips

- **Slack:** Use Slack's scheduled messages or a Zapier → Slack automation to deliver the briefing to #competitive-intel every Monday at 8am
- **Notion:** Store running history of briefings in a Notion database — tag each entry by competitor and threat level for searchability
- **Salesforce/HubSpot:** Create a "Competitive Mention" tag in CRM. Train reps to tag deals when a competitor is mentioned; use this as input to next week's brief
- **Gong/Chorus:** Set keyword alerts for all competitor names — weekly digest feeds directly into intelligence input
- **Google Alerts:** Set up for "[Competitor Name] announcement," "[Competitor Name] press release," "[Competitor Name] pricing" — pipe to a shared inbox that feeds the prompt
- **G2 Track:** Subscribe to competitor profile change alerts; new reviews surface within 24 hours
- **Klue / Crayon / Kompyte:** If you have a dedicated CI tool, use it to aggregate signals and paste the digest as "WEEKLY INTELLIGENCE INPUT" in the prompt
- **GitHub:** Monitor public competitor repos (if they exist) for new feature commits — leading indicator of product direction

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The briefing is too long and reps stop reading after the first section.
**Solution:** Enforce the 4-minute read target ruthlessly. Cut Deep Dives to the top 2 competitors weekly. Rotate coverage — not every competitor needs a section every week. Lead with the most urgent threat and make the first 100 words the most actionable content in the doc.

**Problem:** I don't have enough competitive intelligence to fill the briefing.
**Solution:** The prompt works even with sparse input — it synthesizes what you have and flags what's missing as Watch List items. Start with 3 sources: G2 review alerts, competitor LinkedIn company page posts, and 1 sales rep debrief per week. That's enough to produce a valuable brief. As the program matures, add Gong keyword triggers and job posting monitoring.

**Problem:** Sales reps say the counter-narratives feel scripted and unnatural.
**Solution:** Run the draft counter-narratives through a secondary prompt asking: "Rewrite these for a 28-year-old AE who has never worked in product marketing. Make them conversational, confident, and easy to deliver in a 30-second window without sounding like a FAQ." Then test 3-5 reps in roleplay before publishing.
