## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI generates generic compliance language ("We take security seriously") instead of specific competitive intelligence.**
Solution: Force specificity by providing actual competitor names and asking the AI to only cite public-facing documentation (trust pages, security.company.com pages, G2 reviews, press releases). If the AI hedges, add: "Only make claims you can support with specific public sources. If you can't verify a claim, note it as 'unverified — needs human validation.'"

**Problem: Regulatory output covers regulations that don't apply to your ICP (e.g., healthcare regulations when you sell to manufacturing).**
Solution: Add a constraint: "Only include regulations that directly apply to buyers in [specific industries]. Exclude: [list of industries to exclude]. For each regulation, confirm which specific ICP persona bears the compliance obligation."

**Problem: Compliance-led email sequences feel fear-mongering or overly alarmist.**
Solution: Reframe the prompt angle from "warning" to "preparation partner." Add: "Position all content as helping the buyer succeed with compliance, not scaring them. Use language like 'be ready for' and 'get ahead of' instead of 'risk of' and 'penalty for.' Include at least one customer success story in every email."
