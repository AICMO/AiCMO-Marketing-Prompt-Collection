## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** LLM responses vary too much run-to-run to establish a reliable baseline.
**Solution:** Use a 3-run average for each query — run the same query three times on the same LLM within 48 hours and take the modal response (most frequently occurring answer). For queries with high variability, flag as "contested" and monitor more frequently rather than trying to establish a single baseline. Temperature settings on API-level queries can be set to 0 for more deterministic responses if you're using the API directly.

**Problem:** Competitors with significantly more G2 reviews and analyst coverage appear uncatchable in AI citations.
**Solution:** Don't try to win category-level awareness queries in the short term — focus on use-case-specific and capability-specific queries where the position is either contested or unclaimed. A market leader may dominate "best conversation intelligence" but if no one owns "fastest conversation intelligence implementation" or "AI-native conversation + pipeline," you can claim those positions in 60-90 days with focused content and review acceleration. Win the specific battles before the general war.

**Problem:** Internal stakeholders don't see AI citation monitoring as a core CI function — they see it as an SEO/content responsibility.
**Solution:** Anchor the business case to win/loss data: "X% of our lost deals in the last quarter involved a buyer who had already decided [competitor] was the market leader before our first call. AI search is one of the primary mechanisms through which that pre-decision is forming. CI's job is to understand and contest where buyers form pre-deal opinions — AI search is now one of the top 3 venues where that happens." Frame AI citation share as a leading indicator of pipeline quality, not a marketing vanity metric.
