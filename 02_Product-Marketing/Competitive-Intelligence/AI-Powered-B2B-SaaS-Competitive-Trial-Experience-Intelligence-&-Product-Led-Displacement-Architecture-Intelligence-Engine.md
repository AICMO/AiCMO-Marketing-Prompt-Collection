## Overview
Deploys an AI agent to systematically sign up for, navigate, and deconstruct competitor trial or freemium experiences — documenting every onboarding friction point, time-to-value gap, and activation failure — then generates a Product-Led Displacement Playbook: messaging, content, and campaign assets that intercept buyers during competitor trials and exploit the specific moments competitors fail to deliver value. Use this when 40%+ of your inbound leads are already evaluating a competitor's trial, when deals stall because prospects are "currently testing another solution," or when you lack systematic intelligence about what prospects actually experience inside competitor products.

6. **Product-Led Displacement Landing Page Brief** — A headline, three value bullets, and a primary CTA for a landing page specifically designed to convert prospects who just finished or abandoned a competitor trial.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI generates generic trial teardown insights that don't match real competitor behavior**
- Fix: Feed the prompt specific G2 and Capterra reviews (copy-paste 5–10 reviews mentioning onboarding from the competitor's page before running the prompt). Also include direct quotes from your sales team's win/loss notes where competitor trial experience was mentioned. The more specific the input, the more precise the displacement strategy.

**Problem: Displacement emails feel confrontational or like they're attacking the competitor**
- Fix: The goal is to intercept, not attack. Review every email and replace any language that names or implies the competitor with language that describes the prospect's emotional state ("you've been in setup mode for 72 hours") or the outcome they're missing ("your team still doesn't have a forecast they trust"). Let the prospect connect the dots — the recognition that their current trial is failing is more powerful when it comes from inside them.

**Problem: Sales team won't use the displacement sequences because they're too long or the messaging feels "marketing-y"**
- Fix: Run a 1-hour workshop with 3 reps to co-edit the sequences. Have them rewrite subject lines in their own voice. The framework and trigger logic should come from PMM; the actual send-level language should feel like it came from the rep. Create a "rep voice edit" version of every template, and let reps choose between the original and the edited version. Track which version gets higher reply rates.
