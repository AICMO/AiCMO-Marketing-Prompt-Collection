Use when your partner ecosystem generates 15%+ of revenue and you need structured ecosystem intelligence to inform product roadmap, competitive positioning, and joint GTM initiatives.

PART 1: PAC MEMBER SELECTION ARCHITECTURE

A. Partner Scoring Model
Score each candidate partner on a 0–10 scale per dimension:

| Dimension | Weight | Scoring Criteria |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Revenue Contribution | 30% | Partner-sourced ARR, deal size, win rate when partner is involved |
| Strategic Importance | 25% | Market coverage, vertical expertise, ICP alignment, complementary capabilities |
| Engagement Quality | 20% | Partner NPS, co-marketing participation, mutual customer satisfaction scores |
| Growth Potential | 15% | Pipeline trajectory, market position, investment signals from partner |
| Ecosystem Influence | 10% | Industry reputation, thought leadership, reference value for prospects |

Output per candidate:
- Total weighted score (0–10)
- Recommendation: Founding Member (top 3–5 who shaped your company's early growth) vs. Rotating Member (12-month term, renewable up to 3 years)
- Risk flag: Competing partners who require conflict-of-interest management

Optimal PAC size: 8–12 members (small enough for genuine dialogue, large enough for market breadth).

B. Invitation Architecture
Sequence:
1. Executive sponsor (CEO or VP Partnerships) sends warm outreach via existing relationship
2. Formal invitation letter + PAC charter within 72 hours
3. 30-minute onboarding call to confirm commitment and set expectations
4. NDA and governance paperwork before first meeting access

Invitation positioning: Frame PAC membership as a selective, invitation-only program — not a free benefit but a mutual commitment. Waitlist creates exclusivity signal.

PART 3: INTELLIGENCE COLLECTION SYSTEM

A. Pre-Meeting Survey (send 2 weeks before each session)
Limit to 8 questions, 10 minutes to complete. Weight toward qualitative responses over ratings.

1. MARKET PULSE: "What is the single biggest change you've observed in how [ICP buyer role] makes purchase decisions in the last 90 days?"

2. COMPETITIVE SIGNAL: "Which competitors are you seeing us lose to most frequently, and what is the primary stated reason?"

3. PRODUCT GAP: "What one capability, if we shipped it in the next 2 quarters, would increase your team's win rate with our joint solution by 20%+?"

4. INTEGRATION HEALTH: "On a 1–10 scale, how would you rate the current integration between our platforms? What would need to be true for it to be a 10?"

5. GTM FRICTION: "Describe the single biggest friction point your sales team experiences when positioning our joint solution in a deal."

6. MARKET OPPORTUNITY: "Name one customer segment or vertical where you believe we are both significantly underpenetrated — and your hypothesis for why."

7. AI BUYER IMPACT: "How is AI changing the buying process or evaluation criteria in accounts you're actively selling into right now?"

8. SESSION PRIORITY: "What's the single most important topic you want us to address in our next session?"

B. During-Session Capture Template
Real-time structured notes using four categories:
- PRODUCT: Feature gaps, integration requests, bug reports
- COMPETITIVE: Competitor mentions, win/loss context, pricing intelligence
- MARKET: New buyer signals, vertical trends, timing indicators
- GTM: Joint pipeline opportunities, co-marketing ideas, partner capacity signals

Use dot-voting at session end to prioritize top 3 themes across all four categories.

C. AI Synthesis Engine
Run this prompt within 24 hours of each session against all captured notes and (with consent) recording transcripts:

- Feature requests ranked by: (number of mentions × partner revenue weight × estimated customer impact)

- Roadmap validation signals (what partners confirmed customers want that we already plan to build)

COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE:
- Competitors mentioned + deal context + frequency
- Win/loss patterns from partner field perspective
- Counter-narrative opportunities to develop immediately

MARKET INTELLIGENCE:

- Vertical or segment opportunity signals
- Market timing indicators (urgency triggers partners are observing)

GTM OPPORTUNITIES:
- Specific joint pipeline opportunities surfaced (company names if mentioned)

- New partnership or integration opportunities

ACTION ITEMS: Ranked by (impact × urgency), with suggested owner and 30-day deadline.

Distribution: Post executive brief to #pac-intelligence Slack within 24 hours. Full report to CPO and VP Sales within 48 hours.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner executives send delegates instead of attending PAC sessions themselves**
Solution: Design PAC value exchange so it's specifically gated to executives. Roadmap previews, CPO 1:1 time, and analyst briefing inclusion are only available to confirmed attendees at executive level. State explicitly in PAC charter: delegate attendance forfeits that session's benefits. When a delegate shows up to an in-person summit, politely but firmly note the membership expectations. Three consecutive delegate appearances triggers a membership review conversation.

**Problem: PAC discussions stay surface-level and produce little actionable intelligence**
Solution: Never start a PAC session with open questions. Pre-seed every discussion with a specific provocation based on pre-meeting survey analysis — for example: "Three of you independently flagged [X] as a top problem. Here's our current thinking on it. Tell us why we're wrong." Use executive dinner (informal setting) as the primary intelligence environment — executives reveal far more in a small group over dinner than in a formal conference room. Follow each group statement with "What's the underlying customer problem driving that?"

**Problem: Partners feel the PAC is extractive — you're getting insights without delivering visible value**
Solution: Close the loop explicitly and publicly. At the start of every PAC session, spend 10 minutes presenting: "Based on Q[X] PAC feedback, here is what we built/changed/prioritized." Show a named roadmap item tied to a PAC member's specific suggestion. Recognition is a currency — partners who see their input translated into action become vocal advocates for the program.
