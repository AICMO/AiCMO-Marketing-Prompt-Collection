## Overview
Design and validate differentiated pricing strategies for each vertical market you serve — because the same product can be worth 3x more in financial services than in education, and pricing uniformly across verticals leaves millions on the table. This engine uses AI to analyze value delivery by vertical, model willingness-to-pay differences, select the right value metrics per industry, and build the internal business case for vertical-tiered pricing without triggering customer backlash.

**FINANCIAL SERVICES PRICING BLUEPRINT:**

RECOMMENDED MODEL: Hybrid — Compliance Platform Fee + Per-Seat
- **Compliance Base Fee**: $3,000/month per organization (covers SOC2 Type II reporting, FFIEC audit trail, unlimited data retention, dedicated security review SLA) — regardless of seat count
- **Seat Pricing**: $185/seat/month (entry), $165/seat/month (11-50 seats), custom (51+ seats)
- **Effective ACV at 28 seats (current avg)**: $3,000×12 + 28×$185×12 = $36K + $62.2K = $98.2K base → vs. current $50.4K → **+95% ACV lift on new logo deals**

Note: This seems like a big jump — but at $98K, we're still 35-60% BELOW Ironclad and DocuSign CLM for the same ICP. The compliance base fee signals enterprise-grade commitment and becomes a retention moat.

VALUE METRIC: Hybrid (platform + seat) is right for FinServ. Moving to per-contract-processed would require customer reporting infrastructure they don't have. Per-seat is legible to their budget process.

PACKAGE DIFFERENTIATION: FinServ SKU includes: FFIEC Audit Trail Module, SOC2 Type II annual report, 99.9% SLA with financial penalties, dedicated security review within 30 days, unlimited data retention (standard SKU: 3 years), and quarterly compliance update briefings. These are table-stakes for FinServ buyers and currently given away free.

ACV IMPACT (new logos): +$47K per deal on average (from $50K to $98K) — 95% uplift
ACV IMPACT (existing customer migration): 60% migrate successfully in year 1. Net: $2.4M incremental on existing 42 FinServ customers over 18 months.

---

**PROFESSIONAL SERVICES / LAW FIRM PRICING BLUEPRINT:**

RECOMMENDED ACTION: Re-segment and re-price DOWN for law firm buyers; redesign value proposition for this vertical.

Law firms using Praxis for contract management have fundamentally different economics than FinServ buyers. Legal work is billable by hour — they have less incentive to automate contract review because time spent reviewing = revenue. The buyer is often a Partner or COO with a tight technology budget, and the primary value is not risk avoidance (law firms ARE risk management) — it's throughput for non-billable contracts (vendor agreements, employment contracts, real estate leases).

RECOMMENDED MODEL: Per-department, $1,200/department/month
- Covers unlimited users within a department (legal ops, HR, facilities)
- Removes per-seat friction in small law firms (who have 2-5 attorneys doing CLM, making per-seat look expensive)
- ESTIMATED NEW ACV: $14,400-$28,800 per firm

This is BELOW current $38K average — but reduces churn from 16% to projected 8% and improves win rate from current 38% to projected 55%.

NET ARR IMPACT: Despite lower ACV, improved retention and win rate projects to +$180K net positive ARR in 24 months vs. continuing at current trajectory (which is -$290K ARR per year due to 16% churn rate on $6.8M ARR base).

## Success Metrics

- **Win rate by vertical post-repricing**: Target ≥5 percentage point improvement in FinServ and Healthcare within 6 months of new pricing
- **Average ACV uplift**: Target ≥25% ACV increase for new logos in repriced verticals within 12 months
- **Existing customer churn from pricing change**: Target ≤5% churn among repriced existing accounts (monitor monthly for 6 months post-rollout)
- **Discount frequency reduction**: Target ≥30% reduction in deals with >15% discount in repriced verticals (signals pricing is landing correctly)
- **Sales cycle impact**: Monitor for changes in average sales cycle length — good vertical pricing should reduce cycles by removing budget ambiguity
- **NRR impact**: Improved NRR in repriced verticals as vertical-specific packaging creates expansion levers (module add-ons, compliance seat adds)
- **Customer satisfaction**: Track NPS in repriced verticals vs. control (non-repriced). Best-in-class pricing changes maintain or improve NPS when value delivery story is communicated clearly

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce CPQ / DealHub / Conga CPQ**: Add a "Vertical" field to the opportunity record that auto-populates the correct price book when the Industry field is set. Build guardrails so reps cannot mix vertical-specific pricing (e.g., cannot apply FinServ compliance base fee to a Manufacturing deal). Automate vertical pricing flags in quote approvals so any deal deviating from vertical list price requires VP Sales sign-off.
- **HubSpot**: Use deal properties and custom product line items to tag vertical pricing. Build a report comparing average ACV by vertical vs. vertical list price to track discount behavior in real time.
- **Chargebee / Stripe Billing / Zuora**: Implement vertical-specific product catalogs in your billing platform. Create separate "FinServ Pro", "Healthcare Pro", and "Manufacturing Standard" SKUs so billing, finance reporting, and renewal flows are automatically accurate per vertical.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Sales team is using vertical pricing as a starting point for negotiation and immediately discounting to the old uniform price**
Solution: This is a change management failure, not a pricing design failure. Three fixes: (1) Update the compensation plan so reps earn higher commission on deals closed at or near list price (tie a multiplier to pricing discipline). (2) Implement quote approval flows that require VP Sales sign-off for any discount above 10% from new vertical list price — friction reduces casual discounting. (3) Run monthly "pricing wins" in the sales all-hands — celebrate reps who closed at list price with the specific deal story and customer quote. Social proof of peers succeeding at new pricing is more effective than any training.

**Issue: Existing customers in high-value verticals are comparing notes and discovering price inequality across verticals**
Solution: In B2B, cross-vertical price comparison almost never happens organically — Financial Services and Manufacturing buyers don't attend the same user conferences or Slack communities. However, if it does surface: your answer is value delivery, not price equality. "We price based on the value we deliver in your industry context. A construction company and a hedge fund have fundamentally different ROI from contract intelligence — it would be unfair to charge them the same." If you have a customer community or user conference, brief your CS leadership before pricing changes go live so they can proactively address questions.

**Issue: Vertical pricing reveals a segment that's unprofitable or low-WTP — the value proposition isn't strong enough to support pricing that covers your CAC**
Solution: This is valuable intelligence. For each vertical where your margin economics are underwater (high CAC, high churn, low WTP), you have three options: (1) Invest in vertical-specific product features that increase value delivered and justify higher pricing; (2) Restructure your go-to-market for that vertical to reduce CAC (e.g., self-serve motion instead of field sales); (3) Sunset the vertical from active pursuit and allow existing customers to continue but stop marketing investment. Option 3 is uncomfortable but often the right call — diluting your ICP across unprofitable verticals destroys the focus needed to win in your best segments.
