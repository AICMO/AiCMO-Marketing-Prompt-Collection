## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Analyst doesn't respond to cold briefing requests.
**Solution**: Analyst briefing inboxes receive hundreds of pitches per week. Improve your hook: lead with a market insight or data point the analyst hasn't seen, not your product. Try the "curiosity gap" opener: "We're seeing something in the [Category] market that doesn't match what's being published — happy to share in 30 minutes." Also try engaging the analyst on LinkedIn by commenting substantively on their posts 3–4 weeks before your cold email.

**Problem**: Analyst agrees to a briefing but says "you're too early for our formal coverage process."
**Solution**: This is actually a win — it means you're on their radar. Ask: "What would make us interesting for your next Emerging Vendor Landscape or Market Guide?" Use their exact language as your roadmap. Request to be added to their inquiry pool for research they're currently conducting even before formal inclusion.

**Problem**: Your customer references are reluctant to speak with analysts.
**Solution**: Reduce friction: have customers fill out a 5-question written questionnaire that you can share with the analyst instead of a live call. Offer to facilitate the call and be on the line. Create a customer "analyst reference ambassador" program with early access to roadmap features or a customer advisory board seat as the incentive for participation.
