## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Community participation gets flagged as self-promotional and trust is lost.**
Fix: Enforce a strict 90-day "give before you take" policy. No mentions of your product for the first 3 months. Only answer questions genuinely, share non-promotional resources, and introduce yourself as a practitioner (which you are). Assign the most credible technical or domain expert on your team — not a marketer — as the community contributor. Consider a dedicated Slack handle that uses a real team member's name, not a company brand name.

**Problem: AI search presence isn't improving despite publishing new content.**
Fix: AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) are primarily trained on web data with a 6-18 month lag and heavily weight authoritative external publications over brand blogs. Shift focus from your own blog to earning placements on Gartner, G2 Learn Hub, Accounting Today, industry associations, and high-domain-authority publications. Also, Perplexity actively crawls the web in real time — optimize for Perplexity's current results by targeting sources it tends to cite (Reddit, review sites, news publications) rather than SEO-optimized brand content.

**Problem: Dark funnel data exists but leadership doesn't believe it influences revenue.**
Fix: Build a "Buyer Path Reconstruction" practice: after every closed-won deal, interview the buyer about their full research journey before first contact. Document all the dark funnel touchpoints they mention and add to a "Buyer Path Database." After 10 documented cases, present leadership with a pattern analysis showing which dark funnel touchpoints appeared in winning deals. Concrete buyer stories are more convincing than attribution models.
