## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Interview recruitment is the bottleneck — we can't get enough participants**
- Solution: Reduce friction by having CS/AE book research interviews on your behalf during renewal or QBR conversations (5-minute warm handoff > cold research recruitment email). Aim for 1 research interview per 10 CS check-in calls. Offer customer advisory board membership (not just one-off interviews) with quarterly roundtable access as the incentive — better for B2B buyers than gift cards.

**Problem: Insights get generated but nothing changes — stakeholders ignore the research**
- Solution: Stop sending long reports — switch to the "3 bullet brief" format (one insight, one quote, one recommended action) delivered in Slack, not email. Make the first stakeholder distribution include a pre-made decision: "Based on this research, here's the one change I'm proposing to the homepage hero. [Link to doc with before/after]. Approve or modify by Friday." Forcing a decision creates engagement; passive information sharing doesn't.

**Problem: AI synthesis quality is inconsistent — some outputs are generic, some are excellent**
- Solution: The quality of AI synthesis scales with the quality of your prompt + the specificity of your input. Two fixes: (1) Add a "Bad output examples to avoid" section to your synthesis prompt showing 3 examples of vague insights you don't want; (2) Always include company name, product category, and ICP role at the top of your synthesis prompt so the AI calibrates to your specific context instead of giving generic qualitative research outputs.
