## Overview
Architects the complete Limited Availability (LA) to General Availability (GA) staged launch journey for B2B SaaS products — from early cohort selection and social proof accumulation through press embargo orchestration, internal readiness, and post-GA pipeline generation. Use this when launching a major new product or platform capability that requires managing design partner customers, building validated proof points before broad market release, and executing a market-moment GA launch that converts momentum into qualified pipeline within 60 days.

**SECTION 2: LA PROGRAM STRUCTURE & MILESTONE GATES**

Design the LA program as a disciplined revenue operation with clear intervention protocols:

KICKOFF PACKAGE (Day 0 of LA):
- Access credentials + sandbox environment (if applicable)
- LA Success Playbook: 2-page guide defining what success looks like by week, what the customer needs to do, what Vantara commits to do
- Dedicated PMM or CSM contact with direct Slack access
- Private LA Slack community: Company channel + general LA cohort channel for peer learning
- Weekly async update template: Structured 5-minute form for customers to complete each Friday
- NDA + Reference Agreement: Signed at kickoff — not as a GA ask

MILESTONE GATES:
Gate 1 — End of Week 2: "Live and Using"
- Criterion: Customer has completed implementation and used core feature at least 3 times
- If missed: Immediate escalation call within 24 hours; identify root cause (onboarding friction, champion capacity, IT delay); deploy engineering or CS resource within 48 hours; if unresolvable by Day 18, activate replacement cohort candidate

Gate 2 — End of Week 6: "First Measurable Value"
- Criterion: Customer can articulate one quantifiable outcome achieved ("We completed 8 contracts in 2 hours that previously took 2 days")
- If missed: PMM conducts structured ROI interview to surface unreported value; if no value found, escalate to VP PMM for program review

Gate 3 — End of Week 10: "Case Study Ready"
- Criterion: Customer has ROI data documented, champion has agreed to a case study interview, has given NPS score ≥7
- If missed: Offer to anonymize case study ("A Fortune 500 logistics company..."); for NPS <7, run structured recovery session; at risk of not meeting GA social proof targets if <60% of cohort passes this gate

Gate 4 — End of Week 12 (GA -2 weeks): "Graduated"
- Criterion: Case study interview completed or quote approved, reference agreement signed, GA pricing confirmed (upgrade or loyalty discount accepted), LinkedIn advocacy post drafted
- If missed: Customer moves to "GA reference" status (will appear at GA but no published case study)

FEEDBACK ARCHITECTURE:
- Weekly async form: 5 questions covering usage, value, blockers, product feedback, NPS — takes 5 minutes
- Bi-weekly 45-minute structured product feedback call: PMM-led, recorded, specific agenda around product gaps, competitor comparisons, positioning language customers actually use
- Monthly executive sponsor check-in (30 minutes): CEO/VP PMM + customer VP/C-Suite — strategic alignment, expansion potential, reference commitment
- Critical issue escalation: Slack direct message to PMM → 2-hour SLA for response → engineering triage within 24 hours for blocking issues

**SECTION 3: SOCIAL PROOF ACCUMULATION ENGINE**

Design a week-by-week system for building press-ready, sales-ready social proof during LA:

BY END OF WEEK 4:
- 5 first-impression testimonials (1–2 sentences from champion, captured via a 3-question email survey)
- 1 measurable "quick win" metric from fastest-progressing customer (even small: "Processed first contract in 12 minutes vs. 3 hours")
- Template: Quote extraction email — "We want to celebrate your early progress. Can you answer 3 quick questions about your experience so far?"

BY END OF WEEK 8:
- 8 detailed case study interviews completed (60-minute recorded calls using ROI interview guide)
- 3 quantified ROI data points across 3 different customers (time saved, cost avoided, revenue protected — whatever metric type resonates with ICP)
- Video testimonials recorded: 2 champion-level videos (60–90 seconds, filmed by customer on phone or by Vantara-funded videographer on-site)
- Social media permission forms signed: Agreement for customers to post on GA day and GA +7 days

BY END OF WEEK 12 (GA -2 weeks):
- 2 published case studies with quantified ROI in headline (e.g., "How [Company] Reduced Contract Review Time by 87% in 60 Days")
- 1 press-ready customer story cleared with legal + comms teams: Executive quote, company name approved for press release attribution
- 5 G2/Capterra reviews published (brief customers in Week 10 check-in; provide review link and 3 suggested talking points)
- 5 LinkedIn advocacy posts drafted and approved by customer social media teams: To be posted on GA Day 0

SOCIAL PROOF PRODUCTION TOOLS:
ROI Interview Guide (6 questions that extract metrics):
1. "What was the specific task this product replaced or improved — and how long did it take before vs. after?"
2. "Over the past 30 days, how many times have you used the product and what was the total time saved?"
3. "What would have gone wrong if you hadn't had this capability?" (surfaces risk-avoidance value)
4. "If you had to justify renewing this contract to your CFO, what 2 numbers would you use?"
5. "Who else on your team has noticed a difference?"
6. "Would you recommend this to a peer at another company? What would you tell them?" (generates quotable language)

Quote Extraction Framework:
- Ask customers about outcomes, not features: "What changed for your team?" not "What features do you like?"
- Ask comparative questions: "How does this compare to how you used to do it?"
- Ask for a specific moment: "Can you tell me about a specific contract or situation where this made a big difference?"

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LA customers aren't hitting usage milestones by Week 4, threatening the social proof roadmap**
Solution: Diagnose the root cause within 24 hours of identifying the miss using a structured 30-minute troubleshoot call with the customer champion and internal CSM. Three root causes require three different responses: (1) Onboarding friction (product UX or implementation issue) — deploy engineering resource within 48 hours and create a fast-path onboarding for remaining customers; (2) Champion access issue (wrong internal contact, no internal authority) — request to escalate to executive sponsor immediately; (3) Competing internal priorities (the customer is too busy) — negotiate a 2-week grace period extension; if a customer cannot commit 4 hours/week during LA, they cannot deliver case study value by GA and should be replaced by Week 5. Maintain a short list of 3–4 replacement cohort candidates pre-qualified during initial outreach for exactly this scenario.

**Problem: Press contacts aren't responding to embargo briefing pitches despite multiple follow-ups**
Solution: Shift your pitch angle from product announcement to customer story — journalists cover people and outcomes, not product releases. Lead with: "How a Fortune 500 company saved $847K by letting AI negotiate their software contracts" rather than "Vantara launches Negotiation Agent GA." Switch outreach channel from email to LinkedIn InMail or direct Twitter/X DM for journalists who are active on social. For your highest-priority outlet, escalate to CEO/executive offering a direct 1:1 interview; journalists who decline PR emails often respond to CEO outreach. If the story still isn't landing, reassess whether your press angle matches what the target outlet actually publishes — trade press (Spend Matters, Procurement Leaders) may outperform tech press (TechCrunch) for a procurement-specific product.

**Problem: LA customers are satisfied but resist signing reference agreements before GA, threatening your press and sales proof strategy**
Solution: Prevention is far more effective than recovery — build reference agreement commitment into the LA acceptance letter so it is a documented expectation before they join the program, not a surprise ask at Week 12.
