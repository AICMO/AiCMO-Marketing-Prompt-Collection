## Overview
Design and optimize your entire go-to-market motion for an era where enterprise buyers delegate initial vendor research, shortlisting, and procurement preparation to AI agents. By 2026, AI assistants embedded in enterprise procurement workflows are filtering the market before human buyers engage. This engine builds the GTM architecture, content strategy, evaluation experience, and conversion workflows that win when an AI agent — not a human — is your first audience. Use when you're losing deals at the shortlist stage, when AI chatbots consistently omit you from category recommendations, or when you're redesigning your GTM for agentic procurement.

- "Starts at $X/month" or pricing model descriptor ("per seat", "usage-based", "flat rate")
- Why it works: AI agents that can't infer pricing tier often exclude vendors from enterprise shortlists or flag "pricing unclear" in comparison briefs — which causes human buyers to deprioritize us at the evaluation stage

A publicly accessible security documentation page at a stable URL:
- SOC2 Type 2 status, last audit date, and certification link

Create [Competitor name] vs. [Our Product] comparison pages for each major competitor:

- Why it works: AI models heavily weight vendor-authored comparison content when it appears authoritative and balanced — these pages become primary citation sources for head-to-head query types

- Why it works: G2 review volume is the single most mechanically reliable driver of AI citation frequency — models treat G2 as structured, verified customer evidence

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: We run AI citation queries weekly and see ourselves cited inconsistently — sometimes included, sometimes completely absent from the same query.**
Solution: AI chatbot citation is inherently probabilistic — the same query generates different results across runs and across AI models. To get a reliable baseline, run each query 10 times (not once) and calculate a "citation rate" (% of runs where we're mentioned). A citation rate below 20% indicates significant optimization work is needed; 40-60% is solid; 70%+ is strong market positioning. The two most mechanically reliable interventions are G2 review volume (each 100 additional reviews measurably increases citation probability in that AI model's category responses) and authoritative comparison content (AI models cite comparison pages from vendors when they appear balanced and specific). Focus on these two before anything else.

**Problem: We published our outcomes data page three months ago but AI tools still don't cite our statistics in comparison briefs.**
Solution: A single owned page is insufficient for AI citation — models require multi-source corroboration to cite statistics confidently. The same quantified outcomes must appear on at least 3 independent surfaces: your website, G2 reviews (customers using similar language), published case studies, and ideally third-party analyst content or press coverage. Brief your CSMs to share specific statistics ("reduced MTTD by 67%") with customers when requesting G2 reviews and to encourage customers to include those statistics in their review text. When an AI model sees "reduced MTTD by 67%" on your website, in a G2 review, and in a case study, citation confidence increases dramatically vs. seeing it only on your marketing page.

**Problem: AI agents are citing us, but the descriptions they generate are inaccurate — they describe our product as an SMB tool or misidentify our category.**
Solution: AI models derive product descriptions from training data, which may include outdated content, competitor comparisons that frame us inaccurately, or old website copy. Audit the top 5 sources where AI models likely learned about us: our website's meta descriptions and H1 tags, our G2 profile description, our Crunchbase description, our LinkedIn company description, and the most-visited pages from organic search. Rewrite all five with consistent, specific language: "[Company] is an enterprise [category] platform used by [ICP title]s at [company size range] companies in [industries]." Consistency of language across owned profiles is the fastest way to correct AI model descriptions of your company.
