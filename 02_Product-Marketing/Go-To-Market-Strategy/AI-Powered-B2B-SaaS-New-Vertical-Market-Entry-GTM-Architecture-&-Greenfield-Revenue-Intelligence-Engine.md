## Overview

This prompt deploys an AI agent to design and execute a complete new vertical market entry GTM for a B2B SaaS company expanding beyond its founding vertical — covering vertical selection validation, ICP translation, positioning adaptation, proof architecture construction, sales motion design, and a phased activation roadmap. Use it when you have strong product-market fit in one vertical and need to systematically enter a second or third vertical without diluting execution focus in your core market.

SECTION 4: PROOF ARCHITECTURE FOR GREENFIELD MARKET

The biggest structural challenge of vertical market entry: you need proof to win deals, but you need deals to build proof. This section breaks the chicken-and-egg cycle.

A. DESIGN PARTNER RECRUITMENT PROGRAM (Days 1-60):

Design partners are not pilot customers. They are co-development partners who get product access and direct attention in exchange for active feedback participation, a case study commitment, and willingness to be a reference. Identify and recruit 3-5 design partners who:
- Fit the Tier 1 ICP you defined above
- Have a champion who is personally invested in solving the problem (not just curious)
- Will commit to a structured 90-day evaluation with defined success criteria
- Have agreed in writing to participate in a case study if success criteria are met
- Are willing to be reference calls for subsequent prospects in the vertical

Design partner sourcing channels:
- Existing network connections in the vertical (executive LinkedIn connections, conference relationships)
- Inbound interest from the vertical that came without dedicated outbound (convert to structured design partnership)
- Vertical-specific investor/advisor portfolio connections (VCs with healthcare or finserv portfolios often facilitate introductions)
- Trade association relationships (partner with the association to co-create a "pilot program" for members)
- Agency or implementation partner connections in the vertical (they have customer relationships you can access)

B. PROOF ASSET SEQUENCING (Months 1-6):

Month 1-2: Capture proof-in-process (blog posts about the problem, not your solution; working with [vertical] companies to understand [pain]; LinkedIn thought leadership from executive about the vertical's biggest challenge)

Month 2-3: Design partner interim results (permission-based data snapshots, "early results" posts on LinkedIn with the design partner's champion as co-author, quote from champion in company newsletter)

Month 3-4: Full case study from first design partner (quantified ROI, executive quote, company logo approved for use, 2-3 sentence success statement the reference will recite verbatim on reference calls)

Month 4-5: Peer review seeding (structured request to design partners to post G2/Capterra reviews focused on vertical-specific use case; target 5-10 reviews before first scaled outbound push)

Month 5-6: Analyst or trade press coverage (pitch vertical trade publication on the design partner's story; pursue Gartner Market Guide inquiry with two completed case studies in hand)

C. CREDIBILITY INFRASTRUCTURE (Months 1-3, parallel to design partner work):
- Join 1-2 key vertical trade associations and attend their regional and national events as a sponsor or exhibitor (not just attendee)
- Identify 2-3 vertical-specific thought leaders (consultants, analysts, former executives in the vertical) to engage as advisors or to co-create a point of view
- Build a dedicated vertical landing page and vertical-specific solutions page on the website within 60 days — even before you have case studies
- Apply for vertical-specific awards or recognition programs (trade publications often have "top solution provider" lists that are achievable without a large customer base)

D. PROOF VELOCITY TARGETS (leading indicators to track monthly):

Month 1: 3 design partner agreements signed
Month 2: First measurable result from a design partner (even a leading metric like time saved, not a business outcome)
Month 3: First public-facing proof artifact (case study excerpt, LinkedIn post with data, partner testimonial)
Month 4: First G2 or peer review from a vertical customer
Month 5: First trade press mention in a vertical publication
Month 6: First analyst inquiry or mention in a relevant analyst report

SECTION 5: SALES MOTION DESIGN FOR THE NEW VERTICAL

The sales motion for a new vertical requires vertical-specific enablement, because your AEs will encounter buyers who ask questions your team has never heard, use procurement processes your team has never navigated, and compare you to competitors your team has never faced.

A. VERTICAL SALES ENABLEMENT KIT (build before first AE call):

1. Vertical Buyer Fluency Guide (2-4 pages): The vocabulary guide every AE must internalize before the first call. Covers: what the buyer calls the problem (their language), the acronyms and regulatory frameworks they reference constantly, the top 5 questions every vertical buyer will ask on a first call, and the 3 things that will instantly signal "this vendor doesn't understand our industry."

2. Competitive Displacement Guide (specific to vertical): How to position against (a) the dominant incumbent in the vertical, (b) the "we built it in-house" objection, and (c) a horizontal competitor already in the vertical. Include specific discovery questions to surface which competitive situation you're in.

3. Vertical Business Case Template: A pre-built internal business case document the champion can customize and present to their Economic Buyer. Pre-populated with the vertical's KPIs, the typical ROI range from your design partners, and the "cost of inaction" framing the Economic Buyer responds to.

4. Vertical Compliance Package: The document bundle required by the vertical's procurement and legal team. Typically: SOC 2 report, relevant compliance certification, DPA, BAA (if required), subprocessor list, data residency documentation, penetration test executive summary. Ready to send within 24 hours of any legal/security inquiry.

5. Vertical Reference Call Guide: A one-page brief for your design partner references covering: what this prospect is evaluating, the 3 questions they're likely to ask, the 2 proof points the reference should emphasize, and how to handle the "what do you wish you'd known before implementation" question without it becoming a negative.

B. VERTICAL DISCOVERY FRAMEWORK:

Before standard product discovery, conduct vertical context discovery to establish credibility and inform how you position the product for this specific buyer:

- "Can you walk me through how your team currently handles [the core workflow your product addresses]? What does that process look like from start to finish?"
- "What systems are you using today to manage [the specific data/workflow]? Are those integrated with [core vertical systems like Epic, Salesforce FSC, etc.]?"
- "What's driving you to look at this now — is there a specific compliance change, operational pain point, or growth trigger?"
- "Who else is typically involved in evaluating a solution like this at your organization? Do you have a procurement or IT security process I should understand upfront?"
- "Are there other companies in [vertical] that you respect that you've seen solving this problem in a way you admire?"

C. VERTICAL-SPECIFIC SALES STAGES AND EXIT CRITERIA:

Stage 1 — Vertical Qualification:
Exit criterion: Confirmed budget range, confirmed the vertical's buying timeline aligns to your capacity to close in current quarter, confirmed champion can access Economic Buyer, confirmed no disqualifying compliance gap (e.g., a required certification you don't have)

Stage 2 — Vertical Discovery & Demo:
Exit criterion: Champion has seen a vertical-specific demo (not generic demo), champion has articulated the business case to you in their own words, AE has mapped the full buying committee (not just the champion), compliance requirements have been surfaced and addressed

Stage 3 — Vertical Business Case & Technical Review:
Exit criterion: Economic Buyer has reviewed the business case, technical review or security review initiated, POC scope defined (if required), competitive evaluation status understood

Stage 4 — Procurement & Legal:
Exit criterion: MSA redlined and in final review, compliance documentation package delivered and accepted, reference calls completed, implementation timeline confirmed with CSM

Stage 5 — Close & Vertical Onboarding:
Exit criterion: Contract signed, kickoff scheduled, success criteria for first 90 days agreed in writing, champion engaged for case study (if committed during design partner phase)

D. VERTICAL AE ENABLEMENT RAMP PLAN:

Week 1-2: Vertical immersion (read the vertical's 3 most important publications, join relevant LinkedIn groups, attend a vertical-specific webinar, interview 2 existing customers in or adjacent to the vertical)
Week 3-4: Sales play practice (role-play the vertical buyer discovery call, competitive displacement scenarios, objection handling for top 5 vertical-specific objections)
Week 5-8: Shadow and assist (AE shadows 3 calls from the design partner engagement process, then leads 2 calls with PMM backup)
Week 9-12: Full vertical AE ownership with weekly coaching session focused on vertical-specific deal progression

SECTION 6: MARKETING PROGRAM ARCHITECTURE FOR VERTICAL ENTRY

A. CONTENT PROGRAM — MINIMUM VIABLE VERTICAL CONTENT PACKAGE (60-day build):

Priority 1 (build first): Vertical solutions page on website — articulates the vertical-specific pain, your solution, key integrations, compliance posture, and a design partner quote or early result. This is the page a prospect evaluates after a cold outreach. It must scream "we understand your world."

Priority 2: Vertical-specific one-pager (PDF and web) — 2 pages max. Lead with the vertical pain, not your product features. Use the vertical's KPIs. One quantified result (even if from a design partner). Clear credibility markers (logo, integration badges, compliance certification icons).

Priority 3: Point-of-view content — A 1,000-word opinion piece on the vertical's biggest unsolved problem, written from your company's CEO or VP Product perspective, published in a vertical trade publication or on LinkedIn. This signals category authority before you have customers.

Priority 4: Vertical-specific case study template — Built before you have the case study, so the design partner conversation and data capture process is structured from day one.

Priority 5: Vertical outbound sequence — Email + LinkedIn message sequences written in the vertical's language, referencing the vertical's specific pain points, with subject lines calibrated to what vertical buyers open (research by looking at what vertical trade newsletter editors write as headlines).

B. CHANNEL AND EVENT STRATEGY FOR VERTICAL ENTRY:

Owned events: Do NOT start with an owned event in a new vertical. You have no brand to draw an audience. Instead, co-host a vertical event with a recognized vertical player (a trade association, an integration partner, or a vertical consultant) where their brand draws the audience.

Sponsored events: Identify 2-3 vertical conferences where your Tier 1 ICP is most concentrated. Target: one large national conference (brand visibility and AE meeting density) and one smaller practitioner or executive event (deeper conversations, easier access to buyers). Budget: $20-40K per event including booth, sponsorship, executive dinner hosting.

Speaking: Submit abstract to speak at vertical conferences 6-9 months in advance. Speaking proposals that get accepted are opinion-forward (a contrarian point of view on the vertical's dominant assumption) or data-driven (original research from your design partners). "Here's how our product helps [vertical]" will not get accepted.

Trade associations: Join the most relevant association in the vertical at a partner or sponsor tier. Attend their working groups. Contribute to their content (whitepapers, webinars, best practice guides). This is a 6-12 month credibility play, not a lead generation play.

C. DEMAND GENERATION IN THE NEW VERTICAL (Months 3-6):

Outbound: Until you have proof, outbound to the new vertical should be insight-led, not solution-led. Lead with a data point or perspective that's relevant to the vertical buyer's world — not your product. The goal of the first outbound touch is to earn the right to a conversation about their problem, not to pitch your solution.

Retargeting: Build a custom audience of vertical-specific job titles on LinkedIn and retarget them with vertical-specific content (the point-of-view article, the design partner case study excerpt). Budget: $3-5K/month at entry stage — enough to maintain visibility in a small, targeted audience without diluting spend on non-vertical accounts.

Intent data activation: If you have access to intent data (6sense, Bombora), create a vertical-specific intent topic cluster around the problems your product solves in this vertical's language. Alert AEs when Tier 1 vertical accounts show intent signals.

D. PARTNER CHANNEL FOR VERTICAL ENTRY ACCELERATION:

In many verticals, the fastest path to credibility is through a vertical-specific implementation partner, consultant, or systems integrator who already has trusted relationships with your target buyers. Identify 2-3 potential channel partners in the vertical who:
- Already sell to your exact Tier 1 ICP (they have the relationships)
- Offer complementary services (not competing products)
- Would benefit from adding your product to their portfolio (increases their average engagement size or service revenue)
- Can co-invest in go-to-market (joint events, co-branded content, shared pipeline)

Partner economics: In vertical market entry, the partner deal economics often need to favor the partner more than in your core channel program — because you're paying for access to their credibility and relationships, not just their distribution.

SECTION 7: VERTICAL ENTRY METRICS AND DECISION GATES

Define the metrics framework for the vertical entry — including the kill criteria (what signals tell you to stop) as clearly as the success criteria (what signals tell you to accelerate).

A. LEADING INDICATORS (tracked weekly):

- Design partner engagement rate (are your 3-5 design partners actively engaged or ghosting?)
- Vertical outbound meeting rate (what % of outbound sequences to vertical Tier 1 accounts result in a first meeting — target: >8% from a tight, well-researched list)
- AE vertical call quality score (are calls passing Stage 1 exit criteria — vertical qualification confirmed?)
- Content engagement from vertical (are vertical-specific blog posts or LinkedIn content generating engagement from vertical buyer profiles?)

B. VALIDATION METRICS (tracked monthly, evaluated at 90-day and 180-day gates):

90-Day Gate Metrics (should have achieved before scaling investment):
- Design partners: 3 active design partners with documented progress against success criteria
- Pipeline: At least $[X] in vertical-specific qualified pipeline (define "qualified" as meeting Stage 2 exit criteria above)
- Proof: At least 1 verbal commitment from a design partner to a case study
- Sales fluency: AEs can pass a vertical buyer fluency test (role-play with PMM scoring)

180-Day Gate Metrics (should have achieved before hiring dedicated vertical AE or PMM):
- Revenue: First vertical closed-won deal (even if smaller than target ACV)
- Proof: At least 1 published case study with quantified ROI
- Pipeline coverage: 3x target vertical ARR in qualified pipeline
- Reference willingness: At least 2 customers willing to take reference calls for new prospects

C. KILL CRITERIA — WHEN TO STOP:

The following signals indicate the vertical entry should be paused or abandoned:
- Zero first meetings after 500+ personalized outbound touches targeting Tier 1 accounts (indicates fundamental ICP mismatch or message failure)
- Design partners repeatedly defer success criteria review (indicates the problem is not urgent enough to prioritize)
- Three consecutive qualified pipeline deals lost to the same objection with no product or positioning solution on the roadmap (indicates structural barriers to win)
- Core vertical NRR declines by >5 points during the period of vertical expansion (indicates the core business is being starved of resources)

D. SCALE CRITERIA — WHEN TO ACCELERATE:

The following signals indicate the vertical entry is ready for increased investment:
- Win rate in vertical exceeds 30% on qualified pipeline (indicates repeatable motion)
- Reference call conversion rate from vertical proof (% of calls that result in advancement to next stage) exceeds current vertical's conversion rate
- Inbound from vertical increases to 20%+ of all inbound without proportional increase in vertical-specific marketing spend (indicates organic credibility building is working)
- NRR from vertical customers at 12 months exceeds core vertical NRR (indicates the vertical's buyers are high-expansion customers)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The sales team is using the new vertical as an excuse to avoid core vertical quota — claiming every CRE deal as "strategic" and deprioritizing core vertical opportunities.**
Solution: This is a classic resource arbitrage behavior that emerges when vertical entry is under-structured. Fix it by separating vertical pipeline from core pipeline in your CRM and tracking each independently. Set explicit rules: the vertical AE owns all vertical pipeline, core AEs own all core pipeline, and any deal that switches between tracks must go through a PMM-adjudicated process. Establish a vertical quota separate from core quota if the vertical AE also carries a core quota. If the team is systematically under-attaining core quota while claiming vertical as the reason, it's a distraction signal — return focus to core until the motion is healthier.

**Problem: Design partners are engaged and happy but will not commit to a case study or reference call — they cite legal, competitive sensitivity, or "not yet."**
Solution: Design partner proof commitment must be agreed at the start of the relationship, not asked for at the end. Rebuild your design partner agreement template to include: a specific case study commitment (even if anonymized), a reference call commitment (at minimum 1 call per quarter), and approval to publish aggregate results with the partner's verbal confirmation of the outcomes. If existing design partners won't commit, do not wait — recruit new design partners under the new terms. One named, quantified, referenceable design partner is worth more than five engaged partners who won't let you use their name.

**Problem: Vertical entry is generating pipeline but deals take 2-3x longer to close than deals in the core vertical — eating the sales team's capacity without converting to revenue.**
Solution: Extended vertical sales cycles at entry stage are almost always caused by one of three things: (1) you're selling to the wrong buyer level — you have a champion but haven't accessed the Economic Buyer, who is 2 levels above your champion in the new vertical's hierarchy; (2) the compliance package isn't ready, forcing a 6-8 week security review that stalls deal momentum; or (3) there's no proof the vertical's procurement team can find independently, so every deal requires extra reference-building time. Diagnose which of these three is causing the delay in your specific pipeline by interviewing the champion in 2-3 stalled deals. Then solve the structural root cause rather than trying to accelerate individual deals.
