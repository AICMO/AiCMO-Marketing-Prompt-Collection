## ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

Apply the following frameworks in your analysis:

**For Need Prioritization:**
Use RICE scoring methodology:
- Reach: Number of customers/prospects affected per quarter (from CRM, support volume, survey data)
- Impact: Revenue impact scale (1=<$10K ARR impact, 2=$10-50K, 3=$50-200K, 4=$200K-1M, 5=>$1M)
- Confidence: Data quality score (Low=25%, Medium=50%, High=80%, Very High=100%)
- Effort: T-shirt sizing relative to your team (XS=1, S=2, M=4, L=8, XL=16)
- RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence%) ÷ Effort

**For Feature Classification:**
Use Kano Model categories:
- Must-Have (Basic): Expected features whose absence causes dissatisfaction
- Performance: Features where more = more satisfaction linearly
- Delighter: Unexpected features that create strong positive surprise
- Indifferent: Features customers don't care about strongly either way

**For Strategic Opportunity Mapping:**
- Core: Deepens value for existing customers in existing segments
- Adjacent: Serves new segments with existing capabilities
- Transformational: New capabilities for new markets (high risk/reward)

## Integration Tips

- **Gong/Chorus:** Export call summaries with custom tags ("product gap," "feature request," "churn risk") and paste tagged excerpts directly into the prompt. Set up a recurring Gong Smart Tracker to auto-flag product feedback mentions.
- **ProductBoard/Aha/UserVoice:** Export feature request votes with requester ARR values from your CRM integration. This gives you ready-made Reach and Impact data for RICE scoring.
- **Salesforce/HubSpot:** Use custom fields for "Primary Loss Reason" and "Feature Gap Cited" on opportunities. Pull quarterly lost deal reports with ARR values — paste directly into Competitive Gaps section.
- **Notion/Confluence:** Output is structured for immediate paste into a product wiki. Create a template page called "PMM Monthly Market Brief" with sections matching this output format.
- **Zapier/Make:** Automate a monthly workflow: Typeform survey → AI analysis → Notion page creation. Set trigger on new NPS response from Delighted to route Detractor verbatims into a running Google Sheet PMMs review weekly.
- **Slack:** Create a dedicated #pmm-product-intelligence channel where the output brief gets posted before each product planning cycle. Pin the RICE-scored needs list for quick reference during sprint planning.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Product team says "we already know this" and doesn't change priorities.**
Solution: The issue is usually framing, not data quality. Shift from listing features to quantifying revenue impact. "47 customers mentioned better reporting" lands differently than "Reporting gaps are causing $380K ARR churn risk in next 90 days — here's the RICE score." Also pre-wire conversations with the CPO and key PMs 1:1 before the all-hands roadmap meeting. Surprises in group settings create defensiveness.

**Problem: Not enough data — I only have a handful of customer interviews.**
Solution: Even 5-10 interviews can be directionally useful if RICE confidence scores reflect the data limitation (use 25-50% confidence). Focus the brief on what you DO have evidence for and explicitly flag gaps: "We have insufficient data on [segment X] — recommend 3 discovery calls before Q3 planning." A brief with honest confidence levels is more credible than one that overstates certainty.

**Problem: The AI produces vague "customers want better UX" output without specific needs.**
Solution: Your input data is too abstract. Paste actual verbatim customer quotes, specific support ticket descriptions, or exact Gong transcript snippets rather than summarized themes. The more specific and raw the input, the more specific and actionable the output. If you only have summarized data, explicitly instruct the AI to "only make claims supported by specific evidence in the data I've provided."
