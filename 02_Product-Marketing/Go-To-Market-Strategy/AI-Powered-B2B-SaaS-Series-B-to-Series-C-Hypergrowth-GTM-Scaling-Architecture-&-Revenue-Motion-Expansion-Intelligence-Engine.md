## Overview
Architects a complete go-to-market scaling system for B2B SaaS companies at the Series B to Series C inflection point ($5M–$30M ARR), designing the multi-motion demand engine, organizational structure, and revenue operations infrastructure needed to reach $50M–$100M ARR. Use this when your founder-led growth is plateauing and you need to build a repeatable, AI-augmented revenue machine before your next raise.

## Example Input/Output

**Input Example:**
Company: Pathfinder AI — conversational AI platform for enterprise procurement teams
Current ARR: $7.2M
Target 24-month ARR: $25M
Primary ICP: VP Procurement and CPO at 500–5,000 employee manufacturing, retail, and distribution companies
Current GTM motion: Outbound-led. 3 AEs, 5 SDRs, 1 marketing coordinator

**Output Example (Section 1 — Revenue Math):**

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

To reach $25M ARR from $7.2M in 24 months, Pathfinder AI needs to generate $1.68M in net new ARR per quarter on average, accelerating to $2.6M in Q7–Q8. At a 18% close rate and $42K ACV, this requires 39 new logos per quarter at Q8 — 4x today's rate.

**The single most important recommendation**: Before building new demand gen motions, improve close rate from 18% to 24%. This single change generates ~$1.4M additional ARR with zero additional headcount or budget. Target: Win/loss analysis in Month 1, updated messaging and demo training by Month 2, measurable close rate improvement by Month 3.

**Revenue Math Summary:**

| Quarter | New Logo ARR Needed | Pipeline Required (at 18% CR) | Gap vs. Today |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------------|---------------|
| Q1 2026 | $1.1M | $6.1M | -$2.3M |
| Q2 2026 | $1.3M | $7.2M | -$3.4M |
| Q3 2026 | $1.6M | $8.9M | -$5.1M |
| Q4 2026 | $1.9M | $10.6M | -$6.8M |
| Q1–Q2 2027 | $2.3M–$2.6M | $12.8M–$14.4M | -$9M+ |

*Note: Gap shrinks to $7M at Month 12 if close rate improves to 24%*

**GTM Motion Portfolio Verdict:**

| Motion | SCORE Total | Verdict | Rationale |
|--------|------------|---------|-----------|
| Outbound SDR | 3.4/5 | Hold + Optimize | Working but CAC is $22K — needs better targeting to reach $16K target |
| Inbound (SEO/content) | 4.1/5 | **Build Now** | Procurement buyers research heavily before engaging; 7 of top 10 customers mentioned organic content |
| ABM (Tier 1, 50 accts) | 3.8/5 | Build Now | ACV justifies $2K/account/quarter spend; currently doing zero ABM |
| Product-Led Growth | 1.9/5 | Kill | Enterprise procurement teams won't self-serve; evaluation requires POC |
| Partner / integrations | 3.2/5 | Pilot Q2 | ERP partners (SAP, Oracle) have warm relationships with ICP; need one champion hire |
| Events | 2.8/5 | Hold | Trade shows convert but $18K CAC; attend strategically, stop sponsoring |

**First 3 Hires (priority order):**
1. **Demand Generation Manager** (hire by Day 30): Build inbound engine from scratch. At current pace, SDRs are doing all pipeline work. Without inbound, you will not hit Q3 targets regardless of outbound investment. Target: $2.5M in inbound-sourced pipeline by Month 9. Budget: $110K–$130K base + $35K demand gen budget.

2. **Product Marketing Manager** (hire by Day 45): Your 18% close rate vs. a 27% B2B SaaS median is a messaging and enablement problem. A PMM who interviews 30 customers in Month 1 and rewrites battlecards, demo scripts, and objection handlers will improve close rate faster than any other investment. Budget: $100K–$120K base.

3. **Content Marketing Manager** (hire by Day 60): Procurement teams read 5–7 pieces of content before requesting a demo. You publish 2 blog posts per month. This hire writes the content that makes inbound possible. Budget: $85K–$105K base.

## Success Metrics

- **Inbound pipeline %**: 12% → 30% of total pipeline by Month 9 (track by source in CRM)
- **Marketing-sourced ARR**: 0% → 25% of new logo ARR by Month 12

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: The revenue math shows you need 5–6x more pipeline than you currently generate, making the target look impossible.
**Solution**: First pressure-test your close rate and ACV assumptions rather than panicking. A 25% improvement in close rate (from 18% to 22.5%) and 10% increase in ACV together reduce the pipeline gap by ~35% with no new headcount. Second, challenge whether the ARR target itself is achievable in 24 months — sometimes the right answer is renegotiating the board target from $25M to $20M and hitting it reliably rather than missing $25M.

**Problem**: The GTM motion portfolio analysis says to kill your current primary motion, but it's the only thing generating revenue right now.
**Solution**: Never kill the primary motion until a replacement generates at least 40% of current motion volume. Transition gradually: move from "all budget on outbound" to "60/40 outbound/inbound" over 6 months. Set explicit milestones — if inbound doesn't reach $X pipeline by Month 4, increase outbound investment again.

**Problem**: You've completed the hiring plan but can't afford all 5 hires within the first 90 days given burn rate constraints.
**Solution**: Hire Priority #1 and #2 as FTEs immediately. For Priority #3–5, engage specialized agencies or senior contractors for 6-month engagements. This costs 30–40% more per hour but avoids 6-month salary commitments before you validate the motion. Convert to FTEs once the motion proves itself.
