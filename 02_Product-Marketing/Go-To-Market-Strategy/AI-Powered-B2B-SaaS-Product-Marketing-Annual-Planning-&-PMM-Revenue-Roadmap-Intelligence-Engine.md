### Deliverables to Produce

**1. 12-Month Launch Calendar with Launch Tier Architecture**

Build the complete launch calendar organized by launch tier:

**T1 — Major Company Launch** (full cross-functional GTM, analyst briefings, PR campaign, sales kickoff training module, paid demand gen activation, customer keynote or summit moment):
- Criteria for T1: projected ARR impact > $1M, opens new market or segment, category-defining capability, or competitive table-stakes catch-up
- Resource requirement: full PMM lead + 60+ days pre-launch runway + exec alignment

**T2 — Mid-Tier Launch** (cross-functional coordination, sales enablement update, email/social campaign, press release, 1 case study or proof asset):
- Criteria for T2: meaningful expansion of current use cases, adds to ICP positioning, addresses a frequent sales objection
- Resource requirement: PMM lead with 30-45 day runway, lighter exec involvement

**T3 — Minor Release Communication** (lightweight: in-app notification, release notes update, rep Slack update, addition to website changelog):
- Criteria for T3: incremental quality-of-life improvement, existing customer value add, minimal competitive or new-logo impact
- Resource requirement: PMM coordinator, 1-2 days prep

**Continuous release digest** (bundled monthly product update for customers and prospects — email + community post + social):
- Cadence: Last Thursday of every month
- Audience: All customers + opted-in prospects
- Format: 3-5 feature highlights, 1 customer spotlight, upcoming roadmap teaser

For each T1 and T2 launch assign:
- Confirmed launch date + internal kickoff date (go back 60 days for T1, 30 days for T2)
- Primary ICP segment targeted
- Revenue hypothesis (quantified): how this launch is expected to affect win rate, ACV, new logo acquisition, or expansion ARR — with math
- PMM DRI name and % allocation to this launch
- Cross-functional DRIs needed: Sales (training), Marketing (demand gen), CS (customer communication), PR (media), Product (technical spokesperson)
- Key milestone gates: messaging complete → assets complete → sales trained → external go-live → 30-day post-launch scorecard

**2. PMM OKR Framework & Revenue Attribution Model**

Design the complete annual OKR set for the PMM function organized by revenue lever:

**Pipeline-Sourced OKRs** (PMM launches and content that generate direct inbound pipeline):
- Launch-driven inbound: target MQLs/SQLs/opportunities sourced from each T1 and T2 launch
- Content and thought leadership: organic traffic, gated asset downloads, content-influenced pipeline

**Win-Rate OKRs** (sales enablement effectiveness):
- Competitive win rate by competitor (baseline → target)
- Rep training completion rate (% of AEs trained within 30 days of each launch)
- Rep confidence score on messaging (sales survey, baseline → target)
- Deal velocity improvement (average days to close, by segment)

**Expansion & NRR OKRs** (customer marketing contribution):
- Upsell/cross-sell pipeline influenced by PMM content or campaigns
- Customer NPS or CSAT impact attributable to PMM-owned customer communications
- Churn saves where competitive PMM content played a role

**Market Position OKRs** (brand, category, analyst coverage):
- Analyst ranking or recognition (Gartner, Forrester, G2 category)
- Share of voice vs. top 2 competitors (branded search, social mentions, analyst mentions)
- Media coverage: tier-1 placements per quarter

For each OKR specify:
- Baseline metric today (pull from CRM, Gong, or analyst report)
- Target metric for end of FY
- Data source and tracking methodology
- Attribution model (how PMM contribution is isolated from sales, CS, and other marketing efforts)
- Review cadence (monthly dashboard / quarterly QBR)
- Single AI agent or tool responsible for monitoring and alerting

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Product roadmap changes mid-year and the launch calendar becomes obsolete within weeks.**
*Solution:* Build the launch calendar with 75% fixed commitments (T1 and T2 launches confirmed with CPO) and 25% flex capacity for unplanned releases. Negotiate a formal roadmap change SLA with your CPO: any modification that affects a T1 or T2 launch requires minimum 6 weeks notice; T3 releases can change with 2 weeks notice. Connect your calendar directly to Jira or Linear with a Zapier integration that pings #pmm-launch-radar whenever an epic status changes to "Ready for GTM" — removes the manual tracking burden.

**Problem: Sales team consistently says PMM deliverables arrive too late or aren't useful in real deals.**
*Solution:* Flip the traditional sequencing — instead of PMM delivering assets at launch, embed a frontline AE or SDR into every T1 and T2 launch team from the 45-day kickoff. Run a "sales readiness checkpoint" 3 weeks before every launch: present the messaging to 3 AEs and ask them to pitch the feature in a mock discovery call. If they can't do it confidently, delay external communications until they can. Use Gong to run keyword analysis on calls from 60 days post-launch to confirm whether reps are actually using the new messaging — this becomes the accountability mechanism rather than training completion rate alone.

**Problem: CFO and CEO don't see PMM as a revenue driver and question the budget at every planning cycle.**
*Solution:* Build your "PMM Revenue Impact Proof" over the first two quarters so you arrive at the first annual planning meeting with data.
