## Overview
Transforms the chaotic post-acquisition period into a structured GTM integration program that unifies messaging, eliminates channel conflict, migrates customers without churn, and accelerates combined pipeline. Use it when your company has acquired a product or been acquired, and the PMM team needs to rationalize two GTM motions, two positioning architectures, and two sales teams into one revenue engine within 180 days.

This enables the revenue team to run targeted cross-sell sequences from Day 31 without manual account research. Build a custom Salesforce report for "Combined Customer Base by Acquisition Segment" before Day 1.
- **Gong / Chorus (conversation intelligence):** Create a "Post-Acquisition Objection Tracker" playlist in Gong to capture every customer and prospect question about the acquisition across all sales calls in the first 30 days. Feed these into the messaging update cycle weekly — this is your fastest feedback loop on narrative effectiveness.
- **Marketo / HubSpot Marketing Automation:** Build acquisition-specific nurture tracks for each of the three customer segments (acquirer-only, acquired-only, both). These tracks should automatically pause if a CSM marks the account "active conversation" in Salesforce, to prevent marketing and sales from creating conflicting messages to the same buyer simultaneously.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build an "Acquisition Integration Wiki" on Day 1 with sections for: unified messaging, channel conflict resolution rules, customer migration timeline, and open decisions. This becomes the single source of truth for all GTM team members and prevents information asymmetry between legacy acquirer and acquired employees.
- **Slack / Teams:** Create a dedicated #acquisition-integration-pmm Slack channel with automated weekly digest of: cross-sell pipeline created, acquired customer churn signals, analyst inquiry queue, and top sales objections from Gong. Keep the entire GTM team informed without requiring meetings.

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem: Sales teams are fighting over accounts and morale is collapsing.** This is the #1 acquisition failure mode and it happens when territory and comp alignment is delayed. Solution: Run the Rules of Engagement exercise (Section 3) in Week 1, not Week 4. Assign a dedicated "integration czar" from sales operations who can make binding territory decisions within 24 hours. Announce the Q1 comp structure for cross-sell deals before Day 30, even if imperfect — ambiguity is more damaging than an imperfect comp plan.

- **Problem: Acquired customers are churning at 2-3x the normal rate in the first 90 days.** This usually means the Day 1 message failed to neutralize fear. Solution: Trigger the executive escalation protocol (Section 4) for any at-risk account immediately. The acquiring company's CEO or CPO should personally reach out to the top 20 acquired customers within 2 weeks of close — this single action dramatically reduces early churn. Then run a "Retention Sprint" — identify every acquired customer with a renewal in the next 120 days and guarantee their current terms regardless of platform migration.

- **Problem: The combined pitch is confusing and reps don't know what to say.** This happens when integration architecture is ambiguous or when "how we sell together" hasn't been decided. Solution: Do not allow reps to freestyle a combined pitch. Issue a single approved "60-second combined pitch" script, test it in a half-day sales workshop with 10 volunteer reps, and iterate until 8 of 10 can deliver it confidently before rolling out to the full team. The pitch must answer three questions in order: (1) What did we acquire and why? (2) What do customers get that they couldn't before? (3) What specifically should you ask your current customers and prospects this week?
