## Overview
Generates targeted positioning, messaging, and proof frameworks specifically engineered for economic buyers — CFOs, Procurement Directors, and Finance/Legal committees — who control final B2B SaaS purchase approval. Use this when deals stall at budget approval, when the business champion loses the internal sale to Procurement, or when you need to proactively arm your champion with CFO-grade justification content before the economic buyer conversation.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The CFO wants a "benchmark" to validate pricing but you don't have published industry data.**
Solution: Use proxy sources: Gartner Magic Quadrant pricing guidance for the category, G2 pricing comparison pages, customer ROI studies from category analysts (IDC, Forrester Total Economic Impact reports are ideal). If none exist, commission a 1-2 customer TEI study — it's the single highest-ROI content asset a PMM can produce for economic buyer conversion.

**Problem: The champion says "I showed the CFO your one-pager and she said the ROI model isn't credible."**
Solution: This means the assumptions aren't grounded in the CFO's specific context. Add a "working session" step before the one-pager: the champion and CFO align on 3-4 baseline metrics together (current agency spend, current time-to-fill, target headcount growth). Then the ROI model uses those actual numbers. Never use industry averages if you can use their own data.

**Problem: Procurement keeps expanding their requirements (scope creep in the vendor assessment process).**
Solution: Set a formal "procurement scope agreement" in week 1 of the procurement stage. Document exactly which questionnaires, reviews, and approvals are required and get procurement sign-off on the list. This creates a mutual completion checklist and prevents unlimited expansion. In your prompt output, include a "Procurement Scope Letter" template the champion can use to establish this agreement.
