## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Competitors lead with FedRAMP authorization and our prospects treat it as a prerequisite we can't meet.
**Solution:** Reframe the FedRAMP conversation from "authorization status" to "security process maturity." FedRAMP is a government-specific framework optimized for a specific compliance environment — it is not a universal security benchmark. Respond with specificity: "FedRAMP authorization indicates a rigorous review process — and we've matched or exceeded those controls in our SOC 2 Type II audit across the availability, confidentiality, and processing integrity trust service criteria. Here's our audit summary." Then offer a side-by-side control mapping if the prospect asks. For government-specific buyers, acknowledge FedRAMP honestly and focus conversation on your roadmap if relevant.

**Problem:** CISO buyers engage early but go silent once the formal security review starts — you lose momentum for months.
**Solution:** The gap happens because you hand off the security review to an asynchronous questionnaire process with no human to maintain deal energy. Fix with three moves: (1) Assign a dedicated Security Review Advocate (a technical PMM or Sales Engineer) whose only job is to maintain momentum in active security reviews. (2) Set a 48-hour SLA on security questionnaire responses and communicate it proactively — this alone differentiates you. (3) Proactively schedule a "security kickoff call" at the start of every formal review — position it as onboarding the security team, not defending your posture. The call creates a relationship, surfaces concerns early, and keeps the deal alive through the review process.

**Problem:** Security messaging converts well for regulated industries (FSI, Healthcare) but falls flat for tech or media buyers who aren't bound by the same regulatory requirements.
**Solution:** For unregulated buyers, reframe compliance from "regulatory requirement" to "operational maturity signal." SOC 2 Type II becomes: "We've had zero security incidents affecting customer data in our four-year operating history, and we have 134 documented controls to prove why." ISO 27001 becomes: "Our security processes are designed to scale with you — as your team grows, our governance grows with you, not against you." These buyers care about vendor stability, contractual protections, and whether your security posture can handle their scale — not whether you're HIPAA compliant.
