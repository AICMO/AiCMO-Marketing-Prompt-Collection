# Category Design & Market Creation Engine - Own the Category, Own the Market

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25 min | **Tags:** category-design, positioning, b2b-saas, thought-leadership, market-creation, gtm

## Overview
Generates a complete category design strategy — including category name, POV narrative, ecosystem map, and lightning strike plan — for companies that want to define and own an entirely new market rather than compete in an existing one. Use it when your product is genuinely differentiated and existing category labels undercut your value or force you into head-to-head commodity competition.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a category design strategist applying the Play Bigger framework (Al Ramadan, Dave Peterson). My company needs to design and own a new category, not compete in an existing one.

My company: [Company Name]
What we do (plain English): [1-2 sentence description]
The problem we solve that no one else solves: [Unique problem statement]
Current category we're lumped into (and why it's wrong): [e.g., "CRM" or "project management"]
Our 3 biggest customers and their outcomes: [Customer 1, outcome; Customer 2, outcome; Customer 3, outcome]
Target buyer: [Job title, e.g., VP of Revenue Operations at Series B–D SaaS companies]

Deliver the following:

1. **Category Name** — a 2–4 word noun phrase that names the problem/solution space we own. It should feel inevitable in hindsight, not invented. Give 3 options with rationale.

2. **Category POV (Point of View) Narrative** — a 300-word manifesto that declares: (a) why the old world is broken, (b) what the new world looks like, (c) why this category must exist now, (d) why our company is uniquely positioned to lead it. Write it for a keynote opening, not a whitepaper.

3. **Ecosystem Map** — list 6–8 partner/adjacent players (complementary tools, analysts, influencers, adjacent categories) that would belong in our category ecosystem. Explain the role each plays.

4. **Lightning Strike Plan** — outline 3 high-impact, simultaneous market conditioning moves we should execute within 90 days to "declare" the category exists (e.g., research report, analyst briefing, community launch, proprietary data release, signature event).

5. **Category Tagline** — one sentence that articulates what category winners achieve that category losers never will.

Format as structured sections with clear headers.

**Section 1: Category Diagnosis**
Answer the 5 category design diagnostic questions:
1. What is the specific pain that no existing category adequately addresses?
2. What does the "before state" (stuck in old category) vs "after state" (category king) look like for a buyer?
3. What emerging technology, regulatory, or behavioral trend makes this category possible NOW (not 5 years ago)?
4. Why will there be one dominant category king (not multiple winners)?
5. What is the "category superconsumer" — the early adopter who most acutely feels the pain?

**Section 2: Category Name Development**
- Generate 5 candidate category names
- For each: pronunciation test, memorability score (1-10), analyst-friendliness score (1-10), defensibility score (1-10)
- Recommend the winner with full rationale
- Include the anti-names to avoid (and why)

**Section 3: Category POV Document**
Structure:
- Headline: [Bold declaration of the new world]
- The Old World (what's broken): 2 paragraphs using specific data points and named industry failures
- The Tipping Point (why now): 1 paragraph explaining what changed in the last 18–24 months
- The New World (what's possible): 2 paragraphs painting a vivid picture of life in the new category
- The Category King Manifesto: 1 paragraph declaring [Company Name]'s role and commitment
- Call to Action: A specific next step for the reader

Apply Jobs-to-be-Done theory: buyers don't buy products, they hire them to do a job. Name the job the old category was hired to do AND the superior job the new category does.

**Section 4: Ecosystem Architecture**
Map the full category ecosystem across 4 rings:
- Ring 1 (Core): Direct category participants (complementary tools buyers use alongside yours)
- Ring 2 (Enablers): Consulting/SI partners who implement and certify
- Ring 3 (Validators): Analysts, media, community leaders who shape category perception
- Ring 4 (Feeders): Adjacent categories that create demand for yours

For each ecosystem player: name, role, how to recruit them into the ecosystem, what's in it for them.

**Section 5: 90-Day Lightning Strike Plan**
Framework: All 3 strikes must land within the same 30-day window for maximum market conditioning effect.

Strike 1 — The Research Anchor:
- Proprietary research report concept (title, 3 core findings, target media outlets)
- Distribution strategy (who publishes it, how it gets amplified)
- Analyst pre-brief list (specific Gartner/Forrester/G2 analysts by name)

Strike 2 — The Community Declaration:
- Community or movement name
- Founding manifesto (3 principles)
- 10 founding member companies to recruit (by profile, not name)
- Launch event format and hook

Strike 3 — The Signature Content Asset:
- Format (e.g., State of [Category] annual report, certification program, benchmark study)
- Why it becomes the definitive industry reference
- How it creates annual renewal/media cycle

**Section 6: Category King Metrics**
Define the 5 KPIs that prove category leadership over 12–24 months:
- Search share of voice (category keywords vs. competitors)
- Analyst mentions/quadrant placements
- Media: category referenced without needing to explain it
- Pipeline: % of deals where buyer used category language unprompted
- Ecosystem: # of partners building native integrations

## Troubleshooting

**"The AI keeps generating generic positioning, not a genuinely new category."**
Fix: Your category insight is probably too weak. A true category insight must be counterintuitive — it should make 30% of people disagree with you. Push the prompt harder: "Give me 5 versions of this category insight, each more contrarian and controversial than the last." Pick the one that would make a category incumbent uncomfortable if published in TechCrunch.

**"The category name options all sound like buzzwords or already exist."**
Fix: Add this constraint to the prompt: "The category name must not appear in any existing Gartner Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave, or G2 category. Run each candidate through these filters and eliminate any that fail." Also try: "Name this category after the buyer's desired OUTCOME, not the technology or method."

**"The lightning strike plan seems too ambitious for our stage/team size."**
Fix: Add your constraints explicitly: "We are a team of 8, pre-Series A, with $15K to spend on category conditioning in the next 90 days. Redesign the lightning strike plan for this constraint. Prioritize earned media and community over paid distribution." The framework scales down — a lightning strike at seed stage might be a viral LinkedIn thread series + a niche Slack community + a co-authored guest post with one analyst.
