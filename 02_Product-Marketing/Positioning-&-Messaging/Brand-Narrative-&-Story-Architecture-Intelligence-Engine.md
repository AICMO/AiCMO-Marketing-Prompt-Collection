## Overview
Constructs a complete, investor-grade brand narrative architecture for B2B companies — from origin story through villain/problem framing through customer-as-hero journey — then cascades the master narrative into product-level stories, campaign-level briefs, and sales conversation frameworks. Use when your messaging feels fragmented, your differentiation isn't landing, or you're preparing for a rebrand, fundraise, or new market entry.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The villain feels generic or everyone nods but no one gets excited**
*Fix:* Make the villain more specific. "Fragmented data" is weak. "The 4am Slack message from your CFO asking why your forecast changed $2M from last week — and you don't know" is visceral. The test: read the villain statement to your best customer over the phone. If they laugh (in recognition) or say "oh god yes" — you have the right villain.

**Problem: The narrative sounds like marketing but doesn't change how sales teams talk**
*Fix:* The narrative cascade (Part 4, Section G) isn't being activated with sales. Run a 45-minute "narrative workshop" with AEs where you role-play the villain opening in discovery calls. Record one call using the narrative framework, listen back together, and iterate. Sales adoption requires behavior change practice, not just document distribution.

**Problem: The origin story feels either too self-promotional or too long**
*Fix:* Good origin stories are uncomfortable to tell. If yours sounds like a press release, you're hiding something authentic. The best origin stories include a failure, a moment of doubt, or a surprising insight. Use the format: "We started because [honest observation about what was broken] — and we failed at first because [what you got wrong] — but then we discovered [the key insight that changed everything]." Time yourself: the spoken version should be under 90 seconds.
