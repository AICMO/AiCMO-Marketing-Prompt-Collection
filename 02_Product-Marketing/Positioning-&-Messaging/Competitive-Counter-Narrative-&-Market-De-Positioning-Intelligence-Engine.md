# COUNTER-NARRATIVE FRAMEWORK
Apply the following frameworks:

**Narrative Judo:** Identify their #1 claimed strength. Find the inherent tradeoff in that strength. Reframe: "The very thing they're optimizing for is what makes them the wrong choice for [our ICP]."

**Category Redefinition:** Define a new category or sub-category whose evaluation criteria align with our strengths. Name it. Make the competitor look like they're solving yesterday's problem.

**FUD Neutralization:** Pre-empt the doubt they're planting about us. Address it directly with proof, then pivot to a counterattack.

**Proof Inversion:** Take their case studies and testimonials and identify what's conspicuously absent. The things they don't prove are often their real weaknesses.

**Velocity Asymmetry:** If we're newer/smaller, use it — frame their scale as bureaucracy, their legacy as technical debt, their market dominance as a reason they don't innovate for your segment.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The counter-narrative feels too aggressive or "slimy" — reps are uncomfortable using it.**
Fix: Shift from attack-mode to "reframe-mode." Instead of tearing down the competitor, focus on why your category definition better fits the buyer's actual problem. "Competitor X is excellent at [thing]. We're built for [different thing that matters more to you]." The best competitive battles are won by making the other horse irrelevant, not by making them look bad.

**Problem: The de-positioning isn't landing — buyers are still choosing the competitor.**
Fix: You may be attacking a perceived strength, not a real buying trigger. Run 5 win/loss interviews and ask: "What was the #1 reason you chose them over us?" The answer is almost always different from what you assumed. Rebuild the counter-narrative around the actual decision driver, not the marketing story.

**Problem: Competitor copies our positioning — now our counter-narrative feels like it applies to us too.**
Fix: This is a sign you're winning. When they copy your language, they validate your frame. Respond by going deeper — move to specific proof points (customer stories, metrics, integrations) they can't replicate. Abstract positioning is copyable. Specific proof is not.
