# Jobs-To-Be-Done Research & Messaging Translation Engine - From Customer Insights to Revenue-Converting Copy

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** jtbd, positioning, messaging, b2b-saas, product-marketing, conversion-optimization, voice-of-customer

## Overview
Applies the Jobs-to-be-Done framework (Christensen/Moesta) to raw customer data — interview transcripts, support tickets, sales call recordings, G2/Gartner reviews — and automatically generates persona-specific messaging matrices, landing page copy, email sequences, and sales talking points that map directly to what buyers are actually "hiring" your product to do. Use it whenever conversion rates are soft, messaging feels generic, or sales says "customers don't get it."

1. **JTBD Analysis** — Extract the top 3 Jobs customers are hiring this product to do:

   - Cold outbound email subject line + opening line (not "I hope this finds you well")
   - LinkedIn ad headline + description
   - One sales talk track opening that references the struggling moment

**MODULE 1: JTBD SYNTHESIS**

A. Identify the top 3–5 Jobs (ranked by frequency and revenue impact):
   For each Job:
   - Job statement (format: "Help me [verb] [object] [context/qualifier]")
   - Functional dimension: The rational, practical need
   - Emotional dimension: Internal feelings — relief, confidence, pride, fear of failure
   - Social dimension: External perceptions — how peers, boss, or board will judge them
   - Job executor: Who is performing this job (the user vs. the buyer vs. the champion)?
   - Job trigger: The specific event that created urgency to find a solution NOW (e.g., "missed a SLA for the second time," "board asked about data security posture," "competitor just cut pricing 30%")

B. Switch Moment Reconstruction:
   For the top Job, reconstruct the "switch interview arc":
   - First thought: When did they first realize they had this problem?
   - Passive looking: What low-commitment research did they do? (Google search terms, communities, Slack channels)
   - Active looking: What triggered them from passive to active evaluation?
   - Decision: What was the moment they decided to buy — and what almost stopped them?
   - Consumption: What happened in the first 30 days that confirmed (or undermined) their decision?

**MODULE 2: COMPETING FORCES & ANXIETIES**

For each of the top 2 Jobs:
- Non-obvious competing alternatives (what were they doing before — including "nothing," "spreadsheet," "offshore team," "internal build")
- Primary push forces (what was painful enough to make them leave the status quo)
- Primary pull forces (what attracted them specifically to you — go beyond "ease of use" and "ROI")
- Anxiety inventory (list 5 specific anxieties buyers have, e.g., "Will this break our existing workflow?" "Will I look bad if the rollout fails?" "Will we outgrow this in 18 months?")
- Habit inventory (list 3 inertia forces keeping them from switching, e.g., "data migration fear," "procurement process," "existing contract with competitor")
- Anxiety countermeasures: For each anxiety, suggest one specific proof point, guarantee, or content asset that neutralizes it

   - Old world: What was true before that's no longer sufficient?
   - Stakes: What's at risk if buyers don't adapt?
   - Promised land: What does winning look like? (describe the outcome, not the feature)
   - Magic: What's the unique capability that makes the promised land achievable?
   - Evidence: 3 proof points (customer stories, data, third-party validation)

B. Messaging Hierarchy:
   - Primary message (one sentence, the "why change, why now, why us" in 15 words or fewer)
   - Secondary messages (3 supporting claims, each tied to a specific Job)
   - Proof points per claim (quantified where possible: "reduced time-to-value from 6 weeks to 4 days")

## Integration Tips

- **Gong / Chorus / Grain**: Export call transcripts directly into Module 1 raw data. Use Gong's "Topics" filter to pull calls where specific objections or competitor names appear, then paste top 10–15 snippets as input.
- **G2 / Gartner Peer Insights**: Use the "What do you like best?" and "What do you dislike?" fields — these are the richest JTBD signals. Filter for your top ICP by company size and industry before exporting.
- **Notion / Confluence**: The output brief drops cleanly into a Notion page. Create a "Messaging Bible" database and link to this brief from every campaign brief template.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Update your deal stages to capture "struggling moment identified" as a field. Require reps to log which Job maps to each deal — this creates a feedback loop for future JTBD iterations.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate a monthly workflow: pull NPS verbatims from Delighted → format for JTBD input → feed into this prompt → post output to #marketing-insights Slack channel for team review.
- **Sales Enablement (Highspot / Seismic)**: Tag new talk tracks and email templates with the Job they address — so reps can filter by "Job: control over deal visibility" when searching for the right asset.
- **Landing Page Testing (VWO / Optimizely / Convert)**: Use the hero section copy from Module 4 as your challenger variant against your current control. Set primary metric as demo request rate, secondary as time-on-page.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "The output messaging sounds like everyone else in our category — generic and interchangeable."**
Solution: Your raw input data is too thin or too surface-level. Paste verbatim customer quotes rather than paraphrased summaries. Remove the features from the input entirely — only share outcomes and emotions. Force the prompt to work from struggling moments, not product capabilities. Add the "Anti-messages" instruction: "Explicitly flag any claim that appears in competitor messaging and do NOT use it."

**Problem: "We have multiple ICPs with completely different Jobs — the output tries to serve all of them and serves none."**
Solution: Run the prompt once per ICP segment. If your product genuinely serves 3 distinct buyer types with different Jobs, you need 3 different messaging tracks, not one unified message. After running per-ICP, use the Messaging Hierarchy Framework prompt to build a master architecture that nests all three without contradiction.

**Problem: "Sales won't use the new talk tracks — they say 'this isn't how real conversations go.'"**
Solution: The discovery call script in Module 4 needs to be tested live, not just reviewed in a doc. Run a 30-minute session where a sales rep role-plays the struggling moment opener with 3 different buyer types. Let them improvise the middle, but keep the opener and the bridge question fixed. Co-create the objection scripts with your top 2 AEs — their buy-in is more important than the perfect script.
