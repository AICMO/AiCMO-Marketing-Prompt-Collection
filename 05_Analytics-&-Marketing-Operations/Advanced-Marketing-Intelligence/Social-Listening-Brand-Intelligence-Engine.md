## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The signal input is too sparse — only a handful of mentions per week, making it hard to detect trends.**
Solution: Broaden your listening scope. Add industry-specific subreddits, niche Slack communities (many B2B verticals have public-facing communities), Quora topics, LinkedIn hashtags, and industry news RSS feeds. For review site gaps, set up G2 and Capterra weekly digest emails. Even 8-10 signals per week can yield actionable intelligence if they're well-curated.

**Problem: The AI is generating insights but they feel generic or not specific enough to act on.**
Solution: Improve signal quality before quality of synthesis. The more verbatim quotes, exact post text, and specific competitor press releases you include in the raw input, the more specific the outputs will be. Avoid summarizing signals before pasting — paste the actual text whenever possible. Also add more company context (ICP, positioning, current strategic focus) so the AI can calibrate relevance correctly.

**Problem: Too many low-priority signals are cluttering the report and burying the important findings.**
Solution: Add a pre-filter step. Before running the full Advanced Version, use a lighter prompt: "From this list of signals, identify the 10 most revenue-relevant items for a company with this ICP: [ICP]. Score each 1-10 for urgency." Then feed only the top-scored signals into the full intelligence engine. This two-pass approach significantly improves signal-to-noise ratio.
