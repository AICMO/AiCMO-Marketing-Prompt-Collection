## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** New hire is passive in week one — observing but not asking questions, pushing back on assumptions, or sharing honest reactions.
**Solution:** Passivity usually signals uncertainty about how much candor is actually welcome. Schedule a dedicated 30-minute 1:1 explicitly framed as "I want to hear what surprised you, confused you, and concerns you — and I will not be defensive." Model vulnerability first by sharing something you are uncertain about. Explicitly state that asking hard questions is a performance signal, not a risk.

**Problem:** The 30-day deliverable is descriptive and surface-level — summarizes what was learned but does not generate insights or make a real recommendation.
**Solution:** This almost always means insufficient raw access: too few customer calls or too little time with unfiltered pipeline and attribution data. Immediately assign three additional customer calls, give direct access to Gong call recordings from the last 90 days, and reframe the deliverable: "Show me one thing you now believe that we should change, and why you believe it."

**Problem:** New hire is excelling at the work but not building cross-functional relationships, particularly with Sales.
**Solution:** Relationship-building with Sales rarely happens organically when a new marketer is focused on output. Require attendance at pipeline review for 30 days as a listener. Assign a named SDR or AE as their "partner" for a shared demand gen experiment. Have the CMO make a direct introduction with a clear message: "I want you to treat [new hire] as a resource, and I want them to treat your pipeline as their responsibility."
