# DELIVERABLE 1: REVENUE-LINKED OKR FRAMEWORK

For each marketing role below, generate a complete OKR set using this three-tier structure:

**Metric Tier Architecture:**
- **Lagging metric (L)** — Revenue or pipeline outcome; measured quarterly/annually; most defensible to CFO
- **Leading metric (Ld)** — Pipeline or intent signals that predict revenue; measurable weekly or biweekly
- **Activity metric (A)** — Inputs the individual controls directly; measurable daily/weekly

**Required OKR design rules:**
1. At least 2 of the 3–5 OKRs must have a lagging or leading metric tied to pipeline or ARR
2. Each OKR must have a numeric target (not "improve," "increase" — give a specific number)
3. Include the data source for each OKR (e.g., "Salesforce pipeline report," "Google Analytics," "Gong call volume")
4. Flag if the metric is "owned" (marketer fully controls), "influenced" (marketer contributes, others affect), or "shared" (joint accountability with Sales or CS)

**Roles to cover:**

**CMO / VP Marketing:**
Build 5 OKRs. Must include: (1) marketing-sourced pipeline as % of total pipeline target, (2) pipeline-to-revenue conversion rate for marketing-sourced deals, (3) NRR contribution (expansion revenue influenced by marketing campaigns), (4) CAC payback improvement, (5) team AI adoption rate or productivity benchmark.

**Demand Generation Manager:**
Build 5 OKRs. Must include: (1) marketing-sourced pipeline ($), (2) MQA (marketing-qualified account) to opportunity conversion rate, (3) cost per opportunity created, (4) pipeline contribution by channel (paid, content, events, email — show each), (5) lead response SLA achievement rate.

**Content Marketing Manager:**
Build 4 OKRs. This role has the most measurement ambiguity — address it directly. Must include: (1) organic search pipeline influence (UTM-tracked sessions from content → demo/trial conversion), (2) content-influenced pipeline (multi-touch attribution), (3) content asset production velocity (# of pillar assets published per quarter at defined quality standards), (4) bottom-of-funnel content impact (comparison page traffic, SEO ranking for evaluation-intent keywords).

**Product Marketing Manager (PMM):**
Build 4 OKRs. Must include: (1) win rate on deals where PMM-produced battlecards were used vs. not used (tracked via Gong call tagging), (2) competitive displacement deals (deals where competitor account was converted), (3) product launch pipeline attribution (new pipeline generated within 90 days of launch), (4) sales cycle reduction in deals with PMM-produced collateral vs. without.

**Marketing Operations Manager:**
Build 4 OKRs. Must include: (1) marketing attribution coverage (% of pipeline opportunities with a documented marketing touchpoint — target: >85%), (2) lead routing SLA compliance (% of MQLs routed to correct owner within defined SLA), (3) campaign execution velocity (% of campaign briefs delivered on time vs. schedule), (4) data quality score (% of CRM records with valid email, industry, company size fields — target: >92%).

**Marketing Designer:**
Build 3 OKRs. Acknowledge this is the hardest role to connect to revenue — provide the most defensible path. Must include: (1) asset production throughput rate (design requests completed vs. submitted per sprint, quality gate: <10% revision requests after first pass), (2) ad creative performance contribution (CTR improvement for A/B tested ad creative vs. control), (3) brand consistency audit score (quarterly audit of published materials vs. brand guidelines — define the rubric).

# DELIVERABLE 2: VARIABLE COMPENSATION ARCHITECTURE

**Design philosophy:** Variable pay should reward outcomes the marketer meaningfully influences — not outcomes entirely controlled by the sales team or market conditions. Build in a "floor guarantee" during Q1 new-hire ramp (protect against pipeline lag) and a "stretch accelerator" above 120% target (reward exceptional performance without ceiling).

- Payment cadence: [Quarterly / Semi-annual / Annual] with rationale

**Variable % benchmarks by role (use these unless company comp bands dictate otherwise):**
- CMO / VP Marketing: 20–35% of OTE variable
- Demand Gen Manager: 15–25% of OTE variable (highest revenue line-of-sight)

**Company-wide variable cap rule:** No marketing role should have more than 35% of OTE as variable unless they have a direct quota-like assignment (e.g., CMO owns a hard pipeline sourcing number). Above 35% variable creates compensation volatility that increases attrition.

# DELIVERABLE 5: PIP PROTOCOL

Produce a structured 30/60/90-day performance improvement plan template with these components:

**PIP Initiation Criteria:**
- Two consecutive review cycles at Level 2 or below, OR
- Single cycle at Level 1, OR
- Documented failure to meet specific pipeline deliverable at >25% miss after 60-day informal coaching period

**Week 1 (PIP kickoff):**
- CMO + HR + employee meeting: deliver PIP document, review specific metrics and behavioral gaps, set weekly check-in cadence
- PIP document must include: current vs. required performance on each metric, specific weekly milestones for each of the 12 weeks, clear pass/fail criteria for each milestone, consequence statements (remediation or role change)

**Days 1–30: Stability Gate**
- Weekly 30-minute manager check-in with written summary to HR
- Milestone targets: [provide role-specific examples — e.g., "Demand Gen: pipeline created $150K in 30 days, 3 campaigns launched, all MQL SLA targets met"]
- Manager decision at Day 30: Continue / Accelerate closure

**Days 31–60: Trajectory Gate**
- Biweekly check-ins; mid-PIP summary delivered to CMO and HR
- Milestone targets: [cumulative — escalating specificity]
- Manager decision at Day 60: Continue / Accelerate closure / 2-week extension if close to pass

**Days 61–90: Final Gate**
- Final assessment meeting at Day 87 (gives 3 days for documentation before Day 90 decision)
- Pass criteria (must meet ALL): [specific metric targets at 90 days]
- Fail criteria: Any single critical metric more than 20% below 90-day target

**PIP Exit Paths:**
*Successful remediation:*
- Remove from formal PIP status; place in 90-day monitoring window
- Next performance review treated as fresh start; manager documents remediation in permanent file
- Variable pay payout for PIP quarter: 50% (not 0%, not 100%) — acknowledge partial success

*Role change (lateral):*
- If performance gap is role fit vs. capability issue, offer alternative role with salary protection for 90 days
- Document as voluntary transfer, not PIP outcome, to protect employee record

*Separation:*
- If Day 90 criteria not met, initiate separation with HR-compliant notice, severance per company policy, and non-disparagement agreement
- Manager does NOT deliver separation — HR conducts with manager as witness only

## Integration Tips

- **Lattice / Leapsome / Workday Peakon**: Import the OKR framework directly into your performance management platform. Map each OKR's data source to an automated data integration (Salesforce → Lattice pipeline metrics sync). This eliminates manual score entry and the recency bias that comes from self-reported OKR progress. Set automated weekly OKR health alerts — any KR more than 15% behind target triggers a Slack notification to the manager.
- **Salesforce**: Create a "Marketing Performance" dashboard with separate views for each marketer's OKR metrics. Add a custom "Marketing Role" field on the Lead and Opportunity objects to enable role-level attribution (e.g., "Demand Gen Manager — EMEA" vs. "Content Marketer" as campaign source). Use Salesforce Reports + Scheduling to auto-deliver each marketer's personal pipeline contribution report to their email every Monday morning — removes disputes over "whose pipeline is it."
- **HubSpot**: Build a Marketing Performance Properties set on the Contact object to track which marketing role touched a contact at each lifecycle stage. This enables "role-level attribution" — essential for content marketer and PMM OKR measurement. Use HubSpot's Revenue Attribution Report to auto-calculate content-influenced pipeline and deliver it monthly to the content team's review dashboard.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Configure keyword groups for each marketing role's impact zone — Content: track mentions of specific blog posts, reports, or white papers in sales calls. PMM: track battlecard topics and competitive mentions. Demand Gen: track campaign references ("we saw your ad," "we downloaded your guide"). Run monthly keyword reports and push results to Salesforce opportunity fields via Gong's native integration. This automates the "influence" attribution that previously required SDR manual logging.
- **Carta / Equity Platform**: When building compensation architectures, include an equity refresh analysis alongside the variable pay structure. High-performing Level 4+ employees who receive annual equity refresh grants are 40% less likely to leave than those relying solely on cash variable. The compensation framework is incomplete without equity — integrate the two systems so that performance review outcomes automatically trigger equity refresh nominations in your cap table management platform.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Create a single "Performance Management Playbook" page that houses all rubrics, OKR templates, review forms, and PIP templates. Version-controlled so managers always reference the current calibrated rubric. Link each marketing team member's individual OKR page to the playbook — one click from any team member's profile to the exact criteria for their role.
- **Slack**: Set up automated pipeline contribution notifications using Salesforce → Zapier → Slack integrations. When a marketing-sourced opportunity is created, the responsible marketer's Slack channel receives a real-time notification: "🎯 New pipeline: [Account Name] | Stage 1 | $84,000 ACV | Source: [Campaign Name]." This creates positive reinforcement loops that make the pipeline-to-comp connection visceral and immediate — rather than abstract during annual review.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Content and brand marketers refuse to accept pipeline-linked OKRs — "My work has long attribution cycles, you can't measure it"**
Solution: They're half right — direct pipeline attribution for content is hard, but "unmeasurable" is false. Counter with a hybrid model: 40% of their variable pay tied to leading indicators they control (organic traffic growth to buyer-intent pages, content asset production vs. plan, brand search volume trend), 40% tied to content-influenced pipeline via multi-touch attribution (measurable in Salesforce with proper UTM tracking), and 20% tied to a qualitative content quality score rated by a calibrated cross-functional panel quarterly. This gives them line-of-sight to payout on inputs they control, while holding a portion accountable to business outcomes. Present the math: at $100K OTE with $12K variable, their 40/40/20 split means the "unmeasurable" portion is only $2,400 at risk — a number that shrinks the resistance dramatically.

**Problem: Sales team disputes which pipeline is "marketing-sourced" vs. "sales-sourced" — OKR measurement is contested**
Solution: This attribution war must be resolved before variable pay goes live, or you will have a legal and morale crisis. Implement a binding attribution rule set in your CRM with finance sign-off: define "marketing-sourced" as "opportunity created within 14 days of a trackable marketing touchpoint (UTM, form fill, event registration, or review platform click) with no prior Sales activity on the account." Document this in a Revenue Attribution Policy signed by CMO and VP Sales. Build a joint "Attribution Disputes" process — any disputed opportunity goes to a weekly 30-minute CMO/VP Sales review, with no more than 5 disputes allowed per quarter before the attribution model is formally reviewed. Remove subjectivity by having the CRM data make the determination automatically — human judgment only for edge cases.

**Problem: Managers give everyone "Meets Expectations" to avoid conflict — performance reviews are meaningless**
Solution: This is the most common failure mode in B2B SaaS marketing performance management. Three interventions: (1) Require pre-calibration documentation — before any manager delivers a review, they must submit a written evidence brief for each direct report (max 2 pages: metrics vs. target, 3 specific behavioral examples, comparison to peer group). This forces confrontation with data before the emotional conversation. (2) Implement a calibration tax — any manager whose team has more than 50% at Level 3 or above must defend each rating in a CMO-chaired calibration call. The social friction of defending "everyone is meeting expectations" to peers is more effective than any process mandate. (3) Connect manager performance ratings to their own variable pay — add "Talent Development Quality" as a 10% weight in the manager's own variable structure, measured by their direct reports' performance improvement rate and internal promotion velocity. Managers who accurately identify and develop talent get paid more.
