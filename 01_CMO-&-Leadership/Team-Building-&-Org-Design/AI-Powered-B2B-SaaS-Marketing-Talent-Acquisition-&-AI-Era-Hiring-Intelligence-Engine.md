## SECTION 2: STRUCTURED INTERVIEW PROCESS

Design a 4-stage interview process that accurately predicts AI-native marketing performance.

**Stage 1: AI Fluency Screen (30-min recruiter call)**
Goal: Eliminate non-AI-native candidates before wasting hiring manager time.

Screening Questions:
1. "Walk me through how you used AI in your last 3 marketing campaigns. Be specific — which tools, what prompts, what outputs."
   - Pass: Specific tools named, describes agentic workflows or multi-step processes, quantifies output improvement
   - Fail: Vague references to "using AI", lists only ChatGPT for brainstorming, cannot describe a repeatable AI workflow

2. "What AI tool changed how you work most fundamentally in the last 12 months? How specifically?"
   - Pass: Describes workflow change with before/after productivity or quality impact
   - Fail: Describes single-use or experimental AI use without workflow integration

3. "How do you evaluate the quality of AI-generated marketing content before publishing or using it?"
   - Pass: Describes systematic QA process — fact-checking, brand voice calibration, audience testing
   - Fail: "I just review it" or shows no awareness of AI output quality risks

4. "Tell me about a marketing campaign you ran in the last year. What was the pipeline contribution and how did you measure it?"
   - Pass: States a specific number, explains attribution methodology
   - Fail: Cannot attribute pipeline to their work, only measures outputs (clicks, impressions)

**Recruiter Scoring (Pass/Fail gate to Stage 2):**
- Pass criteria: Score 3+ on AI fluency, must be able to name specific pipeline number

**Stage 2: Hiring Manager Interview (60 min)**
Goal: Assess strategic thinking, marketing fundamentals, and revenue orientation.

Framework Questions:
1. "Walk me through the most complex marketing program you've owned end-to-end. What was the business problem, your strategy, what you built, and the outcome?"
   - Probe: How did you measure success? What would you do differently?

2. "What does your current/most recent content production or campaign workflow look like? Walk me through from brief to published. Where are the AI handoffs?"
   - Probe: What does your AI prompt system look like? How do you maintain consistency at scale?

3. "Describe a time marketing generated pipeline that sales didn't initially credit to marketing. How did you make the case for attribution?"

4. "You've just joined. It's Day 30. What three things have you audited and what's your preliminary diagnosis of what's broken?"

5. "Walk me through how you'd build a content program that generates $500K in sourced pipeline with a $50K budget and AI tools. No agency spend."

## SECTION 3: AI SKILLS ASSESSMENT EXERCISE

**Exercise Title:** "90-Day Content-to-Pipeline Sprint"

**Setup (send to candidate 48 hours before live assessment or use async):**
"You've just joined [Company] as our [role title]. It's your Day 1. We want to see how you'd approach building a content-led demand generation program from scratch using AI tools you have access to right now."

**Assessment Prompt:**
Using any AI tools you choose (please list them), complete the following in 45 minutes:

Part A — ICP Research (10 min):
Research our ICP [provide brief: e.g., "VP HR at 200-500 person US SaaS companies"] and identify:
- Top 3 pain points they're actively searching solutions for right now
- 2-3 job-to-be-done statements in their own language
- What content format they consume (how you know this)

Part B — Content Architecture (15 min):
Design a 90-day content plan to generate 20 qualified leads per month from organic channels. Include:
- Core content pillar(s) and 5 specific article titles with target keywords
- Distribution channels and why
- How each piece maps to a funnel stage

Part C — One Complete Asset (20 min):
Using AI, write a complete draft of ONE content piece from your plan. It should be ready for editorial review (not just an outline). Include the AI tool and prompt you used to produce it.

**Scoring Rubric:**

Excellent (5):
- Part A: Identifies specific, non-obvious pain points with source rationale; uses language that sounds like actual buyer quotes, not generic marketing copy
- Part B: Plan is feasible, internally consistent, maps content to pipeline outcomes with measurement approach
- Part C: Draft is publication-ready at 80%+ quality, demonstrates voice/brand calibration, could pass editorial QA with minimal edits; prompt is sophisticated (multi-step, structured, includes constraints)

Meets Bar (3):
- Accurate but generic ICP research; basic content plan with reasonable topics; adequate draft with clearly AI-generated structure that needs significant editing

Red Flags (1-2) — Do Not Advance:
- Part A: Cannot name specific pain points or uses generic marketing language ("digital transformation", "efficiency gains") without specificity
- Part B: Plan is a list of blog posts with no pipeline logic or distribution strategy
- Part C: Output is clearly an unedited AI dump, no evidence of prompt engineering or quality judgment; cannot explain the prompt used or why

## SECTION 5: COMPETITIVE OFFER FRAMEWORK

Structure a competitive offer that closes top AI-native marketing talent.

**Offer Architecture:**
1. Base Salary: Anchor at 50th-65th percentile of market for stage — do not anchor at 75th+ without exceptional candidate justification
2. Equity: Lead with total equity value at current valuation AND at realistic 3x and 5x outcomes, not just % or share count
3. Performance Bonus: If applicable, tie to clear pipeline metrics candidate controls directly (not company revenue)
4. AI Tool Budget: $2,000-5,000/year personal AI tool budget is a powerful differentiator — names it explicitly in offer letter
5. Learning & Development: $1,500-3,000/year for conferences, courses, or workshops — signal investment in their growth

**Closing the Offer:**
1. Make the offer within 24-48 hours of final interview (top candidates have multiple offers)
2. Present offer verbally first — call hiring manager, not recruiter
3. Frame the equity narrative: "If we hit our Series C target by [year], your equity is worth $X"
4. Competing offer response: Ask for 48-hour decision window; do not engage in bidding wars above your approved band without VP-level approval
5. Rejection debrief: If candidate declines, conduct 15-min debrief call — this intelligence improves future hiring

**Employer Brand Signals (include in outreach and offer calls):**
- Specific AI tools and budget they'll have on Day 1
- Examples of AI-native work the team has already produced
- Career trajectory of previous marketers in this role
- Real pipeline numbers their work will influence

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: No qualified AI-native candidates applying despite good JD**
Solution: The issue is almost always sourcing, not the JD. Switch from passive job postings to active sourcing: search for marketers who publicly share AI workflows on LinkedIn, follow #AImarketing and #marketingai hashtags, post the AI assessment exercise itself as a LinkedIn post ("Here's the assessment we use to hire AI-native marketers") — your ideal candidates will self-identify in the comments.

**Problem: Strong AI assessment performance but poor revenue orientation**
Solution: This is the most common failure mode — technically AI-fluent candidates who've never owned pipeline. Add a mandatory screen question at Stage 1: "Name a specific pipeline number that your marketing work generated in your last role. Not influenced — sourced." If they can't answer, end the screen. Revise your JD to lead with pipeline expectations, not AI skills.

**Problem: Candidate accepts offer then leaves within 6 months**
Solution: Usually signals misrepresentation of role scope or lack of psychological safety to use AI tools at pace. Add to Stage 4: "We expect you to use AI tools to produce the first draft of almost everything in this role. Tell me about a past employer where that was or wasn't the norm — what happened?" Also audit Day 30/60 check-ins: if they're being asked to do things manually that AI should handle, you're losing them.
