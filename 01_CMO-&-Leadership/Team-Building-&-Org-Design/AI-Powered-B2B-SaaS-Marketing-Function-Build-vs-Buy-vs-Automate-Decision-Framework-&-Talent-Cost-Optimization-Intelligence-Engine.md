## Overview
Gives CMOs and marketing leaders a systematic AI-powered framework to decide which marketing functions to build in-house, outsource to agencies or freelancers, or automate with AI agents — then calculates the true cost, speed, and quality trade-offs of each model per function. Use it during annual planning, budget cuts, headcount reviews, or when standing up a new marketing team.

For each function I list, give me:

1. BUILD score (1-10): Is this worth hiring a dedicated FTE? Consider: strategic value, need for institutional knowledge, competitive advantage, meeting frequency.

2. BUY score (1-10): Is this worth outsourcing? Consider: commodity vs. bespoke, project-based nature, specialized expertise, cost per output.

6. RISK: Top risk of the recommended model and how to mitigate.

Then give me: a PRIORITIZED TRANSITION ROADMAP — what to change first, second, third to maximize output while controlling costs.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** AI gives generic cost estimates that don't reflect your market (e.g., San Francisco vs. Austin vs. remote-first).
**Solution:** Add your hiring location and remote policy to the company context section. Specify "fully remote" or "SF Bay Area" — fully loaded costs vary 40-60% by region.

**Problem:** Recommendations feel too conservative — AI keeps recommending hybrid when you want a clear yes/no.
**Solution:** Add an explicit constraint: "Give me a definitive BUILD, BUY, or AUTOMATE recommendation only. No HYBRID options. Force a single answer for each function based on what would deliver the highest ROI in the next 12 months."

**Problem:** The output doesn't account for transition costs (severance, agency exit fees, learning curve).
**Solution:** Add to your prompt: "For every function where I'm changing models, estimate the one-time transition cost: agency exit fees (typically 30 days notice), severance risk if in-house role is eliminated, ramp time for new model to reach current quality level."
