## Troubleshooting

**Issue: "We're being too negative about the competitor — it feels uncomfortable."**  
Solution: Challenger marketing is not attack advertising. Every comparative claim must be factually grounded in third-party data, customer quotes, or independently verified benchmarks. Replace "their product is bad" with "here's what buyers consistently report experiencing, and here's what our customers experience instead." Let the evidence be the argument, not the accusation. Always lead with your outcome, not their failure.

**Issue: "Our sales team doesn't use the challenger messaging — they go straight to product demo."**  
Solution: The challenger narrative only becomes sales behavior when it is practiced, not just trained. Run quarterly "Challenger Certification" sessions where every AE must deliver the 2-minute challenger story on video. Tie certification to commission plan (e.g., challenger-framed opportunities get 1.0x multiplier; deals that lead with product demo without challenger context get 0.9x). Make using the narrative the path of least resistance, not the optional extra.

**Issue: "The market leader just launched a campaign that directly attacks our positioning."**  
Solution: This is actually a win signal — you have gotten large enough to matter. Do not respond by abandoning your challenger identity; double down on it. The leader's attack ads legitimize you as the main alternative. Respond with customer evidence, not counter-advertising. Publish a "We must be doing something right" narrative to your community. Use the attack as earned media fodder. The challenger always looks better when the incumbent panics.
