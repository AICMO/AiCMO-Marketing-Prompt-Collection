# AI-Powered B2B SaaS CMO Annual Marketing Review & Year-End Revenue Contribution Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** b2b, saas, cmo, annual-review, year-end, reporting, roi, revenue-attribution, board-reporting, marketing-performance, pipeline-analytics, budget-justification

## Overview
Produces a comprehensive annual marketing performance review that aggregates full-year data across every channel, program, and investment to deliver an authoritative, board-ready narrative of marketing's contribution to ARR, pipeline, and company growth. Use this when preparing for year-end board meetings, building the FY+1 budget justification, creating the marketing chapter of investor materials, or conducting the post-fiscal-year retrospective that sets strategy for the coming year.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM**: Export closed-won opportunities with `Lead Source`, `Campaign`, and `Pipeline Stage History` fields. Use `Campaign Influence` reports (Salesforce) or `Revenue Attribution` (HubSpot) to populate channel revenue attribution data. Run a full-year opportunity report filtered by `Close Date = FY2025` and grouped by marketing campaign to build the channel scorecard.

- **Google Analytics 4**: Export annual organic search data with the `Landing Page + Source / Medium` breakdown and associated goal completions (form fills, demo requests). Use `GA4 → Reports → Acquisition → Traffic Acquisition` filtered by `organic search` and date range = fiscal year.

- **Paid Media Platforms**: Export full-year spend and conversion data from Google Ads (Tools → Reports → Predefined reports → Time → Annual), LinkedIn Campaign Manager (Analytics → Export), and Meta Ads Manager (Reports). Match conversion events to your CRM pipeline data for accurate ROAS calculation.

- **Gong / Chorus**: Use the conversation intelligence platform's deal analytics to extract win/loss themes for the competitive position section. Filter by `Closed Won / Lost` and `Competitor mentioned` to quantify which competitors appeared in deals and at what rate.

- **BI Tool (Looker, Tableau, Mode, Metabase)**: Build a fiscal year funnel conversion dashboard pulling from your CRM. Key query: stage transition rates (MQL→SQL, SQL→Opp, Opp→CW) with `created_date` between fiscal year start and end. Export as CSV and paste into the prompt.

- **SEMrush / Ahrefs**: Export branded and category keyword ranking data year-over-year. Use the `Position Tracking` tool with historical comparison to quantify share of voice change and identify ranking gains/losses for the competitive position section.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Attribution data is incomplete or disputed — sales team doesn't agree with marketing's pipeline figures.

**Solution:** Acknowledge dual attribution explicitly in the review. Present marketing-sourced (marketing was first touch on the account) separately from marketing-influenced (marketing touched at least one decision-maker before deal entry). Use the influenced number for the board's revenue contribution narrative, and propose a joint attribution governance project as a FY+1 initiative. This turns a political problem into a strategic project.

---

**Problem:** The output reads like a defensive justification document rather than an honest performance assessment.

**Solution:** Add this instruction to the prompt before running: *"Before writing any section, identify the 3 most uncomfortable truths in this year's data — the numbers that are most damaging to marketing's credibility if left unexplained. Lead with these in the executive summary, and address them directly in the learnings section. The goal is to be the person in the room who says the hard thing before someone else does."* Boards trust CMOs who are honest about misses more than those who spin them.

---

**Problem:** The year+1 priorities feel aspirational rather than data-driven — they look like the same priorities as last year.

**Solution:** Force a constraint: each FY+1 priority must reference a specific FY data point that directly justifies it. Add: *"For each Year+1 priority, cite the specific metric from this year's review that makes this the right investment — not industry trends or aspiration, but our own performance data. If you cannot cite a specific internal data point, the priority is not yet justified."*
