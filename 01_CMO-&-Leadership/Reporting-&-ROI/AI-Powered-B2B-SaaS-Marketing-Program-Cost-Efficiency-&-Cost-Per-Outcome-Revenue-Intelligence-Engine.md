## Overview
Deploys an AI agent to calculate, benchmark, and continuously optimize cost-per-outcome metrics (cost-per-MQL, cost-per-MQA, cost-per-pipeline-dollar, cost-per-closed-won) across every marketing program and channel — then generates a ranked action plan to reallocate budget toward the highest-efficiency programs. Use this weekly or monthly to prove marketing efficiency to your CFO and CRO, and to drive data-backed budget decisions.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: I don't have closed-won data attributed to programs yet (early-stage company)**
Solution: Use pipeline created as your primary efficiency metric and estimate cost-per-closed-won by applying your blended win rate (e.g., if win rate = 20%, multiply CPMQA × 5 to estimate CPClosed-Won). Be explicit in your report that closed-won attribution is estimated. Revisit in 90 days when you have more data.

**Problem: My attribution model is last-touch only and I'm worried it's making email look too efficient**
Solution: Run the analysis twice — once with last-touch data (what you have) and once by evenly distributing pipeline credit across all programs that touched the account before close (simple multi-touch approximation). Compare the two outputs side-by-side. This reveals programs that assist deals without getting last-touch credit. Use the self-reported survey ("How did you hear about us?") as a sanity check on the last-touch numbers.

**Problem: My SDR program costs are blended with Sales' budget and I can't separate them cleanly**
Solution: Use a 70/30 split attribution — allocate 70% of fully-loaded SDR cost to Sales budget (quota-bearing headcount, tools, training) and 30% to Marketing (outbound sequences, content, prospecting intelligence). This is a common convention. Document the split methodology in a footnote so Finance can adjust if they disagree.
