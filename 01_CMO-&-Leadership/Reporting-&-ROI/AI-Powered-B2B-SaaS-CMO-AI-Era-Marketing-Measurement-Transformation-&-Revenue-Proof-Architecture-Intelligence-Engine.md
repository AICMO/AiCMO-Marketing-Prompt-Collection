## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce:** Add a custom "Self-Reported Source" field to the Opportunity object (separate from standard Lead Source) to capture post-close survey data without corrupting your existing attribution data
- **HubSpot:** Use a Custom Property for self-reported attribution and build a Property-based workflow to auto-create tasks for marketing ops to review monthly
- **Segment / CDP:** Instrument the LLM SOV tracking in a simple Airtable or Google Sheet with weekly automated reminders — no CDP integration needed at first
- **Typeform / SurveyMonkey:** Use Typeform's conditional logic to ask follow-up questions based on which attribution source was selected (e.g., if "AI assistant" is selected, ask "What did you ask it?")
- **Looker / Tableau:** Build the 3-tier dashboard (weekly/monthly/quarterly) as separate views; the quarterly board view should be a polished PDF export, not a live link that can show unflattering intermediate numbers
- **Zapier:** Automate the weekly LLM SOV tracking by sending reminders to a dedicated Slack channel with a checklist of the 15 queries to run; log results in a shared Google Sheet

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** CFO pushes back: "Self-reported attribution is just people guessing — it's not real data."
**Solution:** Reframe with precision language: "Self-reported attribution is directionally accurate at the population level, even if imprecise at the individual level. When 61% of 47 customers independently report that peer recommendations influenced their awareness, that signal is far more reliable than our CRM's 'unknown source' field, which is 100% wrong. We're not replacing attribution — we're triangulating it." Cite Nielsen studies showing self-reported brand recall is typically within 15% of observed behavior at sample sizes >30.

**Problem:** Marketing ops team says the LLM SOV tracking is too manual and won't get done consistently.
**Solution:** Simplify to a 10-minute weekly ritual. Run 5 category queries (not all 15) across ChatGPT and Perplexity only (the two most commercially relevant). Assign this to one person as a standing Monday morning task. Build a simple Google Sheet with a tab per month where they log scores. After 30 days of data, you'll have a baseline that justifies investing in an automated LLM monitoring tool.

**Problem:** Incrementality geo-test shows inconclusive results — can't prove brand investment worked.
**Solution:** Inconclusive results are not a failure if you communicate them correctly: "We ran a controlled test. The data was inconclusive due to [sample size / external market factors / short test duration]. This tells us we need a longer test period, not that brand doesn't work. The test itself demonstrates marketing rigor." Extend the test to 2 additional quarters and refine the geo selection to reduce confounds. Document the testing methodology itself as a proof of marketing accountability.
