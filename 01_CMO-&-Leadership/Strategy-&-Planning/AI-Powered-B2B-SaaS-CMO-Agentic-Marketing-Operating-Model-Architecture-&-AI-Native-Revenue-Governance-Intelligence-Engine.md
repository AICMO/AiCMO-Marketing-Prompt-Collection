## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Executives or board resist the "AI runs our marketing" narrative — worried about brand risk or loss of control**
Solution: Reframe as "AI-assisted" rather than "AI-run" in all external communication. Show the governance framework prominently in board updates — emphasize that humans approve all strategic decisions and that agents have strict guardrails. Present the human override rate metric proactively as evidence of control.

**Problem: Agent outputs are consistently off-brand or low quality, causing high override rates (>30%) that eliminate time savings**
Solution: This almost always indicates a prompt engineering failure, not an AI capability limitation. Audit your top 5 most-used agent prompts and add: (1) 3-5 specific brand examples to imitate, (2) explicit "do not" rules for brand violations you've seen, (3) a structured output format that agents can't deviate from. Run 20-prompt validation tests before deploying to production. Also check: are you giving agents enough context about the buyer persona and their pain points?

**Problem: Can't attribute pipeline to agent-driven programs — finance and sales don't believe the ROI numbers**
Solution: Start with self-reported attribution surveys on every demo request and trial signup: "How did you hear about us?" Agents create dark social content that often shows up as "direct" in HubSpot. Also: run controlled experiments — run the same campaign type with agent execution vs. human execution in matched account segments to generate side-by-side ROAS data. This gives you empirical evidence that doesn't depend on tracking.
