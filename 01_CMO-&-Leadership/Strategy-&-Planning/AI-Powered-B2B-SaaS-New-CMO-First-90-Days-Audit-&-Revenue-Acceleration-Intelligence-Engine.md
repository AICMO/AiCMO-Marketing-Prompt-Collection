5. **Team Morale Sensitivity**: Does the Keep/Fix/Kill framework protect high-performing existing team members from feeling threatened while still identifying what needs to change?

- The 30-day listening tour agenda should produce 15+ stakeholder meetings with specific, non-generic questions for each

Produce this in Week 12 and circulate it to the CEO and CRO for pre-read before the board meeting — never walk into a board meeting with slides that the CEO hasn't already reviewed.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The output produces a generic "meet with stakeholders and listen" plan without specifics.**
Fix: Add more detail to the KNOWN ISSUES and BOARD EXPECTATIONS fields. The prompt needs to understand the specific pain points to generate situation-specific language. Paste in exact quotes from your hiring conversations with the CEO and CRO if you have them.

**Problem: The Keep/Fix/Kill matrix doesn't have enough "Kill" recommendations — everything gets a "Fix."**
Fix: Add a field explicitly stating your current marketing budget and what percentage is allocated to each major channel or program. The prompt will surface Kill candidates more aggressively when it can calculate negative ROI against specific spend. Also add your MQL-to-closed-won conversion rate — if it's below 1.5%, the prompt will correctly identify the lead quality problem as a systemic Kill-and-rebuild scenario.

**Problem: The CEO/CRO stakeholder language sounds too corporate and wouldn't survive contact with your actual CEO's communication style.**
Fix: Add a field describing your CEO's communication style (data-driven vs. narrative-driven, formal vs. informal, short weekly updates vs. deep dives). Add a sample quote from a Slack message or email your CEO sent before you joined — this calibration dramatically improves the authenticity of the generated language.
