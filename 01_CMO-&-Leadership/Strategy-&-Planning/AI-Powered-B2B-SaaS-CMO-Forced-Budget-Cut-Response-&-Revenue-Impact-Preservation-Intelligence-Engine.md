## Overview

When a board or CEO mandates a sudden 20–40% marketing budget cut mid-year, this engine analyzes your existing program portfolio by revenue attribution efficiency, identifies the minimum viable marketing investment required to protect pipeline coverage, and produces a cut plan with revenue-impact modeling, stakeholder communications, and a rebuild roadmap — all before a single dollar is moved. Use it the moment you receive a budget reduction mandate and need to respond within 48–72 hours with a defensible, data-backed plan.

Feed the pipeline attribution data directly into the Program Triage Matrix input fields. Create a custom Salesforce report that shows "Pipeline Sourced by Campaign Type" at weekly granularity — this becomes your RAE denominator.
- **HubSpot:** Use the "Revenue Attribution" report (Marketing Hub Enterprise) filtered to last 90 days by channel. Export the "First-touch Revenue" and "Multi-touch Revenue" columns — these are your attribution numerators. Set up a HubSpot dashboard that tracks pipeline coverage ratio weekly so you can monitor the MVMP circuit breakers automatically.
- **Marketo:** Run a Program Performance Report with multi-touch attribution enabled, filtered to the last 90 days. Use this to validate or challenge the attribution confidence scores in the triage matrix — Marketo's program influence data often surfaces hidden attribution that isn't visible in Salesforce campaign influence.
- **6sense / Demandbase:** Export your ABM account tier data before cutting ABM programs. Understanding which accounts are in active "Purchase" or "Decision" stage is critical for the ABM triage card — cutting ABM mid-funnel for active-stage enterprise accounts can kill deals in progress. This data should override the standard RAE score for enterprise programs.
- **Looker / Tableau:** Build a "Budget Cut Impact Simulator" dashboard that lets you toggle programs on/off and see real-time pipeline impact based on the last 90 days of attribution data. This is the visual tool to bring into the CEO meeting — stakeholders can see the tradeoffs interactively rather than reading a spreadsheet.
- **Google Sheets:** Use the Revenue Impact Bridge template as a Google Sheet that auto-updates from Salesforce/HubSpot via a Zapier integration. Share with CEO, CFO, and CRO as a live document that tracks actual vs. modeled pipeline impact weekly for 90 days post-cut.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Store the Reinvestment Trigger Roadmap as a Notion database with a "Status" property that updates weekly. Assign each cut program a "trigger owner" (the person who monitors the reinvestment metric). Set up weekly Notion reminders to check trigger status — this prevents reinvestment decisions from getting lost in quarterly planning cycles.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The board demands larger cuts than the model shows are safe, and the Revenue Impact Bridge doesn't move them.**
Solution: Reframe the conversation using the "net cash impact" lens rather than gross savings. If cutting $1M/year in marketing budget puts $700K in ARR at risk (0.7x multiplier), and each ARR dollar carries a 5x revenue multiple, the board is potentially destroying $3.5M in enterprise value to save $1M. Build a "valuation impact" line into the Revenue Impact Bridge: "These cuts save $1M in cash but put $3.5M in enterprise value at risk at current SaaS multiples." CFOs and investors respond to enterprise value math when revenue math doesn't land. Also offer a phased cut — 15% immediately with another 10% conditional on Q3 pipeline performance — this gives the board the optionality they want without forcing a binary bet on full cuts.

**Problem: Sales leadership refuses to accept pipeline gap and claims marketing should "find a way" to hit targets with less budget.**
Solution: Run a Pipeline Source Analysis for the last four quarters showing the historical correlation between marketing program spend and pipeline volume. Present the data as: "For every $10K we cut from paid search, we have historically generated $X less pipeline in that channel over the following 45 days." This converts the conversation from opinion-based ("I think marketing can do more with less") to math-based ("here is what the data says"). Also offer to run a 30-day experiment — cut one channel 50% and track pipeline weekly — before committing to broader cuts. This builds evidence and buys time.

**Problem: The AI substitution plan works on paper but the team's AI skills aren't sufficient to execute it within the 45-day timeline.**
Solution: Immediately prioritize an AI skills audit: identify which team members have the highest AI literacy and assign them as "AI substitution leads" for their respective program areas. For the highest-gap programs, budget $5–10K for an AI implementation sprint with an external AI marketing consultant who can set up the workflows, prompts, and quality review systems in 2–3 weeks. Include this cost in the cut plan as a one-time transition expense — a $7K AI onboarding investment that enables $25K/month in AI-substituted work pays back in week one. Do not allow the AI transition to become a 90-day project management initiative; it must be treated as an emergency capability build with a weekly checkpoint.
