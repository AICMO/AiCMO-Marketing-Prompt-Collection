## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Executives push back on reducing SEO investment, citing historical ROI.
**Solution:** Run the "AI Search Pipeline Hole" calculation from Deliverable 1 and show the 24-month trajectory. Frame it as *risk mitigation*, not abandonment. Propose a 6-month parallel track: maintain current SEO investment while launching two new channels, then evaluate based on pipeline data. Most executives respond to dollar-denominated risk, not channel theory.

**Problem:** Dark social and LLM attribution are hard to prove; finance doesn't trust self-reported data.
**Solution:** Use a triangulation approach: (1) self-reported attribution on forms, (2) branded search volume as LLM-influenced proxy (buyers who discover you via LLM often then search your brand name directly), (3) direct traffic trend. Show that the combination of these three metrics moves together with known community/LLM campaigns. Run a controlled experiment: publish a piece specifically designed for LLM citation and community sharing; measure branded search volume change in the 30 days following.

**Problem:** Building community takes 12-18 months; the board wants pipeline results in 90 days.
**Solution:** Don't start by building an owned community. Instead, activate in non-owned communities first — your team participates as genuine contributors in existing Slack groups, Reddit threads, and LinkedIn conversations where your ICP already spends time. This generates pipeline within 30-60 days and informs whether an owned community is worth building. Pair this with the partner program, which can generate pipeline faster through warm referrals from existing relationships.
