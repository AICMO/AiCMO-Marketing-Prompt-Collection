### DELIVERABLE 5 — RFM CUSTOMER SEGMENTATION & RETENTION PLAYBOOK

Build an RFM model to score every customer. Implement via Klaviyo custom properties or a SQL query on your Shopify/BigQuery data:

-- RFM Segmentation Query (Shopify + BigQuery)
WITH customer_orders AS (
  SELECT
    customer_id,
    email,
    COUNT(order_id) AS frequency,
    SUM(total_price) AS monetary,
    MAX(created_at) AS last_order_date,
    DATE_DIFF(CURRENT_DATE(), DATE(MAX(created_at)), DAY) AS recency_days
  FROM shopify_orders
  WHERE financial_status = 'paid'
    AND created_at >= DATE_SUB(CURRENT_DATE(), INTERVAL 365 DAY)
  GROUP BY 1, 2
),
rfm_scored AS (
  SELECT *,
    NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY recency_days ASC) AS r_score,   -- 5=most recent
    NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY frequency ASC) AS f_score,       -- 5=most frequent
    NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY monetary ASC) AS m_score         -- 5=highest spend
  FROM customer_orders
)
SELECT
  customer_id, email, recency_days, frequency, monetary,
  r_score, f_score, m_score,
  (r_score + f_score + m_score) AS rfm_total,
  CASE
    WHEN r_score >= 4 AND f_score >= 4 THEN 'Champions'
    WHEN r_score >= 3 AND f_score >= 3 THEN 'Loyal Customers'
    WHEN r_score >= 4 AND f_score <= 2 THEN 'Recent Buyers'
    WHEN r_score <= 2 AND f_score >= 4 THEN 'At-Risk Loyalists'
    WHEN r_score <= 2 AND f_score <= 2 AND m_score >= 4 THEN 'Cant Lose Them'
    WHEN r_score <= 1 THEN 'Lost Customers'
    ELSE 'Potential Loyalists'
  END AS rfm_segment
FROM rfm_scored;

**Marketing motion per segment:**

| Segment | % of list (typical) | Goal | Primary tactic | 30-day KPI |
|---------|---------------------|------|----------------|------------|

### DELIVERABLE 6 — LTV MAXIMIZATION PROGRAM

**a) Subscription conversion playbook:**

The highest LTV lever for D2C brands is subscription penetration. Every 10-point increase in subscription rate improves blended LTV by 35–50% (McKinsey benchmark).

Conversion triggers (implement as Klaviyo or Shopify flows):
1. **Post-purchase Day 45:** "Your [product] is running low — subscribe & save 20%"
2. **Second purchase:** Offer subscribe-and-save at checkout with "compare your costs" calculator showing annual savings
3. **Loyalty threshold:** When customer hits $100 LTV, offer subscription as VIP reward
4. **Win-back:** Lapsed subscribers get a "come back at 25% off for 3 months" reactivation offer

Subscription retention tactics:
- Pause > Cancel: Always offer pause (1, 2, or 3 months) as the first option before cancel
- Swap: Let customers change SKU instead of canceling (kills "I didn't like this specific product" churn)
- Frequency flexibility: Offer 30/45/60-day intervals — 45 days is often the natural replenishment sweet spot
- Skip without penalty: Removing fear of being trapped increases initial opt-in rate by 12–18%

**b) Cross-sell & upsell architecture:**

Customer journey → Cross-sell trigger matrix:

Purchased [Barrier Repair Serum]
  → Day 21: Recommend [Gentle Cleanser] — "complete your barrier routine"
  → Day 45: Recommend [Overnight Mask] — "the final step customers add after 6 weeks"
  → Day 90: Recommend [Eye Cream] — "your skin is ready for this"

Logic: Each cross-sell is positioned as the "next step in your skin journey" — not as a sale, 
as a progression. Customers should feel guided, not upsold.

**c) Bundle strategy:**

Design 3 bundle tiers anchored to customer willingness to pay:
- Starter Bundle: Hero SKU + trial-size companion (entry point, reduce first-purchase friction)
- Core Routine Bundle: 3-step system (25% discount vs. individual, best for conversion page)
- Complete Collection Bundle: Full regimen (highest AOV, best for gifting, VIP segment)

Bundle AOV target: 1.8–2.2x single-SKU AOV (below 1.8x = underpriced; above 2.2x = too bundled)

**d) Loyalty program design:**

Points currency: $1 spent = 10 points; 100 points = $1 reward (10% effective earn rate)
Tier structure:
- Glow (0–499 points): Free shipping on orders $50+, birthday offer
- Radiant (500–1,499 points): 15% off every 4th order, early access to new products
- Luminous (1,500+ points): Free product monthly, co-creation invites, dedicated concierge email

Referral mechanics: Give $20 / Get $20 — both parties get store credit on first referred purchase

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Email flows are running but revenue per subscriber is below $0.40/month — the lifecycle is not converting.
**Fix:** Check three root causes in sequence: (1) Deliverability — if open rates are below 15%, emails are likely in spam; run a deliverability audit via Litmus or Mailgenius and warm the sending domain. (2) Audience mismatch — if welcome series has high opens but zero clicks, your email subscriber acquisition source (e.g., a discount pop-up that attracted bargain hunters) is attracting non-buyers; A/B test a value-lead pop-up vs. discount pop-up and measure first-purchase rate from each source. (3) Message-market fit — if clicks are high but conversions are zero, the landing page is the problem, not the email; check PDP conversion rate for email traffic specifically.

**Problem:** Meta ROAS has been declining month-over-month despite increasing budget — returns are diminishing.
**Fix:** This is almost always creative fatigue combined with audience saturation. Run a creative diagnostics audit: pull frequency by ad set (anything above 3.5x in 7 days is fatiguing), check thumb-stop ratio (should be 25%+) and hold rate (15%+). If ROAS is declining on prospecting specifically, your lookalike seed audience quality may have degraded — rebuild LAL from last 180 days of purchasers, not all-time. If retargeting ROAS is declining, your retargeting window is too long; compress from 14 days to 7 days and exclude buyers.

**Problem:** LTV:CAC is stuck below 2.0 despite healthy new customer acquisition — the economics don't work.
**Fix:** Below 2.0 LTV:CAC means you're paying more to acquire customers than you're making per customer — the business will not survive at scale. Attack LTV first (faster and cheaper than reducing CAC): (1) Increase subscription penetration by 10 points (adds $25–$40 to LTV immediately), (2) Add one high-margin cross-sell email at Day 21 (benchmark: 12–18% attach rate), (3) Reduce churn from subscription by implementing pause-before-cancel (reduces churn 15–20%). Only after LTV improves should you focus on reducing CAC — trying to cut CAC while LTV is broken leads to under-investing in acquisition and starving growth.
