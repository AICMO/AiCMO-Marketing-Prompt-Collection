## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Trial signups are strong (200+/month) but trial-to-paid conversion is stuck below 10%, and most free users never log in after Day 1.
**Solution**: The activation gap is the real problem — low conversion is usually caused by a weak or missing "Day 1" experience. Run a single experiment: send a personalized video (Loom or Vidyard, 90 seconds) from the founder or a customer success teammate within 2 hours of signup, walking the user through their first action step. Companies that implement this typically see a 40–60% increase in Day 1 activation rates. The economics work even at SMB scale because activation improvement multiplies across your entire trial volume: at 200 trials/month, a 5 percentage point conversion lift = 10 additional paying customers = $1,500 in additional MRR from the same traffic spend.

**Problem**: G2 and Capterra show dozens of competitors with more reviews and higher ratings. Your category page ranking is below position 15 and driving zero traffic.
**Solution**: Don't try to out-review the category leader. Instead, find a subcategory where you can rank in the top 3. Search G2 for adjacent categories where your product naturally fits but has less competition (e.g., instead of "Project Management Software" where Asana has 12,000 reviews, target "Client Portal Software" or "Agency Management Software" where the top player may have only 300 reviews). Claim the subcategory, concentrate all review requests on getting to 50+ reviews there first, and achieve High Performer status in a less competitive grid. Once you rank #1–3 in a subcategory, the traffic and trial conversion is often higher because the buyer intent is more specific to your use case.

**Problem**: Paid social (LinkedIn, Meta) CPLs are 3–5x the maximum allowable SMB CAC, making paid channels unprofitable at this ACV.
**Solution**: This is a common SMB trap — most paid social platforms are not economically viable for sub-$2,000 ACV products without a very specific setup. Shift budget entirely to Google Search (bottom-funnel, competitor + category keywords) where intent is higher and CPLs are typically 2–4x lower than LinkedIn. In parallel, double down on organic compounding channels: YouTube SEO, Reddit/community participation, and G2. The rule for SMB: paid channels should only be used to harvest intent (Google Search), not create intent (awareness-stage social). Awareness should come from content and community at this ACV, which allows for near-zero variable CAC on organic volume.
