## Integration Tips

- **Clay**: Set up a Clay workflow to pull company enrichment (headcount, funding, job posts, LinkedIn activity) and auto-generate personalization variables for outbound. Export to Apollo or Instantly for sequencing. Refreshes daily — new triggers fire automatically.
- **HubSpot (Free Tier)**: Track all pipeline conversations in a custom "Marketing Source" field. Run a weekly report on marketing-sourced vs. sales-sourced pipeline to build your investor narrative.
- **Taplio + Buffer**: Schedule LinkedIn posts 2 weeks in advance. Set Taplio to auto-comment on posts from your top 50 target accounts — keeps the founder visible in their feed without manual engagement.
- **Apollo.io**: Build saved searches for your trigger-event ICP (recently funded, recent VP Sales hire, SDR hiring spike). Subscribe to daily email digest of new accounts matching triggers — review and add to sequence in 15 minutes/day.
- **Notion + Claude**: Build a content brief template in Notion. Each Monday, fill in the ICP pain point and one customer story. Paste into Claude with your content factory prompt. Copy outputs directly into your scheduling tools. Full content week produced in 60 minutes.
- **Loom**: Record a 45-second personalized Loom for your top 20 Tier 1 accounts each week. Use Vidyard to embed a clickable thumbnail in your outbound email. Reply rates on Loom emails average 3x standard text emails in B2B.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Cold email outbound gets zero replies despite personalization.**
Solution: The problem is almost always targeting, not copy. Run your last 100 sends through the trigger-event filter — if fewer than 60% had an active buying trigger (recent hire, funding, job posting spike), you're emailing cold-context accounts. Rebuild your list with Clay starting from trigger events, not firmographics alone. Also test subject lines: subject lines with the company name + a specific observation outperform generic "quick question" by 2–4x.

**Problem: Founder is writing LinkedIn posts but getting no engagement and no inbound DMs.**
Solution: Engagement drives reach — and the algorithm rewards comments over likes. For the first 30 days, the founder should spend 20 minutes/day commenting on posts from the 50 target accounts and 10 key influencers in the ICP's industry before publishing. This primes the algorithm and builds reciprocal engagement. Also: posts that share specific numbers ("we found that 78% of Series B companies can't forecast next quarter within 15%") outperform opinion posts 3–5x for professional audiences.

**Problem: Pipeline is growing but deal quality is low — too many small companies or wrong buyer.**
Solution: You've achieved volume but not precision. Go back to the ICP definition and add a negative filter: exclude companies below $X ARR, companies where the buyer title doesn't match (individual contributor vs. VP), and companies in industries with long procurement cycles you can't afford. In Clay, add a filter for "estimated ARR > $3M using LinkedIn headcount proxy" and "VP-level or above in title." Tighten outbound to 100 accounts/week with better fit rather than 300 with mixed fit.
