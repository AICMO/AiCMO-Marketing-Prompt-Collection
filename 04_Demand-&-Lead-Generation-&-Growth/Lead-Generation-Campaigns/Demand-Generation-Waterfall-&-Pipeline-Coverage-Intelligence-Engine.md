## Troubleshooting
**Problem: Conversion rates don't match CRM data — model outputs wildly inaccurate volumes.**
Fix: Most CRMs report stage entry counts, not stage-to-stage conversion (which creates double-counting). Pull "Leads created in date range" and "Leads converted in same date range" separately, then calculate the ratio manually. Use 90-day rolling averages to smooth seasonality. If CRM data is < 6 months old, use industry benchmarks (SiriusDecisions waterfall: MQL→SAL 45-60%, SQL→Close 25-30%) as starting estimates.

**Problem: Demand programs are recommended but they won't close in time for this quarter.**
Fix: Add a "time-to-pipeline" filter in Section 3. Any program with time-to-pipeline > (weeks remaining in quarter × 0.7) should be flagged "next quarter investment." Re-sort recommendations by speed: SQLs already in stage 3 can be accelerated in 2-4 weeks; new MQL programs have a 60-90-day lag to closed revenue. Focus on late-stage acceleration programs first if < 8 weeks remain in quarter.

**Problem: Board doesn't trust the AI waterfall model — prefers gut feel.**
Fix: Run the model in parallel with your current approach for one quarter, tracking model accuracy. When the model predicts "Q close at $2.1M" and you close $2.0M, that's your proof point. Present model confidence intervals (±15%) rather than single-point estimates to increase credibility. Tie model inputs directly to Salesforce report screenshots so the data lineage is transparent.
