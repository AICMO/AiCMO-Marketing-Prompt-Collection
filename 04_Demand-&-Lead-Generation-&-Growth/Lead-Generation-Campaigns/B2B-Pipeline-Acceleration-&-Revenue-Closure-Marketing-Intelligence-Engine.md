Use it when marketing owns pipeline contribution goals and needs to proactively accelerate deal velocity rather than waiting for net-new demand.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** AE pushes back — "marketing interfering with my deal."
**Solution:** Position every output as an "AE-controlled toolkit" — nothing ships without AE approval. Frame marketing's role as content creation and channel execution, not direct outreach. Have VP of Sales co-own the program and present results at QBRs as a joint win. Show AEs which specific deals closed faster (and for higher ACV) when marketing ran acceleration plays.

**Problem:** Champion kit content feels too generic — the CFO won't believe the ROI numbers.
**Solution:** Require AE to fill in 3 specific data points from discovery before generating the kit: (1) their current engineering team size and salary range, (2) one stated business goal from the prospect's public comms, and (3) one number the prospect mentioned in discovery (volume, error rate, time-to-insight metric). These 3 inputs transform a generic one-pager into a credible, deal-specific document.

**Problem:** The urgency plays feel manufactured and the prospect sees through them.
**Solution:** Never invent artificial urgency. Only use real constraints: actual cohort close dates, real executive travel schedules, confirmed fiscal quarter boundaries, or legitimate pricing/feature lock windows. If none of these exist, use peer pressure and FOMO instead — framing competitor activity or peer-company success stories as the urgency mechanism. "Three companies in your peer group signed last quarter" is always true and never fake.
