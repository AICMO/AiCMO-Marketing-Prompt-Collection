# Revenue-Backed Demand Generation Planning & Campaign Architecture Intelligence Engine - AI-Powered Revenue Math, Pipeline Coverage & Annual Demand Gen Blueprint

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25-30 min | **Tags:** demand generation, pipeline planning, revenue operations, campaign architecture, budget allocation, marketing strategy, B2B SaaS

## Overview

Starts from your revenue target and works backward through every conversion rate to calculate exactly how many MQLs, SQLs, opportunities, and influenced pipeline dollars you need — then builds the full campaign architecture, channel mix, and 12-month calendar to hit those numbers. Use during annual or quarterly planning to eliminate guesswork, justify budget with math, and give every channel owner their required contribution.

You are a demand generation strategist with deep expertise in revenue-backed marketing planning. Help me build a complete annual demand generation plan using backward math from revenue targets.

**My Company Profile:**

**Step 1 — Do the Revenue Math**
Work backward from the New ARR target:
1. Deals needed = New ARR Target ÷ ACV
2. Closed-won opportunities needed = Deals Needed ÷ [Win Rate %]
3. Pipeline coverage needed = Opportunities needed × [Pipeline Coverage Ratio, typically 3-4x]
4. SQLs needed = Opportunities needed ÷ [Opp Conversion Rate %]
5. MQLs needed = SQLs needed ÷ [MQL-to-SQL conversion rate %]
6. Leads/Contacts needed = MQLs needed ÷ [Lead-to-MQL conversion rate %]

Use these default benchmarks if I don't provide my own:
- Win rate: 25%
- Pipeline coverage ratio: 3.5x
- Opp conversion rate: 40%
- MQL-to-SQL: 30%
- Lead-to-MQL: 20%

Show the math in a table. Then break the annual numbers into quarterly targets (weight Q1/Q4 heavier for SaaS seasonality unless I specify otherwise).

**Step 2 — Channel Mix Recommendation**
Based on my ICP and budget, recommend a channel mix. For each channel provide:
- Estimated monthly lead volume
- Estimated CPL (cost per lead)
- Estimated CAC contribution
- Recommended budget allocation ($)
- Why this channel fits my ICP

Channels to evaluate: Content/SEO, Paid Search, Paid Social (LinkedIn, Meta), Outbound (SDR), Email Nurture, Events & Webinars, Partner/Referral, Review Sites (G2/Capterra).

**Step 3 — Campaign Architecture**
Design 4-6 integrated campaigns for the year. For each campaign:
- Campaign name and primary goal (Awareness / Consideration / Decision)
- Target audience segment
- Core offer/content asset
- Channels and tactics
- Expected contribution to annual MQL goal (%)
- Quarter to run
- Estimated budget

**Step 4 — 12-Month Campaign Calendar**
Produce a quarterly calendar showing which campaigns run when, key content drops, and major events. Flag sequencing dependencies (e.g., "Brand awareness campaign must precede retargeting launch").

**Step 5 — Success Dashboard**
List the 8 KPIs marketing owns to prove they're on track. Include leading indicators (weekly) and lagging indicators (monthly/quarterly).

**Step 2B: CAC Optimization Layer**
For each channel, model the full CAC contribution:
- CPL → CAC = CPL ÷ (Lead-to-MQL × MQL-to-SQL × SQL-to-Opp × Win Rate)
- Compare channel CAC to ACV and LTV
- Flag any channel where CAC > ACV/3 (unsustainable)
- Identify 2-3 "hidden efficiency" channels (often: SEO, partner, G2)

**Step 2C: Budget Allocation Decision Framework**

Tier your budget across three buckets:
- **Harvest (40-50% of budget)**: Channels capturing existing demand (Google Search, G2, Retargeting) — highest short-term ROI
- **Create (35-45% of budget)**: Channels creating new demand (LinkedIn, Content, Webinars, Events) — builds pipeline 60-90 days out
- **Experiment (5-15% of budget)**: New channels to test (podcast ads, community, CTV) — place bets, measure ruthlessly

Rule of thumb: Never let any single channel exceed 40% of total budget (concentration risk).

MODULE 5: MARKETING-SALES ALIGNMENT SLA & HANDOFF PROTOCOL

Define the exact agreement between Marketing and Sales to make this plan work:

**Lead Lifecycle Definitions:**
- **Raw Lead**: Any contact with name + email
- **Engaged Lead**: Opened 2+ emails or visited 3+ pages in 30 days
- **MQL**: [Define your specific scoring threshold — e.g., score ≥ 75 in HubSpot based on: job title match + company size + engagement activity]
- **SAL (Sales Accepted Lead)**: SDR has reviewed and confirmed ICP fit within [X] business hours
- **SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)**: Discovery call completed, BANT/MEDDIC qualification confirmed
- **Opportunity**: Formal sales stage, next step agreed, decision timeline confirmed

**Marketing → SDR SLA:**
- MQL routing SLA: SDR must action within [4 business hours] of MQL flag
- Marketing alerts SDR with: contact profile, signal summary, recommended outreach angle, relevant content to reference
- SDR rejection protocol: SDR may reject MQL only with documented reason (wrong ICP, already customer, duplicate) — feeds back to refine lead scoring

**SDR → AE SLA:**
- SQL handoff: AE receives warm intro email from SDR + deal card with: BANT summary, stakeholders identified, content consumed, discovery questions to ask
- No "cold" handoffs — AE must have context before first call

**Marketing's Pipeline Commit:**
- Define exactly what % of opportunities and pipeline marketing commits to source vs. influence
- Recommended: Marketing sources [X%] of opps (direct lead gen), influences [Y%] (multi-touch attribution)
- Track separately: fully marketing-sourced pipeline vs. marketing-influenced pipeline

MODULE 6: MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK & WEEKLY OPERATING RHYTHM

**8 Core Marketing KPIs to Track:**

Leading Indicators (review weekly):
1. MQL Volume vs. Target (weekly actual vs. weekly required run rate)
2. MQL Quality Score (% of MQLs accepted by SDRs as SALs — low SAL rate = quality problem)
3. Marketing-Sourced Pipeline Created ($) — opportunities created with marketing as first touch
4. Cost Per MQL by Channel (monitor for efficiency changes week-over-week)

Lagging Indicators (review monthly/quarterly):
5. Marketing-Sourced Closed-Won Revenue ($) and % of total revenue
6. CAC by Channel (calculate monthly, trend quarterly)
7. Marketing ROI: (Marketing-Sourced Revenue - Marketing Spend) ÷ Marketing Spend
8. Pipeline Coverage Ratio: Total Open Pipeline ÷ Quarterly Revenue Target (target: ≥3.5x)

**Weekly Marketing Operating Rhythm:**
- Monday: Channel performance review — paid CPL, organic traffic, email metrics
- Wednesday: Pipeline review with SDR lead — MQL volume, SAL rate, follow-up queue
- Friday: Campaign health check — active campaigns vs. pacing targets
- Monthly: Full funnel review with CRO/VP Sales — marketing contribution to closed deals, attribution analysis
- Quarterly: Plan vs. actuals review + next quarter budget reallocation

**Attribution Model:**
Recommend first-touch + last-touch + multi-touch reporting simultaneously:
- First-touch: Measures which channel initially created awareness (useful for brand/content investment decisions)
- Last-touch: Measures which channel drove conversion (useful for bottom-funnel optimization)
- Multi-touch (W-shaped): 30% first touch / 30% opportunity creation / 40% closed-won attribution (most accurate for B2B)

## Success Metrics

**Plan Quality Indicators:**
- Funnel math is internally consistent (every number traces back to revenue target)
- Budget allocation passes the "Harvest/Create/Experiment" 40-45-10 ratio test
- No single channel exceeds 30% of total budget
- Each campaign has a distinct audience segment (no overlap)
- Content map shows TOFU → MOFU → BOFU continuity for each campaign
- Risk register covers at least 4 distinct risk scenarios with early warning signals

**Execution Readiness:**
- Every campaign has an owner, budget, and start date
- SDR sequences are written for each major campaign's hero offer
- Marketing-Sales SLAs are documented and agreed before plan launches
- Weekly reporting cadence is set up in CRM before campaigns go live

**Financial Validation:**
- Marketing ROI projection: (Projected marketing-sourced closed won ÷ annual marketing budget) × 100 > 4x (target: 5-8x for established companies, 3-5x for early-stage)
- Blended CAC from plan ≤ LTV ÷ 3 (LTV = ACV ÷ churn rate)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The revenue math shows you need 5-10x more MQLs than you're currently generating — the target looks impossible.**
Cause: Either the revenue target is overly aggressive relative to current team/budget capacity, or the funnel conversion rates are severely underperforming benchmarks.
Fix: First, audit your conversion rates before accepting the target — many companies have MQL-to-SQL conversion of 15% when industry benchmark is 25-30%, meaning a quality/scoring fix doubles pipeline without adding budget. Second, model three scenarios: (a) hit target through volume only, (b) hit target through conversion rate improvement only, (c) hit target through ACV increase. Present all three to leadership — often the right answer is a combination. Third, negotiate the target with data: "To hit $5M new ARR with current team, we need $2.1M marketing budget. With $850K, the math supports $2.1M new ARR. Here's the plan for both scenarios."

**Problem: You build the plan, but 60 days in, half the campaigns aren't generating MQLs at the expected rate.**
Cause: Offer-audience mismatch (wrong content for the ICP), distribution problem (correct content not reaching the ICP), or conversion rate assumption was wrong.
Fix: Run the "3C Diagnostic" — Content (is the offer compelling?), Channel (is the ICP actually there?), and Conversion (is the landing page/CTA working?). Test one variable at a time. For content: run a 3-version offer test in 2 weeks. For channel: check audience overlap in LinkedIn — narrow to smaller, higher-fit audience even if reach drops. For conversion: A/B test the CTA — "Download Report" vs. "Get Your Benchmark Score" vs. "See How You Compare." Most underperforming campaigns have a distribution problem, not a content problem — the content is fine but it's reaching the wrong people or through the wrong channel.

**Problem: Marketing generates MQLs but SDRs reject 50%+ as unqualified — creating friction between teams.**
Cause: MQL definition is too loose (behavior-based scoring without firmographic qualification), or SDRs have too-narrow ICP definition relative to what marketing is targeting, or SDR team doesn't have context to understand why a lead is a good fit.
Fix: Hold a "MQL Review" session with SDR lead — review 20 rejected MQLs together to identify the pattern. Then recalibrate: add firmographic requirements to MQL score (company size, industry, job title must match before behavior score can trigger MQL), add a "contextual brief" to every MQL alert (what signal triggered this, what content they consumed, recommended outreach angle). Track SAL rate monthly — target >60% SAL rate. If <50%, MQL definition needs tightening. If >80%, MQL bar might be too high (good quality but potentially leaving volume on the table).
