## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Compliance assets are too generic to differentiate from competitors who also have SOC 2 certification.
**Solution:** Move beyond certification summaries to operational evidence — publish your specific incident response timeline (e.g., "average time to detect and contain a security incident: 4.2 hours"), your specific subprocessor security assessment methodology, and your CISO's personal response availability during their security review. Regulated buyers know that certifications are minimum bars; operational transparency is where trust is actually built.

**Problem:** Regulated buyers engage with compliance content but deals stall at CISO security review with no clear timeline.
**Solution:** Implement a Security Review SLA Program — when a prospect initiates formal security review, send a "Security Review Commitment Letter" from your CISO committing to a 30-day response SLA for all questionnaire components, weekly check-in availability, and a named security team contact for direct communication. This formalizes the review process and creates mutual accountability that accelerates timelines.

**Problem:** Risk committee rejects the deal despite CISO approval and strong business sponsor support.
**Solution:** The risk committee was never marketed to before they saw the deal — they're evaluating a vendor they know nothing about. Build a "Risk Committee Pre-Introduction Program": 60-90 days before risk committee consideration, identify risk committee members through LinkedIn and regulatory board databases, and deliver a 1-page "Vendor Risk Profile" directly to them through a third-party channel they trust (e.g., a Big4 partner's industry newsletter, a regulatory body publication). By the time the deal reaches the risk committee, they've already encountered your company in a context that pre-established legitimacy.
