## PART 5: LEAD QUALIFICATION & PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION

### LinkedIn Lead Gen Form (LGF) vs. Landing Page Decision Framework
Use LGF when:
- Offer is gated content, newsletter, or event registration
- Audience is cold (TOFU/MOFU) — reduce friction
- Mobile traffic is significant (LGFs convert 2–3x better on mobile)

Use Landing Page when:
- Offer is demo request or free trial (qualify intent with form length)
- You need multi-question progressive profiling
- Retargeting campaigns where visitor already has context

### Lead Scoring for LinkedIn-Sourced Leads
Grade A (route immediately to SDR):
- Job title matches VP/Director/C-suite ICP
- Company matches ICP firmographic criteria
- Source: BOFU campaign (demo request, pricing page visitor)
- LinkedIn engagement: viewed video 75%+, clicked pricing ad

Grade B (enter 5-day SDR sequence):
- Job title is Manager/Senior IC in buying committee role
- Source: MOFU campaign (downloaded benchmark report, webinar registrant)

Grade C (enter 30-day nurture sequence):
- Job title outside primary ICP but adjacent
- Source: TOFU content engagement

### CRM Integration & UTM Architecture
UTM structure for LinkedIn:
- utm_source=linkedin
- utm_medium=paid-social
- utm_campaign=[Campaign name — use consistent naming convention]
- utm_content=[Ad creative ID or name]
- utm_term=[Audience segment name]

HubSpot workflow: LinkedIn LGF → HubSpot integration → Contact created with LinkedIn original source → Lead score applied → SDR task created if Grade A

Salesforce workflow: LinkedIn LGF → Salesforce lead record → Lead source = "LinkedIn Paid" → Lead source detail = [Campaign name] → Assignment rule triggers based on territory

### Attribution Model
Primary: First-Touch + Last-Touch dual reporting (report both to understand LinkedIn's role in initiating vs. closing demand)
Secondary: Multi-touch linear attribution (equal credit to each marketing interaction in deal lifecycle)
Executive reporting: Pipeline Influenced (any LinkedIn touch in the 90 days before opportunity creation) + Pipeline Sourced (LinkedIn was first known touch)

Reporting cadence and dashboard:
- Weekly: Spend, CPL, lead volume by campaign
- Monthly: Pipeline sourced/influenced, cost-per-opportunity, MQL-to-SQL rate by LinkedIn campaign
- Quarterly: LinkedIn Ads ROI (pipeline closed-won attributable to LinkedIn / total LinkedIn spend), CAC contribution

## Success Metrics

- **Cost-per-pipeline-opportunity:** Primary ROI metric — LinkedIn Ads program is working if cost-per-opp is less than 10% of ACV (e.g., $2,500 for $25K ACV deal)
- **MQL-to-SQL conversion rate:** LinkedIn-sourced leads should convert at 20%+ to sales conversations; below 15% signals audience or offer quality issues
- **Lead-to-demo show rate:** Demo requests from LinkedIn should show at 60%+ (lower rates indicate lead form is too easy or audience is too broad)
- **Retargeting CPL vs. Cold CPL:** Retargeting campaigns should deliver CPL 40–60% lower than cold audiences; if not, retargeting audiences are too broad
- **Creative CTR benchmarks:** TOFU content ads: 0.35–0.7% CTR; BOFU demo request: 0.5–1.0% CTR; below 0.3% signals creative fatigue or audience mismatch
- **Pipeline influence rate:** At steady state (6+ months), LinkedIn Ads should be influencing 20–35% of all new pipeline opportunities via multi-touch attribution
- **Frequency vs. performance:** TOFU campaigns: maintain 2–4 frequency/month; above 6 = creative fatigue; BOFU retargeting: 4–8 frequency/month acceptable

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High CPL but leads aren't converting to sales pipeline**
Solution: The audience is too broad or the offer is too low-friction. Audit job titles on leads converting — if Manager and below are majority, tighten seniority targeting to Director+. Switch MOFU offer from open-access content to gated benchmark report or ROI calculator to pre-qualify intent. Add 2–3 qualifying questions to Lead Gen Forms (company size, current solution in use) to filter out non-ICP leads before they enter CRM.

**Problem: LinkedIn campaigns get impressions but very low CTR (below 0.3%)**
Solution: This is a creative problem, not an audience problem. The hook is not resonating. Test 3–4 new introductory text openings with radically different angles: stat-led ("87% of [Title]s say..."), question-led ("Still managing [pain point] in a spreadsheet?"), outcome-led ("How [Company] reduced [metric] by [%] in 90 days"). Also test Document Ads vs. Single Image — Document Ads typically achieve 2–3x higher engagement for content offers. Check frequency: if > 6, audience is burned; expand targeting or pause for 2 weeks.

**Problem: Retargeting audiences never reach 300-member minimum for activation**
Solution: Website traffic volume is likely too low for LinkedIn retargeting to function at campaign launch. Workaround: Start with contact upload audiences (CRM leads, event attendees, trial users) which activate immediately regardless of size. Simultaneously, run TOFU campaigns to build LinkedIn Engagement Audiences (video viewers, ad engagers) which populate faster than website pixel audiences for low-traffic B2B sites. Revisit website retargeting at 60-day mark when pixel has had time to accumulate sufficient audience size.
