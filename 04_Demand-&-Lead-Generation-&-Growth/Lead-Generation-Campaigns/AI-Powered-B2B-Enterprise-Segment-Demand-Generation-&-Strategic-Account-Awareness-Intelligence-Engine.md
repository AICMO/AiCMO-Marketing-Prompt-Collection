## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** MQA scores are high but sales acceptance rate is below 50% — SDRs say marketing leads aren't "enterprise quality."
**Solution:** Run a win/loss analysis on declined MQAs to identify which scoring criteria are false signals. Common fix: increase the firmographic scoring weight (revenue threshold, not just employee count) and reduce behavioral scoring for early-stage content like blog posts. Require at least one high-intent behavioral signal (pricing page, ROI calculator, demo request) for MQA qualification.

**Problem:** Executive roundtable acceptance rates are below 20%.
**Solution:** The invitation channel or framing is wrong. Enterprise CXOs respond to peer invitations — have your own CXO or board members personally invite contacts from their network first. Reframe the event from "industry dinner" to "advisory board session" or "peer exchange" — research participation, not vendor marketing. Send the invitation via LinkedIn message from your CEO, not email from your marketing team.

**Problem:** Research report downloaded widely but generating few MQAs or pipeline conversations.
**Solution:** The report is attracting wrong-level buyers (practitioners vs. economic buyers) or wrong-size companies. Adjust gate to require business email and company size field. Build a segmented follow-up sequence: if company meets enterprise ICP, SDR outreaches within 48 hours with the specific finding most relevant to their industry. Also create an executive summary version (1-page PDF) specifically for distribution to GC/CPO contacts by your SDR team.
