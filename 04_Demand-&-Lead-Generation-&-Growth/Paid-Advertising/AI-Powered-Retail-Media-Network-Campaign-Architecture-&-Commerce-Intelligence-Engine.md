## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Claimed ROAS is 8x but actual sales uplift is unclear.**
Fix: Run a retailer-native lift study (Walmart Brand Lift, Instacart Measurement, 84.51° Lift Study) with a matched-market holdout. Claimed ROAS includes halo and organic purchases; true incremental ROAS is typically 40-70% of claimed. Budget decisions should use incremental ROAS, not claimed.

**Problem: New-to-Brand % is below 20% — mostly reaching existing buyers.**
Fix: Shift from branded keywords (mostly loyalists) toward category generics and competitor conquesting keywords. Expand DSP/off-site audiences to "category buyers — brand not purchased in 12 months." On Instacart, add "Competitive Shoppers" audience overlay. On Kroger, activate "Brand Switcher" segment from 84.51°.

**Problem: Walmart Connect ROAS dropped during a Rollback promotion.**
Fix: During price promotions, ROAS drops because AOV is lower — but unit velocity and new buyer acquisition typically spike. Switch primary KPI to Cost Per Unit Sold and NTB Buyer Count during promotional windows. Increase bids +30-50% to capture the demand surge, then normalize post-event. Also ensure item shows "Rollback" badge in ad creative — this significantly lifts CTR.
