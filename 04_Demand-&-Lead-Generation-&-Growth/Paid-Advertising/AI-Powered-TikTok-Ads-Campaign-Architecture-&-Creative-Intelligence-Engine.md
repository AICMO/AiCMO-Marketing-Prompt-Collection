DELIVERABLE 3: TIKTOK PIXEL & SERVER-SIDE TRACKING SETUP

Standard Web Events (fire via TikTok Pixel JavaScript):
1. ViewContent — fires when product page loads (include content_id, content_type, value)
2. AddToCart — fires on add-to-cart click (include content_id, quantity, value)
3. InitiateCheckout — fires when checkout page loads (include total cart value)
4. AddPaymentInfo — fires on payment entry (signal for payment intent)
5. Purchase — fires on order confirmation page (include order_id, value, currency, content_ids)
6. Lead — fires on form submission for lead gen flows (include email hash)
7. CompleteRegistration — fires on account creation or trial signup

Server-Side Events (TikTok Events API — critical for iOS 14+ signal recovery):
- Mirror all Purchase and Lead events via server-side postback
- Pass hashed email + phone for matching (sha256)
- Include external_id (CRM contact ID) for cross-device attribution
- Recommended tools: TikTok's Shopify app (automatic), Elevar, or custom webhook via your backend

Shopify Integration Steps:
1. Install TikTok for Shopify app (Settings → Apps)
2. Enable pixel auto-configuration — all standard events fire automatically
3. Enable Events API (server-side) in TikTok Business Center → Data Sources
4. Verify event matching quality score in Events Manager — target > 7.0/10
5. Set 7-day click, 1-day view attribution window for conversion campaigns

CRM / Non-Shopify Integration:
- Use TikTok Pixel Helper Chrome extension to verify all events fire correctly
- Map Purchase event value to actual revenue (not product price) for ROAS accuracy
- Connect TikTok lead gen forms to CRM via Zapier or native integration
- Pass lead data to CRM within 60 seconds of form submission for SDR speed-to-lead SLA

DELIVERABLE 4: SPARK ADS STRATEGY (Boosting Organic Content)

Spark Ads allow you to boost existing organic TikTok posts (yours or creators') as paid ads — delivering the highest native quality score and social proof (real likes, comments, shares accumulate on the ad itself).

Organic Content Eligible for Sparking:
Priority 1: Customer testimonials with genuine engagement (>500 organic likes)
Priority 2: Founder/team content showing product in authentic use
Priority 3: Creator UGC that performed well organically (>1,000 views, >3% engagement rate)
Priority 4: Tutorial/how-to content that already ranks in TikTok search

Spark Ads Process:
1. Identify top-performing organic content (brand account + creator posts)
2. Request authorization code from creator (valid for 30 days; renew for ongoing ads)
3. In TikTok Ads Manager: Create Ad → Select "Use TikTok Post" → Enter post URL
4. Layer paid targeting on top of the organic post
5. The post's existing likes/comments display on the ad — amplifying social proof

Spark vs. Dark Post (Custom Identity) decision framework:
- Use Spark when organic post has >3% engagement rate OR creator has strong audience trust
- Use Dark Post (custom creative, no public post) for direct response offers, pricing mentions, or competitive claims you don't want on public organic content
- Recommended split: 60% Spark Ads (trust/social proof) / 40% Dark Posts (direct response)

DELIVERABLE 5: BIDDING STRATEGY & BUDGET OPTIMIZATION

BID STRATEGY SELECTION:

Lowest Cost (Algorithm-led):
- When to use: First 30-60 days; new campaigns; when establishing CPM/CPA benchmarks
- Behavior: TikTok spends your full daily budget at the lowest achievable CPA
- Risk: CPAs can spike; no ceiling; works best with learning phase of 50+ conversion events/week

Cost Cap (Target CPA control):
- When to use: After 50+ purchases/week on a campaign; want to maintain CPA while scaling
- Set cap at: [Your target CPA + 20%] to allow algorithm flexibility
- Risk: Underspend if cap is too aggressive; start high and lower by 10% every 5-7 days

Value Optimization (ROAS-focused):
- When to use: Shopify/e-commerce with varying AOVs; want highest-LTV buyers
- Requires: 50+ purchase events per week with value data
- Set target ROAS at 80% of break-even ROAS to allow learning room

Minimum daily budget per campaign: $50 (below this, algorithm cannot exit learning phase)
Recommended starting daily budget: $[Monthly budget ÷ 30 × 1.5] — over-allocate early to collect data faster

BUDGET SPLIT:
Baseline allocation for a $10,000/month budget:
  - TOFU (cold awareness): 20% = $2,000
  - MOFU (retargeting/consideration): 25% = $2,500
  - BOFU (conversion): 55% = $5,500
After 60 days with stable CPA: shift BOFU to 65-70%, reduce TOFU to 15%

SCALING PROTOCOL:
- Never increase budget by more than 20% in a single day (disrupts algorithm)
- Scale by duplicating the winning ad set with increased budget rather than editing the original
- Horizontal scaling: duplicate and vary audience (not creative)
- Vertical scaling: increase daily budget 15% every 3 days if CPA holds within 15% of target

MONDAY — Creative Performance Review:

□ Review audience overlap between ad sets (>50% overlap = internal competition)
□ Check frequency: if MOFU/retargeting audience seeing ad > 5× in 7 days, expand or refresh creative
□ Review TikTok Pixel Events Manager: confirm all events firing with matching quality > 7.0
□ Check budget utilization: campaigns spending < 70% of daily budget indicate bid or audience issue

FRIDAY — Budget Reallocation & Test Launch:
□ Reallocate 20% of budget from worst-performing ad set to top-performing
□ Launch 2-3 new creative tests (new hook only — keep everything else constant)
□ Add any high-intent retargeting audiences generated this week (new cart abandoners, engaged viewers)
□ Review creator content pipeline: confirm next week's UGC briefs are in production

MONTHLY — Strategic Review:
□ Attribution audit: TikTok reported CPA vs. actual CPA in Shopify/CRM (note view-through inflation)
□ Creative fatigue analysis: replace any creative > 30 days old or with frequency > 4 in core audience
□ Audience refresh: build new lookalikes from last 30 days' purchasers
□ Competitive creative audit: analyze top competitors' TikTok ads via TikTok Creative Center
□ Organic-to-paid pipeline: review which organic posts are candidates for Spark Ads promotion

## Success Metrics

**Creative Performance Metrics (track daily):**
- 2-second video view rate: Target > 15% (below = hook failure, replace creative immediately)
- 6-second video view rate: Target > 8% (measures sustained interest past the hook)
- CTR (click-through to landing page): Target > 1.0% for TOFU, > 2.0% for BOFU
- Video completion rate at 50% mark: Target > 20%
- Cost per 1,000 impressions (CPM): Benchmark $4-12 (varies by audience, placement, day of week)

**Conversion Performance Metrics (track weekly):**
- CPA (cost per purchase/lead): Must track both TikTok-reported AND actual CRM/Shopify CPA
- ROAS: Target varies by business model; use LTV-adjusted ROAS for subscriptions
- Click-to-purchase CVR: Target > 2.5% (if below, landing page or offer is the problem, not TikTok)
- Add-to-cart rate: Target > 6% (signal of product-audience fit)
- Checkout completion rate: Target > 55% (if lower, shipping cost or friction issue)

**Attribution Quality Metrics (track monthly):**
- TikTok Events API matching quality score: Target > 7.0 (below = signal loss on iOS devices)
- View-through vs. click-through attribution split: Flag if view-through > 60% of conversions (can indicate over-attribution)
- New-to-file customer rate from TikTok: Target > 65% (TikTok should be acquiring new customers, not just retargeting existing)
- TikTok CAC vs. blended CAC: TikTok should be within 30% of your blended CAC target

**Shopify / E-commerce:**
- Install TikTok for Shopify app for zero-code pixel + Events API setup
- Enable TikTok Shop (if eligible) to enable in-app checkout — removes friction, typically improves CVR 15-30%
- Sync product catalog to TikTok Business Center for Dynamic Showcase Ads (retarget with exact products viewed)
- Use Elevar or Triple Whale for server-side event verification and cross-channel attribution alongside TikTok native reporting
- Connect Klaviyo to TikTok Custom Audiences: sync "Abandoned Cart" and "Lapsed Purchaser" segments as retargeting audiences (update daily)

**HubSpot (B2B/Lead Gen):**
- Connect TikTok Lead Generation forms to HubSpot via native integration (TikTok Ads Manager → Tools → Lead Generation → Connect CRM)
- Map form fields: email, first name, last name, company → HubSpot Contact properties
- Set Lead Source = "TikTok Ads"; Lead Source Detail = [Campaign Name]
- Create HubSpot workflow: TikTok lead created → assign to SDR → send automated follow-up email within 5 minutes
- Track TikTok lead-to-MQL rate; if under 20%, tighten audience targeting or qualify harder on the form

**Northbeam / Triple Whale / Rockerbox (Multi-Touch Attribution):**
- Connect TikTok Ads via API integration for cross-channel view in single dashboard
- Use modeled attribution (not last-click) to capture TikTok's contribution to cross-channel conversion paths
- Set TikTok view-through attribution window to 1 day in your MTA tool (TikTok's native 7-day VTA window over-attributes)
- Compare TikTok self-reported ROAS vs. MTA-attributed ROAS — expect 20-40% gap; use MTA for budget decisions

**Zapier / Make Automation:**
- Trigger: New TikTok Lead Gen Form submission → Create/update contact in CRM → Notify SDR in Slack
- Trigger: TikTok CPA exceeds 2× target for 48 hours → Pause campaign → Alert paid media manager in Slack
- Trigger: New Shopify purchase with utm_source=tiktok → Tag contact in Klaviyo → Enroll in post-purchase upsell sequence
- Trigger: Weekly performance report pull via TikTok API → Populate Google Sheets dashboard → Email to marketing team Monday 8am

**TikTok Creative Center (Research Tool):**
- Use TikTok Creative Center (ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter) to spy on competitor ads
- Filter by industry, region, objective, and time period to find top-performing creative in your category
- Analyze hook patterns of top-performing ads — build your creative brief around what's already proven to work
- Use "Best Practices" section for your industry: TikTok publishes category-specific creative guidelines

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CPA is 2-3× above target after 2 weeks of running**
Solution: Diagnose the conversion funnel in this order — (1) CTR check: if CTR < 0.8%, the creative hook is failing — pause and replace with new hook variants before any other changes; (2) Landing page CVR: if CTR is healthy (>1.2%) but CPA is high, the landing page is the problem — test a TikTok-specific landing page that mirrors the ad creative's tone and offer (visitors expect native experiences); (3) Audience quality: pull the "Demographics" and "Interests" breakdown — if clicks are coming from demographics outside your ICP (wrong age, wrong country), add exclusions and tighten the audience; (4) Pixel verification: confirm Purchase events are firing and matching quality score is > 7.0 — poor pixel signal causes algorithm to optimize for wrong users.

**Problem: Great CTR but poor purchase conversion rate (< 1.5%)**
Solution: This is almost always a landing page or offer mismatch problem. TikTok audiences have high entertainment expectations — a generic product landing page kills purchase intent. Test three fixes: (1) Create a TikTok-specific landing page that matches the ad's visual style, uses social proof prominently, and has a single CTA; (2) If you're sending to your homepage, switch to a dedicated product/offer page — reduce clicks-to-purchase to maximum 2; (3) Check offer clarity: TikTok audiences are deal-responsive; if your offer isn't immediately clear in the first screen, add a banner or hero callout with your best offer (first order discount, free trial, free shipping).

**Problem: Performance dropped significantly after 30+ days despite no changes**
Solution: Creative fatigue is the most common TikTok performance killer. Check: (1) Frequency in your core retargeting audiences — if > 5 impressions per person per 7 days, your creative is over-served; pause and replace; (2) Check the "New vs. returning user" breakdown in audience insights — if >70% of your clicks are from users who have clicked before, expand or refresh your cold audience; (3) Launch minimum 3-4 new creative variants per month on always-on programs — TikTok's algorithm is particularly creative-dependent, with top creatives typically having a 3-6 week peak performance window before fatigue sets in.
