**Attribution Framework Selection:**
Choose methodology based on available technology and budget:

Option A — Pixel-Based Web Attribution:
- CTV exposure → IP-matched web visit within [X]-day window
- Platform: TVSquared (now Innovid), iSpot.tv, Comscore Campaign Ratings
- Limitation: IP address matching accuracy ~60-70%, household-level not individual
- Best for: Direct response campaigns with high-traffic websites (100K+ monthly visitors)

Option B — Incrementality Testing (Gold Standard):
- Geo-based holdout: split DMAs into exposed vs. unexposed test cells
- Matched market methodology: control for underlying demand differences
- Minimum duration: 4-6 weeks continuous flight
- Metrics: incremental site visits, incremental conversions, incremental revenue
- Cost: 10-15% of campaign budget sacrificed to control cell

Option C — Brand Lift Study:
- Platform-native (Hulu, Roku, Amazon) or third-party (Lucid, Dynata)
- Measures: aided/unaided awareness, message recall, purchase intent, favorability
- Sample size requirement: minimum 250 exposed + 250 control respondents
- Best for: Brand awareness campaigns where direct response metrics are secondary

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: VCR is below 85% on what should be non-skippable inventory**
Diagnosis: Check if "non-skippable" designation is being enforced across all supply partners — open auction OTT sometimes mislabels inventory. Run a supply path analysis in your DSP and exclude publishers with VCR below threshold. Also check creative file encoding — poorly compressed files cause buffering, leading to drop-off.

**Problem: Post-view attribution is showing high conversion numbers but incrementality test shows minimal lift**
Diagnosis: View-through attribution (VTA) is almost always over-crediting CTV. Users who would have converted anyway are being attributed to CTV exposure that happened to precede their purchase. Trust your incrementality holdout over VTA. Adjust attribution window down to 7 days VTA maximum and use cross-device match rate as a reliability proxy. Report incrementally-adjusted ROAS to stakeholders.

**Problem: CRM household match rate below 10%**
Diagnosis: Match rates depend on the data completeness of your CRM (work email vs. personal email, address hygiene) and the identity graph you're using. Steps to improve: (1) upload personal email addresses in addition to work emails — household matching uses home identity, not work; (2) append home address data via data enrichment vendors (ZoomInfo, Clearbit with residential data); (3) try alternative identity providers — LiveRamp vs. Neustar vs. platform-native match may vary 2-3x in match rate; (4) for B2B, consider IP-to-company matching as an alternative to household matching.
