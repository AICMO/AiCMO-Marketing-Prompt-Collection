## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Use native LinkedIn Lead Gen Form sync (Settings → Integrations → LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms)
- Map lead source = "LinkedIn Ads", lead source detail = campaign name using hidden fields
- Create a LinkedIn Ads workflow: auto-assign to SDR within 5 minutes of form submission
- Build LinkedIn touchpoint property to track pipeline influence across all contacts
- Use LinkedIn Ads ROI report in HubSpot to see pipeline and revenue per campaign

**Salesforce:**
- Install LinkedIn Sales Insights app for matched audience syncs from SFDC
- Create LinkedIn Ads campaign as a Campaign object; add members as leads/contacts convert
- Set Primary Campaign Source on opportunity when LinkedIn is first touch
- Build Campaign Influence report: opportunities with LinkedIn touchpoint vs. without
- Use Pardot/Marketing Cloud Account Engagement: sync lead gen forms directly to Pardot forms

**6sense / Bombora (Intent Data):**
- Export in-market accounts from 6sense at Stage 4-5 (Decision/Purchase) as CSV
- Upload to LinkedIn as Matched Audience (Company List)
- Overlay ICP persona targeting on top
- This combination — intent + job title — typically cuts CPL by 40-60% vs. demographic-only targeting

**Zapier / Make Workflows:**
- Trigger: New LinkedIn Lead Gen Form submission → Action: Create contact in CRM + Slack alert to SDR
- Trigger: New LinkedIn lead → Action: Enrich with Clearbit/Apollo → Update CRM fields → Route to correct SDR by territory
- Trigger: Lead status = "Disqualified" → Action: Add to LinkedIn exclusion audience (prevents wasted spend)

**LinkedIn Campaign Manager API:**
- Pull performance data daily into your BI tool (Looker / Tableau / Google Sheets)
- Key endpoints: /adCampaignsV2, /adAnalytics, /adConversions
- Automate weekly performance report: campaign spend, CPL, leads by creative, pipeline attributed

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CPL is 3-5× above target after first 2 weeks**
Solution: Diagnose in this order — (1) Check audience size: if under 50K, LinkedIn delivery becomes erratic — broaden or combine audiences; (2) Check lead quality in CRM — if leads are poor ICP, the issue is targeting not CPL; switch to TAL-based targeting; (3) Check offer relevance — if CTR is fine but form completion rate is under 8%, your offer or form fields are the problem — simplify the form to 3 fields and test a higher-value offer.

**Problem: LinkedIn leads don't convert to pipeline (low lead-to-MQL rate < 20%)**
Solution: The problem is almost always audience targeting, not creative. Pull the "Demographics" breakdown in Campaign Manager — if job titles showing conversions are not your ICP (e.g., students, recruiters at staffing firms, consultants), add exclusion audiences: company industries to exclude (staffing, consulting, education), seniority to exclude (entry level, training), and company sizes to exclude. Also add your existing customer list as an exclusion.

**Problem: Ads performing well but pipeline isn't showing LinkedIn as the source in CRM**
Solution: This is a tracking gap, not a performance problem. Check four things: (1) Is the LinkedIn Insight Tag firing on all pages (verify in LinkedIn Insight Tag Helper Chrome extension); (2) Are UTM parameters being captured by your CRM forms — test by submitting a test form and checking the contact record; (3) For Lead Gen Form leads, are the hidden UTM fields mapping to CRM source fields; (4) Is your CRM attribution window long enough — LinkedIn should get credit if any touchpoint occurred within 90 days of opportunity creation.
