DELIVERABLE 2: FULL-FUNNEL CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE

STAGE 1 — TOFU: CONTENT DISCOVERY (Traffic & Awareness)
Objective: Drive qualified readers to cornerstone content (blog, native article, tool)
Why content-first: Native ads perform 3-5× better when landing on editorial-style content vs. direct product pages — the ad-to-content transition must feel natural
Campaign type: Traffic / Content recommendation
Content strategy options (choose one or test multiple):
  - Option A — Native article/advertorial: Write a 700-1,200 word editorial piece on your site that addresses a burning question your ICP has; lead with insight, end with soft CTA
  - Option B — Existing blog post: Drive traffic to your highest-performing SEO article that maps to buyer intent
  - Option C — Data report / original research: Lead with surprising stat from your proprietary data
  - Option D — Free tool / calculator: Drive to an interactive asset that delivers instant value

Targeting parameters:
  - Interest categories: [e.g., Business & Finance / Technology / Healthcare / Entrepreneurship]
  - Behavioral segments: [e.g., Small business owners / IT decision makers / Recent home buyers]
  - Publisher categories: [e.g., Business news / Trade publications / Mainstream news]
  - Exclude: Low-quality traffic categories (entertainment gossip, clickbait sites, foreign language if English-only)
  - Geography: [Primary markets — be specific to minimize irrelevant spend]

KPIs for TOFU:
  - CTR target: > 0.10% (Taboola/Outbrain benchmark) | > 0.15% (MSAN benchmark)
  - CPC target: $0.30-$1.50 depending on audience and publisher quality
  - Time on page: > 90 seconds (indicates content resonance)
  - Bounce rate: < 65% (key indicator of audience quality)
  - Pages per session: > 1.5 (secondary engagement signal)
  - Pixel fire rate: > 85% of sessions should fire your retargeting pixel

STAGE 2 — MOFU: RETARGETING ENGAGEMENT
Objective: Re-engage TOFU visitors with deeper content and social proof
Audience rules:
  - All website visitors (last 30 days) — served gated content offer
  - TOFU article readers who spent > 60 seconds (last 14 days) — served case study / comparison content
  - Pricing/demo page visitors who did NOT convert (last 7 days) — served testimonial-heavy creative
  - Video viewers (25%+ completion) — served next video in sequence
  - Email list upload (matched audience) — served upsell/nurture content
Exclusions:
  - Existing customers (exclude customer CRM segment)
  - Recent converters (exclude from conversion campaigns for 30 days post-conversion)
  - Suppression window: 24 hours after clicking same ad (prevent overexposure)

Offer progression for MOFU retargeting:
  Touchpoint 1 (1-3 days after TOFU): Social proof content — customer story, outcome data
  Touchpoint 2 (4-7 days): Gated asset — report, checklist, template, or tool
  Touchpoint 3 (8-14 days): Demo/trial offer with specificity — "See how [Customer] achieved [Outcome]"
  Touchpoint 4 (15-30 days): Urgency/social proof combo — limited cohort, peer benchmark data

STAGE 3 — BOFU: CONVERSION CAMPAIGNS
Objective: Convert highest-intent audiences to trial, demo, or purchase
Target audiences (priority order):
  1. Pricing/features/demo page visitors — last 7 days
  2. High-engagement TOFU readers (3+ pages, 3+ minutes) — last 14 days
  3. Gated asset downloaders who have NOT booked demo — last 21 days
  4. CRM matched contacts at MQL/SQL stage
  5. Lookalike audiences built from converted customers (suppress originals)

Offer strategy:
  - Lead directly with outcome + social proof in headline
  - Use risk-reversal language: "free trial," "no credit card," "30-day money back"
  - Tight audience + tight offer = higher CPL but dramatically higher conversion rate downstream
  - BOFU landing page must match the headline promise word-for-word (zero native-to-page disconnect)

DELIVERABLE 3: NATIVE AD CREATIVE SYSTEM

HEADLINE STRATEGY (native success is 80% headline + thumbnail):

Proven headline formulas for native (use platform-specific character limits: Taboola 100 chars, Outbrain 90 chars, MSAN 90 chars):

CURIOSITY/PATTERN-INTERRUPT HEADLINES (highest CTR, best for TOFU):
Template: "[Specific group] are [doing surprising thing]. Here's why."
Template: "The [industry] strategy [Big Brand] doesn't want you to know"
Template: "Why [common belief] is costing [persona] [specific amount]"
Example: "Why 73% of B2B SaaS CMOs Are Wrong About Pipeline Attribution"
Example: "The Paid Ads Strategy That's Replaced Webinar Funnels at 500-Person Companies"

BENEFIT/OUTCOME HEADLINES (best for MOFU/BOFU, highest quality readers):
Template: "How [Company like theirs] [achieved specific outcome] in [timeframe]"
Template: "[Number] [persona] used [method] to [achieve result] — here's the playbook"
Template: "The [job title] guide to [outcome] without [common objection/pain]"
Example: "How Drift Generated $4.2M Pipeline from a Single Native Ad Campaign in 90 Days"
Example: "The VP of Marketing's Guide to Attribution That CFOs Actually Believe"

PROBLEM-LED HEADLINES (best for mid-awareness audiences):
Template: "If [problem they experience], you're not alone — here's what's changed"
Template: "Stop [doing thing they think works] — [better alternative] is working better"
Template: "The real reason [desired outcome] isn't happening for most [persona]"
Example: "Stop Blaming Your SDRs for Low Pipeline — Your Demand Gen Has a Data Problem"
Example: "If Your CPL Keeps Rising on Meta, Native Advertising May Be Your Answer"

DATA/RESEARCH HEADLINES (highest credibility, works in premium publisher environments):
Template: "[Research source]: [counterintuitive finding] — what it means for [persona]"
Template: "New data: [Percentage] of [persona] are planning to [action] in [year]"
Example: "Gartner: 70% of B2B Buyers Complete Their Journey Before Talking to Sales"
Example: "New Survey: The #1 Reason SaaS Companies Miss NRR Targets Isn't What You Think"

THUMBNAIL IMAGE STRATEGY (images drive 40-60% of CTR variance):
- Faces with emotion outperform product screenshots by 2-3× on consumer platforms
- Data visualizations (charts, stats in white space) perform best for B2B/finance audiences
- Contrasting colors (red, orange, yellow) against muted publisher environments drive CTR
- Avoid: stock photos of smiling people in suits, generic tech imagery, all-text thumbnails
- Test: close-cropped human faces, bold text over solid color, before/after data comparisons, product UI screenshots with callouts
- Taboola-specific: test 1:1 square (600×600) and 16:9 landscape (1200×628) in parallel
- Always A/B test: 2 headline angles × 2 thumbnail styles = 4 ad variants minimum per campaign

CREATIVE ROTATION PROTOCOL:
- Week 1-2: Run 4-6 variants, minimum 500 impressions each before pausing underperformers
- Week 3: Pause bottom 50% by CTR (not just CPL — CTR signals content-audience match)
- Week 4: Scale 2 winners + introduce 2 new variants based on winning angle
- Monthly: Full creative refresh for any ad running > 45 days (fatigue accelerates with small audiences)

DELIVERABLE 4: LANDING PAGE & NATIVE CONTENT STRATEGY

NATIVE QUALITY SCORE REQUIREMENTS:
All platforms score your content/landing page and adjust your effective CPC accordingly (up to 40% cost reduction for high scores):
- Page load speed: < 3 seconds mobile (run Google PageSpeed Insights — target 70+ score)
- Content depth: Minimum 600 words on landing page (editorial feel preferred)
- Ad-to-page congruence: Headline promise must be fulfilled within first screen
- Navigation present: Do not strip all navigation — native audiences expect editorial feel
- No aggressive popups: No exit-intent popups within 3 seconds of landing
- HTTPS: Required on all platforms
- No excessive ads on landing page: < 3 display ads on any content page you're sending traffic to

NATIVE ARTICLE / ADVERTORIAL STRATEGY:
When to use a native article landing page (vs. direct product landing page):
- Use native article when: Top-of-funnel, audience is cold, product requires education
- Use direct landing page when: Retargeting warm audiences, BOFU conversion campaigns, direct response offers

Native article structure (proven framework):
  Headline (same as ad or closely related): Deliver on the curiosity/promise
  Lede (first 2 sentences): Confirm the problem or insight — don't start selling
  Body (400-800 words): 80% education, 20% brand/product relevance
    - Section 1: Define the problem with data (2-3 specific stats)
    - Section 2: Show how the problem plays out (persona story or scenario)
    - Section 3: Introduce the solution category (not your brand yet)
    - Section 4: Introduce your brand as the best example of the solution category
    - Section 5: Social proof (customer quote or outcome metric)
  CTA (clear, single): Low-friction next step — "Download the full playbook," "See a 10-min demo," "Calculate your ROI"
  Author byline: Attribute to a named person (improves trust and engagement vs. "Sponsored Content")
  Related articles widget: Link to 2-3 more of your blog posts (increases pages-per-session and pixel opportunities)

DELIVERABLE 5: BIDDING & OPTIMIZATION STRATEGY

STARTING BID RECOMMENDATIONS BY PLATFORM:
Taboola:
  - CPC bidding (recommended for start): Begin at $0.40-$0.80 for US English audiences
  - SmartBid (AI optimization): Enable after 50+ conversions in 30-day window
  - Recency boost: +20% bid on audiences who visited your site within 24 hours
  - Publisher block list: Start with Taboola's default blocks + add your own as you identify low-quality sources

Outbrain/Teads:
  - CPC bidding: Begin at $0.35-$0.70 for US/UK/AU audiences
  - Conversion bid (SmartAds): Enable after 30+ pixel conversions
  - Bid multiplier by device: Mobile typically 20-30% lower CPC but higher volume; test desktop-first for B2B

MSAN:
  - CPC bidding: Begin at $0.80-$2.00 (premium for LinkedIn data overlay — expect higher CPC but better lead quality)
  - Target CPA: Enable after 30+ conversions; start at 2× your manual CPC ÷ historical conversion rate
  - LinkedIn audience layering: Job function + seniority + industry = highest precision, smallest audience (minimum 1,000 users required)

OPTIMIZATION RULES (set as automated rules where platform supports):
Rule 1: Pause any campaign placement (publisher site) with > 200 clicks and 0 conversions
Rule 2: Increase bid by 15% on any audience segment with CPL < 80% of target
Rule 3: Decrease bid by 20% on any campaign with CTR < 0.05% after 10,000 impressions
Rule 4: Pause any ad creative with CTR < 0.04% after 5,000 impressions (regardless of CPL — low CTR tanks Quality Score)
Rule 5: Reallocate 25% of budget from lowest-performing platform to highest-performing platform weekly

QUALITY SCORE OPTIMIZATION PROTOCOL:
Taboola Campaign Score and Outbrain Content Score both affect effective CPC:
- Score 7+/10: You pay minimum CPC; algorithm favors your content for premium placements
- Score 4-6/10: You pay standard CPC; limited premium inventory access
- Score < 4/10: Algorithm deprioritizes delivery; CPC spikes despite high bids

To improve score:
  - Increase page engagement (time on page, scroll depth, pages per session)
  - Reduce bounce rate below 60%
  - Ensure mobile page speed > 70 PageSpeed score
  - Maintain CTR > 0.08% (Taboola) or > 0.06% (Outbrain) — pause underperforming creative immediately

DELIVERABLE 6: TRACKING, ATTRIBUTION & CRM INTEGRATION

PIXEL IMPLEMENTATION REQUIREMENTS:

Universal site pixel (fire on all pages):
  - Taboola Pixel (base code on every page)
  - Outbrain Pixel (base code on every page)
  - MSAN UET Tag (Universal Event Tracking — fire on all pages)

Conversion events to fire:
  1. Lead form submission — value: $[ACV × lead-to-close rate] or flat value
  2. Demo scheduled / trial signup — value: $[ACV × demo-to-close rate]
  3. Content download (gated asset) — value: lower than demo (engagement signal)
  4. Pricing page visit — signal event only (no value; used for audience building)
  5. Thank-you page load — primary conversion event for post-form tracking

UTM PARAMETER STRUCTURE:
For all native ads (standardize across all platforms):
  utm_source: taboola | outbrain | msan | stackadapt
  utm_medium: native | content-discovery
  utm_campaign: [funnel stage]-[audience type]-[content topic] (e.g., tofu-finance-execs-attribution-guide)
  utm_content: [headline variant ID] (e.g., curiosity-v1 | benefit-v2)
  utm_term: [publisher category or placement if available]

CRM FIELD MAPPING:
  - Lead Source = "Native Advertising"
  - Lead Source Detail = UTM source (taboola / outbrain / msan)
  - Campaign Name = UTM campaign value
  - Content = UTM content (ad variant that drove conversion)
  - First Page Visited = referrer URL (captures which article they landed on)

ATTRIBUTION MODEL FOR NATIVE:
Native advertising operates on a different attribution logic than search or social — use these settings:
- Click-through window: 30 days (native influences decisions over longer consideration cycles)
- View-through window: 1 day ONLY (view-through credit is overused; be conservative to avoid attribution inflation)
- Multi-touch model: Position-based (40/40/20) — native often plays TOFU/MOFU role; give first and last touch credit
- Pipeline influence tracking: Report on opportunities where a native touchpoint occurred within 90 days of opportunity creation, regardless of whether native was the "last" touch

WEEKLY REPORTING DASHBOARD (build in Google Sheets, Looker, or Tableau):
Platform-level metrics (pull from each ad platform API or manually weekly):
  - Spend | Impressions | Clicks | CTR | CPC | Conversions | CPL | ROAS
  - Quality/Content Score by campaign
  - Top 5 publishers by CTR and by CPL (identify best + worst)
  - Creative CTR ranking (which headlines + thumbnails are winning)

CRM-sourced business metrics:
  - Leads from native (by platform)
  - Lead-to-MQL rate for native leads vs. other channels
  - Pipeline generated/influenced by native (by campaign)
  - Cost-per-pipeline-dollar from native

WEEK 3 (Days 15-21): Scaling Winners
- Identify top 2 performing ad variants by CTR (minimum 5,000 impressions each)
- Pause bottom 50% of creative
- Increase budget by 25% on winning campaigns
- Introduce 2 new headline tests based on winning message angle
- Build lookalike audiences from pixel-based converters (if platform has > 50 converters)

WEEK 4 (Days 22-30): Revenue Attribution & Optimization
- Pull full 30-day CRM report: leads by native platform, lead-to-MQL rate, pipeline generated
- Compare native lead quality to other channels (time-to-meeting, ICP score, pipeline conversion)
- Set Month 2 targets: CPL reduction goal, pipeline contribution target, new audience tests to run
- Present results to leadership: frame as cost-per-pipeline-dollar, not just CPL

- CTR: Target > 0.10% on Taboola/Outbrain, > 0.15% on MSAN; pause creative below 0.05% after 5K impressions

- Publisher block rate: Block any publisher with CTR < 0.03% after 2,000 impressions or CPL > 3× target

**Business Impact Metrics (track monthly):**
- Native-sourced leads: Number and % of total lead volume

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High CTR but very high bounce rate (> 80%) — traffic comes but immediately leaves**
Solution: This is almost always an ad-to-page mismatch problem, not a targeting problem. Debug in this order: (1) Read your ad headline out loud — does your landing page deliver that exact promise within the first 5 seconds? If someone clicked "Why CFOs Are Ditching Corporate Cards" they expect editorial content about CFO frustrations, not a product signup page; (2) Check mobile page speed — native drives 60-70% mobile traffic, and a 5-second load time causes most users to bounce before the page renders; run Google PageSpeed Insights and target 70+ mobile score; (3) Check if your page has aggressive interstitials or popups within 3 seconds of landing — native audiences expect an editorial reading experience, not a lead-capture wall. Move any form CTA below the fold.

**Problem: Traffic quality is low — leads don't match ICP, high CPL with zero pipeline conversion**
Solution: Native quality control is publisher-level, not just audience-level. Take these three steps: (1) Pull your publisher report and sort by CPC-to-conversion ratio — identify the 10 publishers driving highest volume with zero or near-zero conversions and add them to your block list; common offenders are gaming sites, quiz sites, viral content farms even within "business" categories; (2) Tighten your audience targeting — add behavioral qualifiers (e.g., "small business owners" + "B2B" + "financial services interests") rather than relying on broad interest categories alone; (3) Test MSAN's LinkedIn audience overlay if B2B is your target — LinkedIn job title data on top of native publisher inventory is the fastest way to dramatically improve lead ICP match in native.

**Problem: Campaign spend is low — platform isn't spending the full budget despite adequate bids**
Solution: Native platforms are inventory-constrained and Quality Score-dependent — low scores result in limited auction eligibility. Check these four causes: (1) Audience size too small — native requires minimum 10,000+ users in audience; if retargeting is too narrow, expand to 30 or 60-day windows; (2) Content/Campaign Score below 5 — the algorithm throttles delivery for low-quality content; run a full page speed and content quality audit and lift score to 7+; (3) Bid too low for target inventory — if targeting premium publishers (CNN, Forbes, Bloomberg), you need bids at or above their floor CPCs; increase bid by 30% and observe delivery improvement; (4) Creative fatigue — if the same ads have been running > 45 days with declining CTR, the algorithm deprioritizes delivery; refresh creative immediately.
