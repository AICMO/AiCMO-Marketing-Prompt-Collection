DELIVERABLE 3: SNAPCHAT PIXEL & CONVERSIONS API SETUP

Standard Web Events (fire via Snapchat Pixel JavaScript):
1. PAGE_VIEW — fires on every page load (include page_url, user_data)
2. VIEW_CONTENT — fires when product page loads (include item_ids, price, currency)
3. ADD_TO_CART — fires on add-to-cart click (include item_ids, price, currency, quantity)
4. ADD_TO_WISHLIST — fires on wishlist action (purchase intent signal)
5. INITIATE_CHECKOUT — fires when checkout page loads (include total cart value)
6. ADD_BILLING — fires on payment entry (strong purchase intent signal)
7. PURCHASE — fires on order confirmation (include transaction_id, price, currency, item_ids)
8. SIGN_UP — fires on account creation or trial signup (include email hash)
9. LEAD — fires on form submission for lead gen (include email hash, phone hash)
10. APP_INSTALL — for mobile app advertisers (track in Snap MMP integration)

Advanced Pixel Matching (APM) — Critical for Attribution Accuracy:
- Hashes and passes user data (email, phone, name, IP, user agent) with every pixel event
- Improves Snap's ability to match conversions back to ad exposures across devices
- Implementation: Add user-data object to your Snap pixel base code (Snap provides code snippet)
- Expected impact: 15-25% increase in attributed conversions vs. basic pixel

Server-Side Events (Conversions API — CAPI):
- Mirror all PURCHASE, LEAD, and SIGN_UP events via server-side postback
- Pass hashed email + phone (sha256) for cross-device matching
- Include client_dedup_id to prevent double-counting when pixel and CAPI both fire
- Recommended tools: Snapchat's Shopify app (native CAPI support), Elevar, or custom webhook

Shopify Integration Steps:
1. Install Snapchat Ads app from Shopify App Store (Settings → Apps)
2. Enable pixel auto-configuration — all standard events fire automatically
3. Enable Conversions API in the Snapchat Ads app settings
4. Verify event matching in Snap Ads Manager → Events Manager — target >70% match rate
5. Set attribution window: 1-day swipe, 1-day view for purchase campaigns (default is too broad at 28-day view)

CRM / Non-Shopify Integration:
- Use Snap Pixel Helper Chrome extension to verify all events fire correctly
- Map PURCHASE event value to actual revenue (include tax/shipping decision) for ROAS accuracy
- Connect Snap lead gen forms to CRM via Zapier or native integration (Snap supports HubSpot, Mailchimp, and Salesforce natively)
- Pass lead data to CRM within 5 minutes of form submission; SDR speed-to-lead SLA critical for Snap's younger, less patient audience

DELIVERABLE 4: SNAP CATALOG & DYNAMIC ADS SETUP

Dynamic Ads are the highest-efficiency format for e-commerce and retail brands — they auto-generate personalized ads based on your product catalog, showing users the exact products they viewed or similar items from your catalog.

CATALOG SETUP:
1. Create product catalog in Snap Ads Manager → Catalogs
2. Upload via: Shopify integration (automatic sync) / Google Merchant Center import / CSV/XML feed upload / Pixel-based catalog (auto-build from product page events)
3. Required catalog fields: id, title, description, availability, condition, price, link, image_link
4. Recommended catalog fields: sale_price, product_type, brand, gtin, item_group_id (for variant management)
5. Sync frequency: Daily minimum; real-time via Shopify app recommended (inventory accuracy prevents ads for out-of-stock items)

DYNAMIC AD CAMPAIGN TYPES:

Retargeting Dynamic Ads (highest ROAS, lowest CPA):
- Target: Users who viewed specific products on your website (pixel must fire VIEW_CONTENT with item_id)
- Ad format: Automatically generated creative showing the exact product viewed + price + brand name
- Audience: "Product Viewers, last 14 days" — exclude purchasers
- Bid strategy: Target Cost — set at your target CPA
- Expected performance: 30-50% lower CPA vs. static ads targeting same audience

Upsell Dynamic Ads:
- Target: Recent purchasers (last 60 days)
- Ad format: Show complementary products from catalog based on purchase history
- Audience: "Purchasers" custom audience — exclude products already purchased
- CTA: "Complete your look" / "You might also like" / "Customers also bought"

Prospecting Dynamic Ads:
- Target: Cold audiences based on interest or lookalike
- Ad format: Top-performing products from catalog (ranked by revenue or conversion rate)
- Audience: Broad interest targeting + lookalike from purchasers
- Best for: Brands with large catalogs where manual creative testing is cost-prohibitive

DELIVERABLE 5: BIDDING STRATEGY & BUDGET OPTIMIZATION

BID STRATEGY SELECTION:

Auto-Bid (Algorithm-led):
- When to use: First 30-60 days; new campaigns; when establishing CPM/CPA benchmarks
- Behavior: Snap spends your full daily budget at the lowest achievable CPA across all eligible impressions
- Risk: CPAs can be unstable early; no ceiling — monitor daily during learning phase
- Learning phase exit: Requires 50+ optimization events (purchases/leads) per week per ad set

Target Cost (CPA-controlled):
- When to use: After 50+ purchases/week on a campaign; ready to defend CPA while scaling spend
- Set target at: [Your target CPA + 15%] — Snap needs room to optimize; too tight causes underspend
- Lowering the target: Drop by 5-10% every 5-7 days once stable delivery is achieved
- Note: Target Cost on Snap is more forgiving than Meta's cost cap — Snap will occasionally go 20-30% above target during competitive auctions

Max Bid (Impression-controlled):
- When to use: Hyper-targeted audiences where you need guaranteed exposure (e.g., ABM-style targeting of a small specific audience)
- Set at: 2-3× target CPA if unknown; adjust based on delivery
- Risk: Can cause underspend if max bid is too low for the auction

Minimum daily budget per ad set: $20 (below this, algorithm cannot exit learning phase efficiently)
Minimum daily budget for conversion campaigns (recommended): $50-100
Starting daily budget: [Monthly budget ÷ 30 × 1.3] — over-allocate early to collect data faster

BUDGET SPLIT:
Baseline for a $15,000/month Snapchat budget:
  - TOFU (cold awareness): 20% = $3,000
  - MOFU (retargeting/consideration): 25% = $3,750
  - BOFU (conversion): 55% = $8,250
After 60 days with stable CPA: shift BOFU to 65%, reduce TOFU to 15%

SCALING PROTOCOL:
- Never increase budget by more than 20-25% in a single day (disrupts algorithm learning)
- Scale by duplicating the winning ad set at higher budget rather than editing the original
- Horizontal scaling: duplicate and vary audience (not creative)
- Vertical scaling: increase daily budget 15-20% every 3-4 days if CPA holds within 15% of target
- Creative refresh trigger: if CTR drops 20%+ week-over-week, launch new creative batch immediately

DELIVERABLE 6: WEEKLY OPTIMIZATION PROTOCOL (AI-Ready Checklist)

MONDAY — Creative Performance Review:
□ Pull Snap Ads Manager report: CPM, swipe-up rate, CPA, ROAS by creative
□ Flag any creative with swipe-up rate < 1.0% after 10,000 impressions — pause or replace
□ Check 2-second and 5-second video view rates: low rates = hook problem
□ Review story completion rate (target >40%): low = narrative or pacing problem, not just hook
□ Identify top 3 creatives by CPA — document the hook, visual style, and offer pattern

WEDNESDAY — Audience & Delivery Check:
□ Review audience overlap between ad sets (>50% overlap = internal competition — consolidate)
□ Check frequency: if retargeting audience seeing ad > 4× in 7 days, expand audience or refresh creative
□ Review Snap Pixel Events Manager: confirm all events firing with >70% match rate
□ Check budget utilization: campaigns spending < 70% of daily budget indicate bid or audience size issue — expand audience or raise max bid
□ AR Lens performance (if running): check plays, play duration, share rate — share rate >3% indicates viral potential

FRIDAY — Budget Reallocation & Test Launch:
□ Shift 20-25% of budget from worst-performing ad set to top-performing
□ Launch 2-3 new creative tests (new hook only — keep everything else constant for clean data)
□ Add new retargeting audiences generated this week (new cart abandoners, story engagers)
□ Review catalog feed health: check for out-of-stock items serving in Dynamic Ads — add inventory exclusion rules

MONTHLY — Strategic Review:
□ Attribution audit: Snap-reported CPA vs. actual CPA in Shopify/CRM (note view-through attribution inflation — Snap's default 28-day view window over-attributes; shorten to 1-day view for accuracy)
□ Creative fatigue analysis: replace any creative > 21 days old showing >25% week-over-week CTR decline
□ Audience refresh: build new lookalikes from last 30 days' purchasers
□ Competitive creative audit: analyze competitor Snap Ads via Snap's Creative Center (free competitive intelligence tool)
□ AR Lens performance review: if lens has <5-second average play duration, brief creative team to redesign interaction
□ Catalog sync audit: confirm all products syncing with correct pricing and inventory status

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CPA is 2-3× above target after 2 weeks of running**
Solution: Diagnose the conversion funnel in order — (1) Swipe-up rate check: if SUR < 1.0%, the creative hook is failing — pause all underperforming creatives and replace with 3-5 new hook variants before any audience or bid changes; (2) Landing page CVR: if SUR is healthy (>1.5%) but CPA is high, the post-click experience is the problem — Snap's young, mobile-first audience expects fast-loading, visually rich landing pages (target < 2 second load time on mobile); create a Snap-specific landing page that mirrors the ad's visual tone, features the exact offer from the ad, and has a single prominent CTA; (3) Audience quality: pull the Demographics breakdown — if clicks are skewing outside your ICP (wrong age, wrong geography), add exclusions and tighten interest targeting; (4) Attribution window: verify your Snap attribution window is set to 1-day click / 1-day view for purchase campaigns — the default 28-day view window inflates reported conversions and masks true CPA performance.

**Problem: Great swipe-up rate but poor purchase conversion rate (< 1.5%)**
Solution: This is almost always a landing page or offer-message mismatch. Snap's audience swipes up impulsively based on creative appeal, then needs immediate reinforcement on the landing page. Test these fixes in order: (1) Match the landing page visual style exactly to the ad creative — if the ad was raw UGC, the landing page should feel similarly authentic, not corporate; (2) Ensure the offer from the ad (free shipping, discount code, exclusive item) is the first thing visible on the landing page — above the fold, impossible to miss; (3) Eliminate every click between landing and checkout — Snap's mobile audience has extremely low patience; direct product pages with add-to-cart as the primary CTA outperform homepage or collection page destinations by 2-3×; (4) Check mobile page speed — use Google PageSpeed Insights; Snap traffic is 100% mobile; below-80 score on mobile will measurably hurt CVR.

**Problem: Performance declined significantly after 3-4 weeks despite no campaign changes**
Solution: Creative fatigue on Snapchat sets in faster than most platforms — Snap's young audience is highly sensitive to repetitive content and skips known ads aggressively. Diagnose and fix: (1) Check frequency in retargeting audiences — if > 3-4 impressions per person per 7 days, pause and rotate in fresh creative immediately; (2) Audit your creative age — any creative running for more than 21 days showing a 20%+ week-over-week CTR decline is fatigued; archive and replace; (3) Launch minimum 4-6 new creative variants per month for always-on programs — unlike Facebook or Google, Snapchat's algorithm has a shorter creative peak performance window of 2-4 weeks before audience recognition kills performance; (4) Diversify format: if you have been running only Snap Ads, test Story Ads (sequential narrative format often re-engages fatigued audiences with a new ad experience) or launch an AR Lens campaign to generate a new touchpoint with the same audience.
