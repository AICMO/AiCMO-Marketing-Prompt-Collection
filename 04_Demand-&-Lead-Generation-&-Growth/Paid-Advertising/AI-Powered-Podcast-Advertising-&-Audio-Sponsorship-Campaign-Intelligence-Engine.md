DELIVERABLE 2: AD FORMAT & PLACEMENT STRATEGY

AD PLACEMENT DECISION FRAMEWORK:

Pre-roll (first 60 seconds):
- Best for: High-intent offers, promo codes, time-sensitive campaigns
- Listener attention: High (hasn't started content yet), but skip intent also high
- Recommended length: 15-30 seconds
- CPM premium vs. mid-roll: 0-10% lower (supply is higher)
- Use when: Budget is limited, direct response focus, strong offer with promo code

Mid-roll (between 10-30 min mark):
- Best for: Brand building + conversion hybrid, complex product explanations
- Listener attention: Highest of all placements (fully engaged, invested in content)
- Recommended length: 60-90 seconds
- CPM premium: 20-40% higher than pre-roll
- Use when: Complex product, longer consideration cycle, brand-building goal
- RECOMMENDATION: Always prioritize mid-roll for B2B and high-ACV products

Post-roll (end of episode):
- Best for: Highly loyal listeners, super fans of the show, community/subscription offers
- Listener attention: Lowest (many listeners stop before episode ends)
- Recommended length: 30-60 seconds
- CPM: 30-50% lower than mid-roll
- Use when: Adding incremental reach at low cost; community-driven offers

HOST-READ VS. PRODUCED SPOT:
Host-read (STRONGLY RECOMMENDED for all Tier 1 and Tier 2 buys):
- Why: 63% higher recall than produced spots (Edison Research); feels like trusted endorsement
- Requirement: Provide talking points + key messages, let host use their voice/style
- Risk: Quality varies; always request a sample read for approval before airing
- Negotiation: Include approval right in contract — you can request one re-read if key messages missed

Produced/scripted spot (use for Tier 3 or programmatic):
- Why: Consistent brand message, AB-testable, works at scale in programmatic networks
- Limitation: Listeners recognize it as ad immediately — lower trust factor
- Use case: Programmatic audio (Spotify, streaming), show hosts who don't do live reads
- Length: 15-30 seconds for pre-roll programmatic; 30-60 seconds for embedded scripted

BAKED-IN VS. DYNAMIC AD INSERTION (DAI):
Baked-in ads (embedded permanently in episode file):
- Lives in episode forever — cumulative downloads over time
- Better for evergreen shows with catalogue audiences
- No expiry = better CPM value for longer-running shows
- Recommended for: Tier 1 shows with strong back-catalogue

Dynamic Ad Insertion (DAI):
- Ad is served fresh based on listener location, date, and targeting parameters
- Better for time-sensitive campaigns (product launches, events, seasonality)
- Allows geo-targeting (useful for event marketing or regional campaigns)
- Recommended for: Tier 2-3, programmatic, any campaign with expiry date or geo-component

DELIVERABLE 4: ATTRIBUTION & MEASUREMENT SYSTEM

VANITY URL SYSTEM:
URL structure: [yourcompany.com/[showname]] for each podcast
Example: yourcompany.com/hubermanlab, yourcompany.com/jtbd, yourcompany.com/founders
- Set up 301 redirect in your CMS pointing to the relevant landing page
- Append UTM parameters on the redirect: utm_source=podcast&utm_medium=audio&utm_campaign=[showname]&utm_content=[episode-number]
- Create unique landing page per show (or per show tier) with:
  - Headline that mirrors the podcast ad's hook
  - CTA matching the ad offer (free trial / demo / download)
  - Auto-populated promo code field if applicable

PROMO CODE SYSTEM:
- Create unique promo code per show: [SHOWNAME] (e.g., FOUNDERS, JTBD, HUBERMAN)
- Track code redemptions in your CRM and eCommerce system
- Set code to expire 90 days after flight ends (creates urgency, clean tracking)
- Use codes as primary attribution for D2C/eCommerce; vanity URLs as primary for B2B

PIXEL-BASED ATTRIBUTION SETUP:
Podscribe (B2B recommended):
- Install Podscribe pixel on your website (all pages, especially thank-you/confirmation pages)
- Podscribe matches IP address of listener to website visitor within 7-30 day attribution window
- Provides: visit lift, conversion lift, frequency, reach by show
- Connect to CRM via Zapier or API to import attributed conversions

Chartable (now Spotify for Podcasters):
- Used for tracking downloads and listener behavior at the show level
- Provides SmartLinks: trackable links hosts can use that confirm which show drove the click
- Attribution model: click-based, 30-day window

Spotify Attribution (if running Spotify Ad Studio):
- Native attribution to Spotify user profiles
- Streaming Conversion Events: tracks if a Spotify user heard your ad then visited your website
- Requires Spotify Pixel on website (similar to Meta Pixel implementation)

LIFT STUDY DESIGN (for brand measurement):
- Use Veritonic, Signal Hill Insights, or Podtrac for brand lift studies
- Split audience: 50% hears podcast ads (exposed), 50% control group
- Measure: unaided brand recall, aided recall, brand favorability, purchase intent
- Run study after minimum 4-week flight with at least 200K total impressions
- Budget: $5-15K for a statistically significant study
- Benchmark: B2B podcast ads deliver 8-14% unaided recall lift vs. 2-4% for display ads

CRM PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION:
B2B attribution model:
1. Visitor arrives via vanity URL with utm_source=podcast → UTM captured in CRM
2. Visitor fills in demo form or trial → Lead created with lead source = Podcast
3. Show name pulled from utm_campaign field → Tracks pipeline to specific show
4. Create CRM property: "Podcast Attribution Show" — populate via hidden form field
5. Build report: Pipeline sourced / influenced by podcast, by show, by episode
6. Attribution window: 90 days (podcast audience has longer consideration cycles)

D2C attribution model:
1. Promo code redemption → Orders tagged with code in Shopify/eCommerce platform
2. Vanity URL → Google Analytics / GA4 reports on sessions, goals, revenue by utm_campaign
3. Northbeam / Triple Whale: model-based attribution accounting for podcast halo effect
4. Post-purchase survey: "Where did you hear about us?" — captures pod listeners who didn't use code

OPTIMIZATION TRIGGERS:

Weekly checks:
□ Pull vanity URL visits and promo code redemptions per show
□ Calculate cost-per-visit and cost-per-conversion per show
□ Check Podscribe dashboard for IP-matched visitor lift by show
□ Review Spotify Ad Studio: completion rate, click rate, cost per click
□ Flag any show with cost-per-desired-action > 3× target → pause or renegotiate

Monthly decisions:
□ Shows delivering CPL within target → renew at same or increased frequency
□ Shows delivering CPL 1.5-3× target → test new creative/offer; give 2 more episodes
□ Shows delivering CPL > 3× target → pause; test different slot or move budget to programmatic
□ Top-performing shows → negotiate rate card reduction in exchange for longer commitment
□ Programmatic: shift budget from low-completion sources to high-completion sources

Quarterly strategic review:
□ Pull full 90-day attribution report: pipeline sourced, pipeline influenced per show
□ Calculate blended Podcast CPL vs. CPL on LinkedIn / Google / Meta
□ Identify shows that drive listeners who convert faster (shorter time-to-close)
□ Run brand recall survey with existing customers: "Where did you first hear of us?"
□ Renegotiate annual contracts with top 2-3 shows for 15-25% rate discount

  - Run 2-episode test on 8-12 shows; minimum $250/show

Phase 2 (Months 3-4): Optimize
  - Double down on top 3 shows from Phase 1

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Vanity URL traffic is near zero even though the episode aired and the show has 50K+ downloads**
Solution: Three most common causes — (1) The host mispronounced or didn't clearly state the URL; request the episode file from the host, listen to the actual ad read, and ask for a makeup read in the next episode if the URL was unclear; (2) The episode was published but Podscribe IP-match data shows listeners DID visit your site — they just didn't use the vanity URL (typed yourcompany.com directly instead); pull Podscribe lift data to see true site visits and don't rely on vanity URL alone; (3) The landing page is broken or not properly set up — test the vanity URL yourself from a mobile device before the episode airs.

**Problem: Podcast leads have low CRM-to-pipeline conversion rates compared to other channels**
Solution: Podcast listeners are typically earlier in the funnel than paid search or intent-based leads — they heard an ad passively, not while actively searching for a solution. Fix in two ways: (1) Adjust your nurture sequence for podcast leads — they need 2-3 more educational touchpoints before a sales call makes sense; create a "Podcast Listener" lead segment with a longer nurture sequence emphasizing education and proof before asking for a demo; (2) Reallocate budget toward shows where the host personally endorses you vs. scripted reads — authentic host endorsements produce listeners who are 40-60% more likely to convert because they trust the host's recommendation.

**Problem: It's impossible to prove podcast ROI to leadership because attribution is unclear**
Solution: Build a three-pronged attribution case for your next leadership review: (1) Direct attribution — vanity URL visits + promo code redemptions from that flight period; (2) Modeled attribution — Podscribe's IP-matched visits and conversions; (3) Survey attribution — add "How did you hear about us?" to your demo request form and tally podcast responses over 90 days. Present all three numbers together with a confidence range rather than claiming one definitive number. Also benchmark your podcast CPL against LinkedIn or Google CPL for the same ICP — podcast often wins on a blended basis when you account for the brand halo effect.
