DELIVERABLE 3: BIDDING STRATEGY & BUDGET OPTIMIZATION

BID STRATEGY BY CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE AND MATURITY:
New account / < 50 conversions/month:
  - Use Maximize Conversions with daily budget cap
  - Manual CPV floor: $0.02-0.05 for skippable formats
  - Do NOT use Target CPA until 30+ conversions accumulated in the bid strategy

Scaling account / 50-200 conversions/month:
  - Graduate to Target CPA (set 15-20% above actual CPA from previous 30 days)
  - Use Maximize Conversion Value if ROAS data is available
  - Enable Enhanced CPC on Discovery Ads

Mature account / 200+ conversions/month:
  - Target ROAS (set 15% below actual ROAS to maintain volume)
  - Portfolio bid strategies across campaign types
  - Experiment with Search and YouTube unified bid strategies via Performance Max

BUDGET ALLOCATION RECOMMENDATION:
If total monthly budget = $10,000:
  - TOFU (awareness): 20% = $2,000
  - MOFU (consideration): 35% = $3,500
  - BOFU (conversion): 45% = $4,500
Scale BOFU to 60% once Cost-per-Conversion is stable and quality is validated.

BUDGET PACING RULES:
- Never let campaigns underspend by more than 25% in the first 7 days (signals to Google's algorithm)
- Pause ad groups with 0 conversions after $[3× target CPA] in spend
- Budget increase ceiling: no more than 20% increase per week to avoid auction disruption
- Dayparting: analyze performance by hour of day after 30 days; pause lowest-performing 20% of hours if CPV/CPA skews heavily

DELIVERABLE 4: VIDEO CREATIVE TESTING FRAMEWORK

CREATIVE TESTING PROTOCOL:
Month 1 — Hook Testing:
  - Launch 3+ ad variants differing ONLY in the first 5 seconds
  - Keep body, CTA, and landing page identical
  - Determine winner at 5,000 impressions per variant
  - Metric: VTR (view-through rate) + CTR combination score

Month 2 — Offer Testing:
  - Take winning hook from Month 1
  - Test 2-3 different offers or CTAs
  - Metric: Cost-per-conversion + conversion rate

Month 3 — Format Testing:
  - Scale winning hook + offer into additional formats (Bumper 6-sec version, Shorts version)
  - Metric: Reach expansion, frequency management, incremental conversions

CREATIVE REFRESH TRIGGERS:
- VTR drops 15%+ week-over-week: new hook variant needed
- CTR drops 20%+ from baseline: new offer or CTA needed
- Frequency exceeds 7 per user per 30 days: new creative set needed
- Any ad running > 60 days: mandatory creative refresh

AD SCORING MATRIX:
Score each ad 1-5 across four dimensions:
1. Hook score: % who watch past 5 seconds (target: > 70%)
2. Retention score: % who watch to 50% (target: > 40%)
3. Intent score: CTR to landing page (target: > 1.5% for BOFU)
4. Conversion score: Cost-per-conversion vs. target (target: within 30% of goal)
Kill ads scoring below 2.5 average. Scale ads scoring above 3.5 across additional audiences.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low view-through rate (VTR < 15%) — ads being skipped immediately**
Solution: The hook is failing. Pull the YouTube Analytics audience retention graph for the underperforming ad — if you see a massive drop-off at exactly the 5-second mark (when skip becomes available), your first 5 seconds aren't compelling. Fix in this order: (1) Start with a direct, specific statement of the problem your audience faces — not your product name or a question; (2) Use a human face making direct eye contact in the first frame — faces outperform graphics/logos by 2-3× in YouTube hook tests; (3) Begin mid-sentence or mid-scene to create a curiosity gap — the viewer has missed context they want to recover.

**Problem: High VTR but low CTR — people are watching but not clicking**
Solution: The narrative is working but the offer or CTA is weak. The ad is entertaining but not driving action. Fix in this order: (1) Audit your CTA — is it specific?
