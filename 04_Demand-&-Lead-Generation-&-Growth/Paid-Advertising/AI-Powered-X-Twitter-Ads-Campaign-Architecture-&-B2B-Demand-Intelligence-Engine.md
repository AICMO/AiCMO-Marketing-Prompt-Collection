Design a 3-stage X Ads campaign architecture using the Attention → Interest → Intent → Action model:

STAGE 1 — AWARENESS (Reach decision-makers in active conversations)
Objective: Reach or Video Views
Target: Cold audiences — people discussing problems you solve, following category leaders
Audience build options:
  OPTION A — Keyword Targeting (best for in-market demand capture):
    - Exact match keywords: [problem-specific terms buyers tweet about]
    - Broad match keywords: [category + pain terms]
    - Negative keywords: [terms to exclude — job seekers, students, irrelevant industries]
    - Language: [English / regional languages]
    - Recency: Target users who tweeted/engaged with keyword in last [7/14/30 days]
  OPTION B — Follower Lookalike Targeting (best for awareness expansion):
    - Target users similar to followers of: [@Competitor1, @CategoryLeader1, @IndustryMedia1]
    - Add niche accounts: [industry-specific handles your ICP follows]
    - Minimum lookalike audience size: 100,000 users
  OPTION C — Interest Targeting:
    - X Interest categories: [Technology / Business and Finance / Science / etc.]
    - Sub-interests: [Cloud computing / B2B software / SaaS / Fintech / etc.]
Budget allocation: 20% of total monthly budget
Formats: [Video Ad / Single Image Ad / Promoted Post from best organic content]
KPIs: CPM target, video completion rate (target > 25%), engagement rate (target > 1%)
Frequency management: Cap at 3 exposures per 7 days per user

STAGE 2 — CONSIDERATION (Engage warm audiences and nurture intent)
Objective: Website Clicks or Engagements
Target: Retargeting audiences + warm lookalikes
Retargeting segments:
  - Website visitors who did NOT convert (last 30 days)
  - Users who engaged with TOFU ads (liked, retweeted, video 25%+ completion) — last 14 days
  - CRM custom audience: contacts at MQL or opportunity stage
  - Users who clicked any organic post in last 60 days
Audience exclusions: Current customers (uploaded CRM suppression list), team members
Minimum retargeting audience: 1,000 users (X minimum for custom audiences)
Budget allocation: 30% of total monthly budget
Formats: [Single Image Ad with proof point / Quote Tweet amplification / Thread promotion / Poll Ad]
Messaging theme: Social proof, competitive differentiation, use case specificity
KPIs: CPC (target: $[X]), click-through rate (target > 0.7%), website session quality (pages/session > 2)

STAGE 3 — CONVERSION (Drive pipeline from highest-intent buyers)
Objective: Website Conversions or App Installs
Target: Highest-intent segments + competitor audience targeting
Audience priority order:
  1. Retargeted users who visited pricing, features, or demo pages (last 14 days)
  2. CRM contacts who are MQL/SQL but not yet opportunity
  3. Follower lookalikes of your own account (most similar to existing buyers)
  4. Keyword targeting: competitor brand names + "alternative" / "vs" / "pricing" terms
Budget allocation: 50% of total monthly budget
Offer hierarchy by persona:
  - Technical buyer: Free trial / developer sandbox / API documentation
  - Business buyer: ROI calculator / benchmark report / demo request
  - Late-stage: Customer case study in their vertical / head-to-head comparison guide
KPIs: Conversion rate (target > 2%), CPL (rule of thumb: < 1.5% of ACV), cost-per-pipeline-dollar (target < $0.05)

DELIVERABLE 2: AD CREATIVE SYSTEM FOR X

X ads succeed or fail in the first 0.3 seconds. On X, copy IS the creative — the hook line determines everything. Build ads that look and feel native to X, not polished corporate content.

CREATIVE PRINCIPLE FOR X:
- Write like a human, not a brand
- Lead with a counterintuitive insight, bold claim, or compelling data point
- Use natural X language: short sentences, line breaks for readability, no jargon
- The best X ads look like content someone bookmarked and shared, not ads they skipped

For each of the 3 funnel stages, produce a complete creative brief:

CREATIVE BRIEF TEMPLATE (repeat for TOFU, MOFU, BOFU):
Stage: [TOFU/MOFU/BOFU]
Audience mindset: [What they're thinking about right now — what conversation are they in?]
Job-to-be-Done: [The outcome they want to achieve — functional + emotional]
Core message: [The single most important thing to communicate in < 10 words]
Proof point: [Specific stat, customer outcome, or counterintuitive finding]
CTA: [Specific action — not "Learn More", instead: "See the full breakdown" / "Get the calculator" / "Watch the 3-min demo"]

AD VARIANT 1 — The Bold Claim:
Hook line (tweet-style, max 120 chars): [Provocative, specific, makes them stop scrolling]
Body (2-4 lines): [Support the claim with data or story]
CTA line: [What happens when they click + why now]
Hashtags (0-2 max for paid): [Only if highly relevant — avoid hashtag stuffing on ads]

AD VARIANT 2 — The Data Drop:
Hook line: [Surprising statistic or benchmark from your research]
Body: [Context for the stat + implication for their business]
CTA line: [Access to the underlying report or calculator]

AD VARIANT 3 — The Story/Social Proof:
Hook line: [Customer result in specific, concrete terms — e.g., "How Acme went from 90 days to 11 days to close"]
Body: [2-3 lines on how they got there, without giving away the answer]
CTA line: [Read the full story / Watch the 2-min breakdown]

VISUAL DIRECTION:
- Option A: Bold typographic cards (white text on dark background, one stat per frame) — high-performing for B2B SaaS
- Option B: Clean product screenshots with outcome overlaid (before/after interface)
- Option C: Human face with quote — customer testimonial thumbnail
- Option D: Short-form video (15-30 seconds, no audio required, captions mandatory)
Video specs if applicable: MP4/MOV, 1:1 or 16:9, max 140 seconds, 1GB max, captions baked in

THREAD PROMOTION STRATEGY (advanced):
If your company has organic X threads with > 500 engagements, promote them as ads:
- Top-of-thread becomes the ad unit
- Remaining thread visible if user engages
- Thread ads generate 2-3× lower CPE vs. standalone image ads
- Best for: educational frameworks, benchmark reveals, controversial takes on the category

DELIVERABLE 3: X ADS TARGETING DEEP DIVE

KEYWORD TARGETING STRATEGY (unique to X — not available on LinkedIn/Meta):
X keyword targeting captures users who are actively in a conversation, making it closer to search intent than social intent.

Tier 1 — High-Intent Problem Keywords (BOFU):
These users are experiencing the pain right now:
- [Pain-specific terms: "dealing with X", "X keeps breaking", "need a better X tool"]
- [Category buying terms: "best X software", "X alternatives", "comparing X tools"]
- [Competitor brand + alternative/switching terms: "@Competitor pricing", "switching from X"]
Match type: Exact phrase match
Recency: Last 7 days (captures peak intent)

Tier 2 — Solution-Aware Keywords (MOFU):
Users know the category exists but aren't actively buying:
- [Category terms: "X automation", "X management", "X platform"]
- [Related workflow terms: tools, processes, methodologies they discuss]
Match type: Broad match
Recency: Last 14 days

Tier 3 — Problem-Aware Keywords (TOFU):
Users experiencing the problem but don't know your category yet:
- [Symptom terms: describing the problem not the solution]
- [Frustration language: "why is X so hard", "X is killing us", "can't scale X"]
Match type: Broad match
Recency: Last 30 days

FOLLOWER LOOKALIKE TARGETING STRATEGY:
X's follower lookalike system creates audiences similar to the followers of any X account. This is one of the most powerful and underused B2B targeting capabilities.

Priority accounts to use as seeds (in descending value):
1. Your own @handle followers — people like your buyers who already found you organically
2. Top 3 competitor @handles — buyers actively evaluating alternatives
3. Category media/analysts: [@categorymagazine, @analystfirm, @industrypodcast]
4. Category hashtag owners / community builders
5. Top individual thought leaders your buyers follow

Minimum seed account follower count for lookalike quality: 5,000 followers
Combine follower lookalikes with interest layer for precision: e.g., "similar to @Competitor followers" + "interested in B2B Software"

CUSTOM AUDIENCE STRATEGY (CRM Upload):
- Upload customer list (emails) → suppress from all campaigns or use as lookalike seed
- Upload MQL list → MOFU/BOFU targeting
- Upload opportunity list → BOFU with high urgency messaging ("teams like yours")
- Upload closed-lost list (last 12 months) → win-back campaign with updated proof points
- Upload event registrants → nurture campaign post-event
File format: CSV with hashed emails, max 5 million records, 24-hour processing time
Minimum audience size for targeting: 1,000 matched users (aim for 10,000+ for reliable delivery)

DELIVERABLE 4: X PIXEL & CONVERSION TRACKING SETUP

Website events to fire via X Pixel (JavaScript snippet, one per page type):
1. PageView — fire on all pages (baseline traffic tracking)
2. ViewContent — fire on blog/resource pages (engagement signal for TOFU optimization)
3. AddToCart — fire on pricing page (strong intent signal, use for BOFU retargeting)
4. Purchase/Lead — fire on demo confirmation, trial signup, or contact form thank-you page
   Set conversion value: $[ACV × lead-to-close rate] for revenue-based bidding
5. Custom — fire on free trial activation or product signup (distinct from marketing lead)

Conversion Window Configuration:
- Click-through attribution window: 7 days post-click (standard for B2B with longer consideration cycles)
- View-through attribution window: 1 day post-impression (conservative — avoids overcrediting awareness)
- Set "App purchase" event as primary conversion for algorithm optimization
- Use "Lead" event as secondary conversion for volume-based bid optimization

X Pixel QA Steps:
- Install X Pixel Helper browser extension to verify all events fire correctly
- Use X Pixel → Test Events feature before going live
- Verify UTM parameters populate into CRM on all click-throughs
- Test with fresh browsing session (incognito) on each page type

CRM Integration:
- Capture UTM parameters from X Ads into CRM contact/lead record:
  utm_source=twitter, utm_medium=paid, utm_campaign=[campaign-slug], utm_content=[ad-variant-id]
- Tag lead source = "X Ads" in CRM when utm_source=twitter
- Map ad campaign names to CRM campaign objects for pipeline attribution
- Set attribution window: give X credit for pipeline created within 90 days of ad touchpoint
- For HubSpot: use UTM tracking URLs in all X Ads destination URLs + enable "Original Source" and "Original Source Drill-Down" properties

DELIVERABLE 5: BIDDING STRATEGY & BUDGET OPTIMIZATION

BID STRATEGY BY OBJECTIVE (X Ads options: Autobid / Target Cost / Maximum Bid):

Awareness campaigns (Reach objective):
- Use: Autobid (maximum delivery)
- Goal: Minimize CPM while maintaining audience quality
- Benchmark CPM range: $8–$20 for B2B audiences in North America
- Switch to Maximum Bid if delivery is too broad and pulling non-ICP impressions

Engagement campaigns (Engagement objective):
- Use: Target Cost — set target CPE at [benchmark: $0.50–$2.00 for B2B]
- Start 20% above Target Cost, reduce weekly as volume builds
- Pause ad sets with CPE > 3× target after 500+ impressions

Website Click campaigns (Clicks objective):
- Use: Target Cost — set based on: target CPL ÷ site-to-lead conversion rate
- Example: Target CPL $200, site conversion rate 5% → target CPC = $10
- Benchmark CPC range: $3–$15 for B2B SaaS targeting

Conversion campaigns (Conversions objective):
- Use: Target Cost initially; switch to Autobid once 50+ conversions collected in 30 days
- Minimum 50 conversions required for X's algorithm to optimize effectively
- If below 50 conversions/month, use Clicks objective instead and optimize offline

BUDGET SPLIT RECOMMENDATION:
If total monthly budget = $10,000:
  - TOFU (awareness/engagement): 20% = $2,000
  - MOFU (consideration/retargeting): 30% = $3,000
  - BOFU (conversion): 50% = $5,000
Scale BOFU to 60% once CPL is validated and lead quality confirmed by sales team.

DAILY BUDGET PACING RULES:
- Set campaign-level daily budget, not ad group level, for flexible delivery
- X may spend up to 20% over daily budget on high-intent days — build this into targets
- Pause campaigns spending < 50% of daily budget by noon (delivery issue, widen targeting)
- Pause campaigns exceeding CPL by > 2× for 24 hours, then relaunch with tighter audience

DELIVERABLE 6: WEEKLY OPTIMIZATION PROTOCOL (AI-Ready Checklist)

MONDAY — Performance Review:
□ Pull X Ads Manager report: impressions, engagements, CPC, conversions, CPL
□ Cross-reference with CRM: leads from X this week, lead-to-MQL rate, pipeline created
□ Flag any ad with CTR < 0.3% after 2,000 impressions — pause or rotate creative
□ Flag any campaign with CPL > 2× target — narrow audience or improve offer

WEDNESDAY — Creative & Audience Refresh:
□ Check engagement rate by ad variant — promote winner to more budget, pause loser
□ Review follower lookalike audience sizes (X audiences decay — refresh seeds monthly)
□ Add any new high-performing organic X posts to TOFU campaign as promoted posts
□ Update keyword lists: add new in-conversation terms discovered from brand monitoring

FRIDAY — Bidding & Budget Reallocation:
□ Reallocate 15-20% of budget from lowest-performing to highest-performing campaigns
□ Adjust bid targets: if hitting volume targets, lower Target Cost by 10% to reduce CPL
□ Review competitor share of voice: check if competitor is running ads on your keywords
□ Update retargeting audiences: ensure website pixel data is refreshing properly

MONTHLY — Strategic Review:
□ Pipeline attribution report: X Ads-sourced vs. X Ads-influenced pipeline
□ Audience performance breakdown: which follower lookalike seeds produced best CPL
□ Keyword performance report: add high-converting terms to match list, remove waste
□ Creative audit: replace all ads > 30 days old (X audiences have high content velocity — refresh faster than LinkedIn)
□ Competitive scan: review what ads competitors are running in X Ads Transparency Center

## Success Metrics

**Campaign Health Metrics (track weekly):**
- CTR: Target > 0.50% for BOFU conversion ads, > 0.30% for TOFU/MOFU
- CPM: Target $8–$18 for B2B North America audiences; escalation above $25 signals audience too narrow
- Engagement rate: > 1.0% for promoted posts and threads (X benchmark for B2B)
- Video completion rate (if running video): > 25% at 50% mark, > 10% through to end
- Frequency: Keep below 4 exposures per user per 7-day period — X audiences see content faster than LinkedIn

**Business Impact Metrics (track monthly):**
- Pipeline sourced by X Ads: $ amount attributed to X first-touch or last-touch
- Pipeline influenced: $ amount where X touchpoint occurred in 90-day pre-opportunity window
- CPL trend: Target 10-15% reduction per month after first 60 days as algorithm learns
- X Ads contribution to total pipeline: benchmark 5-15% for most B2B SaaS companies
- Free trial / MQL quality: Average ICP score > 3.5/5 from sales team rating

**Audience & Creative Quality Signals:**
- Follower lookalike conversion lift vs. interest-only targeting: X lookalikes should outperform by 30-50%
- Keyword tier performance: Tier 1 (high-intent) should produce CPL 2-3× lower than Tier 3
- Creative fatigue signal: CTR declining > 25% week-over-week = rotate creative immediately
- Organic amplification: promoted posts with high paid CTR should be tested organically for free distribution

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Enable UTM tracking on all X Ads destination URLs: append ?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=[slug]&utm_content=[variant]
- Create HubSpot workflow: if Original Source = "Paid Social" AND utm_source = "twitter" → assign lead source = "X Ads", add to X Ads nurture sequence
- Build X Ads campaign ROI report: filter Contacts by lead source "X Ads" → join to Deals → calculate pipeline and closed-won revenue per campaign
- Use HubSpot's UTM parameter properties (hs_analytics_first_url, hs_analytics_last_url) to track both first-touch and last-touch X Ads attribution

**Salesforce:**
- Create Campaign object for each X Ads campaign; campaign type = "Paid Social"; sync spend data monthly
- On Lead/Contact creation: if utm_source = twitter, set Lead Source = "X Ads", Lead Source Detail = utm_campaign value
- Primary Campaign Source on Opportunity: set to X Ads if X was first or last paid touch before opportunity creation
- Build Campaign Influence report: Opportunities influenced by X Ads campaign within 90 days (multi-touch attribution)

**Zapier / Make Automation Workflows:**
- Trigger: New X Ads lead (via X Lead Gen form webhook or CRM tag) → Action: Slack alert to SDR with lead details + X profile link → Auto-enroll in 5-touch SDR sequence in Outreach/Salesloft
- Trigger: Website conversion with utm_source=twitter → Action: Enrich with Apollo/Clearbit → Add intent score to CRM → Route to SDR by company size
- Trigger: Lead status = "Disqualified" in CRM → Action: Add email to X Custom Audience suppression list → Remove from all active X Ads campaigns (prevent waste on non-ICP)
- Trigger: Opportunity stage = "Closed Won" AND lead source = "X Ads" → Action: Update X Ads conversion value for offline conversion import → Improves algorithm optimization

**X Ads API (for automation and reporting):**
- Key endpoints: /accounts/{account_id}/campaigns, /stats/accounts/{account_id} (performance data), /accounts/{account_id}/tailored_audiences (custom audiences)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low delivery — campaigns spending < 50% of daily budget despite broad audiences**
Solution: X delivery issues typically have three causes: (1) Bid is too low — increase Target Cost bid by 25% to unlock more inventory; X is more bid-sensitive than LinkedIn; (2) Audience is too narrow — if targeting under 50,000 users, X's auction doesn't have enough scale, especially for conversion objectives; combine keyword tiers or expand follower lookalike seeds; (3) Ad quality score is low — X rewards high-engagement ads with lower CPMs; test 3-5 creative variants simultaneously to find the high-engagement format quickly, then allocate budget to the winner.

**Problem: High click volume but zero or near-zero conversions (website visit-to-lead rate under 1%)**
Solution: This is a landing page and offer alignment problem, not an X Ads problem. Diagnose in order: (1) Check if destination URL matches the promise in the ad copy — if ad says "free trial" but lands on a generic homepage, CTR is inflated by curiosity clicks; use dedicated landing pages per campaign objective; (2) Check mobile optimization — X traffic is 80-90% mobile; if your signup or trial flow is not mobile-optimized, you will see near-zero conversions on mobile; (3) Check page load speed — X users expect < 2 second load times; use Google PageSpeed Insights to test; (4) Audit the offer itself — if CTA is a gated form requiring 7 fields, convert to a 3-field progressive form or offer a lighter-weight CTA (watch a 3-min demo vs. book a 30-min call).

**Problem: X Ads generating volume but sales team says lead quality is poor — wrong personas, tiny companies**
Solution: X's targeting is less deterministic than LinkedIn (no professional profile data), so audience drift happens. Fix in three layers: (1) Add company size filtering via interest + behavior layering — although X doesn't have explicit firmographic targeting, combining "business owner/senior executive" interest + "enterprise software" interest + follower lookalikes from known enterprise-skewing accounts strongly filters toward larger company decision-makers; (2) Upload your existing customer list as a lookalike seed — this is the most accurate signal of who your real buyers are on X; (3) Add keyword exclusions aggressively — job-seeking language ("looking for a new role", "hiring", "open to work"), student language, and unrelated industry hashtags; these reduce non-ICP impressions significantly within 48 hours of adding exclusions.
