## Overview

CFOs are now the decisive gatekeeper in 83% of B2B SaaS deals above $50K — yet most marketing teams build campaigns for champions and end-users while ignoring the person who ultimately controls the budget approval. This prompt builds a complete, AI-orchestrated CFO economic buyer ABM program: from identifying CFOs in active buying cycles, to creating finance-specific proof assets, to running multi-touch sequences that surface your solution as CFO-validated rather than vendor-pushed. Use it when deals are stalling at approval stage, when CFOs are killing late-stage opportunities, or when you want to proactively engage CFOs before they become deal blockers.

DELIVERABLE 4: CFO DEAL RESCUE PLAYBOOK

For opportunities stalled at finance approval for 30+ days:

**Triage Assessment (use in Salesforce to auto-trigger):**

Day 30 of stall → Trigger "CFO Deal Rescue Workflow":
1. Automated Slack alert to AE + Marketing: "[Company] deal stalled 30 days at CFO approval — rescue protocol activated"
2. AE completes 5-question deal health survey in Salesforce (CFO objection, meeting held?, champion confidence level, competitive threat, urgency driver)
3. Based on survey responses, AI routes to one of 3 rescue tracks:

**Rescue Track A: CFO Hasn't Met with Us (most common)**
- Trigger: AE confirms CFO has not had direct meeting with vendor
- Actions: Executive sponsor outreach (CEO/CFO reaches out to CFO directly — "from CFO to CFO"), peer reference call setup (24 hours to arrange), personalized ROI model sent directly to CFO with cover message from AE
- Email template: "Hi [CFO name] — [Champion name] and I have been working together on the compliance evaluation, and I wanted to reach out directly. I've built a [Company-specific] version of the TCO model — would 20 minutes help you get comfortable with the numbers? I can send the model first so you can review before we speak."

**Rescue Track B: CFO Has Objection (budget timing, risk concern, competing priority)**
- Budget timing: Offer structured payment ramp (40% Year 1, 60% Year 2) and OpEx reclassification language for contracts; 30-day pilot at $0 with full contract execution following
- Risk concern: Customer CFO reference call within 48 hours; implementation risk one-pager with rollback clause language; vendor financial stability brief (ARR, retention rate, investors)
- Competing priority: "Wait package" — quarterly check-in cadence, compliance cost escalation alert service, reserved pricing for 90 days

**Rescue Track C: Champion Confidence Has Dropped**
- Trigger: AE survey indicates champion is less confident than 30 days ago
- Actions: Multi-threader strategy — identify second champion at CFO level; AE + VP Sales joint call with champion to rebuild confidence; produce "internal selling guide" for champion to re-approach CFO

**Complete Deal Rescue Email Templates:** Produce word-for-word email copy for CFO direct outreach in all 3 rescue tracks.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champions are resistant to sharing CFO-specific content, worried it will "alarm" finance before they're ready to present.**
Solution: Reframe the CFO content as "preparation help" rather than a pitch escalation. Provide champions with a specific script: "I want to give you the financial model our finance team typically reviews so you can anticipate their questions and prepare your own answers before they ask." Emphasize that the ROI calculator and executive summary are tools for the champion to own the finance conversation — not for the vendor to reach around them. When champions feel they're getting a competitive advantage (not losing control), adoption increases dramatically.

**Problem: CFOs don't respond to LinkedIn connection requests from AEs or VP Sales — especially at enterprise accounts.**
Solution: Shift outreach to CEO-to-CFO or CFO-to-CFO channel. The single most effective CFO outreach tactic is a direct email or LinkedIn message from your company's own CFO (or CEO) to the target CFO — peer-to-peer, not salesperson-to-CFO. If your CFO is unavailable, identify a board member or advisor who has a relationship with the target CFO's network. Simultaneously, invest in the "ambient awareness" strategy: run LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads targeting your CFO audience 4–6 weeks before any direct outreach so CFOs have seen your company's financial perspective content before the first personal touch.

**Problem: The ROI model numbers don't resonate because the CFO challenges our assumptions as vendor-biased.**
Solution: Replace all vendor-calculated assumptions with publicly auditable data sources. In the ROI model, cite sources for every benchmark: "Average compliance team FTE cost $95K loaded (PayScale 2025 Financial Services Benchmark)", "Average data breach regulatory fine in EU: €4.1M (GDPR Enforcement Tracker 2025)", "Compliance regulatory cost growth rate: 23% annually (Gartner GRC Spending Forecast)". Offer to run the model live in the CFO meeting where they control all input assumptions — when CFOs build their own numbers, they own the output. A $0 conservative scenario built by the CFO in a live meeting is worth more than a $500K ROI calculation built by the vendor.
