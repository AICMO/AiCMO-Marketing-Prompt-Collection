## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Event gets strong registrations but under 20% attendance rate**
Solution: This is almost always a reminder sequence failure. LinkedIn only sends one native reminder. Add a 3-touch email reminder sequence: 24 hours before (personal from founder), morning of event (include agenda + speaker bio), 30 minutes before (mobile-optimized with one-tap join link). Test different event times — Tuesday–Thursday 10–11am local time or 12–1pm typically outperforms Monday/Friday and early morning for B2B ICP.

**Problem: LinkedIn Live views are high but no pipeline attributed in CRM**
Solution: Pipeline attribution requires a completed conversion action post-event — not just attendance. Audit whether: (a) your post-event email CTA links include UTM parameters tracked in HubSpot/Salesforce, (b) your form or calendar link captures Lead Source field on submission, (c) the SDR follow-up creates a contact/lead record with the correct Campaign attribution before the activity ages out. Fix UTMs first — that is the root cause of LinkedIn Live attribution failure in 80% of cases.

**Problem: Guest speakers or co-hosts don't promote the event on their own LinkedIn profiles**
Solution: Remove friction completely. Write all their promotional posts for them, pre-schedule them, and share via a single Google Doc with copy-paste ready for each platform. Brief co-hosts 10 days before the event (not 2 days). Provide a 60-second Loom video explaining exactly what to post and when — busy practitioners will follow a clear script but won't create content from scratch for an event they're helping with. Offer to cross-promote their content in return.
