## Troubleshooting
- **Problem: Videos getting under 300 views despite following all best practices.** Solution: LinkedIn's algorithm requires engagement velocity in the first 60 minutes — cold publishing does not work. Before posting, notify 8-10 highly engaged connections to expect a new video and respond within the hour. Seed comments from genuine peers who add perspective. Even 5-6 comments in the first hour can 10x distribution. Never post without an activation plan.
- **Problem: Getting strong view counts but zero DMs and no pipeline.** Solution: Your CTA is either missing, vague, or buried. "Let me know your thoughts in the comments" generates engagement but not pipeline. Replace with a single explicit keyword trigger ("comment MAP and I'll send you the template") or a direct DM prompt ("DM me the word AUDIT and I'll show you where your close is leaking"). One specific ask with one specific response creates measurable conversion. Test different CTAs across the first 12 videos to find what your ICP responds to.
- **Problem: Strong start but video performance declining after the first 6 weeks.** Solution: LinkedIn video authority compounds over 90+ days, not 6 weeks. Declining performance in weeks 5-8 is normal — the algorithm is establishing your baseline. The accounts that win on LinkedIn video have 50-100+ videos published. If content quality is consistent and posting cadence is maintained, performance typically rebounds at the 10-12 week mark when LinkedIn's "creator" classification activates increased distribution to new audiences.
