## 3. FIVE COMPLETE TIKTOK SCRIPTS (PRODUCTION-READY)

**Script 1: Talking Head Education Video (60-90 seconds)**
Format:
- Hook (0-3 sec): On-screen text + spoken word designed to generate "wait, what?" in the first breath — must create a curiosity gap or articulate a pain so precisely that ICPs feel personally called out
- Problem expansion (3-20 sec): Explain the problem dimension the ICP hasn't named yet. Use specific numbers. Use industry language that signals you are one of them.
- Insight delivery (20-55 sec): The "aha moment." One counterintuitive insight, framework, or benchmark the ICP will want to save and share. Deliver it in the ICP's language, not marketing language.
- CTA bridge (55-90 sec): Soft close to next action — "If this is your situation, I put together a [free resource/walkthrough] — link in bio." No hard sells.
- Caption: 150-200 character caption with 3 primary TikTok SEO keywords woven naturally, 1 question to drive comments, hashtags at end
- On-screen text overlays: Mark 4-5 specific moments in the script where on-screen text reinforces the spoken word for muted viewers
- TikTok SEO keywords: List 5 keywords this video should rank for in TikTok search

**Script 2: Screen Recording Walkthrough (45-75 seconds)**
- Hook: "I'm going to show you exactly how [ICP job title]s at [Company] are [specific outcome] — watch this."
- Screen recording narration: Walk through a real (or realistic fictional) workflow moment that crystallizes the before/after value. Show the messy "before" state first — recognition is the conversion moment.
- Narration script: Full word-for-word narration timed to each screen moment
- On-screen text cues: What to highlight/annotate at each moment
- Caption with TikTok SEO keywords and comment-bait question

**Script 3: Hot Take / Opinion Video (30-45 seconds)**
- The take: One contrarian opinion about the ICP's industry, category, or common vendor behavior that is defensible but will generate comment debate
- Hook: Stated as the most direct, confrontational version of the take possible — TikTok rewards conviction, not nuance, in the first 3 seconds
- Defense (15-35 sec): 2-3 data points or logical arguments that back the take without completely backing down
- Engagement trap (35-45 sec): End with a direct question that forces a binary response ("Agree? Tell me I'm wrong in the comments.")
- Caption: Mirror the controversy, include the question again
- Note: This format is specifically designed to generate comment velocity — TikTok interprets high comment rates as "this content is polarizing but relevant" and distributes it to larger audiences

**Script 4: Comment-Reply Stitch/Duet Response (30-60 seconds)**
- Setup: Write a fictional comment from an ICP account that asks a common objection or question your product addresses (e.g., "We tried [category solution] and it didn't work because [common failure mode]")
- Stitch response script: Acknowledge the comment, validate the frustration, add new information they didn't have, and gently reframe — do NOT pitch your product in the response
- Why this format: Comment-reply videos inherit distribution from the original video's audience, show authenticity (you're responsive and thoughtful), and generate their own comment threads where ICPs self-identify
- Caption format for Stitch/reply: "Replying to @[username] — [one-sentence summary of the insight added]"
- TikTok SEO keywords to embed in the spoken response

**Script 5: Day-in-the-Life / Company Culture Video (45-60 seconds)**
- Format: POV or "a day in the life of [team member role]" — shows the human side of the company while signaling the type of work environment ICP buyers would want to be associated with
- Hook: Make it specific and unexpected — not "a day at [Company]" but "what a [VP Engineering] at a 50-person AI company actually does at 6am before anyone else is online"
- Narrative arc: 3-4 distinct moments, each revealing something authentic about company culture, product obsession, or customer impact
- Dual purpose: Employer brand + ICP brand familiarity (the same LinkedIn audience that evaluates your product also evaluates your culture)
- Caption: Culture-forward narrative with one call to action for job seekers AND one for prospects (two CTAs served by one post)

## 7. COMMENT CONVERSION PLAYBOOK

TikTok's primary pipeline conversion mechanism is not the link in bio — it's the comment section. ICP buyers who are genuinely in-market reveal themselves in comments. This section is your playbook for converting comment activity into pipeline.

**Comment Monitoring Automation:**
- Set up keyword alerts for comments containing: pricing-intent words ("how much," "pricing," "cost"), solution-intent words ("we need this," "our team has this exact problem"), and competitor mentions ("we tried [competitor]," "switching from [X]")
- Tool recommendations: TikTok's native comment management + third-party tools (Sprinklr, Hootsuite, or manual monitoring in the first 90 days)
- Response SLA: Respond to every ICP-sounding comment within 2 hours during business hours — TikTok's algorithm rewards comment engagement velocity and early responses generate notification traffic back to the video

**Comment-to-DM Bridge Scripts:**

Write ready-to-send scripts for 3 scenarios:

**Scenario A — ICP comments describing their exact pain:**
"[ICP job title] at [company type] comments: 'We literally had this problem last quarter and our CFO is losing it.'" 
Write a 2-step response: (1) public reply that adds specific value and ends with a DM invitation, (2) the DM itself that qualifies, provides a tailored resource, and opens a low-friction conversation.

**Scenario B — ICP comments with a skeptical objection:**
"[Decision-maker] comments: 'We tried something like this before and it didn't work because [common failure mode your product solves].'"
Write a response that validates without being defensive, adds new information that reframes the failure, and invites a conversation without ever mentioning your product by name in the public comment.

**Scenario C — ICP comments asking for the resource/tool mentioned in the video:**
"[User] comments: 'Can you share the framework/template you mentioned?'"
Write: (1) a public reply with a teaser and a DM prompt, (2) the DM that delivers the resource AND includes a next-step CTA (demo, newsletter sign-up, or consultation offer depending on ICP intent signals in their profile).

**Video Reply to Comments:**
TikTok's unique feature — replying to a comment by creating a new video — is your most powerful B2B pipeline acceleration tool.
- Write a template for identifying which comments deserve a video reply (qualifying criteria: comment from a verified ICP account, question that 10+ other ICPs would have, pain articulation that lets you demonstrate deep expertise)
- Script structure for a comment-reply video: Acknowledge → validate → expand with one new insight → soft CTA
- Why this works: Comment-reply videos are distributed to everyone who saw the original video AND shown to followers of the commenter — double distribution

**TikTok LIVE Strategy for Pipeline Generation:**
- LIVE format recommendation: "Office Hours" — 20-30 minutes of Q&A where ICP viewers can ask questions about their [category] problems in real-time
- Pre-LIVE promotion: 48-hour countdown posts with a specific question teaser ("Going LIVE Thursday 12pm ET to answer your questions about [specific pain point] — comment below with your question to guarantee it gets answered")
- During LIVE: Pin your link-in-bio CTA as a comment, mention it twice during the session, end with a 30-second explicit CTA for the specific resource or demo offer
- Post-LIVE: Repurpose the 3-5 best moments as standalone clips — LIVE clips get 30-40% higher FYP distribution than pre-recorded content within 24 hours of the LIVE session

**CRM Handoff Protocol:**

1. Create a "TikTok Organic" lead source field in HubSpot/Salesforce
2. Tag contacts with "TikTok DM," "TikTok Comment," or "TikTok → Bio Link" as first-touch source
3. For bio link traffic: UTM parameters auto-populate the source; build a HubSpot workflow that triggers a TikTok-specific nurture sequence (not your standard inbound nurture — TikTok leads are warmer on product awareness but may have lower purchase urgency)

CONSTRAINTS:
- Never create polished corporate content — TikTok's algorithm and its users penalize production over substance. A founder filming at their desk outperforms a produced ad every time.
- Sound-on design: Unlike Instagram (muted-first), TikTok viewers are predominantly sound-on. Scripts must be engaging to listen to, not just read. Tone of voice, pacing, and authenticity in the spoken word matter as much as the content.
- Still design for sound-off: The ~30% who watch muted need on-screen text to follow the narrative. Every key point should appear as on-screen text within 2 seconds of being spoken.
- The first 3 seconds are non-negotiable: TikTok's "interested" signal fires within 3 seconds. If the hook doesn't work, the video dies — it will not be distributed regardless of content quality after the hook.
- Never hard-sell in organic TikTok content: The fastest way to lose a B2B TikTok audience is to turn your channel into a product ad feed. Every video should provide standalone value. Your product is mentioned naturally when it's the solution to the problem you've just educated the audience about — maximum 1 product mention per 4 organic videos.
- Comment engagement is mandatory, not optional: TikTok's algorithm interprets comment velocity as relevance signal. Respond to every comment within the first 2 hours of posting.
- Consistency over virality: TikTok rewards accounts that post 5-7x per week with sustained compound distribution. One viral video on an inconsistent account produces a follower spike with no pipeline impact. Five videos per week for 12 weeks builds a distributable audience.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Videos are getting FYP distribution but the viewers are not the ICP — lifestyle content consumers, personal finance enthusiasts, or student demographics, not B2B decision-makers.**
Solution: TikTok's algorithm is learning the wrong audience from your early content signals. Audit your hashtag Tier 2 selection — if you're using community hashtags that attract consumer audiences (#PersonalFinance instead of #FinanceOps, #BusinessTips instead of #CFOLife), the algorithm is distributing your content to the wrong cluster. Replace broad consumer tags with the specific professional community hashtags where your ICP already consumes content. Also: add ICP-specific language and job titles into your on-screen text ("For People Ops leaders" or "If you're a VP Finance at a SaaS company...") — TikTok uses on-screen text to determine content relevance for audience matching. The more specific you are about who the content is for, the more accurately TikTok finds that audience.

**Problem: Educational videos are performing well (high completion rate, saves) but generating zero comment activity — people are watching and leaving silently.**
Solution: Your content is valuable but not controversial or incomplete enough to generate a comment impulse. Add a deliberate "comment trigger" to the final 5 seconds of every video: a direct question that requires a binary or short answer ("Is your team still doing this in spreadsheets — yes or no?"), a statement that most viewers will either strongly agree or disagree with, or an explicit invitation ("If this describes your company, drop the word 'YES' in the comments so I know to make more of this"). Comments are a learned behavior on professional content — you must ask for them explicitly in the first 30 days while the algorithm is still calibrating your account's community relevance signals.

**Problem: TikTok content is getting views and even some bio link clicks, but sales has no visibility into TikTok-sourced leads — attribution is broken and the channel isn't getting credit for pipeline it's influencing.**
Solution: TikTok's primary pipeline influence is dark social — it doesn't show up in last-touch attribution models. Fix this in three ways: (1) Add "Where did you first hear about us?" as a required free-text field on every demo request form — prospects who saw a TikTok video will say so; (2) Train SDRs and AEs to ask "Have you seen any of our content online before this call?" during discovery — TikTok-warmed buyers will often mention a specific video; (3) Track weekly branded search volume in Google Search Console and correlate with TikTok posting weeks — a consistent 15-25% branded search lift during active TikTok weeks is your channel-level attribution signal. Document these correlations in your monthly marketing report to build the business case for continued TikTok investment.
