## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts getting under 200 impressions consistently despite good content**
Solution: X algorithm heavily weights reply velocity in the first 30 minutes after posting. Build a "signal squad" — 5-8 colleagues or founders in a reciprocal engagement pod who commit to replying (not just liking) within 30 minutes of each post. Genuine replies from real accounts trigger the algorithm to push the post to a wider audience. Also audit posting time: for B2B audiences in the US, 7-9am ET Tuesday-Thursday drives 40-60% more impressions than Monday or Friday. Avoid posting after 3pm local time — by then, the algorithm has already decided your post's reach ceiling for the day.

**Problem: Generating engagement and followers but zero DMs or pipeline signals from ICP accounts**
Solution: Your content is optimized for reach and general appeal, not ICP qualification. Audit your last 30 posts: what percentage include language that only your specific ICP would care about (e.g., job titles, specific pain metrics, named tools they use)? Content that gets liked by marketing generalists but not your ICP buyers is the wrong content. Add ICP-qualifier language in the body of every post — specific numbers, named scenarios, or questions that only someone living your ICP's reality would engage with. Repelling non-buyers is a feature, not a bug.

**Problem: Can't convince leadership that X organic is generating pipeline — deals show up as "inbound" or "outbound" with no X attribution**
Solution: This is a measurement problem, not a performance problem. Implement three fixes immediately: (1) Add X as an explicit option in the "Where did you first hear about us?" CRM field and make it mandatory on all demo request forms; (2) Add to SDR call scripts: "Before we dive in, I'm curious — are you following our content on X? What did you see that made you want to connect today?"; (3) Run a 90-day retrospective analysis on all closed deals — look for X profile visits, link clicks via UTM, or DM history that preceded opportunity creation. In most B2B SaaS companies with an active X presence, 15-30% of pipeline has an X touchpoint that was previously untracked. Surfacing this data retroactively builds the case for continued X investment.
