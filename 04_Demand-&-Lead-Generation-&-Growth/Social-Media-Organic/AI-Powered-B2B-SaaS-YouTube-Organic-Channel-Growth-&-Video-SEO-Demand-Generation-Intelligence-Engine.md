## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Videos getting views but zero demo requests or lead magnet downloads**
Solution: Your CTAs are weak or misaligned to the viewer's buying stage. Audit each video: if it's informational/awareness content, the CTA should be a free resource download, not a demo request. B2B buyers watching "what is X" content are not ready to talk to sales. Map CTAs to intent: awareness videos → lead magnet → consideration videos → case study or ROI calculator → decision videos → demo. Also, check where in the video you're placing CTAs — the 1-minute mark CTA is often watched; end-screen CTAs are frequently skipped. Add a verbal CTA between minutes 1 and 3 in every video.

**Problem: Videos not ranking in YouTube search after 30 days**
Solution: Your keyword targeting is likely off — either targeting too-high-volume terms you cannot yet compete for (established channels with millions of subscribers dominate) or targeting zero-volume terms nobody searches. Use TubeBuddy's keyword score and filter for keywords with ≥20 monthly searches and a competition score below 40. Also verify your channel has enough topical authority: YouTube needs to see 5-10 related videos before it ranks new content in a cluster. Publish 3-4 videos on the same topic cluster before expecting ranking results. Finally, audit your video's retention graph in YouTube Studio — if viewers drop off before 40% of the video, YouTube depresses search ranking; identify the drop-off point and rewrite that section.

**Problem: Can't get team or founder on camera consistently; production keeps stalling**
Solution: Switch to a hybrid production model. Record one long-form interview (founder or subject matter expert, 30 minutes, conversational) per month → AI-edit into 4 distinct 8-12 minute videos using Descript. Use AI voiceover tools (ElevenLabs, Murf) for faceless screen-recording videos between recorded sessions. Faceless B2B YouTube channels (screenshare + voiceover, no face) routinely outperform face-on-camera channels in search ranking — YouTube's algorithm doesn't distinguish. The bottleneck is almost always editing, not recording; outsource editing to a Fiverr editor ($75-150/video) or use Descript's AI edit to reduce editing time to under 45 minutes per video.
