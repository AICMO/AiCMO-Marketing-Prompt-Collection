## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts getting low reach (under 300 impressions consistently)**
Solution: LinkedIn algorithm heavily weights early engagement in first 60 minutes. Build a "launch crew" — 5-10 colleagues who agree to react/comment within 1 hour of each post. Use a Slack channel to ping them. Once posts hit 10+ comments in 60 minutes, organic distribution amplifies 3-5x. Also audit your posting time — for B2B audiences, Tuesday-Thursday 8-10am local time outperforms Monday and Friday by 40%.

**Problem: Getting vanity engagement (likes from non-ICPs, random followers) but no pipeline**
Solution: Your content is optimized for reach, not qualification. Redesign posts to include ICP-qualifying language in the body — specific job titles, company sizes, or situations. Add a comment-bait question at the end that only your ICP would answer (e.g., "What's your current no-show rate?" filters for operations leaders, not general audiences). Repel non-buyers, attract buyers.

**Problem: Can't attribute pipeline to LinkedIn — deals say "outbound" or "inbound" but LinkedIn influence is invisible**
Solution: Add a mandatory "Where did you first hear about [Company]?" field to every demo request form AND to SDR call scripts. Score the answer: LinkedIn = 100 points, "colleague recommendation" or "heard about you but can't remember where" = often LinkedIn dark social. Track the percentage of new opportunities where LinkedIn appears anywhere in the contact's activity history before opportunity creation. Run a 90-day cohort analysis comparing deals with LinkedIn activity to those without — win rate and cycle time typically differ by 20-35%.
