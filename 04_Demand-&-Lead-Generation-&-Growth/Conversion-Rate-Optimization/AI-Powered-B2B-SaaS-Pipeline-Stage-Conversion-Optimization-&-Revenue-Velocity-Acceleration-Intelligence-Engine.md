### Analysis Framework

**Stage 1 — Conversion Gap Quantification**
For each funnel stage, calculate:
- Absolute conversion rate gap vs. benchmark (in percentage points)
- Deal count gap: how many more deals would advance if you hit benchmark
- Revenue impact: deal count gap × win rate downstream × ACV = estimated ARR at risk or opportunity
- Velocity gap: days vs. benchmark at each stage
- Priority ranking: (revenue impact × achievability) ÷ investment required

**Stage 2 — Root Cause Attribution**
For each underperforming stage, diagnose root causes using this framework:
- *Quantity problem*: Volume issue — not enough input driving this stage
- *Quality problem*: Wrong audience, wrong persona, wrong intent level entering the stage
- *Content/message problem*: Right audience, wrong message, assets, or value proof
- *Timing/cadence problem*: Right audience, right message, wrong timing or frequency
- *Competitive problem*: Losing ground specifically to competitor at this stage
- *Internal buying process problem*: External barrier (procurement, security review, legal) creating artificial stage drag

Use CRM data, Gong/Chorus call analysis, win/loss interviews, and sales feedback to attribute each gap to 1–2 root causes.

**Stage 3 — Marketing Intervention Design**
For each root cause, prescribe marketing interventions using this taxonomy:
- *Content asset*: Case study, ROI calculator, competitive guide, implementation guide, executive summary, third-party validation
- *Campaign motion*: Nurture sequence, retargeting campaign, ABM play, direct mail, executive engagement program
- *Sales enablement*: Battlecard update, champion deck, objection-handling guide, reference customer program, POC (proof-of-concept) marketing support
- *Experience design*: Demo environment, sandbox, interactive product tour, peer reference call facilitation
- *Process change*: Lead scoring threshold adjustment, SLA update between marketing and sales, MQL definition revision

For each intervention, specify: asset type, production timeline, target segment, distribution motion, measurement approach, and expected impact (with source/benchmark if available).

**Stage 4 — Prioritization Matrix**
Score each intervention on:
- Revenue impact (1–5): How many dollars does this unlock if successful?
- Speed to deploy (1–5, with 5 = fastest): Can this be running in 2 weeks or 6 months?
- Effort required (1–5, with 5 = lowest effort): Production cost, cross-functional dependencies
- Confidence level (1–5): How certain are you this will work, based on data?

Combined score = (Revenue Impact × Speed × Effort × Confidence). Build the 30/60/90-day roadmap based on this ranking, with the highest-scoring items first.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My CRM data is too messy to calculate accurate stage conversion rates.**
Solution: Start with a 90-day cohort analysis rather than point-in-time conversion rates — pull all MQLs created in Q1, then track what percentage reached each subsequent stage by Q2 end. This avoids deals-in-progress distorting your numbers. If data quality is a root cause, that's itself a finding: add "CRM data hygiene program" as an intervention in your 30-day roadmap, with Marketing Ops owning a weekly data audit.

**Problem: Sales pushes back on my root cause diagnosis and blames lead quality for everything.**
Solution: Separate the "MQL quality" argument from the "mid-to-late stage conversion" argument — they require different evidence. For MQL quality, pull the MQL Rejection Rate by lead source, persona, and content asset; if rejection reasons cluster around a specific source or persona, the data supports the quality argument. For mid-to-late stage, show deals that entered Stage 3 with high ICP fit scores — if these also stall at Proposal → Close, the issue is not lead quality but late-stage marketing support. Use Gong data (late-stage call themes) to make the case for champion enablement investment.

**Problem: I can't tell if my interventions are working fast enough.**
Solution: Don't wait for stage conversion rates to move — these are lagging indicators on 90-day sales cycles. Instead, track leading indicators weekly: (1) champion engagement rate with new enablement assets (tracked via Salesforce email clicks), (2) sales rep adoption of new battlecard (tracked via Salesforce/Seismic usage), (3) reduction in "no decision" language in Gong Stage 3+ calls. These signals move within 2–4 weeks and predict conversion rate changes 60 days out.
