## Overview
Analyzes your current pricing page (or designs one from scratch) using behavioral economics, value-based pricing psychology, and buyer self-qualification frameworks to maximize conversion rate from pricing page visit to demo request, free trial, or purchase. Use this when your pricing page converts below 3–5% of visitors, when you're launching a new pricing tier, or when pricing page drop-off is your top conversion bottleneck.

**2. BEHAVIORAL ECONOMICS ARCHITECTURE**
Apply the following principles to the pricing page design:

Anchoring Bias:
- What should the highest anchor price be? (Display highest tier first, or use a "Premium" plan shown crossed out)
- How to frame the middle tier against the anchor to make it feel like the rational choice
- Annual vs. monthly toggle psychology: position annual as savings vs. monthly as the expensive alternative, not just different billing cycles

Decoy Effect (Asymmetric Dominance):
- Design a tier that exists primarily to make another tier look more attractive
- Specify which tier is the decoy, which is the target, and why
- Price point spread recommendation (research shows a 3:1 ratio between adjacent tiers drives middle tier selection above 65%)

Compromise Effect:
- Design the tier structure so the middle tier is the "safe" choice
- What features must be in the middle tier to make it obviously correct for the modal buyer
- How to use the "Most Popular" badge without triggering buyer skepticism

Prospect Theory (Loss Aversion):
- Reframe pricing copy from "what you get" to "what you avoid losing"
- Specific copy formula: "[Outcome they fear losing] + [Feature that prevents it] + [Social proof from peer]"
- Annual commitment framing: "Lock in today's price before [trigger]" vs. generic "Save 20%"

Hyperbolic Discounting:
- How to design the free trial or money-back guarantee to reduce present-moment payment anxiety
- Payment timing language that reduces friction for budget-approval cycles (e.g., "invoiced quarterly" vs. "pay upfront")

CTA Design per Tier:

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My pricing is fully custom or usage-based and I can't display fixed numbers on the pricing page.**
Fix: Replace static tier cards with a pricing calculator widget as your primary conversion asset. The Value Calculator sentence in Section 3 gives you the formula — extend it into an interactive slider (Outgrow, Typeform, or a custom embed). Add one anchor range statement: "Most companies your size invest between $X and $Y per year" to set expectations, then direct all CTAs to "Get your custom estimate in 2 minutes." Calculator completions typically convert at 2–4× the rate of static pricing pages for usage-based and custom-quote models, because the buyer has already self-qualified and calculated ROI before speaking to sales.

**Problem: My pricing page receives mostly paid traffic, which makes A/B test results noisy and unreliable.**
Fix: Segment all A/B tests to organic + direct traffic only — exclude paid, which shows pricing page intent at a very different stage of the funnel. In parallel, apply the copy and behavioral architecture changes (anchoring, decoy tier, value metric reframe) directly without waiting for controlled test results — the structural improvements are so foundational they will lift conversion without a test. For paid traffic specifically, implement a dedicated paid-media pricing landing page with a lighter-touch CTA ("See pricing →" rather than the full pricing grid) to reduce bounce rate and improve ROAS on the acquisition side.

**Problem: The sales team is resistant to transparent pricing and believes "showing price loses deals."**
Fix: Run a 90-day controlled experiment: show transparent pricing to 50% of visitors; gate pricing behind a form for the other 50%. Track which group generates more SQLs, faster sales cycles, and higher close rates. In most B2B SaaS markets, transparent pricing reduces raw inbound volume by 20–30% while increasing ICP qualification rate by 40–60% — resulting in higher close rates and shorter cycles. Present this data. If resistance remains, use a "value range" approach ("starting from $X/mo" with "Get exact pricing" CTA) to anchor expectations and filter low-intent visitors without full price disclosure — a compromise that typically captures 80% of the conversion benefit of full transparency.
