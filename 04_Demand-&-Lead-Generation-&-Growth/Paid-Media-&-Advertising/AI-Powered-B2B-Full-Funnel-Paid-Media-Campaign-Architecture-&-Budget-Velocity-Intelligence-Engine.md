## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn CPL is 2x target after first 30 days.**
Fix: Check audience size — if < 50,000, LinkedIn is restricting delivery and inflating CPL. Broaden audience by removing one targeting layer (e.g., drop industry and keep title + seniority). Also check creative CTR: if < 0.3%, replace creative before blaming audience. Finally, test Lead Gen Form vs. website conversion — LGF typically reduces CPL 30–50% vs. website conversion campaigns.

**Problem: Google Ads spending budget but generating no MQLs.**
Fix: Audit landing page conversion rate (< 2% = landing page problem, not ads problem). Check search term report for irrelevant queries consuming budget; add 50+ negative keywords. Verify conversion tracking is firing correctly — a broken conversion pixel is the most common silent budget killer. If conversion rate is > 3% but SQLs are low, the issue is ICP mismatch: traffic is converting but from non-ICP audiences — tighten keyword match types to exact/phrase.

**Problem: Paid media MQL volume is strong but sales team says lead quality is poor.**
Fix: This is an attribution and ICP mismatch problem. Pull a paid-lead-to-SQL report segmented by campaign and keyword group — quality issues are almost always concentrated in 1–2 campaigns (usually broad match or TOFU LinkedIn). Add company size and industry filters to LinkedIn audiences. Create a "Paid MQL Score" in your CRM that discounts small companies and unqualified titles. Share the ICP definition with the paid media team in writing — vague briefs produce low-quality leads.
