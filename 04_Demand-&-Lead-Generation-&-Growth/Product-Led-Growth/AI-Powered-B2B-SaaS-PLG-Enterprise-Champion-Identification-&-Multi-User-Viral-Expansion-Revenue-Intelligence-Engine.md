# AI-Powered B2B SaaS PLG Enterprise Champion Identification & Multi-User Viral Expansion Revenue Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** plg, product-led-growth, champion-identification, viral-expansion, enterprise-conversion, b2b-saas, revenue-intelligence, seat-expansion

## Overview

This prompt transforms raw product usage data into a systematic enterprise expansion engine — identifying power-user champions inside accounts, detecting organic team-level viral spread, and orchestrating targeted marketing programs that accelerate bottom-up adoption into top-down enterprise contracts. Use it when your PLG product has individual or team adoption but you need to convert fragmented usage into structured, high-ACV enterprise deals without triggering premature sales friction.

## DELIVERABLE ARCHITECTURE

### Module 1: Champion Identification & Scoring

**Champion Score Formula:**
Calculate a Champion Score for each of the top 5 users using this weighted model:

- Usage Frequency (25%): logins per week × consistency bonus
- Feature Depth (20%): % of advanced features adopted; power users unlock features others haven't discovered
- Viral Behavior (25%): organic invites sent + content shared externally + colleague referrals generated
- Organizational Influence Proxy (20%): inferred seniority from job title + cross-functional collaboration (do others respond to their shared content?)
- Integration Adoption (10%): has this user connected enterprise integrations (SSO, CRM, Slack), suggesting they have IT/admin access?

Output: Ranked champion list with individual scores and specific champion activation strategy for #1.

### Module 3: Expansion Readiness Score

Score this account 0-100 across five dimensions:

1. **Organizational Breadth** (0-20): Multi-department vs. single-team usage
2. **Champion Strength** (0-20): Quality and influence of top champion
3. **Viral Momentum** (0-20): Current organic growth trajectory

5. **Business Context** (0-20): Company growth signals (hiring, funding, expansion news)

Score Interpretation:
- 0-39: Organic maturation needed — trigger nurture campaigns, not expansion outreach
- 40-59: Champion activation phase — engage champion directly, avoid sales introduction
- 60-79: Team expansion phase — champion-led invite programs + executive sponsor identification
- 80-100: Enterprise conversion ready — sales assist warranted, executive sponsor warm-up begins

### Module 4: Champion Activation Campaign Design

Design a 6-touch, 21-day champion activation sequence. This campaign targets the #1 champion ONLY and focuses on making them more successful — NOT on selling enterprise:

**Touch 1 (Day 1):** In-app message or email — celebrate a usage milestone ("You've automated X workflows this month — you're in the top 5% of [feature] users"). Include a personalization tip to unlock more value.

**Touch 2 (Day 3):** Educational content push — send the champion a use case story from a peer at a similar company (same role, same industry). Subject line: "How [Similar Company]'s [Same Title] used [Product] to [Specific Outcome]."

**Touch 3 (Day 7):** Team feature spotlight — introduce a collaboration or multi-user feature the champion hasn't activated. Frame it around helping their team, not adding seats: "Most teams at companies like [Company Name] unlock [X Feature] to share [specific benefit] across their [function] team."

**Touch 4 (Day 10):** Social proof + invite incentive — highlight peer companies where champions shared [Product] with their team and what happened. Include a frictionless invite mechanism (pre-filled invite template, one-click team import).

**Touch 5 (Day 15):** Champion recognition play — offer something that makes the champion feel elite (beta access, expert certification, community spotlight, direct line to product team). This deepens their identity investment in the product.

**Touch 6 (Day 21):** Soft ROI prompt — send a personalized "Your [Product] Impact Report" showing the champion their personal productivity gains. Include a sentence: "Imagine if your entire team had these results — [link to team calculator or case study]."

For each touch, specify: channel (in-app / email / LinkedIn), subject line or message hook, CTA, and fallback if no engagement.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion identified but not responding to activation emails (open rate <15%)**
Solution: Switch primary channel to in-app messaging — power users interact with the product more than their inbox. Reduce email frequency to 1 per week maximum. Check if champion's email was captured correctly (many PLG users sign up with personal emails not corporate). LinkedIn outreach from a CS or product team member (not sales) can break through if email and in-app are not converting.

**Problem: High viral coefficient in product data but no actual team invites being sent**
Solution: The invite flow likely has friction. Audit: (1) Is the invite CTA prominent in the product UI? (2) Does the invite email arrive in spam? (3) Is the invite onboarding experience good enough that champions feel confident sharing? (4) Is there a perceived "cost" to inviting colleagues (fear of forcing people onto a paid plan)? Most solutions: add free collaborator tier, simplify invite flow to one click, and add social proof in the invite email ("Join 847 teams at SaaS companies using [Product]").

**Problem: Account champion identified but executive sponsor unreachable via warm-up campaigns (low LinkedIn ad engagement, no email opt-in)**
Solution: Use job change tracking (Warmly, Keyplay, or 6sense) to identify when the champion's manager changes jobs — this often creates a re-evaluation window. Alternatively, identify a second-tier executive sponsor: instead of the VP, target a director-level manager who is one step closer to the champion and more accessible. Use the champion themselves to facilitate an introduction ("Would you be open to connecting your [Title] with our team to share how [Company X] used this across their org?").
