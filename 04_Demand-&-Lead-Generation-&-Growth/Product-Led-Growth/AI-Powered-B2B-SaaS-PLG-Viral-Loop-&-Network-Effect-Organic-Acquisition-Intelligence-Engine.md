# FRAMEWORKS & METHODOLOGIES

Apply the following frameworks explicitly in your recommendations:

**Viral Loop Design**: Andrew Chen's viral loop anatomy (trigger → mechanics → incentive → share → landing → conversion → back to trigger). Map each loop step explicitly.

**K-Factor Mathematics**: Bryan Balfour's growth system framework — decompose K into invite rate (what % of users send invites), invite volume (how many invites per inviter), and invite conversion rate (what % of invitees convert). Identify which lever has the highest improvement ceiling.

**Network Effect Classification**: NFX Bible by James Currier — explicitly classify network effect type and identify the "nfx moat" depth on a scale of 1–10 (platform nfx = 10, marginal nfx = 3).

**Product-Led SEO**: Kyle Byers / Backlinko programmatic SEO framework — user-generated content pages, template galleries, and embed/widget discovery pages as the three primary PLG SEO surfaces.

**Referral Psychology**: BJ Fogg's Motivation-Ability-Trigger (MAT) model applied to referral behavior — ensure referral prompts appear when motivation is highest (post-success milestone), ability is lowest friction (one-click share), and trigger is contextually relevant (not random pop-up).

## Integration Tips

- **Amplitude / Mixpanel / PostHog**: Create a "Viral Loop Events" taxonomy — instrument `invite_sent`, `invite_link_copied`, `share_link_created`, `share_page_viewed`, `share_page_signup_clicked` as separate events with properties including `loop_type`, `share_surface`, and `inviter_user_id` for attribution
- **Customer.io / Intercom**: Trigger referral program emails using product milestone events (not time-based — fire when the user hits the achievement, not on day 30). Set `referral_program_enrolled = true` as a user property to suppress re-sending
- **HubSpot CRM**: Create a custom property `acquisition_source_loop` on Contact records to distinguish Invite Loop, Content Loop, and Public Page signups. This enables cohort analysis comparing viral vs. paid CAC and LTV ratios
- **Viral Loops / ReferralHero (referral infrastructure)**: If using a third-party referral tool, pass your product milestone events via Segment or direct API rather than relying on email-only tracking — product-triggered referrals outperform email-only by 2–3x invite rate
- **Webflow / Next.js (public pages)**: Use ISR (Incremental Static Regeneration) for public dashboard/template pages to ensure SEO indexability without sacrificing load performance. Pair with dynamic OG image generation (Vercel OG, Cloudinary) to create thumbnail previews that drive social sharing CTR
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate the referral reward fulfillment — when referral converts to paid in CRM, trigger account credit webhook to billing system (Stripe) and send "Your reward is here" email from Customer.io

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: K-factor is being measured but invites sent per user is very low (<0.3) even after implementing prompts.**
Solution: The share moment is occurring before users experience value — users who share too early share low-quality content that doesn't impress recipients. Audit the trigger event: move the invite/share prompt 1–2 steps later in the activation sequence (after the first "aha moment" milestone, not before). Run a cohort analysis: compare invite rate for users who hit the activation milestone vs. those who haven't — if milestone users invite at 3–5× the rate, the timing fix will dramatically increase effective i.

**Problem: Invite-to-signup conversion rate is below 10% — referred users land on the share page but don't convert.**
Solution: The recipient landing experience is not demonstrating value fast enough. Test two changes in sequence: (1) Remove the signup wall — let recipients view the shared content without signing up first (Figma model), which increases share-page engagement by 40–60% and improves subsequent signup intent; (2) Add the inviter's name and company to the landing page headline ("Sarah from Acme Corp shared this Dashify dashboard with you") — personalized social proof dramatically increases conversion for warm referrals. Benchmark: B2B referral landing pages with personalization convert at 22–35% vs. 8–15% for generic signup walls.

**Problem: Public pages are getting indexed but organic traffic is not converting to signups.**
Solution: The conversion path has too much friction or the wrong CTA framing for cold organic visitors (who have zero context vs. warm referral recipients). Test: (1) Replace "Start Free Trial" CTA with "Use This Template Free" — outcome-oriented CTAs convert 25–40% better for template/tool pages; (2) Add inline social proof directly on the public page ("Used by 4,200+ teams") rather than requiring visitors to navigate to the homepage; (3) Create a lightweight "preview signup" that lets visitors enter just their email to save the template — full profile completion happens in the product after the conversion event.
