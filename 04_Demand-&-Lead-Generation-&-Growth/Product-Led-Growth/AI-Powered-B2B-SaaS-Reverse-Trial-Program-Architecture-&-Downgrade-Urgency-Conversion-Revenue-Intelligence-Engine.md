## Overview
This prompt engineers a complete reverse trial marketing program for B2B SaaS companies — from trial architecture design and pre-downgrade urgency sequences to feature lock-out communication frameworks, downgrade-day campaigns, and post-downgrade win-back orchestration. Use it when you run (or are evaluating) a reverse trial model where users receive full product access for a limited period and need a systematic, psychologically-informed marketing engine to maximize conversions before, during, and after the downgrade event.

## SECTION 2: PRE-DOWNGRADE URGENCY SEQUENCE

Apply the Prospect Theory Conversion Arc:
- Days 1–3: Gain frames ("here is what you have access to")
- Days 4–7: Progress frames ("here is how far you have come")
- Days 8–[X-3]: Hybrid frames (light loss preview + progress reinforcement)
- Days [X-2]–[X-1]: Hard loss frames + deadline urgency + offer

### Touchpoint 1 — Day 1: Welcome and Full Access Anchor
- **Channel:** Email + Day 1 in-app banner
- **Subject Line:** "Your [Product] Business plan is active — here's what to do first"
- **Message Frame:** Gain — anchor the user on full access value before it becomes expected
- **In-App Banner Copy:** "You have full Business plan access for the next [X] days. Start here →"
- **CTA:** [Core onboarding action — not upgrade]
- **Automation Trigger:** `trial_started`
- **Personalization:** Universal

### Touchpoint 2 — Day 3: First Value Checkpoint
- **Channel:** Email
- **Subject Line:** "Did you get your first [output] yet, [First Name]?"
- **Message Frame:** Progress — celebrate or encourage based on activation status
- **Behavioral Branch A (aha moment hit):** "You've already [completed action]. Here's what to do next to get even more value."
- **Behavioral Branch B (aha moment not hit):** "Most [ICP role] get their first [output] done in 15 minutes. Here's the fastest path."
- **CTA Branch A:** Next high-value action
- **CTA Branch B:** In-product guided workflow link
- **Automation Trigger:** `trial_day_3 AND aha_moment_completed = [true/false]`

### Touchpoint 3 — Day 7: Midpoint Progress Report
- **Channel:** Email (personalized data digest)
- **Subject Line:** "Your [Product] week 1 recap — [X] things you accomplished"
- **Message Frame:** Progress with subtle endowment effect ("look at what you've built")
- **Content:** Personalized usage data: outputs created, team members active, integrations connected, hours saved (modeled estimate)
- **CTA:** "Keep building — invite your team" (team expansion = strongest conversion predictor)
- **Automation Trigger:** `trial_day_7`

### Touchpoint 4 — Day [X-7]: Countdown Warning
- **Channel:** In-App notification badge + email
- **Subject Line:** "7 days left — make the most of your Business plan"
- **Message Frame:** Mild urgency + feature spotlight
- **In-App:** Notification badge on premium features with tooltip: "This feature is part of your Business plan — [X] days remaining"
- **CTA:** See everything included in your plan
- **Automation Trigger:** `trial_days_remaining = 7`

### Touchpoint 5 — Day [X-4]: Feature Lock-Out Preview
- **Channel:** Email
- **Subject Line:** "In 4 days, you'll lose access to [Most-Used Premium Feature]"
- **Message Frame:** Loss frame — specific, factual, tied to their actual usage
- **Personalization:** Identify the #1 most-used premium feature for this user from product analytics, name it explicitly
- **Body Direction:** "You've used [Feature] [X] times this week. On [Date], it gets locked. Here's exactly what changes — and how to keep it."
- **CTA:** "Upgrade now — keep [Feature] active"
- **Automation Trigger:** `trial_days_remaining = 4`

### Touchpoint 6 — Day [X-2]: Final Warning and Offer
- **Channel:** Email + in-app modal (fires on next login)
- **Subject Line:** "48 hours left — your [specific feature] access expires [Date]"
- **Message Frame:** Hard loss frame + deadline + offer
- **In-App Modal Copy:**
  > "Your Business plan expires in 48 hours.
  > After [Date]:
  > ❌ [Feature 1] turns off
  > ❌ [Feature 2] becomes read-only
  > ❌ Your team loses access
  >
  > [Upgrade Now — Keep Everything] [Not now]"
- **Offer:** First month at 20% off or extended trial for teams with 3+ members
- **CTA:** "Upgrade before [Date]"
- **Automation Trigger:** `trial_days_remaining = 2`

### Touchpoint 7 — Day [X-1]: Last Chance
- **Channel:** Email (send at 9am recipient local time)
- **Subject Line:** "Tomorrow at [time]: your [Product] Business plan ends"
- **Message Frame:** Final urgency — specific timestamp, specific loss, one clear action
- **Body:** Three sentences maximum. State exactly what happens tomorrow, name the single highest-value feature they will lose, provide one CTA.
- **CTA:** "Keep my Business plan — upgrade now"
- **Offer:** Same offer from Touchpoint 6, now with visible expiry countdown
- **Automation Trigger:** `trial_days_remaining = 1`

## SECTION 4: DOWNGRADE-DAY CAMPAIGN

### T-24H: Final Pre-Downgrade Email
- **Send time:** 24 hours before downgrade fires, 9am recipient local time
- **Subject:** "[First Name], your [Product] plan changes tomorrow — here's what to expect"
- **Headline:** "Tomorrow is the last day of your Business plan"
- **Body direction (max 120 words):** Open with the exact feature list that disappears. Use a 3-item bulleted list with ❌ icons. Acknowledge they built real work in the product. End with one CTA: upgrade now to preserve everything.
- **CTA:** "Keep my full access — upgrade now"
- **Offer:** Best available pricing, no expiry pressure yet (save hard deadline for T-0 and T+24H)

### T-0: Downgrade Confirmation Email
- **Trigger:** Fire within 5 minutes of `downgrade_event` product event
- **Subject:** "Your [Product] plan has been updated — here's what changed"
- **Tone:** Factual and calm — no alarm, no apology, no aggressive sales
- **Structure:**
  1. Confirm the change ("Your account is now on the [Free/Starter] plan")
  2. Value anchor: "In the last [X] days you [created X outputs / invited X teammates / connected X integrations]" — use real product data
  3. Feature restore section: "To restore [Feature 1], [Feature 2], and [Feature 3], upgrade below"
  4. One-click upgrade button: "Restore full access"
- **Personalization:** Inject actual usage data from product analytics API

### T+24H: Day-After Habit Disruption Email
- **Send time:** 24 hours after `downgrade_event`
- **Subject:** "How's [Product] feeling today, [First Name]?"
- **Frame:** Empathetic habit disruption — the user has now tried to do something the restricted tier does not support
- **Body direction:** "You've been building in [Product] for [X] days. Yesterday your plan changed. A lot of teams tell us Day 1 after the transition is when they notice it most — especially when they go to [most-used premium feature] and it's restricted." → Surface one specific feature they used most frequently → Offer a 72-hour restore window with a clear deadline
- **Offer:** "For the next 72 hours, you can restore full access at your trial rate. After [exact date and time], standard pricing applies."
- **CTA:** "Restore full access — offer expires [Date] at [Time]"

## SECTION 5: POST-DOWNGRADE WIN-BACK SEQUENCES

### Sequence A: Day 7 — Habit Disruption Moment

Research basis: 7 days post-downgrade is when users have experienced enough workflow disruption to reconsider their decision. They have attempted tasks that require premium features and been blocked.

**Email A1 — Day 7:**
- **Subject:** "It's been a week — how is [Product] working for you?"
- **Frame:** Empathy + practical solution
- **Body direction (80 words):** Acknowledge that the transition happened. Name the most common friction point teams report on the free tier. Offer a single, low-friction path to restore access. No pressure.
- **CTA:** "Restore full access — starting at $[price]/mo"

**Email A2 — Day 9:**
- **Subject:** "How [Similar Company] uses [Product] to [specific outcome relevant to ICP]"
- **Frame:** Social proof from a peer company in their segment
- **Body:** 2-sentence company story, 3 specific results (quantified), 1 CTA
- **CTA:** "See the full case study — then restore your access"

**Email A3 — Day 12:**
- **Subject:** "Last email for a while, [First Name] — one thing before we go quiet"
- **Frame:** Closing sequence — honest, not desperate
- **Body:** "We won't keep emailing. But we wanted to share one thing: [single strongest ROI data point relevant to their ICP]. If the timing is ever right, we're here."
- **Offer:** If budget is the blocker, offer a payment plan or starter tier option
- **CTA:** "Explore plans — or tell us what's holding you back" (link to both upgrade page and a 1-question survey)

---

### Sequence B: Day 30 — Personalized Impact Report

Research basis: 30 days post-downgrade, users have rationalized their decision. A personalized data story showing what they accomplished during the trial reactivates the endowment effect and loss salience.

**Email B1 — Day 30:**
- **Subject:** "Your [Product] impact report — what your team built in [X] days"
- **Content:** Fully personalized data digest using trial-period product analytics:
  - [X] [outputs] created
  - [X] team members collaborated
  - [X] integrations connected
  - Estimated hours saved: [X] hours (use industry benchmark model)
  - Most-used feature: [Feature name]
- **Frame:** "Here is what your team accomplished. With a paid plan, teams like yours typically achieve [2x/3x result] in the following 30 days."
- **CTA:** "Resume where your team left off — restore full access"

**Email B2 — Day 32:**
- **Subject:** "[ICP role] teams who don't [capability] fall behind — here's the data"
- **Frame:** Category urgency + competitive risk
- **Body:** Industry benchmark: "B2B marketing teams using [category] tools reduce [metric] by [X%] vs. teams operating without. Here's what that looks like for a team your size."
- **CTA:** "Run the ROI calculation for your team"

**Email B3 — Day 35:**
- **Subject:** "We have a plan designed for teams exactly like yours"
- **Frame:** Personalized plan recommendation based on team size and usage during trial
- **Offer:** Most relevant pricing tier for their profile + one-time promotion if applicable
- **Deadline:** Offer expires in 48 hours
- **CTA:** "[Plan Name] — [Price]/mo — Start now"

---

### Sequence C: Day 90 — Quarterly Budget Reset

Research basis: At 90 days, non-converted users are in a new quarter with potential new budget. Reframe the conversation around new product capabilities and a fresh ROI case rather than recapping the trial.

**Email C1 — Day 90:**
- **Subject:** "A lot has changed in [Product] since you last used it, [First Name]"
- **Frame:** New capabilities + time has passed + fresh start
- **Body:** 3 feature improvements or new launches since their trial ended. Position as "if you evaluated us before, the product is meaningfully better today."
- **CTA:** "Explore what's new — no commitment required" (links to changelog or new feature demo)

**Email C2 — Day 93:**
- **Subject:** "How [Company in their industry] went from [pain point] to [result] in 60 days"
- **Frame:** Relevant case study from their exact industry/role/company size
- **Body:** Story format: problem → solution → specific quantified result. One quote from the customer.
- **CTA:** "Read the full story" → Upgrade CTA at end of case study page

**Email C3 — Day 97:**
- **Subject:** "New quarter — new [Product] plan for your team"
- **Frame:** Fresh-start framing tied to Q[X] budget cycle
- **Offer:** Best-available pricing for win-back accounts (e.g., 2 months free on annual plan, or discounted first quarter)
- **Urgency:** Offer available for the first [X] teams that activate this quarter
- **CTA:** "Claim your Q[X] plan — offer ends [Date]"

### Cohort Analysis Dimensions
Segment every conversion cohort by:
- Trial start week
- Signup source (organic search / paid / referral / product viral loop)
- ICP segment (company size tier, industry, job function)
- Aha moment achieved (yes / no)
- Features activated at downgrade (breadth score)
- Post-downgrade action taken (no login / returned but no upgrade / upgraded)

### Recommended A/B Test Roadmap
1. **Trial duration:** 14 vs. 21 days — measure pre-downgrade CVR and 30-day post-downgrade reactivation
2. **Urgency frame:** Loss-framed subject lines vs. gain-framed subject lines — measure open rate and click-to-upgrade conversion
3. **Offer presence:** First month 20% off vs. no offer — measure CVR and downstream 12-month LTV
4. **Sales-assist threshold:** 60 points vs. 75 points — measure conversion rate and deal size vs. SDR capacity cost
5. **Downgrade-day email timing:** Immediate (T+0) vs. 4-hour delay — measure open rate and click rate
6. **Post-downgrade Day 7 message frame:** Empathy frame vs. social proof frame — measure reactivation CVR

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Pre-downgrade conversion rate remains below 8% despite running all sequences**
Solution: The problem is almost certainly in activation, not messaging. If users are not reaching the Day 7 aha moment, no urgency sequence can fix structural onboarding failure — users who have not experienced real value have no loss to fear. Pull a Mixpanel or Amplitude "path to aha" analysis comparing behavioral events for converters vs. non-converters. The divergence point (the action converters take that non-converters skip) is your broken onboarding step. Fix the in-product experience first — add a guided onboarding checklist, reduce time-to-first-output, eliminate setup friction. Only then will urgency messaging have a product-experience foundation to work from.

**Issue: Users are unsubscribing from pre-downgrade sequences at rates above 3%**
Solution: You are applying loss framing too early. If your Touchpoint 4 or 5 (Days [X-7] and [X-4]) is already warning about losing access, users feel manipulated and opt out before you reach the critical final 48-hour window. Audit your sequence: Touchpoints 1–5 should use gain and progress frames exclusively. Hard loss framing should appear only in Touchpoints 6 and 7 (final 2 days). Additionally, segment your suppression list: users who have not logged in for 5+ consecutive days should receive a reactivation email ("we haven't seen you in a while") rather than a conversion urgency email — they need activation help, not upgrade pressure.

**Issue: Post-downgrade sequences generate strong open rates but near-zero conversion**
Solution: The messaging is connecting but the pricing is blocking. Post-downgrade users have experienced the free tier, rationalized their decision not to pay, and require a different conversion lever than messaging alone. Run a Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter survey (4-question format) with a 10% sample of downgraded non-converters. If your entry paid tier exceeds their stated "acceptable price" by more than 1.5x, the sequence cannot overcome price objection through copy. Test a win-back-specific pricing offer: a 3-month introductory rate at 35% below standard pricing typically outperforms full-price annual offers for this segment by 3–4x, with no measurable LTV degradation at 12 months.
