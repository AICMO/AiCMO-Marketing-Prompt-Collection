## Overview
Designs and operationalizes a full Ecosystem-Led Growth (ELG) motion that converts your technology integration partner network into a reliable, compounding pipeline and revenue source. Use this when you have 5+ active technology integrations but are generating fewer than 15% of new pipeline through partners, when you are competing for share-of-wallet in a crowded category and need ecosystem moats, or when you want to accelerate land-and-expand by leveraging existing customer relationships at partner companies.

**ELG ARCHITECTURE — FULL BUILD:**

**Section 1: Partner Ecosystem Audit & Tier Prioritization**

Evaluate each integration partner on four dimensions (score 0–10 each, maximum 40):

Dimension 1 — ICP Overlap Score (0–10):
What % of the partner's customer base matches your ICP? Sources: Crossbeam overlap data, LinkedIn audience analysis, review of partner's publicly stated customer base. Scoring: >40% ICP overlap = 10 pts / 25–40% = 7 pts / 10–24% = 4 pts / <10% = 1 pt.

Dimension 2 — Partner Installed Base Reach (0–10):
How large is the partner's installed base, and how accessible are those customers through marketplace listings, co-marketing, or co-sell? Scoring: >10,000 customers accessible = 10 pts / 2,000–10,000 = 7 pts / 500–2,000 = 4 pts / <500 = 1 pt.

Dimension 3 — Integration Adoption Among Mutual Customers (0–10):
What % of your customers who are also customers of this partner have activated the integration? High adoption = proof of value, making co-sell easier. Scoring: >30% mutual customer integration adoption = 10 pts / 15–30% = 7 pts / 5–14% = 4 pts / <5% = 1 pt. If adoption is <5%, the integration needs improvement before co-marketing investment.

Dimension 4 — Partner Go-To-Market Willingness (0–10):
Has the partner engaged in co-marketing activities, responded to co-sell referrals, or invested in listing optimization with you in the past 12 months? Scoring: Active co-sell agreement + dedicated partner manager assigned to your company = 10 pts / Active co-marketing with joint events = 7 pts / Listed on marketplace but no active engagement = 4 pts / Technical integration only, no GTM engagement = 1 pt.

**Section 2: Ecosystem-Led Pipeline Playbooks — Three Core Motions**

**Play 1: The Account Mapping & Warm Introduction Play**

Objective: Identify prospects in your CRM who are already customers of your Strategic partners, and request warm introductions from partner account teams.

Step 1 — Account mapping setup: Connect your CRM to Crossbeam (or Reveal) and share your full customer list and prospect list with Strategic partners under a mutual NDA and data sharing agreement. Request the partner share the same. Crossbeam will identify overlaps without exposing the underlying data until both parties confirm sharing consent.

Step 2 — Identify warm introduction targets: Filter the overlap report to find companies that are: (a) in your CRM as an active prospect (stage: Awareness or Consideration), (b) already a customer of your Strategic partner, (c) match your ICP firmographic criteria (use the same MQA firmographic score criteria from your qualification framework). These accounts are your highest-priority warm introduction targets because you have proof the partner relationship exists.

Step 3 — Request warm introduction protocol: Build a templated email for your AE or Partner Manager to send to the partner's AE or CSM covering the target account. Template:

Subject: Mutual customer [Account Name] — warm intro opportunity?

Hi [Partner Rep Name], I noticed [Account Name] is a customer of yours and has been exploring [Your Product] — we think there's a strong use case given how well [Your Integration] works inside [Partner Product]. Would you be open to a warm introduction to their [Title] team? We're happy to reciprocate — I can pull a list of your prospects currently in our customer base if helpful.

Step 4 — Track and attribute: Create a custom field in CRM ("Partner Introduction Source") and log every deal where a warm introduction was provided. Track: warm intro → meeting booked rate, warm intro → opportunity rate, warm intro → closed won rate, average deal size for warm intro deals vs. cold outbound deals. Benchmark: warm intro from a Strategic partner should yield a 3–5x higher opportunity conversion rate than cold outbound.

**Play 2: The Integration Trigger Play (New Customer Activation)**

Objective: When a new customer activates your integration in a partner's marketplace, trigger an outbound sequence to that new joint customer to drive full product adoption and expansion.

Why this works: A company that just connected your integration inside their Salesforce or HubSpot instance has just demonstrated active intent to use your product with that specific workflow. They are at maximum receptiveness for expansion conversation — and they have never heard your full value proposition beyond what the integration does.

Step 1 — Marketplace install event capture: For each Strategic partner marketplace, configure a webhook or API call to fire to your CRM/MAP whenever a new install event occurs. Salesforce AppExchange, HubSpot Marketplace, and Slack App Directory all support install event notifications. Log the install as a new Lead or Contact record with source = "[Partner] Marketplace Install."

Step 2 — Integration trigger sequence: Immediately upon install event, enroll the account in a 5-touch integration welcome sequence:
- Day 0 — Automated email from your Head of Partnerships (not generic marketing): "Congrats on connecting [Your Product] with [Partner] — here are the 3 workflows our best customers set up in the first 48 hours." Include specific, tactical setup guides.
- Day 3 — Personalized email from AE or SDR: "Quick question — are you using [Your Product] primarily for [Use Case A] or [Use Case B]? The answer changes which setup template I'd recommend."
- Day 7 — Video walkthrough email: Loom video (< 3 minutes) from a Solutions Engineer showing the specific integration configuration that drives the highest ROI for companies with their stack.
- Day 14 — Case study email: "How [Similar Company] reduced [specific metric] by X% in 60 days using [Your Product] + [Partner] — happy to share the configuration template they used."
- Day 21 — Meeting request: "Would it be worth 20 minutes to review your current [Your Product] + [Partner] setup? Most customers find 2–3 quick changes that save X hours per week."

Benchmark: Best-in-class integration trigger sequences achieve 35–50% email open rates (vs. 22% industry average) and 15–25% meeting booked rates because the timing and context are perfect.

Step 3 — Upsell identification: Flag any integration install where the account is on your lowest pricing tier or free plan. Prioritize AE outreach for these accounts — integration activation is the highest-confidence signal that they are ready to buy the full product.

**Play 3: The Co-Sell Acceleration Play**

Objective: For every open deal in your pipeline where a Strategic partner is already deployed at the prospect, activate the partner's sales team to accelerate the sale.

Step 1 — Open deal partner overlay: Weekly, run a Crossbeam report to identify open opportunities in your CRM where the prospect is also a customer of a Strategic partner. Export this list to a shared Slack channel with your partner-aligned AEs.

Step 2 — Partner co-sell request: Your AE sends a co-sell activation email to the partner's rep or CSM covering that account:

Subject: Co-sell opportunity — [Prospect Name]

Hi [Partner Rep], I'm working with [Prospect] on a [Your Product] evaluation. I see they're using [Partner Product] — if there's a natural point to mention that [Your Product + Partner Product] integration delivers [specific outcome], that would be incredibly helpful. Happy to provide you with a reference case study and integration demo link. Would a 15-minute call to align on talking points work this week?

Step 3 — Partner co-sell tracking: Log all co-sell activation requests in CRM with a custom field "Co-Sell Partner" on the Opportunity record. Track co-sell win rate vs. non-co-sell win rate for deals at comparable stage and deal size. Benchmark: Co-sell deals should close at 15–25% higher win rate and 20–35% faster sales cycle than comparable solo deals.

**Section 3: App Marketplace Listing Optimization — High-Priority Partner**

For your highest-priority Strategic partner marketplace, complete the following optimization:

Listing title formula: [Your Product] for [Partner Product] — [Primary Outcome in 5 Words or Fewer]
Example: "Gong for Salesforce — Close More Revenue With AI"

Subtitle formula: [What it does] + [Who it is for] + [Time to value]
Example: "Automatically sync Gong call intelligence to Salesforce — built for revenue teams who close deals, not admin work. Set up in under 10 minutes."

Three-paragraph listing description structure:
- Paragraph 1 (The Problem): Name the specific pain your joint customer experiences when using Partner Product without Your Product. Use language your buyer uses, not your own marketing language. Example: "Revenue teams using Salesforce spend 4–6 hours per week manually updating CRM records after calls — time that should go to selling."
- Paragraph 2 (The Solution): Describe exactly what your integration does inside the partner's environment. Be specific about features, not benefits. Buyers on partner marketplaces want to know what it does, not what it is. Example: "Gong automatically transcribes every call and writes the deal status, next steps, risks, and competitor mentions directly to the Salesforce Opportunity record — no rep action required."
- Paragraph 3 (The Differentiation + Social Proof): Include one specific customer outcome with real numbers and one differentiating capability that competitors do not have. Example: "Drift customers using Gong + Salesforce close 22% more deals than those using CRM alone, with 60% less manual data entry per rep. Unlike other conversation intelligence tools, Gong's Salesforce integration supports real-time push — not batch sync — so your pipeline data is always current when your VP checks on Friday afternoon."

Review generation strategy:
- Identify your top 10 happiest customers (NPS 9–10, high product engagement, strong ROI case) who are also active users of both your product and the partner product
- Send a personalized email from your CEO or VP of Customer Success requesting a review — not a generic ask
- Provide a review guide: 3–4 specific talking points about the integration use case they care most about
- Benchmark: Target minimum 25 reviews within 60 days of listing optimization launch; most marketplaces begin featuring listings with algorithmic priority at 15+ reviews

Five metadata fields that drive discovery ranking on HubSpot Marketplace (adjust for your specific marketplace):
1. Category tags: Select all relevant categories, not just the most obvious one — multi-category listings get 3x more impressions
2. Keywords in listing title: Include the primary use case keyword your buyer searches (e.g., "call recording," "revenue intelligence," "sales forecasting")
3. Certified/Verified badge: Complete the partner certification process even if it takes 4–6 weeks — certified listings rank 40% higher in most marketplaces
4. Integration use case tags: Select every use case template your integration supports — each tag makes you discoverable in a new filter
5. Supported Hubs/Products: Specify every partner product module your integration supports — integrations listed as supporting 3+ modules appear in more discovery queries

**Section 4: 90-Day Strategic Partner Co-Marketing Campaign**

Joint campaign architecture with your top Strategic partner, targeting 50 net-new partner-influenced MQAs:

**Month 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4):**

Week 1: Partner alignment meeting — agree on shared ICP, joint value proposition, mutual pipeline targets, and campaign timeline. Identify 2–3 mutual customer reference accounts willing to participate in case study or webinar.

Week 2: Co-branded content production — create one joint solution brief (2–3 pages) and one mutual customer case study (1 page). Your team writes first draft; partner's PMM reviews and adds their logo, customer quotes, and approved statistics. Both teams own distribution rights.

Week 3: Account mapping run — use Crossbeam to identify the specific companies you will target together. Aim for a target account list of 200–500 companies that are current partner customers, match your ICP, and are not yet your customers.

Week 4: Joint outbound launch — your SDR team and partner's ISR team each email the target account list from their respective domains with a personalized outreach referencing the joint solution. This "two-sender" approach yields 40–60% higher reply rates than single-sender campaigns because prospects recognize the partner brand.

**Month 2 — Activation (Weeks 5–8):**

Week 5–6: Joint webinar — "How [Company Type] Teams Use [Your Product] + [Partner Product] to [Achieve Outcome]." Co-host with a product expert from each company and one mutual customer as a live reference. Target 200+ registrants split between your respective email databases.

Webinar promotion split: Your team promotes to your email list and LinkedIn audience. Partner promotes to their customer newsletter and partner email list. Both teams run LinkedIn event ads targeting the shared ICP. Expected 25–35% registration-to-attendance rate; plan for 45-minute content + 15-minute Q&A.

Week 7: Post-webinar follow-up sequence — within 24 hours, all attendees and registrants receive a personalized follow-up from the co-hosting team member most relevant to their job title, plus a link to the joint solution brief and a one-click meeting scheduling link.

Week 8: Marketplace promotion push — partner adds a "Featured Integration" spotlight to their customer newsletter (target: 30,000+ subscribers) with a direct link to your marketplace listing. Reciprocate by featuring the integration prominently in your own customer newsletter and in-app onboarding flow.

**Month 3 — Pipeline Conversion (Weeks 9–12):**

Week 9–10: Co-sell activation — for all webinar attendees and warm introduction targets who have not yet become opportunities, run the co-sell activation play (Play 3 above) with both your AE team and the partner's rep team simultaneously.

Week 11: Mutual customer case study launch — publish the joint case study as a co-branded landing page on both company websites and promote to LinkedIn with paid amplification (budget: $3,000–$5,000 split between both teams). Gate with email capture to generate 30–50 net new contacts.

Week 12: Program measurement and QBR — both teams review: total partner-influenced MQAs generated, webinar attendee-to-opportunity conversion rate, co-sell win rate, marketplace lead volume from the joint promotion, and partner satisfaction score. Present joint results to both leadership teams and agree on next 90-day program.

Lead sharing and attribution protocol:
- Use Crossbeam's "Shared Pipeline" feature or a simple Google Sheet protected by NDAs to share contact names and opportunity details (not financial data) for co-sell coordination
- Create a CRM field "Partner-Influenced" (Yes/No) and "Influencing Partner" (picklist) on the Opportunity object
- A deal is "partner-influenced" if: the prospect was on a co-produced asset, attended a joint event, received a warm introduction from the partner, activated the integration, or the partner's rep was engaged in co-sell conversations

**Section 5: Partner Data Activation Without Compliance Risk**

The three legally safe, high-value data activation methods:

Method 1 — Crossbeam/Reveal Overlap Data (Safest, Most Scalable):
Crossbeam and Reveal use a secure data escrow model where neither party can see the other's full customer list — only the confirmed overlapping accounts. This makes them safe to use without a full data-sharing agreement because no raw customer data is exchanged. Use overlap data to: prioritize outbound sequences, identify warm introduction targets, and flag open deals where partner co-sell can accelerate.

Method 2 — Co-Marketing Audience Sharing (Safe with GDPR/CCPA consent framework):
Agree with partner to co-produce a gated asset (webinar, report, calculator). Both companies promote to their respective audiences. Both companies receive the list of contacts who engaged with the joint asset — these contacts have explicitly consented to hear from both companies (if your joint registration form includes disclosure language such as: "By registering, you agree to receive follow-up communication from [Your Company] and [Partner Company]."). Use this list to trigger your integration trigger sequence and partner-influenced nurture track.

Method 3 — Integration Install Event Data (Your Own Data, No Agreement Needed):
When a customer activates your integration inside the partner's platform, that install event is your data — you own the relationship with your customer. No data sharing agreement is needed to activate your own customers based on their product behavior. This is the lowest-friction and highest-conversion data source for ELG programs.

Data sharing agreement template (key clauses to include for Method 2):
- Purpose limitation: Data shared under this agreement may only be used for [specific co-marketing campaign name and date range] and will not be added to general marketing databases
- Data minimization: Share only Name, Title, Company, Email, and Engagement Type — no financial, product usage, or behavioral data
- Retention limit: Both parties delete shared contact data within 90 days of campaign end unless the contact has independently opted into the receiving company's database
- Opt-out honoring: Both parties commit to honoring opt-outs and unsubscribes across the shared contact list within 48 hours

**Section 6: ELG Measurement Framework — 7 Core KPIs**

## Integration Tips

**Crossbeam:** Connect your Salesforce or HubSpot CRM to Crossbeam and send partnership invitations to your top 5 integration partners within your first week. Set up "Population" rules to share: (1) all active customers, (2) all open opportunities, and (3) your target account list. Configure Crossbeam Slack alerts to notify your AEs in real-time when a partner-overlap account in their territory enters a co-sell scenario.

**Salesforce (CRM):** Add three custom fields to the Opportunity object: "Partner Influenced" (Checkbox), "Influencing Partner" (Multi-select picklist of your Strategic partners), and "Partner Touch Type" (Picklist: Warm Introduction / Co-Sell Active / Marketplace Install / Joint Event / Co-Branded Content). Build a Salesforce report to track partner-influenced pipeline monthly and include it in your CMO dashboard.

**HubSpot:** Use HubSpot's "Company" object to track partner ecosystem data. Create a custom property "Integration Partner Status" to flag accounts that are customers of Strategic partners. Build a workflow that automatically enrolls accounts tagged as both "Partner Customer Overlap" and "ICP Confirmed" into your warm introduction target list and notifies the assigned HubSpot rep with a task to request a partner introduction.

**Slack:** Create a dedicated #ecosystem-co-sell channel in Slack where your Crossbeam alerts feed in real-time notifications of new partner overlaps entering your pipeline. Invite your Strategic partner managers or ISRs as Slack Connect guests so co-sell coordination happens in real-time rather than through email chains that create 48-hour delays.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner reps are not responding to co-sell requests — you send activation emails and hear nothing back.**
Fix: The core issue is almost always that the partner rep has no personal incentive to engage. Escalate to the partner company's Director of Partnerships or VP of Alliances and propose a formal co-sell incentive structure — ask whether the partner's reps receive any credit or bonus for facilitating successful referrals to your product. If yes, remind reps of this incentive in your activation email. If no incentive exists, propose to the partner leadership that you build one (e.g., you will pay a referral fee or provide Salesforce gift cards to individual reps for every co-sell deal that closes). Most co-sell activation failures are structural, not relational — solve the incentive problem, not the relationship problem.

**Problem: Your app marketplace listing has been live for 6+ months but generates fewer than 3 installs or leads per month.**
Fix: Run a listing audit against these four factors in this order: (1) Review count — if below 10, no optimization of listing copy or metadata will materially move ranking; prioritize getting to 15 reviews before anything else. (2) Category tagging — log out of your partner portal and search as a buyer for your use case; if your listing does not appear in the first 10 results, you are missing key category tags or keywords in your title. (3) Integration description relevance — rewrite paragraph 1 to name the specific pain in the exact words buyers type in marketplace search bars (use the marketplace's own "trending searches" feature if available, or check competitor listing titles). (4) Certification status — uncertified listings are algorithmically deprioritized on every major marketplace; complete certification even if it requires 2–4 weeks of engineering time to meet the partner's security and API requirements.

**Problem: You have account mapping data showing 40+ high-ICP partner customer overlaps, but converting those to meetings is taking too long — response rate to warm introduction requests is below 20%.**
Fix: A low warm introduction acceptance rate from partner reps typically indicates one of three issues: (a) You are asking for too much — simplify the request to "Can you send a 2-sentence intro email?" rather than asking them to arrange a full meeting. A lower-friction ask gets a 3–5x higher acceptance rate. (b) The outreach is going to a partner rep who does not cover that account — verify the account assignment before outreaching; sending to the wrong partner rep guarantees a non-response. (c) The partnership is at the wrong level — if you are reaching out to individual partner reps without a formal co-sell agreement at the management level, reps have no context for why they should help you. Secure a co-sell endorsement from the partner's VP of Sales before activating rep-level co-sell outreach. Without top-down buy-in, you are relying on individual partner reps to volunteer their time with no mandate to do so.
