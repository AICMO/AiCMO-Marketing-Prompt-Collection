## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce PRM / Partner Community:** Map the MDF request, approval, POE submission, and reimbursement workflow to Salesforce PRM objects. Use Flows to automate tier-based approval routing and notification emails. Link deal registrations to MDF campaigns via Campaign Member records.
- **Impartner / Allbound / Zift:** Configure the MDF module with the rate card from Section 2 as activity types. Use auto-validation rules to flag POE submissions that don't include required file attachments before they reach the channel manager queue.
- **HubSpot:** Create a Partner MDF Pipeline in HubSpot using custom deal properties for MDF amount requested, approved amount, activity type, POE status, and pipeline attributed. Use Workflows to automate partner notification emails at each stage.
- **Google Sheets / Notion:** For programs without a partner portal, use a Google Form for MDF requests feeding into a Google Sheet tracker with Zapier automations triggering approval emails and Slack alerts to the channel team.
- **Looker / Tableau:** Build an MDF ROI dashboard pulling from CRM + finance data showing utilization rate by tier, pipeline-to-MDF ratio by activity type, and partner performance rankings updated in real time.
- **DocuSign / PandaDoc:** Automate MDF agreements and POE attestation documents with e-signature workflows triggered at the approval stage — eliminates PDF email chains and creates an audit trail.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Partners claim funds but don't register deals in the CRM, making ROI attribution impossible.
**Solution:** Make deal registration a pre-condition for POE acceptance. If no deal is registered in the CRM within 30 days of campaign completion, the POE is automatically marked incomplete and the reimbursement is placed on hold. Communicate this rule in partner onboarding and the MDF agreement — not as a surprise at reimbursement time.

**Problem:** Small/Silver-tier partners never use their MDF because the request process is too complex.
**Solution:** Create a "Fast Track MDF" lane for requests under $2,000: a 5-field form, 24-hour auto-approval, and simplified POE (single screenshot + meeting notes). Most small partners run 2–3 small activities per year — reducing friction at low spend amounts increases overall utilization rates by 30–40% without meaningful compliance risk.

**Problem:** Channel managers spend 60%+ of their time processing MDF paperwork instead of strategic partner development.
**Solution:** Implement auto-approval for all activities matching pre-defined criteria (existing activity type, within tier allocation, partner not in probation). Reserve human review for exceptions only. Target: ≤20% of requests require channel manager touch. Track this metric in the MDF dashboard and reduce it quarterly through rule refinement.
