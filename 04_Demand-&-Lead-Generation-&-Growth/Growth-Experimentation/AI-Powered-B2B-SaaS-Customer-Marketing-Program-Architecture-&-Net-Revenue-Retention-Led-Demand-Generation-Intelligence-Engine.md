DELIVERABLE 2 — EXPANSION REVENUE CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE

Section 2A: Expansion Signal Scoring Model

Build a weighted expansion signal detection system that scores each customer account 0–100:

Product-Based Signals (require integration with Pendo/Mixpanel/Amplitude):
- Usage ceiling (>80% of any plan limit): +35 points
- Adjacent feature engagement (uses Feature X which is 3x more valuable with Product Y): +25 points
- Power user cluster (3+ users in top decile of activity): +20 points
- Export frequency (exporting data to external tools >5x/week — signals manual work that product can automate): +15 points
- API rate limit approaching: +15 points

Firmographic Signals (require Clay/Apollo/LinkedIn integration):
- Customer company raised funding in past 90 days: +25 points
- Customer company grew headcount >15% in past 6 months: +20 points
- New economic buyer or executive hired: +15 points
- Customer company acquired another company: +20 points

Behavioral Signals:
- Customer opened 2+ expansion-related emails in past 30 days: +15 points
- Customer visited pricing page while logged in: +25 points
- Customer submitted support ticket requesting feature in expansion product: +30 points

Scoring Thresholds:
- Score 0–39: Marketing automation only — educational content drip, no direct pitch
- Score 40–69: CS-assisted expansion — CSM receives Gainsight alert with expansion brief and talking points; marketing runs parallel air cover campaign
- Score 70–100: Sales-assisted expansion — AE receives Salesforce expansion opportunity + marketing begins direct expansion campaign sequence

Section 2B: Expansion Campaign Sequence Template (7-Touch, 28-Day Program)

Design this sequence for each expansion product:

Touch 1 — Day 1 (Educational, Zero Pitch):
- Subject: "How [peer company in same vertical and size] solved [specific problem your expansion product addresses]"
- Content: Mini case study or data point showing outcome — not a product description, a customer result
- AI Agent Task: Select most relevant proof point from case study library based on customer's industry + company size + use case → personalize introduction with customer's actual context → send from CSM name

Touch 2 — Day 5 (ROI Quantification):
- Subject: "What [problem your expansion product solves] is actually costing [Customer Company] — a rough estimate"
- Content: Personalized ROI estimate using customer's actual usage data × industry benchmark data for the problem being solved
- AI Agent Task: Pull customer's usage metrics → apply ROI model for expansion product → generate personalized cost-of-not-having calculation → insert actual numbers into email

Touch 3 — Day 9 (Peer Validation):
- Subject: "[Peer customer first name] at [peer company] added [metric] in [timeframe]"
- Content: Real customer quote about the expansion product — from someone in their industry at their company size
- AI Agent Task: Pull most relevant customer testimonial from advocacy library by matching customer's industry, revenue size, and primary use case

Touch 4 — Day 13 (Low-Friction Demo):
- Subject: "3-minute [Expansion Product] walkthrough — no call required"
- Content: Recorded product tour of expansion product showing the 2–3 features most relevant to their current use case
- AI Agent Task: Identify which expansion product features are most relevant to this customer's usage pattern → serve the custom product tour flow → track video engagement → if >60% watch rate, escalate to Touch 5 immediately

Touch 5 — Day 17 (CSM Enablement Signal):
- AI Agent Task: If customer engaged with Touches 1–4 (2+ opens, 1+ click, or video view >50%), create Gainsight CS task: "Expansion-ready signal detected for [Customer]. Recommended talk track: [AI-generated customized talking points based on their specific usage signals]. Best window: next scheduled customer call."

Touch 6 — Day 21 (Direct Offer — Only After CSM Confirms Readiness):
- Subject: "A proposal for [Customer Company]: [expansion product] trial or add-on"
- Content: Specific offer — trial period, discounted first year, bundled pricing — with a clear single call-to-action
- Gate: This email only sends if CSM confirms expansion readiness in Gainsight within 7 days of Touch 5 alert

Touch 7 — Day 28 (Follow-Up / Objection Handling):
- AI Agent Task: If no response after Touch 6, pull the most common objection type for this customer segment → generate objection-specific follow-up addressing the most likely hesitation (price, timing, ROI uncertainty, IT approval required)

DELIVERABLE 3 — RENEWAL RISK REDUCTION & CHURN PREVENTION MARKETING

Section 3A: Pre-Renewal Marketing Sequence (90 Days Before Renewal Date)

T-90 Days — Strategic Value Anchoring Phase:

Action 1: AI-Generated Annual ROI Report
- AI Agent Task: Pull customer's full 12-month usage data → calculate outcomes delivered using product ROI model → compare against industry benchmark → generate personalized "Annual Business Impact Report" as PDF → send to champion with subject "Your [Company] results with [Product] — Year [N] in review" → CC economic buyer if in CRM
- Goal: Lock in economic buyer's perception of ROI before any competitor conversation or budget review

Action 2: Executive Market Intelligence Brief
- AI Agent Task: Pull 3 recent industry developments relevant to customer's business (from news API or Perplexity) → generate 250-word executive brief → frame product's role in helping customer navigate these trends → send from CEO or VP of CS to customer's economic buyer
- Goal: Re-establish executive relationship at the "thought partner" level before renewal is on the table

Action 3: Peer Benchmarking Report
- AI Agent Task: Aggregate anonymized performance data from comparable customers (same industry, company size, use case) → calculate customer's percentile ranking → generate personalized benchmark report showing how they compare → include specific recommendations to improve their ranking
- Goal: Create competitive anxiety (low performers worry about falling behind) and pride (high performers want to maintain their position) — both drive renewal

T-60 Days — Value Amplification Phase:

Action 1: Year-in-Review Success Story Package
- AI Agent Task: Compile customer's top 5 measurable wins from the year → generate "Your Year with [Product]" email with visual metrics summary → ask champion to share internally before renewal discussion

Action 2: Product Roadmap Preview
- AI Agent Task: Pull customer's most-requested features from support tickets and CSM notes → map against product roadmap → generate personalized "What's coming for you in [Year+1]" preview focused on features relevant to their use case
- Message: Make staying feel like an investment in something better, not a continuation of the status quo

Action 3: Community Spotlight Nomination
- Action: Feature customer in community newsletter or customer spotlight — creates social investment in the relationship and public acknowledgment of their success
- AI Agent Task: Identify customers with strong results AND community activity → draft spotlight feature with their permission → publish → tag customer in social post

T-30 Days — Decision Acceleration Phase:

Action 1: Renewal Readiness Survey (3 questions maximum)
- Question 1: "On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to renew with [Product]?"
- Question 2: "What would make you even more confident in renewing?"
- Question 3: "Is there anyone else at [Company] who should be part of the renewal conversation?"
- AI Agent Task: Send survey → parse responses → route: Score ≥8 → advocate program invitation; Score 6–7 → CSM alert for proactive conversation; Score ≤5 → churn risk protocol activated immediately

Action 2: Objection-Response Campaign (Triggered by Survey Responses)
- Price concern → send ROI report + TCO comparison vs. building in-house or switching to competitor
- Feature gap concern → send product roadmap with specific feature timeline + personal note from Product team
- Low adoption concern → offer complimentary onboarding reset + training session before renewal
- Champion uncertainty → schedule executive alignment call between your VP and their VP

T-14 Days — Renewal Execution Support:

Action 1: Champion Enablement Kit for Internal Renewal Selling
- Package: 1-page ROI summary for CFO, 3-slide "why we're renewing" presentation, peer customer quotes, renewal FAQ for procurement
- AI Agent Task: Generate all assets customized with customer's specific metrics → email to champion with subject "[First name] — here are assets to help get renewal approved internally"

Action 2: Competitor Displacement Protection (If Competitor Activity Detected)
- Trigger: CS notes competitive evaluation / champion mentions competitor name in call transcript
- AI Agent Task: Generate competitive displacement package specific to the competitor named → brief champion on key differentiation points → offer head-to-head trial or reference call with customer who evaluated same competitor

Section 3B: Churn Risk Intervention Marketing Program

Activation Threshold: Health score drops below 60, or renewal score ≤5, or no login in 14+ days

Week 1 — Immediate Re-Engagement:
- CSM alert within 4 hours of threshold breach
- Marketing sends: "We noticed something — can we help?" email from CSM with specific observation ("your team's usage dropped after [date] — is there something we can address?") + offer for dedicated onboarding refresh call
- AI Agent Task: Identify the specific usage cliff — what stopped, when, which users went inactive — and include this diagnosis in the CSM alert + customer email

Week 2 — Value Rescue:
- Offer: Complimentary "Success Intensive" — 90-minute session with implementation specialist to identify why value isn't being realized
- AI Agent Task: Pull customer's onboarding completion status → identify which key features were never fully adopted → generate personalized session agenda focused on those gaps → send to champion with specific outcome promise

Week 3 — ROI Forensics:
- AI Agent Task: Run "value gap analysis" — where were the expected outcomes promised at sale vs. what was actually achieved? Generate honest assessment + specific action plan to close the gap
- Message: "We think you should be getting [X] from [Product]. You're currently getting [Y]. Here's a specific 30-day plan to close that gap."

Week 4 — Recovery Decision:
- If engagement (opens, responses, booked calls): Continue with personalized recovery program
- If no engagement: Escalate to executive save attempt (VP or CEO to customer's executive)
- If executive escalation rejected: Graceful offboarding sequence — protect relationship for future re-engagement, gather churn reason data, request case study if outcome was positive despite cancellation

Section 4A: Promoter Identification & Advocacy Recruitment System

Advocacy Identification Triggers:
- NPS response ≥9 → advocacy invitation within 48 hours (not 2 weeks — strike while sentiment is hot)

- Community power user (top 15% by post count or engagement) → invite to Customer Advisory Board
- Customer hits landmark metric (10th customer success milestone, 1-year anniversary, 50th use case) → advocacy recognition email

Advocacy Tier Architecture:

TIER 1 — Digital Advocates (Low friction, highest volume):
- Ask: Write a G2 or Capterra review
- Incentive: $50 gift card / premium swag / feature early access
- AI Agent Task: Pull customer's most impressive metric from product data → pre-populate G2 review template with their specific outcome as a starting prompt (e.g., "You've reduced [metric] by [X%] — here's a review template that captures your results")
- Target: 5x G2 reviews in 6 months; maintain 4.5+ star average

TIER 2 — Content Advocates (Medium commitment, targeted volume):
- Ask: Participate in case study / webinar speaker / podcast guest / research report contributor
- Incentive: Published thought leadership, speaking visibility, co-marketing amplification, direct access to CPO or product roadmap sessions
- AI Agent Task: Generate case study interview questions personalized to customer's industry + specific outcomes → post-interview, transcribe call → generate first draft case study from transcript → route to PMM for editing

TIER 3 — Reference Advocates (High commitment, strategic value):
- Ask: Take live peer reference calls with prospects in active sales deals
- Incentive: Exclusive VIP advisory council membership, direct product influence, CEO relationship
- Management: Reference coordinator (human or AI-assisted) matches reference customers to prospects by industry + company size + pain point + deal stage

DELIVERABLE 5 — CUSTOMER REFERRAL DEMAND GENERATION PROGRAM

Section 5A: Referral Program Architecture

Program Design:

Referral Incentive Model:
- Cash/credit option: $500 account credit per referred customer who closes (suitable for SMB/Mid-Market customer base)
- Charitable donation option: $500 donation to customer's chosen charity (preferred for enterprise customers where cash incentives create procurement issues)

Referral Ask Timing:
- NOT at onboarding (too early, trust not established)
- YES at first value milestone (Day 30–60 after customer achieves first measurable outcome)
- YES immediately after NPS ≥9 response
- YES at contract renewal (proven ROI creates highest referral intent)
- YES after case study publication (customer's pride in being featured creates sharing behavior)

Referral ICP Brief (what to tell customers who should they refer):
- Industry: [Your 3 primary verticals]
- Company size: [Revenue range or employee count of your ICP]
- Pain point: [2–3 sentence description of the problem your product solves — in customer language, not marketing language]
- "Please don't refer": [Anti-ICP description to prevent low-quality referrals that waste sales time]

DELIVERABLE 6 — NRR ATTRIBUTION FRAMEWORK & CUSTOMER MARKETING REPORTING

Section 6A: Marketing's Contribution to NRR — Attribution Model

NRR Components and Marketing's Role:

Expansion ARR (Marketing Attribution):
- First-touch attribution: which marketing campaign first surfaced the expansion opportunity
- Last-touch attribution: which marketing campaign sent within 30 days before expansion close
- Assisted attribution: any marketing touchpoint in the 90-day expansion journey
- Goal: Attribute 20–30% of total expansion ARR to customer marketing campaigns

Churn Prevention (Marketing Attribution):
- Marketing-assisted save: customer was churn-risk (health score <60) AND received marketing intervention AND renewed
- Attribution rule: if marketing campaign preceded CS renewal conversation by >7 days AND customer renewed → marketing gets "assist" credit
- Metric: Marketing-assisted saves expressed as ARR protected per quarter

New Logo from Advocacy (Full Marketing Attribution):
- Referral-sourced closed-won: 100% marketing attribution
- Reference-influenced closed-won: 50% marketing attribution (shared with sales)
- Case study-influenced closed-won: 25% marketing attribution (multi-touch model)

Section 6B: Customer Marketing Reporting Cadence

Weekly Pulse (15-minute standup metrics):
- New expansion triggers fired this week (by tier: auto / CS-assisted / sales-assisted)
- Health score distribution shift (accounts moved red→yellow, yellow→green, green→red)
- Advocacy activities this week (reviews written, case study approvals, reference calls taken)
- Churn risk accounts in intervention (count + ARR at risk)

Monthly Report (for CMO + CS leadership):
- Marketing-attributed expansion ARR closed
- Marketing-assisted churn saves (ARR protected)
- Advocacy pipeline generated (new logo pipeline from referrals + reference program)
- NRR impact: estimated NRR points contributed by customer marketing vs. baseline

Quarterly Board Report (for CMO to present):
- Customer marketing program ROI: [expansion ARR generated + churn ARR protected] ÷ customer marketing budget
- NRR contribution: marketing's share of NRR improvement
- Advocacy program ROI: [new logo ARR sourced by customers] ÷ advocacy program investment
- Top expanding customer segments: which ICP segments expand most reliably (informs acquisition targeting)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Customers unsubscribe from post-sale marketing emails ("We signed up for the product, not more emails")
**Solution:** The problem is almost always sender identity + content relevance. Separate customer marketing emails from marketing@ entirely — use CSM or CEO as sender name. Make the first 5 customer-marketing emails earn the right to send: ROI reports, peer benchmarks, usage insights — zero promotional content. Establish that these communications are personalized and valuable before a single expansion email is sent. Rate-limit: no more than 2 customer-marketing emails per month per customer unless triggered by a specific behavior.

**Problem:** CSMs resist customer marketing campaigns, saying "marketing is going to mess up my relationships"
**Solution:** Give CSMs full visibility and override control — show them every email before it sends to their accounts (or give 48-hour override window). Start the program with CSMs' greenest accounts (highest health score, longest tenure) so they see expansion pipeline created without relationship risk. Report CSM-level attribution: "Your accounts generated $X in expansion ARR from marketing campaigns last quarter — here's what the outreach looked like." Reframe entirely: "We handle the 1:many so you can protect your time for the 1:1 conversations that only you can have."

**Problem:** Expansion campaigns generate engagement but expansion deals stall with the economic buyer who wasn't part of the campaign
**Solution:** Build the economic buyer into the campaign architecture from T-90 onward. The annual ROI report should CC the economic buyer if in CRM. The Champion Enablement Kit for expansion should include a CFO-ready ROI one-pager, not just champion talking points. Add an explicit "executive alignment" step to every expansion campaign: after the champion engages, trigger a separate executive communication to the economic buyer — framed as a "strategic briefing," not a sales follow-up. The champion needs tools to sell internally, and marketing is responsible for building those tools.
