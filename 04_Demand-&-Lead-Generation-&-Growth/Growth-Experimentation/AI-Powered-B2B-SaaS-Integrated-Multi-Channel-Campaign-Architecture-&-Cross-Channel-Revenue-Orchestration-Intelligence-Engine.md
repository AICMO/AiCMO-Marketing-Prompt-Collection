## Overview
Deploys an AI agent to design, sequence, and orchestrate a fully integrated multi-channel marketing campaign from brief to measurement — coordinating paid media, email, content, events, ABM, and organic social around a unified campaign narrative, with channel-specific timing, budget weights, audience segmentation, and real-time optimization triggers. ## Success Metrics

**Campaign Architecture Quality:**
- Channel coverage score: ≥5 active channels with defined roles and interactions (not just parallel activity)
- Message consistency score: Same campaign theme is recognizable across all channels (PMM audit: 1–5 scale)
- Audience overlap management: <15% of target audience contacted via the same channel twice in the same week
- Asset completion rate: ≥90% of required assets produced before launch date

**Pipeline Performance:**
- MQL volume: Campaign MQLs vs. modeled MQLs (target: within ±15%)
- MQL-to-SQL rate: ≥20% (B2B SaaS benchmark); if below 15%, escalate to sales qualification review
- Cost-per-SQL: ≤2× channel benchmark; flag any channel at >3× for immediate optimization
- Pipeline contribution: Campaign-attributed pipeline as % of total quarter pipeline target (goal: campaign covers ≥25% of gap)

**Cross-Channel Efficiency:**
- Multi-touch attribution: % of SQLs that touched ≥3 campaign channels (indicator of campaign cohesion; benchmark: ≥60% of SQLs have ≥3 touchpoints)
- Channel interaction rate: % of email opens who were also exposed to paid campaign (indicator of cross-channel reach overlap — target: 40–60%)
- Content asset performance: Gated content CVR ≥15% for high-intent traffic sources (Google Search, ABM retargeting)

## Integration Tips

**Marketing Automation (HubSpot / Marketo / Pardot):**
- Create a single campaign object that all channels tag leads to; this is your source-of-truth for campaign pipeline attribution
- Build campaign-specific smart lists for each audience segment; use suppression lists to prevent cross-channel message fatigue
- Set up campaign lifecycle stage tracking: Campaign Aware → MQL → SQL → Opportunity — measure conversion rate at each stage weekly
- Automate SDR enrollment trigger: When contact completes 3+ campaign touchpoints (email open + content download + webinar attendance), auto-enroll in SDR sequence with "high-intent" flag

**Paid Media (LinkedIn Campaign Manager / Google Ads):**
- Import HubSpot/Salesforce contact lists to LinkedIn as matched audiences for retargeting and suppression
- Set up shared UTM taxonomy before launch: utm_campaign=[CampaignName]&utm_source=[Channel]&utm_medium=[Format]&utm_content=[Creative-Variant]
- Create LinkedIn Insight Tag + Google remarketing audiences using campaign landing page URL for cross-channel retargeting

**ABM Platforms (6sense / Demandbase / Rollworks):**
- Pull target account list into ABM platform 2 weeks before campaign launch to allow intent data baseline establishment
- Set buying stage triggers: When account moves from "Awareness" to "Consideration" stage in 6sense, auto-alert the SDR and add account to high-priority LinkedIn ABM audience
- Track account-level engagement: ≥3 contacts from same account engaging with campaign = "hot account" alert for AE and SDR

**Analytics & Reporting (Looker / Tableau / Salesforce):**
- Build campaign performance dashboard with daily refresh: impression volume, CTR, MQL volume, SQL volume, pipeline created
- Set up anomaly detection alerts: If daily MQL volume drops >30% from 7-day rolling average, Slack alert to demand gen manager
- Create campaign waterfall report: Impressions → Clicks → MQLs → SQLs → Opportunities → Closed-Won by channel and by week

**Project Management (Asana / Notion / Monday.com):**

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Channels aren't reinforcing each other — the campaign feels like five separate campaigns running simultaneously**
Fix: The root cause is almost always a missing "campaign handshake" — the mechanism by which audience exposure on one channel triggers activation on another. Implement a 3-step fix: (1) Ensure all channels share a single audience source (CDP or MAP contact list) so you can track cross-channel exposure. (2) Add LinkedIn Matched Audiences from your email list so email subscribers see the same campaign creative on LinkedIn. (3) Build a progression logic rule: contact must touch Channel A before Channel B is activated — enforce this in your MAP with smart list exclusions.

**Problem: MQLs are generating but not converting to SQLs — campaign pipeline is weak**
Fix: This is a qualification problem, not a creative or reach problem. Run a rapid 3-step diagnosis: (1) Check MQL-to-SQL conversion rate by channel — if one channel produces MQLs at 5× cost-per-MQL but converts at 3× the rate, it's worth it; reallocate toward quality over volume. (2) Listen to 5–10 discovery calls from campaign MQLs with Gong/Chorus — identify the most common objection that's killing deals. (3) Check SDR follow-up time: B2B leads that receive first contact within 5 minutes convert at 21× the rate of leads contacted after 30 minutes. Slow follow-up is often the hidden killer of campaign pipeline.

**Problem: Budget runs out in week 4 of a 10-week campaign — pacing is off**
Fix: Set daily budget caps at (Total Budget / Campaign Days) × 1.2 to allow 20% acceleration on high-performing days. In LinkedIn Campaign Manager, use "Daily budget with scheduling" rather than lifetime budget — lifetime budgets front-load spend in the algorithm's learning phase. Reserve 20% of total campaign budget for weeks 7–10 (conversion and close-support phase) as a hold-back that isn't allocated to any channel at launch — activate only when mid-campaign data shows which channel has the best SQL conversion rate.
