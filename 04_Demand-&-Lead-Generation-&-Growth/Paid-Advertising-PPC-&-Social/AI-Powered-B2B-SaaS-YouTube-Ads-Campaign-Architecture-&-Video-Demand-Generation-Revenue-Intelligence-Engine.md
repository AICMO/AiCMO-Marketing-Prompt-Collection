## Overview
Designs a complete, pipeline-attributable YouTube Ads program architecture for B2B SaaS — including full-funnel video campaign structure, audience targeting strategy, ad format selection by funnel stage, video creative frameworks with second-by-second scripting logic, bidding configuration, budget allocation, and Google Ads/CRM attribution setup — producing a ready-to-execute playbook an AI agent can build directly via the Google Ads API. Use when launching a new YouTube Ads program, scaling a proven paid search motion into video demand creation, or building a full-funnel paid media system that pairs video brand-building with search-based demand capture.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior B2B SaaS YouTube Ads strategist. Design a complete YouTube Ads campaign architecture for [Your SaaS Product] — a [brief product description] targeting [ICP: job title/department] at [company size/type] companies.

Build a full campaign structure with these components:

1. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE (full funnel):
   - Awareness: Skippable TrueView In-Stream ads to cold ICP audiences (15–60 sec)
   - Consideration: Non-skippable bumper ads + TrueView for Action to warm audiences (6–15 sec)
   - Conversion: TrueView for Action with demo/trial CTA to high-intent retargeting segments

2. AUDIENCE TARGETING STRATEGY:
   - Custom Intent Audiences: keywords buyers use when actively researching your category
   - Customer Match: upload CRM contact list (prospects, churned customers, open opportunities)
   - In-Market Audiences: Google's buyer intent signals for your software category
   - Similar Audiences: lookalikes from your customer list uploads
   - YouTube Remarketing: viewers who watched 50%+ of previous ads but didn't convert

3. AD FORMAT PLAN (one per funnel stage):
   - Top-of-funnel: Skippable TrueView In-Stream (30–60 sec) — problem framing, category education
   - Mid-funnel: 6-sec Bumper Ads — brand recall reinforcement in high-frequency rotation
   - Bottom-of-funnel: TrueView for Action (15–30 sec) — product demo snippet + strong CTA overlay
   - Retargeting: Non-skippable In-Stream (15 sec) — direct response for warm audiences

4. VIDEO CREATIVE FRAMEWORK (per format):
   - Hook (0–5 seconds): Must work BEFORE the skip button — open with the problem or a bold stat
   - Problem amplification (5–15 sec): Make the pain vivid and specific
   - Solution introduction (15–30 sec): Show the product in action, not describe it
   - Proof (30–45 sec): Customer name + specific outcome
   - CTA (final 5 sec): Single, clear action with companion banner

5. BIDDING & BUDGET:
   - Awareness: CPV (cost-per-view) bidding — target $0.05–0.12 CPV
   - Conversion: tCPA bidding for demo requests — set initial tCPA at 1.5x target CPL
   - Budget split across funnel stages with monthly pipeline math

6. MEASUREMENT & ATTRIBUTION:
   - Google Ads conversion tracking events to implement
   - View-through attribution window settings
   - CRM UTM structure and YouTube attribution in HubSpot/Salesforce

Output a complete implementation brief an engineer or Google Ads AI agent can execute directly, including audience configurations, ad scripts, creative briefs, and tracking setup.

C) PRODUCT DEMONSTRATION — WARM AUDIENCES (Mid-to-Bottom Funnel):
- Campaign type: Video action campaign
- Ad format: Skippable TrueView for Action (15–30 seconds) with CTA overlay and companion banner

- Primary KPI: View-through conversion rate, cost-per-demo at or below target tCPA

- Campaign type: Video action campaign
- Ad format: TrueView for Action (20–30 seconds)
- Audience targeting: Custom Intent audience built from competitor keywords — "[Competitor A] pricing", "[Competitor B] reviews", "[Competitor] alternative", "[Competitor] vs" — these searchers are in active evaluation

- CTA: Single action only — "Book Your Demo" — no choices, no soft CTAs
- Budget: 15–20% of total (retargeting audience is small — CPMs will be higher, but conversion rates 3–5x cold)

F) CUSTOMER EXPANSION & UPSELL (Existing Customer Remarketing):
- Campaign type: Video action campaign
- Ad format: Skippable TrueView for Action (15–20 seconds)
- Audience targeting: Customer Match — upload current customer list; suppress from all acquisition campaigns
- Content angle: Feature education and upsell — introduce new capability, advanced use case, or higher-tier value
- CTA: "See What's New" → product update page or upgrade landing page
- Budget: 5% of total (if expansion revenue is a priority, scale this separately)

YouTube ads must earn attention before the skip button appears at 5 seconds. Every creative decision in the first 5 seconds determines whether your ad gets watched or skipped. Design each video asset around this framework:

Hook for [Your Product]:
[Write the specific 0–5 second hook — 15–25 words maximum. The viewer's finger is on the skip button. Every word must fight for the next word.]

SECOND 5–15 (PROBLEM AMPLIFICATION):
Expand the pain. Make it vivid. Name specific job titles, situations, or dollar amounts that resonate with ICP.
Do NOT introduce your product yet — the viewer hasn't earned the solution.
[Write 2–3 sentences that deepen the problem with specificity.]

SECOND 15–30 (SOLUTION INTRODUCTION — SHOW, DON'T TELL):
Introduce [Product] by showing it solving the exact problem named in seconds 5–15.
Rule: Use real product UI — screen recording — not illustrated animations or stock footage.
Voiceover: "[Product] [does the specific thing] — and here's what that looks like in practice."
Show 1–2 specific product features that deliver the core value prop, in real time.
[Describe what to show in the product UI. What screen, what action, what result appears.]

SECOND 55–60 (BRAND CLOSE):
Logo + tagline only. Do not add new information.

NOTE ON tCPA BIDDING: YouTube's tCPA requires a minimum of 30 conversions in the past 30 days to function correctly. In the first 4–6 weeks, use Manual CPV or Max Conversions to gather conversion data before switching to tCPA. Set initial tCPA at 1.5–2x your target CPL — Google will optimize down as the model learns. Never set tCPA below actual average CPA in the first 30 days.

VIEW-THROUGH ATTRIBUTION NOTE: YouTube generates significant view-through conversions (users who see the ad but don't click, then convert later). - Map UTM source=youtube → HubSpot Lead Source = "YouTube Ads"

DELIVERABLE 7 — 90-DAY LAUNCH ROADMAP:

WEEKS 1–2 (FOUNDATION):
- Install Google Ads global site tag + GTM conversion triggers on all key pages
- Build website remarketing audiences (need 1,000+ visitors for YouTube targeting — if insufficient, launch awareness campaign to build lists in parallel)
- Upload Customer Match lists (CRM contact export — requires Google Ads account in good standing)
- Create Custom Intent audiences from keyword lists above
- Set up Google Analytics 4 ↔ Google Ads linking for view-through attribution
- Launch Cold ICP Awareness campaign with 2 creative variations for A/B test
- Brief video production team on first creative batch (Awareness TrueView + 5 bumpers)

WEEKS 3–4 (CONVERSION LAUNCH):
- Launch Competitor Displacement campaign (high-intent keyword targeting — no video required initially; test with screen-recording format)
- Launch Website Retargeting campaign (non-skippable 15s) targeting existing website visitors
- Set up CRM UTM attribution mapping for YouTube lead source
- Enable Google Brand Lift study if spend projects to $10K+ in month 1

MONTH 2 (OPTIMIZATION):
- Review Awareness campaign view rates — if <25%, test new hook in first 5 seconds (hook is always the optimization lever)
- Review tCPA performance — if Cost-per-demo >2x target, check audience overlap between campaigns (exclude converted users from all acquisition campaigns)
- Launch Product Demo campaign with warm audiences built from Awareness engagement (50%+ viewers)
- Rotate bumper ad creative — test 2 new bumper variations based on best-performing proof points
- Upload refreshed Customer Match list with new pipeline contacts and trial users

MONTH 3 (SCALE):
- Enable YouTube Select or YouTube TV placement if ACV >$50K (higher CPMs justified for brand-safe, premium placement)
- Expand Custom Intent keyword lists based on Google Search Terms report from parallel Google Search campaigns
- Launch Google Performance Max campaign with YouTube inventory (test with 10% of budget — PMaX is opaque but often surfaces YouTube placements with strong conversion signal)
- Activate Brand Lift survey results — measure aided/unaided awareness lift among ICP audiences
- Evaluate Thought Leader ad format (YouTube Channel Memberships and Creator partnerships) if awareness goals justify influencer investment

CREATIVE REFRESH SCHEDULE:
- Awareness TrueView (30–60 sec): Refresh every 6–8 weeks or when view rate drops 15%+ from baseline
- Bumper ads: Rotate weekly — 6-second formats burn through audience attention quickly in small B2B segments
- Retargeting (non-skippable 15 sec): Refresh every 4 weeks — retargeted audiences saturate rapidly
- Competitor displacement: Refresh when competitor runs significant product launches or price changes

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Awareness campaign view rate below 20% — ads are being skipped immediately**
Solution: The hook (first 5 seconds) is failing. This is always the problem when view rate is low — YouTube viewers skip anything that doesn't immediately create tension or curiosity. Audit your 0–5 second opening: if it starts with a logo animation, company name, generic statement ("We help companies..."), or talking head introduction, it will be skipped. Rewrite the hook to open with either (1) the buyer's specific pain in their own language, (2) a provocative statistic that challenges an assumption they hold, or (3) a pattern interrupt — an unexpected visual or statement that breaks their passive scroll. Test 3 different hooks with identical body copy and let them run for 1,000 impressions each before selecting the winner. Hook optimization alone typically lifts view rate from 15% to 35%+.

**Problem: tCPA bidding is overspending or not delivering despite sufficient budget**
Solution: tCPA requires a minimum of 30 conversions in a 30-day window to function properly — below this threshold, Google's algorithm cannot learn efficiently and either overspends chasing conversions or stops delivering. Check your conversion count: if under 30, switch to Max Conversions bidding (no CPA cap) for 4–6 weeks to accumulate conversion data. If your account has enough conversions but tCPA is still unstable, your target CPA may be set too low — Google cannot find conversions below a floor price determined by auction dynamics. Raise tCPA by 20–30% above your target to give the system room to bid, then gradually lower as volume stabilizes. Also verify conversion tracking is firing correctly — a broken conversion tag is the #1 cause of tCPA campaigns failing silently.

**Problem: Customer Match audiences showing "insufficient size" — campaign not serving**
Solution: YouTube Customer Match requires a minimum matched audience size of 1,000 active YouTube users. If your uploaded list is below this threshold after matching, Google won't serve the campaign. Matching rate is typically 40–60% (not all CRM emails match to Google accounts). Expand your source list: combine multiple CRM segments (prospects + open pipeline + past trial users), ensure list emails are personal/work emails linked to Google accounts (generic info@ addresses don't match), and verify the CSV format uses SHA-256 hashed emails or plain-text emails uploaded via the Google Ads UI. If list size is genuinely too small, layer Customer Match with Custom Intent or In-Market audiences to reach similar buyers at scale while your match list grows.
