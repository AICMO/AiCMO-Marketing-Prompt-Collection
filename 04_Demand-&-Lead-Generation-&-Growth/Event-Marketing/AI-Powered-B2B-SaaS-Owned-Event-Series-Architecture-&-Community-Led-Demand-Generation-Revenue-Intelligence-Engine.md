## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Event attendance is strong but pipeline conversion is weak (<5% to qualified opp in 90 days)**
*Diagnosis:* ICP is wrong (wrong seniority, wrong company size), or the event experience feels too vendor-y (attendees enjoy the event but don't associate it with a buying context.
*Fix:* Audit attendee list composition — if >60% are existing customers or non-ICP contacts, tighten the invitation qualification. Add a "Business Challenge" prompt to the RSVP flow: "What's the #1 revenue operations challenge you're trying to solve in the next 6 months?" Use responses to qualify and personalize follow-up. Ensure AE team is doing warm introductions, not cold follow-up emails.

**Problem: Sponsor revenue isn't materializing — prospects decline or offer lowball amounts**
*Diagnosis:* Sponsorship is positioned as advertising (logo on banner, booth) rather than community underwriting. B2B executives resist paying to be perceived as sponsors of a vendor event.
*Fix:* Reframe the offering entirely. Call it "Community Partner" not "Sponsor." The benefit: "Exclusive access to facilitate one 20-minute working session with your top 5 target accounts in a non-commercial setting." Charge for the access, not the logo. Package: $25,000 for 1 city dinner co-hosting rights + category exclusivity in 3 monthly roundtables.

**Problem: Community engagement drops sharply after the first event — members go quiet**
*Diagnosis:* Community programming between events is either non-existent or feels like marketing content distribution.
*Fix:* The community must feel peer-to-peer, not brand-to-audience. Shift from "We're sharing an article" to "Here's a question 3 members asked us this week — who has experience with this?" Hire or designate a Community Manager (even part-time) whose job is to seed conversations, not broadcast content. Run a monthly "Anonymous benchmarking" survey (5 questions, results shared with community only) — this creates genuine value and recurring reason to participate.
