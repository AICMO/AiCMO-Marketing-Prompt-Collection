## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI-generated content sounds generic and doesn't capture the speaker's voice.**
Solution: Feed the prompt a 500-word verbatim transcript excerpt — not a summary. The more raw the input, the more authentic the output. Ask the AI to "preserve the exact vocabulary and sentence rhythm this speaker used." Before publishing, have the speaker spend 10 minutes reading and adding one personal anecdote the AI couldn't have known.

**Problem: Sales team isn't using the event enablement snippets in follow-up emails.**
Solution: Don't deliver a document — deliver a Slack message with a copy-paste snippet and a specific instruction: "Paste this as a P.S. in your follow-up to anyone who attended [Event Name] this week. One click. Done." Adoption increases dramatically when the friction is removed and the message arrives in the tool reps already use.

**Problem: The blog post isn't ranking for the target keyword despite being published.**
Solution: The event topic keyword (e.g., "revenue operations best practices") is likely too competitive. Use the Q&A questions from the event as keyword targets instead — audience questions map directly to long-tail search intent. The question "How do you get sales and marketing to agree on MQL definitions?" becomes the H1: "How to Align Sales and Marketing on MQL Definitions (Without a 6-Month Project)." These convert at higher rates and rank faster.
