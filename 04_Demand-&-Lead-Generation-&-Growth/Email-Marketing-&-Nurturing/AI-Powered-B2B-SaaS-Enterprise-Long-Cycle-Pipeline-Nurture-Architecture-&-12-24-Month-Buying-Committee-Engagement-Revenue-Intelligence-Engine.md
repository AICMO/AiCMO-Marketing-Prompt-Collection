## Overview
Designs a complete 12-24 month nurture architecture for enterprise B2B deals where the buying cycle spans multiple fiscal quarters, budget cycles, and buying committee changes. Use it when your ACV exceeds $150K, your average sales cycle is 9+ months, or when marketing-originated pipeline is sitting cold in CRM with no clear re-engagement plan. This system prevents long-cycle deals from dying silently while maintaining relevance through market shifts, budget freezes, champion turnover, and fiscal year resets.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Accounts have been in CRM for 18 months with no contact information updates — champions have left and we're sending into the void.**
Fix: Before building the nurture program, run a database audit using LinkedIn + ZoomInfo/Apollo/Clay to validate all buying committee contacts are still in their roles. For any contacts where email bounces, use Clay to waterfall through alternative contact information. Prioritize re-validating the top 20 accounts by ACV first. A 2-week data hygiene sprint before launch will dramatically improve engagement rates and prevent burning your sending domain on dead addresses.

**Problem: The economic buyer won't engage with any marketing content — they only respond to their direct reports who champion projects internally.**
Fix: This is normal for senior enterprise buyers. Shift your economic buyer track from direct outreach to "air cover" strategy: instead of emailing the CFO directly, equip the champion with content specifically designed to be forwarded to or shared with the CFO. Subject your champion emails with "FYI: I thought this might be useful for your CFO conversation" frames. When the CFO does see your content, it arrives through a trusted internal source rather than a vendor — which dramatically increases engagement rate. Reserve direct economic buyer contact for high-value, highly personalized executive briefings — not nurture content.

**Problem: The nurture program is running well but deals are still not advancing — we're maintaining relationships but not creating movement.**
Fix: Relationship maintenance without deal advancement usually means you're missing a specific buying trigger or your champion lacks internal authority. Audit your stalled accounts for two things: (1) Is there an internal budget or prioritization decision your champion needs to win before this can advance? If yes, equip them with the exact internal selling language, business case templates, and executive FAQ docs they need to make that case. (2) Does the economic buyer know this is on the table? In long-cycle deals, champions sometimes protect their relationship with you by not escalating until they're sure the timing is right — check whether the economic buyer has had any direct contact with your company, and if not, create a reason for an executive-level introduction.
