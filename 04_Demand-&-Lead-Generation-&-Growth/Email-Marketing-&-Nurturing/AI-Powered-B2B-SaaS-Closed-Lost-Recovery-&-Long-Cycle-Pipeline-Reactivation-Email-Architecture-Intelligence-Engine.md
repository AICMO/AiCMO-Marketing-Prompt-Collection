## Overview

Builds a fully automated email nurture architecture that takes closed-lost CRM opportunities — segmented by loss reason, deal stage, persona, and competitive context — and re-engages them with precision-targeted sequences timed to when their situation is most likely to have changed. Use this when your CRM holds thousands of dead opportunities that represent qualified intent you never converted, when pipeline generation costs are rising and you need to extract more value from existing prospect relationships, or when your sales team is throwing away months of marketing investment by never feeding lost prospects back into nurture.

**Sequence-level optimization metrics:**
- **Open Rate by Loss Reason Segment**: Aim for >32% on calendar-triggered emails (expect higher than cold email because prospect recognizes sender)
- **Click-to-Open Rate**: >22% indicates content relevance; below 12% means asset quality or CTA mismatch
- **Unsubscribe Rate**: Keep below 0.4% per send. Spikes signal wrong timing or irrelevant messaging for that segment
- **Sales Alert Action Rate**: % of re-engagement alerts where sales follows up within SLA. If below 60%, the handback briefing or SLA needs work

**Confidence testing:**
- Run a 20% holdout group (enrolled in suppression, not nurture) for first 6 months to measure incremental lift vs. natural re-engagement rate
- Attribute revenue only after confirming prospect had no other active marketing or sales touchpoints during the re-engagement window

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Loss reason data in CRM is inconsistent or mostly blank, making segmentation impossible.**
Solution: Before building sequences, run an AI-assisted data cleanup pass. Export all closed-lost deal notes and opportunity names from the past 18 months. Use a GPT prompt to classify each into your 5-6 segments based on the text in the notes field, then import classifications back to CRM. Expect 70-80% accuracy; spot-check a 10% sample manually before using for enrollment. For ongoing data quality, add a required Salesforce field validation that prevents reps from saving Closed Lost without selecting a loss reason.

**Problem: Sales team ignores re-engagement alerts, and prospects expire without follow-up.**
Solution: The briefing quality is usually the problem — reps don't act on a generic notification. Redesign the alert to include (1) the specific email the prospect clicked and what the CTA was, (2) a one-line re-engagement opening line the rep can copy-paste, and (3) a direct comparison to the prospect's original deal value. If SLA compliance is still below 60%, route alerts to sales manager CC and add a 3-day escalation to manager Slack message.

**Problem: Prospects are getting double-touched — reactivation nurture emails AND cold SDR outreach happening simultaneously, creating a confusing and annoying experience.**
Solution: Implement a suppression list in both your MAP and your sales engagement tool (Outreach/Salesloft). Any contact enrolled in closed-lost recovery nurture must be added to a "Hands Off — In Marketing Nurture" list that blocks SDR sequence enrollment. Run a weekly audit for the first 90 days to catch leakage. The trigger for SDR involvement should come exclusively from the re-engagement alert, not from SDR prospecting.
