## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Videos are getting impressions but low CTR (under 3%) — views aren't growing**
*Solution:* The bottleneck is thumbnail or title. Run a 2-week experiment: update the thumbnail on your 3 highest-impression but lowest-CTR videos. Use Canva A/B testing or simply swap to a higher-contrast thumbnail with a face and a bold 3-word text overlay. For titles, test reformatting from descriptive ("Understanding FP&A Software Features") to outcome-first ("Cut Budget Cycles from 8 Weeks to 10 Days"). CTR response is visible in YouTube Studio within 48-72 hours of a change.

**Problem: Good views and watch time but zero pipeline — no form fills from YouTube**
*Solution:* The conversion path is broken. Audit three things: (1) Is your lead magnet in the description actually linked and working? Test the link in an incognito browser. (2) Is the CTA delivered verbally in the video at the 70-80% mark? A description-only CTA captures only 15% of viewers who read descriptions. Add a spoken CTA: "If you want the exact checklist I mentioned, link is in the description." (3) Is the lead magnet relevant to the video's topic? A generic "Book a Demo" CTA on an educational TOFU video converts at 0.1%. A topic-specific template or calculator converts at 2-5%. Replace generic CTAs with video-specific lead magnets.

**Problem: Channel growth stalled after initial burst — subscriber growth plateaued at 200-300**
*Solution:* This is an algorithmic reach problem, not a content quality problem. YouTube's algorithm limits distribution of channels under 1,000 subscribers to search results only — recommendation engine exposure starts around 1,000 subscribers. Run a 30-day subscriber sprint: (1) Publish 2-3 YouTube Shorts per week (repurposed from long-form content) — Shorts are the fastest path to recommendation engine exposure. (2) Host a YouTube Live — live viewers convert to subscribers at 5-10x the rate of VOD viewers. (3) Actively promote your YouTube channel in the email newsletter, LinkedIn posts, and email signature for 30 days straight. Most B2B YouTube channels that stall simply haven't told their existing audience the channel exists.
