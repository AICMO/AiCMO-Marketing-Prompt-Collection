## Integration Tips
- **HubSpot/Salesforce:** Use UTM parameters on all links shared by advocates; create a custom field "Social Advocacy Touch" on Contacts and Opportunities; set up a workflow that tags contacts who engage with employee posts (via social listening tools) as "Social Engaged"
- **Slack:** Deploy a daily Slackbot that DMs each advocate their personalized content suggestion for the day with a one-click copy button — use Zapier + OpenAI API to generate persona-specific variations
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Cross-reference post commenters against your target account list; route matches directly to the owning AE with context in Slack
- **Everyonesocial / Bambu:** Set up content libraries organized by advocate tier; enable one-click sharing with pre-populated captions; use built-in analytics to feed your advocacy scoring dashboard
- **Gong / Chorus:** Analyze sales call transcripts for mentions of "saw your post" or "connected on LinkedIn" — these are attribution signals that advocacy influenced the deal; create a Gong tracker for this phrase
- **Google Sheets / Notion:** Build a shared Advocacy Leaderboard that updates weekly via Zapier pulling from LinkedIn Analytics API; display in a company-wide Slack channel for visibility

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Employees post once, then go quiet after 2 weeks.
**Solution:** The content effort is too high. Implement a "5-minute Friday" ritual — every Friday at 3pm Slack sends each advocate a ready-to-post caption + graphic that requires zero editing. Make the path of least resistance = posting. Pair with a public monthly leaderboard reset to keep motivation fresh.

**Problem:** Posts get good likes but no ICP-quality comments or DMs.
**Solution:** The content is too passive/educational — it needs more tension and a clear "reply bait" mechanism. Add a question at the end of every post that invites ICP-specific responses: "VPs of Sales — what's your current forecast accuracy? Be honest 👇" This filters self-selection and surfaces high-fit prospects in the comments.

**Problem:** Dark social DMs feel awkward and advocates don't send them.
**Solution:** Reduce the social pressure. Build a Slack workflow: when an ICP commenter is identified, the Slack bot drafts the DM and sends it to the advocate with a single "Send this?" button. The advocate reviews and sends in one tap. Reduce friction to near-zero. Also provide a 2-hour training session on social selling psychology — show real examples of DMs that led to pipeline.
