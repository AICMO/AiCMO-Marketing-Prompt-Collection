## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn posts get low reach despite posting consistently**
Solution: The first 60 minutes after posting are algorithm-defining. Ensure 3-5 employees (not bots — real colleagues with authentic connections) comment substantively (not just "great post!") within the first hour. If engagement velocity is low early, LinkedIn suppresses organic reach. Also audit format: LinkedIn currently (2026) rewards document posts and native video over link-in-post formats. Move links to first comment.

**Problem: Can't prove LinkedIn's revenue contribution — finance team dismisses it as "brand spend"**
Solution: Implement the three-layer attribution stack immediately: (1) UTM tagging on every LinkedIn link, (2) self-reported attribution question on all demo/trial forms, (3) LinkedIn Insight Tag retargeting pool size as a proxy for engaged audience. Present the "dark social proxy" metric: month-over-month growth in self-reported "LinkedIn" attribution on forms correlates with LinkedIn investment. Run a 30-day test where you pause LinkedIn and show the drop in self-reported attribution — this is the most compelling proof point for finance skeptics.

**Problem: Executives won't post consistently — always "too busy"**
Solution: Reduce the time ask to 20 minutes per week. Use a "voice capture" workflow: 15-minute Loom recording where the executive answers 3 questions about their week → AI transcribes and drafts 3 LinkedIn posts in their voice → executive reviews and approves in 5 minutes via a shared Notion doc. The barrier is creation, not approval. Once the workflow proves it works (watch for inbound LinkedIn DMs from prospects they'd never have reached through cold outreach), executive buy-in compounds.
