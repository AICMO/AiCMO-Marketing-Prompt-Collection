## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts get impressions but no comments or clicks**
*Root cause:* Content is informational, not opinionated. Followers consume it passively.
*Fix:* Add a forcing function at the end of every post — a direct question, a polarizing take to react to, or an explicit call-to-comment ("Drop your answer below"). For carousel posts, make the last slide ask a specific question. Test posts that make a slightly controversial claim your ICP will either agree or disagree with strongly.

**Problem: High follower count but wrong audience (low ICP match)**
*Root cause:* Content is too broad (appealing to other marketers or job seekers, not buyers) OR page was grown with tactics that attract non-ICP followers (generic "we're hiring" posts, viral content unrelated to your category).
*Fix:* Run a follower demographics audit in LinkedIn Analytics → Follower tab. If ICP match is <30%, shift content to be hyper-specific to your buyers' daily work. Stop posting content about company culture, awards, or general marketing tips — post content so specific to your ICP's job that only they would find it valuable.

**Problem: LinkedIn organic generates engagement but zero tracked pipeline**
*Root cause:* UTM parameters are missing from post links, OR links lead to pages with no conversion path, OR the attribution window is too short (LinkedIn organic has a 30–90 day influence cycle, not same-day).
*Fix:* Audit every CTA link in posts from the past 30 days — confirm UTM parameters are present and resolving correctly in your CRM. Add a LinkedIn follow-up sequence for all demo requests where "LinkedIn" appears in UTM source — often buyers visit multiple times before converting. Extend attribution window to 90 days in your CRM to capture the full influence cycle.
