## Troubleshooting

**Problem: TikTok videos get strong views but zero pipeline — no bio link clicks, no form fills**
Solution: The gap is between content consumption and conversion intent. TikTok viewers are in discovery mode, not purchase mode — most will not self-initiate a demo request after watching an educational video. Fix this in three layers: (1) Add a friction-free lead magnet to the bio (a benchmark report, free template, or calculator that delivers immediate value — not a "request a demo" CTA) to capture email addresses from warm but not-ready-to-buy viewers; (2) Include explicit verbal CTAs in the last 5 seconds of every video ("link in bio to download the full framework"), not just overlay text; (3) Build a TikTok-specific nurture sequence that sends 4-6 educational emails over 3 weeks before any sales-touch content — TikTok-sourced leads need more nurturing runway than paid search leads because their intent is lower at the point of capture.

**Problem: Can't prove TikTok's revenue contribution — leadership dismisses it as "top-of-funnel brand spend"**
Solution: The evidence that converts skeptics is sales call testimony, not analytics reports. Implement a mandatory "social media mention" field in your sales discovery call template: reps must log any mention of TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, or specific content pieces by prospects during calls. After 60 days, aggregate this data — when leadership sees that 15-20% of active opportunities mention TikTok content unprompted, the attribution argument becomes qualitative proof that's harder to dismiss than UTM attribution alone. Supplement with the self-reported attribution trend: show month-over-month growth in "TikTok" responses on demo forms correlated with view growth. The correlation is the story; it doesn't require perfect attribution modeling.

**Problem: Videos get low completion rates (under 20%) — TikTok stops distributing to FYP**
Solution: Low completion rate is the single biggest algorithm kill signal on TikTok. The cause is almost always a weak hook or a slow-paced middle section. Audit your top 10 lowest-completion videos and identify the timestamp where drop-off spikes (visible in TikTok Studio video analytics). If drop-off happens in seconds 0-3, refilm with a stronger hook that creates an immediate knowledge gap ("You're doing X wrong — here's what actually works"). If drop-off happens in seconds 10-20, the video has no narrative momentum — restructure with faster cuts, on-screen text changes every 3-4 seconds, and a "payoff promise" stated at the 5-second mark that the viewer knows is coming. Target completion rate above 35% on 30-60 second videos before increasing posting frequency; algorithm trust must be earned video by video.
