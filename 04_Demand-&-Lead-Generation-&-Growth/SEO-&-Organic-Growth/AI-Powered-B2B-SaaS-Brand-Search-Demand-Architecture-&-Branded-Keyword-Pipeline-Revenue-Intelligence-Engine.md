## Overview

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Branded search volume is flat despite significant brand awareness investment**
Solution: Branded search is a lagging indicator — it lags awareness spend by 4–12 weeks. First verify you're measuring correctly (check Google Search Console for all brand name variations including misspellings). Then look at geographic correlation: if you ran a LinkedIn Ads campaign targeting Chicago, did branded search impressions increase in Illinois 6 weeks later? If yes, the flywheel works but you need more volume or longer duration to see aggregate impact. If no correlation exists after 12 weeks, your awareness channels aren't reaching buyers who care enough to search — consider ICP targeting accuracy on LinkedIn Ads as the culprit.

**Problem: Branded search CTR is below 55% (industry benchmark is 65-75%)**
Solution: This is the clearest signal that competitors are bidding on your brand and capturing clicks. Pull Google Auction Insights report in Google Ads to confirm which competitors appear alongside your branded terms. Activate or increase branded defense bidding — even a $500/month branded defense campaign typically generates 5–15x ROI by recapturing pipeline you're currently losing. Also check if a negative news story or Glassdoor content is appearing for your brand name (reputation issue) and if a common noun confusion exists (if your brand name is "Prism" or "Catalyst" — generic words appear in unrelated searches, suppressing your CTR average).

**Problem: Branded organic conversion rate is lower than generic organic rate**
Solution: This is unusual and signals a fundamental page experience problem for branded traffic. Your homepage likely loads slowly, has ambiguous positioning, or doesn't acknowledge that the visitor already knows who you are. Branded searchers don't need to be "introduced" to your product — they need proof, pricing, and comparison content immediately. A/B test a version of your homepage that assumes brand awareness: no explainer video at the top, replace with customer logo carousel and a demo CTA above the fold, and add a "Why companies switch to [Brand]" section in the hero. The other common cause: most of your branded searches are from existing customers (login, support, billing) — create separate /login and /support pages and submit them to Google Search Console to suppress these from your "new visitor" branded conversion calculations.
