## Overview

Designs and executes an end-to-end AI-powered digital PR and link building program that systematically builds domain authority for B2B SaaS companies, translating backlink acquisition into measurable organic pipeline. Use when your B2B SaaS site has strong on-page SEO but lacks the domain authority to rank for competitive keywords, when organic traffic growth has plateaued despite publishing quality content, when you need to build topical authority in a crowded category, or when you want to generate earned media coverage that simultaneously builds SEO authority and brand awareness. Distinct from PR for coverage: every tactic here is engineered to produce followed backlinks from high-DR publishers that move organic pipeline metrics.

**SECTION 5: LINK OUTREACH OPERATIONS**

5.1 TECH STACK CONFIGURATION
- Ahrefs (link prospecting, competitor backlink analysis, referring domain tracking): weekly exports of new competitor referring domains, automated alerts when you gain or lose referring domains above DR 40
- Pitchbox or Buzzstream (outreach automation): configure sequences for resource page requests (3-touch), broken link building (2-touch), journalist pitches (2-touch), and guest post submissions (2-touch)
- Hunter.io or RocketReach (contact finding): find editor and webmaster email addresses for target publications
- Clay.com (AI-powered personalization at scale): pull recent articles from journalist targets, use AI to generate a personalized first line referencing their most recent relevant article, merge into Pitchbox sequences
- Google Search Console (track organic ranking improvements correlated to link acquisition): weekly export of keyword position data, correlation analysis with new referring domain acquisition

5.2 OUTREACH SEQUENCES BY TYPE
- Resource page link building: Email 1 (personalized, reference the page, explain why your resource fits), Email 2 after 7 days (brief follow-up with one additional compelling reason), no Email 3 (two touches maximum for resource pages)
- Broken link building: Email 1 (genuinely helpful — tell them they have a broken link, offer your content as a replacement — no hard sell), single touch only
- Journalist pitch (new story idea): Email 1 (pitch), Email 2 after 5 days (brief follow-up with one additional data point), no further follow-up if no response
- Guest post pitch: Email 1 (pitch with 3 topic options + your bio + 2 published sample articles), Email 2 after 7 days (follow-up with one more topic option), Email 3 after 14 days (final follow-up, offer to jump on a 10-minute call)

5.3 EMAIL DELIVERABILITY RULES
- Send from a separate subdomain (e.g., pr@veridian.com vs. hello@veridian.com) to protect main domain reputation
- Warm up new email domain for 4 weeks before large-scale outreach using Lemwarm or Mailreach
- Maximum 30 outreach emails per day per inbox, rotating across 3 inboxes for scale
- Personalization minimum: the first line of every outreach email must reference something specific to the recipient (their recent article, their publication's coverage focus, a specific page on their site) — no batch-and-blast

**SECTION 6: LINK QUALITY GOVERNANCE**

6.1 LINK ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA (Minimum Standards for Any Link You Pursue)
- Domain Rating ≥ 35 (using Ahrefs DR)
- Organic traffic to the linking page ≥ 500/month (using Ahrefs)
- Topical relevance score: the linking page must be about business software, legal technology, enterprise operations, or an adjacent topic where your ICP audience would realistically visit
- Do-follow link (no-follow links accepted only from DR 80+ publications where the editorial value outweighs the SEO value)
- Not penalized: zero overlap with Google manual action history (check Ahrefs Site Audit for toxic link markers)
- No paid links: zero budget for paid link placements, sponsored posts labeled as advertising, or link schemes — every link must be earned through content quality or relationship value

6.2 LINK DISAVOWAL PROCESS
- Monthly audit of new referring domains using Ahrefs; flag any DR < 20 links from irrelevant or spammy sites
- Quarterly disavowal file submission to Google Search Console for confirmed toxic links

## Integration Tips

**Ahrefs Integration:**
- Configure weekly automated exports of competitor referring domains to Google Sheets via Ahrefs API or Zapier connection
- Set up Ahrefs Alerts for: new links to competitor sites, lost links from your site (potential disavow candidates), and new keyword ranking changes for priority terms
- Use Ahrefs Content Explorer to identify journalists who have written about your topic area in the last 90 days (filter by DR ≥ 40, last published ≥ 30 days)

**Pitchbox / Buzzstream Integration:**
- Import prospecting lists from Ahrefs directly into Pitchbox outreach sequences; assign sequence type (resource page, journalist pitch, guest post) at import
- Connect to Hunter.io for automatic contact finding on import
- Configure automatic sequence pausing when a prospect responds (prevents follow-up emails to someone who has replied)

**Clay.com for AI Personalization:**
- Pull journalist's last 3 published articles automatically from their publication URL
- Use Claude or GPT-4 within Clay to generate a personalized first line: "I noticed your recent piece on [article topic] — [specific observation that shows you actually read it]"
- Export personalized rows directly into Pitchbox campaign

**HubSpot Attribution:**
- Create a HubSpot custom property "Link Building Influenced" for contacts sourced from organic pages that improved rankings during the program
- Build a HubSpot report: organic contacts → opportunities → closed-won ARR, filtered for contacts created after link building program launch vs. 12 months prior

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Outreach response rates are below 5% even after personalization**
Solution: Audit the personalization quality — AI-generated "personalization" that references the article title without demonstrating that you actually read it is worse than a direct pitch. Test two approaches: (1) a 3-sentence ultra-concise pitch that respects the journalist's time and makes a single strong hook, versus (2) a longer, more detailed pitch with the data. Journalists at trade publications often prefer short; editors at business publications often want more context. Also audit your sender domain — if your domain is new or has low engagement history, emails may be landing in spam. Switch to a warmed-up sending domain with ≥6 months of consistent email activity.

**Problem: Acquired links are not improving rankings — DR is going up but keywords aren't moving**
Solution: This typically means the links being acquired are from irrelevant or low-traffic pages despite having decent DR. A DR 55 page on a legal tech site with 200 monthly visitors and direct topical relevance is worth 10× more for ranking "CLM software" than a DR 70 page on a general business news site that mentions software in a tangential sentence. Audit your last 50 acquired links for topical relevance and page-level traffic. Shift acquisition efforts toward topically relevant publishers even if their DR is lower. Also check whether your target pages have adequate internal links from high-authority pages on your own site — link building amplifies existing internal authority, it doesn't substitute for it.

**Problem: Data asset earned strong coverage but links are no-follow or not indexed**
Solution: Journalist-sourced links from major publications (WSJ, Forbes, NYT) are almost always no-follow — this is expected and still valuable for brand authority, referral traffic, and downstream citation by other publications who do provide do-follow links. The real link-building value from Tier 1 media is the downstream effect: when a WSJ article cites your research, 15–30 other publications will reference that coverage and often provide do-follow links from their own pieces. The PR asset creates a "link magnet" effect over 6–12 months. For Tier 2–3 publications, negotiate do-follow links as a condition of data exclusivity or contributor relationships. Track both no-follow citations (brand value) and do-follow links (SEO value) separately in your reporting.
