## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom "Influencer Partner" contact and deal property set; build workflows that auto-tag any contact with influencer UTM parameters; set up a campaign for each creator to track sourced contacts in one place
- **Salesforce:** Use Campaign Member records with creator-specific campaigns; build an Influencer Attribution dashboard in Tableau or native reports showing pipeline by creator tier and name
- **Notion:** Build a creator CRM in Notion — one database for all partners with fields for tier, compensation, content schedule, UTM codes, performance metrics, and renewal date
- **Slack:** Create a #influencer-program channel with Zapier automations: new influencer-tagged demo request → instant notification; creator content goes live → team amplification reminder
- **Gong/Chorus:** Tag discovery calls where prospects mention an influencer by name — captures dark social attribution that UTMs miss
- **Google Sheets:** Build a creator performance scorecard updated monthly: investment, sourced demos, influenced pipeline, closed ARR, ROI ratio — shareable with finance for budget justification

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem:** Influencer content feels like obvious sponsored ads and gets low engagement from ICP
  **Solution:** Shift from product-first briefs to problem-first briefs. The creator's job is to educate about the problem category, not sell your product. Give them 3 pain points and let them write in their voice. Review and approve for accuracy, not style. The moment it sounds like ad copy, ICP audiences disengage.

- **Problem:** UTM tracking shows traffic and form fills but no pipeline shows up in CRM
  **Solution:** Audit the full funnel: (1) confirm UTM parameters survive the form redirect; (2) check that HubSpot/Salesforce workflows are correctly triggering on UTM source = "influencer"; (3) add self-reported attribution to your demo request form as a backup (drop-down: "How did you hear about us?" with influencer partner names listed); (4) cross-reference creator's direct DMs or email referrals which bypass UTMs entirely

- **Problem:** Tier 1 micro-influencers are active but driving zero demo requests or pipeline
  **Solution:** Micro-influencer ROI in B2B is almost always audience quality, not audience size. Run an audience audit: pull the list of everyone who engaged with the last 5 posts and check their job titles. If less than 20% are in your ICP, the creator has an audience mismatch regardless of how relevant their content topic seems. Either shift the creator to brand awareness goals (not pipeline goals) or exit the relationship.
