DELIVERABLE 1 — CONTENT PERFORMANCE INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM

A. Content Scoring Rubric (100-point framework applied within 24 hours of any post going live):

HOOK QUALITY (25 pts):
- Opens with a contrarian statement, credible data point, or specific pain scenario — not a product mention (10 pts)
- First sentence creates pattern interrupt vs. standard LinkedIn posts in category (8 pts)
- Hook is specific enough to trigger "this is about me" recognition in ICP reader (7 pts)

PROOF ARCHITECTURE (20 pts):
- Includes at least one named customer outcome with company size/title context (8 pts)
- Social proof is verifiable — company logo, real metric, recognizable brand (7 pts)
- Creator's own credibility is visible (current/former title in ICP role, not just "consultant") (5 pts)

BUYER JOURNEY ALIGNMENT (20 pts):
- Content addresses a top-3 pain point from win/loss interview data, not a feature benefit (8 pts)
- Positions the problem as costly/urgent without explicitly selling a solution (7 pts)
- Implicitly positions our category as the solution without naming competitors (5 pts)

CTA CONVERSION DESIGN (15 pts):
- Single, low-friction CTA — link in comments, DM for resource, not "book a demo now" (7 pts)
- CTA offers genuine value to the reader (benchmark report, template, calculator) (5 pts)
- UTM-tagged link routes to a dedicated influencer landing page with creator social proof (3 pts)

ENGAGEMENT ENGINEERING (20 pts):
- Post structure creates natural comment opportunities (question in final line, poll, fill-in-the-blank) (8 pts)
- Creator responds to every comment within 2 hours of posting — algorithmic amplification signal (7 pts)
- Comments within first hour include ICP titles (VP, Director, Head of) from target company sizes (5 pts)

B. Automated Performance Tracking Stack:
- UTM taxonomy: `utm_source=influencer&utm_medium=[platform]&utm_campaign=[creator-handle]&utm_content=[post-id]`
- CRM contact source tagging: create custom field "Influencer First Touch" that captures creator handle
- Pipeline influence query: any opportunity where a contact has a session from an influencer UTM at any point in the last 180 days = "influencer influenced pipeline"
- Weekly report automation: pull from HubSpot/Salesforce API, send to Notion or Google Sheets dashboard

DELIVERABLE 2 — HIGH-CONVERTING CONTENT ARCHETYPES (B2B-SPECIFIC)

For each archetype, provide: the psychological mechanism, the structural template, an example headline, and the optimal platform + format:

ARCHETYPE 1 — THE CONTRARIAN PRACTITIONER TAKE
- Mechanism: Challenges conventional wisdom held by the buyer's peers, creating tribal validation for a minority view the reader already privately holds
- Structure: "Everyone says X. I've seen 200+ companies try X. Here's what actually happens: [counterintuitive finding]. What works instead: [3-step alternative]."
- Ideal for: LinkedIn text post, 600-900 words, mid-week
- Pipeline mechanism: Generates comments from buyers who "agree" — surfaces qualified leads through self-identification

ARCHETYPE 2 — THE BEFORE/AFTER TRANSFORMATION STORY
- Mechanism: Jobs-to-be-Done narrative — buyer hires the story to validate their desired transformation is achievable
- Structure: "[Company type] was spending [pain metric] every [time period] on [painful activity]. 6 months later: [specific outcome metric]. Here's the exact change they made."
- Ideal for: LinkedIn carousel (7-9 slides), customer-narrated YouTube Short, newsletter deep-dive
- Pipeline mechanism: Buyers in identical situations self-identify in comments: "This is exactly us"

ARCHETYPE 3 — THE ORIGINAL DATA REVEAL
- Mechanism: Provides buyers with proprietary data they can use internally to build business cases — creates reciprocity and positions you as the category authority
- Structure: "We analyzed [X] companies in [category]. The finding that surprised us most: [counterintuitive stat]. Full breakdown: [link to gated benchmark report]"
- Ideal for: LinkedIn post + gated PDF lead magnet; newsletter sponsorship
- Pipeline mechanism: Gated download = verified intent signal; highest lead quality of all archetypes

ARCHETYPE 4 — THE TOOL COMPARISON FRAMEWORK
- Mechanism: Buyer at evaluation stage needs a decision framework; creator provides it, we are the contextual anchor
- Structure: "After testing 7 [category tools] for [use case], here's the only framework that matters for [ICP type]: [decision matrix with 5 criteria]. My take on each option."
- Ideal for: YouTube long-form (12-20 min), Substack deep-dive, LinkedIn document post
- Pipeline mechanism: High purchase intent signals; convert via "comment for my template" CTA

ARCHETYPE 5 — THE PRACTITIONER HOW-TO
- Mechanism: Teaches the buyer something immediately useful — goodwill and credibility at maximum; product is mentioned as a tool in the workflow, not the point
- Structure: "Here's exactly how I [achieved specific outcome] in [timeframe] — step by step: [numbered process]. Step 3 is where most teams fail."
- Ideal for: LinkedIn carousel or long post, X/Twitter thread, YouTube tutorial
- Pipeline mechanism: Creator comments reveal ICP buyers asking operational questions = warm outbound list

Apply this workflow to any post scoring 75+/100 on the scoring rubric above:

STEP 1 — QUALIFY FOR AMPLIFICATION (48 hours after posting):
- Minimum organic engagement rate threshold: [X% of creator's follower count]
- ICP comment quality check: Are commenters from target job titles at target company sizes?
- Click-through rate on UTM link: Above [X%] baseline
- If passes: Escalate to paid amplification. If fails: Archive, feed learning back to content brief.

STEP 2 — WHITELISTING SETUP (LinkedIn and Meta):
- LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads: Creator grants ad account access. Post ID is boosted directly as a "thought leader ad" — appears as creator's authentic content with a "Promoted" label. Retargeting audience: LinkedIn Matched Audience built from CRM contacts + lookalike from won customers.
- Meta Dark Post: Creator grants Facebook Page/Instagram account access. Existing post is run as a paid ad without appearing on creator's organic feed — zero disruption to their content strategy.
- Audience targeting parameters: Job title = [ICP titles], Company size = [X-Y employees], Industry = [target sectors], PLUS retargeting custom audience of website visitors 90 days.

STEP 3 — BUDGET AND DURATION:
- Micro-tier amplification: $500-$1,500 per post, 10-14 day run
- Mid-tier amplification: $2,000-$8,000 per post, 14-21 day run
- Kill switch trigger: If CPL exceeds [$X] by day 5, pause and reallocate budget to better-performing variant

STEP 4 — A/B TESTING:
- Variable 1: Headline/hook (first line of post vs. alternative hook written by marketing team)
- Variable 2: Thumbnail image (creator headshot vs. data visualization vs. customer logo)

STEP 5 — INCREMENTALITY MEASUREMENT:
- Create holdout group: 10-15% of target audience excluded from paid amplification
- Compare pipeline creation rate in exposed vs. holdout group at 30 and 60 days
- Incrementally attributed pipeline = (pipeline rate of exposed group − pipeline rate of holdout) × total audience size × ACV

- Benchmark: 15-25% conversion on influencer-specific landing pages (higher than standard because of pre-warmed trust from creator)
- Intervention if below: Gate is too high (asking for too much info) or lead magnet misaligns with content topic

STAGE 4 → LEAD TO MQL
- Benchmark: 30-45% of influencer-sourced leads reach MQL threshold within 14 days (vs. 15-20% for cold inbound)

STAGE 5 → MQL TO SQL/DEMO
- Benchmark: 25-35% MQL-to-SQL rate (higher quality than paid — buyer is pre-educated by creator)

STAGE 6 → DEMO TO CLOSED-WON
- Benchmark: No significant difference from other channels at this stage (close rate should match company average once in pipeline)
- Influencer content use in late-stage: Send prospects relevant creator content during sales cycle as "educational" touchpoints — not sales content

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Creators consistently score below 60 on the content rubric despite receiving optimized briefs.**
Solution: The bottleneck is almost always the hook. Run a "hook workshop" — send creators 10 examples of high-scoring B2B hooks from your category (not your brand) and ask them to rewrite their last 3 posts using those patterns. If score doesn't improve after 2 months, replace creator with one who has demonstrated pattern recognition in existing content.

**Problem: Paid amplification of top-scoring posts generates clicks but no leads (high CTR, low conversion).**
Solution: The disconnect is between the content narrative and the landing page. The influencer post creates a specific emotional context (e.g., "forecasting is broken") and the landing page must immediately continue that story — not reset to a generic product page. Create a dedicated landing page for each content archetype (pain-aware page for contrarian posts, transformation page for before/after stories) with the creator's face/name visible for trust continuity.

**Problem: ICP buyers are commenting and engaging but not converting to pipeline within 30 days.**
Solution: B2B influencer content often operates in a longer consideration window than paid search. Check pipeline at 60 and 90 days, not just 30. The more actionable fix: have your SDR team monitor creator comments daily and proactively reach out to ICP commenters with a hyper-personalized message referencing the specific post and their comment — this converts at 3-5x the rate of any automated follow-up.
