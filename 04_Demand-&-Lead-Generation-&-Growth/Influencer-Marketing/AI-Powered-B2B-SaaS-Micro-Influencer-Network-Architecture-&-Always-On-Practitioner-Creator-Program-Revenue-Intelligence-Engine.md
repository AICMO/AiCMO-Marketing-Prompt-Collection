## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Creators consistently produce off-brand or overly promotional content, damaging their audience trust and association with your brand.**
Solution: Shift from a "brief" model to a "principles + anti-examples" model. The brief should show 3 examples of excellent category content (not brand-forward) and 3 examples of what you don't want — promotional, scripted, or inauthentic posts. Hold a 30-minute creative kickoff at the start of each month for Tier 1 creators where you discuss the theme and share your reasoning. Great creators want to create great content — they need permission to not be promotional more than they need guardrails on what to say.

**Problem: Cannot measure dark social impact — self-reported attribution surveys show some creator mentions, but it's hard to build a credible ROI case for the program.**
Solution: Implement a three-source attribution triangulation approach: (1) UTM-tracked clicks from all creator content with CTA links, (2) "How did you hear of us?" survey data from demo requests with an explicit "practitioner content / LinkedIn influencer" option, (3) a quarterly ICP panel survey of 100 non-customers in your ICP asking unaided awareness and source of first hearing. Combine these three streams into a "creator influence multiplier" — typically 3–6× the directly tracked pipeline — and present as a range estimate with methodology disclosed.

**Problem: Significant creator churn after the first 90 days — early excitement fades when creators realize the program requires consistent effort with delayed personal benefit.**
Solution: The first 90 days are critical for creator habit formation. Structure onboarding around three wins: (1) their first piece of program content gets amplified by your company page with explicit shout-out (immediate social proof value for them), (2) within 60 days, connect them to at least one other Tier 1/2 creator in the community (peer value compounds program stickiness), (3) by day 90, share a personalized "impact report" showing their content's engagement, estimated reach, and any attributed pipeline — creators who see their content working become advocates for the program, not churned participants.
