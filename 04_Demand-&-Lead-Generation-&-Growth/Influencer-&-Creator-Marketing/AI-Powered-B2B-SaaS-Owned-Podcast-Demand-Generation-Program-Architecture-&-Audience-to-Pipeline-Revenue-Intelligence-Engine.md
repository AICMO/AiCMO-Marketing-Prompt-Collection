## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Download numbers plateau after initial launch spike and won't grow past 200 per episode.**
Solution: This almost always indicates a distribution problem, not a content problem. Audit: (1) Are you publishing consistently on the same day/time each week? Algorithms reward consistency. (2) Is your show listed in all major directories (Spotify, Apple, Amazon Music, iHeart, Pocket Casts)? (3) Are guests actively sharing their episodes? 90% of new listeners come from guest social shares in months 1–6. Send guests a promotional kit (pre-written posts, audiogram clip, email template) within 24 hours of publish and follow up if they haven't shared within 48 hours. (4) Are you cross-promoting in LinkedIn posts with the audio clip natively embedded? LinkedIn's algorithm suppresses external links — post the audiogram as a native video with the link in comments.

**Problem: We're generating listeners but zero pipeline attribution — sales says the podcast "isn't doing anything."**
Solution: Podcast pipeline is dark-funnel by nature — it won't appear in standard attribution models for 6–9 months. Fix this immediately with three actions: (1) Add a "How did you first hear about us?" field to your demo request form with "Podcast" as an explicit option — self-attribution typically captures 30–40% of actual podcast-influenced pipeline. (2) Run a quarterly survey to your email subscriber list asking which leads have had sales conversations — this surfaces pipeline that bypassed standard UTM tracking. (3) Brief your sales team weekly with a "podcast conversation starter" — a 1-sentence episode reference they can use in outreach to prospects who are subscribers. Track reply rates and log manually in CRM.

**Problem: We can't get Tier 1 guests to accept — response rate is under 15%.**
Solution: Tier 1 guests (analysts, authors, keynote speakers) receive dozens of podcast requests weekly. Cold outreach rarely works at sub-1,000 download volume. Instead: (1) Start with Tier 2 ICP guests (VP Sales/CROs at your actual ICP companies) — they have smaller audiences but higher acceptance rates and direct pipeline value. (2) Build "social capital" with Tier 1 prospects 4–6 weeks before asking: comment insightfully on their LinkedIn posts, share their content, quote them in your episodes without inviting them. When you reach out, reference the relationship you've already built. (3) Offer Tier 1 guests something beyond "exposure" — promise to promote their upcoming book/conference talk/course, offer to co-create a piece of research, or connect them with 3 people in your network they'd value. (4) Use warm introductions through Tier 2 guests who've already appeared — "I was just on [SHOW NAME] and thought you'd be a perfect guest — want me to intro you to the host?"
