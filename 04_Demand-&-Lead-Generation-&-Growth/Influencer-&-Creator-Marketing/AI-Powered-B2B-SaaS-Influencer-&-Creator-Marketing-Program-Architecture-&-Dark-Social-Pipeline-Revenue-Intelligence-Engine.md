DELIVERABLE 4 — CONTENT CO-CREATION PLAYBOOK

Design 6 content formats optimized for B2B creator programs. For each format:
- Format name and mechanics
- Why this format outperforms standard sponsored content for B2B audiences
- Psychological mechanism: why it builds trust and drives buying intent vs. awareness only
- Creator brief template (specific enough that an AI agent can generate 10 unique briefs from it)
- FTC compliance guidance: how to disclose the partnership authentically without killing engagement
- Distribution amplification: how the brand should reshare, remix, or promote the creator content post-publication

**Format 1 — The Honest Review**
Creator shares their genuine experience using the product to solve a specific problem relevant to their audience. No hype. Flaws acknowledged. Outcomes documented with real numbers.

**Format 2 — The ICP Pain Point Breakdown**
Creator produces an educational piece (thread, video, article) on a problem the ICP faces. Brand's product is mentioned as one legitimate solution among 2-3 alternatives — not as the only answer. This format builds trust precisely because it's not a hard sell.

**Format 3 — The Customer Story Amplification**
Creator interviews or features one of the brand's customers — not a brand-scripted testimonial but a genuine practitioner-to-practitioner conversation about how they solved a problem.

**Format 4 — The Comparison or Evaluation Framework**
Creator builds a framework their audience can use to evaluate solutions in the category (e.g., "5 questions to ask any [category] vendor before signing"). Brand's differentiators map naturally to the evaluation criteria without the creator naming the brand as the winner.

**Format 5 — The Dark Social Drop**
Creator shares exclusive, non-public insights, data, or early access with their community in private channels (Slack, Discord, newsletter, closed LinkedIn groups). Designed specifically to generate private sharing and DM conversations — the dark social demand signal that's impossible to manufacture with direct brand content.

**Format 6 — The Live Event Activation**
Creator hosts or co-hosts a webinar, roundtable, or AMA where the brand provides expertise, tools, or data. Creator's audience trust transfers to the brand through the live collaboration format.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Creators are producing content that feels like obvious sponsored posts, generating low engagement and audience backlash.
**Solution:** Brief creators on specific authentic content formats (the Honest Review, the Pain Point Breakdown) that explicitly allow them to mention competing solutions, acknowledge product limitations, and share genuine opinions — even mild criticisms build more trust than perfect testimonials. Require FTC disclosure but never require creators to hide that it's a partnership. Add a "creator editorial independence" clause to contracts that protects their voice.

**Problem:** Creator-attributed pipeline looks strong in early metrics but deal quality is poor (low ACV, high churn, misaligned ICPs).
**Solution:** Add audience ICP fit scoring as a primary creator qualification criterion — not just follower count or engagement rate. Use LinkedIn Audience Insights, newsletter subscriber data, or YouTube analytics to verify that ≥50% of a creator's audience matches your target job titles and company profiles before signing. Also review deal notes for creator-influenced opportunities to identify which creators are driving high-ACV, low-churn customers vs. which are generating volume but poor fit.

**Problem:** Can't measure dark social ROI — leadership wants hard attribution and the program appears to have no trackable impact.
**Solution:** Implement a multi-signal attribution model rather than requiring last-touch or UTM tracking. Track five measurable dark social proxies: (1) direct traffic spikes on creator content publish dates, (2) demo request "how did you hear" field mentions of creator names, (3) Gong/Chorus keyword mentions in sales calls, (4) website visitor company identification spikes in creator audience ICP segments, (5) G2 review surges with language echoing creator content. Present leadership with pipeline influence (not just pipeline source) as the primary metric, with deal velocity and CAC as supporting proof points.
