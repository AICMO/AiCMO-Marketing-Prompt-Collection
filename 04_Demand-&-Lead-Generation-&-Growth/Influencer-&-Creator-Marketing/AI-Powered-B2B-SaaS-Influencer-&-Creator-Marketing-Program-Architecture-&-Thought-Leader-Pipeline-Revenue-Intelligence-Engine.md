## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Creator content sounds like an advertisement — engagement tanks**
Solution: Enforce the 80/20 rule — 80% of the content should be the creator's genuine perspective, 20% product integration. Remove required product mentions from brief and replace with "naturally integrate if relevant." Review your approval process — if you're editing out the creator's voice, you're breaking the authenticity that drives creator marketing ROI. Consider a "native integration" call with creators to help them find their own authentic angle to your product.

**Problem: Can't track dark social attribution — pipeline attribution is artificially low**
Solution: Add a mandatory self-reported attribution question to every demo booking form and free trial sign-up. Use the exact question: "Where did you first hear about [Company]?" with a dropdown that includes your top Tier 1 creator names. Additionally, run a monthly correlation analysis: did LinkedIn search volume for your brand spike 3-7 days after a major creator post? This proxy measurement helps quantify dark social impact for CFO reporting even without direct tracking.

**Problem: Creators are underperforming after strong first month — engagement declining**
Solution: Check for "creator fatigue" — if you're asking for sponsored content too frequently, audiences start to tune it out. Reduce sponsored post frequency and increase co-creation depth (longer-form content, original research, joint webinars). Consider giving creators a 2-3 month content vacation from sponsored posts while keeping them on product access. Also audit whether the topic alignment has drifted — if their content focus has shifted away from your ICP's pain points, it may be time for a content strategy realignment call.
