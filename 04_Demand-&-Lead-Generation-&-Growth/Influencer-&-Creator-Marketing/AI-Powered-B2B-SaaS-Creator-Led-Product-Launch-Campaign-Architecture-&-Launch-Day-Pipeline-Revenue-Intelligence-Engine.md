This prompt engineers a complete creator-led product launch campaign for B2B SaaS — coordinating pre-launch seeding, launch-day activation, and post-launch amplification across your creator network to generate qualified trial demand, accelerate pipeline creation, and drive rapid category adoption at launch velocity. Use it when you have a significant product, feature, or category announcement and need to multiply launch impact through trusted practitioner voices instead of (or alongside) traditional paid and owned channels.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Three Tier 1 creators go silent in the week before launch — not responding to messages, content not submitted for review.
**Solution:** Activate the 72-hour creator recovery protocol: (1) Direct message via their preferred channel (LinkedIn DM, WhatsApp, email — try all three) with urgency framing: "We're 5 days from launch and need [X] by [specific date/time] to keep your slot in the launch sequence." (2) If no response in 24 hours, escalate to backup creator from your pipeline (always maintain a 20% backup pool of qualified creators who have been briefed but not yet onboarded). (3) Redeploy the silent creator's brief to an adjacent creator or to your brand channel as original content. Never delay a launch for a single creator dependency.

**Problem:** Launch day creator content engagement is dramatically below pre-launch baseline — posts getting 20% of the usual engagement.
**Solution:** First, check LinkedIn/platform algorithm status — sometimes platform outages or algorithm updates suppress new content temporarily. If no platform issue, activate the cross-creator engagement chain immediately (even if not all creators have posted yet) — authentic comment threads from practitioners often restart algorithmic distribution. For YouTube and newsletter content where engagement is slower, extend the amplification window: brief creators to publish follow-up "response" or "I went deeper on this" content in Week 2 rather than waiting for organic pickup. If the problem persists beyond 48 hours, analyze which content angles are outperforming and redistribute remaining scheduled briefs toward those angles.

**Problem:** A Tier 2 creator posts accurate content but frames the product favorably in a way that doesn't include FTC disclosure — a sponsored post that looks organic.
**Solution:** Contact the creator immediately (within 2 hours of discovering the post) and ask them to edit the post to add the required disclosure language ("Sponsored" or "Revenox Partner" in the first 3 lines visible without clicking "see more"). Provide the exact language to add. If the platform doesn't allow edits (rare), ask them to post a comment on their own content with the disclosure. Document the incident in your creator compliance log. Conduct a pre-publish disclosure audit for all remaining launch creators: require creators to submit their draft posts including disclosure language before publishing, rather than trusting post-hoc compliance for the remainder of the campaign.
