DELIVERABLE 1 — B2B PODCAST ECOSYSTEM INTELLIGENCE MAP

Identify and score the target podcast landscape for this specific ICP across 4 tiers:

**Tier A — High-ICP-Density Shows (Target Guest Priority):**
- Shows where the listening audience, not just the guest roster, matches the target ICP title and company profile
- Methodology for distinguishing "where ICP appears as guests" vs. "where ICP listens and learns"
- Listener size range representing the B2B sweet spot: large enough for reach, small enough for genuine ICP density (typically 2,000-25,000 listeners/episode for B2B)
- Show format analysis: interview-only vs. panel vs. solo host commentary — which formats generate highest post-episode buyer action rates and why
- Host archetype evaluation: practitioners (actively doing the work), executives (have done the work), analysts (interpreting the space), provocateurs (challenging conventional wisdom) — which archetype audiences trust most when making vendor evaluations
- Red flags that disqualify a podcast despite ICP demographic fit: engagement fraud indicators, monetization-first editorial approach, competitor lock-in sponsorship agreements, declining episode frequency

**Tier B — Emerging ICP Shows (Partnership Opportunity):**
- Newer shows (sub-18 months) with strong ICP audience trajectory but currently underpriced for sponsorships
- Host relationship signals indicating openness to creative partnerships beyond standard CPM advertising
- First-mover advantage calculation: estimated CPM cost differential between securing host partnerships now vs. in 12 months as show grows
- Founder/host background indicators that signal a creator who will build a long-term audience vs. burn out in 6 months

**Tier C — Adjacency Shows (Awareness Layer):**
- Shows where partial ICP demographic overlap exists (e.g., DevOps shows for a DevSecOps ICP, general SaaS operator shows for a VP Sales ICP)
- Strategy for using adjacency shows to intercept buyers in their research phase before category awareness is formed
- Content angle modifications required to resonate with a non-primary ICP audience without diluting message integrity

**Tier D — Competitive Intelligence Shows (Monitor, Don't Partner):**
- Shows where competitors are entrenched sponsors or hosts have public brand affiliations that create conflicts
- Intelligence extraction value: what insights these shows reveal about competitor positioning, ICP pain evolution, and category narratives to monitor without direct involvement

**For Each Tier, Provide:**
- Discovery methodology: exact search strings for Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Podchaser, Listen Notes, and social listening tools to surface qualifying shows
- Qualification scoring rubric (0-100):
  - ICP listener density (40 points): estimated % of audience matching target titles based on host guest roster analysis, episode topic clustering, and social media audience data
  - Audience engagement quality (25 points): review volume, listener-to-reviewer ratio, social discussion frequency, DM/community activity around episodes
  - Host partnership receptivity (20 points): existing sponsor approach (CPM-only vs. integrated partnerships), host social engagement pattern, creator business model signals
  - Show trajectory (15 points): episode frequency consistency, subscriber growth indicators, guest roster quality trend
- AI agent discovery protocol: automated process for finding, evaluating, and scoring 50+ candidate shows per ICP category without manual search

DELIVERABLE 2 — EXECUTIVE GUEST BOOKING SYSTEM

Design an AI-executable system for booking your executives as guests on Tier A and Tier B shows:

**Executive Positioning Architecture:**

For each available executive, define:
- Core thought leadership angle: the specific perspective, data point, or contrarian take that makes this executive a must-book guest for ICP-focused shows (not "we help companies do X" — a specific, debatable thesis about how the industry is wrong about something)
- Talk tracks by ICP pain stage:
  - Problem Aware stage: angle for shows where listeners know they have a pain but haven't evaluated solutions
  - Solution Aware stage: angle for shows where listeners are actively evaluating options and need differentiation signals
  - Product Aware stage: angle for shows where listeners know the category and are comparing vendors
- Guest category positioning: which archetype each executive naturally fits (practitioner, executive, analyst, provocateur) and how to pitch them within that archetype
- Content differentiation by show format: how to adapt the executive's angle for solo-host interview shows vs. panel shows vs. recorded roundtable formats

**AI-Powered Pitch Generation System:**

Step-by-step AI agent workflow for generating personalized booking pitches:

1. SHOW RESEARCH LAYER — for each target show, an AI agent extracts:
   - Last 20 episode topics and recurring themes
   - Host's publicly stated opinions, LinkedIn posts, and interview style indicators
   - Audience questions from reviews, social comments, and host-engaged threads
   - Gaps in show coverage: topics the ICP cares about that the show hasn't covered recently
   - Guest roster gaps: executive archetype or company stage representation that's underrepresented in recent episodes

2. PITCH CONSTRUCTION FORMULA — for each executive/show pairing, generate:
   - Subject line (for email pitches): 8-12 words, topic-first, name-agnostic, curiosity-triggering
   - Opening hook: specific reference to a recent episode, host comment, or audience question that demonstrates genuine listenership
   - Angle statement: the exact episode concept being proposed, framed as "a conversation about [specific thesis]" not "an interview with [executive title]"
   - Credibility anchors: 2-3 specific proof points (not generic claims) that validate the executive's authority on this specific angle
   - Audience value articulation: explicit statement of what the host's specific audience will learn or be able to do differently after the episode
   - Social proof elements: media logos, listener counts from previous appearances, review quotes from past guest episodes
   - Call to action: specific, low-friction next step with timeline

3. OUTREACH SEQUENCING — 5-touchpoint sequence per show:
   - Touchpoint 1 (Day 1): Email pitch with full angle concept
   - Touchpoint 2 (Day 4): LinkedIn connection + genuine engagement on host's most recent post (non-transactional)
   - Touchpoint 3 (Day 8): Follow-up email referencing a specific listener comment or episode development since original pitch
   - Touchpoint 4 (Day 14): Alternative angle offer — a different episode concept for the same executive if the first angle wasn't compelling
   - Touchpoint 5 (Day 21): Final email with social proof addition (new media appearance or relevant industry development) and explicit move-on signal
   
   Write all 5 touchpoints in full for a sample executive/show pairing — not templates with empty placeholders.

**Guest Booking Logistics Automation:**
- Pre-appearance checklist: what the AI agent coordinates 2 weeks, 1 week, and 48 hours before recording
- Episode framing brief: how to brief each executive to naturally incorporate 3-5 ICP-resonant points without scripted promotion
- Organic product mention framework: how to reference the company and product once per episode in a way that builds credibility rather than triggering listener skepticism
- Post-recording amplification protocol: AI agent workflow for repurposing guest appearances into LinkedIn posts, short-form clips, newsletter mentions, and sales enablement assets within 24 hours of episode publish

DELIVERABLE 3 — PODCAST HOST INFLUENCER PARTNERSHIP PROGRAM

Structure partnerships with B2B podcast hosts as creator-influencers whose audience trust represents a persistent demand generation channel:

**Partnership Model Architecture:**

Define 3 partnership tiers with distinct value exchange structures:

*Tier 1 — Organic Integration Partnerships (Earned Trust Model):*
- Host has ≤15,000 listeners/episode but ≥60% ICP audience density
- Partnership structure: product access + exclusive data/research access + revenue share on referred deals (not CPM-based advertising)
- Content formats: host-driven product commentary in regular episodes, dedicated "tool deep-dive" episodes, community-exclusive content drops
- Exclusivity terms: category exclusivity (no direct competitor partnerships) without platform exclusivity
- Expected pipeline contribution: 2-5 influenced pipeline opportunities per quarter at this tier
- Investment range: $500-$2,500/month in product value + $200-$800 cash retainer + rev share structure

*Tier 2 — Strategic Creator Partnerships (Authority Amplifier Model):*
- Host has 15,000-60,000 listeners/episode with 40%+ ICP audience density OR strong community amplification (active Slack/Discord, newsletter list, LinkedIn following)
- Partnership structure: monthly retainer + integrated content commitment + co-marketing investment (joint webinar, research report, live event)
- Content commitment: 2 integrated mentions/month + 1 co-produced asset/quarter + cross-promotion to host's adjacent channels
- Dark social strategy: host shares exclusive insights and offers in their private community channels (Slack, newsletter, Discord) that are not in the public episode feed
- Expected pipeline contribution: 8-15 influenced pipeline opportunities per quarter
- Investment range: $3,000-$8,000/month total program cost including production support

*Tier 3 — Flagship Creator Partnerships (Category Authority Model):*
- Host has 60,000+ listeners/episode OR is the defining voice in the ICP's professional community
- Partnership structure: significant annual commitment + co-production involvement + speaking/event integration
- Use cases: category creation campaigns, product launch amplification, competitive displacement narratives
- Risk management: exclusivity negotiation, editorial independence protections, reputational alignment audit
- Expected pipeline contribution: 20-40+ influenced opportunities per quarter plus brand search volume lift
- Investment range: $10,000-$35,000/month

**Host Onboarding System:**
- Proof of value package: what to deliver to a host partner on Day 1 (product license, dedicated customer success contact, original research access, speaking opportunity calendar)
- Creative brief library: 8 pre-built episode integration formats the host can execute immediately with their specific audience angle
- FTC compliance architecture: disclosure language that satisfies regulatory requirements without damaging audience trust or episode engagement
- Authentic content guardrails: what hosts can and cannot claim, how to brief on ICP-specific pain points without scripting responses, how to let host voice drive episode framing while ensuring brand safety

**Dark Social Amplification Through Podcast Hosts:**
- Private channel strategy: how to equip host partners to create exclusive content drops in their Slack communities, Discord servers, and newsletter segments that never appear in public episode feeds
- Community AMAs: quarterly live sessions where the brand's subject matter experts join the host's private community for unscripted Q&A — creates peak dark social pipeline activity
- Listener referral architecture: how to create attribution-trackable referral pathways for host audiences to discover your product without requiring listeners to click a sponsored link

DELIVERABLE 5 — PODCAST PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION ARCHITECTURE

B2B podcast influence is primarily dark: listening happens privately, sharing occurs in Slack DMs and meeting conversations, and most pipeline influence is never directly attributed. Build a multi-signal attribution system:

**Signal Layer — What to Capture:**
- Vanity URL and episode-specific UTM traffic: listener-specific landing pages per show and episode to track click-through from show notes and episode CTAs
- "How did you hear about us" field analysis: flag mentions of podcast names, host names, and episode topics in CRM inbound form submissions
- Sales call conversation intelligence: Gong/Chorus keyword monitoring for podcast show names, host names, episode titles, and key phrases from recent episodes
- Website de-anonymization correlation: using 6sense/Clearbit/Demandbase to identify company-level traffic spikes on days following high-ICP-density episode publications
- LinkedIn engagement signals: track inbound connection requests and profile views from ICP titles/companies following episode publications featuring relevant guests
- Brand search volume monitoring: Google Search Console and SEMrush brand term search spikes correlated with podcast episode publish dates
- G2/Capterra review language analysis: review submissions containing language echoing episode themes or featuring guest names as reference points

**Attribution Model Construction:**

*Podcast-Sourced Pipeline:* First-touch attribution where the verifiable first contact was a podcast-specific tracking link or explicit self-reported podcast mention

*Podcast-Influenced Pipeline:* Any opportunity where ≥1 podcast signal (UTM visit, sales call mention, "how did you hear" attribution, de-anonymized website visit correlated with episode publish) appeared within 45 days before opportunity creation

*Podcast-Assisted Revenue:* Closed-won opportunities where podcast influence signals appeared at any point in the buyer journey — tracked via CRM custom fields added at deal creation or update

*Podcast Halo Revenue:* Brand awareness lift attributable to podcast program — measured via brand search volume growth, share of voice improvement in ICP communities, and blind survey data from win/loss interviews

**CRM Integration Protocol:**
- HubSpot/Salesforce custom field architecture: "Podcast Influence Source" (show name), "Podcast Influence Date," "Podcast Signal Type" (UTM, sales call, self-report, inbound correlation), "Podcast Episode Title"
- Workflow automation: auto-tag contacts who visit podcast episode landing pages; enroll in accelerated sales nurture; alert assigned rep with podcast context when contact converts to MQL
- Campaign attribution taxonomy: UTM structure for podcast channels (utm_source=podcast, utm_medium=guest-appearance|host-partnership|owned-show, utm_campaign=[show-name]-[episode-id])
- Podcast attribution dashboard: exact metrics, data sources, and update cadence for a CMO-ready podcast ROI view

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Podcast pitches are being ignored by target show hosts — no response after full outreach sequence.
**Solution:** Diagnose the pitch angle before blaming the outreach cadence. Most B2B podcast pitch failures are angle problems, not delivery problems. Replace any pitch framing "your executive" as the subject with the episode concept as the subject. Add a reference to a specific recent episode that establishes genuine listenership — hosts respond to evidence that you've listened, not just that you've googled them. If angle quality is confirmed, try reaching hosts through adjacent channels: engage substantively with their LinkedIn posts for 2-3 weeks before the pitch arrives in their inbox.

**Problem:** Podcast-influenced pipeline numbers are being challenged by leadership who only trust last-touch attribution.

**Problem:** Host partner content feels overly promotional and is generating negative listener comments about the brand.
