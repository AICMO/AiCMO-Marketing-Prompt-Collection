## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Employees are afraid to post and say "I don't know what to say"**
Solution: Never start with "go post whatever you want." Start with a structured first-post challenge: "Share one thing you learned this week that would help a [ICP job title] do their job better." Provide the full post template with blanks. Then review and amplify their first post immediately to reward early action. Fear disappears when employees see engagement on their first post within hours.

**Problem: Executive leadership is skeptical — "this isn't a real business driver"**
Solution: Run a 30-day pilot with 10 pre-selected employees who already have 500+ LinkedIn connections in your ICP. Track every demo request that mentions any pilot participant's name. Present the number — even 2-3 influenced meetings from 10 people posting for 30 days demonstrates the model. Always show the counterfactual: how many impressions would it cost in LinkedIn Ads to generate equivalent reach?

**Problem: Posts sound too corporate or promotional after employees use AI tools**
Solution: The problem is the AI prompt, not the AI. Add this instruction to every content generation prompt: "Write this as if the employee is texting a smart colleague — no corporate jargon, no buzzwords, never use the words 'excited,' 'thrilled,' 'game-changing,' or 'innovative.' The employee should sound like they're sharing something useful, not selling something." Run a monthly "authenticity audit" where program managers flag 3-5 posts that sound corporate and work with the employee to rewrite.
