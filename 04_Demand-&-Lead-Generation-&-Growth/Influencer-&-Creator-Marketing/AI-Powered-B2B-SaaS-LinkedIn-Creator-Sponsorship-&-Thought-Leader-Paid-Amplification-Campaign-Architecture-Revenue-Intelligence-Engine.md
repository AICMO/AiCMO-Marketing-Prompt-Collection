DELIVERABLE 1 — LINKEDIN CREATOR IDENTIFICATION & QUALIFICATION SYSTEM

**Creator Landscape Map for This ICP:**

Define the LinkedIn creator archetype that drives ICP buying intent for this specific category. Not all LinkedIn creators are equal — the creator who reaches VPs of Sales effectively is a different profile than the creator who reaches DevSecOps engineers. Specify:

**ICP-Aligned Creator Archetypes (in priority order):**

1. **The Practitioner-Turned-Creator** — Current or recent VP/Director who does the exact job your buyer does, now sharing frameworks, failures, and hard-won lessons publicly. Why this archetype drives the most pipeline: peers trust peers. Their audience skews heavily toward the same roles they held, and their content triggers "this is exactly my problem" recognition — the fastest path from awareness to demo request.

2. **The Operator-Analyst** — Individual who spent 5-10 years at a company in your buyer's function, now advises or consults across multiple companies and shares cross-company patterns. Why valuable: their audience includes active buyers comparing approaches, not just passive learners.

3. **The Revenue Leader Turned Investor/Advisor** — Former VP or C-suite now operating as angel investor, advisor, or board member who posts about the category your product serves. Why valuable: their audience includes buyers who respect their operating credibility, and they have reach into senior levels of the buying committee.

4. **The Category Educator** — Creator who teaches specific skills, frameworks, or processes directly tied to your product category (e.g., a creator teaching "how to run effective pipeline reviews" for a forecasting tool). Why valuable: high buyer intent in the audience because content is directly relevant to the buying trigger.

**Creator Qualification Criteria Matrix:**

Provide a weighted scoring rubric (0-100) for each creator evaluated:

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring Guidance |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Audience ICP fit | 40 pts | % of creator's followers in target titles + company size; use LinkedIn Audience Insights or creator's own analytics screenshot if available |
| Engagement quality | 25 pts | Comment-to-like ratio ≥0.15 = strong; comments from ICP-level job titles = premium signal; generic comments ("great post!") discount the score |
| Content topic alignment | 20 pts | % of last 30 posts directly relevant to ICP's professional pain points (not just general "leadership" or "productivity" content) |
| Posting consistency | 10 pts | Posts ≥3x/week consistently = full score; irregular posting or >2-week gaps = deductions |
| Competitive entanglement | 5 pts (disqualifier) | Active sponsorship with direct competitor = automatic disqualification; evaluate last 90 days of posts for competitor mentions |

**LinkedIn Creator Search Protocol (AI-Executable):**

Step-by-step search methodology for an AI agent or team member to surface 50+ qualified candidates:

1. **LinkedIn Search Strings:** Exact search queries using title filters, keyword combinations, and Boolean operators to surface practitioners who post actively. Example structure: Search for "[Target Title] OR [Adjacent Title]" + filter by "Content creators" engagement signal (LinkedIn's built-in filter) + industry filters matching ICP company profile

2. **Signal Mining:** Search LinkedIn content (not just profiles) for high-engagement posts on your ICP's specific pain points. Posts with 500+ reactions on niche topics indicate creators with genuine ICP audiences. Use search terms matching the specific problem your product solves.

3. **Competitor Sponsorship Intelligence:** Monitor competitors' LinkedIn activity for sponsored posts. Any creator they've sponsored with audience overlap to your ICP is a qualified candidate for outreach — competitor validation reduces your due diligence burden.

4. **Cross-Platform Validation:** Check if LinkedIn creator also writes a newsletter (Beehiiv, Substack, LinkedIn Newsletter), hosts a podcast, or appears as a conference speaker. Multi-platform creators have proven audience trust and provide amplification beyond LinkedIn.

5. **Disqualification Red Flags:**
   - Follower-to-engagement ratio suggesting purchased followers (1M followers, 10 likes per post)
   - Content primarily about personal branding and "LinkedIn growth" — these creators attract other content creators, not buyers
   - Active competitor sponsorship within last 60 days
   - Posts averaging more than 3 sponsored posts per week — audience is trained to ignore ads

DELIVERABLE 2 — SPONSORSHIP RATE NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORK

**Market Rate Benchmarks by Creator Tier (2025-2026):**

Note: LinkedIn creator sponsorship rates are less standardized than YouTube or podcast rates. These benchmarks represent negotiated B2B SaaS sponsorship deals, not influencer marketing platform list prices:

| Creator Tier | Follower Range | Avg Engagement Rate | B2B Sponsored Post Rate (Market Range) | Efficient Target Price | Estimated CPM (ICP audience) |
|-------------|---------------|---------------------|----------------------------------------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| Emerging | 10K-30K | 3-8% | $300-$1,000/post | $400-$700/post | $50-$120 CPM |
| Mid-Tier | 30K-100K | 2-5% | $1,000-$3,500/post | $1,200-$2,500/post | $80-$200 CPM |
| Authority | 100K-300K | 1.5-4% | $3,000-$8,000/post | $3,500-$6,000/post | $60-$180 CPM |
| Macro | 300K+ | 1-2.5% | $6,000-$20,000/post | $7,000-$12,000/post | $80-$250 CPM |

**Why Emerging-tier creators often deliver better B2B ROI than Macro:**
- Higher engagement rates mean higher actual ICP impressions per dollar spent
- Smaller creators have more authentic relationships with their audience — sponsored posts feel less like ads
- Lower rates allow you to sponsor 8-12 creators simultaneously vs. 1 macro creator for the same budget
- Portfolio diversification reduces campaign risk: if one creator underperforms, others compensate

**Negotiation Levers That Reduce Cost Without Reducing Quality:**

1. **Exclusivity Limitation:** Offer category exclusivity (competitor exclusion) rather than platform exclusivity. Most creators will accept $200-$500/month in category exclusivity fees vs. the $1,000-$2,000 premium for broad exclusivity.

2. **Content Repurposing Rights:** Offer to pay a 20-30% premium in exchange for the right to promote the creator's post as a LinkedIn Thought Leader Ad (TLA) with your own LinkedIn Ads budget. This dramatically extends reach beyond organic post views.

3. **Multi-Post Packages:** Commit to 3-post or 6-post packages in exchange for 20-35% discount vs. per-post pricing. Creators value predictable revenue and multi-post deals de-risk their income — use this as leverage.

4. **Product Access + Fee Structure:** For Emerging and Mid-Tier creators, offer free product access ($500-$2,000 ARR value) + a reduced cash fee instead of full cash payment. Position it as "partner access" rather than gifting.

5. **Revenue Share on Verified Pipeline:** For creators willing to participate in a more sophisticated partnership, offer a base fee + pipeline bonus ($200-$500 per SQL generated through tracked creator link, capped at a defined amount per campaign).

6. **First-Mover Pricing:** If you're approaching a creator before they have active B2B sponsorships, you can often establish a "founding sponsor" rate 30-50% below what they'll eventually charge. Lock in multi-post agreements before their market awareness grows.

DELIVERABLE 3 — NATIVE CONTENT BRIEF ARCHITECTURE

**The B2B LinkedIn Sponsored Post Brief Framework:**

The brief is the highest-leverage document in a creator sponsorship program. A weak brief produces generic, ad-like content that damages both engagement and brand association. A strong brief produces creator-native posts that feel authentic, drive comments from ICP buyers, and generate dark social sharing.

**Brief Structure Template:**

---
**[Creator Name] x [Your Company] — Sponsored Content Brief**
**Campaign:** [Campaign Name / Objective]
**Post Live Date:** [Target Date]
**Budget / Terms:** [Post fee + repurposing rights / exclusivity terms]
**FTC Disclosure:** All posts must include "#ad" or "#sponsored" + verbal disclosure in the post body (see examples below)

---

**WHAT YOUR AUDIENCE CARES ABOUT (use this, not our product messaging):**

*Describe the specific problem this creator's audience faces — in the language the creator uses, not your company's language. Show the creator you understand their audience.*

Example for a sales forecasting product: "Your audience is VP Sales and CROs who've built forecasts on gut feel and outdated spreadsheet models, are now on the hook to board-level pipeline accuracy, and have failed at 3 different CRM implementations that were supposed to 'fix' the data problem. They're skeptical of another vendor claiming AI will solve forecasting."

---

**THE INSIGHT OR STORY WE WANT IN THIS POST (not the product pitch):**

*Give the creator the intellectual raw material to build their post — a counterintuitive insight, a framework, a failure story, a data point — that is genuinely interesting regardless of the product mention.*

Option A (Insight-Led): "Most sales forecasting failures aren't data problems — they're workflow problems. The data exists in Salesforce. The problem is reps entering deal data to satisfy a manager review, not because it helps them sell. That gap between data-for-managers and data-for-reps is where forecast accuracy dies."

Option B (Story-Led): "Give us permission to share a story from one of our customers — we'll send you the full anonymized story and you can rewrite in your voice. The story is about a CRO who cut her weekly forecast prep from 6 hours to 40 minutes and increased forecast accuracy from 58% to 91% in one quarter — and the 3 counterintuitive things she changed that had nothing to do with data quality."

Option C (Framework-Led): "We've built a 5-question 'forecast culture audit' that predicts whether a B2B sales org's forecast accuracy will improve or decay over the next 2 quarters. You can share the 5 questions and let your audience self-diagnose — our product is optional context at the end."

---

**HOW TO MENTION [COMPANY] (minimum mention / maximum authenticity):**

*Give the creator clear guidance on the minimum required brand integration and maximum flexibility to maintain their voice:*

- **Minimum:** Mention [Company Name] once with a brief description of what it does and one specific outcome (not vague claims — a real metric from a real customer)
- **Preferred:** A sentence in your own words about why you find the approach interesting or worth testing
- **What NOT to do:** Don't say [Company] is "the best," "the only," or "the most powerful" — your audience will immediately recognize it as paid content and engagement will drop
- **What WORKS:** Something like: "I've been looking at [Company Name]'s approach to [specific mechanism] and found the data on [specific outcome] surprising enough to test it with a few clients"

**Sample FTC Disclosure Language (use one):**
- "This post is sponsored by [Company Name]."
- "[Company Name] partnered with me on this post. All opinions are my own."
- "#ad — [Company Name] sponsored this post. They make [one-sentence description]."

---

**TRACKING LINK:** [Unique UTM-tagged URL for this creator and this post — include both a direct link and a bitly/short link for cleaner post appearance]

**PERFORMANCE FEEDBACK:** We'll share engagement data and demo requests from your tracking link within 14 days of publish. We use this to optimize future briefs — your posts get better for your audience as we learn what resonates.

---

**Post Format Recommendations (creator's choice, but these outperform based on our data):**
- 3-5 bullet insights with a contrarian take in the opener
- Personal story with a specific moment, decision, and quantified outcome
- "Unpopular opinion in [category]: [counterintuitive claim]" as the opening hook
- Framework post: "The 3-step [process] I use when [specific buying trigger situation]"

**Target post length:** 900-1,400 characters (LinkedIn's sweet spot for B2B engagement before "see more" truncation)
**Recommended posting time:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am or 12-1pm in creator's primary timezone
**Do NOT request:** Specific hashtags (looks inauthentic), exact product feature descriptions (sounds like a press release), more than 2 brand mentions (over-saturation)

DELIVERABLE 4 — MULTI-CREATOR CAMPAIGN ORCHESTRATION

**30-Day Creator Sprint Architecture:**

Running simultaneous creator sponsorships without budget waste, audience overlap, or message dilution requires systematic orchestration. This is especially critical in B2B where the ICP pool is small — your buyers likely follow multiple creators in your category and will notice if they see 5 different creators all mentioning your product in the same week.

**Audience Overlap Management:**

Before launching, build an audience deduplication analysis:
- Request LinkedIn Audience Insights screenshots from each candidate creator (top company names, top job titles, demographics)
- If two creators have >40% audience overlap with each other and your ICP represents ≤30% of their combined audience, run them in different months — not simultaneously
- For smaller ICP pools (<50,000 total LinkedIn members), limit to 3 simultaneous creator sponsorships to avoid "I keep seeing [Company Name] everywhere" overexposure that signals paid campaign rather than organic buzz

**30-Day Sprint Calendar (5 Creator Example):**

| Week | Creator | Post Content Type | Tracking Link | LinkedIn TLA Amplification |
|------|---------|-------------------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Week 1 | Creator A (Emerging, 18K followers) | Insight-led: ICP pain framework | UTM-A1 | No (test organic performance first) |
| Week 1 | Creator B (Mid-tier, 65K followers) | Story-led: Customer outcome narrative | UTM-B1 | Yes — boost with $500 TLA budget |
| Week 2 | Creator C (Mid-tier, 42K followers) | Framework-led: Diagnostic tool | UTM-C1 | No |
| Week 3 | Creator D (Authority, 120K followers) | Contrarian insight: Category misconception | UTM-D1 | Yes — boost with $1,500 TLA budget |
| Week 3 | Creator E (Emerging, 25K followers) | Personal story: Specific buying trigger scenario | UTM-E1 | No |
| Week 4 | Creator B (repeat) | Follow-up: Response to comments from Week 1 post | UTM-B2 | Yes — $750 TLA budget |
| Week 4 | Creator A (repeat) | Data point: Benchmark that surprises your ICP | UTM-A2 | No |

**LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads (TLA) Integration:**

When a creator post performs organically (200+ reactions in 48 hours), immediately promote it as a Thought Leader Ad:
1. Request creator permission (include in original sponsorship contract)
2. Set up the TLA in LinkedIn Campaign Manager using the creator's post URL
3. Target: First-party contact list of ICP accounts + lookalike audience built from CRM contacts
4. Budget: $500-$2,000 per amplified post
5. Duration: 7-14 days
6. Expected result: Extend organic reach by 3-8x while maintaining "from a real person" credibility vs. standard LinkedIn Ads creative

**Content Variety Protocol:**

Avoid audience fatigue by varying content types across simultaneous creators:
- No two creators should post with the same framing (e.g., avoid 2 insight-led posts in the same week)
- Rotate between: Insight → Story → Framework → Data → Contrarian Opinion across the 5-creator portfolio
- Brief each creator on what other creators are posting (with permission) to prevent accidental duplication

DELIVERABLE 5 — PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION ARCHITECTURE

**LinkedIn Creator Sponsorship Attribution Model:**

LinkedIn creator-driven pipeline is notoriously difficult to attribute with precision. Most B2B conversions from creator content happen through paths that standard UTM tracking misses:
- ICP buyer sees creator post on LinkedIn → shares link in Slack to colleague → colleague Googles your brand → converts on direct traffic
- ICP buyer reads creator post → DMs your sales rep directly → rep logs a call in CRM without noting the source
- ICP buyer sees 3 creator posts over 4 weeks → searches "[Company Name] vs. [Competitor]" → converts on branded paid search → gets attributed to Google Ads

**Five-Signal Attribution System:**

To build a board-ready creator sponsorship attribution model, track all five signals simultaneously:

**Signal 1 — Tracked Conversions (Last-Touch)**
- UTM-tagged links in creator posts → demo request, trial signup, or free tool conversion
- This will undercount creator influence by 60-80% but is your most defensible hard number
- CRM setup: Campaign source = "linkedin-creator", Campaign medium = "creator-sponsorship", Campaign content = creator name, Campaign term = post date

**Signal 2 — Direct Traffic Correlation Analysis**
- Pull direct traffic volume (sessions, demo requests from direct traffic) in 72-hour windows following each creator post publish
- If direct traffic spikes 25%+ vs. trailing 7-day average on creator publish days, that is creator attribution
- This works because B2B buyers who see a LinkedIn post they want to act on often type the brand name directly into their browser rather than click a link

**Signal 3 — LinkedIn Company Page Signal**
- Pull LinkedIn Company Page analytics for: page views, follower growth, "About" section visits, and "Product" page visits on creator post days
- Significant lifts (>50% day-over-day on post day) indicate creator-driven brand research
- LinkedIn Campaign Manager's Website Demographics (if your LinkedIn Insight Tag is installed) can show the job titles of people visiting your site on creator post days

**Signal 4 — Sales Rep Self-Report Tagging**
- Add required field in CRM opportunity creation: "How did this prospect first hear about us?"
- Brief sales team monthly on active creator sponsors so reps can probe during discovery calls: "Did you see any content about [Category] on LinkedIn recently?"
- Gong/Chorus keyword tracking: set alerts for creator names, "I saw a post about," "someone on LinkedIn shared," and "I've been seeing [Company Name] a lot lately"

**Signal 5 — Branded Search Volume Lift**
- Google Search Console: monitor branded query volume weekly during creator campaign periods
- Google Trends: compare branded search trends before, during, and after creator campaigns
- A sustained 15-30% increase in branded search volume during a creator campaign period indicates creator-driven awareness → intent conversion

**Creator Program ROI Calculation Framework:**

Creator-Sourced Pipeline = Sum of UTM-tagged demo request value from creator links
Creator-Influenced Pipeline = Any pipeline where Signal 2, 3, 4, or 5 appeared within 30 days of creator post
Creator-Influenced Revenue = Closed-won deals in Creator-Influenced Pipeline
Campaign Efficiency = Creator-Sourced Pipeline ÷ Total Creator Spend (target: >10x)
ICP CPM = Total Creator Spend ÷ (Estimated ICP impressions from creator posts × 1,000)
Creator CAC = Total Creator Spend ÷ Customers acquired in creator-influenced pipeline

DELIVERABLE 6 — 90-DAY CAMPAIGN ROADMAP

**Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-21)**

*Week 1 — Creator Sourcing & Qualification:*
- Run AI-assisted LinkedIn search protocol to surface 50 candidate creators across target tiers
- Score all 50 candidates on qualification matrix; shortlist top 15
- Request LinkedIn Audience Insights or analytics screenshots from shortlisted creators via initial outreach
- Begin competitor sponsorship monitoring: identify 3-5 creators competitors are actively sponsoring

*Week 2 — Outreach & Negotiation:*
- Begin outreach to top 15 creators (non-transactional first message acknowledging specific content)
- Follow-up with 8-10 receptive creators with value-first offer (product access or exclusive data asset)
- Present sponsorship opportunity with package pricing to 5-7 creators who expressed interest
- Negotiate: target 3-post packages or 6-post packages for budget efficiency

*Week 3 — Contracts & Onboarding:*
- Finalize agreements with 5-8 creators across tier mix
- Deliver onboarding package: product access, messaging guide, brief library, UTM tracking links
- Set up CRM campaign taxonomy for creator attribution (UTM structure, campaign source naming, pipeline tagging)
- Configure LinkedIn Insight Tag and baseline Google Search Console data pull

**Phase 2: Activation (Days 22-60)**

*Weeks 4-5 — First Post Cycle:*
- Brief Creator A and B for Week 4 posts (at least 7-day brief lead time minimum)
- Publish Creator A (Emerging tier) — no TLA amplification; measure organic performance baseline
- Publish Creator B (Mid-tier) — activate TLA if 200+ reactions in 48 hours
- Run Signal 1-5 attribution tracking; document direct traffic lift

*Weeks 6-7 — Optimization Cycle:*
- Review first-post performance data: which creator drove the most tracked conversions? Direct traffic lift? Branded search lift?
- Brief Creators C, D, E based on learnings from first cycle
- Adjust content brief format for underperforming creators (shift from insight-led to story-led if first posts underperformed)
- Scale TLA amplification budget to top 2 performers

*Week 8 — Mid-Campaign Review:*
- Compile all 5 signals from first 30 days
- Calculate preliminary: creator-sourced pipeline, creator-influenced pipeline, ICP CPM by creator
- Go/no-go checkpoint: Are any creators performing below 0.5x expected pipeline contribution? Replace or brief differently.
- Report to leadership: leading indicators (engagement from ICP job titles, demo requests by source) + lagging indicators (influenced pipeline value)

**Phase 3: Scale & Attribution (Days 61-90)**

*Weeks 9-10 — Creator Portfolio Expansion:*
- Add 2-3 net-new creators based on audience data from Phase 2 best performers
- Test a repeat post from best-performing creator — second posts typically outperform first posts by 20-40% with the same audience because brand familiarity reduces skepticism
- Expand TLA amplification to include first-party contact upload targeting (existing CRM contacts see your creator's post as a sponsored ad)

*Weeks 11-12 — 90-Day Program Report:*
- Compile full 5-signal attribution analysis
- Calculate program ROI: creator-sourced pipeline vs. total spend; creator-influenced pipeline vs. total spend
- Benchmark against LinkedIn Ads CPM and demo request CPL for same period
- Executive summary: 3 best-performing creator archetypes, average CPM by tier, pipeline ROI multiple, recommendation for Month 4 budget allocation

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Creator posts are generating high engagement but zero tracked demo requests — UTM links are getting clicks but no conversions.
**Solution:** The issue is almost always post-click landing page friction, not the creator campaign. Run the UTM link to a creator-specific landing page with a social proof headline that matches the creator's audience language ("Built for revenue leaders who've tried the manual forecasting fix"). Remove form fields to first name + email + company + demo time slot only. Also check whether the creator's audience is primarily practitioners vs. buyers — high engagement from ICs who enjoy the content but can't champion a purchase will show the same pattern. Request a creator audience breakdown screenshot and validate that ≥40% of audience are director-level and above.

**Problem:** Creator rates are inflating — creators who quoted $1,000/post in January are now asking $3,500 because another vendor paid that rate.
**Solution:** Anchor new negotiations to the full-package value you're providing: TLA amplification rights (which extend their post's reach and can increase their personal follower count), product access, co-marketing inclusion (case study credit), and speaking/event invitations. Creators with smaller but highly engaged audiences often deliver better B2B pipeline ROI than newly-expensive mid-tier creators — rebuild your portfolio toward 8-12 Emerging tier creators at $500-$900/post rather than 3 mid-tier creators at $3,500/post. The pipeline ROI often improves because you get audience diversification and portfolio risk reduction.

**Problem:** Leadership doesn't believe the creator program is working because UTM-tracked demo requests (Signal 1) look low, and they're attributing revenue to Google Ads (which shows up as last-touch in CRM).
**Solution:** Present the 5-signal attribution report rather than arguing about last-touch CRM data. Show the direct traffic correlation analysis (Signal 2) with screenshots from GA4 — the lift on creator publish days is the most compelling visual evidence for non-marketing executives. Pull a sample of "direct traffic" demo requests from creator campaign periods and show the deal timeline: prospect engaged with creator post (via Gong self-report or "how did you hear" field) → visited direct → converted. Build a 3-month comparison showing branded search volume growth during creator campaign periods vs. pre-campaign baseline (Signal 5). Request that finance and CRO run the controlled comparison: deal velocity and ACV in creator-influenced vs. non-influenced opportunities closed during the same period.
