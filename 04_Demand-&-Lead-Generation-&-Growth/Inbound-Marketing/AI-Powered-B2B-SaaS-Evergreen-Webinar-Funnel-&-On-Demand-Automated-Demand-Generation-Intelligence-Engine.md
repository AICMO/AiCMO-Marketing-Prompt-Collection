## Overview

Transforms your existing webinar library — and every future live session — into a fully automated, always-on demand generation machine that generates qualified pipeline 24/7 without additional headcount. Use this when you want to multiply the ROI of your event marketing investment by converting one-time live webinar traffic into a compounding inbound lead asset that runs autonomously while your team sleeps.

Design the following:

1. WEBINAR CONTENT AUDIT & PRIORITIZATION FRAMEWORK
Evaluate your existing webinar library and identify which recordings are best suited for evergreen automation:
- Selection criteria: topic evergreen relevance score, original registrant-to-attendee conversion, lead quality rating, audience engagement score, and educational vs. promotional content ratio
- Prioritization matrix: rank by topic search demand, ICP relevance, and sales cycle stage coverage (awareness / consideration / decision)
- Refresh triggers: when to re-record vs. update an existing webinar vs. retire it
- Content gap map: which buyer journey stages and personas have zero evergreen webinar coverage

2. EVERGREEN WEBINAR FUNNEL ARCHITECTURE
Build the full funnel from discovery to demo request:
- Registration page optimization: headline, subheadline, bullet proof points, speaker credibility signals, and urgency mechanics for on-demand content (hint: "watch instantly" vs. "join live")
- Thank-you page sequence: what to show immediately after registration to maximize same-session conversion
- On-demand delivery experience: how to simulate live urgency in an automated format (simulated Q&A, timed polls, chapter navigation)
- Post-watch email sequence: 5-email follow-up sequence triggered by watch completion percentage (watched <25%, 25–75%, >75%, full watch + clicked CTA, watched but didn't click)
- Demo offer placement: exactly when during the webinar and in the post-watch sequence to introduce a demo or trial offer

3. TRAFFIC GENERATION & DISTRIBUTION ARCHITECTURE
Build the multi-channel traffic engine that fills the evergreen funnel:
- SEO landing page strategy: how to create a keyword-targeted, indexable registration page for each evergreen webinar (target informational + "how to" keywords at 500–2,000 monthly searches)
- LinkedIn organic promotion: 3 post templates per webinar (hook post, value excerpt post, social proof post) for company page and employee advocacy amplification
- Email nurture integration: how to embed evergreen webinar CTAs into existing nurture sequences at the right buyer journey stage
- Retargeting audience build: creating custom audiences from webinar visitors, partial viewers, and registration completers for paid retargeting campaigns
- Partner co-promotion: how to get technology partners and industry associations to promote your evergreen webinars to their audiences

4. AUTOMATION & TECHNOLOGY STACK
Configure the tools that run the system autonomously:
- Webinar platform selection criteria: EverWebinar, Demio, Zoom Webinars, WebinarJam — specific features required for evergreen automation (scheduled replays, behavioral email triggers, analytics by watch depth)
- CRM/MAP integration: how to pass registrant data, watch depth, engagement score, and CTA clicks to HubSpot or Salesforce for automated lead routing
- Lead scoring rules: specific point values for webinar registration (+5), watch >75% (+15), watched multiple webinars (+20), clicked demo CTA (+30)
- Automated SDR alert: when watch depth + engagement score triggers a "hot lead" alert in Salesforce/HubSpot with full viewing history for personalized follow-up
- Monthly performance digest: automated report delivered to demand gen team every 4 weeks with registrations, completion rates, MQLs generated, and pipeline influenced

5. CONTENT ATOMIZATION ENGINE
Extract maximum value from each webinar recording:
- Clip extraction strategy: identify the 3–5 highest-value moments per webinar for LinkedIn/YouTube/TikTok organic clips (criterion: standalone educational value, no product demo dependency)
- Blog post conversion: how to turn each webinar transcript into a 1,200–1,800 word SEO-optimized blog post using AI
- Slide deck repurposing: transform webinar slides into LinkedIn document carousels for organic amplification
- Podcast episode creation: strip audio, add intro/outro, publish to podcast directories as standalone episodes
- Email newsletter excerpt: 300-word email digest version for subscriber nurture

6. PERFORMANCE OPTIMIZATION LOOP
Build the continuous improvement system:
- North Star metric: pipeline influenced per evergreen webinar (target: $X pipeline per 100 registrants, calibrated to your ACV)
- A/B test calendar: what to test each month (registration page headline, email subject lines, CTA timing, watch depth required before demo offer)
- Churn analysis: identify which webinars have high registration but low completion (content quality issue) vs. high completion but low demo conversion (offer timing issue)
- Annual refresh audit: systematic review to retire, update, or re-record every webinar on a rolling 12-month basis

Provide specific tool recommendations (webinar platforms: EverWebinar, Demio; email automation: HubSpot, Marketo, ActiveCampaign; video hosting: Wistia, Vidyard; transcription: Otter.ai, Rev; repurposing: Descript, Opus Clip) for each component. ## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Registration rates are high but watch depth is consistently below 25%**
Solution: This signals a "bait-and-switch" expectation gap — your registration page is promising something different from what the webinar delivers. Audit the registration page description against the actual first 15 minutes of content. The first 5 minutes of any evergreen webinar must deliver an immediate, high-value hook — share your most surprising insight or stat in minute 1, not minute 45. Also check: is the on-demand player loading slowly? Buffering above 3 seconds causes 40% abandonment on mobile.

**Problem: High watch completion but near-zero demo requests from post-watch sequences**
Solution: The webinar content is educational but not positioned close enough to your product's value. Review your CTA placement — for MOFU webinars, the demo CTA should appear at 85–90% of total runtime (not at the end), and the post-watch Email 2 should lead with a customer story (not a feature list). Also review your email sequence timing: if Email 4 (hard demo ask) arrives 8+ days after viewing, the prospect has cooled. Compress BOFU sequences to 4 days maximum.

**Problem: SEO traffic to registration pages is minimal after 3 months**
Solution: Most evergreen webinar registration pages are optimized for conversion, not search. They use marketing language ("Join 5,000+ leaders") rather than keyword-targeted language. Create a separate SEO landing page for each webinar that targets the informational keyword, includes 800+ words of educational content around the webinar topic, and embeds the registration form as a secondary CTA below the content. Internal linking from existing high-traffic blog posts to these new pages accelerates ranking significantly.
