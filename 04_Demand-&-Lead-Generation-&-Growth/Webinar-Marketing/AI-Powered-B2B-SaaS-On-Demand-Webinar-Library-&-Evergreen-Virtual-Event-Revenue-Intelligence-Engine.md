## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Create a "Replay Viewer" contact property (multi-select: which replay categories they've watched) — update via workflow triggered by form submission with hidden field for webinar category
- Build a "Replay-Influenced" deal report: filter for closed-won deals where associated contact has a replay form submission date before or during the opportunity creation date
- Use Contact Activity Timeline to see replay viewing history in the prospect record — arms SDRs with conversation context

**Vidyard / Wistia:**
- Enable "Heatmap" analytics on every replay to identify which segments have the highest drop-off — these are signals for content editing and future topic selection
- Set up viewer ID sync: when a HubSpot contact watches a Vidyard video, sync their view data (percentage watched, number of replays) back to HubSpot contact record
- Use Vidyard's "in-video CTAs" to embed clickable demo request buttons at the 80% and 95% completion marks

**Salesforce (if applicable):**
- Create a Campaign record for each on-demand replay (Campaign Type = "On-Demand Webinar") and associate CampaignMember records when contacts submit the gate form — enables native Salesforce campaign influence reporting
- Build an "Engaged with Replay" lead score component (+15 points for gated form submit, +5 for replay page visit without gate)

**Google Search Console + Looker Studio:**
- Add all replay landing page URLs to a GSC URL group — track impressions, clicks, and average position monthly
- Build a Looker Studio dashboard pulling GSC data + HubSpot form conversion data: columns = URL, impressions, clicks, CTR, form submissions, demo requests — updates automatically, no manual reporting

**Zapier / Make.com:**
- Zap: New HubSpot form submission on replay page → send Slack notification to SDR with contact name, company, job title, and webinar topic → SDR adds context-rich note to contact before reaching out
- Zap: Contact reaches "Replay Viewer Score ≥ 50" in HubSpot → auto-create task for assigned SDR → task description auto-fills with top replays watched and last activity date

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Replay landing pages are getting traffic but few form submissions (gate conversion <10%)**
Fix: Audit the gate timing. If the gate appears before any video plays, conversion rates collapse — visitors have no reason to submit. Implement a "preview then gate" model: allow 90–180 seconds of free viewing (hook section), then trigger the gate with a soft overlay. Test email-only gating vs. full form — email-only gates typically convert 2–4× higher. Also check page load speed: video embeds that take >3 seconds to load lose 40%+ of visitors before the gate appears.

**Problem: Post-replay nurture sequence has low engagement (open rates below 12%)**
Fix: The most common cause is misaligned timing — the email arrives 24–48 hours after someone watched a 45-minute webinar on a complex topic, and the email treats them like a cold prospect. Rewrite Email 1 to acknowledge their specific viewing behavior ("You just spent 47 minutes learning about [Topic] — here's what our most engaged viewers do next"). Personalize Email 2 with the specific replay category they watched (merge tag). If your tech stack allows it, pull in the attendee's Q&A question from the live session and reference it in the follow-up — this 1:1 relevance dramatically increases reply rates.

**Problem: On-demand replay content becomes outdated (product screenshots, pricing, features are inaccurate)**
Fix: Implement a quarterly content audit using a "freshness flag" system. Add a metadata field to each replay page: "Last Verified Date" and "Content Expiry Date" (default: 12 months from publication). At 9 months, trigger an internal Slack reminder to the content owner to review the replay for accuracy. For product deep-dives specifically, add a banner to the replay page: "Recorded in [Month Year] — for our latest product features, visit [link to docs/product tour]." This preserves SEO value while managing expectations. Only unpublish when the core narrative is fundamentally inaccurate, not just when minor features have changed.
