## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner doesn't promote their agreed email sends, leaving you with 30% fewer registrants than projected.**
Solution: Build a "Promotion Accountability Check-in" into your partnership agreement — a mid-campaign sync 10 days before the event to verify sends are scheduled. Add a mutual performance clause: if either party delivers less than 70% of their agreed promotion, both parties commit an additional paid budget to compensate. This creates symmetrical accountability rather than one-sided pressure.

**Problem: After the event, your partner is slow to share the lead list, creating a cold-outreach problem for your SDRs.**
Solution: Automate the lead exchange. Both companies should use the same webinar platform (Zoom, ON24, Goldcast) with a shared event, so both have simultaneous access to the attendee report the moment the session ends. If technical access isn't possible, specify in the partnership agreement: "Lead data must be delivered via shared Google Sheet within 2 hours of session end." Include a penalty clause (partner forfeits next event lead sharing rights if they miss the SLA twice).

**Problem: Co-webinar topics become too product-focused because both vendors want to demo their tools, making the content feel like a double sales pitch — causing registration and attendance rates to drop.**
Solution: Enforce a "60/40 content rule" in your co-production agreement — 60% of session content must be category education, research, or case study driven; only 40% can be vendor-specific. Assign a neutral moderator (a customer, analyst, or industry practitioner) to chair the session, which naturally keeps the conversation in neutral territory. Position vendor CTAs ONLY in the last 5 minutes and in the post-event follow-up, not embedded throughout the session narrative.
