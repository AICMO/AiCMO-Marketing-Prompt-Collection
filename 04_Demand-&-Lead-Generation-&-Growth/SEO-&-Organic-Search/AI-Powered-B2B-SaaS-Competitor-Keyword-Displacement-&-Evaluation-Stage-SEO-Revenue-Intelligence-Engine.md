## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Comparison pages rank poorly despite solid keyword targeting and adequate word count**
Solution: Evaluation-stage comparison pages face Google's Helpful Content scrutiny hard — thin or biased content is algorithmically demoted. The fix: (1) Include genuine, balanced pros AND cons for your own product — pages that only praise you and trash competitors are flagged as low-quality. (2) Add an "Updated [month, year]" timestamp and commit to a 90-day refresh cadence — stale pricing or features trigger freshness penalties. (3) Earn editorial backlinks by reaching out to publications that cover your category for link inclusions — comparison pages with zero inbound links rarely break top-10 regardless of content quality.

**Problem: Competitor sends a legal notice about comparison content**
Solution: B2B SaaS comparison content is legitimate nominative fair use in most jurisdictions, but it requires factual accuracy. Follow these guardrails: (1) Use competitor brand names only to identify, not to imply endorsement or affiliation. (2) Source all pricing claims from the competitor's official public pricing page — date-stamp citations. (3) Never use competitor logos without explicit permission (link to their site instead). (4) Avoid subjective claims without evidence ("worst support in the industry"); use factual statements with cited sources ("3.2 stars on G2 as of [date]"). Keep a dated evidence file for every factual claim. Consult legal before publishing comparisons involving brands known for aggressive trademark enforcement.

**Problem: High traffic to comparison pages but very low demo or trial conversion (under 0.5%)**
Solution: Visitors arrived in research mode, and the page is treating them like they're ready to buy — friction mismatch. Diagnose: (1) Check scroll depth — if visitors leave before reaching CTAs, the page needs a sticky CTA bar or above-fold conversion element. (2) Add a progressive CTA ladder: first offer a low-friction "Free Comparison Guide PDF" (email capture), then "Watch a 3-minute product video" (mid-funnel engagement), with "Book a Demo" only as the tertiary ask. (3) A/B test CTA copy — on competitor-displacement pages, action verbs that acknowledge the switch intent ("Switch to Corvia," "Make the move") consistently outperform generic verbs ("Try," "Get Started") by 25-45%.
