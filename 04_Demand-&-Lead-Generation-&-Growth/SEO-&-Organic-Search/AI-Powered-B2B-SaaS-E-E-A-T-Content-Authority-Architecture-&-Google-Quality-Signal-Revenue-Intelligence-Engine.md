## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Our content is written by AI and we don't have enough SMEs to review everything.**
Solution: You don't need human-written content — you need human-reviewed and human-attributed content. Build a Tier 3 Editorial Oversight layer: assign each content category to a named internal reviewer (even if they don't write). Their job is a 30-minute quality check: verifying key claims, adding 1-2 sentences of personal insight, and adding their name as "Reviewed by." Add the reviewer's credentials to all published AI-assisted content. Google's guidance explicitly acknowledges AI can assist with high-quality content creation — the requirement is that a human with relevant expertise is accountable for its accuracy.

**Problem: We're a startup with no analyst coverage, no press, and no famous executives — how do we build authority from zero?**
Solution: Start with Experience, not Authoritativeness. You don't need Gartner to validate you. Publish original data from your product (even if it's a small customer base — "Based on analysis of [X] companies using [PRODUCT]" is legitimate). Get your founder to publish a detailed practitioner blog post about how they personally solved the problem your product solves, using specific numbers. This experience-led content can rank and earn authority even at early stage. Authority compounds — prioritize getting one excellent placement in a mid-tier industry publication rather than chasing HBR in year one.

**Problem: We updated our author bios and added schema but still haven't seen ranking recovery after 3 months.**
Solution: E-E-A-T remediation takes 3-6 full months to reflect in rankings because Google re-evaluates content during Core Updates (which run on a quarterly cycle). Check if your content quality issues go deeper than attribution: run the Quality Matrix on your top 20 pages and honestly score the "Depth" and "Experience" dimensions. If depth scores are 1-2, no amount of better bios will help — the content itself must be substantially improved. Also audit for thin page patterns at the site level: if >40% of your indexed pages are below 800 words with no unique insight, consider a strategic depublication and consolidation of weak content, as a site-level quality signal problem may be suppressing your entire domain.
