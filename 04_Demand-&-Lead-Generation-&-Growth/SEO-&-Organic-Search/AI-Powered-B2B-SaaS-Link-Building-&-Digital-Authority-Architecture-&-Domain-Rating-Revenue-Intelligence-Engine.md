## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Outreach reply rates below 3% despite high volume.
**Solution:** The issue is almost always personalization depth, not volume. Audit your last 50 outreach emails — if the prospect's name and site URL are the only custom fields, you need deeper personalization. Use AI to summarize each target site's last 3 articles and include a specific, genuine reference to their recent work. Expect 3x reply rate improvement when personalization includes a specific content reference vs. generic flattery.

**Problem:** Earning many low-DR links (DR 5-15) but DR isn't improving.
**Solution:** Low-DR links have minimal impact on your own DR — they count as referring domains but don't move the needle. Shift outreach budget to Tier 1-2 prospects exclusively. A single DR 70 link from an industry trade publication moves your DR more than 50 DR 15 links. Evaluate your linkable assets: if they only attract niche blogs, you likely need a higher-quality asset (original data research earns Tier 1 links; listicles don't).

**Problem:** Digital PR campaign produced press release but zero journalist pickup.
**Solution:** Press releases alone almost never generate editorial links in B2B. The issue is the distribution method, not the content. Instead: (1) identify 5-8 specific journalists who cover your topic at target publications, (2) send a personalized pitch email with your data headline and a 2-sentence "why your readers care" angle, (3) offer an exclusive first-publication window to your top target, (4) follow up once at 72 hours. Reporters at trade publications receive hundreds of releases weekly — only direct, personalized pitches with a strong data hook cut through.
