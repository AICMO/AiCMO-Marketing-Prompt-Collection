**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30-45 min | **Tags:** channel-sales, var, reseller, partner-activation, channel-marketing, b2b-saas, partner-sourced-pipeline, mdf, partner-enablement, indirect-sales

## Overview
Designs and executes a complete channel partner activation program for B2B SaaS companies that sell through Value-Added Resellers (VARs), managed service providers (MSPs), and independent software vendors (ISVs) — transforming a dormant reseller network into a proactive, measurable pipeline engine. Use this when you have signed reseller agreements but most partners are inactive, when you need to scale channel-sourced revenue without proportional headcount growth, or when you want to move from reactive partner management to a data-driven activation system where the right partners receive the right enablement, MDF investment, and co-sell support at precisely the right moment in the sales cycle.

COMPONENT 2 — TIER 1 PARTNER ONBOARDING & ACTIVATION PROGRAM

Design the 30-60-90 day activation sequence for newly-identified or newly-elevated Tier 1 partners:

DAY 0-5: EXECUTIVE ALIGNMENT KICKOFF

Joint Executive Kickoff Call Agenda (45 minutes, your VP Channel or CRO + partner principal):
- Minutes 0-5: Relationship framing — not a vendor pitch, a mutual revenue plan
  Opening: "The reason we wanted to get our leadership teams together today is that we've looked carefully at your customer base and we believe there's a $[X] revenue opportunity for [Partner Name] selling [Your Product] to the accounts you already have relationships with. We want to build a plan together to capture that — and we want to start with your business goals, not ours."
- Minutes 5-20: Partner business review — understand their business: top 5 verticals by revenue, ACV of their typical deal, how they make money (margin, services, referral fee), who sells in their organization and how they're compensated, what solutions they already prioritize
- Minutes 20-35: Mutual opportunity mapping — share the ICP account overlap analysis (see Component 3): "Here are [X] accounts you likely already have relationships with that match the profile of our best customers. These aren't cold targets — they're existing partners or clients of yours who are likely experiencing [specific pain]. We want to work with you to approach these accounts together."
- Minutes 35-45: Agreement on 90-day plan — commit to: (1) training completion timeline; (2) first joint pipeline review date; (3) first co-branded campaign launch; (4) deal registration target for 90-day period

DAY 5-20: PRODUCT & SALES ENABLEMENT

Certification Track (to be completed by all partner reps who will carry the product):

LEVEL 1 — PRODUCT AWARENESS CERTIFICATION (4 hours; all partner reps):
- Module 1 (1 hour): The problem your product solves — 3 real customer stories in the partner's target verticals; focus on business outcomes, not features
- Module 2 (1 hour): How to identify a qualified prospect in a conversation — the 3 qualification questions and what the "yes, this is an opportunity" answer sounds like
- Module 3 (1 hour): Demo environment walkthrough — basic product demonstration using the partner's most relevant use case; all reps should be able to walk a customer through a 10-minute product story
- Module 4 (1 hour): Deal registration process and margin structure — exactly how the partner makes money, how to register a deal, what happens after registration
- Certification test: 15-question multiple choice; must pass with ≥ 80%; partner reps who pass get added to co-sell overlay eligible list

LEVEL 2 — TECHNICAL SALES CERTIFICATION (8 hours; partner reps who will run discovery and demos):
- Module 1 (2 hours): Advanced discovery — how to run a discovery call that uncovers specific technical and business requirements your product addresses; 10 discovery questions with annotated ideal answers
- Module 2 (2 hours): Competitive positioning — how your product compares to the 3 most common alternatives partners encounter; what to say when prospects mention competitors; proof points and customer references by competitive scenario
- Module 3 (2 hours): Advanced demo skills — live demo of complex use cases; how to handle common demo objections; configuring the sandbox environment to match the prospect's specific stack
- Module 4 (2 hours): Proposal and commercial process — how to structure a proposal, what pricing information you can share and what requires CAM involvement, how to handle procurement, how to co-present with your AE when deal size warrants it
- Certification: Live role-play call with CAM (partner rep plays the discovery call with CAM playing a qualified prospect); pass = certified Level 2

DAY 20-45: FIRST PIPELINE GENERATION

ICP Account Mapping Session (2 hours, CAM + partner principal + partner sales lead):
- Input: Partner brings their customer/prospect list (or verbal list of top 30 relationships); you bring your ICP scoring model and intent data on those accounts
- Process: Match partner's known accounts against your TAL; score each against ICP criteria; identify the top 10 accounts where: (1) the partner has a named relationship; (2) the account matches your ICP; (3) there are buying signals (intent data, company growth signals, job postings, recent technology announcements)
- Output: Joint target account list of 10-25 accounts, ranked by priority; assign each account a "partner-led" (partner owns the relationship, drives the conversation) or "co-sell" (your AE joins the first discovery call) designation based on deal complexity and partner's certification level
- Deliverable: Send the partner a formatted Account Intelligence Brief for the top 10 accounts within 48 hours of the session — for each account, include: contact names, recent activity, ICP fit score, suggested opening talk track referencing their industry and known pain, and a draft LinkedIn message the partner rep can send immediately

First Deal Registration Sprint (Days 20-45):
Goal: Partner registers ≥ 1 qualified deal opportunity within 45 days of kickoff
Support structure:
- CAM sends a "Week 3 check-in" on Day 21: "How's outreach going on the account list we built? Have you had any conversations? I want to be available to join any calls where deal size warrants my involvement — even early-stage."
- If no deal registration by Day 30: CAM schedules a 30-minute "deal sourcing" call to review partner's pipeline and help them identify accounts already in discussion where your product is a fit — often partners have active accounts where your product is relevant but they haven't formalized the opportunity
- If no deal registration by Day 45: Flag partner for "co-sell lift" — CAM or AE proactively reaches out to 3 accounts on the joint target list with the partner copied ("I'm following up with you on behalf of our partner [Partner Name], who I know you have a relationship with...")

DAY 45-90: JOINT PIPELINE REVIEWS & PROGRAM GRADUATION

Monthly Pipeline Review Cadence (30 minutes):
- Minutes 0-10: Pipeline review — for each open deal, cover: stage, next action, where the partner needs support (technical questions, pricing, competitive objection, executive escalation)
- Minutes 10-20: New account sourcing — which accounts did the partner engage this month? What's the feedback from the market on your value proposition? Which messages are resonating?
- Minutes 20-30: MDF planning — what marketing activity will drive next quarter's pipeline? Allocate MDF budget to specific activities with pipeline commitment attached

90-Day Graduation Criteria (to confirm permanent Tier 1 status):
- ≥ 2 qualified deal registrations submitted
- Level 1 certification completed by 100% of partner sales team; Level 2 by ≥ 50%
- Monthly pipeline review attended ≥ 2 of 3 months
- ≥ 1 co-branded marketing activity launched (joint webinar, email campaign, or in-person event)
Partners who don't hit graduation criteria: Reviewed for Tier 2 placement with 60-day improvement plan before tier demotion


COMPONENT 3 — PARTNER DEMAND GENERATION & MDF PROGRAM

Build the co-marketing engine that lets partners generate their own pipeline without requiring heavy CAM time:

A. MDF (MARKET DEVELOPMENT FUND) ALLOCATION RULES:

MDF BUDGET STRUCTURE (designed to maximize pipeline ROI, not reward relationship quality):

Tier 1 Partners — Performance-Accelerated MDF:
- Base allocation: $5,000-$15,000/quarter per Tier 1 partner (calibrated to partner's revenue potential)
- Performance unlock: Additional $5,000 MDF unlocked upon proof of ≥ 3 deal registrations in the prior quarter
- Executive investment: Up to $10,000/year in co-funded executive events (roundtables, advisory dinners) for Tier 1 partners with proven pipeline generation

Tier 2 Partners — Milestone-Gated MDF:
- No base allocation — partners earn MDF by demonstrating engagement and pipeline activity
- Milestone 1: Level 1 certification completed + 1 deal registration → unlock $1,500 MDF (valid for 90 days)
- Milestone 2: Second deal registration → unlock additional $1,500 MDF + eligible for co-branded campaign participation
- Milestone 3: First deal closes → unlock $2,500 MDF investment in a joint marketing event or campaign

Tier 3 / New Partners:
- No MDF until first deal registration is submitted and validated as qualified
- Qualification for MDF: Deal must be in an ICP account, ACV ≥ your minimum deal threshold, with a named champion who has confirmed the problem exists

MDF ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES (ranked by pipeline generation ROI):

HIGH ROI (preferred activities; process MDF requests within 5 business days):
1. Co-hosted virtual webinar or in-person roundtable (target: 15+ qualified attendees from partner's customer base; cost: $2,000-$8,000 depending on format)
2. Joint email campaign to partner's verified customer list (partner sends co-branded emails to their CRM segment matching your ICP; cost: content creation support + $500 co-branded design asset)
3. ICP vertical trade show or user group sponsorship (partner sponsors an event their customers attend; your logo is co-displayed; cost: shared 50/50 up to MDF cap)
4. Partner-sponsored ABM advertising campaign (partner runs LinkedIn or programmatic ads to their target account list with your co-branded content; cost: ad spend eligible for MDF match)

LOWER ROI (eligible but require justification):
5. Partner sales training event (team sales kickoff or product deep-dive with partner's sales team; cost: travel + materials)
6. Co-branded content creation (white paper, case study, solution brief for a specific vertical; eligible only if distributed to ≥ 250 qualified contacts within 60 days)

NOT ELIGIBLE: Generic brand awareness activities with no pipeline conversion mechanism; partner's internal operations costs; activities with no named contact list or measurable pipeline metric

MDF GOVERNANCE:
- MDF request process: Partner submits request via PRM portal (or email if no PRM) with: activity type, target audience (ICP account list), expected pipeline outcome (meetings or deal registrations), and budget breakdown
- CAM approval: CAM has 5 business days to approve, request modification, or decline; approval criteria = does this activity have a pipeline measurement mechanism?
- Proof of performance: Partner provides post-activity report within 30 days: attendee list, meeting requests generated, deal registrations attributed to activity
- Reimbursement: 100% of approved spend reimbursed upon proof of performance; 50% advance available for activities over $3,000

B. CO-BRANDED CAMPAIGN LIBRARY:

Build 4-6 plug-and-play campaigns that partners can launch with minimal effort:

CAMPAIGN TYPE 1 — "THE VERTICAL BENCHMARK" (Highest ROI; quarterly launch):
Format: Partner-branded email + landing page (your content, partner's logo and sending domain) presenting proprietary benchmark data from your customer base for the partner's target vertical
Execution: You create the benchmark report (pull anonymized metrics from your customer data in the partner's primary vertical — e.g., "2025 IT Service Management Benchmark: 127 Mid-Market Healthcare IT Teams"); partner sends to their customer list with their brand on it
Subject line templates:
- "[Partner Company] + [Your Company] Research: How Healthcare IT Teams Are Solving [Top Pain Point]"
- "New Benchmark Data: How Your [Vertical] Peers Are Achieving [Outcome] with [Your Product Category]"
CTA: Download the benchmark report → partner co-hosted webinar to discuss findings → demo request
Pipeline expectation: 2-4 qualified meetings per 200 contacts emailed; benchmark reports outperform promotional emails 4-6x

CAMPAIGN TYPE 2 — "THE CUSTOMER SUCCESS SPOTLIGHT" (Running monthly; low effort):
Format: Partner sends a 2-paragraph case study email featuring a mutual customer (or your direct customer in the partner's vertical) to their prospect list
Execution: You provide the case study content (already written for your vertical library); partner adds a 1-sentence personal intro ("I've been recommending [Your Company] to clients in [vertical] for the past year — here's why"); partner sends from their email address
Subject line: "How [Customer Name] Achieved [Specific Outcome] — Relevant to Your [Vertical] Environment"
CTA: Book a 20-minute conversation with the partner to discuss whether this applies to their environment
Pipeline expectation: 0.5-1.5 qualified meetings per 100 contacts (lower volume, higher intent — case study readers who book are typically further along in evaluation)

CAMPAIGN TYPE 3 — "THE PROBLEM DEFINITION WEBINAR" (Quarterly; highest partner engagement):
Format: 45-minute co-hosted webinar — partner presents for 15 minutes on the industry-specific context; you present for 20 minutes on how the problem is solved; 10 minutes Q&A
Topic selection: The webinar should address a problem your ICP experiences that the partner encounters in their services work (e.g., for an MSP partner: "Why Mid-Market IT Teams Are Losing 3 Hours/Day to Manual Change Requests — And What Actually Fixes It")
Partner's role: Host invitation, moderate Q&A, handle post-event follow-up with their relationship contacts; the partner's credibility with their audience is the primary conversion lever
Your role: Provide slide deck, demo environment, speaker prep, registration landing page, post-event email sequence for joint follow-up
Pipeline expectation: 4-10 qualified meetings per webinar when partner invites ≥ 50 of their customers/prospects; net new pipeline per webinar averages $120,000-$280,000 for mid-ACV products

CAMPAIGN TYPE 4 — "THE TECHNOLOGY MIGRATION ALERT" (Event-triggered; high urgency):
Format: Partner sends a time-sensitive alert email when a relevant industry event creates urgency (vendor end-of-life, regulatory change, competitor pricing increase, industry audit requirement)
Execution: You monitor for trigger events; when one occurs, you produce a "migration brief" within 5 business days; partners in relevant verticals send the brief to their accounts
Example trigger: "[Legacy Competitor Product] announces end-of-life in 18 months — what IT Directors should do now"
Subject line: "Important: [Trigger Event] — What [Vertical] IT Teams Need to Know"
Pipeline expectation: Highest urgency-driven conversion of any campaign type; qualified meetings often book within 48-72 hours of send; expect 3-8 meetings per 100 contacts in affected accounts

COMPONENT 4 — PARTNER HEALTH SCORING & PROACTIVE INTERVENTION SYSTEM

Build a real-time system that detects partner disengagement before it becomes partner churn:

A. PARTNER HEALTH SCORE (updated weekly; powered by CRM + PRM data):

HEALTH SIGNAL CATEGORY 1 — PIPELINE ACTIVITY (0-35 points):
- Deal registrations in last 90 days: ≥ 3 = 35 pts | 2 = 25 pts | 1 = 15 pts | 0 = 0 pts
- Pipeline velocity (deals moving stage): ≥ 1 deal advanced in last 30 days = 35 pts | Stalled (no stage movement) = 10 pts | No open pipeline = 0 pts
- Deal size trend: ACV trending up QoQ = 35 pts | Flat = 20 pts | Declining = 5 pts

HEALTH SIGNAL CATEGORY 2 — ENGAGEMENT ACTIVITY (0-30 points):
- PRM/portal activity in last 30 days: Weekly logins + content downloads = 30 pts | 1-3 logins = 15 pts | No logins = 0 pts
- CAM communication response rate (last 3 outreach attempts): 3/3 responded within 2 days = 30 pts | 2/3 = 20 pts | 1/3 = 8 pts | 0/3 = 0 pts
- Pipeline review participation last 60 days: Attended both scheduled calls = 30 pts | Attended 1 = 15 pts | Skipped both = 0 pts
- Campaign launch activity: Launched ≥ 1 co-branded campaign in last 90 days = 30 pts | None = 0 pts

HEALTH SIGNAL CATEGORY 3 — ENABLEMENT COMPLETION (0-20 points):
- Certification completion rate (% of partner reps who should be certified, based on role): 100% = 20 pts | 75-99% = 15 pts | 50-74% = 8 pts | < 50% = 0 pts
- Product knowledge quiz pass rate among certified reps: ≥ 90% average score = 20 pts | 75-89% = 12 pts | < 75% = 4 pts

HEALTH SIGNAL CATEGORY 4 — STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT (0-15 points):
- Executive relationship temperature: Partner principal proactively engages with your CAM or VP Channel = 15 pts | Responsive but passive = 8 pts | Relationship is only with partner sales reps = 3 pts | No named executive contact = 0 pts
- Competitor activity: No evidence of partner actively selling competitor = 15 pts | Carries competitor but does not lead with them = 8 pts | Actively selling competitor first = 0 pts

HEALTH SCORE BANDS & INTERVENTION PROTOCOLS:

GREEN (70-100): Healthy, performing partner
Action: Standard CAM cadence (monthly pipeline review); proactive MDF campaign planning; quarterly strategic review; thank-you communication from VP Channel at quarterly milestone

YELLOW (45-69): Declining engagement; intervention required within 10 days
Trigger: Score drops from Green to Yellow, OR any single category drops to 0 for 2 consecutive reporting periods
Action: CAM schedules "partnership check-in" (frame as listening call, not performance review): "I wanted to reach out because I want to make sure we're being as useful as possible for your team — do you have 20 minutes to talk about what's working and what isn't?" Goal: Identify root cause (rep turnover, competitive pressure, market shift, CAM responsiveness issue) and co-create a 30-day recovery plan

RED (20-44): Critical intervention required within 5 days
Trigger: Score drops below 45, OR 0 deal registrations in 90 days AND no response to last 2 CAM outreach attempts
Action: VP Channel personally calls partner principal (not just CAM outreach); agenda is honest: "We want to understand if this partnership is still a fit for both organizations. We value the relationship but want to make sure we're investing time on both sides where there's genuine mutual opportunity."
Decision tree after VP Channel call:
- Partner confirms commitment + root cause identified → 60-day recovery plan with specific milestones
- Partner is noncommittal → Place on 90-day probation; reduce MDF allocation; move to Tier 3 management
- Partner says the opportunity isn't right for their business → Thank them, document the feedback, formally sunset the agreement

GRAY (0-19 or 0 activity in 6+ months): Sunset candidate
Action: CAM sends formal "partnership review" email — "We haven't had activity in [X months] and we want to make sure we're both getting value from this relationship. Happy to hop on a call to discuss, but wanted to reach out first to check in." If no response within 14 days → send formal notice of agreement sunset; clean the record in CRM and PRM

COMPONENT 5 — DEAL REGISTRATION ARCHITECTURE & CHANNEL CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Design the rules and systems that make deal registration frictionless for partners and fair for your direct team:

A. DEAL REGISTRATION RULES:

WHAT QUALIFIES FOR REGISTRATION:
- Named account contact: Must have a named buyer contact with title and direct email (not just company name)
- Evidence of opportunity: Partner must document that they have had a conversation with the prospect about your product and there is confirmed need or interest — not a speculative entry
- No existing open opportunity: CRM must confirm that your direct team does not have an existing open opportunity with this account (see conflict resolution protocol below)
- ICP qualification: Account must meet minimum ICP criteria (industry, company size, buyer title)
- Freshness window: Registered account cannot have had any direct sales activity from your team in the last 90 days

REGISTRATION PROCESS (partner-facing):
Submit via PRM portal form (or email to channel-deals@[yourcompany].com if no PRM):
- Account name, website, and headquarters location
- Named contact: Full name, title, direct email
- Opportunity description: 3-sentence description of the prospect's problem and why they're interested in your product
- Estimated ACV: Partner's estimate based on prospect's conversation
- Estimated close date: Partner's estimate
- Deal type: Net new / Expansion of existing customer / Competitive displacement

CAM reviews and approves/declines within 24 hours (business hours):
- Approved: Partner notified, deal tagged in CRM as "Partner Registered," partner receives confirmation with deal ID and next steps
- Declined (duplicate): If your direct team has an existing opportunity → Conflict resolution protocol activated (see below)
- Declined (unqualified): CAM provides specific feedback ("The contact title doesn't meet our ICP minimum — do you have a VP IT or IT Director contact at this account? If so, submit with that contact and I'll approve immediately")

Deal registration protection period: 90 days from registration date; if deal does not progress to "Technical Evaluation" stage within 90 days, partner must re-register to extend protection

B. CHANNEL CONFLICT RESOLUTION PROTOCOL:

SCENARIO 1 — Partner registers an account that is already in your direct pipeline:
Rule: Direct team retains ownership; partner is notified with specific reason ("Our team already has an active opportunity with [Account Name] — we want to make sure we don't create confusion in the account. Our AE will let you know if there's a role for you in the deal.")
Compensation: If the direct deal closes AND the partner can demonstrate they influenced the opportunity (introduced a new stakeholder, provided competitive intelligence, co-presented), offer a partial referral fee (negotiate case-by-case; typically 3-5% of ACV for material influence)

SCENARIO 2 — Partner registers an account your team had in pipeline 90+ days ago but has since gone dark:
Rule: Partner registration wins; assign the opportunity to co-sell model (partner leads, your AE supports); your team should not cold-outbound to the same account during this period
Rationale: If your direct team couldn't progress the account in 90 days and the partner has a fresh relationship, the partner-led approach is more likely to close the deal

SCENARIO 3 — Partner and direct team independently develop the same account simultaneously (detected within 30 days):
Rule: Joint review call with CAM, AE, and partner sales lead within 48 hours of detection; determine who has the stronger relationship and primary point of contact at the account → that relationship leads; the other team supports
Compensation: Co-sell credit split — if partner leads, partner earns full reseller margin; if direct leads with partner support, partner earns a co-sell fee (typically 8-12% of ACV for documented co-sell involvement)

CHANNEL CONFLICT HEALTH METRIC:
Track: Number of deal registration conflicts per quarter; target < 8% conflict rate on registrations (more than this signals your direct prospecting is overlapping with partner territory — requires territory design review)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Tier 1 partners complete onboarding and the ICP account mapping session, show enthusiasm, but never submit deal registrations — the activation energy dissipates after 45 days without a deal**
Diagnosis: The gap between "this looks promising" and "I'm submitting a formal deal registration" is almost always one of three things: (1) the partner sales rep doesn't have a live prospect conversation yet and is waiting for a perfect situation rather than practicing the qualification story with existing accounts; (2) the deal registration process itself feels bureaucratic or risky (partner worries about channel conflict if they register and your team has already touched the account); or (3) the partner's comp plan doesn't yet reward effort spent on your product — selling your product takes them away from their primary revenue line.
Fix: Three parallel interventions. First, lower the registration threshold: Tell partners explicitly "Register any conversation where you've discussed IT asset management with a named contact — even if it's early stage. We'd rather see a registration and help you qualify it down than have a deal fall through because it wasn't registered." Second, do a "partner sales ride-along" — join 2-3 of the partner's existing sales calls (with permission) and coach them in real time on where to introduce your product in conversations they're already having. Third, clarify the CAM's role as a revenue tool, not a compliance function: "When you register a deal, I'm your co-pilot, not your oversight. My job is to help you close that deal."

**Problem: Your channel team is generating deal registrations from Tier 1 partners, but direct sales AEs are going around the deal registration system and prospecting the same accounts, creating channel conflict and damaging partner trust**
Diagnosis: Direct AEs have quota pressure and personal commission incentives that make every account look like a direct opportunity; the deal registration system hasn't been integrated into their daily workflow in a way that makes checking for partner registrations as natural as checking for existing opportunities; and the consequences for violating channel conflict policy haven't been made explicit or consistent.
Fix: This requires a CRO-level decision, not just a channel program fix. The technical solution is mandatory: configure Salesforce/HubSpot so that when an AE creates a new opportunity, an automated check runs against the registered partner deal database — if there's a match, the AE gets a hard-stop warning ("This account has an active partner deal registration from [Partner Name]. Contact your CAM before proceeding. Proceeding without CAM approval will be flagged for review.") The management solution: make channel conflict violations a documented performance issue, not an informal correction. One or two AEs bypassing partner deals can poison an entire Tier 1 partner relationship permanently. The VP Channel should escalate the first confirmed violation to the CRO immediately, not as a complaint but as a data point: "We need to decide how seriously we're treating this policy, because I have a partner principal who is asking me directly whether this is going to happen again."

**Problem: MDF budget is being spent on partner events and campaigns, but there's no traceable pipeline from MDF investments — partners are taking the money and running general brand activities with no pipeline conversion mechanism**
Diagnosis: MDF was approved based on activity descriptions rather than pipeline conversion plans; partners submitted and received approval for activities like "sponsoring our local tech meetup" or "creating a co-branded overview brochure" without any named account list, follow-up sequence, or measurement mechanism built in; the MDF approval process is treating MDF as partner goodwill investment rather than demand generation investment.
