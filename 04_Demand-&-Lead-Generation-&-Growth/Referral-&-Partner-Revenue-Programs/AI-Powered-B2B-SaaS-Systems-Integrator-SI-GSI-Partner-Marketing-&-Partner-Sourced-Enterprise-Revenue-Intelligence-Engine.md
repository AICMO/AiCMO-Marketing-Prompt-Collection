**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** b2b, enterprise, partner-marketing, channel, revenue-operations, gtm, sales-enablement

## Overview

This prompt builds an AI-powered systems integrator (SI) and global systems integrator (GSI) partner marketing program — including partner tier architecture, joint go-to-market (JGTM) motions, SI practice enablement, co-selling playbooks, and partner-sourced pipeline attribution — enabling B2B SaaS companies to generate significant ARR through Accenture, Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, regional SIs, and boutique consultancies without relying solely on direct sales. Use it when launching or scaling an SI channel that needs to move beyond informal referrals into a structured, measurable, AI-orchestrated revenue program.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: SI partners register deals but never progress them past Stage 1**
Solution: Implement a "deal health" check at 30 days — if no Stage 2 activity (discovery call completed), CAM schedules joint call within 1 week. Add "First Contact Date" and "Last Activity Date" fields to deal registration. Kill registrations with no activity at 60 days and reopen the account for direct or other partners. Root cause is usually lack of SI internal sponsorship — require the SI Practice Lead (not just an individual contributor) to co-sign deal registrations for deals >$100K.

**Problem: SI partners request MDF but activities don't generate pipeline**
Solution: Require a pre-activity pipeline projection to unlock MDF (e.g., "this webinar targets 200 registrants, we project 12 qualified conversations = $600K pipeline at 2x ACV"). Withhold 25% of MDF until post-activity lead list is delivered and minimum opportunity count is created. Shift MDF toward activities with proven conversion rates (executive dinners and roundtables consistently outperform general webinars for SI-led demand generation).

**Problem: Direct sales team doesn't co-sell effectively with SI partners — treats them as competition**
Solution: This is a culture and incentive problem. Fix: (1) AEs earn full quota credit on partner-sourced deals — no split. (2) Make "partner co-sell activity" a visible metric in quarterly sales reviews. (3) Share partner win rate data (partner deals close 15–20% more often) with sales team. (4) Create a "Partner Champion" program — 2–3 AEs per region who become the go-to partner co-sellers, earn additional SPIFs, and model behavior for the rest of the team.
