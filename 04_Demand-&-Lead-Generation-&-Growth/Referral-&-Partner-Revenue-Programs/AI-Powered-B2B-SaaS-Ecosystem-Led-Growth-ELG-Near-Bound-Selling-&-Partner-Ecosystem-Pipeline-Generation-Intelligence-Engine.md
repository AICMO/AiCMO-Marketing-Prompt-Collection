## Overview
Designs and executes a complete Ecosystem-Led Growth (ELG) motion — a systematic approach to building pipeline by leveraging your integration partner network to identify warm accounts, generate near-bound selling opportunities, and accelerate deals through partner relationship context. Use this when you want to transform your 20-200 technology integration partnerships from passive referral relationships into a proactive, data-driven pipeline engine where partner ecosystem overlap becomes your primary intelligence signal for identifying in-market accounts.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Tier 1 integration partners agree to connect on Crossbeam but their partnership team never follows through on warm intro requests, making the near-bound motion theoretical rather than operational**
Diagnosis: The partner team values the account mapping data but doesn't have incentive, process, or management buy-in to spend CSM/AE time making warm introductions to accounts; the ELG motion feels one-sided to them
Fix: Shift from asking for favors to creating a structured reciprocal exchange. In the next co-sell sync, present the reciprocity data: "In the last 30 days, we've sent you [X] warm intro requests. We've fulfilled [Y] intros for your team. What would make it easier for your CSMs to send the intros?" Often the answer is: (1) The intro email draft is too long or formal — make it 3 sentences and easier to forward; (2) Partner CSMs aren't sure what's in it for them — establish a CSM spiff or team recognition for fulfilled intros; (3) The accounts you're requesting intros for aren't real customers of theirs — validate Crossbeam data and confirm the CSM actually has a relationship with the contact before requesting. Additionally, change your request channel: Slack direct message to the specific CSM (not the partner manager) converts at 2x the rate of a general email request.

**Problem: Near-bound outreach sequences are getting replies but not booking meetings — prospects acknowledge the partner context but don't engage with your product**
Diagnosis: The partner context is getting attention but the value proposition isn't differentiated enough to justify a meeting — "we integrate with [Partner]" is table stakes if many competitors also integrate with the same tools; or the timing is wrong (prospect is still in early adoption of the partner's product and hasn't hit the workflow pain your product solves yet)
Fix: Shift the near-bound messaging from "we integrate with [Partner]" to "here's the specific workflow problem that [Partner] customers hit at 60-90 days of usage and here's how we solve it." Get specific on the pain trigger: interview 5-10 existing customers who use the partner and ask "when did you realize you needed [Your Product] alongside [Partner Product]?" Their answer is your sequence subject line. Also audit the timing of your signal trigger sequences: if you're reaching out within 1-2 days of the partner signal, the prospect may not yet have experienced the pain. Consider a 30-day delay from "new partner customer" signal to first ELG outreach touch — especially for Tier 2 signals — to let the pain develop before you offer the solution.

**Problem: Crossbeam shows significant near-bound overlap but your SDR team isn't prioritizing near-bound accounts over their regular outbound lists — ELG accounts sit in the queue without outreach**
Diagnosis: SDRs have existing quota systems and account assignment methods that don't weight near-bound accounts differently from cold accounts; without a clear incentive and process, ELG accounts compete with cold accounts for SDR attention and often lose
Fix: Create a dedicated ELG SDR designation — even if it's one SDR spending 30% of their time on ELG accounts, the focus dramatically increases follow-through. If you can't dedicate headcount, build the priority signal directly into the SDR's CRM view: configure the SDR's default Salesforce/HubSpot Task queue to sort accounts with Near_Bound_Score ≥ 50 to the top by default. Pair this with a weekly ELG accountability check-in (10 minutes in your weekly SDR standup): "How many near-bound accounts did we contact this week? How many meetings booked? What warm intros are pending?" Making the near-bound motion visible in team rhythm increases SDR prioritization without requiring a formal quota change.
