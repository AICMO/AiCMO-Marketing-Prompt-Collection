## Overview
This prompt architects an integration partner referral program for B2B SaaS companies — turning the companies that build native integrations with your product into a structured, tracked referral revenue channel that generates mutual customer referrals at scale, fully automated from integration launch to ecosystem-sourced pipeline attribution.

## Integration Tips

- **Crossbeam / Reveal:** Use ecosystem intelligence platforms to run automated account mapping with each integration partner. Set up alerts when a partner's customer appears in your pipeline (or vice versa) to trigger the co-sell motion automatically.
- **PartnerStack / Impact.com:** Use a PRM platform to track partner-sourced leads, issue unique referral links per partner, manage tier upgrades, and run the reactivation sequence automatically via built-in email workflows.
- **HubSpot CRM:** Tag all partner-sourced contacts with a custom property `Partner_Source` and create a pipeline view filtered to partner-referred deals. Set up a workflow: if `Partner_Source` is filled AND deal stage reaches "Demo Booked," notify the partner manager via Slack.
- **Salesforce:** Use the Partner Community module or a PRM overlay like Alliances or Impartner to manage deal registration and co-sell workflow. Create a custom Opportunity field `Ecosystem_Partner` for attribution reporting.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate the Day 1 partner onboarding sequence by connecting your integration marketplace (HubSpot App Marketplace, Zapier) to your CRM + email platform. When a partner publishes an integration, trigger the 4-step activation sequence automatically.
- **Slack:** Create a private Slack channel shared with each Tier 3 partner's team for real-time co-sell coordination. Use a Slack bot to send co-sell alerts when a new mutual account appears in Crossbeam.
- **Google Sheets + Looker Studio:** Build a shared partnership dashboard that pulls from CRM data to show each partner their referral performance in real time without requiring CRM access.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Integration partners "list" your product but never actively refer customers — the program is passive.**
Solution: Passive listing only works if your integration marketplace has enough organic traffic to generate discovery. For active referrals, you need to design the "in-app moment" — the exact point inside the partner's product where their user would naturally be introduced to yours. Work with the partner's product team to identify the right in-app prompt (e.g., "Need to manage the projects coming from this HubSpot deal? Try Notionflow →"). This is the difference between a listing and a native co-sell embedded in product UX.

**Problem: Co-sell deals take forever because coordinating two sales teams adds complexity and slows the cycle.**
Solution: Pre-agree on the co-sell protocol before any deal is live. Define: who leads the call (the company whose product is already bought by the prospect), who handles the proposal (joint doc with templated sections), and what counts as "co-sell" vs. "just a referral." Without a documented SLA between sales teams, co-sell dies in coordination overhead. Use a shared Slack channel and a standard 3-line deal summary format to keep co-sell sync to <5 minutes.

**Problem: Attribution disputes — both your CRM and the partner's CRM claim the same customer as "partner-sourced."**
Solution: Establish a pre-agreed attribution arbitration rule in writing before the program launches: "The company whose unique referral link or UTM tag appears FIRST in the prospect's contact record wins the attribution credit." Use Crossbeam's overlap reports to pre-map which accounts are "yours," "theirs," or "mutual" before any deal starts — this eliminates most disputes. For truly contested deals, default to "co-source" designation and share the co-marketing win publicly.
