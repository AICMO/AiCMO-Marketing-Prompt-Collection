# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Strategic Alliance Co-Marketing & Complementary-Vendor Pipeline Partnership Revenue Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30-40 min | **Tags:** strategic-alliances, co-marketing, partner-revenue, b2b-saas, pipeline-generation, joint-gtm, complementary-vendors, partner-sourced-pipeline, co-sell, demand-generation

## Overview
Designs and activates a systematic strategic alliance program between two complementary, non-competing B2B SaaS companies — enabling joint demand generation, co-marketing campaigns, and mutual pipeline creation that generates alliance-sourced deals with 60-80% lower CAC and 40%+ higher win rates than cold outbound. Use this when you want to build a repeatable co-marketing engine with 2-8 strategic partners who share your ICP but don't compete for the same product category.

OBJECTIVE:
Design a complete, launch-ready B2B SaaS strategic alliance co-marketing program across eight components:

COMPONENT 1 — STRATEGIC ALLIANCE PARTNER IDENTIFICATION & SCORING FRAMEWORK

Define the Partner Fit Matrix to evaluate and rank all potential strategic alliance candidates:

A. MUST-HAVE CRITERIA (eliminate any partner who fails these):
- Non-competitive: Zero product category overlap — you must be able to recommend them without cannibalizing your own deal or theirs
- ICP Congruence: Shared buyer persona (same job title, company size, industry segment) at ≥ 70% overlap
- Market Presence: Partner has ≥ 500 customers in your ICP segment OR ≥ 15,000 LinkedIn followers from your buyer persona
- Marketing Capacity: Partner has a dedicated marketing team of ≥ 3 people and has run webinars or joint content in the last 12 months
- Deal Proximity: Both companies appear together in real customer tech stacks (validate via G2 "Used With" data, BuiltWith, or customer interviews)

B. SCORING CRITERIA (0-100 point fit score):

STRATEGIC VALUE (35 points):
- ICP overlap percentage (use LinkedIn Sales Navigator audience analysis or customer survey data): 90-100% overlap = 15 pts | 70-89% = 10 pts | 50-69% = 5 pts
- Complementary product usage sequence (do customers use both products in the same workflow?): Sequential workflow = 12 pts | Parallel usage = 7 pts | Adjacent category = 3 pts
- Partner's market credibility (G2 category leader, analyst recognition, press): Category leader = 8 pts | Recognized = 5 pts | Emerging = 2 pts

PIPELINE POTENTIAL (35 points):
- Partner customer base accessible to your ICP: > 5,000 ICP accounts = 20 pts | 1,000-5,000 = 13 pts | 500-1,000 = 7 pts
- Partner average deal size alignment with your ACV (within 2x): Within 1x = 15 pts | 1-2x = 8 pts | > 2x difference = 3 pts

EXECUTION READINESS (30 points):
- Partner has active co-marketing program with other vendors (signals internal commitment): Yes, ≥ 3 active partners = 15 pts | 1-2 partners = 8 pts | None = 2 pts
- Partner marketing team size and recent activity: ≥ 5 marketers with webinar history = 10 pts | 3-4 marketers = 6 pts | 1-2 = 2 pts
- Executive relationship existing (founder, CMO, VP Partnerships knows someone at your company): Yes = 5 pts | Introduction possible = 3 pts | Cold = 0 pts

PARTNER TIER ASSIGNMENT (use score to set investment level):
- Tier 1 Strategic Partners (Score 75-100): Full co-marketing investment — 4+ joint campaigns per year, executive sponsorship, dedicated partner pages, mutual SDR enablement
- Tier 2 Growth Partners (Score 55-74): Moderate co-marketing — 2 joint campaigns per year, blog content, webinar, joint newsletter
- Tier 3 Exploration Partners (Score 35-54): Lightweight engagement — one joint piece of content, test for ICP validation before deeper investment

COMPONENT 2 — ALLIANCE VALUE PROPOSITION & JOINT MESSAGING ARCHITECTURE

Develop the co-marketing narrative that makes both companies more compelling together than apart:

A. COMBINED VALUE STORY FRAMEWORK (the "1+1=3" narrative):
Build a joint value proposition using the following structure:
- Individual Value: What your product does for the buyer + What their product does for the buyer
- Integration Point: The specific workflow, data connection, or use case where both products work together (even without a technical integration, the workflow connection is the story)
- Combined Outcome: The measurable result the buyer achieves using both tools together that neither delivers alone
- Proof: One customer who uses both products and can articulate the combined value (this is the co-marketing anchor case study)

EXAMPLE COMBINED VALUE STORY TEMPLATE:
"[Partner Company] helps [ICP] [what they do]. [Your Company] helps the same [ICP] [what you do]. Together, [ICP] can [specific combined workflow outcome] — resulting in [specific measurable result: e.g., '47% more accurate Q3 forecasts with 60% less manual data work'] that neither tool delivers independently. [Joint customer name] uses both to [specific workflow]."

B. JOINT BUYER JOURNEY MAPPING:
Map where both products appear in the buyer's workflow and which campaign formats reach the buyer at each stage:
- Problem Awareness (Top-of-Funnel): Joint thought leadership content, co-authored research, co-branded podcast appearance — speak to the problem both products solve
- Solution Evaluation (Mid-Funnel): Joint webinar demonstrating the combined workflow, co-hosted demo day, comparative blog post — show how both products work together
- Decision Acceleration (Bottom-of-Funnel): Joint case study featuring a shared customer, mutual customer references, co-designed ROI calculator — prove combined ROI

C. JOINT CONTENT ASSET LIBRARY (build these before launching campaigns):
Priority 1: One joint case study featuring a shared customer (highest trust signal)
Priority 2: Co-authored "State of [Category]" industry research report (both audiences, both logos)
Priority 3: A 10-minute "Better Together" video walkthrough showing both products in one workflow
Priority 4: Mutual customer testimonial bank (5+ quotes from customers who use both)

COMPONENT 3 — CO-MARKETING CAMPAIGN PORTFOLIO (6 FORMATS, RANKED BY PIPELINE YIELD)

Rank campaigns by pipeline generation per dollar and hour invested:

FORMAT 1 — JOINT WEBINAR (highest pipeline yield for effort: target 40-80 net-new qualified attendees per webinar)
Structure: 45-min live session, both company presenters (1 each), 15-min Q&A
Topic formula: "[Shared Problem]: How [ICP title] at [Company Stage] Are Solving It with [Category 1] + [Category 2]"
Pipeline mechanics: Both companies invite their full ICP contact databases → each attendee is a warm lead for the other company → post-webinar follow-up sequence for both companies' SDRs simultaneously
Attribution: Any attendee from Partner's database who didn't know your company = net-new Partner-Sourced lead; vice versa
Target frequency: Quarterly per Tier 1 partner
Production split: Alternate who hosts (owns the Zoom logistics, landing page, recording) — each partner promotes equally
Cost: $0-$2,000 per webinar (primarily time investment)
Expected pipeline per webinar: 3-8 qualified meetings per company if ICP attendance ≥ 50 relevant titles

FORMAT 2 — CO-AUTHORED RESEARCH REPORT (highest brand authority + top-of-funnel reach)
Structure: 8-12 page original research report based on joint survey data (200-500 respondents from shared ICP)
Topic formula: "The [Year] State of [Shared Category/Problem]: How [ICP] Companies Are [Trend]"
Pipeline mechanics: Gated report generates email leads for both companies; ungated sections drive SEO for both; report becomes anchor asset for 6+ months of derivative content
Attribution: Track download source (your database vs. partner database vs. organic) and enrich with intent signals
Target frequency: Once per year per Tier 1 partner
Cost: $3,000-$8,000 (survey platform + design + joint promotion)
Expected pipeline: 50-200 net-new email captures per partner; 5-12 demo requests over 90 days

FORMAT 3 — JOINT CUSTOMER CASE STUDY (highest bottom-funnel conversion)
Structure: 800-1,200 word written case study + 90-second video testimonial featuring a shared customer
Focus: "How [Customer Company] Used [Your Product] + [Partner Product] to Achieve [Specific Outcome]"
Pipeline mechanics: Both companies use as sales collateral for deals where prospect uses the other company's product; embed in mutual "Resources" sections on each company's website; LinkedIn posts by both companies drive inbound interest
Attribution: Track case study views and downloads from each company's website, attribute closes where this asset appeared in the deal
Target frequency: 1-2 per Tier 1 partner per year (limited by customer willingness)
Cost: $1,500-$3,000 (interviews, writing, design, video production)
Expected pipeline: 2-5x higher win rate when shared in active deals vs. no co-branded proof

FORMAT 4 — MUTUAL INTEGRATION LAUNCH OR WORKFLOW SPOTLIGHT
Structure: Even without a deep technical integration, a documented "workflow recipe" showing how customers use both products is a high-value asset
Content: Step-by-step workflow guide ("How to Connect [Your Product] + [Partner Product] for [Specific Use Case]") published on both company blogs, both app marketplaces, and both product documentation sites
Pipeline mechanics: Captures high-intent searches from customers already using one product who are evaluating the other; strong for SEO and product-led referral traffic
Cost: $500-$1,500 (primarily copywriting and design)
Expected pipeline: 3-10 inbound inquiries/month from partner's customer base once published and indexed

FORMAT 5 — CO-SPONSORED EXECUTIVE ROUNDTABLE OR DINNER
Structure: 15-25 invite-only ICP executives at a curated dinner or in-person workshop; both companies co-host
Topic: Not a product demo — a peer exchange on a strategic challenge both products address
Pipeline mechanics: High-trust environment for relationship building; post-event follow-up from both companies' AEs; attendees are pre-qualified ICP contacts who agreed to attend
Attribution: Track all attendees in both CRMs as "Alliance Event" source; measure meetings booked within 60 days
Target frequency: 2x per year per Tier 1 partner (tied to major industry conferences or regional clusters)
Cost: $4,000-$12,000 (venue, catering, logistics — split equally)
Expected pipeline: 4-10 qualified meetings per company per event; 1-3 closed deals within 90 days

FORMAT 6 — CROSS-NEWSLETTER SPONSORSHIP OR AUDIENCE SWAP
Structure: Each company features the other in their customer/prospect newsletter as a "Partner Spotlight" or "Tool We Recommend" section
Pipeline mechanics: Zero-cost audience access to the other company's subscriber base; highest quality when the recommending company has personally vetted the partner (not just a paid placement)
Attribution: Use UTM-tagged links and a dedicated landing page to track clicks and conversions from each partner's audience
Target frequency: Quarterly per active partner
Cost: $0 (audience swap) or $500-$2,000 (if one partner has significantly larger list)
Expected pipeline: 0.5-3% click-through → 10-30% landing page conversion → 3-15 net-new qualified contacts per newsletter feature

COMPONENT 4 — PARTNER OUTREACH & ALLIANCE AGREEMENT ARCHITECTURE

Design the end-to-end process from first contact to signed alliance agreement:

PHASE 1 — WARM OUTREACH SEQUENCE (4 touches, 18 days)

TOUCH 1 — LINKEDIN CONNECTION + COMMENT (Day 1)
Action: Connect with VP of Partnerships OR CMO of target partner on LinkedIn; leave a substantive comment on their most recent post demonstrating you understand their market
Goal: Get on their radar before the cold email

TOUCH 2 — PERSONALIZED EMAIL PITCH (Day 3, send after LinkedIn connection accepted)
Subject: "[Your Company] + [Partner Company]: shared customers, shared pipeline opportunity?"
Structure:
- Open with specificity: Mention 1-2 mutual customers you know use both products ("We have 14 customers who use [Partner Product] alongside [Your Product] — including [Customer Name if shareable]")
- Pitch the mutual value: "I think there's a genuine co-marketing opportunity here. Our ICPs are identical — [shared buyer title] at [shared company size/stage]. Our products solve adjacent problems, so our customers often ask us about each other."
- Propose a single, low-commitment first step: "Would you be open to a 25-minute call to explore whether a joint webinar or co-authored piece of content makes sense? No commitment — just want to see if there's a fit."
- Social proof: Name 1-2 other partners you've successfully co-marketed with (if any), or mention your audience size
- CTA: "Happy to send over a one-pager on what a typical co-marketing arrangement looks like for us — or just book time here: [Calendly link]"

TOUCH 3 — LINKEDIN DM FOLLOW-UP (Day 8, if no email reply)
Content: 3 sentences — reference your email, mention a specific data point about shared customer overlap (e.g., "When we surveyed our customers on their tech stack, 34% mentioned using [Partner Product]"), and ask for a 20-minute call

TOUCH 4 — FINAL EMAIL (Day 18, if no reply to any prior touch)
Content: 4 sentences — acknowledge they're likely prioritizing existing partnerships, offer to send the co-marketing one-pager for their reference, suggest reconnecting at a shared industry conference, and close gracefully

PHASE 2 — DISCOVERY CALL FRAMEWORK (25-minute initial call)
Agenda:
- Minutes 0-5: Brief company overviews (you go first — 90 seconds each)
- Minutes 5-15: ICP overlap validation questions ("Who is your primary buyer? What company stage do you focus on? What tools do your customers use alongside yours? Which customers of yours do you know that also use our product?")
- Minutes 15-22: Co-marketing program concept walkthrough ("Here's how we structure partnerships — here are 2-3 formats we think would work for us. What resonates?")
- Minutes 22-25: Next steps ("Would you want us to put together a one-page proposal for a 90-day pilot starting with a joint webinar?")

PHASE 3 — ALLIANCE AGREEMENT STRUCTURE (keep it simple — start with a Letter of Intent, not a contract)
A well-structured alliance starts with a 1-2 page Letter of Intent (LOI) or Partner Kickoff Brief that covers:

ALLIANCE BRIEF TEMPLATE:
Partnership Tier: [Tier 1 Strategic / Tier 2 Growth / Tier 3 Exploration]
Program Duration: [90-day pilot → review → annual renewal]

MUTUAL COMMITMENTS:
Both companies agree to:
- Promote each other in at least [X] marketing touchpoints per quarter (newsletter mention, webinar, blog post, social)
- Share leads/contacts generated through joint campaigns within [5] business days of capture
- Participate in at least [2] joint campaigns per quarter (one of which must be a webinar or live event)
- Maintain a shared Slack channel for partner communications and co-marketing coordination
- Provide monthly report on partner-attributed pipeline and closed deals

YOUR COMMITMENTS:
- You bring [X] ICP contacts from your database to each joint campaign
- You promote [Partner] to your [customer / prospect newsletter / LinkedIn audience] audience
- You provide [X hours/month] of co-marketing execution support

PARTNER COMMITMENTS:
- Partner brings [X] ICP contacts from their database to each joint campaign
- Partner promotes [Your Company] to their equivalent audiences
- Partner provides [X hours/month] of co-marketing execution support

PIPELINE TRACKING AGREEMENT:
- Both companies agree to track and report partner-attributed pipeline using [CRM system]
- Any contact introduced through a joint campaign is tagged with source = "Alliance - [Partner Name]"
- Both companies share pipeline status monthly (no obligation to share deal details — just stage and whether the deal is progressing)
- Neither company will poach the other's customers or target active deals in the other's pipeline

COMPONENT 5 — MUTUAL PIPELINE TRACKING & ATTRIBUTION SYSTEM

Build a lightweight partner pipeline tracking system without dedicated PRM software:

SALESFORCE SETUP:
- Create custom Lead/Contact field: Partner_Source__c (picklist: [Partner Name 1], [Partner Name 2], etc.)
- Create custom field on Opportunity: Alliance_Partner__c and Alliance_Campaign__c
- Build a Report: "Alliance-Sourced Pipeline by Partner by Quarter" — filters on Alliance_Partner__c not null
- Create Dashboard widget: "Partner Pipeline Contribution %" showing Alliance pipeline vs. total pipeline
- Process Builder trigger: When Contact record has Partner_Source__c populated → create a Task for the assigned SDR: "Partner Lead: follow up within 24 hours with co-marketing context"

HUBSPOT SETUP:
- Create a Contact property: "Partner Source" with dropdown options per partner
- Create a Deal property: "Alliance Partner" and "Alliance Campaign"
- Build a HubSpot Dashboard: "Alliance Revenue Intelligence" with cards for:
  - Contacts added per partner this month
  - Deals created from partner leads
  - Partner pipeline value at each stage
  - Partner-sourced closed-won ARR rolling 12 months
- Use HubSpot Lists to segment partner-sourced contacts for targeted nurture sequences separate from your general lead database

LEAD HANDOFF PROTOCOL (how both companies share leads without a PRM):
- Method A (Shared Google Sheet / Airtable): Weekly, both companies input new ICP contacts generated from joint campaigns into a shared tracking doc with: Name, Title, Company, Contact Info, Campaign Source, Who They Were Matched To, and Status
- Method B (Bidirectional CRM Tagging): Both companies tag contacts with the other's name in their own CRM; share a weekly CSV export to sync status
- Method C (Crossbeam or Reveal): Use a free partnership intelligence tool (Crossbeam, Reveal, or PartnerTap) to securely match and share overlapping account lists without exposing your full CRM to the partner

ATTRIBUTION RULES (agree on these upfront with your partner):
- First Touch Rule: The company that first introduced the contact to the other company gets "Alliance Source" credit
- Assisted Attribution: Any deal where the alliance partner's content, case study, or reference was used in the sales cycle gets "Alliance Influenced" tag (a secondary attribution, not primary)
- Joint Event Attribution: All attendees at a co-hosted event are tagged as "Alliance - [Event Name]" source for both companies

COMPONENT 6 — 90-DAY ALLIANCE LAUNCH PLAN

MONTH 1 — FOUNDATION (Days 1-30)
Week 1: Partner Identification & Prioritization
- Score top 10 potential partners using the Partner Fit Matrix → select 2-3 Tier 1 candidates
- Map mutual customer overlap: Survey your top 100 customers on tech stack; cross-reference with partner's public customer lists and G2 "Used With" data
- Draft your co-marketing one-pager and partner pitch email

Week 2: Partner Outreach
- Send personalized outreach to your top 3 Tier 1 partner candidates
- Connect on LinkedIn and begin warming the relationship with comments and engagement
- Set goal: Book discovery calls with ≥ 2 of the 3 targets within 2 weeks

Week 3: Discovery Calls & Partner Selection
- Conduct 25-minute discovery calls with interested partners
- Evaluate: Do they have the ICP database, marketing capacity, and executive commitment you need?
- Select your first strategic alliance partner and propose a 90-day co-marketing pilot

Week 4: Alliance Agreement & Kickoff
- Finalize and exchange the Alliance Brief (LOI format — no legal review required at this stage)
- Create shared Slack channel for partner co-marketing collaboration
- Set up CRM tracking fields and attribution rules in both companies' CRMs
- Schedule joint kick-off call with both companies' marketing leads

MONTH 2 — FIRST JOINT CAMPAIGN (Days 31-60)
Week 5: Co-Marketing Campaign Planning
- Co-develop joint webinar concept: Agree on topic, format, speaker lineup (one from each company), target attendee goal, and landing page owner
- Begin building the joint audience: Each company pulls their ICP email list for co-promotion; estimate combined reach

Week 6-7: Campaign Build
- Build landing page (alternating — one company hosts this quarter, other hosts next)
- Write co-branded email invitation sequences (each company sends 2 emails to their list)
- Create co-branded social media content calendar (5-7 posts per company in the 3 weeks before the webinar)
- Draft post-webinar follow-up sequences for both companies' SDRs

Week 8: Launch & Promote
- Send first invitation email (4 weeks before webinar date)
- Post first organic social promotion
- Begin SDR outreach to high-priority target accounts for personal invitation to the webinar

MONTH 3 — MEASURE, ITERATE, EXPAND (Days 61-90)
Week 9-10: Execute Joint Webinar
- Run the webinar; target ≥ 60% attendee show rate (higher than typical because both companies are promoting)
- Send co-branded recording to all registrants within 24 hours
- Execute personalized post-webinar follow-up from both companies' SDRs within 48 hours for all ICP attendees

Week 11: Measure & Report
- Both companies report: Registrants sourced, attendance rate, post-event meetings booked, pipeline attributed
- Calculate: Partner-sourced leads as % of total new leads this quarter; joint webinar CPL vs. your blended CPL
- Share results in a joint 30-minute retrospective call

Week 12: 90-Day Review & Program Expansion
- Evaluate: Does this partnership meet your pipeline targets? Recommend escalate to Tier 1 or maintain at Tier 2
- Propose next quarter's co-marketing campaign calendar (2 additional formats: one joint research piece + one executive roundtable)
- Identify 1-2 additional Tier 1 partner candidates to begin outreach for Quarter 2

COMPONENT 7 — JOINT SALES ENABLEMENT ARCHITECTURE

Equip both companies' sales teams to leverage the alliance in active deals:

A. PARTNER BATTLECARD (1-page format, one per partner, stored in your CRM):
- What [Partner Name] does and why it's relevant to our buyer
- What our product does that [Partner Name] cannot (protect your value proposition — never let reps confuse the products)
- How to mention [Partner Name] in discovery: "Do you currently use [Partner Name]? We work with a lot of their customers, and there's actually a really interesting workflow between our two products that you'd want to see."
- How to use a shared customer reference: "We have a customer, [Name], who uses both [Partner] and us. They mentioned they'd be happy to take a call with you — want me to make that intro?"
- What [Partner Name] is NOT: Prevents over-selling or misrepresenting the partnership

B. JOINT CUSTOMER REFERENCE POOL:
- Build a shared list of 10-15 customers willing to serve as references for deals where the prospect uses the other company's product
- CRM tag: "Joint Reference – [Partner Name]: Yes"
- Briefing call: Quarterly 15-minute call with each joint reference to ensure they're updated on both companies' recent product developments and happy to speak

C. SDR ENABLEMENT — PARTNER-TRIGGERED PROSPECTING SEQUENCES:
Create a specific SDR sequence for prospects who are known users of a Tier 1 partner's product:
- Email 1 subject: "Heads up from a [Partner Name] partner"
- Open: Reference the partner relationship, establish credibility through the association
- Body: Lead with the combined value story — what the prospect is likely getting from [Partner Name] and what remains unsolved that you address
- CTA: "Would it be worth 20 minutes to see how [Partner Name] users at [similar company names] are using both tools to achieve [specific outcome]?"
- Expected: 3-5x higher reply rate vs. cold outreach when correctly identifying that the prospect already uses the partner

COMPONENT 8 — MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK & ALLIANCE ROI MODEL

PRIMARY KPIs (track monthly, report to CMO and CRO):

1. Alliance-Sourced Pipeline Contribution %
Formula: Opportunities with Alliance_Partner field populated / Total pipeline created
Target: Grow from baseline to 15-20% of total pipeline within 12 months (for companies with 3+ Tier 1 partners)
Action threshold: < 3% after 90-day pilot = partner selection problem or campaign quality issue

2. Alliance Lead-to-Meeting Rate
Formula: Alliance-sourced contacts → demos booked
Benchmark: Should be 35-55% (vs. 8-15% for cold outbound) due to warm introduction context
Action threshold: < 20% = ICP mismatch or SDR follow-up not leveraging the partnership context

3. Alliance Win Rate vs. Blended Win Rate
Formula: Alliance-sourced opportunities closed-won / Alliance-sourced opportunities created
Benchmark: Alliance win rates should be 30-60% higher than your blended win rate
Insight: If alliance win rate is NOT higher than blended, the ICP overlap claim is weaker than assumed — re-evaluate partner fit

4. Co-Marketing Campaign CPL (Cost Per Alliance Lead)
Formula: Total co-marketing spend on joint campaign / Total net-new ICP contacts generated
Benchmark: Target CPL ≤ 40% of your paid media CPL benchmark (the shared audience access dramatically reduces cost)
Track per campaign type: Webinar CPL, research report CPL, roundtable CPL

5. Partner Reciprocity Score
Formula: Alliance-attributed leads received from partner / Alliance-attributed leads you sent to partner
Target: 0.8-1.2 (parity); flag any partner where you're consistently receiving < 0.5 (they're underinvesting in promotion)
Action: Quarterly partnership review — address reciprocity gaps before the imbalance creates resentment

SECONDARY METRICS:
- Time from co-marketing event to first meeting booked (target: < 5 days)
- Alliance-sourced customer retention rate vs. non-alliance customers (hypothesis: higher retention due to deeper workflow integration)
- Repeat co-marketing campaign participation rate per partner (> 80% = partner is engaged; < 50% = at risk of churn)
- Net Promoter Score from partner contacts for your company (surveyed at 6-month mark)

ALLIANCE PROGRAM ROI MODEL:
Build a simple quarterly ROI model to justify the program investment to your CFO and board:
- Revenue attributed to alliance program = (Closed deals with Alliance_Partner tag) × ACV
- Total program cost = Partner manager time (% of salary) + Co-marketing spend + Events
- Alliance CAC = Total program cost / Customers acquired through alliance channel
- Compare Alliance CAC to your blended CAC and present ratio (target: Alliance CAC < 30% of blended CAC)
- Present to board: "Our 3 Tier 1 alliance partners generated $[X] in pipeline and $[Y] in closed ARR at a CAC that is [Z]x lower than our average — we're proposing to expand to 6 Tier 1 partners next year with a budget of $[N]."

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner agreed to the alliance in principle but keeps deprioritizing the joint campaigns and missing deadlines**
Diagnosis: The partnership lacks executive sponsorship on their side — the person you're working with doesn't have the authority or internal priority to execute; alternatively, the value exchange feels asymmetrical to them
Fix: Request a 30-minute "Alliance Business Review" with their VP Marketing or CMO present — present the co-marketing ROI model showing what 90 days of joint campaigns could generate for both companies. If they can't commit to a call at this level, consider downgrading them to Tier 3. Partnerships only work when both companies have a senior champion who feels accountable for the outcome. Additionally, consider proposing a simplified "minimum commitment" — one joint social media exchange and one newsletter mention — to build early momentum and demonstrate the value before asking for a larger webinar investment.

**Problem: The joint webinar generates registrants but few meetings — post-webinar SDR follow-up isn't converting**
Diagnosis: SDRs aren't leveraging the alliance context in their outreach; they're treating these leads like cold outbound prospects instead of warm alliance introductions; or the webinar content itself attracted early-stage awareness buyers rather than active evaluators
Fix: Audit the follow-up email sequence — the first sentence must explicitly reference the webinar and the shared partner connection: "Following up from the [Partner] + [Your Company] session on [Topic] — you attended for a reason." Require SDRs to call alliance-sourced leads before emailing (phone-first, then email): use the script "Hi [Name], this is [SDR] from [Company] — I saw you attended our webinar with [Partner Name] on [Topic] and I wanted to personally follow up." Alliance leads expect a higher-quality follow-up than cold contacts do — meet that expectation. Also review attendee titles post-webinar: if > 40% were non-ICP titles, adjust the next webinar promotion targeting.

**Problem: You're consistently generating more leads for your partner than they're generating for you — the reciprocity score is < 0.5**
Diagnosis: The partner has a significantly larger audience than you, is less invested in the promotion, or is treating the partnership as a one-way lead source
Fix: Address this directly in the next monthly alliance sync — share the reciprocity data transparently: "We've sent you 43 ICP contacts this quarter; we've received 18 from you. For this to be sustainable, we need both companies to be generating for each other. What would help you increase promotion on your side?" If the gap is due to audience size (they have 10x more subscribers), negotiate a different value exchange: they provide more leads, you provide more co-marketing production work, an exclusive case study, or a speaking slot at your user conference. If after 6 months the reciprocity doesn't improve, downgrade the partner to Tier 3 and reallocate your Tier 1 investment to a partner who generates comparable value.
