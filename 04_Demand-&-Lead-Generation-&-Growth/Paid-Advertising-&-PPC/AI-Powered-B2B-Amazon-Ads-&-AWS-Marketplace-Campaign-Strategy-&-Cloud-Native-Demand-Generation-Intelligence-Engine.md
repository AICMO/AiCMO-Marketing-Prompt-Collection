6. BIDDING STRATEGY & CAMPAIGN LIFECYCLE

Phase 1 — Launch (Days 1-30): Discovery and baseline
- Start all Sponsored ads on Dynamic Bids — Down Only to cap overspend
- Set manual bids 20% above estimated first-page bid (Amazon will show suggested bid in console)
- For DSP: use CPM bidding, start at $8-12 CPM for awareness, $15-25 CPM for retargeting
- Do NOT pause ads with low CTR in first 14 days — let the algorithm gather impression data
- Minimum data threshold before optimization decisions: 1,000 impressions per ad, 10 clicks per keyword

Phase 2 — Optimization (Days 31-60): Efficiency and scaling
- Run a Search Term Report: identify converting terms to add as exact match, wasteful terms for negatives
- Increase bids on exact match keywords with ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sale) below your target
- Decrease bids or pause keywords with 0 clicks after 500 impressions
- Test Dynamic Bids — Up and Down on top-performing exact match keywords once you have conversion data
- For DSP: rotate creative every 30 days (CTR benchmarks: 0.05-0.10% for display is healthy for B2B)

Phase 3 — Scaling (Day 61+): Compound growth
- Budget rule: increase winning campaigns by 20-30% per week maximum
- Expand to new keyword clusters based on Search Term Report discoveries
- Layer DSP lookalike audiences on top of proven keyword campaigns for multi-channel reach
- Test Sponsored Brands Video ads if video asset is available (B2B buyers over-index on video for complex software)

Benchmarks for B2B technology on Amazon Ads (use to set stakeholder expectations):
- Sponsored Products: CPC $2-6, CTR 0.3-0.7%, Conversion rate (click-to-trial) 2-5%
- Sponsored Brands: CPC $3-8, CTR 0.2-0.5%, primarily a reach and recall vehicle
- Sponsored Display: CPM $4-12, CTR 0.05-0.1%, value is in retargeting efficiency
- Amazon DSP: CPM $8-25, CTR 0.05-0.15%, value is audience quality and cross-site reach
- AWS Marketplace conversion (trial to paid): industry average 8-15%; top performers 20-30%

7. MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK & ATTRIBUTION

TECHNICAL SETUP:
- Amazon Attribution tag: create one tag per external campaign (Google Ads, LinkedIn, email) driving traffic to your Marketplace listing — measures impressions, clicks, detail page views, and purchases attributed to each channel
- Conversion events with values: Trial start ($150 value), Demo request from Marketplace ($200), Private offer request ($500), Marketplace subscription activation ($ACV x 0.25 as pipeline proxy)
- CRM tagging: ensure all Marketplace leads carry `utm_source=amazon_marketplace` and `utm_medium=paid` or `utm_medium=organic_marketplace` for source attribution
- Pixel placement: Amazon DSP pixel on homepage, product pages, pricing page, and thank-you/confirmation pages

REPORTING CADENCE:
- Weekly: ACoS by campaign, search term performance, budget pacing, CTR fatigue check, DSP frequency report
- Monthly: Pipeline influenced by Amazon channels (cross-reference CRM opportunities with Amazon lead source), Marketplace trial start rate, trial-to-paid conversion rate, CPO by channel vs. LinkedIn/Google benchmark
- Quarterly: Amazon Ads share of total pipeline sourced, AWS Marketplace GMV trend, co-sell pipeline generated with AWS field team, channel ROI vs. other paid channels

1. AWS Marketplace trial starts, private offer requests, and GMV (direct revenue signal)
2. Pipeline sourced or influenced by Amazon channels (CRM pull, last-touch and multi-touch)

## Success Metrics

**Green (campaign is healthy):**
- Marketplace trial start rate from Amazon Ads traffic: > 3%
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate: > 15% (above Marketplace average of 8-12%)
- ACoS on Sponsored Products: < 30% of ACV (for $38K ACV, ACoS target < $11,400 per closed deal)
- DSP Marketplace detail page view rate: > 0.15% (clicks-to-detail-page from display)
- Amazon-influenced pipeline: ≥ 3x total Amazon Ads spend

**Yellow (investigate):**
- Trial starts declining week-over-week despite stable ad spend (listing conversion issue, not ad issue)
- ACoS rising >20% week-over-week (audience exhaustion or bid competition from competitors)
- DSP frequency exceeding 8x per week (creative fatigue — rotate immediately)
- Competitive keywords seeing impression share decline (competitors raising bids — evaluate counter-bid strategy)

**Red (pause and diagnose):**
- ACoS > 60% of ACV for 4+ consecutive weeks with no improvement trend
- Trial-to-paid rate below 5% (product-market fit or onboarding issue — not an ads problem)
- Zero pipeline influence after 60 days of Marketplace Listing Ad spend (listing quality issue)
- DSP budget delivering less than 70% (inventory issue — expand publisher whitelist or raise floor CPM)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High Marketplace ad spend but low trial start rate (< 1.5% click-to-trial)**
Solution: The bottleneck is the Marketplace listing itself, not the ads. Ads are driving traffic but the listing is not converting. Diagnose in this order: (1) Check your listing title — does it include the primary search term the buyer used? Mismatched intent is the #1 cause. (2) Read your top 3 customer reviews — if reviews mention confusion about what the product does, rewrite your short description to lead with the outcome, not the feature. (3) Audit your free trial setup — is it truly friction-free to start? Any credit card requirement kills trial conversion for B2B SaaS (industry standard is no credit card for trials). (4) A/B test your feature bullets — run Seller Central's A/B listing experiment tool to test two versions for 4 weeks. Never change the listing and ad campaign simultaneously, or you won't know which variable moved the metric.

**Problem: Amazon Ads spend scaling but no CRM pipeline attribution — can't prove ROI to leadership**
Solution: This is an attribution setup problem, not a campaign problem. Implement in this order: (1) Install Amazon Attribution tags on every external page that drives to your Marketplace listing — create unique tags per campaign so you can see LinkedIn vs. Google vs. email contribution separately. (2) Add `utm_source=amazon_marketplace&utm_medium=organic` to your Marketplace listing's product landing page link for organic Marketplace traffic attribution. (3) In your CRM, create a workflow that auto-tags any contact who submits a form after visiting a URL containing `amazon` or `marketplace` in the referrer. (4) Build a quarterly attribution model in a Google Sheet: match Marketplace trial start dates with CRM opportunity create dates — any opportunity created within 30 days of a trial start qualifies as Marketplace-influenced. Present as "influenced pipeline" to leadership, not just direct attribution.

**Problem: Competitor keywords driving clicks but poor conversion — high spend, low trial rate**
Solution: Buyers clicking competitor keywords are in research mode comparing options — they are high-intent but need a reason to switch.
