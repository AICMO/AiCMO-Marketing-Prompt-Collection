## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low named-account reach despite targeting correct ZIP codes**
Solution: Enterprise buyer ZIP codes are too broad — switch from ZIP-level to POI (Point of Interest) targeting. Upload the exact addresses of your top 50 target accounts' HQs and the corporate campuses of key contacts. Request POI-proximity targeting at 250-meter radius within Vistar or StackAdapt. Also validate your named account list against actual office locations (employees often work at satellite offices, not registered HQs).

**Problem: Can't prove DOOH contribution to pipeline — CFO/CMO skeptical**
Solution: Run a matched market test: select 3-4 cities with equivalent ICP density — activate DOOH in 2 cities, hold the other 2 as dark markets. After 90 days, compare pipeline generated per $1M TAM in test vs. control markets. Also enable vanity URL tracking (brand.com/ooh) on all DOOH creatives and report separately on visits and conversions from that source — even 200 high-quality visitors from DOOH can justify the spend when average deal size is $80K+.

**Problem: Creative is getting lost in busy DOOH environments (airports, transit)**
Solution: Apply the "5-foot, 5-second, 5-word" rule — your creative must communicate its core message from 5 feet away in 5 seconds with 5 or fewer words as the dominant element. Remove subheadlines from transit/airport formats. Increase brand logo size by 40% over what you'd use in digital display. Use color contrast ratios ≥ 7:1 (WCAG AAA level) and avoid white backgrounds in airport environments (they wash out). Test creative by viewing on a smartphone screen at arm's length — if you can read it instantly, it will work at scale.
