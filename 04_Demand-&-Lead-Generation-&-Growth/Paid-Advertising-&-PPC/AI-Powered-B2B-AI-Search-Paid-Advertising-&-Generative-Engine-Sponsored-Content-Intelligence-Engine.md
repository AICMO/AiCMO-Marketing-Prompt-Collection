## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sponsored answers being rejected or flagged as low quality by Perplexity or Microsoft Copilot.**
*Fix:* The most common cause is over-promotional content. Rewrite the answer so that the first 70% is purely educational and genuinely helpful — the brand mention should appear in the final 30% as a recommendation supported by specific data. Run the content through a quality check: "Would this answer be helpful if my company name was removed?" If not, rewrite it.

**Problem: AI search ad clicks are not converting at expected rates compared to traditional search.**
*Fix:* AI search traffic arrives with high education-level but may be at an earlier decision stage than the equivalent traditional search query suggests. Check if your landing page assumes too much buyer intent. Add a softer MOFU conversion path (e.g., an ROI calculator or comparison guide) alongside your primary CTA. Also verify your landing page content explicitly reinforces the answer that drove the click — misalignment between the sponsored answer promise and landing page content kills conversion rates.

**Problem: Difficulty measuring true pipeline influence of AI search advertising across multi-touch journeys.**
*Fix:* Since AI search often appears mid-journey (not first-touch), rely on time-decay or data-driven attribution models rather than last-touch. Set a 90-day attribution window specifically for AI search touchpoints. Supplement with a simple survey question in your demo scheduling form: "How did you first hear about us?" — you'll often find AI search attribution is being undercounted by 30–50% in automated tracking alone.
