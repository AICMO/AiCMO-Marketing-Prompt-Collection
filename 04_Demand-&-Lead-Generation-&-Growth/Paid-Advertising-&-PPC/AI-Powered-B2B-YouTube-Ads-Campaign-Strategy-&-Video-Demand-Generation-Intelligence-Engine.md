3. VIDEO AD CREATIVE SPECIFICATIONS (produce all scripts and creative direction)

   THE 5-SECOND HOOK FRAMEWORK (non-negotiable — must stop the skip):
   For every skippable ad, the first 5 seconds determines whether anyone watches the rest. Design hooks using one of these patterns:
   - DISRUPTION: Challenge a belief your buyer holds ("Most [job title]s still believe [wrong assumption]. They're losing [consequence] every quarter because of it.")
   - CURIOSITY GAP: Tease an insight without completing it ("We analyzed 400 field service companies. The ones growing fastest all do one thing differently...")
   - DIRECT ADDRESS: Speak directly to their pain ("If you're managing a field service team on spreadsheets and group texts, this video will save you 12 hours a week.")
   - PROOF STATEMENT: Lead with a specific customer outcome ("Redwood Services went from 40% first-time fix rate to 87% in 90 days. Here's exactly how.")
   - PROVOCATIVE QUESTION: ("Why do 73% of field service companies lose their best techs within 2 years? We figured it out.")

   For each ad provide:

   - CALL TO ACTION (final 5 seconds): Spoken CTA + on-screen CTA card + end screen CTA alignment
   - COMPANION BANNER copy (300x60px text that appears alongside the video): headline + URL
   - OVERLAY CTA (text overlay at seconds 5-20 for mobile): 10-word action phrase
   - Thumbnail description for In-Feed version of this ad
   - Psychological principle applied: (pattern interrupt, social proof, authority, loss aversion, FOMO, reciprocity, scarcity)
   - Production notes: on-screen text, b-roll direction, talking-head vs. animation vs. screen recording recommendation

   Apply these frameworks by funnel stage:
   - TOFU (Awareness): No product name in first 10 seconds. Lead with the problem or insight. CTA = "See how" or "Learn more." Length: 30-60 seconds.
   - MOFU (Consideration): Reference the problem, introduce product category. Use customer proof points. CTA = "Watch the demo" or "Get the guide." Length: 60-90 seconds.
   - BOFU (Conversion): Direct response. Name the product. Specific offer (demo, trial, assessment). Urgency or specificity element. CTA = "Book a demo" or "Start free trial." Length: 30-60 seconds.

   NON-SKIPPABLE IN-STREAM ADS (15 seconds max — use for retargeting only):

   - No filler, no intro, no "Hi, I'm..." — every second is paid real estate

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Very low View Rate (< 20%) on skippable ads despite adequate budget**
Solution: The first 5 seconds aren't working — this is almost always a hook problem, not a targeting problem. Viewers click skip at the first sign of a corporate intro, product name, or generic claim. Fix: Rewrite the hook to open with a direct pain statement addressed to the viewer's job title ("If you're managing field techs and you're still manually dispatching..."), a specific customer stat ("One HVAC company with 40 techs reduced no-shows by 63% in 60 days — here's how"), or a provocative question ("Why do contractors who use scheduling software actually lose more jobs in year one?"). Never open with your company name, a jingle, or a wide-angle office establishing shot. Test 3 different hooks in separate ad groups and cut the two losers after 500 impressions each.

**Problem: High view rate and completion but zero demo conversions after 30 days of BOFU spend**
Solution: There are three likely culprits. (1) Tracking is broken — verify your Google Ads conversion tag is firing on the demo confirmation page (use Google Tag Assistant Chrome extension to confirm). Check that the view-through conversion window is set to at least 7 days, not the default 1 day. (2) The CTA-to-landing-page experience is broken — does your CTA in the video match exactly what the viewer sees on the landing page? Mismatched offers ("Get a free assessment" in the video → generic demo page) destroy conversion rates. (3) Wrong funnel stage for the audience — if your BOFU audience includes cold viewers who have never heard of your brand, the direct-response ask is too strong. Segment your BOFU campaign to only include: Customer Match (existing contacts), website retargeting (pricing/demo page visitors), and video viewer retargeting (watched 50%+ of a product video). Cold audiences should flow through TOFU → MOFU → BOFU over 30-60 days before seeing a direct demo ask.

**Problem: Good demo volume but leads say they don't remember seeing the video ad**
Solution: This is a view-through attribution and attribution window calibration challenge. YouTube often gets credit for conversions where a user watched a video days or weeks before converting through another channel. To validate true influence: (1) Add a "How did you first hear about us?" self-reported attribution question on your demo request form and analyze what percentage mention YouTube or video. (2) Run a brand lift study (available in Google Ads for budgets > $5,000/month) to measure incremental ad recall, brand awareness, and purchase intent among exposed vs. unexposed audiences. (3) Use holdout testing: pause YouTube for one month in a secondary geography and compare demo volume to your primary market running YouTube. The delta is YouTube's true incremental contribution. Present this data to leadership instead of last-click CPL, which will always undercount video's role in long B2B sales cycles.
