## Success Metrics

**Green (campaign is healthy):**
- CTR > 0.6% on cold audiences, > 1.0% on retargeting
- CPL < 2x your target (target = 10% of ACV; for $45K ACV, target CPL = $450, caution at $900)
- Lead-to-MQL rate > 30% (LinkedIn leads should be higher intent than form fills)
- Demo show rate from LinkedIn leads > 55%
- LinkedIn-influenced pipeline ≥ 3x LinkedIn Ads spend (pipeline ROI ratio)

**Yellow (investigate):**
- Frequency > 8 on cold audience (rotate creative immediately)
- CTR declining week-over-week for 2+ weeks (ad fatigue)
- CPL rising >20% week-over-week (audience exhaustion or bidding issue)
- Lead-to-MQL rate < 20% (targeting too broad or form fields too loose)

**Red (pause and diagnose):**
- CPL > 3x target for 3+ consecutive weeks
- Zero pipeline influence after 60+ days of spend
- Audience size < 10,000 (too narrow to optimize)

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Connect LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms natively via HubSpot's LinkedIn integration (Settings → Marketing → Ads)
- Map LinkedIn Lead Gen Form fields to HubSpot contact properties including `hs_analytics_source` = "PAID_SOCIAL" and `hs_analytics_source_data_1` = "LinkedIn"
- Create a HubSpot workflow: trigger on LinkedIn Lead Gen Form submission → assign lifecycle stage = Marketing Qualified Lead → enroll in LinkedIn Lead nurture sequence → notify assigned SDR within 5 minutes
- Use LinkedIn Company Insights in HubSpot to see which accounts are engaging before they convert

**Salesforce:**
- Use the LinkedIn Sales Insights integration to sync account engagement data (impressions, clicks) to Salesforce Account records
- Create a Salesforce Campaign for each LinkedIn campaign → use Campaign Member records to track lead source attribution
- Build a Salesforce report: "LinkedIn-Influenced Opportunities" — filter on Campaign = LinkedIn and Opportunity stage ≠ Closed Lost
- Add a LinkedIn Engagement Score field to Lead/Contact records: sum of impressions (1pt) + clicks (5pt) + form submits (20pt) in last 90 days

**Marketo:**
- Use Marketo's LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms native integration for real-time lead sync (< 2 min delay)
- Build a Marketo Smart Campaign: trigger = "Fills Out LinkedIn Lead Gen Form" → change program status to "Responded" → add to nurture stream → alert SDR
- Use Marketo tokens to personalize the first nurture email with LinkedIn campaign source and content asset downloaded

**Zapier (for non-native integrations):**
- Trigger: "New Lead Gen Form Response" in LinkedIn → Action: "Create Contact" in CRM → Action: "Send Slack notification" to demand gen channel
- Include UTM parameters in all website destination URLs: `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=paid-social&utm_campaign=[campaign-name]&utm_content=[ad-variant]`

**Google Sheets (for reporting):**
- Use LinkedIn's API export or manual CSV download → paste into Google Sheets dashboard
- Build a weekly reporting tab: Spend, Impressions, Clicks, CTR, Leads, CPL, MQLs, Cost per MQL, Pipeline Influenced
- Color-code cells: green (at/below target), yellow (within 1.5x), red (above 1.5x target)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High CPL with low lead volume despite adequate budget**
Solution: Your audience is likely too narrow (under 50K members) or your creative isn't resonating. First check audience size — if under 50K, broaden by adding more job title variants or loosening industry filters. If audience size is fine, run a creative A/B test: change the intro text hook from feature-focused to problem-focused. Lead with a statistic ("The average enterprise contract takes 42 days to close") rather than a product claim. If CPL is high but leads are qualified, the issue is efficiency — test Cost Cap bidding with a cap at 1.5x your target CPL.

**Problem: High lead volume but low lead-to-MQL rate (leads not converting to qualified)**
Solution: Your targeting is too broad or your Lead Gen Form is collecting quantity over quality. First, add a qualifying custom question to your form (e.g., "How many contracts does your team manage annually?" with options: Under 100 / 100-500 / 500-2000 / 2000+). Route only respondents who select high-volume buckets. Second, review which job titles are actually submitting — you may need to add exclusions for individual contributor roles if you're targeting director+ personas. Third, enable "Lead Quality" optimization in Campaign Manager if you have 50+ conversions — LinkedIn will optimize toward leads similar to your converted ones.

**Problem: Good CTR and CPL but no pipeline influence (leads not converting to opportunities)**
Solution: The bottleneck is post-click, not LinkedIn. Audit your lead follow-up: (1) Are SDRs calling LinkedIn leads within 5 minutes of submission? Speed-to-lead has 10x impact on connect rate. (2) Is the nurture sequence referencing the specific asset or pain point that drove the form fill? Generic sequences kill conversion. (3) Are you targeting the right seniority? If your ICP is VP+, director-level leads from LinkedIn may never convert to pipeline. Tighten targeting to senior director and above, accept lower volume for higher conversion rate.
