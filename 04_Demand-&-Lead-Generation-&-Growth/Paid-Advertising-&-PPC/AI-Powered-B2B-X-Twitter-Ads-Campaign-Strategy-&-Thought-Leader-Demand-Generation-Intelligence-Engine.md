## Success Metrics

**Green (campaign is healthy):**
- CTR > 0.5% on cold keyword/follower audiences, > 1.0% on tailored/retargeting audiences
- CPL < 2x your target (target = 8% of ACV; for $18K ACV, target CPL = $1,440 — but for self-serve trial, use $120 as trial CPL target)
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate > 12% (X trials should be high-intent buyers in evaluation mode)
- X-influenced pipeline ≥ 4x X Ads spend (CPM efficiency compensates for lower conversion intent vs. LinkedIn)
- Thought Leader Amplification: engagement rate > 2% on amplified posts (signals audience resonance)

**Yellow (investigate):**
- Frequency > 6 impressions per user on cold audiences in under 30 days (rotate creative immediately)
- CTR declining for 2+ consecutive weeks (creative fatigue — refresh copy and visual)
- CPL rising > 25% week-over-week (audience saturation or bid competition increase)
- Keyword-targeted audiences outperforming follower lookalikes by > 3x CPL (consolidate budget to keywords)

**Red (pause and diagnose):**
- CPL > 3x target for 4+ consecutive weeks
- Zero pixel conversions after $500+ spend (pixel misfire — audit implementation immediately)
- Negative earned engagements on promoted posts (ratio, critical replies) — pause creative, review messaging for tone-deaf language or inaccurate claims

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low CTR on cold campaigns despite relevant keyword and follower lookalike targeting**
Solution: On X, creative quality determines amplification — the algorithm rewards engagement before it rewards targeting precision. First, audit your tweet copy for corporate language: if your ad reads like a press release, it will be ignored or muted. Rewrite using X-native voice — short sentences, contrarian angles, specific numbers, no jargon. Second, check your visual: static images from Canva templates perform poorly on X — native-feeling screenshots, dark-mode UI captures, or terminal-style text graphics outperform polished brand assets. Third, consider whether your offer matches where the audience is: cold keyword audiences are early-stage — TOFU content (insight posts, free tools, benchmark reports) consistently outperforms direct demo offers with cold traffic on X.

**Problem: Good CTR and CPL but pipeline isn't attributing back to X Ads (zero pipeline in CRM)**
Solution: This is almost always a UTM tracking gap or a form that doesn't capture the first-touch source. Audit in this order: (1) Verify your X Pixel fires correctly using X Pixel Helper browser extension — confirm the conversion event fires on your thank-you page, not just your landing page. (2) Check that your HubSpot or Salesforce form captures `utm_source` in a hidden field mapped to "Original Source" or a custom Lead Source field. (3) Add a self-reported attribution question to every X-driven form: "How did you first hear about Driftlock?" — X is heavily underreported in last-touch attribution because buyers see X ads early and convert weeks later through organic search or direct. (4) Build a multi-touch attribution view in your CRM: any opportunity with a contact who ever had utm_source=twitter as any touchpoint in the last 90 days.

**Problem: Thought Leader Amplification posts are getting negative engagement (replies criticizing the company or content)**
Solution: X's public discourse amplification is a double-edged sword — paid promotion exposes you to hostile audiences at scale. First, audit the content angle: thought leader posts that make bold claims without evidence, criticize competitors by name, or use marketing buzzwords ("game-changer," "paradigm shift") attract disproportionate negative engagement on X. Switch to data-first, opinion-second formats: share a genuine finding or benchmark result, then offer your interpretation. Second, review targeting: if your amplification is reaching outside your ICP (e.g., reaching a general tech audience instead of security practitioners), tighten targeting to tailored audiences only (website retargeting + email list) before running to cold keyword or follower lookalike audiences. Third, monitor reply sentiment daily during the first two weeks of any amplification campaign — pause and revise any post with a replies-to-likes ratio above 0.3 before spending more on promotion.
