## Overview
Most B2B paid media programs blast the same ad creative to broad audiences and call it "ABM." This prompt engineers an AI-powered account-based advertising system that dynamically personalizes paid media creative, messaging, and targeting across LinkedIn, programmatic display, YouTube, and retargeting channels — at the individual account and persona level — based on real-time intent signals, deal stage, industry, and buying committee role. The result is a fully automated system where each of your top 300 target accounts sees ads specifically designed for their context, simultaneously nurturing multiple stakeholders toward consensus.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn audience size too small for account-level targeting (< 300 people)**
Solution: LinkedIn requires a minimum audience size of 300 to serve ads. For small target accounts (<500 employees), combine multiple smaller accounts into a "micro-segment" campaign grouped by industry + persona. Alternatively, use LinkedIn's "lookalike audiences" for Tier 3 accounts where individual account targeting is impractical. For must-win named accounts too small for LinkedIn targeting, shift budget to direct outreach with retargeting pixels instead.

**Problem: 6sense/Demandbase intent scores are not moving despite ad impressions (accounts stuck in "Awareness" stage)**
Solution: Intent score stagnation often means the prospect is not doing independent research — they're consuming your ads passively but not searching for solutions. Pivot from awareness creative to direct-response creative (ROI calculator, product demo offer, assessment tool) designed to provoke active research. Also audit whether the intent keywords you're tracking match what your actual buyers search — run a win/loss interview analysis to identify the exact search terms buyers use during their research.

**Problem: Creative personalization is generating low CTR despite account specificity — personalization isn't resonating**
Solution: Over-personalization (e.g., mentioning the company name too explicitly in ad copy) can feel intrusive and reduce CTR by 20-30%. Keep personalization at the industry/pain point level rather than explicit company name insertion for display and LinkedIn ads (exception: LinkedIn InMail and Conversation Ads where personalization is expected). Run a 2-week A/B test: generic-but-targeted (same account audience, generic creative) vs. personalized creative to identify whether your personalization variables are actually resonating. Often, persona-level messaging outperforms account-level messaging in CTR while still outperforming generic creative in pipeline influence.
