## Integration Tips

**The Trade Desk / DV360:**
- Export audience segments from your CDP (Segment, mParticle, BlueConic) directly via SFTP to DSP for CRM matching
- Set up automated creative trafficking via API if running >20 line items
- Connect DSP reporting to Google Looker Studio or Tableau via API for real-time dashboards

**HubSpot / Salesforce:**
- Create a UTM parameter structure for all display click-throughs: `utm_source=programmatic&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=[campaign_name]&utm_content=[ad_unit]`
- For CTV view-through attribution, use DSP's offline conversion upload — match on email or phone hash to your CRM contacts
- Build a "Programmatic Influenced" pipeline report: any deal where CRM contact was matched to an exposed impression within 90-day lookback window

**Zapier / Make.com Workflow:**
- Trigger: Lead submits demo request form
- Action 1: Add to HubSpot as MQL, assign to SDR
- Action 2: Remove from prospecting audience segments in DSP (suppress converted leads)
- Action 3: Add to "Customer" exclusion list to prevent wasted retargeting spend

**Google Analytics 4:**
- Create a custom channel grouping for "Programmatic / CTV"
- Set up view-through conversion import from DSP (requires manual upload or Zapier automation)
- Build an Explore report comparing assisted conversions from programmatic vs paid search vs organic

**Segment/mParticle CDP:**
- Sync first-party audiences to The Trade Desk or DV360 in real-time as users qualify for segments
- Suppress converted customers automatically within 24 hours of deal close
- Build lookalike seeds from your highest-LTV customer cohort

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CTV campaigns are delivering but I see zero view-through conversions in my attribution reports.**
Fix: View-through attribution requires a pixel fire on the conversion page AND the DSP must match the device that saw the CTV ad to the device that converted. CTV-to-web matching rates are 40-60% (not 100%) — if you see zero, check: (1) Is the conversion pixel correctly placed? (2) Is your attribution window set to at least 72 hours view-through? (3) Are you matching to the same identity graph the DSP uses? Try uploading a CRM match list of converted customers from the past 90 days to see if the DSP can retroactively match impressions.

**Problem: My CPMs are far higher than industry benchmarks and I'm burning budget fast.**
Fix: You're likely over-targeting with too many layered audience restrictions. Each data overlay (behavioral + firmographic + intent + recency) reduces available inventory and inflates CPMs via auction pressure. Remove one targeting layer at a time and re-check CPMs. Start with removing the intent data overlay (most expensive) and use contextual targeting as a cheaper proxy. Also check if you have too many geo restrictions — national campaigns typically clear inventory at lower CPMs than hyper-local.

**Problem: Video completion rates on CTV are below 70% but I thought CTV was non-skippable.**
Fix: Not all CTV inventory is non-skippable. FAST channel inventory often allows skip after 5 seconds on some platforms. Check your DSP's placement-level reporting — you likely have open exchange inventory mixed with premium placements. Filter for only non-skippable inventory in your targeting settings, or activate a PMP deal with guaranteed non-skippable inventory. Also check creative length — :60 second spots have significantly lower completion rates than :15 or :30.
