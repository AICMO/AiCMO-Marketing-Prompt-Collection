## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Budget under-delivery (spending only 60–70% of planned budget)**
*Cause:* Bid too low for auction competition, audience too narrow, or creative disapproval rate >15%
*Fix:* Check impression auction win rate in Amazon Ads console (target ≥50%). Increase dynamic bid setting from "down only" to "up and down." Broaden audience to include in-market + lookalike. Audit creative rejection report — common failures: unsubstantiated health claims, price guarantee language, competitor brand names in ad copy.

**Problem: High ROAS in dashboard but flat or declining actual sales**
*Cause:* Attribution window set too wide (14-day click + 14-day view) is capturing organic purchases and crediting them to ads
*Fix:* Switch to 7-day click + 1-day view attribution window for a more conservative signal. Run Amazon geo holdout to establish true incremental lift baseline. Compare same-period, same-geography sales velocity between test and control. True iROAS is typically 40–60% lower than reported ROAS for mature brands.

**Problem: NTB% consistently below 20% despite conquest audience targeting**
*Cause:* Conquest audiences are matching to existing customers, or brand buyers are also purchasing competitors and appearing in "competitive buyer" segments
*Fix:* Layer an exclusion audience of all brand purchasers (past 365 days via Customer Match) on top of conquest campaigns. In Amazon DSP, use AMC to build a custom exclusion audience of anyone with prior Verdana purchase. In Walmart, use "Exclude brand buyers" toggle in audience builder. Expect NTB% to increase 8–15 percentage points after exclusion layers applied.
