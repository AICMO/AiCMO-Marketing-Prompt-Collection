**Days 1–14 (Learning Phase — Minimal Intervention):**

**Days 15–30 (First Optimization Gate):**

- Placement transparency check: use the "Where your ads appeared" report to identify any low-quality placements to exclude

**Days 31–60 (Scaling Phase):**
- Budget increase protocol: increase in ≤20% increments, allow 7-day stabilization between increases
- Asset group splitting decision: if one theme is outperforming others by >50% CPA differential, split it into its own campaign
- Audience signal expansion: test adding the 3rd-tier interest-based signals to see if volume increases without CPA degradation
- Demand Gen creative refresh: replace any video with <30% engaged view rate

**Days 61–90 (Optimization & Competitive Moat Building):**
- Seasonality adjustments: use Google's seasonality adjustment feature if a major conversion event is coming
- Competitor conquest test: launch a separate asset group with competitor-themed messaging (competitor URL as audience signal, competitor mention in headlines) — measure CPA vs. generic asset groups
- LTV signal injection: if using Maximize Conversion Value, upload customer LTV values to CRM list and enable "Optimize for high-value new customers" setting

## Integration Tips

- **Google Ads Editor**: Build all asset groups in Editor before uploading — use the bulk asset upload feature to load 15 headlines, 5 descriptions, and all image assets simultaneously. Editor also lets you duplicate asset groups and swap themes without going through the UI campaign wizard.
- **Google Merchant Center (E-commerce)**: For P-Max with a Shopping feed, connect Merchant Center to the campaign and set listing group rules by product category, margin tier, or inventory level. High-margin products should be in their own asset group with a lower Target ROAS to allow more aggressive bidding.
- **HubSpot → Google Offline Conversions**: Use the HubSpot Google Ads integration (Settings → Ads → Google Ads) to automatically push "Lead became MQL" and "Deal Created" events back to Google as offline conversions. This is the single highest-impact configuration change for B2B P-Max — it tells Google's ML which users actually became pipeline, not just form-fillers.
- **Salesforce → Google Ads**: Use the Google Ads Salesforce connector or a middleware (Zapier, Make) to push Opportunity Stage changes back as conversion events. Map: Contact → Lead → MQL (micro-conv), MQL → SQL (primary conv), SQL → Closed Won (max value event). Weight each with approximate deal values.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Create a GA4 audience for "High-intent visitors" (2+ page views + visited pricing or demo page) and publish to Google Ads as a remarketing list. Use this as a P-Max audience signal and as a Demand Gen retargeting segment.
- **Looker Studio**: Build a P-Max performance dashboard combining: asset group performance by conversion (from Google Ads API), placement transparency data (from "Where ads showed" report), and offline conversion attribution (from Salesforce). Export weekly and feed into the optimization checklist.
- **Triple Whale / Northbeam (D2C)**: For e-commerce brands, use these platforms' blended attribution models to assess P-Max's true incrementality rather than Google's self-reported ROAS. Set up a geo holdout experiment — pause P-Max in 2 markets for 30 days and compare conversion rate vs. control markets.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: P-Max is spending the majority of budget on Display and YouTube but generating very few conversions — CPA is 3–4x target.
**Solution**: This indicates Google's algorithm has insufficient conversion data to find high-intent Search and Shopping inventory, and is defaulting to cheap impression-based placements. Add this fix: (1) Ensure Enhanced Conversions is enabled — unmatched conversions starve the algorithm. (2) Upload your Customer Match list immediately — this is the single most powerful signal you can give. (3) Add micro-conversion events (pricing page visit, video 75% completion) as secondary conversions so the algorithm has more frequent training signals. (4) If still failing after 30 days and 50+ micro-conversions, add an account-level placement exclusion for mobile app categories (Games, Utilities, Tools) which account for 40–60% of low-quality P-Max Display spend.

**Problem**: P-Max is taking credit for conversions that were clearly driven by branded search — brand CPC has risen 25% since P-Max launched.
**Solution**: Brand cannibalization is happening. Immediate fix: (1) Create an account-level negative keyword list with all brand variants in [Exact] and [Phrase] match and apply it to P-Max. (2) Check "Search terms insight" report in P-Max — look for brand-adjacent queries. (3) Raise your Brand Search campaign's bid adjustment to give it priority. (4) Temporarily reduce P-Max budget by 30% while the brand exclusion list takes effect (takes 24–48 hours). Long-term: set up a geo holdout to measure P-Max's true incremental contribution vs. what branded search would have captured organically.

**Problem**: Google Demand Gen campaign is not spending its full daily budget — consistently under-delivers at 40–60% of allocation.
**Solution**: Under-delivery on Demand Gen typically means audience targeting is too narrow. Fix: (1) Expand lookalike audience from "Narrow" to "Balanced" similarity setting. (2) Add one broader in-market audience to the ad group alongside your tight custom intent segment. (3) Check if frequency cap is too aggressive — Demand Gen defaults to 3 impressions/user/day; raise to 5 for the first 30 days. (4) If video creative has <15% view rate, the algorithm won't spend — your hook is failing. Recut the first 3 seconds: the opening frame must show a person or a problem, not a logo or product screenshot.
