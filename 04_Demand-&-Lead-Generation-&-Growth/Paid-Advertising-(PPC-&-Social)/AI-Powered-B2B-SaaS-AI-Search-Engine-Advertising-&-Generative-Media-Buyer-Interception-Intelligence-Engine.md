- Bidding: CPM and emerging CPC/CPA models depending on campaign type

### 3. Microsoft Copilot Ads Strategy

**Copilot Ads Architecture (Built on Microsoft Advertising):**
Microsoft Copilot Ads extend your existing Bing/Microsoft Advertising campaigns into Copilot's AI answer surfaces. If you are already running Microsoft Advertising, activation is largely a matter of enabling Copilot network targeting and adapting creative.

**Integration with Existing Microsoft Advertising Campaigns:**
- Enable "Microsoft Copilot" placement option in your existing Microsoft Search campaigns (Settings → Network → Include Copilot)
- Review which ad groups are eligible: Copilot tends to serve ads from ad groups with high Responsive Search Ad (RSA) scores — prioritize your best-performing RSA structures
- Budget: Copilot inventory is included within your Microsoft Advertising spend — no separate budget silo required initially; monitor CPCs which may differ from standard search

Copilot answers user queries in a conversational tone. Your ads appearing within Copilot answers perform better when:
- Headlines lead with the outcome/benefit, not the product feature ("Reduce manual forecasting by 80%", not "AI-Powered Forecasting Tool")
- Descriptions include a specific number, customer name/logo reference, or analyst citation — Copilot users are in research mode and respond to evidence
- Sitelinks should point to: Comparison pages, Case study library, ROI calculator, Analyst report — not homepage

### 4. Google AI Overviews Paid Visibility Strategy

**How Google AI Overviews (AIOs) Affect Paid Ads:**
Google AI Overviews appear above traditional paid search ads for informational and research queries. For transactional queries (bottom-funnel), Shopping Ads and Search Ads still surface within or immediately adjacent to AI Overviews. The implication for B2B SaaS:

- Top-of-funnel research queries ("how does [category] work," "best [category] software") → AIO dominates the SERP; paid ads have reduced visibility
- Mid-funnel comparison queries ("[Brand] vs [Brand]," "[Category] pricing") → AIO + Sponsored ads co-appear; paid placement remains valuable
- Bottom-funnel conversion queries ("[Brand] demo," "[Brand] pricing," "[Category] free trial") → Standard paid search ads still dominate; AIO presence minimal

**Paid Strategy for Each AIO Query Type:**

For queries where AIO dominates (top-funnel informational):
- Shift budget from these keyword groups to mid-funnel comparison and BOFU terms
- Use Google's AI-powered ad features (Broad Match + Smart Bidding) to catch intent signals that surface alongside AIO content — Google's algo learns which users clicking adjacent to AIO are likely to convert
- Test Google's "broad match in AI search" — Google now uses LLM-based intent matching for broad match, which catches query variants that appear within AI Overview contexts

For comparison and mid-funnel queries:
- Ensure your Shopping ads or Search ads are structured to appear immediately below the AIO container — these receive significant CTR from users who want a direct comparison after reading the AI summary
- Create dedicated landing pages for comparison intent ("[Your Brand] vs [Competitor]") — Google's AIO pulls from your site content, and a well-structured comparison page both feeds AIO organic and supports paid landing page relevance

For brand and BOFU queries:
- Protect branded search aggressively — AI Overviews for branded queries sometimes cite competitor alternatives, making branded SEM defense more critical
- Ensure your Google Business Profile, G2 profile, and structured data are optimized — AIO pulls citations from these sources, and paid relevance scores are partially influenced by your broader brand authority signals

**Google Ads Configuration for AIO Compatibility:**
- Enable "Automatically created assets" (ACA) — Google uses your landing pages to generate AI-powered ad assets that match the conversational query intent appearing in AIO contexts
- Responsive Search Ads: Ensure all campaigns have RSAs with 15 unique headlines and 4 descriptions — Google's AI selects the combination most relevant to the AIO context
- Page experience signals: AIO-adjacent paid clicks have higher bounce rates if landing page relevance is low — audit top-of-funnel landing pages for alignment with the research intent of queries co-occurring with AI Overviews

### 5. AI Search Ad Creative Framework

**The ARCS Framework for AI-Native Ad Copy** (Answer-Resonant, Credibility-Led, Specific):

Traditional PPC copy formula: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action (AIDA)
AI search ad copy formula: **A**nswer alignment → **R**eference-worthy proof → **C**ontext-specific CTA → **S**oft landing

**A — Answer Alignment:**
Your first sentence must align with the query intent as if you are answering the question, not advertising. AI search users are in research mode — they tolerate ads that extend the answer, not interrupt it.

Bad: "TrustLayer is the #1 compliance automation tool."
Good: "Most compliance teams spend 15+ hours per week manually tracking vendor certificates — automation reduces this to under 2 hours with no loss in audit coverage."

**R — Reference-Worthy Proof:**
Include one piece of verifiable, specific evidence in every ad:
- Customer outcome with company name or recognizable attribute ("A 600-person FinTech reduced close time by 34%")
- Third-party validation ("Gartner Peer Insights score: 4.8/5 | 312 reviews")
- Original research statistic ("In our 2025 State of [Category] survey, 67% of [ICP] said...")

AI search users who click ads from AI platforms have higher intent than average display users — they clicked because the proof resonated, not because of the headline. The proof IS the hook.

**C — Context-Specific CTA:**
Match the CTA to the research stage the query implies:
- Awareness/research query → Low-friction asset CTA: "Get the 2025 [Category] Benchmark Report"
- Comparison query → Decision-enablement CTA: "Compare [Your Brand] vs [Top 2 Competitors] → Side-by-side"
- Solution-aware query → Direct CTA: "Book a 15-min discovery call → [Company] for [ICP role]"

Avoid generic CTAs ("Learn more," "Get started") — AI search users have already "learned more" from the AI answer. Your ad must offer specific value beyond what the AI answer already provided.

**S — Soft Landing:**
Landing pages for AI search ad traffic should open with the insight the user was researching, not a hero banner and generic value proposition. Consider:
- Query-matched landing pages: If the user asked "how to reduce SaaS procurement risk," your landing page should open with data about procurement risk — not your product homepage
- Progressive CTA architecture: First fold = insight/proof → Second fold = product connection → Third fold = conversion CTA
- Social proof placement: Reviews, logos, and case study thumbnails above the fold — AI search ad clickers are in trust-verification mode

### 6. Attribution Architecture for AI Search Advertising

**The Attribution Challenge:**
AI search advertising creates a more complex attribution problem than traditional paid search because:
1. AI search is often used in the middle of a longer research journey — not as the first or last touch
2. The AI answer itself (organic) may have already influenced the buyer before the paid ad appeared
3. Perplexity and Copilot referral traffic often appears as "direct" or "(not set)" in GA4 without proper UTM tagging

**UTM Framework for AI Search Ad Attribution:**

| Platform | utm_source | utm_medium | utm_campaign | utm_content |
|----------|-----------|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Perplexity Ads | perplexity | paid-ai-search | [campaign-name] | [topic-cluster] |
| Microsoft Copilot | copilot | paid-ai-search | [campaign-name] | [ad-group] |
| Google AIO adjacent | google | paid-ai-search | [campaign-name] | [query-type] |
| ChatGPT Search Ads | chatgpt | paid-ai-search | [campaign-name] | [placement] |

Configure these UTM parameters in all AI search ad destination URLs and train your CRM/analytics team to recognize `utm_medium=paid-ai-search` as a distinct attribution bucket — separate from traditional paid search and display.

**Multi-Touch Attribution Model for AI Search:**
AI search ad touches typically appear in positions 2–4 in a multi-touch journey. Recommended attribution model:
- Use a data-driven attribution (DDA) model in GA4 if you have >400 conversions/month — DDA algorithmically weights the AI search touch appropriately
- For smaller accounts: use a Position-Based model (40% first touch / 20% middle touches / 40% last touch) — this gives AI search ad touches appropriate credit when they occur mid-journey
- Track "AI Search Influenced Pipeline" as a separate CRM metric: flag any opportunity where the account had a paid-ai-search session in the 30 days prior to conversion — report this to leadership as a new metric

**Incrementality Testing for AI Search Ads:**
Because AI search organic and AI search paid often appear simultaneously to the same buyer, it is critical to measure true incrementality:
- Run a 30-day geo holdout test: pause AI search ads in 2–3 matched markets, compare conversion rates vs. markets where ads remain active
- Track organic AI search referral traffic separately (Perplexity organic referrals appear as referral traffic from perplexity.ai in GA4 — tag these separately from paid)
- Measure "share of AI recommendation" both with and without paid — if organic share of voice is low and paid is driving all AI search conversions, paid investment is high-value; if organic already dominates, paid incremental value may be lower

### 7. 60-Day AI Search Advertising Launch Roadmap

**Week 1–2: Foundation**
- Audit current AI search brand presence: run 20 buyer research queries in Perplexity, Copilot, and ChatGPT — document which queries mention your brand, which mention competitors, which produce no relevant results
- Configure UTM framework and GA4 AI search attribution
- Enable Microsoft Copilot placement in existing Microsoft Advertising campaigns (lowest effort, fastest activation)
- Baseline Google AI Overviews: identify which SERP queries trigger AIOs for your category and map your paid ad positions relative to AIO containers

**Week 3–4: Perplexity AI Ads Launch**
- Apply for Perplexity AI Ads access (self-serve or managed — currently limited beta for some verticals)
- Launch 2 topic clusters: one comparison query cluster, one problem-aware cluster
- Deploy ARCS framework copy — write 3 ad variations per cluster
- Set initial CPM bid: start at $12–18 CPM for B2B — Perplexity AI Ads are relatively early-stage, CPMs are lower than LinkedIn; expect 0.3–0.8% CTR for well-matched sponsored answers

**Week 5–6: Optimization & Google AIO Adaptation**
- Review Microsoft Copilot performance: compare CPA from Copilot placement vs. standard Microsoft Search — if >30% CPA difference, segment Copilot into its own campaign for separate bidding
- Audit Google Ads RSA asset quality for AIO-context relevance — add ARCS framework headlines to existing RSAs
- Build 3 query-matched landing pages for top AI search entry points
- Perplexity first optimization: identify which topic clusters are generating clicks vs. impressions only — shift budget to highest CTR clusters

**Week 7–8: Scale & Attribution Review**
- Pull 30-day multi-touch attribution report: quantify "AI Search Influenced Pipeline" — share with leadership as a new pipeline source category
- Incrementality assessment: compare conversion rates in geo holdout markets
- Scale winning Perplexity topic clusters by 30–50% budget
- Identify 2 new topic clusters based on buyer query audit data
- Prepare 60-day performance brief: Impressions / Clicks / CTR / Conversions / CPA / Pipeline Influenced — present AI search advertising as a distinct investment category alongside Google and LinkedIn

CONSTRAINTS:
- Never write AI search ad copy that reads like traditional display advertising — answer-native copy outperforms interruptive copy by 2–4x in early Perplexity Ads data
- Never conflate GEO (organic AI search optimization) with AI search advertising — they require different tactics, different metrics, and different teams; manage them separately
- Do not launch Perplexity Ads without configuring UTM attribution first — the default referral data from Perplexity is unreliable for CRM attribution
- For deal sizes under $5K ACV: AI search advertising ROI is likely insufficient at current CPMs unless you have a strong PLG funnel — prioritize Google and LinkedIn first
- AI search ad platforms are evolving rapidly: build a monthly platform review cadence — ad products, targeting options, and pricing on Perplexity, Copilot, and Google AIO change quarterly

## Integration Tips

- **Microsoft Advertising**: Enabling Copilot network targeting requires no new campaign structure — go to campaign Settings → Network Settings → check "Microsoft Copilot" under "Extended network." Monitor Copilot performance via the "Network" segmentation in the Microsoft Ads reporting tab. Separate Copilot into its own campaign only if CPA diverges from standard search by >30% — at that point, independent bid management becomes necessary.
- **HubSpot**: Create a custom contact property "AI Search Ad Source" and a workflow that sets this property when a contact has a session with `utm_medium=paid-ai-search` at any point in their history. This feeds into deal influence reporting — marketing can show leadership that AI search ad touches exist across the pipeline without requiring last-touch credit.
- **Salesforce**: Build an "AI Search Influenced Opportunities" report using a custom Opportunity field populated by an automated process tied to HubSpot contact history. Present this report in the monthly CMO dashboard as "AI Search Influenced Pipeline $X" — a new KPI category distinct from "AI Search Sourced Pipeline."
- **Google Analytics 4**: Create a custom dimension `ai_search_channel` populated from the UTM `utm_medium=paid-ai-search` parameter. Build a dedicated GA4 exploration report: AI search session → engagement rate → goal completion rate — compare to overall paid search and display to demonstrate the higher intent quality of AI search ad visitors.
- **Perplexity AI Ads**: Access via perplexity.ai/ads or through approved reseller partners. As of 2025, self-serve access is available for US-targeted campaigns; international targeting may require managed service agreements. Minimum test budget: $2,500/month for statistically valid CTR data within 30 days. ## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Perplexity AI Ads are generating impressions but near-zero clicks — CTR below 0.1% despite running for 3 weeks.
**Solution**: Near-zero CTR on Perplexity sponsored answers almost always indicates a copy-context mismatch — the ad reads like a traditional paid search ad rather than an answer-native extension. Audit your ad copy against the ARCS framework: Does the first sentence align with the query intent as if answering a question? If your ad opens with your brand name, a superlative ("the #1 platform"), or a generic CTA, rewrite it to open with the insight or data point the user was seeking. Additionally, check topic targeting — if your topics are too broad (e.g., "SaaS software") rather than intent-specific (e.g., "procurement cost reduction strategies"), you are reaching users whose query intent does not match your solution stage.

**Problem**: Microsoft Copilot Ads have a 3x higher CPA than standard Microsoft Search — making the channel appear uneconomical.
**Solution**: Before concluding Copilot is uneconomical, check whether you are comparing the right conversion events. Copilot users are often earlier in the research journey than branded-search clickers — the Copilot visitor who clicks through today may convert via branded search in 3 weeks. Pull a 60-day multi-touch attribution report and check whether Copilot sessions appear as first or second touches on closed opportunities — if so, the "last-touch CPA" comparison is misleading. If Copilot truly is lower-intent and not appearing anywhere in the conversion path, segment it out of your Microsoft Advertising campaign into a separate campaign with its own landing pages designed for research-stage visitors (not conversion-stage pages), and measure micro-conversion rates (content downloads, pricing page visits) rather than demo requests as the primary Copilot KPI.

**Problem**: UTM parameters from Perplexity Ads are not appearing consistently in HubSpot — approximately 40% of sessions show as "direct" or "unknown" despite proper UTM configuration.
**Solution**: Perplexity's AI answer interface sometimes strips UTM parameters when it renders the outbound link in a sponsored answer, especially on mobile. Fixes: (1) Use HubSpot's tracking URL builder and ensure the UTM parameters are appended to the destination URL in your Perplexity Ads campaign settings, not added post-click. (2) Implement UTM capture via a first-party cookie at the landing page level using a lightweight JavaScript snippet — this captures the referrer even when Perplexity strips the UTM from the URL. (3) Configure Perplexity referral traffic (perplexity.ai as referring domain) as a separate attributed source in HubSpot, even without UTM data — this at least allows you to track the organic vs. paid split once you have a baseline of organic Perplexity referrals established.
