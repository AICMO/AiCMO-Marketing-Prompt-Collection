## Overview
Generates a complete multivariate creative testing system across Google, Meta, and LinkedIn — producing 20–40 statistically distinct ad variants, a structured test matrix, statistical significance thresholds, winner identification logic, and an autonomous iteration loop. Use it when launching a new campaign, refreshing stale creatives, or scaling spend where creative fatigue is limiting performance.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: No statistical significance after 3 weeks and $5K+ spent**
Solution: Sample size is likely too small for your CPL target. Calculate minimum conversions needed: for 95% confidence with 20% relative uplift, you need ~85 conversions per variant. At $400 CPL, that's $34K per variant — reduce variants to 3–4 in Wave 1, or lower the significance threshold to 80% for early-stage creative learning (acceptable for directional signals, not final budget decisions).

**Problem: CTR is high (>1.5% LinkedIn, >3% Meta) but CPL is terrible**
Solution: This is a post-click problem, not a creative problem. The winning hook is attracting clicks from the wrong audience or the landing page has a messaging mismatch. Run the winning ad copy through the Landing Page Copy Generation prompt and ensure the first headline mirrors the ad hook exactly. Check audience exclusions — high CTR with low CVR often means irrelevant traffic clicking out of curiosity.

**Problem: All variants are performing similarly — no clear winner**
Solution: Your variables aren't differentiated enough. Regenerate the hook variants with more extreme contrast — if current hooks are all benefit-led, add one pure pattern interrupt (e.g., a counterintuitive statistic) and one pure social proof (a specific named customer metric). Similarity in results = similarity in inputs. Use the Advanced Version's "creative intelligence audit" section to score existing variants and identify which dimension has the lowest variance, then amplify contrast there.
