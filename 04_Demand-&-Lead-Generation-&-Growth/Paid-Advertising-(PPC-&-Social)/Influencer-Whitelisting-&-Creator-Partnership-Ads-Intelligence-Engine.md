## Overview
This engine designs end-to-end influencer whitelisting and creator partnership ad programs — where brands run paid ads from creators' accounts (whitelisting), boosting organic creator content as media. It handles creator vetting for paid amplification, permission/contract structures, platform-specific execution (Meta Partnership Ads, TikTok Spark Ads, Pinterest Idea Ads, LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads), creative brief writing, audience targeting overlays, and performance attribution — turning creators into always-on paid media channels at a fraction of traditional CPM.

## Integration Tips

- **Meta Business Manager**: Link creator's Page or Instagram Professional Account as a partner asset to enable Partnership Ads. Under Business Settings → Accounts → Ad accounts, request access at "Advertiser" level. Creator keeps content ownership; you get amplification rights.
- **TikTok Ads Manager**: Request Spark Ads authorization code from creators via TikTok Creator Marketplace or direct DM. Codes expire in 30/60/90 days — set calendar reminders 10 days before expiration to renew.
- **Grin / Modash / Upfluence**: Export creator audience analytics (demographics, authenticity score, engagement breakdown) directly into your performance tracking spreadsheet for unified creator scoring.
- **Google Sheets + Zapier**: Build an automated creator performance dashboard — Zapier pulls Meta/TikTok API data, populates per-creator ROAS, flags underperformers via Slack alert when ROAS drops below threshold.
- **Triple Whale / Northbeam**: Tag all creator whitelist campaigns with UTM parameters `utm_source=creator-whitelist&utm_campaign=[creator_handle]` to isolate creator contribution in your MTA model.
- **Notion**: Store creator agreements, content briefs, and approval workflows in a Notion database with status columns (Briefed → Filming → Review → Approved → Live → Reporting).

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Creator content gets flagged or rejected in Meta Ads Manager after whitelisting**
- Root cause: Usually missing paid partnership disclosure, or ad copy makes health/earnings claims that trigger policy review
- Fix: Ensure creator has enabled "Paid Partnership" label on the original post BEFORE generating the ad. Remove any superlative claims ("best," "fastest," "guaranteed results") from both caption and script. Re-submit with manual review request noting FTC-compliant partnership disclosure is active.

**Issue: Creator whitelist ads show lower ROAS than brand-owned creative**
- Root cause: Likely an audience mismatch — creator's audience doesn't overlap with your ICP — or the content brief was too polished and lost authenticity signal
- Fix: Pull audience demographics from creator's Instagram/TikTok analytics before launching. If <60% audience-ICP match, don't whitelist that creator. For brief quality, brief for authentic reaction, not scripted testimonial. Test 50% budget on creator's native audience via their account vs. 50% on your cold audiences using creator's creative.

**Issue: Creator refuses to grant whitelisting access or demands $5K+ for a micro-influencer**
- Root cause: Creator unfamiliarity with whitelisting model and fear of losing account control; or inflated rate expectations from brands misquoting market rates
- Fix: Educate creator that they retain full content ownership and can revoke access anytime. Provide a one-page explainer on "what whitelisting means for your account" (no follower risk, no data access, only ad amplification). For rate negotiation: micro-influencer (15K–100K) whitelisting fee industry benchmark is $500–$2K/month — anchor to this with performance bonus clause if ROAS exceeds threshold.
