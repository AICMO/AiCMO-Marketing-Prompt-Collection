## Overview
Generates a data-driven paid media budget allocation plan across all active channels (Google, Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, programmatic, etc.) by analyzing historical performance, channel saturation signals, and funnel stage gaps — then outputs a reallocation roadmap with projected ROAS improvements, channel-level spend caps, and a weekly optimization cadence. Use it monthly or when performance plateaus, budgets shift by more than 20%, or a new channel is being tested.

## Integration Tips

- **Google Ads**: Pull 90-day performance data via Google Ads API or Looker Studio → paste directly into the Advanced prompt's channel performance table. Enable auto-tagging and offline conversion imports for Salesforce/HubSpot sync.
- **Meta Ads Manager**: Export campaign-level data via the Custom Report builder (Columns: Spend, Results, CPA, ROAS, Frequency, Reach, CPM). High frequency (>4.0) is your saturation warning signal.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Use the Revenue Attribution Report (requires CRM integration) to see pipeline influence — this corrects for last-click distortion before feeding into the prompt.
- **HubSpot/Salesforce**: Build a paid media attribution dashboard that shows "first touch + last touch + multi-touch influenced" by channel. Export as CSV and paste into prompt context to dramatically improve accuracy.
- **Supermetrics / Funnel.io**: Automate the 90-day performance table extraction — schedule weekly exports to Google Sheets, then paste current snapshot into prompt on the 1st of each month.
- **Google Looker Studio**: Build a media mix dashboard with channel-level CPA trend lines — identify the inflection point where CPA starts rising (the saturation signal) and paste that trend data into the prompt.
- **Northbeam / Triple Whale (D2C)**: For e-commerce, use MTA data models from these platforms as inputs instead of platform-reported ROAS, which overcounts due to view-through attribution.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: The AI recommends cutting LinkedIn entirely because the CPA looks terrible, but I know it drives pipeline.
**Solution**: Provide the attribution-adjusted data — pull LinkedIn's "influenced pipeline" from Salesforce (filter Opportunities where LinkedIn was any touchpoint). Paste that figure into the prompt context. LinkedIn influence ratios of 50-70% for B2B are normal and completely invisible to last-click models. Tell the prompt your attribution model is last-click and add: "LinkedIn's attribution-adjusted CPA is estimated at $[X] based on pipeline influence data."

**Problem**: The budget allocation comes back with too many small incremental changes — feels like it's not making decisive calls.
**Solution**: Add this constraint to the Advanced prompt: "I have a high risk tolerance for reallocation. Make bold recommendations — minimum ±25% change per channel unless the channel is already perfectly sized. I would rather over-rotate and correct than under-optimize."

**Problem**: The projections seem too optimistic — it's promising 30% ROAS improvement in 30 days.
**Solution**: Add: "Apply a conservative confidence discount — assume 60% of expected improvement materializes in 30 days due to algorithm learning periods, creative iteration time, and audience ramp-up. Show a 'base case' (60% achievement) and 'full case' (100% achievement) scenario in the projection table."
