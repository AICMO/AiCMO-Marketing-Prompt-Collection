## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Create a custom "Podcast" source in HubSpot's Source property for leads who enter via podcast vanity URLs
- Set up workflow: contact submits form with UTM source=podcast → assign to Podcast nurture sequence → notify SDR with podcast context
- Build a Podcast ROI dashboard: contacts, MQLs, SQLs, and pipeline by podcast show using Deal Source field

**Salesforce:**
- Create custom Campaign type "Podcast Sponsorship" with sub-type for each show
- Use Salesforce Campaign Influence to attribute podcast touches across multi-touch deals
- Build a report: Opportunities with any Podcast Campaign Influence → pipeline and closed-won by show

**Podscribe / Chartable:**
- Embed the Podscribe pixel across your website for automatic listener identity resolution
- Connect Podscribe to your HubSpot or Salesforce to auto-create contacts from verified listeners
- Use Podscribe's show discovery tool to find shows where your ICP actually concentrates

**Spotify Audience Network:**
- Build B2B firmographic audience: Business & Technology interest + Job Seniority = Manager+ + Company Size = 200+
- Layer with behavioral: users who stream business/news podcasts in the last 30 days
- Exclude: audience segments that overlap with your current retargeting pool to avoid waste

**Notion/Airtable Campaign Tracker:**
- Build a Podcast Sponsorship Tracker with fields: Show Name, Host, Flight Dates, CPM, Budget, Vanity URL, Promo Code, Weekly Listeners, Attributed Leads, Attributed Pipeline, CPL, CPCL, Renewal Decision
- Connect to HubSpot via Zapier: when a podcast-source deal closes-won in HubSpot, auto-update the tracker

**Zapier Automation:**
- Trigger: New HubSpot contact with UTM source = podcast
- Action: Send Slack notification to revenue team with contact details + podcast show attribution
- Action 2: Add contact to Salesforce Campaign for the specific show

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Vanity URLs get very few visits even though the show has 50K listeners.**
Solution: This is normal — podcast ad response rates are low (0.5-2% click-through) because audio doesn't allow clicking. The real value shows up in brand search lift and direct traffic lift. Check Google Search Console: did branded search queries increase during the flight? Did direct traffic spike on episode release days? If yes, the ad is working. If no, the audience-to-ICP match is poor — cut the show.

**Problem: Host-read ads sound stilted or too corporate when the host reads the script.**
Solution: Send the script as a brief, not a script. Provide: (1) the key points you need covered, (2) the vanity URL and promo offer, (3) one customer result you want mentioned. Let the host riff in their own voice. Baked-in host-read ads that sound native to the show outperform corporate scripts by 40-60% in recall studies. Trust the host — that's what you're paying the premium for.

**Problem: Finance team is pushing back on podcast ROI because attribution is murky.**
Solution: Run a 30-day holdout test. Identify 2 shows with similar audiences — run ads on Show A, go dark on Show B. Measure the difference in brand search volume, direct traffic, and inbound demo requests between the two periods. Alternatively, use Podscribe's deterministic attribution to show exactly how many listeners visited your website and converted. Present the CFO with CPCL vs Google Ads CPC — podcast-attributed leads typically have 2-3x higher close rates because they arrive with pre-built brand trust.
