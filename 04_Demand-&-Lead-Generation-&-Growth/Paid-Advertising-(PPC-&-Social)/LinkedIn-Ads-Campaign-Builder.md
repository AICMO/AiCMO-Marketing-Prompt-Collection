## Success Metrics

- **Click-Through Rate (CTR):** Target >0.5% for Sponsored Content (LinkedIn average: 0.35–0.45%); >30% open rate for Message Ads

- **Cost Per Lead (CPL):** B2B SaaS benchmark $60–$120; enterprise ($50K+ ACV) accept up to $200 CPL if lead quality is high
- **Lead-to-MQL Rate:** Track in CRM — target >40% of LinkedIn leads becoming marketing-qualified
- **Cost Per Opportunity (CPO):** Connect LinkedIn spend to Salesforce opportunity creation; target CPO < 20% of ACV
- **Pipeline Influenced:** Monthly LinkedIn-attributed pipeline value reported to revenue team
- **Frequency:** Monitor impressions per member — keep below 4/week per campaign to avoid ad fatigue

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce (Native LinkedIn Integration):** Enable the native LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms sync in HubSpot or Salesforce — leads flow in under 60 seconds with no Zapier required. Map LinkedIn "Company Size" field to CRM company size property for automatic MQL scoring.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager + Salesforce Revenue Attribution:** Use LinkedIn's Revenue Attribution Report (requires Salesforce connected) to see pipeline and closed-won revenue influenced by LinkedIn — the most credible metric for CFO budget conversations.
- **Zapier / Make:** For CRMs without native LinkedIn sync, use Zapier to trigger: New Lead Gen Form submission → create/update Contact in CRM → enroll in nurture sequence → notify assigned SDR in Slack within 5 minutes.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Sync engaged LinkedIn Ads audiences to Sales Navigator lists so SDRs can see which prospects interacted with ads before cold outreach — increases reply rates 30–50% when referenced in outreach.
- **Google Analytics 4 / Segment:** Pass LinkedIn click UTMs through to GA4 and your CDP. Tag all LGF submissions with `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=paid-social` to unify attribution across sessions, not just last-click.
- **Looker Studio / Tableau:** Build a weekly LinkedIn Ads performance dashboard pulling Campaign Manager API data (via Porter Metrics or Supermetrics) alongside Salesforce pipeline data — gives revenue team a single view of spend-to-pipeline efficiency.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CPL is high ($200+) and leads are not converting to MQLs**
Solution: Audit audience targeting first — check if LinkedIn Audience Expansion is enabled and diluting your ICP with off-target members. Review Lead Gen Form custom questions: are they filtering for unqualified job functions or company sizes? If LGF is the culprit, test a landing page-click variant to let your own qualification form filter harder. Consider tightening seniority to Director+ only.

**Problem: Low CTR (<0.3%) across all Sponsored Content ads**
Solution: The hook (first 150 characters) determines whether members expand "...see more." Test a radically different hook style: if you've been running stats-led hooks, try a provocative question or contrarian statement. Ensure the visual is thumb-stopping — LinkedIn feeds are text-heavy, so a bold single-color graphic or a real customer photo outperforms generic stock imagery. Check that your audience size is not too small (<10,000 members), which causes rapid frequency fatigue.

**Problem: Lead Gen Form has high impression-to-open rate but low completion rate (<5%)**
Solution: The form is losing people at field review. Audit: are you asking for more than 6 fields? Remove anything not essential for sales routing. Replace custom free-text questions with dropdown menus (reduces friction by 40%). Rewrite the "Details" copy (160 chars) to emphasize what the lead receives — shift from describing your product to describing the specific value of the offer (e.g., "3-page cheat sheet, instant PDF download, no sales call required").
