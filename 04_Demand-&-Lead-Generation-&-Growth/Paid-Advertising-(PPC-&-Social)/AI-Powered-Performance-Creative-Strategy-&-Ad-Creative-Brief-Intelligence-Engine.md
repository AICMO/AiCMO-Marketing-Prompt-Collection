## Success Metrics

- **Creative production efficiency:** Time from brief to launch-ready asset (target: under 48 hours with AI tools)
- **Hook rate (video):** 3-second view rate ≥ 35% on Meta/TikTok; 15% on YouTube pre-roll
- **CTR benchmark:** LinkedIn ≥ 0.5%; Meta ≥ 1.5%; Google Display ≥ 0.35%
- **A/B test velocity:** Minimum 2 tests running simultaneously; winner declared within 2 weeks
- **Creative coverage ratio:** 1 active creative concept per $3,000 monthly spend (prevents fatigue)
- **ROAS lift:** New creative strategy should lift ROAS 20-40% vs. pre-strategy baseline within 60 days
- **Concept hit rate:** At least 2 of 6 concepts should outperform account historical CTR by ≥ 15%

## Integration Tips

- **Figma / Canva / Adobe Express:** Feed the visual brief directly into your design tool's project notes. Add platform dimensions and safe zones from Section 5 as the template constraints before briefing a designer or AI.
- **Midjourney / DALL-E 3 / Ideogram:** Use the AI generation prompts from Section 5 verbatim — they're engineered to produce brand-safe, scroll-stopping visuals without revision loops.
- **HubSpot Ads:** Tag each creative concept in HubSpot's ad library using the concept name from this engine (e.g., "Monday Morning Problem") to enable cross-campaign performance comparison by message theme.
- **Meta Ads Manager / LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Upload all 6 concepts as separate ad sets under one campaign in Dynamic Creative Testing mode. Let the platform optimize delivery; use your A/B hypotheses to interpret why the winner won.
- **Notion / Google Docs:** Create a Creative Performance Log using the concept names from this engine as row headers, and track CTR, CPM, CVR, and spend weekly. Feed performance data back into the advanced prompt's "Current creative performance" field on the next refresh cycle.
- **Loom / Vidyard:** For B2B video concepts, record a 60-second founder or CSM walkthrough using the hook and body copy from the brief as your script skeleton. Authenticity outperforms polish on LinkedIn and YouTube.
- **Slack → Zapier → Ads Platform:** Set up a Zapier workflow that fires a Slack alert when any active creative's CTR drops below your fatigue threshold (Section 6), prompting immediate creative refresh before ROAS degrades.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The generated concepts feel too similar — they all sound like the same ad.**
Solution: In the Advanced Version, ensure your "Conversation intelligence" field includes verbatim customer quotes (not paraphrased summaries). Emotional differentiation comes from actual customer language. Also explicitly name 2 emotional territories you do NOT want to use (e.g., "exclude fear-based angles — we've tested them and they underperform in our category").

**Problem: The visual briefs are too vague for a designer to execute without follow-up.**
Solution: Re-run Section 3 with more brand context in the "Visual brand assets available" field. Specify exactly what assets exist (product UI screenshots, customer headshots, logo colors). Also add: "My designer has zero brand context — brief as if handing to a cold freelancer on Upwork." The model will add more production specificity.

**Problem: A/B test results aren't statistically significant before creative fatigue sets in.**
Solution: This is a budget threshold problem. Below $5,000/month per concept, you rarely reach significance before fatigue kills the data. Use the prompt's A/B plan but run tests directionally at low budgets — call winners at 60% confidence (not 95%) and use qualitative signals (comments, engagement, save rates) to supplement quantitative data.
