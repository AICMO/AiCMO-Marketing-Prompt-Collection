## Success Metrics

- **ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sales):** Primary efficiency metric. Target varies by goal phase: 35–50% during rank acceleration, <25% during profitability mode. Benchmark: category average ACOS typically 20–35% for competitive categories.
- **TACOS (Total ACOS including organic sales):** True efficiency signal. A declining TACOS while ad spend holds flat = organic rank improvement working. Target: TACOS <15% for established products.
- **New-to-Brand Orders %:** For Sponsored Brands. Target >60% NTB orders = ads are acquiring new customers, not just re-converting existing buyers.
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR):** SP benchmark: 0.35–0.55% on category keywords. <0.20% signals a listing image or title problem, not a bid problem.
- **Conversion Rate (CVR):** Strong benchmark is 12–18% for well-reviewed products in competitive categories. <8% = listing optimization needed before scaling spend.
- **Keyword Rank on Priority Terms:** Track weekly in Brand Analytics or third-party tools (Helium 10, DataDive). Movement from page 3 → page 2 → page 1 validates rank acceleration investment.
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS):** Revenue ÷ Ad Spend. Target 4x+ ROAS for profitable scaling; use as primary DSP metric.

## Integration Tips

- **Helium 10 / DataDive / Jungle Scout:** Export keyword data from these tools to pre-populate your Exact and Phrase campaign keyword lists. Use Cerebro (Helium 10) to reverse-engineer competitor ASIN keywords before bidding.
- **Amazon Seller Central Bulk Operations:** Download the Bulk Operations template from Campaign Manager, populate keyword bids and campaign settings from your AI output, and upload directly — bypasses manual campaign creation for 50+ keywords.
- **Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC):** Run SQL queries to analyze multi-touch paths (e.g., shoppers who saw SB → clicked SP → converted), cross-device reach, and audience overlap between campaigns. Requires DSP or SB+ spend.
- **Klaviyo / Postscript (SMS):** After purchase, trigger a review request sequence at day 5 and day 14 post-delivery. Higher review velocity improves organic CTR and reduces required ad spend over time. Add Amazon Attribution links to email flows to credit email-driven sessions.
- **Google Sheets + Amazon Ads API:** Build a live bid management dashboard by pulling Amazon Ads API data into Google Sheets via a Supermetrics or custom connector. Set conditional formatting to flag keywords above target ACOS in red.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate weekly Search Term Report export → Google Sheets → negative keyword update workflow. Schedule every Monday morning; reduces manual negative keyword hygiene from 2 hours to 15 minutes.
- **Amazon Attribution:** Create attribution tags for every external traffic source (TikTok, Meta, Google, email). Amazon rewards external traffic with organic rank boosts — quantify this lift using the Amazon Attribution dashboard's "attributed sales" metric.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: SP campaigns spending budget by 10am daily with poor ACOS**
Solution: Your Auto or Broad campaigns are matching to irrelevant high-volume terms. Immediately download the Search Term Report, identify the top spend/no-conversion terms, and add 20–30 exact-match negatives. Reduce Auto campaign bid on "Loose Match" from your current bid to $0.25–$0.35 — Loose Match is notoriously inefficient for discovery. Shift 30% of budget from Auto to your Exact campaign which has proven terms.

**Problem: Zero impressions on newly launched Exact match campaigns**
Solution: Your bids are below the first-page threshold. In Campaign Manager, check the "Suggested Bid" and "Bid Range" columns — Amazon shows the range needed for page 1 placement. Increase bids to match or exceed the lower end of the suggested range. Also verify your listing is in the correct category and is Buy Box-eligible (FBA products almost always win Buy Box; FBM listings may need competitive pricing). Finally, confirm the keywords you're targeting have sufficient search volume — niche keywords under 1,000 monthly searches may show zero impressions for weeks.

**Problem: Great ACOS on Sponsored Products but overall profitability declining (TACOS rising)**
Solution: High ACOS on ads but low organic sales means ad-driven conversions are not converting to organic rank. Diagnose: pull your weekly keyword rank for priority terms in Brand Analytics (Search Frequency Rank). If rank is not improving after 30 days of sustained SP spend, your listing conversion rate is too low to benefit from increased traffic. Prioritize listing optimization first: A/B test main image using Manage Your Experiments, improve bullet points to address top review concerns, and ensure pricing is within 10% of top-3 competitors. A 2% CVR improvement will lower ACOS more than any bid change.
