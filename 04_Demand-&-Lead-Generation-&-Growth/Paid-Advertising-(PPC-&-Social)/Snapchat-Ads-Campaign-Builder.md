## Success Metrics

- **Cost Per Action (CPA):** Primary conversion metric. Benchmark varies by category: DTC beauty $18–$45; gaming app install $2–$8; fashion apparel $25–$60. Snap-attributed CPA is typically 20–40% lower than last-click — reconcile with GA4 for true CPA.
- **Hook Rate (2-Second View Rate):** % of impressions that watch 2+ seconds. Top performers: >25%. Below 10% signals a hook that fails to stop the scroll — creative must be replaced immediately.
- **Swipe-Up Rate (SUR):** % of viewers who swipe up to destination. Strong benchmark: 1.5–2.5% for DTC; 0.8–1.2% for brand awareness. Below 0.5% signals CTA/creative-destination mismatch.
- **Video Completion Rate (VCR):** % of viewers watching the full video. Benchmark for 10-second ads: 25%+ (top quartile); 15-second ads: 18%+. High VCR + Low SUR = engaging content with weak CTA.
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS):** Revenue ÷ Ad Spend. Snap-attributed ROAS for DTC e-commerce: 2.5x–5x is typical; below 1.5x is generally unsustainable. Validate against blended ROAS (total revenue ÷ total Snap spend) to account for view-through attribution.
- **Audience Growth Rate:** New unique reach added per week within target demo. Declining reach on a fixed budget = audience saturation; expand targeting or increase budget to maintain CPM efficiency.
- **Creative Fatigue Score:** Track CPM trend per creative — rising CPM (>20% week-over-week) with no targeting changes = creative fatigue. Retire and replace.
- **Lens Engagement Rate (if AR):** Lens plays per impression, play time, and share rate. Benchmark: top AR Lenses average 20+ seconds of play time and 30%+ share rate — sharing is a virality multiplier unique to Snapchat.

## Integration Tips

- **Shopify:** Install the Snap Sales Channel app from the Shopify App Store. This automatically syncs your product catalog, enables Snap Pixel firing on key events, and activates Conversions API (CAPI) via server-side events — significantly improves match rate on iOS 14.5+ traffic. Takes 15 minutes to configure.
- **Klaviyo / Attentive:** After a Snapchat-driven click, serve a timed email/SMS capture popup (5–8 seconds post-scroll) with a 10% first-order incentive. Snap traffic converts to email at 8–15% with a strong incentive — building a first-party list reduces platform dependency significantly.
- **Appsflyer / Adjust / Branch (for app campaigns):** Configure Snap as an integrated partner in your MMP. Pass install and in-app event data back to Snap for SKAN-compliant attribution on iOS and deterministic attribution on Android. Set postback windows to match your Snap attribution model.
- **Google Analytics 4:** Add UTM parameters (utm_source=snapchat, utm_medium=cpc, utm_campaign=[name], utm_content=[creative_id]) to all Snap destination URLs. Build a GA4 Snapchat channel group to track user behavior post-click, session quality, and revenue attribution independently of Snap's self-reported numbers.
- **Supermetrics / Funnel.io:** Connect Snap Ads Manager API to pull CPM, SUR, Hook Rate, and ROAS data into Google Sheets or Looker Studio alongside Meta and TikTok. Build a weekly paid social dashboard that normalizes performance across platforms using comparable metrics (cost per landing page view, cost per 2-second view, cost per add-to-cart).
- **Snap Conversions API (CAPI) Direct Integration:** For brands not on Shopify, implement CAPI via server-side API calls from your e-commerce backend. Passes hashed PII (email, phone) for privacy-safe event matching. Typically recovers 15–25% of purchase events lost to iOS ATT signal loss — directly lowers Snap-reported CPA.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate weekly creative performance export from Snap Ads Manager → Google Sheets → conditional format flag any creative with Hook Rate <10% or CPM rising >30% week-over-week → auto-generate Slack alert to paid media team with recommended action ("pause" or "refresh creative").

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Campaigns stuck in "Learning" phase with slow delivery and high CPAs for 2+ weeks**
Solution: Snapchat's algorithm requires a minimum of 50 conversion events per week per ad set to exit the learning phase. If you are below this threshold, you have three options: (1) Broaden your audience targeting — remove restrictive age, interest, or geo layers to increase auction eligibility; (2) Switch to a higher-funnel objective (ADD_TO_CART or PAGE_VIEW instead of PURCHASE) to accumulate events faster, then graduate to PURCHASE optimization once the algorithm learns your audience; (3) Consolidate ad sets — running 8 small ad sets at $50/day each will never exit learning individually; merge into 2–3 ad sets at $200/day. The learning phase resets every time you change budget by more than 20%, creative, or audience — minimize edits in the first 7–14 days.

**Problem: Hook Rate is acceptable (>20%) but Swipe-Up Rate is extremely low (<0.5%) with minimal purchases**
Solution: Your creative is stopping the scroll but failing to motivate action — this is a CTA and destination mismatch problem, not a creative quality problem. Audit three things: (1) Does the CTA button text match what the user expects to find? "Shop Now" leading to a homepage kills conversion; it must link directly to the product detail page (PDP) with an above-the-fold add-to-cart button; (2) Is the landing page mobile-optimized with <3-second load time on 4G? Snap users will abandon immediately if the page is slow or desktop-designed; (3) Is the offer compelling enough? Test adding a time-limited discount or free shipping threshold visible in the last 2 seconds of the ad. Also test changing the CTA from "Shop Now" to "See Price" or "Get 15% Off" — specificity outperforms generic calls to action on Snapchat.

**Problem: CPA is hitting target in Snap Ads Manager but GA4 shows zero or minimal attributed revenue from Snapchat**
Solution: This is a platform attribution inflation issue — extremely common on Snapchat. Snapchat's default attribution model (7-day swipe + 1-day view) captures many conversions that other platforms (Meta, Google) also claim credit for in their own windows. To reconcile: (1) Pull a Snap "Cross-Network Report" in Ads Manager to see impression-only vs. click-driven conversions; if >40% of attributed conversions are view-through, your true incremental CPA is likely 1.5–2x the Snap-reported number; (2) Run a Snap Conversion Lift Study (minimum $50K spend recommended) to measure true incrementality — Snap provides this as a self-serve feature in Ads Manager; (3) Implement Snap CAPI alongside GA4 UTM tracking to build a parallel data set — use GA4's Snapchat-attributed revenue as your conservative floor and Snap Manager as your ceiling; true performance lives between the two.
