**3. CREATIVE STRATEGY AND PRODUCTION**

*The 2-Second Rule — Hook Architecture:*
TikTok users make a keep/scroll decision in under 2 seconds. The hook must achieve one of:
- Visual disruption: unexpected or unusual footage that triggers curiosity ("What is that?")
- Personal relevance trigger: on-screen text that calls out the exact viewer ("POV: you're a [descriptor]")
- Bold result claim: lead with the outcome, not the product ("I lost 15 lbs in 6 weeks — here's exactly how")
- Pattern interrupt sound: distinctive audio in the first 0.5 seconds that arrests the scroll reflex

*The TikTok Creative Framework (AACE Model):*
All high-performing TikTok ads follow this structure:
- **A**ttention (0–2 sec): Hook stops the scroll
- **A**gitate (2–8 sec): Deepen the problem or amplify curiosity — make them feel they MUST see where this goes
- **C**onvert (8–20 sec): Product reveal, demonstration, or social proof — show, don't tell
- **E**xit (20–30 sec): Clear CTA with urgency element, on-screen text overlay reinforces action

*Creative Concept 1 — Problem-First (Best for Awareness):*
[Write a full production brief for [Company Name]'s product using this structure:]
- Visual hook (0–2 sec): [Describe specific visual or scenario that captures ICP's daily frustration]
- On-screen text hook (0–2 sec): [Overlay text in TikTok caption style — short, punchy, relatable]
- Agitate script (2–8 sec): Expand on the frustration without mentioning product — make audience nod
- Product reveal (8–20 sec): Natural, in-context demonstration — avoid demo-reel corporate style
- Social proof insert (15–20 sec): Quick testimonial clip, review overlay, or "X,XXX sold" badge
- CTA (20–30 sec): "[Specific action] — link in bio / [product name].com" + on-screen button overlay
- Audio direction: [trending sound / original voice-over / licensed music — specify which]
- Text overlays throughout: every 3–4 seconds, new text overlay to re-engage silent viewers

*Creative Concept 2 — Social Proof / Results-Led (Best for Consideration):*
[Write a full production brief following this structure:]
- Hook (0–2 sec): Lead with a specific result — "I tried [product] for 30 days — here's my honest review"
- Trust establishment (2–6 sec): Establish creator/reviewer credibility (real person, relatable situation)
- Demo/proof (6–20 sec): Show product in real use — unboxing, before/after, comparison, results
- Objection handling (18–22 sec): Address the #1 objection in a natural, conversational way
- CTA (22–30 sec): Direct response with price or offer mention if applicable
- Format recommendation: UGC-style, handheld camera, no brand logo until the end

*Creative Concept 3 — Direct Response / Offer-Led (Best for Conversion):*

- Hook (0–2 sec): Price or offer-first — "Get [product] for $[X] — ends tonight" or discount reveal
- Product benefit stack (2–12 sec): Rapid-fire benefit list with visual demonstration of each
- Urgency amplifier (12–18 sec): Scarcity element (limited stock, time-bound offer, sold X units today)
- Social proof flash (18–22 sec): Star rating, review count, or 3-second testimonial clip
- CTA (22–30 sec): Hard CTA — "Tap the link now" + shop now button visible on screen

*Spark Ads Strategy:*
- Creator sourcing criteria: nano (10K–100K followers) and micro (100K–500K) creators in your niche outperform macro for cost-efficiency and authenticity
- Creator brief must include: what NOT to say (no scripted lines), key message to convey organically, product usage scenario, disclosure requirement (#ad)
- Whitelist process: request creator's TikTok Ads authorization code; import into TikTok Ads Manager → use their content as ad with their profile as the "sender"
- Spark Ads perform 142% better than regular in-feed ads for CTR on average (TikTok internal data)
- Always test 5–10 creator versions simultaneously; expect 1–2 to outperform by 3× or more

*On-Screen Text and Caption Strategy:*
- First text overlay must appear within 1 second — assume viewers watch on silent
- Use TikTok's native text style (white text, colored background) to blend into organic feed
- Caption (beneath video): lead with hook statement, include 3–5 relevant hashtags, end with CTA
- Hashtag strategy: 1–2 niche hashtags (#skincaretok, #gymtok), 1–2 trend hashtags (#fyp), 0 overly broad (#viral)

*Video Production Specifications:*
- Format: 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920px minimum
- Length: 15–30 sec for direct response; 30–60 sec for storytelling/consideration; 7–15 sec for retargeting
- Text-safe zone: keep all critical text and CTAs within center 75% of frame (avoid top 15% and bottom 15% for TikTok UI overlap)
- File: MP4 or MOV, H.264 codec, < 500MB
- Sound-on assumption: always include audio, but ensure visual storytelling works without sound
- Captions: always hard-code captions or use TikTok's auto-caption feature — increases completion rate by 25%

- Trust signals for TikTok-sourced traffic: customer review count + rating, social proof ("As seen on TikTok"), user count or orders placed

1. TikTok Shop product page (zero friction — native checkout within app)
2. Instant Page (TikTok's native fast-loading LP — 11× faster than external pages on mobile)
3. External website with TikTok Pixel optimized for conversion event

*For E-commerce:*

**5. TRACKING AND MEASUREMENT SETUP**

*TikTok Pixel and Events API Implementation:*
TikTok Pixel events to implement (in priority order):
1. PageView — fire on all pages
2. ViewContent — fire on product/service pages
3. AddToCart — fire on cart adds
4. InitiateCheckout — fire on checkout page load
5. CompletePayment (Purchase) — fire on order confirmation with revenue value
6. Lead — fire on form submissions
7. Subscribe — fire on email opt-ins

Events API (server-side): Required for iOS 14+ attribution accuracy — implement alongside pixel to deduplicate and capture events that pixel misses. TikTok Events API captures 15–30% more conversions than pixel-only for iOS traffic.

*Attribution Window Settings:*
- Click-through attribution: 7-day (recommended for most campaigns)
- View-through attribution: 1-day (for awareness-driven purchases)
- Note: TikTok's attribution tends to over-report vs. last-click — always cross-reference with GA4 and Shopify/CRM source data

*Key Performance Benchmarks by Objective:*

Awareness/Discovery Campaigns:
- CPM: $5–$15 (US audience, varies by niche — beauty/fashion higher, B2B adjacent lower)
- Video Completion Rate (VCR) for 6-sec: 60%+ | 15-sec: 40%+ | 30-sec: 25%+
- CTR on in-feed awareness: 0.5–1.5% (above 1.5% = strong creative)

Consideration Campaigns:
- CTR: 1–3% (top creative can achieve 5–8%)
- Cost Per Click (CPC): $0.10–$0.50 for most consumer categories (D2C, beauty, fashion)
- Video Completion Rate 75%+: benchmark 30–40% of views

Conversion Campaigns:
- Cost Per Purchase: varies dramatically by AOV and vertical — target at 10–20% of AOV
- ROAS: 1.5–4× for cold audiences; 4–8× for retargeting (TikTok Ads Manager reported)
- CVR (click to purchase): 1–5% for product pages; higher with Instant Page or TikTok Shop

*90-Day Optimization Roadmap:*

Days 1–21 (Creative Discovery Sprint):
- Launch 6–9 creative concepts across 3 hook types (2–3 variations each) in ABO structure
- Budget: $30–50/day per ad group to ensure adequate delivery
- Week 1: Establish baseline VCR and CTR by hook type — do NOT pause ads until $50+ spent each
- Week 2: Identify top 2–3 creatives by CTR × VCR composite score; pause bottom performers
- Week 3: Introduce first retargeting campaign (video 25%+ viewers); test Spark Ads from organic content

Days 22–45 (Audience and Bidding Optimization):
- Scale winning creatives to 2× budget; introduce lookalike audiences seeded from purchasers
- Shift from ABO to CBO across winning ad groups
- Test Cost Cap bidding on conversion campaign; set at 120% of Day 1–21 average CPA
- Launch creator UGC sourcing: brief 5–10 micro-creators for Spark Ads content

Days 46–90 (Scale and Systematize):
- Implement creative refresh: retire ads showing > 15% VCR decline week-over-week
- Launch new creative batch using insights from winning concepts (iterate hooks, not entire concepts)
- Expand geographic targeting if US-only to UK, CA, AU
- Implement Value Optimization bidding if 50+ purchase events per week
- Establish "creative pipeline": always have 3 untested concepts ready to replace fatigued ads

**6. TIKTOK-SPECIFIC BEST PRACTICES**

*Creative Fatigue Management (Critical on TikTok):*
- TikTok audiences exhaust creative 3–5× faster than Meta — expect 2–3 week shelf life per creative
- Fatigue signal: CTR declining > 15% week-over-week while spend holds steady
- Refresh strategy: first change only the hook (first 2 sec) — same middle and CTA — often restores 70–80% of original performance
- Build a creative production cadence: new creative batch every 2–3 weeks minimum
- Creative testing rule: never run fewer than 3 active creative variants per ad group

*TikTok Algorithm Rules:*
- The algorithm needs 3–5 days of data per ad group before optimizing — resist pausing or editing during this window
- Any change to an ad group (budget, targeting, placement) resets the learning phase — batch changes
- Audience size matters: audiences under 1M users limit delivery even with adequate budget — broaden before increasing spend
- Disapproved ads do not pause the campaign — monitor for policy violations in first 24 hours and correct immediately

*Brand Safety Configuration:*
- Inventory Filter: Standard inventory for most brands; Limited inventory for conservative verticals (finance, healthcare, children's products)
- Content exclusions: enable "Emotionally Sensitive" and "Mature & Controversial" category exclusions for brand protection
- Comment management: disable comments on direct response ads; enable on brand awareness/Spark Ads for social proof accumulation
- App placement: exclude Pangle (TikTok's network of third-party apps) if brand safety is critical — reduces reach but improves placement quality

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low CTR (under 0.5%) on all in-feed ad creatives**
Fix: The hook is failing — 90% of TikTok ad performance is determined in the first 2 seconds. Do not change audience or budget; change only the hook. Test 3 new hook types: (1) start mid-action with no preamble, (2) open with the result/outcome before showing the product, (3) use text-only hook ("the skincare routine that actually cleared my skin") over a relatable slow-motion shot. If CTR does not exceed 0.5% after 3 creative iterations, the problem is likely product-audience fit — the offer itself may not resonate with the TikTok demographic you are targeting.

**Problem: TikTok-reported ROAS looks strong (3×+) but Shopify revenue from TikTok is much lower**
Fix: This is an attribution gap, not a campaign failure. TikTok uses view-through attribution by default, which credits TikTok for purchases that happened up to 1 day after a user saw (but did not click) your ad. Fix in two steps: (1) In TikTok Ads Manager, switch your reporting view to "Click-through only" attribution to see a more conservative, comparable number. (2) Add a post-purchase survey to your Shopify checkout page ("How did you hear about us?") — TikTok is frequently cited as a discovery touchpoint that doesn't show up in click-based attribution. Use this qualitative signal alongside your MMP's blended attribution model to get truth.

**Problem: Ad delivery is restricted — spending only 20–30% of daily budget despite adequate audience size**
Fix: Three common causes: (1) Cost Cap or Bid Cap is set too low — the algorithm can't win auctions at your set price. Raise bid cap by 20% and monitor for 48 hours before making further changes. (2) Creative has been reviewed and flagged for a policy violation (even minor ones restrict delivery silently) — check the "Delivery" column in Ad Group view for yellow warning icons. (3) Ad group audience is under 1M users — expand interest targeting or loosen behavioral filters. If none of these apply, reset the ad group by duplicating it and relaunching (TikTok's algorithm occasionally gets stuck in local minima during the learning phase).
