## Overview
This engine analyzes your competitors' paid search presence — keywords, ad copy, landing pages, and estimated spend — then generates a complete conquest strategy including brand-defense campaigns, competitor keyword targeting, counter-ad copy, and Quality Score optimization tactics. Use it when a competitor is eating your impression share, when entering a new market, or when quarterly pipeline targets require extracting more revenue from existing paid search budgets.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Google Ads flags competitor brand name in ad headlines (trademark policy violation)**
Solution: Use "alternative to [competitor category]" language instead of direct brand name in headlines (e.g., "Jira Alternative for Dev Teams"). Brand names ARE permitted in ad text/descriptions under comparative advertising rules, and as keyword targets — just not always in headlines of direct competitor brand campaigns. Check Google's Trademark Complaint exceptions for your specific competitor; many SaaS companies (Salesforce, HubSpot) have open trademark policies allowing competitor use.

**Problem: Conquest campaign QS is stuck at 4-5/10 because landing page relevance is low**
Solution: Build dedicated "Competitor vs. My Brand" comparison landing pages for each Tier 1 competitor (e.g., `/stackly-vs-jira`). These pages need: (1) the competitor's name in the H1, (2) a feature comparison table, (3) migration/switching story, (4) social proof from customers who switched. A/B test this against your generic homepage — comparison pages typically see 2-3x higher QS and 40-60% higher CVR on conquest keywords.

**Problem: Competitors immediately counterbid when we enter their brand terms, causing CPC war**
Solution: Don't fight a pure CPC war — compete on Quality Score instead. A QS 9/10 on competitor brand terms beats a QS 5/10 competitor bidding 2x your max CPC. Focus Component 5 of this prompt on landing page and ad relevance improvements. Additionally, diversify conquest strategy to Tier 2/3 keywords where competitors are less aggressive; these often deliver better CPA than direct brand conquesting.
