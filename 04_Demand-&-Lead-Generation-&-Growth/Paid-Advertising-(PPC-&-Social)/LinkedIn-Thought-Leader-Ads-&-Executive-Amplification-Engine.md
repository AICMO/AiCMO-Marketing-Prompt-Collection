## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn rejecting Thought Leader Ads for compliance reasons**
Fix: TLA posts must meet LinkedIn's ad policies even though they appear as personal posts. Common rejections: posts mentioning specific stock prices or financial promises, health claims without disclaimers, competitive disparagement ("Competitor X is terrible"). Review LinkedIn's advertising policies before writing content. If a post is rejected, rephrase to remove comparison language or add required disclaimers. LinkedIn's review team is separate from organic — a post that passes organic won't automatically pass paid review.

**Problem: Low engagement rate on TLA despite good organic performance of original post**
Fix: The TLA paid audience is colder than the contributor's organic followers. A post that resonated with the contributor's existing audience (who already trust them) may not immediately land with strangers. Solution: ensure the first 2 lines of the post are self-contained hooks that work without prior context. Add a one-sentence context-setter at the top of posts being converted to TLA ("For context, I lead Marketing at Claros AI, a QMS platform for medical device companies — and we just analyzed 1,200 FDA audit outcomes. Here's what surprised us:"). This warm-up dramatically improves cold audience engagement rates.

**Problem: Difficulty getting executives to participate / approve content quickly**
Fix: Build a zero-friction approval workflow. Use a Notion or Google Doc with a simple table: [Post Draft | Contributor | Due Date | Status | Comments]. Send a Slack message with a direct link, asking only for a thumbs-up or one edit. Set the expectation up front: "I'll only send you 2 posts per week, each under 2 minutes to review. Your posts are driving X pipeline." Show the attribution report monthly. Executives who see "your LinkedIn post influenced $400K in pipeline this month" become enthusiastic participants.
