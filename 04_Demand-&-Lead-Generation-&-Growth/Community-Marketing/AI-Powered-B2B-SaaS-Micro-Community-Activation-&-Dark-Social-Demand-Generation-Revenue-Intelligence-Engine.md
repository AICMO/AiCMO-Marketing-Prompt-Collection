## Overview
This prompt engineers a systematic AI agent program for identifying, infiltrating (ethically), and activating niche third-party micro-communities — Slack groups, Discord servers, private LinkedIn groups, Substack comment sections, Telegram channels, and vertical forums — where your ICP has real, unfiltered conversations. Unlike owned community programs, this targets demand generation through authentic participation in communities you don't control, capturing dark social pipeline that never appears in attribution models.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Community admins are banning your team for spammy participation**
Solution: Review your participation ratio. If you're mentioning your product more than 1 in every 10 posts, you're over-promoting. Reset with 30 days of pure value contributions (no brand mentions, no product links). Build a personal brand for your team members, not a company brand — people tolerate a practitioner sharing knowledge; they ban companies promoting products.

**Problem: Dark social assets aren't spreading — nobody is sharing the content**
Solution: The content isn't "share-worthy" enough. Test three characteristics: (1) Does it make someone look smart for sharing it? (2) Does it save someone significant time or thinking? (3) Does it validate something the audience believes but hasn't seen articulated? Reformat your top-performing asset as a one-page visual instead of a blog post. Visual frameworks spread 3-5x more in Slack DMs than text links.

**Problem: Pipeline attribution is showing zero results from communities**
Solution: Your attribution capture is failing, not your community program. Add the community source question to your demo request form ("How did you first hear about us?"), train SDRs to ask in discovery calls, and add it to your post-onboarding survey. Community pipeline is typically 30-60% self-reported — if you're not asking, you're not capturing. Run a 30-day attribution audit: manually interview your last 10 closed-won customers about where they first heard of you.
