## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Community joins are high but monthly active rate is below 20%**
Solution: The community has a "sign-up and abandon" problem, almost always caused by unclear value proposition. Run a 10-customer interview asking: "What would make you log in here every week instead of Googling or emailing your CSM?" The answer is almost always exclusive content, faster peer answers than support tickets, or professional development — not product help. Rebuild your onboarding sequence around the most common answer and eliminate generic "explore the community" CTAs.

**Problem: AI expansion signals are triggering sales outreach but conversion to expansion is below 15%**
Solution: The signal definition is too broad. Audit the last 20 expansion signals that converted to closed-won expansion vs. the last 20 that didn't — identify the 2-3 signal combinations that actually predict intent. Typically, expansion signals only convert at high rates when 3+ signals are present simultaneously (e.g., researching advanced feature + upcoming renewal within 90 days + champion has been active for 3+ months). Tighten signal scoring before routing to sales.

**Problem: CSMs resist using community as a retention tool because it feels like "extra work"**
Solution: The program must save CSMs time, not create it. Redesign the integration so community AI handles the first three customer interactions (unanswered questions, onboarding follow-up, re-engagement sequences) before CSM involvement. Show CSMs the data: community-engaged accounts require 40% fewer reactive support calls. Frame community as a digital CS teammate, not another channel to manage. Assign one "community win" per CSM per week — celebrating a customer outcome from community activity — to build internal advocacy.
