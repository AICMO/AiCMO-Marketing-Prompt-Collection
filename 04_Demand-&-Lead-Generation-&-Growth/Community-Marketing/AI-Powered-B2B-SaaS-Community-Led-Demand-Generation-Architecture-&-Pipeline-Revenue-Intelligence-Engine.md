## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Use Zapier or Make to sync community platform member activity to CRM contact records. Create a custom "Community Member" contact property (boolean + date joined). Build a CRM workflow that triggers SDR task creation when a contact's community intent score crosses your threshold. Tag all community-sourced leads with "Lead Source = Community" for attribution reporting.
- **Slack + Zapier + HubSpot:** Build a zap that fires when a member posts in a product-adjacent channel (e.g., #product-feedback or #use-cases) → creates a HubSpot note on the corresponding contact → updates a custom "Community Intent Score" property → if score crosses threshold, creates a task for the assigned SDR.
- **Clay.com:** Enrich community member profiles automatically. Pull new member names/emails from the community platform → run through Clay enrichment (LinkedIn profile, company data, funding stage, tech stack) → score against ICP criteria → sync enriched data to HubSpot. This transforms anonymous community joiners into fully scored contacts without manual research.
- **Notion / Airtable Editorial Board:** Build a community content calendar in Notion where the AI agent populates each week's discussion prompts, AMA topics, and engagement programs. Human moderator reviews the weekly brief (10-minute task) and approves content before posting.
- **Circle / Slack + Beehiiv:** Run your community as the engagement layer and a bi-weekly email digest as the nurture layer. Members who are less active on Slack stay engaged via email. Digest content is auto-generated by AI agent from that week's top community threads — creating a flywheel where the most active members generate content that re-engages less active ones.
- **LinkedIn Matched Audiences:** Export your community member list to LinkedIn Campaign Manager as a matched audience. Run Thought Leader Ads targeting community members with your top-performing content — keeping your brand top-of-mind even when members aren't active in the community platform.
- **Gong / Chorus:** When a community member enters a sales cycle, flag their community history in the call intelligence tool. SDRs and AEs see which threads the prospect posted in, what questions they asked, and what resources they engaged with — giving them warm context for every call.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The community launches with 100+ members but engagement drops to near zero after week 3.
**Solution:** This is the "ghost town" problem — the most common community failure mode. The root cause is almost always a lack of community gravity: the community wasn't seeded with enough active contributors before public launch. Fix it with a "Community Revival Sprint": (1) Identify your 10 most ICP-qualified members and personally DM each one with a specific, answerable question ("I'm writing a benchmark report on [topic] and wanted to ask you specifically — what's your take on [question]?"). (2) Their responses become public thread content that signals activity to passive members. (3) Run an AMA with a genuinely respected external expert (not a company employee) — this drives a spike in engagement and re-activates lurkers. (4) Temporarily reduce community channels by 50% — fewer channels with more activity feels healthier than many channels with none.

**Problem:** Members engage with community content but no pipeline signals are appearing in the intent scoring model.
**Solution:** Your channel architecture is likely missing product-adjacent channels where intent naturally surfaces. Add two channels: #[your-category]-benchmarks (where members share metrics that reveal their pain level) and #tool-stack (where members discuss what tools they use and evaluate). Both channels generate organic intent signals without feeling like a sales environment. Separately, audit your intent scoring model — if your threshold is too high (100+ points), you may have qualified members who never breach the threshold. Lower the medium-intent threshold to 50 points and activate the nurture sequence for that tier to test whether it generates pipeline.

**Problem:** Community members are valuable but the company leadership doesn't see it as a demand generation channel — it's perceived as a "nice to have" community relations program.
**Solution:** This is a reporting problem, not a performance problem. Build a 1-page "Community Pipeline Contribution Report" that shows: (1) Pipeline $ from community first-touch contacts (filtered view in CRM), (2) Deals closed where prospect was an active community member during the sales cycle (influenced pipeline), (3) Community CAC vs. paid channel CAC side-by-side, (4) Time-to-close comparison: community-sourced leads vs. paid leads (community leads typically close 20-35% faster due to pre-existing trust). Present this monthly to CMO and CRO. The data usually converts skeptics within 2-3 quarters.
