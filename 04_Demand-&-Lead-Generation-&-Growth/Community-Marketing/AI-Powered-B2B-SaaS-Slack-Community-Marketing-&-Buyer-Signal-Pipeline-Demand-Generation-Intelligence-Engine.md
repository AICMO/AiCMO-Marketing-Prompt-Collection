## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts are ignored or getting zero engagement in industry communities.**
Solution: You're either posting too early (before building relationship credibility), the content is too promotional in tone, or the topic doesn't match the community's current conversations. Solution: (1) spend 2 more weeks listening before posting, (2) reply to existing threads before starting your own, (3) run your draft through the "Helpful Expert" checklist (no product mentions, solves a specific problem, cites data or experience). First 5 posts should ONLY be replies to others, never new threads.

**Problem: Community-attributed pipeline is hard to prove — finance/CRO won't credit community as a source.**
Solution: Implement self-reported attribution immediately (add "Slack community" as a dropdown option on all demo request forms). Additionally, run a quarterly survey of your CRM contacts asking "How did you first encounter [Your Company]?" — community will surface. Use Gong to search call transcripts for "Slack," "community," and specific community names — this surfaces attribution your forms miss. Present the data as "community-influenced" (any touchpoint) alongside "community-sourced" (first touch) to show full impact.

**Problem: Owned community membership is growing but engagement is low — it's a ghost town.**
Solution: Over-indexed on passive content (announcements, links). Fix: (1) post 3 direct questions per week that require a 1-sentence answer — low barrier to participation, (2) personally DM 5 members per week asking for their opinion on the latest topic, (3) feature member content rather than your own (repost members' LinkedIn articles, ask them to share a win), (4) run a weekly "question of the week" thread with an @channel mention on Mondays. Target: 10 human-response threads per week minimum before any growth investment.
