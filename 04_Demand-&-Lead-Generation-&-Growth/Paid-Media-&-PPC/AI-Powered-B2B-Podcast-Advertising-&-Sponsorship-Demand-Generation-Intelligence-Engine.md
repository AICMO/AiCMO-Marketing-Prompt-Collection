## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low vanity URL traffic despite active sponsorships**
Solution: First, verify the host is actually including the URL in the episode notes and saying it correctly on air (pull the episode and listen). Second, check if your landing page is converting — high URL visits with low form fills is a landing page problem, not an audio problem. Third, review your offer — B2B podcast listeners respond to "live demo with your actual data" or "free X-day trial" far better than "learn more" or "schedule a call." If traffic is genuinely low (<0.06% CTR), the script hook is likely failing — replace the first 10 seconds with a stronger, more specific pain statement.

**Problem: Can't prove ROI to CFO because attribution is fuzzy**
Solution: Reframe the conversation. Podcast advertising has three measurable signals: (1) direct vanity URL attribution — directional but real, (2) branded search lift — correlate Search Console weekly impressions with flight dates using a simple overlay chart, (3) account-level halo — Clearbit/RB2B showing target account visits to podcast landing pages. For CFO-level proof, run a 90-day lift study: pull 500 target accounts from your ABM list that have heard the podcast (IP exposure through DSP data) vs. 500 matched accounts that haven't — compare MQL rate, pipeline coverage, and win rate. This is the only causal attribution method for audio.

**Problem: Host-read ad feels off-brand or the host reads it incorrectly**
Solution: Send every host a two-page "host brief" before each recording — include: brand name pronunciation guide, three key messages (choose one to emphasize), phrases to avoid (don't say "cutting-edge" or "game-changing"), a sample script they can adapt (make clear they should make it their own), your vanity URL said phonetically, and two examples of great B2B podcast ads in your category they can reference for tone. Follow up post-episode: listen within 24 hours of air, and if there's a significant error (wrong URL, wrong product description), contact the host immediately to request a re-read or correction in show notes.
