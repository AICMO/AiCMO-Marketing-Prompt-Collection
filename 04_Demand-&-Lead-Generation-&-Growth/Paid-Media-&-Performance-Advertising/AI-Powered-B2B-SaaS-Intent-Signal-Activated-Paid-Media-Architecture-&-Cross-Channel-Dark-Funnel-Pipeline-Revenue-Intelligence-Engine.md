## Overview

Architects a full-funnel paid media program driven entirely by third-party and first-party intent signals — reaching B2B SaaS buyers who are actively researching solutions but have never visited your website or submitted a form. Use this when your ICP has a long dark-funnel research phase before raising their hand, when conventional retargeting audiences are too small to scale, when you want to reach in-market buyers at competitor evaluation stages, or when your CMO needs to prove paid media pipeline contribution beyond last-touch form fills.

**The Intent-Tier Budget Allocation Formula**

Step 1 — Calculate Your Maximum Tolerable CPL (Cost Per Pipeline):
Max CPL = ACV × Win Rate × (1 / Pipeline Multiple Needed)
Example: $72,000 ACV × 22% Win Rate = $15,840 revenue per pipeline opp
Max CPL = $15,840 / 4.5 pipeline multiple = $3,520 max cost per pipeline opportunity

Step 2 — Estimate Pipeline Influence Rate by Tier:
- Tier 1 (Active Buyer): 15–25% of impressed accounts will enter pipeline within 90 days
- Tier 2 (Researcher): 5–10% of impressed accounts will enter pipeline within 180 days
- Tier 3 (Category Interested): 1–3% of impressed accounts will enter pipeline within 12 months

Step 3 — Reverse Engineer Max CPM by Tier:
At 20 impressions/account/week for 4 weeks = 80 impressions per account in Tier 1
If 20% of Tier 1 accounts pipeline, and average CPM on programmatic is $8:
Cost to impress 1 account (80 impressions): $8 CPM × 80/1000 = $0.64
Cost to generate 1 pipeline opp: $0.64 / 0.20 = $3.20 per pipeline opportunity
Well below the $3,520 max — scale aggressively

**The Attribution Problem — And Why Most B2B Companies Get It Wrong**

Standard last-touch attribution assigns 100% of pipeline credit to the form fill. For intent-activated paid media, this creates a systematic undercount. A buyer who sees 40 LinkedIn impressions, 15 programmatic display ads, and reads 3 Google-clicked articles over 90 days — then requests a demo via a cold email follow-up — will show "Email" as the pipeline source in your CRM. Your paid media investment gets zero credit.

**The Three-Layer Attribution Model:**

Layer 1 — Last-Touch Attribution (what your CRM tracks automatically):
Report this to sales for lead routing and campaign optimization. Do not use this as the primary paid media ROI metric.

Layer 2 — Pipeline Influence Attribution (what your CMO reports to the CFO):
For every new pipeline opportunity created, query your ad platforms: did this account receive at least one paid impression in the 90 days before the opportunity creation date? What was the total impression count, channel mix, and estimated CPM invested?
Build this report monthly: "Paid Media-Influenced Pipeline" — all deals where the account received paid impressions before becoming a pipeline opportunity.
Expected benchmark: 40–65% of total pipeline at mature program stage will show paid media influence (not sourced — influenced).

Layer 3 — Incrementality Measurement (what your board will trust):
Hold out 20% of your intent-qualified account list from receiving any paid media for 90 days. Compare pipeline creation rate, demo request rate, and average deal size between the exposed group and the hold-out group.
If exposed accounts show 30%+ higher pipeline creation rate — that's your incrementality proof.
Run one incrementality test per quarter and include results in CMO board report.

**Technical Implementation:**

LinkedIn Insight Tag: Deploy on all pages; configure conversion events for demo request, content download, pricing page visit, and contact page; activate View-Through Conversion tracking with 30-day attribution window.

Google Ads: Import offline conversions from Salesforce via the Offline Conversion Import API; connect Google Ads to Salesforce via native connector or Zapier; report pipeline value (not just form fills) back into Google Ads for bid optimization.

Programmatic DSP: Enable Account-Level Reporting in DSP — most enterprise DSPs can report on impressions delivered by account domain, enabling account-matched pipeline reporting.

CRM Field Implementation: Create custom Salesforce fields: "Paid Media Impression — LinkedIn" (date of first impression, total impressions), "Paid Media Impression — Programmatic" (date, impressions), "Intent Tier at First Impression" (Tier 1/2/3), "Days from First Paid Impression to Opportunity Created". Populate via webhook from ad platforms or bi-weekly data sync.

- Launch 90-day incrementality hold-out test: segment 20% of Tier 1 accounts from receiving any paid media

**Three KPIs That Determine Scale vs. Pause vs. Pivot:**

Scale: Cost Per Influenced Pipeline Opportunity ≤ 15% of ACV AND Influenced Pipeline as % of Total Pipeline ≥ 40%
Pause & Optimize: Cost Per Influenced Pipeline Opportunity 15–25% of ACV OR specific channel CPM exceeds target without proportional pipeline influence
Pivot: Influenced Pipeline as % of Total Pipeline < 20% after 90 days → indicates intent data quality issue, audience build error, or creative-message mismatch — conduct full audit before increasing spend

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn Matched Audience match rate below 40%**
Fix: Your account list file needs to use the exact company names as they appear on LinkedIn. Export a test list of 50 known accounts and manually verify LinkedIn company name spelling. Also ensure you're uploading company domain (not just name) for better matching. LinkedIn's match rate for company lists typically ranges 40–70%; below 40% indicates formatting issues in your upload file (check for extra spaces, inconsistent country codes, duplicate rows).

**Problem: Intent-activated campaigns showing high impression volume but zero pipeline influence after 60 days**
Fix: This is either a creative-message mismatch (your ads don't speak the language your buyer uses when they're in evaluation mode — go back to win-call transcripts and G2 reviews to verify your messaging) or an audience quality problem (your intent vendor is classifying accounts as Tier 1 that aren't actually evaluating — validate by calling 10 Tier 1 accounts to confirm active evaluation). Also check whether your CRM pipeline influence field is being populated correctly — many implementations have a data pipeline error that prevents impression data from flowing into Salesforce.

**Problem: CFO challenges the pipeline influence methodology ("you can't prove paid media caused these deals")**
Fix: This is why you ran the incrementality hold-out test. Present the control group results: Tier 1 accounts that received paid media had X% higher pipeline creation rate than the 20% hold-out group that received zero paid media impressions. If you haven't run an incrementality test yet, commit to a 90-day test with the CFO's participation in designing the methodology — getting their buy-in on the test design means they'll trust the results.
