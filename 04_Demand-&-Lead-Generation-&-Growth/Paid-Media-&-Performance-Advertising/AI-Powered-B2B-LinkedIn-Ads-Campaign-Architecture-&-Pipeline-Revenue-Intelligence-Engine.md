## Overview

Architects a full-funnel B2B LinkedIn Ads program that targets buying committees by job title, function, seniority, company size, and firmographic criteria — running Sponsored Content, Lead Gen Forms, Conversation Ads, and Document Ads simultaneously — with every dollar mapped to pipeline revenue in Salesforce or HubSpot. Use when building a LinkedIn Ads program from scratch, scaling spend beyond $10K/month with disciplined structure, running account-based advertising campaigns, or proving LinkedIn's pipeline contribution to a CFO who only sees cost-per-lead.

**A. Audience Tier Mapping**

Architect the complete audience targeting hierarchy across three conversion-readiness tiers:

TIER 1 — WARM (Retargeting & Matched): Highest intent, lowest CPL target
- Website retargeting segments: all visitors (90 days), pricing page visitors (30 days), demo page abandoners (14 days), blog readers by topic cluster (60 days)
- Lead Gen Form openers (didn't submit) — 30-day window
- LinkedIn Company Page visitors (90 days)
- Video viewers: 25%, 50%, 75%+ completion audiences
- Contact List Matches: upload CRM contacts segmented by lifecycle stage (Leads, MQLs, Open Opportunities, Churned Customers for win-back)
- Event attendee audiences (LinkedIn Event registrants and attendees)

For each warm segment: estimated audience size based on provided traffic/contact data, recommended weekly frequency cap (aim for 3–5 impressions per week maximum for retargeting), and the single most relevant offer for each segment's intent level.

TIER 2 — ACCOUNT-BASED (TAL Targeting): Account-matched precision, mid CPL target
- Sync target account list from CRM to LinkedIn Matched Audiences (LinkedIn Company List upload)
- Expected match rate: 55–70% of accounts will match
- Segment TAL into: Tier 1 Accounts (top 50–100 named accounts — maximum budget allocation), Tier 2 Accounts (next 200–500 accounts — secondary allocation)
- For Tier 1 accounts: run separate campaigns per account cluster (by vertical or deal stage) to enable account-level frequency management
- Persona layering within TAL: add job function, seniority, and title filters on top of the matched account audience to reach only buying committee members — not all employees
- Exclusion logic: exclude existing customers (upload customer account list), current open opportunities (exclude contacts in active deal stages from CRM sync)

For each TAL tier: campaign structure recommendation, weekly impression target per account, escalation trigger (when to move an account from Tier 2 to Tier 1 based on engagement signals).

TIER 3 — COLD PROSPECTING (Layered Targeting): New audience reach, highest CPL, lowest volume requirement
- Primary targeting layer: Job Title OR Job Function + Seniority (Title is more precise; Function + Seniority captures job title variation)
- Recommended title list: provide 20–30 exact job titles from ICP, including common variations (e.g., "VP Sales" / "Vice President of Sales" / "VP of Sales" / "Head of Sales")
- Firmographic filters: Company Size (employee count ranges), Industry (LinkedIn industry categories mapped to your ICP verticals), Geography
- Interest and Skill layering (secondary filter only — do not stack too many layers): LinkedIn Member Skills relevant to your product category, LinkedIn Groups where your ICP is active
- Lookalike Audiences: build from best customer contact list (minimum 300 contacts recommended), closed-won contacts (past 24 months), and Lead Gen Form submitters (past 90 days)

For cold prospecting: estimated audience size at each targeting layer, recommended overlap analysis (use LinkedIn's Audience Insights to validate ICP reach), and the TOFU content offer that will generate the lowest-friction first engagement.

**A. Format-to-Funnel Assignment**

Map each LinkedIn ad format to a specific funnel stage, content type, and KPI:

TOFU (Awareness & Interest):
- Single Image Ads: thought leadership content, industry trend angles, problem-agitation hooks; KPI: CTR > 0.45%, CPM < $35
- Video Ads (15–60 seconds): customer story clips, "the problem we solve" narratives, founder perspective; KPI: Video Completion Rate > 30% for 30-second videos
- Document Ads: native lead magnets served directly in the LinkedIn feed (no click required to read first 3 pages); KPI: Document Opens, Lead Gen Form conversion rate when gated at page 3–5

MOFU (Consideration & Evaluation):
- Carousel Ads: multi-step product walkthroughs, before/after transformation stories, feature comparison frameworks; KPI: Click-to-Card engagement rate, lead quality from subsequent retargeting
- Thought Leader Ads: amplify specific employee or founder LinkedIn posts that are resonating organically; KPI: Post engagement rate, profile visits from ICP audience
- Conversation Ads (Sponsored Messaging): multi-choice drip sequences sent to LinkedIn Inbox; use for webinar invitations, exclusive content offers, and event invitations to warm audiences; KPI: Open Rate > 40%, CTA Click Rate > 5%

BOFU (Decision & Purchase):
- Lead Gen Forms (on Sponsored Content): primary demand capture format — pre-fills LinkedIn profile data; KPI: Lead Gen Form Completion Rate > 12%, CPL < target, MQL rate from LinkedIn leads > 25%
- Single Image Ads with direct CTA: demo request, free trial, pricing page; use for high-intent retargeting audiences only; KPI: Conversion Rate > 2.5%, Cost per Demo Booked
- Message Ads: one-to-one direct messages for Tier 1 named accounts with executive-level personalization; KPI: Open Rate > 55%, Reply Rate > 8%

**A. Bidding Framework by Campaign Objective**

AWARENESS CAMPAIGNS (TOFU):
- Bid type: CPM (Cost Per 1,000 Impressions)
- Target CPM: $25–$50 for cold audiences; $15–$30 for warm retargeting
- Budget: 15–20% of total LinkedIn budget
- Optimization signal: Video completion rate, Document open rate — not CTR

CONSIDERATION CAMPAIGNS (MOFU):
- Bid type: Maximum Delivery (LinkedIn auto-optimizes for engagement)
- Fall back to Manual CPC if auto-bidding inflates costs above $12 CPC
- Budget: 20–25% of total LinkedIn budget
- Optimization signal: Content engagement rate, retargeting pool growth rate

CONVERSION CAMPAIGNS (BOFU):
- Bid type: Automated Bidding with Target Cost per Lead (set 20% above your acceptable CPL to give LinkedIn's algorithm room — then tighten as data accumulates)
- Budget: 55–65% of total LinkedIn budget (concentrate spend where pipeline is generated)
- Lead Gen Form campaigns: start with Maximum Delivery for first 2 weeks to generate initial conversion data, then switch to Target Cost per Lead once 20+ form submissions accumulated
- Optimization signal: Lead Gen Form Completion Rate, Cost per Lead Gen Form Lead, Lead-to-MQL conversion rate (tracked weekly via CRM report)

**B. Budget Pacing Rules**

Daily budget calculation: Monthly budget ÷ 30.4 = daily cap. Set daily budget at 1.3× the calculated daily cap to account for LinkedIn's delivery pacing algorithm (LinkedIn can spend up to 50% over daily budget on any given day but balances over the calendar month).

Budget reallocation triggers:
- If a campaign's CPL exceeds target by 40% for 7+ consecutive days → pause and reallocate to best-performing campaign
- If a campaign's Lead Gen Form Completion Rate falls below 9% → test new creative before pausing
- If retargeting audience size drops below 300 (LinkedIn's minimum for Matched Audiences) → expand retargeting window or merge audience segments

**B. Offline Conversion Import (Pipeline Attribution)**

Map LinkedIn's impact beyond the form submit:

STEP 1 — CRM-to-LinkedIn connection: Use LinkedIn's Revenue Attribution Report (RAR) integration with Salesforce or HubSpot. Connect CRM via native integration or through Zapier/Make workflow.

STEP 2 — Opportunity creation tracking: When a LinkedIn-sourced lead converts to an opportunity in CRM, pass the opportunity amount and stage to LinkedIn's Offline Conversion event ("Opportunity Created"). This populates LinkedIn's Revenue Attribution dashboard.

STEP 3 — Closed-Won attribution: When an opportunity closes, pass the Closed Won event and revenue amount to LinkedIn via API or CSV upload (LinkedIn supports both). This enables true pipeline ROI calculation: LinkedIn Spend ÷ LinkedIn-Attributed Closed Won Revenue = LinkedIn ROAS.

STEP 4 — Attribution window configuration: Set LinkedIn's click-through attribution window to 30 days and view-through attribution to 7 days for B2B programs. This captures multi-touch influence without over-crediting LinkedIn for deals it only touched once.

MODULE 7: 90-DAY OPTIMIZATION ROADMAP
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

**Weeks 1–2: Foundation & Baseline**
- Launch all campaigns with conservative budgets (50% of target) to gather clean data without overspending
- Establish performance baselines: CTR by format, CPL by campaign, Lead Gen Form completion rate, audience match rates
- Verify Insight Tag firing correctly on all conversion pages; confirm CRM integration is passing lead source correctly
- Do not make optimization changes in Week 1 — let LinkedIn's algorithm learn

**Weeks 3–4: Audience & Creative Refinement**
- Review LinkedIn's Demographic Breakdown report: confirm actual impressions are reaching your ICP (not just the audience you targeted) — sort by job title, seniority, company size, and industry
- Remove any non-ICP job functions or seniority levels that are consuming budget without converting
- Pause underperforming creative (CTR below 0.30% on cold audiences for 7+ days) and replace with new variations
- Add exclusions based on any lead quality feedback from sales ("these titles are not qualified")
- Scale retargeting budgets if retargeting CPL is below cold prospecting CPL (it almost always should be)

**Month 2: Format Expansion & A/B Testing**
- Launch Thought Leader Ads amplifying your highest-performing organic posts from your CEO/founder
- Test Conversation Ads for webinar invitations to MOFU warm audiences
- A/B test Lead Gen Form offers: demo request vs. gated content (test which generates better MQL rate, not just volume)
- Test Document Ads natively serving your best-performing lead magnet — benchmark against gated landing page CPL
- Begin Lookalike Audience expansion using accumulated Lead Gen Form submitters as the seed

**Month 3: Scale & Efficiency Optimization**
- Transition best-performing campaigns to Automated Bidding (Target Cost per Lead) now that conversion data is sufficient
- Scale budget 20–30% on campaigns hitting CPL target with consistent lead quality
- Launch competitive audience strategy: target LinkedIn members who follow your top 3 competitors' company pages
- Implement creative refresh cycle: rotate new creative every 3–4 weeks to combat LinkedIn ad fatigue (frequency >8 per member per month correlates with significant CTR decline)
- Build quarterly performance report for leadership: LinkedIn spend vs. LinkedIn-attributed pipeline vs. LinkedIn-attributed closed won, benchmarked against Google Ads and other paid channels

**Three metrics that determine Scale / Pause / Pivot:**
1. **Lead-to-MQL rate** below 20% for 2+ weeks → Pause and review audience targeting (reaching wrong people) or offer (wrong stage for the audience)
2. **Cost per SQL** above 3× your target → Pivot creative and offer immediately; do not continue scaling
3. **LinkedIn-attributed pipeline** below 5× monthly spend after Month 3 → Escalate to leadership for budget review; investigate whether LinkedIn is the right primary channel for this ICP or whether it should be a supplemental channel only

## Troubleshooting

**Problem 1: Lead Gen Form Completion Rate is below 8% consistently**

Cause: Either the form has too many custom questions (more than 2 custom questions reliably drops completion rate below 8%), the offer isn't compelling enough for the audience's funnel stage (BOFU demo request served to TOFU cold audience), or there's audience targeting mismatch (reaching non-ICP personas).

Fix: Step 1 — Remove all custom questions and test a form with only pre-filled LinkedIn fields; if completion rate jumps above 12%, the form friction was the issue. Step 2 — If the form-only test doesn't improve completion rate, the offer is wrong for the audience; serve a TOFU content offer (benchmark report, checklist) to cold audiences and reserve demo request forms for retargeting audiences only. Step 3 — Check the LinkedIn Demographic Breakdown report for your campaign; if job titles showing impressions don't match your ICP, refine audience targeting before optimizing the form.

**Problem 2: LinkedIn leads are consistently rated "low quality" by sales**

Cause: Most common causes are: (a) reaching the right companies but wrong personas within those companies (champions downloading content but economic buyers not reached), (b) offering TOFU content that attracts researchers rather than active buyers, or (c) Lead Gen Form pre-filled data being inaccurate (LinkedIn profile job titles don't always reflect current role).

Fix: Step 1 — Pull a demographic breakdown report filtered to "Conversions" only (not impressions) to see which job titles are actually submitting forms. If the submitting titles differ from your ICP buyer, add job title filters to your targeting (not just function + seniority). Step 2 — For sales quality issues related to TOFU content downloads, create a second campaign with demo request CTA exclusively for retargeting those content downloaders — this segments researchers (content only) from active evaluators (demo request). Step 3 — Add 1 custom qualifying question to the Lead Gen Form ("What's your timeline for evaluating solutions like ours?") — leads selecting "3–6 months" or "Just researching" can be routed to long-cycle nurture instead of immediate SDR follow-up.

**Problem 3: LinkedIn CPL is 2–3× higher than Google Ads CPL and CFO is questioning the budget**

Cause: LinkedIn CPL is almost always higher than Google Ads CPL in isolation because LinkedIn reaches buyers before they search — LinkedIn influences demand; Google captures it. Comparing CPL across channels without comparing downstream lead quality and pipeline value is a measurement failure, not a LinkedIn performance failure.

Fix: Build the pipeline-adjusted comparison. Pull from CRM: average opportunity size for LinkedIn-sourced leads vs. Google Ads-sourced leads, lead-to-SQL conversion rate by source, average sales cycle length by source, and win rate by source. Calculate cost-per-closed-won-revenue by channel (LinkedIn Spend ÷ LinkedIn-Attributed Closed Won = LinkedIn effective CPA). In B2B enterprise programs, LinkedIn-sourced deals are frequently 25–40% larger ACV than Google Ads-sourced deals because LinkedIn reaches senior economic buyers while Google captures lower-funnel researchers. Present this analysis to CFO as "LinkedIn cost-per-pipeline-dollar" vs. "LinkedIn cost-per-lead" — the former almost always justifies the budget; the latter never will.
