## Overview
Transforms a single podcast episode concept into a fully produced content machine — generating guest research briefs, interview scripts, show notes, SEO transcripts, social clips, newsletter copy, and blog posts. Use this when you want to operate a B2B podcast without a dedicated production team, turn every episode into a week's worth of pipeline-generating content across every channel, and eliminate the post-production bottleneck that kills most company podcasts within 12 months.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Guest interviews are compelling live but feel flat and meandering in post-production**
Solution: The culprit is missing segment discipline during recording. Before you hit record, tell the guest: "I'm going to keep us tight — I'll give you a 30-second signal when I want to wrap a segment. This helps the edit come out clean." Then actually enforce it. Meandering interviews aren't edited into great episodes — they're cut down to mediocre ones. The Module 2 outline solves this structurally: each segment has a clear exit ramp question that naturally closes the loop.

**Problem: Podcast has listeners but no pipeline — CTA isn't converting**
Solution: Audit three things. First, is the CTA placed only at the end of the episode? Move a secondary CTA to the 50–60% mark — this is when listener intent is highest (they've invested time and are in peak value-extraction mode). Second, is the CTA offer generic ("book a demo") or specific to the episode topic ("get the forecasting audit we discussed in this episode")? Episode-specific CTAs convert 3–5x better. Third, check if your CTA appears in the show notes with a direct, frictionless link — 40% of podcast CTA conversions happen via show notes, not audio.

**Problem: Content production bottleneck — show notes, social clips, and blog take 10+ hours per episode**
Solution: Restructure the production order. The moment the Descript transcript is clean, paste it into this prompt system to generate all 7 modules simultaneously. Do not write show notes from scratch — use the AI output as a first draft and spend 30 minutes editing, not 3 hours creating. For social clips, don't screen-record and manually caption — use Descript's Clip feature to export audiograms with embedded captions in one click. Target: 90 minutes of human time per episode after recording, all remaining work AI-assisted.
