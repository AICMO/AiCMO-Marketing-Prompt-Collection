## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Lead magnets aren't converting — listeners hear the CTA but don't opt in.**
Solution: The CTA is likely too generic. Make the lead magnet hyper-specific to the episode topic (not a generic "podcast resource guide"). If Episode 22 is about forecasting, the CTA should be "Download the 3-scenario forecasting template we built with today's guest" — not "Subscribe to our newsletter." Test placing the CTA at minute 12–15 (post-hook, pre-main content) when engagement is highest, not at the end when listeners have already stopped.

**Problem: Email nurture sequence has high open rates but zero demo requests.**
Solution: The sequence is educating without creating urgency or qualifying intent. Add a diagnostic question in Email 3: "What's your biggest forecasting headache right now — reply and I'll send you the most relevant episode." Replies identify your hottest leads. Shift Email 4 from pure education to social proof (a specific customer outcome with numbers). Ensure Email 5's CTA is low-friction ("15-minute conversation, no deck, no demo") not a full sales demo request.

**Problem: Attribution model shows "Direct" traffic for most podcast-sourced conversions — can't prove ROI.**
Solution: Listeners who hear a podcast episode often Google the company directly or type the URL — killing UTM data. Fix this with dedicated episode landing pages using vanity URLs (forecastly.com/show/ep22) that redirect through UTM tracking. Add a "How did you hear about us?" field to your demo request form with "Podcast" as an explicit option. Implement a JavaScript listener that fires a HubSpot/Salesforce tracking event when a contact visits 3+ episode pages in one session — this is high-intent behavior worth capturing even without a conversion event.
