## Troubleshooting

**Problem: A-list guests won't respond to cold outreach for a show with zero episodes.**
Solution: Lead with your committed guest list, not the show itself. If you've booked 3 respected names in the industry, your outreach becomes "join [Name A], [Name B], and [Name C] in the founding cohort" — social proof by association. Record episodes with your internal team, customers, or warm network connections first, publish 2–3 episodes before outreach resumes, and reference those live episodes with download numbers as credibility.

**Problem: Pre-launch campaign generates a waitlist but launch week subscriptions are far below target.**
Solution: Most pre-launch subscribers will not convert on day 1 without a direct, personal prompt. Send a text or LinkedIn DM (not just email) to your top 50 waitlist contacts on launch day asking them to subscribe and leave a review in the next 2 hours. Platform algorithms weight subscribe velocity in the first 24 hours disproportionately — personal activation of a small group outperforms bulk email for chart placement.

**Problem: 90-day pipeline attribution shows zero SQLs from the podcast despite strong listenership.**
Solution: The bottleneck is almost always listener identification, not listenership. If listeners can't be tied to CRM contacts, pipeline attribution is impossible. Audit your in-episode CTAs — are they specific enough? Are you offering episode-relevant lead magnets (not generic ones)? Add a mid-roll CTA in your top 5 episodes offering a high-value asset (benchmark report, calculator, or assessment) with a dedicated landing page. Consider a listener survey (Typeform embedded in show notes) that asks for company and role in exchange for access to bonus episode content.
