## Overview
Transforms your B2B SaaS company podcast from a cost center into a dual-revenue engine — generating both direct sponsorship income and accelerated product pipeline — by designing a complete sponsorship program architecture including media kit construction, sponsor tier pricing, outreach sequences, campaign delivery standards, and ROI attribution frameworks. Use this when you have an established podcast with a growing audience (500+ downloads/episode) and want to convert that asset into a monetized media property while keeping the show authentic and pipeline-contributing.

- Brands your audience would distrust (low-quality software, MLM, crypto, anything our listeners would call out on LinkedIn)

PHASE 5 — CAMPAIGN DELIVERY & SPONSOR RELATIONSHIP SYSTEM

PRE-CAMPAIGN WORKFLOW:
Week -3: Sponsor completes Creative Brief (messaging, offer, landing page, promo code)
Week -2: Host writes ad read draft (host voice, not sponsor copy)
Week -1: Sponsor reviews and approves (one round of feedback only; host retains final creative control)
Recording week: Ad read recorded; show notes written; social assets prepared

AD READ QUALITY STANDARDS:
- Host-read only — no pre-produced sponsor audio
- Personal endorsement required: host must have genuine experience or trial of product
- Story format preferred: "Here's how I actually used [PRODUCT]..." over feature lists
- Disclosure: "This episode is brought to you by [SPONSOR] — and here's why I recommend them..."
- Duration: Tier 1 pre-roll 75-90 seconds; mid-roll 55-65 seconds; Tier 2 pre-roll 55-65 seconds; mid-roll 40-50 seconds

SPONSOR REPORTING CADENCE:
Monthly Report includes:
- Episode-level downloads (30-day and all-time)

- Estimated CPM effective rate
- Listen-through rate (from hosting platform analytics)

Quarterly Business Review (for Tier 1 sponsors):
- Full campaign performance vs. benchmark
- Audience evolution (any shifts in demographics)

## Success Metrics

- **Sellout rate**: % of available ad inventory sold within 30 days of availability (target: 60%+ by Month 6, 80%+ by Month 12)

- **Sponsor-reported conversion**: Promo code redemption and landing page conversion rate from sponsored episodes (target: above sponsor's category benchmark by 20%+)
- **Dual revenue ratio**: Ratio of pipeline-attributed revenue to sponsorship revenue (healthy benchmark: 3:1 — $3 pipeline per $1 sponsorship)
- **Time to first sponsor**: Days from launch of outreach to first signed agreement (target: 60 days or less)
- **Email list growth from sponsorship**: % of sponsor promotions that drive net new email subscribers (target: positive net adds per episode)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Prospective sponsors say downloads are "too low" compared to larger shows**
Solution: Reframe the conversation from raw downloads to audience quality and CPM efficiency. A show with 3,000 downloads of 70% VP-level RevOps leaders delivers more qualified impressions than a show with 50,000 downloads of mixed business professionals. Prepare a "quality vs. quantity" one-pager that calculates the effective cost to reach a single decision-maker across channels: LinkedIn Ads typically cost $120-$180 per VP-level click; your podcast delivers the same impression for $15-$25 at your CPM. This math usually changes the conversation. Also reference the Engaged Listener Index: fewer listeners who complete 70%+ of episodes are more valuable than passive listeners who skip.

**Problem: Host-read ad reads sound unnatural or sponsors complain the messaging is off-brand**
Solution: Implement a two-document system: (1) Sponsor provides a "Truth Brief" — facts, stats, and key benefits they want communicated; (2) Host writes an "Experience Script" in their natural voice using that truth as source material. Send the Experience Script to the sponsor for factual accuracy review only — not tone or style edits. If sponsors attempt to rewrite the ad into corporate speak, remind them this is explicitly why listeners trust your show and why their ad will convert: authentic host voice is the product they're buying.
