## Troubleshooting

**Problem 1: Posts consistently get below 1% engagement despite following the framework**
*Cause:* Hook isn't specific enough to create pattern interrupt, or distribution base is too small for algorithm lift
*Fix:* Before publishing, read your hook out loud and ask "Would I stop scrolling if I saw this from a stranger?" If no, rewrite. Then check your follower base quality — 2,000 highly relevant followers outperform 10,000 random connections. If your following is under 500 genuine ICP profiles, prioritize growing the audience before optimizing for virality. Engage in 5 ICP conversations daily for 30 days; watch reach multiply as algorithm learns your audience.

**Problem 2: Content goes viral but generates zero pipeline**
*Cause:* Viral content is attracting the wrong audience (practitioners at wrong stage, non-buyers, or geographic misfit)
*Fix:* Audit your commenters and sharers — are they your ICP? If your viral post about "CFO life" is being shared by accounting students and not by Series B CFOs, you've attracted the right topic but wrong audience lens. Reframe the same content with ICP-specific specificity: Not "CFO problems" but "Series B SaaS CFO problems." Specificity repels the wrong audience and attracts the right one. Also add a clearer ICP-specific CTA: "If you're a finance leader at a [stage] company, here's what to do about this: [specific action]."

**Problem 3: Great first post, then engagement crashes on everything after**
*Cause:* One-hit virality without sustainable content architecture — the post "found" the audience but you haven't built a reason to follow
*Fix:* After a viral post, publish a follow-up within 24 hours that delivers on the implied promise of the original (if the first post identified a problem, the second post provides a framework or solution). This converts new profile visitors into followers. Then maintain a strict 2-3x/week cadence — the algorithm rewards consistency, and your new audience expects follow-through. The #1 mistake after a viral post is going silent for 2 weeks while "working on the next big one." Post something smaller, useful, and on-brand instead.
