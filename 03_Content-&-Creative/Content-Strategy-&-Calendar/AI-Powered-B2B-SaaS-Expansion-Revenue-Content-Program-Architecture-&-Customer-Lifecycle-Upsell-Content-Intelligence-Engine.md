DELIVERABLE 1 — CUSTOMER LIFECYCLE CONTENT STAGE MAP

Map content to five customer lifecycle stages. For each stage, specify what the customer is experiencing, what content moves them forward, and what signals indicate readiness to advance:

**Stage 1 — Activation (Days 0-30):**
- Customer psychology: "Did I make the right purchase? Can I make this work?"
- Content purpose: Reduce activation anxiety, establish early wins, prevent early churn
- Content types that drive Stage 1 forward:
  - Onboarding guides specific to their use case (not generic product tours)
  - "First 30 days" milestone checklists that create visible progress
  - Quick win case studies from similar customers in their industry/team size who saw value in week 1
  - Implementation "gotcha" guides that pre-answer the top 5 support tickets before they're submitted
- Content delivery: In-app contextual prompts + onboarding email sequence + CSM-sent content package
- Advancement signal: Core feature activation (define the "activated" threshold for this product) + at least one meaningful data output from the product

**Stage 2 — Adoption (Days 31-120):**
- Customer psychology: "This is working. I want to get more out of it."
- Content purpose: Expand feature utilization, establish product as essential workflow, build internal champions
- Content types that drive Stage 2 forward:
  - Advanced feature deep-dive guides (not the basics — assume they can already do the basics)
  - "Power user secrets" content: features 80% of customers haven't discovered that deliver disproportionate value
  - Peer use case spotlights: how customers at the same stage used Feature X to achieve Outcome Y
  - Internal champion kit: content that helps your customer's internal advocate explain and justify the product to their teammates (without you being in the room)
  - Integration-specific guides: if they haven't connected to [CRM/Slack/etc.], guides showing the outcome value of doing so
- Content delivery: Monthly customer newsletter feature spotlight + CSM-triggered "you haven't tried this yet" email + community posts
- Advancement signal: Weekly active usage by ≥3 team members + at least 2 integrations enabled + submitted a support ticket (indicates they're going deep, not just surface-level using)

**Stage 3 — Power User (Days 121-270):**
- Customer psychology: "We've gotten real value. Our team is dependent on this. I want to know what else it can do."
- Content purpose: Surface product depth they haven't explored, create natural discovery of expansion-eligible capabilities, generate internal proof points
- Content types that drive Stage 3 forward:
  - "What your most successful customers have in common" benchmark data reports (positions outlier customers as the aspirational comparison, creating natural pull toward behaviors that correlate with expanded use)
  - ROI documentation templates: make it easy for them to calculate and communicate their own ROI internally — this creates the business case for expansion before CS initiates the conversation
  - Adjacent capability showcases: if they're using Module A, show the specific outcomes other customers achieved when they added Module B — with specific metrics, not generic benefits
  - Executive summary content: one-page, board-ready materials summarizing the business impact of the product — gives economic buyers visibility into value, which opens budget conversations
  - Power user certification: structured learning path that creates a recognized credential for users who master the platform at an advanced level (drives depth, advocacy, and stickiness simultaneously)
- Content delivery: Milestone-triggered emails ("You've been an active user for 6 months — here's what other 6-month customers unlocked") + customer newsletter advanced section + community "power users" track
- Advancement signal: >90% utilization of current tier's feature set + unsolicited internal sharing (employees outside the original buyer team start accessing the product) + positive NPS response + no support tickets about missing features (they've found the ceiling of their current tier)

**Stage 4 — Expansion Ready (Days 270+, usage-triggered):**
- Customer psychology: "We need more. The current tier is holding us back." OR "I've been hearing about [Product B] from other customers — I want to know more."
- Content purpose: Remove friction from the expansion decision by providing business justification content, cost comparison models, and peer validation
- Content types that convert Stage 4 to expansion revenue:
  - Tier comparison content: NOT a sales pitch — a practical "what you get in each tier and what types of teams benefit from each" guide written from the customer's perspective, not the vendor's
  - "Cost of limitation" calculator: quantifies what the customer is NOT getting by staying at their current tier — missed outcomes, manual workarounds, team inefficiencies caused by tier limits
  - Peer expansion stories: anonymized or named case studies specifically structured as "We were where you are. Here's what triggered our upgrade decision and what we got in the first 90 days afterward."
  - Executive briefing document: one-page summary designed for the economic buyer to justify the expansion investment — ROI projection, peer references, implementation timeline
  - Migration/upgrade guide: reduces upgrade anxiety by showing exactly what changes, what stays the same, and what onboarding support is available for the expanded tier
- Content delivery: CSM-sent at CS-identified expansion opportunity moment + CS email cadence linking to specific content assets + customer newsletter "what's possible" section for upgrade-eligible accounts

**Stage 5 — Multi-Product (Post-expansion):**
- Customer psychology: "We're all in. We want to be your most successful customer. What else should we know?"
- Content purpose: Deepen platform adoption, build advocacy, unlock referral and case study participation
- Content types that serve Stage 5:
  - Platform mastery roadmap: a documented path from "power user of Product A" to "fully integrated across Product A + B + C"
  - Executive advisory content: strategic-level content that positions them as partners, not just customers (market intelligence, benchmark data, industry trend analysis)
  - Advocacy enablement kit: make it easy for them to speak at events, write bylines, participate in case studies — provide templates, topic suggestions, PR support
  - Co-creation opportunities: advisory board participation, beta program access, research report contribution — turns most engaged customers into brand assets

DELIVERABLE 2 — EXPANSION CONTENT ASSET LIBRARY (10 HIGHEST-LEVERAGE ASSETS)

Design the 10 specific content assets that generate the most expansion revenue per content unit produced. For each asset, specify the production brief, distribution trigger, and CS activation protocol:

**Asset 1 — The "Your Cohort" Benchmark Report:**
- Purpose: Shows each customer where they rank compared to customers at a similar company size/industry/tenure. Customers who see they're in the bottom 40% of product utilization develop natural pull to improve — and improvement requires more of the product.
- Production brief: AI-synthesized anonymized benchmark data across your customer base, segmented by company size (SMB/MM/Enterprise), industry vertical, and product tier. Updated quarterly.
- Personalization: Each customer receives the version benchmarked against their cohort — not the overall average. Specific language: "Among 180 [Industry] companies using [Product] for 6+ months, your team uses [N] of [M] available features. The top 25% of companies in your cohort use [X] features and report [Y% better outcome]."
- Distribution trigger: Automatically sent at 6-month customer anniversary. CSM alerted when a customer opens the report more than once (indicates they're studying it, not just skimming).
- CS activation: When a customer opens the benchmark report 2+ times within 7 days, CSM sends: "Saw you've been reviewing the benchmark report — happy to walk through specifically which capabilities move teams in your cohort from the middle tier to top performer. Would a 20-minute call make sense?"

**Asset 2 — The Adjacent Product Discovery Guide (Cross-Sell Specific):**
- Purpose: Introduces a complementary product in terms of outcomes, not features. Most customers don't know what else you sell until a CSM pitches it — and a pitch feels like upselling. A discovery guide that leads with peer outcomes feels like a service.
- Production brief: For each major cross-sell path (Product A → Product B), create a 1,200-word editorial guide structured as: (1) "The problem customers like you run into after they've mastered Product A" → (2) "What Product B does, explained in terms of what Product A customers already understand" → (3) Two peer case studies from Product A customers who added Product B and what changed within 90 days → (4) "How to know if Product B makes sense for your team right now" (self-qualification criteria, not a sales qualification process).
- Distribution trigger: Sent when a customer reaches 85%+ utilization of their current tier's feature set in a category that connects to the adjacent product. AI agent monitors product usage data weekly and queues the guide automatically.
- CS activation: CSM receives a Slack/email alert: "[Customer] just received the [Product B] discovery guide based on their [Product A] usage pattern. Their last QBR mentioned [relevant pain point]. If they open the guide, follow up within 48 hours."

**Asset 3 — The ROI Documentation Template:**
- Purpose: Most customers feel the value but can't quantify it for their CFO or economic buyer. This template does the ROI math for them, making internal expansion justification effortless.
- Production brief: A fillable template (Google Sheets or web-based) with pre-populated formulas for the 3 most common ROI drivers for this product. The customer enters their own numbers (hours saved, deals won, errors reduced) and the template calculates ROI, payback period, and 3-year NPV. Produced in PDF and interactive web format.
- Personalization: Include an industry-specific version for each of your top 5 customer verticals (e.g., the ROI drivers for a cybersecurity company are different from a marketing agency).
- Distribution trigger: Sent proactively at 90-day post-purchase mark ("Here's how to calculate and communicate the business impact you've generated so far") AND when a CSM tags an account as "expansion in progress" (CSM sends it before the executive conversation).
- CS activation: Template completion (tracked via web analytics) triggers an alert to CSM. A customer who fills out the ROI calculator and reaches a positive result number is highly expansion-ready — they're building their own business case.

**Asset 4 — The "What Our Top 10% Customers Do Differently" Editorial:**
- Purpose: Aspiration content that shows the gap between where a customer is and where the best customers are — without making them feel judged. Creates natural pull toward behaviors (and product usage) that correlate with expansion.
- Production brief: Qualitative editorial piece (1,500-2,000 words) based on CS-gathered insights about the 5-7 specific behaviors, workflow designs, or product configurations that distinguish your top-performing customers. Written in first person from the perspective of those customers' experiences. Includes specific metrics where available.
- Distribution trigger: Published in customer newsletter at 4-month customer anniversary. Sent directly by CSM to accounts at risk of plateauing (using flag in CS platform).
- CS activation: None required — the content itself creates pull. Track customers who read this piece twice and engage CSM to follow up within 14 days.

**Asset 5 — The Upgrade Readiness Self-Assessment:**
- Purpose: Customers who self-qualify for an upgrade are dramatically more likely to convert than customers who are pushed through a sales process. A self-assessment empowers the customer to reach their own conclusion.
- Production brief: A 10-question interactive assessment embedded on your customer portal or sent via email. Questions probe: (1) current utilization patterns that indicate tier ceiling, (2) team growth signals that create natural need for more seats or capacity, (3) business outcomes desired that require capabilities in the next tier. Assessment outputs a simple score with a recommendation: "Based on your responses, your team is showing signals that [X customers in similar situations] found [Next Tier] addressed. Here's what changed for them in the first 60 days."
- Distribution trigger: Sent at 6-month anniversary AND when product usage monitoring detects a customer hitting tier limits 3+ times in a 30-day period.
- CS activation: Assessment completion + "upgrade recommended" result triggers immediate CSM notification and scheduling of "upgrade readiness call" within 5 business days.

**Asset 6 — The Peer Expansion Story (Case Study Variant):**
- Purpose: Different from standard case studies (which are written for prospects). These stories are specifically framed as "a customer like you, at the stage you're at, made a decision to expand, and here's what happened." The reader is the protagonist before the decision; the case study shows the outcome of making it.
- Production brief: 800-word case study with a specific structure: (1) "[Company] at [relevant stage]: same tier you're on, same usage patterns, similar business context" → (2) "The decision trigger: what pushed them to evaluate upgrading" → (3) "The 90-day reality: specific outcomes, metrics, team adoption story" → (4) "12-month view: how expansion ARR and team impact compares to the investment." One peer expansion story per major expansion path (Starter → Professional, Professional → Enterprise, core product → each major module).
- Distribution trigger: CSM-activated when an expansion conversation is opened with a customer. CSM selects the most relevant peer story from a library organized by customer profile (industry + company size + expansion path).
- CS activation: CSM email template: "Thought this might be useful as you're evaluating whether [Next Tier] makes sense. [Customer Name] at [Company] was in a similar position 8 months ago and was kind enough to share their experience. Happy to make an intro if that would be valuable."

**Asset 7 — The Power User Certification Program:**
- Purpose: A structured, credentialed learning path that drives deep product mastery, creates internal advocacy, and is strongly correlated with expansion because certified power users naturally discover and request the capabilities in the next tier.
- Production brief: 6-module learning program with a final assessment and a sharable digital badge. Each module covers an advanced capability cluster. Final assessment requires demonstration of outcome-based application (not just feature knowledge). Badge issued to LinkedIn and internal profile.
- Distribution trigger: Offered automatically to any user who completes the core onboarding curriculum and reaches 60 days of active usage.
- CS activation: When a user earns the certification, CSM sends a congratulations note AND internally flags the account for potential expansion — certified power users are 2.3x more likely to initiate expansion conversations within 90 days of certification.

**Asset 8 — The Executive Impact Report (Quarterly):**
- Purpose: Most expansion decisions are made by economic buyers who rarely use the product themselves. This report is written exclusively for them — one page, business outcomes only, no feature language, designed to be forwarded to the CFO or CMO.
- Production brief: Auto-generated quarterly report (using product usage data + outcomes data from CS notes) with: (1) key business outcomes achieved in the quarter attributed to the product, (2) team adoption metric, (3) benchmark comparison (how this company compares to peers), (4) two or three capability highlights from the next tier that other companies at this stage have found valuable. Must be deliverable in under 5 minutes of reading time.
- Distribution trigger: Automatically sent to the economic buyer contact in CRM on the first day of each new quarter. CSM is CC'd.
- CS activation: If the economic buyer doesn't open the report within 72 hours of receipt, CSM sends a follow-up: "Wanted to make sure the Q[N] impact summary got to you — happy to walk through the highlights in 10 minutes if helpful."

**Asset 9 — The Integration Showcase Series:**
- Purpose: For platforms with integration ecosystems, integrations are often the highest-value expansion trigger — connecting the product to other tools in the customer's stack dramatically increases stickiness and value. Most customers don't know which integrations are available or what they accomplish. Each showcase makes one integration's outcome value visible.
- Production brief: One 600-word piece per major integration, structured as: (1) "What changes when [Product] connects to [Integration]" (lead with the workflow outcome, not the technical steps) → (2) Two specific outcomes customers have seen from the integration → (3) 10-minute setup guide. Published as a web page, customer newsletter feature, and CSM email asset.
- Distribution trigger: AI agent monitors which integrations a customer has NOT enabled that are commonly used by customers with similar profiles. Automatically queues the relevant showcase guide and CSM alert.
- CS activation: "Noticed [Customer] hasn't connected [Integration] yet — [X customers in their industry] report [specific benefit] after enabling it. Would it be worth sending them the 10-minute setup guide?"

**Asset 10 — The Expansion Business Case Deck Template:**
- Purpose: When a champion inside a customer organization wants to make the case for expansion, they need to present to stakeholders who weren't part of the original purchase. This template gives them everything they need to run that internal sales process without needing to involve your team until the decision is nearly made.
- Production brief: A 12-slide PowerPoint/Google Slides template, pre-populated with: (1) current product value summary (pull from Executive Impact Report), (2) "what we'd get in [Next Tier]" slides with specific capability callouts, (3) cost analysis (including opportunity cost of staying at current tier), (4) peer reference slide (2-3 company names/outcomes, if approved), (5) implementation timeline, (6) proposed next steps. Champion fills in 4-5 blanks; everything else is pre-written.
- Distribution trigger: CS or sales sends when a champion explicitly says they need to "take this to their leadership" — intercepts the moment they'd otherwise try to build the deck themselves and fail.
- CS activation: Explicitly ask in every expansion discovery call: "If this makes sense for your team, what would it take to get leadership buy-in? Would it be helpful to have a deck you could customize and bring into that conversation?"

DELIVERABLE 3 — EXPANSION CONTENT ENGAGEMENT SIGNAL SCORING SYSTEM

Build a 100-point scoring model that predicts expansion readiness from content engagement behavior. Signal score ≥65 triggers automatic CS notification for expansion outreach:

**Content Consumption Signals (50 points):**
- Benchmark report opened: 8 points; opened 2+ times: +5 bonus points (15 total: indicates active study)
- ROI documentation template completed and submitted: 18 points (highest single signal — building their own business case)
- Upgrade readiness self-assessment completed: 15 points
- Adjacent product discovery guide fully read (scroll depth >80%): 10 points
- Peer expansion story opened and read >3 minutes: 7 points
- Power user certification completed: 8 points
- Executive impact report forwarded (to someone outside the original contact): 12 points (indicates internal visibility)

**Integration and Usage Signals (30 points):**
- Core tier feature utilization >85% for 30+ consecutive days: 15 points (hitting the ceiling)
- Cross-product integration discovery page visited 2+ times: 8 points
- Visited the pricing or tier comparison page (even if just browsed): 12 points (active evaluation)
- Submitted a support ticket requesting a feature that's only available in the next tier: 20 points (strongest usage signal)

**Relationship and Advocacy Signals (20 points):**
- Responded positively to NPS survey (score 9-10): 5 points
- Volunteered for customer reference, case study, or event speaking: 10 points
- Invited colleagues to the platform who are outside the original licensed team: 8 points
- Engaged with customer community content related to advanced capabilities: 4 points

**Score Interpretation and CS Protocol:**
- 0-40 points: No action required. Continue standard lifecycle content cadence.
- 41-64 points: CSM receives weekly digest of signals. No immediate action unless score jumps >15 points in 7 days.
- 65-79 points: CSM receives real-time alert. Send Asset 2 (Adjacent Product Discovery Guide) or Asset 5 (Upgrade Readiness Self-Assessment) within 48 hours. Schedule expansion discovery call within 14 days.
- 80-100 points: Immediate CSM alert + sales team notification. Expansion-ready. Priority outreach within 24 hours. CS sends Asset 6 (Peer Expansion Story) + Asset 10 (Business Case Deck Template) in first outreach.

DELIVERABLE 6 — EXPANSION CONTENT ROI MODEL

**Measurement Framework:**

*Content-Sourced Expansion:* Expansion opportunities where the last marketing touchpoint before expansion conversation initiation was a content asset engagement.

*Content-Influenced Expansion:* Expansion opportunities where at least one content asset was engaged within 90 days prior to expansion conversation, even if other touchpoints also occurred.

*Signal-Triggered Expansion:* Expansion opportunities initiated by CS based on an expansion signal score crossing the 65-point threshold — directly attributable to the content engagement monitoring system.

**Unit Economics (target benchmarks for B2B SaaS programs at maturity):**
- Content-influenced expansion as % of total expansion ARR: target ≥35% at 12-month program maturity
- Average expansion cycle time for content-engaged accounts vs. non-content-engaged: target 25-35% shorter cycle for content-engaged
- Expansion conversion rate for accounts that completed ROI template: target 2-3x baseline expansion rate
- Expansion deal size premium for certified power users vs. non-certified: target 15-25% higher ACV

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Customers are engaging with expansion content (high open rates on benchmark reports, ROI template downloads) but not converting to expansion conversations or upgrading.
**Solution:** The most common root cause is that the content is reaching the wrong person — the practitioner who uses the product daily, not the economic buyer who controls the expansion budget. Audit your CRM contact records: are the economic buyers (VP People, CFO, CEO at SMB) even receiving the content, or does it only go to the primary product user? Reroute your Executive Impact Report specifically to the economic buyer contact field in your CRM. For accounts where you don't have the economic buyer's email, use the CSM to request a warm introduction: "I'd like to send [Economic Buyer] our quarterly impact summary — would you be able to make that introduction, or should I reach out directly?" Also review whether your upgrade frictionis the conversion gap — customers who want to upgrade but face a procurement or approval process without tools (no business case deck, no ROI model) will stall regardless of intent. Asset 10 (Business Case Deck Template) addresses this directly.

**Problem:** CSMs aren't using the content assets in their conversations — the content library isn't getting adopted internally.
**Solution:** Content adoption by CS teams fails for one of three reasons: (1) the content is hard to find — solve by building a searchable, tagged library in whatever tool CSMs already live in (Notion, Confluence, Salesforce sidebar) rather than a separate content portal they have to log into; (2) the content isn't mapped to the conversation moment — CSMs don't want to browse a library in the middle of a call, they want a one-click answer to "what do I send a customer who just asked X?"; solve by building the CS Quick Reference table (in Deliverable 5) as a pinned Slack resource and adding it to the CRM account view; (3) the value hasn't been demonstrated — once you have one clear example of a CSM sending a content asset that accelerated an expansion conversation, feature it in your next CS team meeting and attribute the expansion ARR to the content. Social proof within the CS team drives adoption more reliably than training or mandates.

**Problem:** The expansion signal score system generates high-scoring accounts that CSMs reach out to, but many of those accounts "aren't ready" and find the expansion outreach premature or pushy.
**Solution:** Signal score calibration typically takes 2-3 quarters to stabilize. In the early period, add a human-review layer: instead of fully automating CS notification at 65+ score, create a weekly "expansion qualified accounts" Slack digest where a senior CSM or Customer Marketing manager reviews the top-scored accounts and filters out any false positives before the list goes to CSMs. Track the outcome of every expansion conversation triggered by signal score — won, lost, or "too early." After 90 days, analyze which specific signal combinations most reliably predict genuine expansion readiness versus false signals. Common false positive: the ROI template download without completion adds only a marginal signal but some CS teams treat it as high-conviction. Weight completed templates (18 points) vs. downloaded/opened templates (3 points) to avoid this. Also segment signal scoring by customer tenure — a 3-month customer hitting tier ceiling signals is likely a data issue or onboarding gap, not expansion readiness; a 9-month customer hitting the same ceiling is almost certainly expansion-ready.
