## Overview
This prompt deploys an autonomous AI agent that continuously monitors your entire B2B SaaS content portfolio, diagnoses performance gaps against revenue KPIs, generates optimized variants for underperforming assets, and builds a perpetual improvement loop that compounds content ROI over time — without requiring human intervention at every cycle.

# AUDIT FRAMEWORK

## Phase 1: Revenue Impact Classification
Score each asset 1-10 across five dimensions, then average for composite score:

Dimension 1 — Traffic Velocity (1-10)
  10: Top quintile traffic + positive 90-day trend
  5: Average traffic, flat trend
  1: Bottom quintile, declining trend

Dimension 2 — Conversion Efficiency (1-10)
  10: MQL conversion rate 2x+ category average
  5: At category average
  1: Below 25% of category average

Dimension 3 — Pipeline Attribution (1-10)
  10: Direct pipeline contribution in top 20% of portfolio
  5: Middle 40%
  1: Bottom 20% or zero attribution

Dimension 4 — Keyword Opportunity (1-10)
  10: Ranks 4-10 for high-volume commercial intent keyword (big rank-up opportunity)
  5: Ranks 1-3 for medium-volume or 11-20 for high-volume
  1: Ranks 20+ for low-volume keyword

Dimension 5 — Strategic ICP Alignment (1-10)
  10: Perfectly targets primary ICP, commercial/decision stage, upcoming campaign theme
  5: Partially aligned
  1: Wrong audience, wrong stage, or contradicts positioning

Tier Assignment:
  8.0-10.0: Revenue Engine — Protect, amplify, update defensively
  6.0-7.9: Sleeping Giant — High-priority optimization
  4.0-5.9: Marginal — Optimize if low effort, monitor otherwise
  2.0-3.9: Misaligned — Consolidate with stronger asset or redirect
  0-1.9: Sunset Candidate — Archive or noindex

## Phase 2: Root Cause Diagnosis (7 Failure Modes)
For each Sleeping Giant, identify the primary failure mode:

Failure Mode 1 — INTENT MISMATCH
  Signal: High bounce rate (>70%) despite ranking for target keyword
  Cause: Content serves informational intent but keyword is commercial; or vice versa
  Fix: Restructure content to match SERP intent; or target different keyword

Failure Mode 2 — THIN COVERAGE
  Signal: Low average session duration (<90 sec) for MOFU/BOFU topic
  Cause: Insufficient depth for competitive topic; fails EEAT scoring
  Fix: Expand to 2,500+ words; add original data, expert quotes, framework diagrams

Failure Mode 3 — STALE DATA
  Signal: Last updated >18 months ago; stats pre-date major industry shift
  Cause: Content was strong but has decayed; competitors published fresher versions
  Fix: Systematic refresh of all statistics, examples, screenshots, product references

Failure Mode 4 — WEAK CTA ARCHITECTURE
  Signal: Good traffic + engagement, low MQL conversion; CTA doesn't match buyer readiness
  Cause: TOFU offer on MOFU page; generic CTA not connected to content topic
  Fix: Match CTA to funnel stage; use Jobs-to-be-Done language; add inline micro-CTAs

Failure Mode 5 — DISTRIBUTION FAILURE
  Signal: Great content metrics (time on page, scroll depth) but low external traffic
  Cause: Insufficient backlinks; not promoted in email; no internal linking from high-traffic pages
  Fix: Run targeted backlink outreach; add to email newsletter; build 5+ internal links from Traffic Engines

Failure Mode 6 — FORMAT MISMATCH
  Signal: High bounce despite right keyword; competitor video/interactive content outranks
  Cause: Topic better served by interactive tool, video, or comparison table format
  Fix: Transform into interactive calculator, embed video explainer, or restructure as decision guide

Failure Mode 7 — EEAT DEFICIT
  Signal: Underranking on author-sensitive topics (health, legal, financial, security)
  Cause: Anonymous author, no credentials, no external validation, no original research
  Fix: Add author bio with credentials; cite primary research; get quotes from named experts; add trust signals

## Phase 3: Revenue Impact Prioritization
For each Tier 2 (Sleeping Giant) and Tier 3 (Gap) opportunity:

Revenue Impact Score (RIS) =
  (Current Monthly Sessions) × (Target Conversion Rate − Current Conversion Rate)
  × (ACV ÷ 12) × (Ranking Improvement Probability 0-1)

Ranking Improvement Probability Guidelines:
  - Currently ranks 4-10, strong domain: 0.75
  - Currently ranks 11-20, moderate competition: 0.55
  - Currently ranks 21-50, creating new content: 0.35
  - Currently ranks 51+, highly competitive: 0.20

Strategic multipliers (apply additively, max 2.0x):
  +0.5x: Asset in primary growth ICP segment
  +0.3x: Competitor ranks above us on this keyword
  +0.2x: Keyword trending up 30%+ in past 90 days
  −0.3x: High editorial effort required (reduces speed-to-impact)

Final ranking: Sort by RIS × multipliers, descending. Top 10 are your optimization sprint queue.

## Phase 5: Perpetual Loop Architecture

Design the autonomous optimization system with the following AI agent roles:

AGENT 1 — Performance Monitoring Agent
  Trigger: Weekly, every Monday 8am
  Inputs: GA4 traffic report, Ahrefs rank tracker export, HubSpot attribution report
  Process: Compares this week vs. 4-week average for each asset; flags anomalies (>15% change)
  Outputs: Slack digest with top 5 movers (up and down); updated asset score in Airtable/Notion
  Escalation trigger: Any Revenue Engine asset drops >20% — immediately alerts Content Lead

AGENT 2 — Diagnosis Agent
  Trigger: When Performance Monitoring Agent flags a Sleeping Giant (drops into underperform threshold)
  Inputs: Asset performance data + content text + SERP comparison (top 3 ranking competitors)
  Process: Runs 7-failure-mode classification; identifies primary and secondary failure mode
  Outputs: Diagnosis card in Airtable with failure mode, evidence, recommended action, RIS score
  Escalation trigger: If new Tier 2 asset identified, moves to optimization queue

AGENT 3 — Content Generation Agent
  Trigger: When Diagnosis Agent creates optimization card; after human approval gate
  Inputs: Current content URL, diagnosis card, brand voice guide, ICP definition, competitor content
  Process: Generates full reoptimized draft or detailed rewrite brief (based on effort flag)
  Outputs: Google Doc draft with tracked changes; meta title/description options; CTA recommendations
  Human checkpoint: Content lead reviews and approves within 48 hours before publishing

AGENT 4 — Distribution Agent
  Trigger: 24 hours after optimized content is published
  Inputs: Published URL, target audience definition, content angle
  Process: Generates LinkedIn post (3 variants), email newsletter tease paragraph, 
           internal linking recommendations (3 pages), backlink outreach email template
  Outputs: Content in HubSpot Social tool queue; Airtable record updated with distribution plan

AGENT 5 — Loop Review Agent
  Trigger: Monthly (1st of each month)
  Inputs: All optimization records from prior 30 days + performance delta data
  Process: Calculates ROI of optimization work; identifies winning optimization patterns;
           updates scoring model based on actual vs. projected impact
  Outputs: Monthly Content Intelligence Report (portfolio health trend, ROI of optimizations,
           model accuracy score, next-month priority queue)

12-WEEK SPRINT CALENDAR:

Weeks 1-2: BASELINE & CLASSIFY
  Week 1: Run full audit; pull all performance data; score all assets
  Week 2: Validate classification with content lead; lock top 10 optimization queue; set KPI baselines

Weeks 3-4: PRIORITY OPTIMIZATIONS — BATCH 1
  Week 3: Generate prescriptions + drafts for top 5 Sleeping Giants (Agent 3 output)
  Week 4: Human review, edit, publish; activate Agent 4 for distribution

Weeks 5-6: DISTRIBUTION AMPLIFICATION
  Week 5: Execute backlink outreach for top 3 optimized assets; push internal linking
  Week 6: Newsletter promotion; LinkedIn organic push; paid amplification if budget allows

Weeks 7-8: MEASURE & ITERATE
  Week 7: Compare week-over-week performance vs. baselines (Agents 1+2)
  Week 8: Identify early winners; double down distribution; pause underperformers

Weeks 9-10: PRIORITY OPTIMIZATIONS — BATCH 2
  Week 9: Prescriptions + drafts for next 5 Sleeping Giants and top 2 Gap fills
  Week 10: Human review, edit, publish; distribution activation

Weeks 11-12: FULL CYCLE REVIEW & LOOP CALIBRATION
  Week 11: Agent 5 generates monthly review; calculate actual vs. projected RIS
  Week 12: Update scoring model; identify next sprint queue; present to CMO

Weekly Review Ritual (30 minutes every Thursday):
  Min 1-5: Performance anomaly review (Agent 1 output)
  Min 6-15: Optimization queue status (which assets are in progress, blocked, or published)
  Min 16-25: Early results from recent optimizations (vs. baselines)
  Min 26-30: Decision gate: approve next batch for Agent 3 to generate

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI diagnoses are too generic and don't reflect our specific competitive landscape**
Solution: Enrich the system context before running diagnosis. Feed the agent: (1) your ICP definition with buyer language examples, (2) top 3 competitor content examples for each asset's keyword, (3) your product positioning doc, (4) 3-5 examples of your highest-converting content so the agent learns your winning pattern before analyzing underperformers. Store this as a reusable "context packet" in your Content Ops Hub.

**Problem: Optimization sprint priorities keep shifting before the team can execute**
Solution: Institute a 4-week "sprint freeze" after prioritization — no new assets added to the top 5 queue mid-sprint. Use the Revenue at Risk metric (not just traffic) to justify locking priorities.
