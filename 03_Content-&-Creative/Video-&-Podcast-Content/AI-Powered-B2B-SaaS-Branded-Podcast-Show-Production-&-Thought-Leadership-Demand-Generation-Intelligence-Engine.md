## Overview
This prompt engine automates the full lifecycle of a B2B branded podcast — from show concept and positioning through episode production, guest sourcing, distribution, and pipeline attribution. Use it when you want to build a long-running audio/video content channel that compounds audience trust, generates organic pipeline, and positions your brand as the definitive voice in your category.

**HubSpot:**
- Create a "Podcast" campaign in HubSpot and tag all UTM-sourced contacts automatically via workflow

- Add custom fields: "Podcast Source" (picklist), "Episodes Listened" (number), "Guest in CRM" (checkbox)

- Use Spotify's audience data to validate that ICP personas match actual listener demographics

- Episode production template: Brief → Recording → Edit → Show Notes → Distribution → Metrics Review

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Episode downloads are flat after month 2 with no growth.
**Solution:** Downloads plateau without active distribution. Check: (1) Are you submitting to all 20+ podcast directories beyond Apple/Spotify? (2) Are guests actively sharing — or just re-posting your clip without their own caption? Create a "Guest Amplification Kit" with 3 pre-written LinkedIn posts and ask guests to personalize and post within 48 hours of air date. (3) Start a LinkedIn newsletter that sends show notes as issues — each subscriber becomes a reliable episode open regardless of algorithm. Target 10% episode-download-to-newsletter-subscriber conversion.

**Problem:** Guests from target accounts aren't converting to sales conversations.
**Solution:** The podcast creates warmth but rarely creates urgency on its own. Build a structured 30-day post-episode sequence: Day 1 — thank-you email with episode link + listener stats; Day 14 — share a specific insight from their episode that sparked listener questions (demonstrates credibility); Day 30 — introduce a relevant customer success story related to the guest's company type + soft offer for a "follow-up conversation." Keep it relationship-first. Sales should see the guest as warm relationship, not hot lead.

**Problem:** The show sounds too similar to competitors and isn't getting shared organically.
**Solution:** Generic formats don't compound. Introduce a signature recurring element that becomes the show's trademark — a ritual that listeners anticipate and discuss. Examples: a "Worst financial decision that taught you the most?" closing question that always surfaces vulnerable, viral stories; a "One metric you obsess over that nobody else is watching" segment; or a "Hot take" opening where the guest defends a contrarian industry belief. One memorable recurring element, consistently executed, is what makes listeners tell colleagues "you have to hear this show."
