## Overview

Generates publication-ready video scripts for the full portfolio of B2B SaaS marketing video assets — hero explainers, feature deep-dives, use-case walkthroughs, and persona-targeted product stories — using a proven problem-solution-proof-CTA architecture. Use it when your product page needs a converting explainer, when launching a new feature, or when building the video content library your sales team and demand gen team rely on daily.

DELIVERABLE 2: HOMEPAGE HERO EXPLAINER SCRIPT (90-second, ~220 words)
═══════════════════════════════════════

Write a full production-ready script using the Problem → Agitate → Solution → Proof → CTA structure.

Script format for each beat:
[VISUAL]: Specific direction for animator or director
[VOICEOVER]: Exact spoken words (time-stamped)
[ON-SCREEN TEXT]: If different from VO (used for emphasis, data callouts, product labels)
[MUSIC NOTE]: Emotional direction for this beat (not specific song)

BEAT 1 — HOOK (0:00–0:05):
- Open with a single, visceral sentence that names the exact pain the buyer woke up worried about this morning
- NO company intro in the first 5 seconds (the viewer doesn't care who you are yet)
- Test: Would your buyer say "that's exactly my problem" in the first 5 seconds?
- Visual: Abstract representation of the problem state (not a product screenshot)

BEAT 2 — PROBLEM AGITATION (0:05–0:15):
- Name the status quo behavior and its hidden cost
- Use a specific number to quantify the pain (hours wasted, incidents missed, dollars at risk)
- Create urgency without fear-mongering: the cost of waiting, not the apocalypse
- Visual: Show the old way — spreadsheets, inbox chaos, manual work — in a compressed, recognizable montage

BEAT 3 — SOLUTION REVEAL (0:15–0:30):
- Introduce the product by the outcome it creates, not the features it has
- Lead with the category-level promise: "What if you could always know exactly which vendors pose real risk — before they cause a breach?"
- Name the product once, clearly
- Visual: The product interface appears — clean, calm, purposeful (contrast to the chaos of Beat 2)

BEAT 4 — HOW IT WORKS (0:30–1:05):
- 3 capabilities, 10–12 seconds each — specific enough to be credible, simple enough to be understood by a non-technical executive
- Each capability follows: [What it does] → [What the user sees] → [What changes as a result]
- Avoid feature names — use outcome language: "Instantly see which of your 2,300 vendors are high-risk" not "Automated vendor scoring dashboard"
- Visual: Animated product walkthrough — show the actual UI moving, not static screenshots. Use a named fictional user ("Maria, Vendor Risk Manager at a fintech with 400 active vendors")

BEAT 5 — PROOF (1:05–1:20):
- One specific, believable outcome from a real or representative customer
- Include the company type, not the name (if no permission to use logo): "A Series D fintech with 800 vendors reduced critical incidents by 64% in their first 6 months"
- Proof should map to the problem stated in Beat 2 — if you said "hours wasted," your proof should quantify time saved
- Visual: Customer logo (if approved) or abstract customer representation + stat callout animation

BEAT 6 — CTA (1:20–1:30):
- One action, low-friction, specific: "See how it works with your vendor list — book a live demo in 2 clicks"
- Never end on "Learn More" — it signals no confidence in the product
- Visual: Clean UI of demo booking flow or trial signup — make the next step as obvious as clicking the button you just showed

DELIVERABLE 3: FEATURE VIDEO SCRIPT SERIES (2 minutes each)
═══════════════════════════════════════

Write scripts for the 3 highest-priority features. Each feature video follows the "Day in the Life → Problem Moment → Feature in Context → Outcome" structure.

For EACH feature video:

A) OPENING SCENARIO (0:00–0:20):
- Drop the viewer into a specific, realistic work situation your user lives through
- Name a character: "It's Tuesday morning. Priya, a Vendor Risk Manager at a healthcare SaaS company, has 47 new vendor questionnaires in her inbox."
- Do NOT start with "Meet Priya" — start with Priya already in the problem
- Create emotional recognition: "She knows she'll spend the next 3 weeks copying risk data from email PDFs into the same spreadsheet she's used since 2021."

B) PROBLEM MOMENT (0:20–0:35):
- Show the specific friction point this feature eliminates (not the general problem — the exact moment)
- Make it feel familiar and slightly painful: "She opens vendor #1's questionnaire. 200 questions. 47 vendors. She does the math."

C) FEATURE IN CONTEXT (0:35–1:30):
- Show the feature as Priya uses it — real workflow, real interface
- Every action she takes should have an immediate visible result: she clicks → something meaningful happens
- Use specific, realistic data in the UI (vendor names like "Stripe," "Salesforce," "Zendesk" — not "Vendor A," "Vendor B")
- Include one "aha moment" — the specific screen or result that makes someone watching say "oh, I didn't know it could do that"
- Voiceover narrates what Priya is experiencing, not what the product is doing: "Instead of reading 200-page security policies, Priya sees one risk score — and the three specific clauses that drove it."

D) OUTCOME (1:30–2:00):
- Zoom out from Priya to the business impact: what changed for the team/company as a result
- Use a "before / after" in the same visual frame: side-by-side, or a before state that fades into after
- End with a metric: "Teams using automated questionnaire generation complete third-party assessments 8x faster and with 40% fewer compliance gaps"
- CTA tied to this specific feature: "See how it works for your vendor program → Start a free assessment"

## Integration Tips

**Loom / Vidyard (SDR Prospecting Video):**
- Upload the SDR prospecting script template to your sales enablement platform as a "script card" reps can reference while recording — Vidyard supports teleprompter overlays natively
- Set up Loom notifications to alert the SDR when a prospect rewatches the video — a second view within 48 hours is a buying signal for immediate follow-up
- Tag each Loom video send in HubSpot with a custom property "Video Prospecting Sent" (Yes/No) and track reply rate and meeting-booked rate by persona to iterate the script

**Wistia / Vimeo (Website Video Hosting):**
- Wistia's heatmap feature shows exactly where viewers pause, rewind, or drop off — use this data to identify which script beat is losing viewers and rewrite or re-edit that section within 30 days of launch
- Set a Wistia conversion goal on your demo CTA end screen; this creates a trackable conversion event you can pass to HubSpot as a "Video CTA Click" activity
- Add Wistia's "Email Capture" gate at the 60-second mark of feature videos (not the hero explainer — let that one play freely) to capture emails from engaged viewers before they leave

**HubSpot:**
- Create a "Video Engagement" smart list: contacts who watched 75%+ of the hero explainer but have NOT requested a demo — trigger a 3-email nurture sequence with a "still have questions?" CTA
- Use Wistia-HubSpot integration to log video watch time as a contact timeline event — your SDRs will see "watched the treasury forecasting feature video for 1:47" before calling, enabling smarter personalization
- Build a lead scoring rule: +10 points for watching any video 50%+; +25 points for clicking the video CTA

**Salesforce:**
- Add a custom opportunity field "Video Assets Shared" (multi-select: Hero Explainer, Feature: Forecasting, Persona: CFO, Customer Story) — AEs update this at each stage and RevOps uses it to correlate video exposure with win rate
- Create a Salesforce report "Video-Influenced Won Opportunities" filtered by accounts where the primary contact has Wistia video events — present this quarterly as part of content attribution reporting

**Notion / ClickUp (Script Library):**
- Build a "Video Script Library" database in Notion with fields: Video Type, Persona Target, Last Updated, Production Status, A/B Variant, Performance Link
- Each script should have a "Next Review" date — hero explainers should be reviewed every 6 months as product positioning evolves; feature videos should be reviewed with every major product release
- Create a "Script Change Log" section in each Notion page so the team can track what changed between script versions and why (prevents re-introducing removed messaging)

**Frame.io / Dropbox (Video Review Workflow):**
- Upload rough cut to Frame.io and send to 2 real customers for review before final production — ask specifically "did you recognize yourself in the problem we described?" and "was there anything confusing about how we explained [Feature X]?"
- Require sign-off from one Sales rep and one Customer Success Manager before locking the final script — they hear objections and confusion daily that marketing doesn't

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The hero explainer tested well internally but homepage conversion rate didn't move after launch.
**Solution:** Internal viewers already understand your product — they aren't a valid test audience. Run a 5-second test (UsabilityHub or equivalent) with 50 people who match your ICP but have never heard of your company. Ask: "What does this company do?" and "Who is this for?" If they can't answer correctly, the first 10 seconds of your video have failed. The fix is almost always the hook: it's either too abstract (talking about the industry problem, not the buyer's daily experience), too jargon-heavy (using your product category name before establishing why it matters), or too slow to deliver the value statement. Rewrite the opening 15 seconds, re-record only that section, and retest before committing to a full reshoot.

**Problem:** Feature videos are getting clicks but few demo requests — viewers watch but don't convert.
**Solution:** Feature videos are showing capability without connecting capability to business outcome. Viewers understand what the feature does but don't know why they should care enough to book a demo today. Fix: add a "Before/After" slide at the 1:45 mark of every 2-minute feature video — a split-screen showing the old way (time, friction, risk) next to the new way (time, clarity, control). Add one specific metric from a customer who uses this feature. Then make the CTA directly tied to this feature's value: "See this in your data — book a 20-minute feature walkthrough" instead of the generic "Request a demo." Feature-specific CTAs consistently outperform generic CTAs by 22–40% in B2B video.

**Problem:** SDRs recorded the prospecting videos using the script template but reply rates didn't improve.
**Solution:** The script is right but the personalization is wrong. Review 10 videos SDRs have sent and check: (1) Are they reading the script verbatim? Robotic delivery kills authenticity — coach reps to internalize the structure and speak naturally from memory, not script. (2) Is the personalized hook in the first 5 seconds actually specific? "I saw your LinkedIn profile" is not personalization. "I saw Cascade just opened a second engineering hub in Berlin — that typically means your vendor count goes up 30–40% in the next 12 months" is personalization. Require reps to identify one company-specific trigger event before recording. (3) Is the video under 75 seconds? Anything longer loses the viewer before the CTA. Enforce a strict time limit and do mock recordings in onboarding until every rep can nail the structure in under 70 seconds.
