## Overview

Design an AI-driven adaptive learning system that analyzes individual user behavior, role, and feature adoption patterns to auto-generate personalized education journeys — accelerating time-to-value, reducing churn, and driving expansion revenue. Use this when you need to move beyond static one-size-fits-all training toward intelligent, behavior-triggered education that scales without a proportional CS headcount increase.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Trigger fires but users don't complete the module (low response rate)**
Solution: The module format doesn't match where the user is in their workflow. If they triggered in-product, serve in-app content — not email. Check trigger channel alignment. Also audit module length: if completion rates drop after 90 seconds, shorten immediately. Run a "Why didn't you complete this?" micro-survey to the non-completers.

**Problem: Educated users still churn at similar rates to non-educated users**
Solution: You may be measuring the wrong "educated" definition. Module completion alone isn't education — behavioral change is. Redefine "educated" as: completed module AND completed the featured action (CTA) within 48 hours. Cohort analysis on this stricter definition will likely show a stronger churn delta. Also audit whether your content is teaching the RIGHT features — only features that drive actual customer outcomes (not just engagement) will move the churn needle.

**Problem: CS team doesn't trust the AI recommendations and ignores escalation triggers**
Solution: Build CSM trust through early wins. Start with 3 triggers that are obviously high-signal (e.g., "Account had zero logins in 14 days" is unambiguous). Track and report the "trigger-to-outcome" rate in your team review. When CSMs see that acting on triggers correlates with account saves, adoption increases. Also give CSMs the ability to snooze or dismiss triggers with a reason — this feedback loop improves trigger quality over time.
