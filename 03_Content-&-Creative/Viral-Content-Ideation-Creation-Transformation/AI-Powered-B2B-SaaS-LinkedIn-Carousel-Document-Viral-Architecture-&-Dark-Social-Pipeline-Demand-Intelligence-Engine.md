## Overview

Transforms any B2B SaaS insight, data point, or piece of content into a LinkedIn carousel/document post engineered for maximum virality, dark social distribution, and pipeline attribution. Use this when you need high-reach organic LinkedIn content that generates warm pipeline without paid amplification — specifically the document format (PDF slides) that consistently outperforms all other LinkedIn post types for B2B audiences because the swipe-engagement event is heavily weighted by the algorithm.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The carousel gets 15,000+ impressions but almost no DM or CTA conversions — the reach is there but zero pipeline.**
Solution: Two likely causes. (1) CTA commitment is too high for cold-audience trust level — "Book a 30-minute demo" from organic content almost never works; replace with zero-friction offers like "DM me 'TEMPLATE' and I'll send it instantly, no form" or "Comment 'YES' and I'll reply with the link." (2) The offer isn't specific enough — "a useful guide" performs 60-70% worse than "the exact 47-question contract audit checklist used by 3 Fortune 500 procurement teams." Audit Slide 1: if the hook doesn't make your ICP feel the document is specifically for them within 3 seconds, they won't read to the CTA regardless of content quality. A/B test two different cover slides on the same carousel content by posting the same document twice 3 weeks apart with different captions and different Slide 1 imagery.

**Problem: Post performs well on Day 1 (1,000+ likes) but drops to zero engagement by Day 3 — no long-tail traction or saves.**
Solution: Low save rate (under 1%) means the content feels timely but not reusable. Fix: add a "you'll want to reference this later" element — a checklist, a benchmark table, or a named framework diagram they'll reuse in their own work. Carousels with reusable assets get 3-4x more saves than narrative-only posts. Also: your amplification window is too narrow. Reply to every comment within 6 hours for the first 3 days (not just Day 1). Add a pinned first comment at the 24-hour mark with one additional data point not in the carousel. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards sustained engagement signals — a post getting comments on Day 4 looks very different to the algorithm than one that peaked on Day 0.

**Problem: Sales team either won't engage at all, or writes generic "Great post, Sarah! 🔥" comments that add no algorithmic or audience value.**
Solution: Three structural fixes: (1) Give them 3 fully-written, personalized comment options they can copy-paste and lightly adapt — removing all friction is the only thing that produces reliable compliance; (2) Show them the business case with specific past numbers ("Sarah's last carousel drove 47 DMs from VP Procurement leads — 3 converted to demos — your comment gets seen by your 800 followers who don't follow Sarah yet"); (3) Make LinkedIn amplification a scheduled, recurring team habit rather than an ad-hoc Slack ping — add a standing 15-minute "amplification window" to the team calendar for every carousel publish day. Track which reps participate and report carousel-influenced deals quarterly to the sales leadership team.
