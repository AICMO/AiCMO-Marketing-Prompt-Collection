## Overview
Designs and generates a complete interactive ROI calculator program — from financial model architecture and input variable design to AI-generated personalized ROI summaries, lead capture mechanics, and downstream pipeline sequences. Use this when you need a website or landing page asset that gives prospects a credible, personalized "what will this cost me vs. what will I gain?" answer in under 3 minutes, captures their financial context as first-party qualification data, and routes high-intent completions directly into sales pipeline.

### MODULE 1: ROI CALCULATOR STRATEGY & POSITIONING

**Calculator Concept & Buyer Promise**
Write the headline, subheadline, and 2-sentence value proposition for the calculator landing page. Answer the prospect's question: "Why should I spend 90 seconds filling this out — what will I get?" The answer must be specific ("See your estimated ROI in 90 seconds") not generic ("Discover the value of [Product]").

**Value Driver Hierarchy**
Identify and rank your 3-6 value drivers by (a) magnitude of financial impact for the typical buyer and (b) believability — the most impactful claims must also be the most defensible. For each value driver:
- Driver name (buyer's language, not product feature language)
- Financial impact mechanism: How exactly does this driver reduce cost or increase revenue?
- Quantification approach: What inputs are needed to calculate this driver's impact?
- Benchmark anchor: What customer data or industry research makes this number credible?
- Believability risk: What would make a skeptical CFO reject this calculation, and how do you preempt it?

**Calculator Positioning vs. Competitive Alternatives**
Design two versions of the calculator:
1. **Status quo version:** Shows the cost of the current approach (spreadsheets, manual processes, incumbent tool) vs. your product
2. **Competitive displacement version:** Shows the delta in value between a named competitor's approach and yours

For the competitive version, identify 3 calculation inputs where your product systematically outperforms the competitor, and design the calculator to surface those differences without appearing biased.

### MODULE 2: INPUT ARCHITECTURE (Complete Input Set)

Design 6-12 calculator inputs organized into 2-3 sections. The goal: collect enough data to generate a credible personalized output while keeping completion time under 3 minutes.

**Design Principles to Apply:**
- Every input must map to at least one output calculation
- Default values should represent the average prospect (not best-case or worst-case)
- Sliders are better than blank fields — they anchor the prospect at a reasonable starting point and reduce form fatigue
- Never ask for data prospects don't know without looking it up (e.g., "exact headcount in your SDR team" is OK; "exact annual salary burden rate" is too hard)
- Pre-fill what you can from firmographic data (company size from Clearbit enrichment, for example)

For each input:

**INPUT [N]: [Input Label]**
- Input type: [Slider / Dropdown / Numeric field / Toggle / Pre-populated]
- Label (buyer-facing): [The question or label exactly as it appears in the calculator — write it like a conversation, not a form]
- Default value: [What you pre-populate for an "average" prospect in your ICP]
- Range: [Min and max values — set the slider range to anchor around realistic numbers]
- Tooltip copy: [The help text that appears on hover — explain why you're asking and how to estimate if they don't know exactly]
- Calculation it feeds: [Which ROI output(s) this input contributes to]
- Qualification signal: [What this answer tells your sales team about the prospect's pain intensity, company stage, or budget]
- Enrichment potential: [Can this field be pre-populated from firmographic data sources like Clearbit, ZoomInfo, LinkedIn?]

**Input Sections:**
1. **Your Business Context** (2-3 inputs) — Company/team size inputs that calibrate the scale of the ROI. Keep this section under 60 seconds. Use dropdowns and sliders, not blank fields.
2. **Your Current State** (2-4 inputs) — How much time, money, or pipeline they're currently losing to the problem you solve. This is the "cost of inaction" section — where the emotional weight of the calculator lives.
3. **Your Goals** (2-3 inputs) — What improvement they're hoping to achieve. This section sets up the "what you could gain" side of the ROI equation and reveals sales intent level.

### MODULE 3: ROI FINANCIAL MODEL

**Core Calculation Architecture**

Design the complete financial model that powers the calculator. For each value driver, provide:

**VALUE DRIVER [N]: [Driver Name]**

Annual Cost of Status Quo:
- Formula: [Write the exact formula using input variable names — e.g., (Weekly Hours Lost × Team Size × 52 × Hourly Fully-Loaded Rate) = Annual Cost of [Problem]]
- Input variables used: [List which Module 2 inputs feed this formula]
- Default output at median inputs: [What the typical prospect sees — e.g., "$127,000 in annual productivity loss"]
- Credibility anchor: [The customer data or industry benchmark that validates this calculation methodology — cite source if possible]

Expected Gain with [Product]:
- Formula: [Write the formula for the improvement your product delivers — e.g., (Status Quo Cost × Improvement Rate) = Annual Savings]
- Improvement rate assumption: [The % improvement your product delivers — use conservative customer median, not best-case]
- Assumption transparency copy: [The sentence that appears next to this number explaining how you derived it — e.g., "Based on the median improvement reported by 200+ customers in your segment"]

**Aggregate ROI Summary Calculations:**

Total Annual Value:
- Formula: Sum of all value driver annual gains

Net Annual ROI (after product cost):
- Formula: (Total Annual Value − Annual Product Cost) / Annual Product Cost × 100
- Display as: Dollar amount + percentage (e.g., "$187K net benefit — 680% ROI")

Payback Period:
- Formula: Annual Product Cost / (Total Annual Value / 12) = Months to Break Even
- Display language: "You recover your full investment in [X] months"

3-Year Total Value:
- Formula: (Total Annual Value × 3) − (Annual Product Cost × 3)
- Display as: Dollar amount with copy: "Over 3 years, [Product] generates [X] in net value"

**Model Integrity Safeguards:**
- Cap logic: Define maximum ROI percentages your model will display (e.g., never show >2,000% ROI — it destroys credibility). Write the cap logic and the copy that appears when a prospect inputs extreme values.
- Conservative framing: Write the standard disclaimer copy that appears below all outputs (e.g., "Based on median outcomes reported by customers with similar profiles. Individual results vary.")
- Footnote documentation: For each value driver, write a footnote-style methodology note (appears in an expandable "How we calculated this" section) that documents assumptions, data sources, and limitations.

## Success Metrics

- **Calculator completion rate:** Target 55-70% of visitors who start the calculator complete it (below 40% = inputs are too complex or time-consuming; above 75% = you may be underselling the value of completion)
- **Gate conversion rate:** Target 40-55% of calculators completed → email captured (for post-gate model); 20-35% of visitors → email captured (for pre-gate model on cold traffic)
- **Lead-to-MQL rate:** ROI calculator completions should MQL at 60-75% — significantly higher than whitepaper downloads (15-25%) because the intent signal is in the inputs
- **AE usage in discovery:** Track what % of AEs pull up the prospect's calculator results before their first call. Target >80% adoption within 60 days of launch
- **Deal velocity impact:** Measure time-to-demo and time-to-close for deals where the prospect completed the ROI calculator vs. didn't. Expect 20-35% faster deal cycles in calculator cohort
- **Model accuracy:** Quarterly, compare calculator-estimated ROI at deal close vs. actual ROI achieved at 6-month customer review. If systematic gap > 25%, recalibrate model assumptions
- **CFO/executive forward rate:** Track how often the PDF report is opened by a second email address (indicating prospect forwarded it to their boss or CFO) — target 15-25% of completions

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Completion rate is below 40% — prospects start the calculator but don't finish it.
**Solution:** Run a drop-off analysis in your calculator tool to identify exactly which input causes abandonment. Most common culprits: (1) Inputs ask for data prospects don't have at their fingertips — rewrite to ask for ranges or estimates, not exact figures; replace "What is your annual marketing budget?" with a dropdown showing ranges; (2) Calculator feels too long — if completion time exceeds 3 minutes, cut inputs ruthlessly; each input must justify its existence by materially improving output accuracy; (3) The value proposition is unclear upfront — add a "What you'll get in 90 seconds" section above the calculator with a screenshot of the output, showing prospects exactly what they're working toward.

**Problem:** AEs don't trust the calculator outputs and aren't referencing them in sales calls.
**Solution:** This is a calibration problem, not a technology problem. (1) Run a calibration session with 3-5 customer success managers: have them complete the calculator using real data from closed-won accounts, then compare calculator output to actual ROI those customers achieved. If the gap is >25% systematically, adjust your improvement rate assumptions.
