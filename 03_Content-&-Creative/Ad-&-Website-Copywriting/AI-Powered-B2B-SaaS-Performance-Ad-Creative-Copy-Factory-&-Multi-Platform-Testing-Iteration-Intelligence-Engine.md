## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom property on the Campaign object for "Messaging Angle" and tag each UTM source/medium/campaign combination with the angle being tested. Build a report comparing deal close rates by messaging angle to understand which copy creates not just clicks but actual revenue.
- **Salesforce + Pardot:** Use the UTM parameters from this system to populate a custom Opportunity field "First Touch Copy Angle" — then run a win/loss report by angle quarterly to feed your next creative sprint.
- **Google Ads Editor:** Export the full headline and description matrix from Output 3 directly into the Google Ads Editor bulk upload template — this saves 2-3 hours of manual ad creation per campaign.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Use the "Creative Rotation" setting on "Optimize" (not "Rotate Evenly") only AFTER you've manually determined winners — LinkedIn's algorithm tends to over-optimize early and can kill variants before they reach statistical significance.
- **Supermetrics / Funnel.io:** Build a cross-platform creative performance dashboard that pulls impression, click, conversion, and CPL data by ad creative ID into a Google Sheet. Run the iteration protocol against this dashboard every 14 days.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Maintain a "Creative Intelligence Log" as a running database that stores: angle name, platform, launch date, peak CTR, peak CR, CPL at peak, and the copy variant that drove it. Reference this before every new campaign brief to avoid reinventing winning angles.
- **Zapier / Make:** Build an automation that triggers a Slack notification to the paid media team when any ad variant's CTR drops more than 25% week-over-week — this is the creative fatigue early warning system.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** All 5 messaging angles underperform benchmarks in Week 1, making it impossible to identify a winner.
**Solution:** The issue is almost always audience quality, not copy quality. Before iterating copy, audit the audience targeting: verify ICP job titles are exactly right (LinkedIn's job title targeting is notoriously imprecise — use both exact titles AND job function + seniority combinations), check company size filters, and confirm your bid strategy isn't limiting reach. Run a 48-hour audience diagnostic before changing a single word of copy.

**Problem:** LinkedIn copy performs well (high CTR) but demo requests from LinkedIn are low-quality and rarely convert to pipeline.
**Solution:** The copy is attracting the wrong job titles or too-junior buyers. Add explicit ICP qualification to the copy itself — naming the exact seniority level ("For VP-and-above RevOps leaders at SaaS companies over $10M ARR") will lower CTR but dramatically improve lead quality. Also audit your landing page: LinkedIn traffic responds to social proof differently than Google traffic — ensure your landing page leads with peer proof (recognizable logos, job-title-specific testimonials) rather than generic value props.

**Problem:** Creative fatigue hits faster than expected — winning variants burn out in 10-14 days instead of 6 weeks.
**Solution:** This indicates your total addressable audience is too small for the budget level. Either reduce daily budget to extend reach curve, expand audience by loosening targeting constraints (test "Revenue Operations" as a job function vs. only specific titles), or rotate the visual asset first (same copy, new image/video) before retiring copy entirely. Visual refresh alone typically extends creative lifespan by 2-3 weeks without requiring new copy production.
