*Universal Negative Prompts:* [--no stock footage aesthetic, no cheesy business handshake, no people pointing at whiteboards, no stock photo lighting, no cartoon or animation, no watermarks, no text in AI-generated frame (text added in post), no competitor branding, no green screen artifacts, no temporal flickering, no morphing faces]

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** AI-generated video clips have temporal flickering, morphing faces, or artifact distortion — unusable for professional B2B content.
**Solution:** This is the most common AI video generation failure mode in 2026 and requires a multi-layered fix: (1) Add `--no temporal flickering, no morphing, no distortion` to all prompts explicitly — most tools respond to these negative prompts. (2) For face-containing scenes, switch from text-to-video to image-to-video: generate a high-quality still face using Midjourney or DALL-E first, then use Runway ML's image-to-video mode for more controlled, stable motion. (3) Reduce camera motion complexity — the slower and simpler the camera move, the fewer temporal artifacts. Eliminate "dramatic push-in" in favor of "slow micro-drift" for face-containing scenes. (4) If artifacts persist after 3 regenerations, replace AI-generated face scenes with product UI closeups or abstract data visualization scenes — these generate cleanly and often perform as well or better in B2B video conversion testing.

**Problem:** The AI-generated video assets look visually inconsistent across formats — the hero video, LinkedIn square, and pre-roll all look like they came from different brands.
**Solution:** The master brand modifier string from Section 5 must be applied with 100% consistency — check that it was appended to every single prompt submitted. The most common cause of visual inconsistency is that modifier strings were omitted from "quick" short-form scene generations done outside the system. Additionally, run a post-generation color grade in CapCut or DaVinci Resolve using the same LUT (create a custom LUT based on your brand color modifier description) applied to all clips before assembly. This single step aligns color grade across all generated clips regardless of tool or session.

**Problem:** LinkedIn paid video is generating views but not driving demo requests — video completion rate is good but CTR on CTA is below 0.5%.
**Solution:** The most common cause is a misalignment between the video's closing CTA and the friction in the destination experience. Check three things: (1) Is the CTA in the video specific ("Book a 30-Minute Demo" performs 2x better than "Learn More" for B2B demo-stage audiences)? (2) Does the landing page load in under 2 seconds on mobile — LinkedIn video viewers who click often do so immediately on mobile while the emotion from the video is fresh? (3) Is the demo booking form under 3 fields? Every additional field after Name/Email/Company reduces conversion by 15-20% for video-sourced traffic. If all three are correct, try testing a "content offer" CTA as a secondary conversion (download the benchmark report) to capture demand from viewers who aren't ready to book a demo — this often uncovers 3-5x more conversions hiding in the audience.
