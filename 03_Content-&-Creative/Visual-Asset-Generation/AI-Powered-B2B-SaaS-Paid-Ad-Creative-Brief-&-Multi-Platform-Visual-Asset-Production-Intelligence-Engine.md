### 5. YOUTUBE PRE-ROLL AD CREATIVE PACKAGE (Bonus: if budget includes video)

YouTube pre-roll (skippable after 5 seconds) demands a distinct creative logic: the first 5 seconds must compel a "skip-resistant" reaction, and the remaining 25-55 seconds must earn attention through genuine value delivery.

**15-Second Non-Skippable Script:**
- Second 0-3: Hook (the pattern interrupt — state the problem in the buyer's exact language or show a recognizable frustrating moment)
- Second 3-10: Agitate (why the status quo fails — make the pain visceral, not abstract)
- Second 10-13: Resolution preview (the solution exists — don't explain it, tease it)
- Second 13-15: CTA (specific, urgent, friction-free)

**30-Second Skippable Script:**
- Second 0-5: Skip-resistant hook (ask a question, make a bold claim, or show something visually unexpected — the viewer is 1 click from skipping)
- Second 5-20: Story arc (introduce the character/company, establish the stakes, demonstrate the transformation — use the "Pixar story spine": "Every day... Until one day... Because of that... Until finally...")
- Second 20-28: Proof moment (customer quote, metric, before/after visual)
- Second 28-30: CTA with specific URL or action

**Visual Direction Spec:**
- Scene-by-scene shot list with AI image/video generation prompts for each key frame
- B-roll suggestions: 3-5 stock footage search terms that align with the concept
- Text overlay timing: When and where to use on-screen text to reinforce key messages for "sound-off" viewers (40% of YouTube is watched muted)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** AI-generated images look generic and don't reflect the brand.
**Solution:** The most common cause is an incomplete brand modifier string (Section 7). Add specific negative prompts that exclude the aesthetic failures ("--no stock photo clichés, no handshakes, no people pointing at whiteboards, no warm earth tones"). Also, add your brand's primary hex color as a natural language description ("dominated by deep navy blue, equivalent to midnight corporate blue") since most AI image generators don't accept hex codes directly. Run 4-6 variations of each prompt and select the best rather than accepting the first output.

**Problem:** LinkedIn CTR is below 0.3% across all creative variants after 2 weeks.
**Solution:** The issue is usually one of three things: (1) Audience mismatch — the creative is right but the targeting is too broad or too narrow; verify your LinkedIn audience size is between 50K-300K for B2B campaigns. (2) Creative is too product-focused — LinkedIn audiences respond better to problem-first creative that validates their experience before introducing the solution; rewrite introductory text to lead with a pain statement. (3) Visual stopping power is insufficient — test a "data shock" creative concept that leads with a specific, surprising statistic in large text on a simple background rather than a photographic scene.

**Problem:** The creative testing matrix is generating inconclusive results — no clear winner.
**Solution:** You likely have insufficient sample sizes or too many variables changing simultaneously. Enforce the single-variable rule ruthlessly — if you changed both the image and the headline between variants, you cannot attribute performance differences to either. Pause all but two variants and wait for the minimum impression thresholds from the testing matrix to be reached. For LinkedIn specifically, do not declare a winner until each variant has at least 5,000 impressions AND 20+ clicks — LinkedIn's audience size makes underpowered tests the #1 cause of false signals.
