## Overview
Generates a complete, production-ready visual asset package for B2B SaaS trade shows, conferences, and sponsored events — from booth design briefs and digital display scripts to pre/live/post-event social graphics, swag concepts, and speaker visual templates. Use this whenever a B2B SaaS company is preparing for a conference or trade show and needs all creative assets briefed, scoped, and production-ready without hiring an events agency.

## Integration Tips

- **Canva Pro:** Use the "Brand Kit" feature to lock hex codes and fonts before briefing designers. Save all event templates as a branded "Events Kit" template set reusable across future conferences. Use Canva's "resize" feature to automatically adapt booth copy to social media dimensions.
- **Figma:** Create an event component library from your booth design elements — frames, color chips, headline treatments — so any designer can remix assets for future events in under an hour.
- **AI Image Tools (Midjourney/DALL-E/Firefly):** Generate 5-10 background image variations, then use Canva's "background remover" and overlay tools to composite your copy onto selected images. Always generate at 4:1 ratio for banner graphics (e.g., 4000x1000px) to account for print resolution requirements.
- **HubSpot/Salesforce:** Set up a pre-event meeting booking workflow triggered by LinkedIn ad clicks pointing to a Calendly or HubSpot meetings link. Tag all event leads with the event name and booth interaction type for post-event nurture segmentation.
- **Typeform/Jotform:** Build your in-booth risk assessment as a tablet-based Typeform with conditional logic. Connect to HubSpot via Zapier to auto-enrich leads with risk score data and trigger personalized follow-up sequences based on score tier.
- **Slack:** Create an internal event-day Slack channel for real-time reporting. Post hourly: lead count, most common ICP conversation topic, and any competitor intelligence from the floor. This feeds post-event content creation.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The booth headline feels generic and sounds like every other SaaS company at the event.
**Solution:** Run this test — replace your company name with a direct competitor in the headline. If it still works, the headline isn't specific enough. Good event headlines contain either (a) a specific number, (b) a named pain that only your ICP experiences, or (c) a provocative claim that contradicts industry conventional wisdom. Return to the messaging architecture section and use the "contrarian angle" — what does every competitor say? Say the opposite with evidence.

**Problem:** AI image generation prompts are producing visuals that don't match brand identity or look too generic.
**Solution:** Add negative prompts explicitly to every AI image prompt (e.g., "–no stock photography, no diverse-handshake imagery, no generic office scenes, no blue technology hexagons"). Also specify the artist or aesthetic reference: "in the visual style of The New York Times data journalism graphics" or "reminiscent of Apple's clean product photography" gives AI tools a cultural reference that overrides generic training data.

**Problem:** Pre-event social content is underperforming — low engagement before the event starts.
**Solution:** Event announcement posts chronically underperform because they're broadcasting, not engaging. Switch from announcement format ("We'll be at [Event]!") to opinion/question format. Example: instead of "We're excited to attend ManufacturingWorld 2026," post "Hot take: 80% of supply chain compliance risk sits in Tier 2 and Tier 3 suppliers that most manufacturers have never audited. What's your take?" — then add the event tag in a comment. This drives comments first, then announces your presence naturally in the thread.
