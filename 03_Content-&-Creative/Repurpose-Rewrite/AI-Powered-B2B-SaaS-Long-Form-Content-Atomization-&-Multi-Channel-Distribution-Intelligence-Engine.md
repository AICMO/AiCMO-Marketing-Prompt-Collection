Use this when your team produces high-quality cornerstone content but lacks bandwidth to maximize its reach, or when you need to increase content output velocity without increasing headcount.

### Atomization Framework

Execute the following atomization architecture:

**PHASE 1 — ASSET AUDIT & INSIGHT EXTRACTION (run first)**

1. Extract the 7–10 most statistically surprising, counterintuitive, or opinion-challenging insights from the source asset
2. Identify the 3–5 "quotable moments" — statements that work as standalone social proof or thought leadership
3. Map each insight to a buyer stage: awareness (educates on the problem), consideration (frames the solution category), decision (validates the vendor)
4. Flag the 2–3 insights with highest virality potential (data points, contrarian takes, or emotional resonance)
5. Identify any customer success stories or proof embedded in the source that can be extracted as mini case studies

**PHASE 2 — DERIVATIVE ASSET INVENTORY**

Generate a complete inventory table with these columns: Asset Name | Format | Channel | Buyer Stage | Word/Character Count | Estimated Effort (hours) | Priority Tier (1/2/3)

- 2× document carousel posts (10–12 slides each) — one TOFU educational, one MOFU tactical

*Email & Newsletter (4–6 pieces):*

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The source asset is thin — only 10–15 minutes of content or a 4-page PDF.**
*Fix:* Run the Insight Extraction phase first and identify if you have at least 5 distinct insights. If yes, reduce target derivative count to 12–15 pieces. Supplement with "what this means for [ICP]" editorial framing to add depth. Do NOT pad content — fewer high-quality pieces outperform many thin ones.

**Problem: The AI-generated LinkedIn posts sound generic — lots of "In today's landscape..." openers.**
*Fix:* Add a voice calibration prompt before the drafting phase: paste 3 examples of the executive's highest-performing LinkedIn posts and instruct the AI to match cadence, vocabulary, and rhetorical style. Specify "start with a data point, a counterintuitive claim, or a specific story — never a generic scene-setter."

**Problem: The 90-day calendar is too aggressive for the team's publishing capacity.**
*Fix:* Apply the 60% rule — take the Tier 1 and Tier 2 assets only (typically 15 pieces) and stretch the calendar to 90 days at 1–2 pieces per week. Identify which 5 pieces have the highest pipeline connection and prioritize those as non-negotiable. Defer Tier 3 assets to the following quarter or reassign to an external contractor with the outlines already written.
