## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The customer won't provide a quote or go on the record by name — we only have anonymous metrics**
Solution: Anonymous customer stories still convert if you lead with the role, company size, and industry rather than the name. "A 450-person property management firm in the Southeast" is enough specificity to trigger recognition in a peer. Use the metrics and the before/after narrative verbatim — the numbers do the convincing, the name is a bonus. For the cold email specifically, anonymized specificity ("a Director of Finance at a firm similar to yours") can actually outperform named references because it forces the prospect to self-identify with the scenario. Push the customer for a quote you can use with their first name and title only (no company name), or offer to let them review the final version — most champions will approve attribution once they see how well the story is framed.

**Problem: The customer's results are real but modest — nothing dramatic enough to lead a campaign**
Solution: Reframe around the ratio, not the absolute number. "$18K saved annually" feels small; "89% reduction in billing errors that had been costing the team 6 hours a week" feels significant. If the financial outcome is genuinely small, lead with time savings or risk reduction — these resonate differently by buyer persona. CFOs care about financial outcomes; VP Operations care about team hours; IT leaders care about integration speed and reliability. Run three different campaign versions, each leading with the metric most relevant to that buyer — you may find one persona responds at 3x the rate of the others. Also: fast time-to-value (e.g., "ROI realized in week 4") is often more compelling than large absolute numbers for mid-market buyers with limited patience for long payback periods.

**Problem: Sales team isn't using the enablement assets — the case study is sitting in a shared drive no one opens**
Solution: The adoption problem is almost always a discoverability and format problem, not a content quality problem. Three fixes: (1) Load the discovery opener (Module 4C) into Outreach/Salesloft as a named template with the customer company name in the template title — reps will find it when searching for that account type. (2) Brief the team in the weekly sales meeting with a 5-minute "here's how to use this" walkthrough — live demonstration beats async documentation every time. (3) Ask your CRO to send the pipeline acceleration email (Module 3B) personally to 3-5 stalled deals — when reps see it work once, adoption jumps immediately.
