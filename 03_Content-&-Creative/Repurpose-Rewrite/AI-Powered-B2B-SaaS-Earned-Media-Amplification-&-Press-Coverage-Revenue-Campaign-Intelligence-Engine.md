**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, earned-media, pr, content-amplification, press-coverage, demand-generation, brand-authority, repurpose, pipeline-acceleration

## Overview

An autonomous AI system for transforming earned media placements — press mentions, analyst inclusions, industry award wins, podcast guest appearances, speaking engagements, and customer story features in third-party publications — into fully orchestrated 90-day multi-channel amplification campaigns that drive qualified pipeline, accelerate open deals, and compound brand authority long after the original publish date. Most B2B SaaS companies get great press coverage and extract less than 5% of its commercial value. This engine captures the other 95%.

## MISSION: PHASE 1 — EARNED MEDIA ASSET DISSECTION

### 1A. Credibility Claim Extraction

Before producing any content, extract and catalog every commercial-grade claim from the coverage:

**Tier 1 Claims (use in primary assets — highest credibility impact):**
- Direct analyst/journalist quotes about your company (exact language)
- Any rankings, placements, or scoring that position you above or equal to competitors
- Specific capabilities or outcomes highlighted by the third party
- Customer stories or data referenced in the coverage

**Tier 2 Claims (use in supporting assets — strong secondary proof):**
- Category trends the coverage places you within (e.g., "Mosaic is part of a wave of AI-native FP&A platforms replacing legacy tools")
- Publication/platform authority credentials (reach, audience, brand recognition)
- Quotes from customers or experts within the coverage
- Any recognition of company growth, team, or trajectory

**Tier 3 Claims (use in long-tail content — contextual reinforcement):**
- Implicit endorsements (being covered at all by this outlet)
- Context about the market the coverage validates (your ICP's problems are real and urgent)
- Competitor weaknesses or gaps the coverage implies (if you ranked above them or were noted for areas where they weren't)

**Claim Amplifiability Score:**
For each Tier 1 claim, rate:
- Buyer relevance: how much does this claim directly address what your ICP cares about? (1-10)
- Competitive differentiation: does this claim distinguish you from your top 3 competitors? (1-10)
- Emotional resonance: does this make your buyer feel relieved, excited, or validated? (1-10)
- Use across funnel: which funnel stages does this claim work best at? (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU)

Rank all Tier 1 claims by combined score. The top 3 become your amplification campaign's core proof pillars.

### 1B. Audience Intent Mapping

Map the earned media to your buyer journey:

| Buyer Stage | What They're Thinking | Which Claim Addresses It | Which Channel Reaches Them Here |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware (TOFU) | "Do I even have this problem?" | [Coverage validates the category urgency] | LinkedIn organic, LinkedIn ads, podcast sponsorships |
| Problem-Aware (MOFU) | "I know I need to fix this, but who do I trust?" | [Analyst/press validation builds category trust] | Email nurture, targeted LinkedIn ads, retargeting |
| Solution-Aware (MOFU) | "Which vendors are credible enough to evaluate?" | [Inclusion in analyst report / press = shortlist-worthy] | Sales outreach sequences, AE follow-ups, SDR outbound |
| Vendor Evaluation (BOFU) | "Can I trust this specific company?" | [Third-party validation = safe choice signal] | Sales deck, deal room, procurement stage emails |
| Decision/Procurement | "How do I justify this internally?" | [External proof = ammunition for CFO/board approval] | Business case templates, ROI calculator, executive briefings |

## MISSION: PHASE 2 — MULTI-CHANNEL AMPLIFICATION ASSET PRODUCTION

### 2A. Owned Social Media Assets

**LinkedIn Company Page — Announcement Post:**

Write one master announcement post for the company LinkedIn page:

- Body: context, the specific recognition, what it means for customers, and a subtle credibility flex

- Best posting time for B2B SaaS audiences

**Executive/Founder LinkedIn Posts (3 posts, 3 different angles):**

*Post 1 — The Gratitude + Credibility Angle:*
Written from the CEO or CMO voice. Personal, not corporate. References the journey that led to this recognition. Ends with insight about what this means for the category, not just the company. Target: 200-300 words.

*Post 2 — The Customer Obsession Angle:*
Written from a founder or VP CS voice. Pivots from the recognition to the customers who made it possible. Includes a specific customer outcome (real or illustrative). Subtly ties the coverage to a product capability customers love. Target: 150-200 words.

*Post 3 — The Category/Market Trend Angle:*
Written from a CMO or CPO voice. Uses the coverage as proof of a broader market shift. Positions the company as ahead of the curve, not just lucky. Targets ICP buyers who are thinking "maybe I should be evaluating this category." Target: 200-250 words.

For each post:
- Provide exact copy, ready to paste
- Recommend whether to include a visual (yes/no + brief)
- Suggest 3 employees or partners to tag for amplification
- Include comment-starting question to seed the first comment

## MISSION: PHASE 3 — PIPELINE ACCELERATION SEQUENCES

### 3A. Open Opportunity Sequences

For the current open CRM pipeline, produce three stage-specific email templates from the AE:

**Template A — Discovery Stage Prospects (aware, not yet committed to evaluate):**
- From: AE (first name only)
- Subject: 4-6 words, not "I wanted to share..." — actually surprising
- Body (4-5 sentences max): references the coverage, connects it to a specific pain they mentioned in discovery, offers a relevant next step (not generic demo — specific to their expressed problem)
- Signature line: includes coverage badge as link

**Template B — Evaluation Stage Prospects (in active evaluation, comparing vendors):**
- From: AE, CC Sales Director (the recognition warrants senior attention)
- Subject: frames the coverage as relevant to their evaluation criteria
- Body (6-8 sentences): acknowledgment of evaluation, third-party validation as relevant evidence, specific capability referenced in coverage that matches their stated requirements, remove one friction point from their evaluation process (offer a reference call, offer a POC scope, offer a side-by-side comparison doc)
- P.S. line: invite executive-to-executive conversation if appropriate

**Template C — Stuck/Cold Pipeline (deals that have gone quiet for 3+ weeks):**
- From: AE or Sales Director (depending on deal size)
- Subject: pattern interrupt — the coverage becomes a legitimate reason to re-engage without being needy
- Body (3-4 sentences max): not "just checking in" — the news is the reason for contact. One proof claim. One low-friction re-engagement invitation.
- No CTA buttons. Just a reply-to-this-email ask.

### 3B. SDR Outbound Templates

**Cold LinkedIn InMail (for ICP accounts not yet in pipeline):**
- Subject line: 6 words or less (LinkedIn InMail has limited preview)
- Body: 4-6 sentences. Lead with coverage credibility. Connect to their specific role's job-to-be-done. One question to open dialogue (not "Are you interested in a demo?")
- Connection request note (if InMail budget is limited): 300 character version of the same message

**Cold Email with Coverage as Hook:**
- Subject: test two approaches: (1) name-drop the publication; (2) lead with the outcome they care about
- Preview text: supports subject without repeating it
- Body: 5-7 sentences. Coverage as social proof. ICP-specific relevance. Problem-oriented framing. Soft CTA.
- P.S.: one additional claim from the coverage, casually referenced
- Follow-up #2 (day 5): angle shift — focus on a specific customer outcome, reference coverage briefly
- Follow-up #3 (day 12): break-up format, different voice, asks if timing is wrong not if they're interested

## CONSTRAINTS AND QUALITY STANDARDS

- Never misrepresent the coverage. If an analyst report had caveats, the content must be accurate. Overstating analyst or press recognition is the fastest way to destroy credibility with sophisticated buyers who will read the original.
- Every asset must pass the "Would a cynical CMO from a competitor approve this if they saw it?" test. If it sounds like puffery, it won't work.
- Do not use the coverage to bash competitors unless the coverage itself made direct comparisons. If it did, use their exact language — never editorialize.
- The sales templates must sound like they were written by a human AE who actually read the coverage and cares about the prospect — not a marketing team blast. Personalization cues are mandatory.
- Every piece of content should include a clear "so what" for the ICP buyer. The recognition is only interesting if it proves something the buyer cares about. Always bridge from the coverage to their job-to-be-done.

## Success Metrics

- **Announcement Email Open Rate**: Target 35-45% open rate on prospect list (vs. typical 22-28% for cold campaigns). The publication brand in the subject line is your lift driver — measure it by A/B testing subject lines that include vs. omit the publication name.
- **Pipeline Velocity Acceleration**: Track time-to-next-stage for evaluation-stage deals that received the AE follow-up email vs. those that did not. Target: 15-25% faster stage advancement for deals where coverage was used.
- **SDR Reply Rate Lift**: Cold outbound sequences using the coverage hook should achieve 8-15% reply rate vs. 3-5% baseline for similar sequences without the coverage hook. A/B test in Outreach or Salesloft across a minimum of 200 sends before drawing conclusions.
- **Demo Request Uplift**: Track demo requests from LinkedIn ad campaigns featuring the coverage vs. standard product ads in the same period. Coverage-based ads typically achieve 20-40% lower CPL in B2B SaaS because authority reduces purchase risk.
- **Influenced Pipeline**: Tag all opportunities created or re-engaged within 30 days of the amplification campaign launch with "Earned Media Q2 2026" in your CRM. Review influenced pipeline at 60 and 90 days. Target: 10-20% of new pipeline that quarter influenced by earned media amplification.
- **Coverage Shelf Life**: Measure how long the Forrester/press mention continues to generate inbound inquiries (track UTM links in the LinkedIn profile, email signatures, and website trust bar). High-authority publications often drive passive inbound for 6-18 months.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Create a campaign object called "Earned Media — [Publication Name] — [Quarter]." Tag all contacts who receive any amplification asset (email, sales outreach, LinkedIn ad click) to this campaign. Report on influenced pipeline from this campaign at 30/60/90 days. This turns "PR value" into a CFO-friendly attribution story.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Upload your ICP audience as a Matched Audience (CRM contacts, account list, or website visitors). Run the three ad variations against this warm audience — the CPL will be dramatically lower than cold prospecting because you're putting third-party proof in front of people who already know your brand.
- **Outreach / Salesloft**: Build a cadence specifically for "Earned Media — Reactivation" that SDRs can enroll dormant leads into when new coverage drops. The coverage gives SDRs a legitimate, non-needy reason to re-engage accounts that went cold.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Search your call library for prospects who have mentioned the publication or award you just received — or who mentioned the competitors that appear in the same coverage. Use those recordings as coaching material for your team on how to position the coverage in live conversations.
- **Notion or Confluence**: Build an "Earned Media Vault" that documents every coverage placement, extracted claims, and the amplification assets created. When a new AE joins, this becomes instant credibility training. When a prospect asks "who else uses you?" your SDRs have a curated list of proof at their fingertips.
- **Zapier Automation**: Set up a Zap that fires when you're tagged in a media mention (via Mention.com or Google Alerts). It can auto-draft a Slack notification to the sales team, create a task for the content manager to initiate the amplification playbook, and create a CRM campaign object — reducing the lag between when you get coverage and when your pipeline team knows about it.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The coverage is positive but generic — doesn't include specific claims that differentiate from competitors.**
Solution: Generic coverage is still useful for authority but weak for differentiation. Extract the publication's authority (TechCrunch, Forbes, Forrester) as the proof point itself, not the specific claims. Reframe the asset library to focus on "recognized by [publication] as part of the [market trend]" rather than specific capability claims. Simultaneously, pitch the journalist or analyst for a follow-up interview where you can provide the specific differentiation narrative on record.

**Problem: My competitors were also named in the same analyst report or press piece — how do I amplify without promoting their coverage too?**
Solution: Never link to the full coverage in outbound-facing assets if competitors are featured. Instead, excerpt and deep-link to the specific section about your company. In your ads and emails, reference "the [Publication] report" but link to a landing page on your site that presents the relevant excerpt and your own commentary. This keeps all traffic on your domain and controls the narrative context.

**Problem: The sales team isn't using the coverage in their outreach — they default to their old templates.**
Solution: Do not send templates via email and hope for adoption. Add coverage-specific snippets directly into your sales engagement platform (Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo) as a "template insert" or "snippet" that reps can inject with one click. Host a 15-minute sales all-hands the week of coverage launch — share the top 3 talking points and role-play one live objection scenario using the coverage. Adoption follows training, not email attachments.
