## Overview

Designs and executes a complete B2B SaaS podcast program engineered as a measurable demand generation channel — from show concept and ICP-aligned episode architecture to guest pipeline conversion, SEO-optimized distribution, and listener-to-pipeline attribution. Use when launching a new podcast, transforming a hobbyist show into a revenue asset, or building a category authority play through owned audio content.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Downloads plateau below 300 per episode after Month 3 despite consistent publishing**

Solution: Diagnose using Spotify analytics — check completion rate first. If under 45%, the content itself needs work (tighten first 3 minutes, cut filler, increase tactical density). If completion is 60%+ but downloads plateau, the distribution is broken: audit your LinkedIn clip cadence (should be 3 clips per episode spread across 2 weeks), check if show notes pages are indexed in Google Search Console, and activate guest amplification more aggressively (create a guest asset kit with pre-written captions they can post day-of-launch). Also: guest swap 2 episodes per month with complementary non-competing podcasts in your space — their audience is your ICP.

**Problem: Guests are enthusiastic during recording but don't convert to pipeline conversations**

Solution: The post-show sequence is too transactional. Audit your Day 0 email — if it includes anything resembling a product pitch or demo request, remove it immediately. The first 7 days should be 100% value delivery: send them 3-5 listener responses specifically referencing their episode, connect them with another guest in your network who could help their business, and amplify their content on LinkedIn without asking for anything. The pipeline conversation becomes natural when the relationship is warm. Also audit guest selection: if guests are too aspirational (enterprise companies that can't afford your product) or too junior (can't influence a purchase), your conversion will always disappoint regardless of sequence quality.

**Problem: Cannot get finance/leadership to credit pipeline to podcast because attribution is unclear**

Solution: Implement a three-layer attribution stack that satisfies skeptical CFOs: (1) Direct attribution — UTM-tracked companion asset downloads and show notes form submissions that create HubSpot contacts, directly mapped to opportunities if they progress; (2) Self-reported attribution — add "Wrench & Revenue Podcast" as a named option in your "How did you hear about us?" demo form field; (3) Assisted attribution — ask sales to tag any contact who mentions the podcast during discovery, even if it wasn't the first touch. Then build a board-ready calculation: total podcast-sourced and podcast-assisted contacts × your average contact-to-opportunity conversion rate × average ACV = estimated podcast-influenced pipeline value. Present this alongside branded search volume lift and newsletter subscriber growth as a composite demand signal. Most finance teams accept "influenced pipeline" as a metric once you show the methodology is consistent and conservative.
