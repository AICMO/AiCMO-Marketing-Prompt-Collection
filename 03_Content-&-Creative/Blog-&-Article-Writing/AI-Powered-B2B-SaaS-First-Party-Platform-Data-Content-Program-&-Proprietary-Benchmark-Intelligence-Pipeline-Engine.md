## Overview
Transforms your B2B SaaS platform's aggregate anonymized usage data into a systematic "proprietary intelligence" content program — the same strategy that lets Gong publish "State of Sales" conversation insights, Salesforce release "State of the Connected Customer," and LinkedIn publish its "Workforce Report." Use this when you have platform-level behavioral data (usage patterns, adoption rates, aggregate outcomes, workflow behaviors) that no competitor can replicate, and you want to build a sustainable thought leadership franchise that generates pipeline through data no one else can produce.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Data volume is too small for credible segmentation — sample sizes below 50 in some segments produce unreliable benchmarks**
Fix: Apply a minimum threshold rule and disclose it explicitly: "We only report findings for segments with 50+ customers." For small segments, either (1) aggregate up — combine "SMB" and "Mid-Market" into "Emerging" vs. "Enterprise" to reach threshold, (2) use directional language ("smaller sample sizes suggest...") rather than precise percentages, or (3) supplement with a targeted customer survey for the underrepresented segment and disclose the hybrid methodology. Never publish a statistic based on fewer than 30 records as a standalone finding — the confidence interval is too wide.

**Issue: Legal or privacy team blocks publication citing customer data concerns**
Fix: Align on three principles before pulling data: (1) No individual customer's data should be identifiable from any published finding — test this by asking "Could a sophisticated reader use this table to identify us?" for your 10 smallest customers; (2) Review your Terms of Service and Data Processing Agreements for the phrase "aggregate, anonymized, or de-identified data" — most modern B2B SaaS agreements permit this use; (3) Apply k-anonymity principles: never publish a breakdown where a single customer could represent more than 10% of any segment. If concerns remain, add a customer advisory review step — offer your top 10 customers a preview of findings before publication and address any concerns directly.

**Issue: The findings are interesting but not actionable — data reveals what's happening without suggesting what to do about it**
Fix: Every finding needs a "so what" that goes beyond observation. Use this formula: "[Finding stat]. Companies that [do the behavior associated with top performance] are [X]x more likely to achieve [desired outcome]. The implication: [specific action a reader can take in the next 30 days]."
