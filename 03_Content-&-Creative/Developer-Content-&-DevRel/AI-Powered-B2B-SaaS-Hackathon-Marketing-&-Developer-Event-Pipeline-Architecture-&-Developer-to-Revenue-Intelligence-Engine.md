## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low registration numbers despite promotion — event is 3 weeks out and registrations are 30% of target.**
Solution: Activate partner co-promotion immediately — reach out to your co-host communities and ask for a dedicated dedicated email blast or pinned Discord announcement. Offer infrastructure credit sweeteners for early registrants (additional API credits, exclusive office hours). Post a "Show HN: We're running a hackathon..." on Hacker News on a weekday morning. Reduce registration friction to a single-field email capture with OAuth login. Consider shifting the registration requirement to a lighter "interest form" that converts to full registration as a two-step process.

**Problem: Many registrations but low activation — developers sign up but don't build anything during the event.**
Solution: The gap between registration and activation is almost always a "getting started" problem, not a motivation problem. Diagnose by: (1) checking if participants successfully retrieved API keys (add a key-retrieval confirmation step to onboarding), (2) reviewing the quickstart tutorial for friction points (ideally <5 minutes to first successful API call), (3) checking Discord activity — if developers are asking questions, they're engaged but blocked; improve technical support response time. For immediate impact: send a personal "Are you stuck?" email from a real team member's address 6 hours into the event to all registered-but-not-activated participants.

**Problem: Strong hackathon engagement but post-event conversion is flat — participants loved the event but aren't upgrading to paid.**
Solution: The conversion gap usually has one of three causes: (a) The hackathon use case was too "demo-oriented" — participants built proof-of-concepts that don't have a natural path to production. Fix by redesigning the challenge brief to require production-scale thinking (mandatory performance requirements, real data sets, deployment to a public URL). (b) The post-event email sequence is generic. Personalize each email by referencing the specific project they submitted and the specific Synapse Flow features they used — this dramatically increases open rates and click-through. (c) The pricing feels disconnected from hackathon usage. Review whether your free tier thresholds are too generous (participants can stay on free forever) or too restrictive (participants hit the limit before feeling value). A targeted "hackathon alumni" discount (40% off first 3 months) sent at Day 30 post-event to active participants converts 2–4× better than standard pricing.
