## Overview
Builds an AI-generated Cost-of-Inaction (COI) calculator that quantifies the financial and operational cost of maintaining the status quo — making "do nothing" the most expensive option on the table. Use this when B2B deals consistently stall at late stages, your biggest competitor is "no decision," or you need interactive content that compresses sales cycles by making inaction concretely painful.

## Troubleshooting
**Problem:** Buyers say the COI numbers "feel too high" or seem exaggerated, which undermines trust.  
**Solution:** Always anchor every calculation to a published third-party benchmark (SPI Research, Gartner, Forrester, IDC, TSIA). Make the formula visible and every input buyer-controlled — people trust numbers they calculated themselves far more than numbers presented to them. Add a "conservative estimate" toggle that runs the calculation at 50% of the industry gap. If a buyer halves your number, they're still looking at a substantial cost — and they now own the math.

**Problem:** Sales reps aren't using the COI framework in discovery calls — they revert to ROI and feature selling.  
**Solution:** Don't ask reps to run the full 5-category model in discovery. Reduce the ask to a single COI question per call stage: one opening question at Stage 2 discovery, one quantification bridge at Stage 3 pre-proposal. Use the full calculator as a leave-behind email the rep sends within 2 hours of the call with the buyer's own numbers pre-populated. This removes friction without sacrificing impact.

**Problem:** Completion rates drop below 40% mid-calculator — buyers start but don't finish.  
**Solution:** Reduce the questionnaire to 5 high-impact inputs (not 10+), use industry benchmark defaults for every field so buyers can complete with zero data lookup, and show a live "running total" of their inaction cost as they fill in each field. The real-time accumulating number creates a psychological pull to see the final score. A progress bar ("You're 60% of the way to your inaction risk score") also reduces abandonment significantly.
