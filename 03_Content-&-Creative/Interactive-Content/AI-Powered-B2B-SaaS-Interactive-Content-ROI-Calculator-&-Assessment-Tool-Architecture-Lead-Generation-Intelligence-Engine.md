DELIVERABLE 1 — INTERACTIVE TOOL ARCHITECTURE

Section 1A: Tool Concept Brief

Before building the full question flow, establish the tool's strategic foundation:

TOOL PREMISE:
Every high-converting interactive tool is built on a single diagnostic premise — a question your ICP is actively asking themselves, to which they don't yet have the answer. Frame the tool as the answer to that question.

Format: "[ICP role] who [symptom/situation they're experiencing] can now discover [specific, quantified insight] in [time commitment] by answering [N] questions."

Example (Revenue Intelligence platform):
"VP Sales and RevOps leaders who suspect pipeline visibility gaps are costing them forecast accuracy can now discover exactly how many deals are at risk — and the estimated revenue impact — in under 4 minutes by answering 8 questions about their current sales process."

TOOL NAME OPTIONS (generate 3 options):
- Option A: Outcome-first naming — "[Outcome] Calculator" (e.g., "Pipeline Risk Exposure Calculator")
- Option B: Problem-first naming — "[Problem] Diagnostic" (e.g., "Forecast Accuracy Gap Diagnostic")
- Option C: Aspirational naming — "[Desired State] Readiness Score" (e.g., "Revenue Predictability Readiness Score")

Recommendation: Choose the name that most directly matches the language your ICP uses when they search for solutions — not the language your marketing team uses internally.

Section 1B: Question Flow Architecture

QUESTION DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
1. Every question must serve dual purpose: (a) provide input for the personalized output, AND (b) qualify the lead as high/medium/low value — no vanity questions that only satisfy curiosity
2. Start with a high-resonance question that makes the user feel immediately understood — do not begin with company size or contact info
3. Limit total questions to 6-10 for a calculator; 10-18 for a multi-dimension maturity assessment — above these thresholds, completion rates fall below 40%
4. Use progressive disclosure: start with conceptually simple questions → build toward the question that reveals the core business problem

QUESTION FLOW TEMPLATE (ROI Calculator — 8 questions):

BLOCK 1 — SITUATION QUALIFICATION (Questions 1-2):
These questions establish the context for personalized output AND reveal whether the user is an ICP-qualified prospect.

Question 1: [Establishes company profile context]
- Format: Slider or dropdown
- Purpose: Defines the size/scale of the business problem (larger = higher ICP value)
- Example: "How many quota-carrying sales reps does your team currently have?"
  - Options: 1-10 / 11-25 / 26-50 / 51-100 / 100+
- Scoring: Responses map to company revenue tier → weights the financial output in the calculator

Question 2: [Establishes process maturity context]
- Format: Multiple choice (select one)
- Purpose: Identifies where in the maturity spectrum the prospect sits (determines message angle in output)
- Example: "How does your team currently track deal progress against the forecast?"
  - A) Spreadsheets and manual rep updates
  - B) CRM activity data (calls logged, emails sent, stage movement)
  - C) CRM + conversation intelligence (calls recorded and analyzed)
  - D) AI-driven pipeline intelligence with automated risk scoring
- Scoring: A = high pain / B = moderate pain / C = low pain (likely not ICP) / D = disqualify from ROI calc (already has solution)

BLOCK 2 — PROBLEM QUANTIFICATION (Questions 3-5):
These questions collect the raw financial inputs the calculator needs to generate a credible, personalized ROI estimate.

Question 3: [Primary business volume metric]
- Format: Slider with smart defaults (pre-populate with ICP averages so users who don't know the exact number can still complete)
- Purpose: The foundational financial variable — everything else multiples against this
- Example: "What is your team's current quarterly pipeline coverage ratio? (Total pipeline ÷ quarterly quota)"
  - Slider: 1x → 5x, default: 2.8x (ICP average)
  - Helper text: "Don't know exactly? Use 2.5-3.5x — the typical range for teams your size"

Question 4: [Win rate / conversion metric]
- Format: Slider with benchmarks visible
- Purpose: Calculates the baseline conversion the user is operating at, then the output models improvement
- Example: "What is your team's current pipeline-to-close win rate?"
  - Slider: 5% → 40%, default: 18%
  - Benchmark callout: "Median win rate for B2B SaaS companies your size: 17-22%"

Question 5: [Cost of failure / volume of problem metric]
- Format: Number input or slider
- Purpose: Quantifies the direct business cost that your product's improvement addresses
- Example: "How many deals per quarter does your team mark as 'at risk' or close to falling out of forecast?"
  - Slider: 1 → 30, default: 6
  - Helper text: "This is deals that were in the forecast but either slipped or closed lost in a given quarter"

BLOCK 3 — VALUE ESTIMATION (Questions 6-7):
These questions add the financial variables needed to translate problem metrics into dollar-denominated ROI output.

Question 6: [Deal value metric]
- Format: Dropdown or text input
- Purpose: Converts deal counts into revenue impact
- Example: "What is your average deal size (ACV)?"
  - Dropdown: Under $10K / $10K-$25K / $25K-$50K / $50K-$100K / $100K+
  - Note: Use ranges if exact number feels invasive — ranges still enable accurate calculation

Question 7: [Time/effort cost metric]
- Format: Slider
- Purpose: Adds a cost-of-current-state dimension to the ROI (labor cost of the problem, not just revenue loss)
- Example: "How many hours per week does your RevOps or sales leadership team spend manually updating and reviewing forecast data?"
  - Slider: 1 → 20 hours, default: 7

BLOCK 8 — CONTACT GATE (Question 8 — Lead Capture):
- Placement: After all value questions are answered, BEFORE the results are shown
- Gate type: Soft gate (show partial results teaser on same page, full report requires email)
- Required fields: First name, Work email, Company name
- Optional enrichment fields (use progressive profiling on return visits): Job title, Phone
- Gate copy template:
  - Headline: "Your Pipeline Risk Report is ready."
  - Subheadline: "We've analyzed your inputs and identified [X] risk factors in your current process. Enter your details to see the full breakdown — including your estimated revenue at risk and the 3 highest-impact improvements."
  - Micro-copy below CTA: "No spam. Your personalized report is sent to your work email and is viewable immediately."
  - CTA Button: "See My Pipeline Risk Report →"

Gate completion rate benchmarks: Soft gate (partial preview visible): 55-70%; Hard gate (no preview): 30-45%; No gate: Not recommended for demand gen purposes — sacrifices lead capture for engagement

Section 1C: Scoring Model Architecture

Every answer maps to two scores:

SCORE 1 — LEAD QUALITY SCORE (LQS): Measures ICP fit and purchase likelihood
- Scale: 0-100
- Used by: Marketing automation to route to Hot/Warm/Cold track

SCORE 2 — PROBLEM SEVERITY SCORE (PSS): Measures intensity of the business problem your product solves
- Scale: 0-100
- Used by: Personalizing the output intensity — higher PSS = more urgent language in the results, more aggressive CTA

SCORING MAP EXAMPLE (Revenue Intelligence Calculator):

| Question | Answer | LQS Points | PSS Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Team size | 26-50 reps | +20 | +10 |
| Team size | 51-100 reps | +25 | +12 |
| Team size | 100+ reps | +30 | +15 |
| Current tracking | Spreadsheets only | +20 | +30 |
| Current tracking | CRM activity data | +15 | +20 |
| Current tracking | CRM + conv intelligence | +10 | +10 |
| Pipeline coverage | Below 2.5x | +0 | +25 |
| Pipeline coverage | 2.5x-3.5x | +10 | +15 |
| Pipeline coverage | Above 3.5x | +15 | +5 |
| Win rate | Below 15% | +0 | +30 |
| Win rate | 15-22% | +15 | +15 |
| Win rate | Above 22% | +20 | +5 |
| ACV | $25K-$50K | +20 | +10 |
| ACV | $50K-$100K | +25 | +12 |
| ACV | $100K+ | +30 | +15 |

LEAD ROUTING THRESHOLDS:

Hot (LQS 70+): AI agent immediately creates a task in CRM for assigned SDR → SDR outreach within 2 hours → sequence: personalized email referencing their specific calculator output → LinkedIn connection request → phone call attempt
Warm (LQS 40-69): Enters automated email nurture track → 5-touch sequence over 21 days → SDR review at touch 3 → demo invitation at touch 5
Cold (LQS <40): Enters long-cycle nurture → monthly value email → retargeted with LinkedIn/Google ads featuring the tool's follow-up content → re-scored at 90 days

PROOF ELEMENT (below CTA): Social proof that makes the tool feel credible before a user has invested time in it.
Examples:
- "[X,XXX] revenue leaders have used this calculator — average time: 3 min 42 sec"
- "Built on benchmark data from 500+ B2B SaaS companies with $10M-$200M ARR"

Section 2B: In-Tool Micro-Copy

QUESTION INTRODUCTION COPY: Each question block should have a 1-sentence framing statement that:
- Tells the user why you're asking
- Sets expectations for how the answer affects their personalized output
- Reduces friction from "why do they need this?" hesitation

Example framing copy for Question 4 (win rate):
"Your current win rate establishes the baseline we use to calculate how much pipeline your team needs to recover with better deal intelligence. Most teams are surprised how small win rate improvements compound into significant revenue."

PROGRESS INDICATOR: Show completion percentage or question count (e.g., "Question 4 of 8") — this alone increases completion rates 12-18%.

CALCULATION ANIMATION (between last question and gate): Show 2-3 seconds of "calculating your personalized report" with a progress bar and flashing metrics (e.g., "Analyzing pipeline patterns... Comparing against 500+ companies... Calculating revenue at risk..."). This pause increases perceived value of the output and gate completion rates by 20-35%.

Section 2C: Post-Gate Results Page Copy Architecture

The results page structure determines whether a prospect books a demo or bounces. Every element must serve conversion.

RESULTS PAGE STRUCTURE:

1. PERSONALIZED HEADLINE (generated from their specific scores):
- High PSS (70+): "[First Name], your team has a significant pipeline risk exposure — here's what the data shows."
- Medium PSS (40-69): "[First Name], your pipeline visibility is better than average — but there are 2 specific gaps worth closing before next quarter."
- Low PSS (<40): "[First Name], your pipeline management is above benchmark — here's where the next performance tier is."

2. PRIMARY METRIC CALLOUT (the most compelling, personalized number):
Display as a large, bold figure — this is the "hook" number that makes users want to share their results.
Example: "Estimated pipeline at risk from forecast blind spots: $847,000"
Supporting copy: "Based on your inputs, [X] deals per quarter at $[Y] ACV may be slipping due to insufficient early warning signals. This is a conservative estimate — teams your size typically recover 22-38% of at-risk deals when they implement automated deal scoring."

3. DIMENSION BREAKDOWN (3-4 scored areas):
Show a visual score (progress bar or meter) for each dimension your tool measured.
Example dimensions:
- Pipeline Coverage Adequacy: 68/100 (Above average — your coverage ratio supports quota, but your win rate creates exposure)
- Deal Risk Visibility: 34/100 (Below average — significant blind spot in late-stage deal health monitoring)
- Forecast Accuracy: 52/100 (Near average — room to improve; top quartile teams achieve 72/100 in this dimension)
- RevOps Efficiency: 71/100 (Strong — manual reporting overhead is lower than peer average)

4. PERSONALIZED RECOMMENDATIONS (2-3 specific, actionable next steps):
Each recommendation must: (a) be specific to their input data, (b) reference a real methodology or framework, and (c) naturally bridge to your product's value proposition without being an obvious sales pitch.

Example recommendation:
"Based on your 17% win rate and 6 deals at risk per quarter: Implement a deal health scoring model that flags at-risk deals 3 weeks before forecast close date. Teams your size who implement early warning scoring recover an average of 1.8 additional deals per quarter — at your ACV, that's approximately $57,600 per quarter, or $230,400 annualized. [See how deal health scoring works →] (links to demo CTA or educational content)"

5. BENCHMARK COMPARISON (makes results feel validated by data, not a sales pitch):
"How You Compare to Peers: [Visual chart showing their scores vs. industry median vs. top quartile]"

6. PRIMARY CTA (prominent, above fold on results):
- Hot leads (LQS 70+): "Talk to a pipeline intelligence specialist — see how top performers in your segment closed their gaps." [Book Demo →]
- Warm leads (LQS 40-69): "Get a personalized pipeline health review — 30-minute session, no pitch, just your data." [Schedule Free Review →]
- Cold leads (LQS <40): "Download the full Pipeline Intelligence Benchmark Report for [their industry/size segment]." [Get The Report →]

DELIVERABLE 3 — LEAD QUALIFICATION SCORING MODEL

Section 3A: AI-Powered Lead Routing Architecture

The following workflow must be configured in your CRM/MA platform to execute without human intervention:

TRIGGER: Interactive tool form submission received

STEP 1 — SCORE CALCULATION (AI agent or CRM workflow):
- Sum LQS points from all question responses (reference scoring map in Deliverable 1C)
- Sum PSS points from all question responses
- Calculate personalized financial output (revenue at risk, ROI estimate, etc.) using the financial model

STEP 2 — LEAD ENRICHMENT (run before routing):
- Enrich company name with Clearbit/ZoomInfo/Apollo: company size, industry, tech stack, funding stage
- Adjust LQS based on enrichment signals:
  - ICP-matching industry (+5 LQS)
  - ICP-matching company size confirmed (+5 LQS)
  - Competitor tech in stack (your key displacement target) (+10 LQS)
  - Non-ICP company size (adjust LQS down by -15)

STEP 3 — ROUTING DECISION:

| Final LQS | PSS | Routing Action | SLA |
|---|---|---|---|
| 75+ | Any | Hot — create SDR task, assign to rep, trigger immediate personalized email | SDR outreach within 2 hours |
| 60-74 | 60+ | Warm-Hot — create SDR task with 24-hour SLA, enter high-touch nurture | SDR outreach within 24 hours |
| 40-59 | Any | Warm — enter automated 5-touch nurture sequence | Automated sequence, SDR review at touch 3 |
| <40 | Any | Cold — enter long-cycle nurture, add to retargeting audiences | Automated monthly nurture |

STEP 4 — CRM DATA POPULATION:
Auto-populate on Contact/Lead record:
- "Interactive Tool Name": [Tool name]
- "Tool Completion Date": [Timestamp]
- "Lead Quality Score (Interactive Tool)": [LQS value]
- "Problem Severity Score": [PSS value]
- "Personalized Output — Primary Metric": [e.g., "Revenue at risk: $847,000"]
- "Tool Segment": [Hot / Warm / Cold]
- All individual question responses as custom fields for SDR personalization

STEP 5 — SDR PERSONALIZATION BRIEFING (for Hot and Warm-Hot leads):
Auto-generate an internal CRM note for the assigned SDR:
"[Contact Name] completed the [Tool Name] on [Date]. Their inputs: [N] reps, [X]% win rate, [Y] deals at risk per quarter, $[Z] ACV. Their estimated revenue at risk: $[calculated figure]. They scored [LQS]/100 on lead quality and [PSS]/100 on problem severity. Recommended opener: Reference their specific pipeline risk figure and ask whether their current system gives them early warning before deals fall out of forecast."

DELIVERABLE 5 — DISTRIBUTION & PROMOTION STRATEGY

Section 5A: 90-Day Launch Promotion Plan

WEEK 1-2: LAUNCH FOUNDATIONS

Technical prerequisites before driving traffic:
- [ ] UTM tracking implemented on all distribution links: utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign, utm_content for each channel
- [ ] CRM custom fields created and workflow configured (test with 5 dummy submissions before launch)

WEEK 3-6: OWNED CHANNEL LAUNCH

Sales team activation (the highest-ROI distribution for interactive tools):
- Send an internal email to all AEs and SDRs with a personal-use invitation: "Complete the calculator yourself so you understand the outputs before sending it to prospects."
- Build a 1-click send template for AEs to share the tool in outbound prospecting: "[Prospect First Name], I put together a quick 4-minute calculator that shows [specific metric] for teams like yours. Want to see what it says for [Company Name]? → [Tool link]"

- SDR sequence integration: Replace one of the existing email touches in active nurture sequences with a personalized tool invitation
- Expected outcome: 15-25% of tool completions via sales-shared links if AE/SDR activation is done properly

Website deployment:
- Embed tool in website navigation: add "ROI Calculator" or "[Tool Name]" as a persistent CTA in the site header
- Inline tool placement on high-intent pages: pricing page, homepage (secondary CTA), product pages
- Exit-intent popup on high-traffic informational pages: "[X,XXX] leaders have used this tool — see your results in 4 minutes." triggers when users show exit intent
- Blog post: Publish a 1,200-word article titled "How [ICP Role] Teams Calculate the Cost of [Problem] — And What Top Performers Do Differently" — embed the tool as the conversion CTA at 60% scroll depth and at end of article

Email list activation:
- Send to full marketing-qualified database: subject line testing variants — "Your [ICP metric] benchmark is ready" vs. "4 minutes to see your [problem area] gaps"
- Segment by persona: customize the email intro for primary vs. secondary ICP job titles
- Re-send to non-openers at 4 days with a different subject line (typically adds 15-25% additional opens)

WEEK 7-12: PAID & ORGANIC AMPLIFICATION

LinkedIn Sponsored Content (highest ROI for B2B SaaS interactive tools):
- Ad creative format: Single-image ad or video ad (15-sec screen recording of the tool in action)
- Audience targeting: Job titles matching ICP, company size matching ICP, exclude existing customers and current leads in CRM (upload suppression list)
- Ad copy framework: "Most [ICP role]s at companies your size underestimate their [problem metric] by [X]%. This 4-minute calculator shows you where you actually stand — and what it's costing you."
- Budget allocation: 40% of interactive content paid budget to LinkedIn
- Bidding strategy: Maximize conversions (tool completions) — not clicks
- Expected CPL benchmark: $45-$120 per tool completion for B2B SaaS ICP audiences via LinkedIn

Google Search retargeting:
- Target branded search terms AND competitor branded terms (users researching alternatives)
- Ad copy: "See How You Compare — Free 4-Minute [Problem Area] Assessment"
- Budget allocation: 20% of paid budget
- Landing page: Direct to tool, not homepage

Content syndication:
- Identify 3-5 B2B media properties where your ICP reads: industry publications, newsletters, analyst firm content sites
- Negotiate sponsored placement of the tool (or a teaser article that links to it) in high-reach newsletters targeting your ICP
- Cost: Typically $2,000-$8,000 per placement for newsletters with 20K+ ICP-targeted subscribers
- Expected CPL: $15-$45 via newsletter sponsorship to a highly targeted audience

Partner distribution:
- Identify 3-5 non-competing software vendors who serve the same ICP (integration partners, complementary tools)
- Propose a co-promotion: "Share our calculator with your list; we'll share your resource with ours"
- Reciprocal newsletter mention or co-branded webinar: "Join [Your Company] + [Partner] for a 30-minute session on [shared ICP problem] — plus get your personalized [tool name] results"

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Tool completion rate is below 30% — most visitors start the tool but abandon before submitting their contact information.
**Solution:** High abandonment almost always has one of three root causes. First, question overload: audit total question count — if above 10 for a calculator or 15 for a maturity assessment, remove questions that aren't essential for either personalization or lead scoring. Second, identify the specific drop-off question using your tool platform's per-question analytics (Outgrow and Ion both show this) — the question with the highest exit rate is almost always one that feels invasive (budget questions, timeline questions, or questions where the user doesn't have the answer readily available). Either remove it or make it optional with a pre-populated default value. Third, gate timing: if you're requiring contact information before showing any results, move the gate to after partial results are revealed — even showing one metric before the gate (e.g., "Your pipeline risk exposure is in the top 35% of companies your size — enter your details to see the full breakdown") increases gate completion rates by 20-40% because the user now has a reason to finish.

**Problem:** High completion rates but low quality leads — most tool completers score as Cold (LQS <40) and SDRs are frustrated with the lead quality.
**Solution:** This is a distribution targeting problem, not a tool problem. Your tool is converting well but reaching the wrong audience. Audit your paid promotion targeting first: LinkedIn campaign audience settings (are job titles matching your actual ICP?), Google retargeting audiences (are you retargeting all website visitors including non-ICP visitors?), email distribution list (does your database contain a high proportion of non-ICP contacts from past gated content downloads?). Implement a "screener question" as Question 1 of the tool that identifies non-ICP users before they complete: "Which of the following best describes your current role?" → if the answer maps to a non-ICP persona, serve them a resource download instead of the full tool. This reduces total completions but increases the Hot lead rate from 15% to 25-35% — which is the outcome that actually matters.

**Problem:** SDRs are not personalizing their outreach with the tool data — they're using the same generic email template regardless of what the prospect scored.
**Solution:** This is a sales enablement and workflow design problem. Three fixes: First, make the CRM personalization brief unavoidable — configure the SDR task notification email to include the prospect's personalized brief in the task body (not a link to click — the actual text, visible immediately). When SDRs have to click through to find the data, 60-70% skip it. Second, build 3-4 SDR email templates (not one) that are pre-written for different score profiles — the SDR selects the template matching the prospect's LQS and PSS tier, then personalizes the one variable (the dollar figure or the lowest-scoring dimension). This reduces cognitive load while ensuring the key personalization happens. Third, track reply rates by SDR and share results monthly — SDRs who use tool data in their first-touch email consistently see 2-3x higher reply rates, and sharing that data motivates adoption.
