## Overview

Designs and deploys a fully automated organizational capability maturity assessment that positions your B2B SaaS product as the proven path from a buyer's current state to best-in-class, generates high-intent gated leads by delivering personalized gap-analysis reports, and creates a proprietary category-definition framework that shapes how the entire market thinks about the problem you solve.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Assessment completion rate is below 40% (too many drop-offs mid-assessment)**
Solution: The most common cause is question length and friction. Audit your assessment for: (1) questions longer than 25 words — rewrite them shorter; (2) questions using internal jargon your ICP doesn't use — replace with the language buyers use in G2 reviews and Reddit threads; (3) more than 5 questions before the first "progress reward" (showing a benchmark comparison or partial score). Add a progress bar and a mid-assessment "insight reveal" at question 10 to re-engage respondents who are losing momentum. Also check mobile — over 35% of assessment completions happen on mobile devices, and poorly formatted answer options cause dropout.

**Problem: Assessment-sourced MQLs are not converting to opportunities at higher rates than other lead sources**
Solution: This usually means the scoring logic is too permissive (marketing-qualified threshold is too low) or the sales handoff is happening without enough context. Fix the threshold first: require a minimum dimension score on your highest-revenue-impact dimension (not just the overall composite score) to qualify as an MQL. For the sales handoff, ensure the SDR receives the full assessment breakdown — not just the overall score — so they can open with "I saw from your assessment that your [specific dimension] scored at Level 1, which typically means [specific pain point]. Is that the biggest bottleneck right now?" Personalized openers that reference assessment-specific responses drive 30-50% higher connect rates than generic outreach.

**Problem: The maturity model feels generic and isn't differentiating from competitor frameworks**
Solution: Generic maturity models fail because they describe capabilities in vendor-neutral language that doesn't anchor to your product's specific approach. Audit your level definitions: does Level 4 describe the specific architectural decisions (automation, integrations, data models) that your product enables — and that competitors cannot enable in the same way? If Level 4 could be achieved with any vendor in your category, your maturity model is not a competitive moat. Rewrite Level 4 and Level 5 with specific technical and operational characteristics that are uniquely enabled by your product's architecture. Buyers who aspire to Level 4-5 will then naturally evaluate your product as the path forward.
