## Overview
This prompt architects a dynamic, AI-driven "Buyer Fit Discovery" interactive experience that helps prospects self-identify which of your product's primary use cases maps to their specific situation — then routes them to a tailored demo track, trial onboarding path, or nurture sequence based on their responses. Use it when your product serves 3+ distinct buyer personas or use cases and generic demo requests are converting poorly because sales reps spend the first 20 minutes of every call doing discovery you could have done before the meeting.

DELIVERABLE 2 — ADAPTIVE QUESTION FLOW ARCHITECTURE

Design a 7–10 question adaptive sequence built on Cognitive Interview Technique principles: each question should feel like a curious consultant asking, never a qualification screen. Apply the "Question Ladder" framework — start broad (role/context), narrow to situation (current state), then descend to problem depth (urgency), before surfacing motivation (desired outcome).

OPENING HOOK (Question 1 — Role Identification):
Do not ask "What is your job title?" Ask a role-function question that reveals both title AND primary motivation. Example: "Which of these best describes the biggest challenge your team owns right now?" with options that map to each persona's primary JTBD.

SITUATION QUESTIONS (Questions 2–3 — Current State):
Reveal the prospect's current solution and its failure mode. Design questions so that selecting any answer triggers a branching path that deepens into the relevant use case territory. Every answer option should carry a Challenger-style reframe that introduces a perspective the prospect hasn't considered.

PROBLEM DEPTH QUESTIONS (Questions 4–5 — Urgency Signal):
Measure urgency with behavioral anchors, not self-reported timelines. Instead of "When are you looking to buy?", ask "Which of these situations has happened in the last 90 days?" with options that serve as urgency triggers.

MOTIVATION QUESTIONS (Questions 6–7 — Success Vision):
Surface the outcome metric the prospect cares about most. Frame as future-state: "If this problem was fully solved 6 months from now, what would the headline metric improvement look like?" Options must map to the measurable outcomes your product delivers, pre-seeding the ROI conversation.

QUALIFICATION GATES (Questions 8–9 — Commercial Signals):
Determine authority and buying stage without asking "Do you have budget?" Use proxy questions: "How does your team typically evaluate new vendors?" and "Who else is involved in a decision like this?" Map answers to buying committee completeness score.

PERSONALIZATION CAPTURE (Question 10 — Use Case Confirmation):
Present 2–3 pre-scored use case matches based on prior answers and ask the prospect to confirm which is most relevant. This makes them feel heard, validates your scoring model, and creates psychological commitment to the personalized next step.

CONTACT CAPTURE GATE:
Position the email gate AFTER Question 7 (post-motivation, pre-qualification). Value exchange framing: "To show you results tailored specifically to [their selected use case] + [their company profile], we need your work email." Include a pre-gate preview of the type of insight they'll receive.

SCORING STRUCTURE:
- Each answer carries a weight (1–5 points) for each use case
- Final score = sum of weighted answers across all use case dimensions
- Primary use case = highest-scoring use case
- Secondary use case = second-highest (triggers "you might also be interested in..." content)
- Routing threshold: define minimum score to qualify for sales routing vs. nurture vs. self-serve

ROUTING DECISION TREE:
For each use case match + urgency combination + buying authority level, define the exact routing action:
- High score + High urgency + Decision authority → Immediate SDR sequence: [sequence name], [first touch within X hours], [subject line template]
- High score + Low urgency + Decision authority → Nurture track: [track name], [first email triggered], [acceleration trigger if they return to site]
- High score + Any urgency + No authority → Champion enablement: [internal selling kit], ["bring in your stakeholders" CTA]
- Moderate score → Education track + ROI calculator CTA
- Low score + Good firmographic → "Not quite ready" track with re-engagement trigger at 30/60/90 days
- Poor firmographic fit → Self-serve resources only, no SDR time

LEAD SCORE INTEGRATION:
Define how fit discovery scores integrate into your existing MQL scoring model in HubSpot/Salesforce. Fit discovery score should override or amplify the behavioral score — a prospect with a strong fit discovery completion but low page view count is higher priority than a prospect who visited 12 pages but completed no qualification.

DELIVERABLE 4 — PERSONALIZED RESULTS PAGE FRAMEWORK

For each of your 3–5 primary use case matches, design the complete results page architecture:

RESULTS HEADLINE: A second-person statement that names their specific situation and outcome. Formula: "[Role] like you at [company profile] use [Product] to [specific outcome] — here's why your situation is a strong fit." Never generic. Never "You scored 82% — great fit!" That tells them nothing.

FIT SCORE VISUALIZATION: Show a fit percentage (85%, 72%, etc.) broken down by 3 dimensions: (1) Use Case Alignment, (2) Urgency Match, (3) Company Profile Fit. Include a one-sentence explanation for each dimension score.

RELEVANT FEATURE SPOTLIGHT: Show only the 3–4 features most relevant to their specific use case. For each feature: the capability, the job it does for their persona, and a 1-line proof point ("Customers using this see X% improvement in Y"). Never show the full feature list.

PROOF STORY MATCH: Surface a customer story from their industry vertical or persona type. Format: Company profile → Problem they had → How they used [Product] → Measurable outcome achieved. Should feel like the prospect is reading about a company in their exact situation.

OBJECTION NEUTRALIZATION: Based on their answers, preemptively address the most likely blocker. Example: If they indicated "IT approval required," include: "How [Product] handles enterprise security reviews" with a link to security documentation and a "schedule a security-focused demo" CTA variant.

PRIMARY CTA: Matched exactly to their routing tier (Persona-specific demo / Sandbox trial / ROI calculator / Executive briefing). CTA copy should reference their use case: "See how [Product] eliminates [their specific pain]" — never "Book a Demo."

SECONDARY CTA: For prospects not yet ready to book: a use-case-specific content asset (case study, ROI teardown, competitive analysis). Gated with progressive profiling (if email already captured, only ask 1 additional field).

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:** Use HubSpot Forms with conditional logic for question branching. Map all responses to custom contact properties. Use Workflows to trigger routing sequences, assign lead scores, and populate the SDR briefing note in the contact record's activity feed automatically.

**Salesforce:** Use Salesforce Web-to-Lead with hidden fields for scoring. Post-submission webhook to Apex trigger that creates a Task on the Lead record with the auto-generated SDR briefing. Use Flow to route Lead to the correct SDR queue based on use case match + territory.

**Outgrow / Typeform:** Build the branching question logic natively in Outgrow or Typeform Logic Jumps. Use their native HubSpot/Salesforce integrations for real-time data sync. Outgrow's calculator feature handles the fit score computation and results page personalization without custom code.

**Outreach / Salesloft:** Create separate sequences per use case match (3–5 sequences total). Route leads from CRM directly into the correct sequence via HubSpot Workflow or Salesforce Flow. First email in each sequence references the specific use case and urgency trigger captured in discovery.

**Intercom / Drift:** Deploy the discovery experience as a bot-assisted conversation rather than a form for higher-traffic websites. Use Intercom Series or Drift Playbooks to run the question flow and trigger the same routing logic via webhook to CRM.

**Segment / CDP:** Pass discovery event data to your CDP to enrich buyer profiles across channels. Use use-case match as a segment audience dimension for retargeting, email, and in-app personalization. Buyers who completed discovery and matched Use Case X can receive personalized homepage experiences on return visits.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Discovery completion rate below 50%**
Fix: Audit Question 1 — if it reads like a form field rather than a genuine business insight question, prospects disengage immediately. A/B test opening the experience with a provocative insight ("Most revenue teams think they have a pipeline problem. 67% actually have a forecast accuracy problem. Which is yours?") before asking the first question. Also check mobile experience — branching question logic often breaks on mobile in form builders.

**Problem: SDRs aren't using the discovery briefings and still running generic discovery calls**
Fix: This is a change management problem, not a technology problem. Run a 2-week "discovery brief competition" — track which SDRs use briefings vs. those who ignore them, compare demo-to-opportunity conversion rates. Share results in the sales all-hands. Make SDR briefing completion a required field before marking a meeting as "confirmed" in Salesforce/HubSpot. The data will make the case.

**Problem: Fit score is routing high volumes to SDRs but demo conversion isn't improving**
Fix: Your routing thresholds are too low — you're qualifying too many people as "sales-ready." Tighten the routing criteria by requiring minimum scores on BOTH use case match AND urgency signal. Route moderate-score prospects to a "self-serve first" track (trial + ROI calculator) and only escalate to SDR if they re-engage. Quality beats volume — a 30% reduction in SDR-routed leads with a 25% conversion lift is a net win.
