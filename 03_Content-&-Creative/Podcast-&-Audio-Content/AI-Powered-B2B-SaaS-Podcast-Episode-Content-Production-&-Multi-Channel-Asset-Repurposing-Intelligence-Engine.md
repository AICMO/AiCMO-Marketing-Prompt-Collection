## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom property "Podcast Episode Source" on contacts; use the UTM parameters from Deliverable 5C to automatically tag podcast-influenced contacts in workflows. Build a podcast-sourced pipeline report in HubSpot reporting.
- **Notion:** Build an episode content tracker with a Notion database — one page per episode with embedded asset checklist, UTM links, and performance fields synced weekly.
- **Riverside/Descript:** Export raw transcript from your recording platform directly into this prompt. The AI can process raw transcripts (including speaker labels) without manual cleanup.
- **Buffer/Hootsuite:** Schedule all four LinkedIn company posts across a 2-week window post-launch — post on Tuesday/Thursday at 9am-11am for maximum B2B reach.
- **Zapier/Make:** Automate UTM link creation — build a Zap that triggers when a new episode is added to your Notion tracker, auto-generates all UTM URLs, and pastes them into the episode page.
- **Salesforce:** Create a Campaign object for each podcast episode; map UTM tracking to Campaign Source on opportunities for accurate podcast pipeline attribution in revenue reporting.
- **ConvertKit/Klaviyo:** Segment your email list by "listened to ≥3 episodes" and trigger a direct outreach sequence from your sales team — this is your warmest non-customer audience segment.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The blog post and LinkedIn posts feel repetitive — too much overlap in content.
**Solution:** Assign each format a distinct "one job" rule: the blog post goes deep on the HOW (tactical framework), LinkedIn posts focus on the WHY (emotional hook or belief change), and email focuses on the WHO (listener identity + credibility). Brief the AI explicitly: "The blog post should teach the framework. The LinkedIn post should challenge a belief. The email should create urgency around the problem." Feed these instructions as constraints in your prompt.

**Problem:** Short-form video clips selected by AI don't perform well — they lack visual energy or hook in the first 5 seconds.
**Solution:** Add this constraint to your prompt: "Only select clips where: (1) the guest makes an unexpected admission or reveals a counterintuitive result, (2) the clip starts mid-sentence or mid-thought (in medias res), or (3) the clip contains a specific number or metric." Clips that begin with "So I think..." or "Great question..." are automatically disqualified.

**Problem:** Sales team isn't using the episode digest or talking points in prospect conversations.
**Solution:** Reframe the digest as a "conversation trigger" instead of content summary. Change the format to: "Use this when a prospect says: [common objection]. Reference: 'We actually had [Guest Name] from [Company] on our podcast — they solved this exact problem by...' Then send them the episode link as a follow-up." Tie each talking point to a specific objection from your sales discovery call recordings.
