DELIVERABLE 1 — GUEST INTELLIGENCE ARCHITECTURE

A. Four-Tier Guest Archetype System

ARCHETYPE 1: THE CUSTOMER TRANSFORMATION STORY GUEST
Who they are: A current customer in an ICP-matched role who has achieved a specific, quantifiable outcome using your category of solution (not necessarily your product). They are 12–24 months post-implementation with a clear before/after narrative.

Why they drive pipeline: Listeners in the same role pattern-match their own situation. Social proof is embedded in the episode itself. Guest becomes a referenceable customer post-recording.

Identification signals:
- Active on LinkedIn with posts about operational challenges in your category
- Has presented at a conference or published a case study in the last 18 months
- Has 500–5,000 LinkedIn followers (influential enough to share content, not so large they're overexposed)
- Company matches ICP criteria (size, industry, GTM motion)

LinkedIn Sales Navigator search string:
`Title: ["VP of Sales" OR "Director of Revenue Operations" OR "Head of Sales Enablement"] + Industry: [Your target industry] + Seniority: Director OR VP + Company Headcount: 201-1000 + Keywords: ["pipeline" OR "forecasting" OR "quota attainment"]`

Post-recording conversion path: Episode → LinkedIn clip shared by guest (tagging your company) → new ICP followers → SDR creates personalized outreach referencing the episode → pipeline opportunity opened within 45 days.

ARCHETYPE 2: THE CONTRARIAN PRACTITIONER
Who they are: A practitioner in an adjacent role who publicly holds a view that challenges conventional wisdom in your category. They have a point of view strong enough to generate debate in comments.

Why they drive pipeline: Contrarian content generates 3–5x more comments and shares than agreeable content. Listeners who disagree still engage; listeners who agree become advocates. The episode becomes a reference point in industry discussion.

Identification signals:
- Posts on LinkedIn that generate 50+ comments (not just likes)
- Has written a "why [common practice] is broken" type article or post in the last 90 days
- Speaks at conferences on the topic your podcast covers
- Their employer is NOT a competitor (no risk of promotional conflict)

Outreach angle: "We've been following your takes on [specific claim they've made]. We want to give you a platform to make that argument at length — including the counterarguments."

Post-recording conversion path: Episode generates significant comment debate → guest shares episode → comments surface additional ICP prospects → retargeted with LinkedIn ads → inbound demo requests from episode-adjacent audience.

ARCHETYPE 3: THE ANALYST DATA HOLDER
Who they are: An industry analyst, researcher, or data journalist who has published original research relevant to your category. They have proprietary data or methodology that your audience wants access to.

Why they drive pipeline: Data-backed episodes get cited, linked, and shared across newsletters and Slack communities. Analyst endorsement (implicit or explicit) acts as third-party credibility. Episodes generate SEO-indexed transcripts that rank for research-related queries.

Identification signals:
- Has published a research report or benchmark study in the last 12 months cited by 3+ other outlets
- Writes a newsletter with 5,000+ subscribers in your category
- Is associated with a research firm, consulting practice, or academic institution
- Their research aligns with the pain points your product solves

Data extraction strategy: Design questions to surface one unpublished data point or early finding from their current research. Frame it as: "What's the most counterintuitive thing your data has shown that you haven't been able to share publicly yet?"

Post-recording conversion path: Episode transcript → SEO-optimized blog post → organic search traffic → podcast listener acquisition → listener nurture sequence → lead capture for data report download → MQL pipeline.

ARCHETYPE 4: THE PRACTITIONER-TO-PRACTITIONER PEER
Who they are: A practitioner at a company similar to your buyer profile who has no financial relationship with any vendor. They share tactical how-to content from lived experience — not vendor-sponsored best practices.

Why they drive pipeline: Trust is highest between practitioners who have no incentive to promote a vendor. Their tactical advice is perceived as authentic. Their audience mirrors your ICP.

Identification signals:
- Posts tactical "here's exactly what I did" content without vendor attribution
- Has a personal newsletter, community membership, or speaking history in your category
- Their company uses tools in your category (visible from job postings or tech stack data)
- They actively engage in community forums (RevOps Co-Op, Pavilion, etc.)

Recruitment angle: "We're building a practitioner-only series where we share unsponsored tactical plays from people actually in the trenches. No vendors, no sales pitches — just what's actually working."

DELIVERABLE 3 — MULTI-TOUCH OUTREACH SYSTEM

TOUCH 1 — LinkedIn Comment (Day 0, before direct outreach):
Find a post they published in the last 7 days. Leave a substantive comment (3–4 sentences) that adds to their argument, names a tension they didn't address, or shares a relevant data point. Do NOT mention your podcast. Goal: get on their radar as a peer, not a promoter.

TOUCH 2 — LinkedIn DM (Day 3, after comment, if they responded or liked your comment):
Character limit: 300 characters.

Template:
"[First name] — your point about [specific claim from their post] has been stuck in my head. I run [Podcast Name], a show for [ICP audience]. We'd love to build a full episode around that argument. No vendor talk — just your playbook. 15 mins to explore?"

Key rule: The first sentence must name something SPECIFIC they said or wrote. Generic openers ("I love your content!") reduce response rate by 60%.

TOUCH 3 — Email (Day 7, if LinkedIn DM not opened or responded):
Subject line options (A/B test):
- "Your take on [specific topic] → episode idea for [Podcast Name]"
- "[Their company] approach to [topic] — would make a great [Podcast Name] episode"
- "Practitioner-only podcast episode: [their stated opinion]"

Body (under 150 words):
"[First name],

I've been following your work on [specific topic] — particularly [cite a specific post, talk, or article with a sentence or two about what stood out].

I host [Podcast Name], a podcast for [ICP description] with [X] listeners per episode. Our audience is [describe the audience they'd be speaking to].

We're building an episode around [the specific topic/tension you want to explore with them]. Based on [what you read/heard from them], I think your perspective would be exactly what our audience needs to hear.

No vendor talk. No product pitches. Your ideas, your framework, 35 minutes.

Would a 15-minute call this week or next work to see if there's a fit?

[Name]
[Title] | [Podcast Name]
[One-line podcast description + download stat]"

TOUCH 4 — LinkedIn Clip DM (Day 14, if no response):
Pull a 60-second clip from a previously recorded episode that is directly relevant to a topic they've written about. DM with:

"[First name] — I recorded this episode with [Guest Name, their title] last month about [topic]. Given your point about [their specific argument], I thought you'd find [specific moment in the clip] interesting. Still open to having you on to challenge or expand this thinking if timing works."

Response rate benchmarks:
- Touch 1 comment: creates recognition, no direct response expected
- Touch 2 LinkedIn DM: 15–25% response rate
- Touch 3 email: adds 10–15% of total responses
- Touch 4 clip DM: closes 5–8% of remaining non-responders

Combined sequence response rate target: 35–40%

DELIVERABLE 5 — INTERVIEW FRAMEWORK FOR MAXIMUM CONTENT YIELD

35-MINUTE RUN OF SHOW:

MINUTES 0–3 — WARM-UP (not recorded for the episode):
- Confirm recording setup and permissions
- Ask one off-record question to warm them up: "What's the most overrated advice you see being shared in [category] right now?"
- Their answer will tell you where to push hardest in the interview

MINUTES 3–8 — CREDIBILITY ESTABLISHMENT:
- "Tell us about [specific project or outcome they're known for] — what was the problem that made it necessary?"
- Goal: Let them establish authority before you introduce tension. Listeners need to trust the guest before they'll accept a contrarian take.

MINUTES 8–18 — CONTRARIAN CORE (primary content extraction zone):
- Introduce their contrarian position: "You've argued that [their stated claim]. A lot of our listeners do the opposite. What are they getting wrong?"
- Extraction technique: After their answer, apply "the specificity probe": "Can you give me the exact step sequence you followed?" and "What's a metric that would tell someone if they're doing this right or wrong?"
- This zone generates: the LinkedIn clip (look for the 60–90 second moment where they deliver a clean, self-contained argument) and the newsletter tactical sequence.

MINUTES 18–25 — DATA AND BENCHMARKS:
- "What numbers have you seen that would surprise most people in this role?"
- "If someone wanted to know if their [process/team/program] was in the top quartile, what would they measure?"
- Extraction technique: Write down every specific number or percentage mentioned. These become stat-led social posts and credibility signals in long-form content.

MINUTES 25–32 — FAILURE AND INSIGHT:
- "Tell me about a time you got this completely wrong. What happened?"
- "What's something you believed when you were earlier in your career that you now think was naive?"
- This zone generates: the authentic storytelling moment for long-form articles and the "humanizing" LinkedIn post that drives the highest emotional engagement.

MINUTES 32–35 — CLOSE AND AMPLIFICATION SETUP:
- "What do you want listeners to walk away with from this conversation?"
- "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"
- Post-recording (off the record): "We'll send you the 3 clips we think are most shareable before we publish — completely fine if you don't want to share any of them. But our listeners who match your audience tend to see strong engagement on [describe the type of content they post]."

KEY TRANSITION PHRASES:
- To redirect to specifics: "That's really interesting — can you give me the step-by-step version of how that actually works?"
- To surface tension: "I want to push on that a little — someone listening might say [counterargument]. How do you respond?"
- To extract a benchmark: "If you had to give a number, what would you say is the benchmark someone should be aiming for?"
- To surface a clip: "If you were going to give someone one thing to remember from everything we've discussed today, what would it be?" (This almost always produces the cleanest standalone clip.)

DELIVERABLE 6 — POST-EPISODE GUEST CONVERSION SYSTEM

DAYS 1–3 (POST-RECORDING, PRE-PUBLISH):
- Send guest 3 pre-selected clip options (30–90 seconds each). Frame: "We're planning to feature these — wanted to give you first look. If any of these aren't right to share publicly, let us know."
- Send a co-promotion brief: their custom sharing kit with pre-written LinkedIn post copy (2 options: one data-focused, one story-focused), a 1200x628 audiogram template with their quote, and episode air date.
- Add guest to CRM as "Podcast Guest" contact with episode title, air date, and guest archetype tag.

DAYS 0–7 (POST-PUBLISH):
- Monitor LinkedIn for the episode share. Comment on it within 2 hours with a unique insight that adds to the discussion.
- Send a thank-you DM with the episode's first-24-hour download count: "Episode is live — already [X] downloads in the first day. Your audience is clearly engaged." Provide the link.
- Tag the guest in your company LinkedIn episode post.

DAYS 8–30:
- If guest is in ICP: create a CRM task for SDR to reach out referencing the episode and their key insights. Template: "I listened to your episode on [Podcast Name] — loved your point about [specific claim]. I work with [relevant customer story]. Worth 20 minutes to see if there's overlap?"
- If guest is an adjacent practitioner: invite them to co-create follow-on content (guest blog post, newsletter collaboration, LinkedIn Live follow-up conversation).
- If guest has a relevant network: ask for 2 introductions to peers who would value the episode's topic. Template: "Based on our conversation, I think [Peer Name] would have found this useful — would you be open to making a warm intro?"

DAYS 31–90:
- If no pipeline activity: send a "what happened next" follow-up: "We'd love to do a follow-up episode in Q[X] — what's evolved in your thinking since we recorded?"
- If pipeline opened: route to account team with full guest context. Add to customer marketing sequence if they convert.

CONVERSION ROUTING DECISION TREE:
- Guest is in ICP role at target account → SDR outreach within 7 days of episode publish
- Guest is in ICP role at non-target account → add to nurture sequence, revisit in 90 days
- Guest is adjacent practitioner with large ICP audience → co-marketing partnership pitch
- Guest is analyst or researcher → licensing conversation for their data in co-branded content
- Guest referred 2+ pipeline introductions → customer advocacy / reference program invitation

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Create a custom "Podcast Guest" contact property with dropdown values for guest archetype
- Set up a workflow: when Contact Source = "Podcast Guest" AND Episode Air Date is set → trigger SDR outreach task 7 days after air date
- Build a "Podcast Pipeline" saved report filtering opportunities where any associated contact has Source = Podcast Guest or Podcast Listener

**Salesforce:**
- Create a Campaign object for each podcast episode (Campaign Type = Podcast)
- Add Campaign Members for every guest and any listener who converts to a lead
- Use Campaign Influence reporting to tie podcast episodes to pipeline without first-touch/last-touch limitations

**Notion:**
- Build a Guest Pipeline database with properties: Name, Archetype, Status (Identified → Outreach Sent → Responded → Booked → Recorded → Published → Pipeline Generated), Score, Episode Title, Post-Publish Actions Completed
- Link each guest record to the episode brief document

**Zapier / Make:**
- Trigger: New row added to Guest Pipeline tracker (Airtable or Google Sheets) with Status = "Published"
- Action 1: Create HubSpot contact task for SDR if guest archetype = "Customer" or "ICP Practitioner"
- Action 2: Send automated thank-you email from founder's inbox with clip sharing kit attachment
- Action 3: Add guest to custom LinkedIn audience for retargeting with episode promotion ads
