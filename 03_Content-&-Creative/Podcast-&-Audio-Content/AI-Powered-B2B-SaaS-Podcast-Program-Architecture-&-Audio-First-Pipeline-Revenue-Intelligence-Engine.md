## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom contact property `podcast_guest_status` (values: invited/confirmed/recorded/aired/nurtured). Build a podcast guest nurture workflow triggered when property = "aired." Add podcast as a contact source option for attribution reporting.
- **Salesforce:** Map podcast touchpoints as campaign members. Create a "Podcast Listener" campaign per episode. Use campaign member status to track listener → MQL → opportunity progression.
- **Transistor / Buzzsprout / Captivate:** Connect your hosting platform's analytics to a Google Sheet via Zapier to auto-log download milestones and pull geographic listener data weekly.
- **Notion:** Build a podcast production Notion database with episode status (concept / outreach / confirmed / recorded / edited / published / repurposed). Link each episode to derivative assets as sub-pages.
- **Descript / Otter.ai:** Automate transcript generation immediately post-recording. Feed transcripts into Claude or GPT-4 with the repurposing prompts from Deliverable 4 above to generate all derivative assets in under 20 minutes per episode.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Upload podcast guest list as a matched audience to serve mid-funnel ads to people who appeared on your show — highest-intent retargeting audience in your stack.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Episodes aren't attracting ICP listeners — downloads are high but audience quality is wrong.**
Solution: Audit your episode titles and guest archetypes against your ICP. Titles written for virality attract generalist content consumers, not buyers. Run a 1-question survey at episode 10 asking "What's your current role?" Compare to ICP profile. Shift 2 of next 4 episodes to hyper-specific ICP topics (e.g., "RevOps at Series B" not "Revenue Operations"). Remove distribution channels that reach broad audiences (Spotify editorial playlists) and double down on channels with ICP density (LinkedIn audio events, industry Slack communities).

**Problem: Guest conversion rate to sales conversations is below 3%.**
Solution: The post-recording nurture is likely too generic or moving too fast. Check touch timing — most shows try to convert guests within 48 hours of airing; the optimal window is Day 8-12 when initial episode excitement has cooled and guests have seen real listener engagement. Personalize Touch 2 with specific download data and a named listener reference ("A VP RevOps at Atlassian listened and shared your episode"). The conversation bridge should reference something the guest said in the episode, not your product.

**Problem: Content repurposing is taking 6+ hours per episode and killing team bandwidth.**
Solution: You're likely editing AI output instead of engineering better prompts. Feed the full transcript into the advanced prompt from Deliverable 4 as a single batch input. Use Claude Projects or ChatGPT Custom GPTs with the repurposing templates saved as system instructions so you paste transcript once and receive all 8 assets in a single response. Target total AI-assisted repurposing time: 45 minutes per episode maximum, with a human review pass of 20 minutes.
