## Integration Tips

- **Transistor / RSS.com / Buzzsprout:** Use the analytics dashboard to pull 7-day and 30-day download data by episode. Export monthly to a Google Sheet that auto-calculates episode-over-episode growth rate and flags outlier episodes (>20% above or below average) for topic pattern analysis.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Create a "Podcast Listener" contact tag. Use form submissions from episode-specific lead magnet landing pages to tag contacts with the episode topic that converted them — this feeds into nurture sequence personalization.
- **Descript:** Set up a templated transcript export workflow: record → Descript auto-transcribes → AI edits remove filler words → export as blog post draft with H2/H3 headers mapped to episode chapters. Connect to your CMS via Zapier.
- **Canva:** Build a master "Guest Promotion Kit" template with your brand colors. Create a Canva Brand Kit so any team member or guest can export on-brand assets without design review.
- **Spotify for Podcasters + Apple Podcasts Connect:** Configure both dashboards in a weekly analytics review calendar event. Key exports: listener age/gender, device type, geographic distribution, and follower count trend. Store in a shared Google Sheet for quarterly ICP match analysis.
- **Zapier:** Automate the guest amplification sequence — trigger: new episode published in Transistor → send Day 0 + Day -7 pre-written emails via HubSpot → log episode promotion status in Notion or Airtable.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Guest promotion kit sent but guests still aren't sharing their episode.
**Solution:** The friction is the "blank page" problem — guests intend to post but never start. Fix: In the Day -7 email, reduce the ask to ONE action: "Post this to LinkedIn — I've already written it for you, just click publish." Include a direct deep link to LinkedIn's "share" draft with the post pre-populated using LinkedIn's posting API or Zapier. Guest compliance jumps from 25% to 55%+ when zero writing is required.

**Problem:** Podcast SEO rewrites haven't moved rankings on Apple Podcasts after 60 days.
**Solution:** Apple Podcasts ranking is dominated by subscribe velocity (new subscribers in the last 7 days) more than description keywords. Run a "subscribe sprint" alongside the SEO rewrite: email your existing list asking for an Apple Podcasts review (not just a subscribe), promote the show in 2 newsletter cross-promotions in the same 2-week window. New subscriber velocity signals to Apple that the show is growing — descriptions help discovery but subscribe velocity determines ranking.

**Problem:** Cross-promotion outreach is not converting — newsletter owners and podcasters aren't responding.
**Solution:** Cold outreach for cross-promotion fails when the offer is vague. Replace "want to cross-promote?" with a hyper-specific offer: "I listened to your last 3 episodes — your audience is [description]. I have a 4-episode series on [topic] that would resonate directly. I'm proposing a 1-for-1 mention swap: I mention your show in this Friday's episode [link to calendar], and you mention ours in whichever episode makes the most sense. No commitment beyond that." Specific + reciprocal + low-risk = 3x response rate.
