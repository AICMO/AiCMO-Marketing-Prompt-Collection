## Overview

Generates a complete, AI-personalized email sequence targeting users of a named competitor — surfacing their known pain points, mapping your differentiated value to each frustration, and guiding prospects through an evidence-based migration journey to conversion. Use this when launching a competitive displacement motion, responding to a competitor pricing change or feature gap, or running a programmatic "switcher" acquisition campaign against a specific rival.

### Objective

Generate a complete 7-touch displacement sequence (6 emails + 1 LinkedIn DM variant) that:
1. Meets the prospect in their current reality — acknowledging [COMPETITOR] as a reasonable prior choice, not attacking it
2. Progressively builds the case for switching using evidence, not claims

### Email Sequence Framework

**Touch 1 — Pattern Interrupt (Day 1)**
- Subject: Reference their known pain without competitor mention
- Body: One insight that makes them think "how did they know?" — benchmark, stat, or trend directly relevant to their KPI
- CTA: Soft — "Does this match what you're seeing?" or share a resource
- Psychology: Curiosity + relevance, zero threat

**Touch 2 — Pain Acknowledgment (Day 4)**
- Subject: Name the specific frustration pattern, framed as a question
- Body: Validate the pain with proof you've seen it before (customer quote or data), introduce your product as one option that solved it
- CTA: 60-second video, one-page comparison, or short case study
- Psychology: Recognition + empathy + social proof of problem

**Touch 3 — Evidence Drop (Day 9)**
- Subject: Named customer + specific result (e.g., "[CompanyName] cut their [METRIC] by 40% in 60 days")
- Body: Tell the migration story in 3 sentences. Focus on the moment the customer decided, the outcome, and something specific they can now do that they couldn't before
- CTA: Read full story or book a brief 15-min comparison call
- Psychology: Proof of outcome, loss aversion (what they're missing)

**Touch 4 — Migration Objection Killer (Day 14)**
- Subject: Address the fear directly ("The real reason people don't switch tools — and why it's overblown")
- Body: Break down migration myth vs. reality. Show timeline, your migration team, and what you migrate for them
- CTA: "Want to see our migration checklist?" or a mini-assessment
- Psychology: Remove friction, reduce perceived risk, demonstrate competence

**Touch 5 — Peer Proof (Day 21)**
- Subject: Name a peer company in the same space who switched
- Body: Peer story — same stack, same team size, same concern before switching, specific outcome after. End with a challenge or question
- CTA: "20-min call to show you exactly what the switch looks like for a team like yours"
- Psychology: Social proof from identical peer, FOMO, specificity builds credibility

**Touch 6 — Value Summary + Genuine Urgency (Day 28)**
- Subject: Clear, direct, no tricks — e.g., "Before I go quiet — one thing worth knowing"
- Body: One-paragraph summary of the core trade-off they're making by staying. Include time-limited offer if genuine (migration concierge, extended trial, pricing lock). If no genuine urgency, give them a backdoor resource.
- CTA: "Let me know if timing changes — here's the resource that helps most when you're ready to revisit"
- Psychology: Reciprocity, respect for their time, leave the door open

**Touch 7 — LinkedIn DM (Day 5, parallel track)**
- 3-sentence format: "Noticed you're using [COMPETITOR]. We just published [INSIGHT] on [PAIN POINT] — thought it might be relevant to your work on [KPI]. Happy to share if useful."
- Tone: Peer-to-peer, no pitch

## Success Metrics

**Sequence-level KPIs:**
- Reply rate: Target ≥ 8% on cold, ≥ 15% on warm/intent-triggered lists (industry cold average: 1–3%)
- Positive reply rate: ≥ 50% of replies should be curious/interested, not unsubscribe
- Meeting booked rate: ≥ 3% of sequence starts convert to discovery call
- Downstream: Track win rate on deals sourced from displacement sequences vs. non-competitive pipeline

**Email-level quality checks:**
- [ ] Subject line under 50 characters (mobile preview threshold)
- [ ] No competitor name in subject line (spam/deliverability risk)
- [ ] Every email passes the "reply > click" test: could a prospect reply without clicking anything?
- [ ] Migration email (Touch 4) includes a specific timeline, not vague reassurance
- [ ] At least 2 personalization tokens per email are populated from enrichment (not just first name)

**Signal to iterate:**
- If Touch 1 open rate is high but reply low: the insight isn't specific enough to their role
- If Touch 3 (case study) gets low engagement: customer story is too generic or company is not a credible peer
- If Touch 4 gets replies but not conversions: migration concern is real — add more specifics or offer a migration risk assessment call

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Open rates are below 20% — sequence isn't getting read**
Fix: Your subject lines are too product-centric or mention the competitor's name (spam signal). A/B test curiosity-first subjects that reference the persona's KPI ("Why forecast accuracy plateaus at ±25% for most ops teams") versus insight subjects ("The tool consolidation that saved one VP-Ops $280K in seat costs"). Also check sender reputation — ensure you're sending from a warmed domain, not a fresh subdomain.

**Issue: Replies are mostly "not interested" or unsubscribes — sequence feels like a pitch**
Fix: Touches 1–2 are converting too early. Remove any mention of features or product benefits in the first two emails entirely. Touch 1 should feel like an industry analyst sent it — pure insight, no CTA beyond "does this resonate?" Re-read each email with this test: "Would a non-salesperson send this?" If not, cut the sales language.

**Issue: Meeting booked rate is low despite positive replies**
Fix: Your CTA asks for too much too early. Map which touch generated the reply — if it was Touch 1 or 2, those contacts are warm but not ready for a demo. Respond to positive replies with a low-friction offer: a migration risk assessment, a free data audit, or a recorded walkthrough they can watch async. Only pitch the live call at Touch 5+.
