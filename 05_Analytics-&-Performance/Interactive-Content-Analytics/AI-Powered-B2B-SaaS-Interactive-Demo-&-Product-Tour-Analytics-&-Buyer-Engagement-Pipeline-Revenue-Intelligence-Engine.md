# ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

## Phase 1: Demo Health Diagnostic (TRAFFIC → ENGAGEMENT → CONVERSION)
Apply the AARRR model to demo performance:
- **Acquisition**: Are the right visitors finding the demo? (ICP traffic quality score)
- **Activation**: Are visitors engaging deeply enough to qualify? (Completion depth + feature exploration)
- **Retention**: Are accounts returning or sharing internally? (Multi-session rate, replay rate)
- **Revenue**: Are demo-engaged accounts converting at higher rates? (Win rate premium)
- **Referral**: Are demos being shared/advocated? (Internal share rate, rep adoption)

## Phase 2: Feature-Interest Signal Intelligence (Jobs-to-be-Done Mapping)
Map screen-level engagement to customer Jobs-to-be-Done:
- Which features have unexpectedly high engagement → possible unmet needs or messaging gaps
- Which features have high drop-off DESPITE being positioned as core differentiators → likely UX or messaging disconnect
- Which feature sequences correlate with downstream MQL/SQL conversion → reveal "demo magic moments" (equivalent to product Aha Moments)

Apply Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) framework: for top 3 engaged modules, articulate the functional, emotional, and social job the buyer is trying to accomplish. This informs demo narrative reordering.

## Phase 3: Intent Tier Scoring & Sales Routing Model
Build a Demo Intent Score (DIS) for each identified account/contact:

**DIS Calculation:**
- Completion depth: <25%=0pts, 25-50%=1pt, 50-75%=2pts, >75%=3pts
- Feature exploration: 1-2 modules=0pts, 3-5=1pt, 6+=2pts
- Return visits: 0=0pts, 1 return=1pt, 2+=2pts
- CTA engagement: No CTA=0pts, CTA viewed not clicked=1pt, CTA clicked=3pts
- Multi-stakeholder: 1 viewer=0pts, 2 viewers=1pt, 3+=3pts
- Time since last session: >30 days=0pts, 8-30 days=1pt, ≤7 days=2pts

**Score Interpretation:**
- 12-13: Tier 1 (Hot) → Immediate SDR outreach within 4 hours, personalized to features viewed
- 7-11: Tier 2 (Warm) → SDR sequence Day 1-3, feature-specific email content
- 3-6: Tier 3 (Nurture) → Add to feature-specific nurture track, retargeting ads
- 0-2: Tier 4 (Awareness) → Re-engage with new demo track for different use case

## Phase 4: Demo Track Optimization (Narrative Sequencing Analysis)
For each active demo track, evaluate:
1. **Narrative arc effectiveness**: Does the demo follow Problem → Implication → Solution → Proof → Call-to-Action? Where does the narrative break?
2. **Feature sequencing**: Are the highest-engagement features shown early enough? Apply "Best First" principle — move top-engaged modules before gate position.
3. **Gate placement optimization**: Is the email capture gate positioned at maximum intent (after "Aha Moment")? Compare: current gate position completion rate vs. estimated completion at Aha Moment screen.
4. **CTA hierarchy**: Is there one primary CTA or are multiple competing CTAs diluting conversion?

## Phase 5: Revenue Attribution Modeling (Demo as a Revenue Asset)
Calculate **Demo Revenue Efficiency (DRE)**:
- DRE = (Total demo-influenced closed-won ARR) / (Total demo program investment)
- Compare to content marketing DRE, paid media DRE, and event DRE for investment prioritization
- Model: If demo completion rate improved by 15%, projected pipeline impact based on current conversion curves

## Integration Tips

- **Navattic / Demostack / Walnut**: Export session data as CSV or via webhook → pipe to HubSpot/Salesforce as custom activity records. Map `completion_percentage` and `modules_viewed` to custom contact/deal fields for DIS calculation.
- **HubSpot**: Create a workflow that triggers when `demo_completion_pct > 75 AND demo_cta_clicked = true` → auto-enroll in Tier 1 SDR sequence AND notify assigned rep via Slack via HubSpot → Zapier → Slack.
- **Salesforce**: Build a Demo Engagement Dashboard in SFDC Reports using custom fields fed from your demo tool webhook. Track DIS score as a number field on the Lead/Contact object; include in lead scoring model alongside MQL criteria.
- **6sense / Bombora / DemandBase**: Cross-reference demo engagement data with intent data — accounts in active buying stage (Stage 4-5 in 6sense) with Tier 1 DIS score = highest-priority outreach list. Feed into ABM sequences immediately.
- **Chorus / Gong**: Pull discovery call transcripts for accounts that completed the demo — look for features they mention that align with their highest-engagement demo modules. Use this for call coaching ("They watched the pipeline forecast feature for 4 minutes — did you demo that live?").
- **Notion / Confluence**: Auto-publish weekly Demo Intelligence Brief using Make.com/Zapier + GPT-4o — pull top Tier 1 accounts, feature engagement highlights, and win rate data into a structured template for the revenue team.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Demo session data isn't matching CRM records — most sessions show as anonymous.
**Solution:** Implement email-first gating earlier in the demo (screen 2-4 vs. screen 7+), add UTM parameter capture to demo tool embed, and integrate reverse IP enrichment (Clearbit Reveal, 6sense, Leadfeeder) to identify anonymous company-level visitors. Even company-level identification (without individual identity) enables ABM-style SDR outreach.

**Problem:** Demo completion rates are high (>60%) but MQL conversion is low (<10%) — demos aren't generating qualified pipeline.
**Solution:** Audit your demo track for ICP-targeting mismatch. High completion with low MQL usually means the wrong traffic is finding the demo (too broad targeting) or the gate is too easy (no friction filters out non-buyers). Add a qualification question at the gate ("What's your team size?"), tighten paid traffic ICP filters (job title + company size), and compare MQL rates by traffic source — typically one source (often direct/broad SEO) is inflating completion volume with non-buyers.

**Problem:** Sales team isn't adopting the demo tool — only 30% of reps send demos.
**Solution:** This is a change management problem, not a product problem. Three interventions: (1) Create three pre-built email templates with demo links embedded for the most common outreach scenarios (post-cold-call, post-discovery, stakeholder introduction) so sending a demo requires zero effort; (2) Share one specific win story weekly in Slack/team meeting showing how a demo engagement signal led to a booked meeting or deal acceleration — social proof from peers drives adoption faster than training; (3) Gamify: track and post demo sends per rep on a leaderboard and tie it to pipeline review conversations ("showed demo to X accounts this week").
