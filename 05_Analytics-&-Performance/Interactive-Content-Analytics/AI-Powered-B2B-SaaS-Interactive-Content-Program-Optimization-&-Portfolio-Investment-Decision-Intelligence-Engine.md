# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Interactive Content Program Optimization & Portfolio Investment Decision Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, interactive-content, analytics, portfolio-optimization, investment-decision, demand-gen, marketing-ops, pipeline, budget-allocation, program-management

## Overview
Turns cross-portfolio interactive content data into an actionable program management system that tells you exactly which assets to refresh, retire, scale, or build next — and how to allocate budget across content types (ROI calculators, maturity assessments, product tour, benchmark reports) for maximum pipeline return. Use quarterly for program planning and monthly to catch underperforming assets before they drain budget.

### Analysis Framework

**Section 1: Portfolio Performance Audit**

Build a master portfolio scorecard. For every asset, calculate and rank:

| Asset Name | Type | Age (mo) | Monthly MQLs | Cost/MQL | Monthly Pipeline | Pipeline/$1 Invested | ICP Fit % | Completion % | Health |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Asset 1] | [Type] | [#] | [#] | [$] | [$K] | [X:1] | [%] | [%] | 🟢/🟡/🔴 |

**Health scoring criteria:**
- 🟢 Green: Pipeline-per-dollar at or above program average AND completion rate ≥ 40% AND ICP fit ≥ 50%
- 🟡 Yellow: One metric below threshold — fixable with targeted intervention
- 🔴 Red: Two or more metrics below threshold — retire or fundamental rebuild required

Calculate the **Program Efficiency Score**: total pipeline generated across all assets ÷ total program investment (build amortized + maintenance + promotion). Compare to your static content and webinar program efficiency for context.

---

**Section 2: Retire / Refresh / Scale Decision Matrix**

For each asset, make a clear decision using the following decision logic:

**RETIRE if any two of these are true:**
- Pipeline-per-dollar invested is below 50% of the program average for 2+ consecutive quarters
- ICP fit rate below 35% (attracting the wrong audience at scale)
- Completion rate below 25% with no fixable single drop-off point
- Asset topic has been superseded by a new product feature or positioning shift that makes the content misleading
- Sales team usage below 20% despite enablement efforts

**REFRESH if any two of these are true:**
- Completion rate was above benchmark at launch but has declined >15 points over 6+ months (content aging signal)
- Product pricing, features, or ICP has shifted since launch — asset data is now inaccurate
- Single fixable drop-off point is causing >25% abandonment (surgical question rewrite vs. full rebuild)
- Asset is 12+ months old with no updates and is still generating ICP-matched leads worth preserving
- Mobile completion rate is 20+ points below desktop (UX/platform fix, not content fix)

**SCALE PROMOTION if all three are true:**
- Pipeline-per-dollar is above program average
- ICP fit rate ≥ 55%
- Completion rate ≥ 45%
- (and promotion spend is below a channel's saturation point — i.e., cost-per-MQL hasn't increased materially with scale)

For each asset: state the decision, list the 2 specific data points that drive it, and define the exact action — e.g., "Retire 'Legacy Security Checklist': ICP fit has dropped to 28% since we pivoted to enterprise segment; pipeline-per-dollar is 0.3:1 vs. program average of 4.2:1. Sunset date: end of current quarter. Redirect traffic to [replacement asset]."

---

**Section 3: Budget Reallocation Plan**

Current state: [Promotion dollars distributed across assets]
Problem: Budget is often equally distributed (or driven by recency bias) regardless of performance.

Build a performance-weighted reallocation:

1. **Zero-base the budget**: Start with $0 for each asset
2. **Allocate by pipeline-per-dollar ratio**: Assets with above-average efficiency receive first dollar allocation
3. **Scale allocation by channel saturation**: For each high-performing asset, what's the maximum efficient spend level before cost-per-MQL starts degrading? (Estimate based on current scale and channel CPMs)
4. **Retired asset savings**: Calculate how much budget frees up from retired assets and allocate to either scaling top performers or funding new builds

Output format:

## Success Metrics

A high-quality output from this prompt will:
- Produce a clear retire/refresh/scale decision for every asset with two specific data points per decision — not generic "performance is low" statements
- Calculate the freed-up budget from retired or reduced assets and explicitly allocate it to higher-performing assets or new builds
- Identify the single portfolio-level metric to move (program efficiency score: pipeline-per-dollar-invested) and show how each decision improves it
- Deliver a new asset recommendation with a believable ROI prediction grounded in existing portfolio conversion rates — not optimistic projections
- Produce a sequenced 90-day plan that a marketing team of 2-3 people can realistically execute without adding headcount

**Healthy program benchmarks (use as scorecard):**
- Program efficiency: ≥ $4 pipeline per $1 invested
- Portfolio average completion rate: ≥ 40%
- Portfolio average lead-to-MQL rate: ≥ 12%
- Portfolio average ICP fit rate: ≥ 50%
- Assets with Green health rating: ≥ 60% of portfolio

If fewer than 40% of your portfolio assets have Green health ratings, the priority is pruning underperformers before building new assets — adding new assets to a weak portfolio dilutes team attention and budget without proportional pipeline return.

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Create a custom HubSpot Dashboard: "Interactive Content Program Portfolio" with panels showing MQL volume, cost-per-MQL, and pipeline sourced per asset — update monthly; share with CMO as a standing report
- Build HubSpot Custom Properties for each contact: "Last Interactive Asset Completed" and "Interactive Asset Result Variant" — these allow you to build cohort lists (e.g., "All contacts who completed Asset 1 with Stage 3+ result in last 90 days") and compare downstream conversion across assets
- Use HubSpot's Attribution Reports to compare First-Touch and Multi-Touch pipeline credit across interactive assets — assets that appear frequently in multi-touch paths (even with low first-touch sourcing) are proving influence value that simple lead attribution misses
- Automate monthly portfolio reports with HubSpot Workflows: first Monday of each month → pull MQL count by asset → compile into a Slack message to the demand gen team lead

**Salesforce + Marketo:**
- Build a Salesforce Custom Object: "Interactive Content Engagement" with fields for Asset Name, Completion Date, Result Variant, and a Lookup to the Contact record — this enables Salesforce Opportunity reporting with interactive content touchpoints across the full buying committee
- Create a Marketo Smart Campaign: daily trigger — if Contact completes interactive asset → update "Last Interactive Asset Completed" → add to asset-specific nurture track → notify SDR if lead score crosses MQL threshold
- Build a Salesforce report: "Interactive Content Pipeline Contribution" grouped by Asset Name, showing Opportunities Created, Pipeline Value, and Closed-Won ARR where Contact had an interactive content engagement in the 60 days prior to opportunity creation — this is the quarterly board-level portfolio ROI report

**Outgrow / Typeform / Interact:**
- Export aggregate question-level response data monthly for each asset — not just lead export — to feed the drop-off analysis in this prompt. In Outgrow, use the "Analytics → Funnel" view and export CSV by question number.
- Use Outgrow's A/B testing feature (or Typeform's hidden fields with Zapier routing) to test gate position changes without rebuilding the full asset — test email gate after Q3 vs. Q5 across 50/50 split for 30 days before committing
- Set up webhook triggers in your interactive content platform: completion event → fires to HubSpot/Salesforce → updates contact record → triggers downstream Zap → notifies SDR in Slack with result variant and real-time enrichment data (Clearbit or 6sense)

**Google Analytics 4 + Looker Studio:**
- Create a GA4 Custom Report: "Interactive Content Funnel" showing Visit → Start → Completion → Lead Capture for each asset URL. Use this as the top-of-funnel companion to the CRM downstream data.
- Build a Looker Studio dashboard combining GA4 (traffic and completion funnel) + Salesforce (pipeline and ARR data) + your promotion spend (manually entered or via marketing data platform) — this unified view enables the portfolio efficiency score calculation in Section 1
- Tag each asset's confirmation/results page as a GA4 Conversion Event — enables GA4 Attribution modeling to show which channels drove completions, informing promotion budget reallocation in Section 3

**Zapier / Make (Formerly Integromat):**

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Two or more assets show consistently similar performance, making retirement decisions feel arbitrary — both are "Yellow" with no clear winner.
**Fix:** Break the tie using the Sales Team Utility metric (% of AEs referencing the asset in discovery calls), not completion rate or pipeline sourced. Assets that AEs actually use in conversations have long-term compounding value that pipeline attribution undercounts — short sales cycles may attribute the deal to the last touch before close, undervaluing the mid-funnel asset that created the conviction to buy. Run a 2-week AE survey: "In the last 10 discovery calls, how many times did you reference [Asset X] output? What did the prospect say when you referenced it?" AEs rarely lie about what helps them close deals. The asset they reference more often is the one to keep and invest in.

**Problem:** Program efficiency score (pipeline-per-dollar) looks strong in aggregate but is being inflated by one exceptional asset that masks poor performers.
**Fix:** Always report median asset efficiency alongside average — a single 10:1 performer can make a portfolio averaging 2:1 look like a 4:1 program. The median tells you what a typical asset in the portfolio is actually returning. If median is below 2:1 but average looks healthy, the portfolio has a star-and-drag problem: the star is subsidizing the underperformers. In this case, the recommendation is aggressive retirement of all below-median performers to raise the floor, not celebration of the average. Cut the drag; let the star run.

**Problem:** New build recommendation is met with "we don't have budget" even though the portfolio analysis shows retiring Asset X would free up funds.
**Fix:** Present the retire-and-rebuild as a swap, not a budget request. Frame it as: "We're currently spending $X/month promoting an asset generating 0.6:1 pipeline return. Redirecting that spend to the new benchmark report build amortizes the build cost in [X months] and is projected to generate 5x the pipeline of the retired asset by month 4." Lead with the opportunity cost of the underperformer, not the cost of the new build. Bring the Asset 3 vs. Asset 1 efficiency gap data ($0.6:1 vs. $5.8:1 pipeline-per-dollar) — that contrast is the CFO's attention-grabber, not the total build price tag.
