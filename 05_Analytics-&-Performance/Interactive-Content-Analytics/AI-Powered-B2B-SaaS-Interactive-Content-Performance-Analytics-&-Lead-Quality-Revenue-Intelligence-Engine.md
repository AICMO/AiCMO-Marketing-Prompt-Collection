## Overview
Transforms raw interactive content data (starts, completions, question-level drop-off, lead submissions, enrichment results) into actionable lead quality scores, funnel conversion diagnostics, and program-level pipeline attribution. Use it after each interactive asset launch for launch postmortems, monthly for portfolio optimization across all active interactive tools, and quarterly for interactive content investment decisions vs. static content alternatives.

**Section 2: Drop-Off Diagnosis & Content Quality Audit**

For each major drop-off point:
1. **Question-level abandonment map**: Which specific question or section causes the steepest abandonment? Is the question too personal (asking for budget), too complex (requiring data users don't have), too time-consuming, or too obviously designed for lead capture rather than user value?
2. **Friction taxonomy**: Classify each drop-off as: *Value friction* (user doesn't see benefit of continuing), *Effort friction* (too many steps, confusing UI), *Privacy friction* (asked for sensitive data too early), or *Relevance friction* (question doesn't apply to their situation)
3. **Mobile vs. desktop drop-off comparison**: Interactive tools often have 2-3x higher abandonment on mobile due to form UX — isolate if this is a platform/UX issue vs. content issue
4. **Return rate signal**: High return rate (>15%) on a high-drop-off question means intent is strong but friction is solvable — these are your highest-priority fixes

Produce a **Content Quality Score (0-100)** using: completion rate weight (35%), question relevance (assessed from drop-off pattern, 25%), personalization depth (15%), lead quality output (25%).

**Section 3: Lead Quality Comparative Analysis**

This is the core value proposition of interactive content — higher quality leads than static content. Measure it rigorously:

- **ICP Fit Score (0-100)**: Weight by: title match (40%), company size match (30%), industry match (20%), buying stage signal (10%)
- **Enrichment quality**: What % of leads have complete company data (name, size, industry, technology stack) via enrichment? How does self-reported data compare to enrichment data — where do contacts misrepresent themselves?
- **Lead velocity**: How quickly do interactive content leads progress from MQL → SQL → Opportunity compared to leads from static downloads, webinars, and paid channels? Faster velocity = asset is filtering for high-intent, high-fit buyers
- **Self-selection quality**: Did the asset's scoring/personalization successfully sort buyers by intent? Compare MQL rate of leads who received a "high urgency" result variant vs. "low urgency" variant — the gap quantifies how well the asset is qualifying

**Section 4: Personalization Effectiveness Analysis**

Interactive content's competitive advantage is personalized output — generic interactive tools that deliver identical results to all users perform no better than static PDFs.

Analyze each result variant:
1. **Variant-to-conversion mapping**: Which result variants (e.g., "You're at Stage 2 Maturity — here's your gap analysis") drive the highest downstream SQL rates? Which drive the lowest? Is there a correlation between "harsh" scores (telling buyers they have serious gaps) and higher conversion?
2. **Self-selection accuracy**: Compare the result variant a user received vs. their actual ACV/fit when they became a customer. Is the asset scoring calibrated correctly — or are Stage 2 users actually churning earlier, suggesting the assessment is miscategorizing buyers?
3. **Personalization depth score**: Does each lead receive a results page/PDF with their name, company, and specific recommendations? Or is it generic? Highly personalized outputs (referencing company-specific benchmarks) drive 2-3x higher sales engagement than generic output
4. **Sales utility rating**: Survey your AEs — are they actually using the interactive content results in sales conversations? If <50% of AEs reference the output in discovery calls, the personalization isn't deep enough to be a sales tool

**Section 5: Pipeline Attribution & Program ROI**

Calculate three revenue layers:
- **Direct sourced pipeline**: Opportunities opened where interactive content was the first meaningful touchpoint (within 60-day window post-completion)
- **Influenced pipeline**: Open opportunities where a buying committee member completed the assessment — these deals close 15-25% faster in B2B SaaS (benchmark)
- **Expansion pipeline**: Customers who completed the asset as part of expansion motion (e.g., re-ran an ROI calculator for upsell scenario) — flag separately for NRR contribution

**Full ROI Calculation:**
- Total investment: build + maintenance + promotion
- Total pipeline created (direct + weighted influenced): $X
- Pipeline-to-investment ratio: X:1
- Closed-won ARR attributable: $X (or projected if < 6 months old)
- Payback analysis: at your close rate and ACV, how many months until this asset ROI-positive?

**Compare vs. program alternatives:**
If the same investment had gone to a static white paper, webinar, or paid ad campaign — what would the expected pipeline output be using your historical cost-per-pipeline ratios? This is the "opportunity cost" analysis that justifies or challenges continued investment.

**Section 6: Optimization Roadmap**

Generate 6 prioritized test recommendations. For each:
- **Hypothesis**: "If we change [X], we expect [Y metric] to improve by [Z%] because [reason grounded in your data]"
- **Test type**: UI/UX change / Question rewrite / Gate repositioning / Result variant redesign / Promotion channel shift / New audience segment
- **Effort**: Low (1-3 days) / Medium (1-2 weeks) / High (3+ weeks)
- **Expected impact**: Quantified in incremental MQLs/month or pipeline $/quarter
- **Measurement**: Specific KPI and measurement window to call the test

Priority ranking order: Gate position optimization → Drop-off question rewrites → Personalization depth upgrade → Mobile UX fixes → Promotion channel mix → Result variant calibration

**Section 7: Portfolio Intelligence**

If this asset is part of a multi-asset interactive content program:
- **Asset comparison matrix**: How does this asset's cost-per-MQL, completion rate, and ICP fit score compare to other assets in the portfolio?
- **Funnel stage coverage audit**: Do you have interactive assets covering TOFU (awareness/education), MOFU (evaluation/comparison), and BOFU (ROI/pricing)? Which stage is underserved?
- **Topic-to-ICP fit**: Are the right asset types covering the right ICP segments? (e.g., maturity assessments resonate most with enterprise buyers; ROI calculators perform best in mid-market where budget justification is the primary blocker)
- **Investment reallocation signal**: Based on portfolio performance, which assets should get more promotion budget, which should be refreshed, and which should be retired?

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Completion rate is high (60%+) but lead-to-MQL rate is below 8%, suggesting the asset is attracting the wrong audience.
**Fix:** The problem is usually upstream — either promotion is reaching the wrong ICP (check LinkedIn audience targeting or email list segmentation) or the asset topic is too broadly appealing. Run an ICP audit: what % of completers match your ICP title and company size? If ICP fit is below 40%, tighten promotion channel targeting before optimizing the asset itself. If ICP fit is above 60% but MQL rate is still low, the asset may be attracting early-stage researchers (Stage 1-2 results) who aren't ready to buy — add a buying stage self-identification question and segment your lead scoring by result variant.

**Problem:** Drop-off at a specific question is above 25% but removing the question would reduce personalization quality.
**Fix:** Test three alternatives before removing the question: (1) Reframe as a range selector instead of exact input ("Roughly how many incidents per year?" vs. "Enter exact incident count"), (2) Make the question optional with a "Skip for now" button — skippers still complete but you lose data quality, (3) Move the high-friction question to after the email gate so you capture the lead before asking for sensitive information.
