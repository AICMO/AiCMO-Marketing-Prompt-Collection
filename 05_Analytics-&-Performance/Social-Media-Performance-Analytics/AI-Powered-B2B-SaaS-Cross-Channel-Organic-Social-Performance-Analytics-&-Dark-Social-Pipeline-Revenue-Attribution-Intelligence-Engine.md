## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Use the "Original Source" and "Latest Source" contact properties alongside a custom "Self-Reported Attribution" property populated from your demo request form to enable the dark social multiplier calculation
- **Salesforce:** Build a Campaign Influence report with a 30-day attribution window; tag all social touchpoints as Campaign Type = "Organic Social" to enable IPC (Influenced Pipeline per Channel) queries
- **LinkedIn Analytics API:** Export via the Campaign Manager API or Hootsuite/Sprout Social integration — push LinkedIn follower demographic data to a Google Sheet for weekly ICP match scoring
- **Google Analytics 4:** Create a custom channel grouping that separates organic social by platform (LinkedIn / YouTube / X / TikTok) to avoid the default "Social" bucket that obscures per-channel performance
- **Koala.io or Primer:** Install website de-anonymization tools to identify which companies are visiting from social referral traffic even without form fills — these are your dark social pipeline signals
- **Notion/Airtable:** Build a Content Performance Database that logs every post, engagement metrics at 48h and 7d, UTM-tracked clicks, and any linked deals — enables the content-to-revenue mapping in Section 3
- **Zapier/Make:** Automate pulling weekly social metrics into your CRM contact timeline when a tracked company employee engages with social content

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: UTM data shows near-zero social pipeline but the team believes social is working.**
Solution: This almost always indicates broken UTM tracking (links shared in posts aren't tagged, or mobile apps strip UTMs). Audit by manually clicking every bio link, post CTA link, and LinkedIn DM link you've shared in the last 30 days — rebuild UTM parameter architecture. Also activate the self-reported attribution survey immediately; your dark social multiplier will be uncalculable without it.

**Problem: Dark social multiplier calculation returns a number below 1.0 (self-reported attribution is lower than UTM tracking).**
Solution: This indicates your UTM tracking is overcounting (possibly counting employee/internal clicks) or your self-reported attribution question is placed too late in the buyer journey (asking after purchase rather than at first contact). Verify your UTM tracking excludes internal IP ranges, and move the attribution question to your demo request form rather than post-purchase survey.

**Problem: Executive team dismisses the dark social pipeline estimate as "made up."**
Solution: Present the range (conservative/base/optimistic) rather than a single number, and anchor it to a methodology: "This uses the same proxy multiplier methodology that Nielsen uses for TV attribution and that LinkedIn uses in their B2B Institute research." Then propose a 90-day validation study — run a buyer survey to 50 recent closed-won customers asking specifically about social touchpoints to calculate an empirical multiplier for your company.
