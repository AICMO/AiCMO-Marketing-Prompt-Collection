1.2 Core LinkedIn KPI Framework
Define the three-tier KPI framework that moves from content performance → audience quality → business impact:

TIER 1 — CONTENT PERFORMANCE KPIs (what the content does):
- Organic impressions per post: raw reach delivered by algorithm
- Engagement rate: (comments + reactions + shares + clicks) / impressions × 100
  - B2B LinkedIn benchmarks by format: text-only 1.5–3.5% / carousel 2–4% / native video 1.8–4% / document 2–5% / image 1–2.5% / poll 3–6%
- Comment-to-impression ratio: comments are the highest-intent engagement signal (prospect is willing to publicly associate with your content)
  - Benchmark: >0.15% is high-quality (1+ comments per 700 impressions)
- Share rate: organic amplification — each share is an endorsement that reaches second-degree network
- Follower conversion rate: impressions → new followers (benchmark: 0.05–0.15% per post)

TIER 2 — AUDIENCE QUALITY KPIs (who is engaging):
- ICP follower match rate: % of followers matching your ICP (job title + seniority + company size + industry)
  - Pull from LinkedIn Analytics "Follower Demographics" — target >35% ICP match for focused B2B companies
- ICP engagement rate: % of post impressions delivered to ICP-matched audience (LinkedIn Audience Insights)
- Competitor account engagement: are people who follow your direct competitors also engaging with your content? (Signal of market relevance)
- Inbound profile visits from ICP companies: LinkedIn company page analytics → visitor demographics → % from ICP company sizes/industries

TIER 3 — BUSINESS IMPACT KPIs (what it produces):
- LinkedIn-attributed website sessions: from UTM-tagged posts, tracked in GA4
- LinkedIn-influenced MQLs: website sessions from LinkedIn resulting in form fills (last-touch)
- LinkedIn-assisted pipeline: opportunities where LinkedIn was NOT last touch but appeared in journey (multi-touch)
- Self-reported LinkedIn influence: % of new pipeline where prospect named LinkedIn as awareness channel
- LinkedIn-influenced closed-won revenue: aggregate deal value from pipeline with confirmed LinkedIn touchpoint
- Dark social contribution estimate: modeled value of untracked LinkedIn influence (typically 3–8x direct attribution)

2.2 Hook Formula Performance Analysis

HOOK TAXONOMY:
- **Data Hook**: "67% of B2B SaaS CMOs say pipeline attribution is their #1 reporting challenge. Here's how to fix it."
  - Performance signal: high impressions (algorithm loves numeric claims), moderate engagement
- **Contrarian Hook**: "Stop obsessing over MQLs. Here's what actually predicts revenue."

SECTION 3 — DARK SOCIAL ATTRIBUTION ARCHITECTURE

3.1 The LinkedIn Dark Social Problem
LinkedIn organic content drives massive awareness that is structurally untrackable through standard web analytics:
- Prospects screenshot carousels and share in Slack channels without clicking any links
- Decision-makers read your CEO's post during their commute, then Google your company name 3 days later (shows as direct/organic search in GA4, with zero LinkedIn credit)
- SDR/AE cold outreach gets a response because prospect "had seen the company on LinkedIn" — not logged anywhere in CRM
- Deals close faster because the buying committee had been consuming LinkedIn content for months — but no attribution exists

Estimate: for every 1 pipeline deal where LinkedIn gets direct attribution credit, 3–8 additional deals were influenced by LinkedIn where the platform receives zero credit. This is the dark social multiplier.

3.2 Self-Reported Attribution Implementation
Build the infrastructure to capture dark social influence through intentional question design:

FORM-LEVEL SELF-REPORTED ATTRIBUTION:
Add to all demo request, trial signup, and content download forms:
- "How did you first hear about [Company]?" — open text OR multi-select including "LinkedIn post," "LinkedIn ad," "saw a LinkedIn post from a colleague," "saw an executive's LinkedIn content"
- "What made you reach out now?" — open text capturing recency trigger
- Tag all self-reported LinkedIn responses in CRM with field: sr_linkedin_attributed = TRUE

SALES DISCOVERY QUESTIONS:
Train AEs to ask during discovery call:
- "Before we connected, what did you know about [Company]?"
- "Had you seen any of our LinkedIn content before this call?"
- "Did anyone in your network share content from us that got you interested?"

Log affirmative responses in CRM custom field: "LinkedIn influenced" = YES/NO/UNKNOWN
Calculate: % of closed-won pipeline with LinkedIn influence flag / % of open pipeline with LinkedIn influence flag

POST-CLOSE WIN SURVEYS:
Email to every closed-won deal within 7 days:
- "In your evaluation process, what sources helped you learn about [Company]? (Select all that apply)" — include LinkedIn as option
- "Which person or piece of content first made [Company] credible to you?"

SECTION 5 — LINKEDIN ALGORITHM OPTIMIZATION INTELLIGENCE

5.1 Algorithm Performance Signals
LinkedIn's content distribution algorithm optimizes for "dwell time" and "meaningful engagement" — not raw likes. Decode which signals move the algorithm for your content:

ALGORITHM PRIORITY SIGNALS (ranked by weight):
1. **Early engagement velocity**: reactions + comments within first 60–90 minutes of posting drive 60–70% of total impressions. Track your average time-to-first-10-engagements — under 30 minutes is the algorithm sweet spot.
2. **Comment-to-like ratio**: posts with high comment rates get 2–4x more distribution than high-like posts with few comments. Target >1 comment per 10 reactions.
3. **Connection vs. follower engagement**: first-degree connections engaging (liking, commenting) drive broader reach into second-degree networks. Build an internal engagement pod of team members who engage authentically within first 30 minutes.
4. **Profile authority score** (estimated): accounts with high recent engagement history get broader initial distribution. Consistent daily posting for 60+ days elevates your "creator authority" baseline.
5. **Dwell time signals**: LinkedIn measures scroll-stop behavior — how long a user pauses on your post before scrolling. Native content (carousels, documents, videos) drives higher dwell time than external link posts.

5.2 Algorithm Performance Diagnostic
For each post in the last 90 days, calculate the "Algorithm Efficiency Score":
- Formula: (actual impressions) / (expected impressions based on follower count × baseline reach rate)
  - Ratio >1.5 = algorithm amplified = high quality signal content
  - Ratio 0.8–1.5 = average algorithm treatment
  - Ratio <0.8 = algorithm suppressed = content quality signal, format issue, or early engagement failure

Identify the top 10 "algorithm amplified" posts and decode the common variables: content theme, hook type, format, publish time, early engagement pattern. These are your "algorithm sweet spot" content formulas.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn Analytics shows high impressions but GA4 shows minimal LinkedIn organic traffic**
Cause: Most LinkedIn engagement (saves, reactions, comments, shares) happens without clicking links. LinkedIn organic is primarily a brand and awareness channel — direct click-through rates of 0.5–2% are normal.
Fix: Do not evaluate LinkedIn organic solely on click-through. Add the self-reported attribution layer and CRM LinkedIn influence flag to capture the dark social pipeline influence that doesn't produce clicks. Calculate "LinkedIn organic assisted" conversions in GA4 (paths where LinkedIn was not last touch but appeared in 90-day journey) — this is typically 4–8x higher than last-touch conversion count.

**Problem: ICP follower match rate is stuck below 20% despite consistent posting**
Cause: Content is attracting general-interest audiences rather than ICP professionals. Common reasons: content themes too broad (general marketing/business content), hook language not specific to ICP pain points, posting at wrong times for ICP timezone.
Fix: Run a 30-day ICP-specific content experiment: every post for 30 days explicitly names the ICP role and pain point in the first 2 lines ("If you're a VP Operations managing field service teams…"). Use ICP-specific jargon and quantify outcomes in ICP-familiar metrics ($X saved per technician, X% fewer missed appointments). Monitor ICP follower acquisition rate — expect 15–30% improvement within 60 days.

**Problem: Executive LinkedIn program has high engagement but zero traceable pipeline**
Cause: Missing UTM infrastructure + no self-reported attribution collection + CRM not capturing LinkedIn source data. Executive content typically drives dark social influence that is structurally invisible without intentional measurement.
Fix: Implement the full Tier 1/2/3 attribution stack (Section 3.2). Critically: train sales team to ask "Had you seen any of our LinkedIn content?" in every discovery call and log in CRM. Run a 90-day experiment: compare deal velocity (days from first touch to close) for deals with LinkedIn touch vs. without — LinkedIn-touched deals typically close 15–25% faster because social proof and thought leadership pre-build credibility.
