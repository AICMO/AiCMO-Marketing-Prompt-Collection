## Overview
Transforms raw affiliate program data into a complete performance management system that identifies which partners drive quality pipeline versus vanity clicks, surfaces commission structure inefficiencies, and produces a ranked action plan to double affiliate-sourced revenue without increasing total payout. Use monthly for program health reviews and quarterly for partner tier restructuring and budget reallocation decisions.

## Integration Tips

- **PartnerStack / Impact.com → Google Sheets:** Export partner performance CSVs monthly and use this prompt with the raw data pasted directly. For highest fidelity, export at the conversion level (not aggregate) so the AI can do cohort-level customer quality analysis.
- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Create a custom field "Affiliate Source" on Contact and Opportunity objects that maps to the partner name from your affiliate platform. This enables direct comparison of affiliate-sourced customer ACV and churn vs. other channels — paste the exported CRM query output into the advanced prompt.
- **PostHog / Mixpanel / Amplitude:** Pull 30-day and 90-day product activation rates by acquisition source to add a "product engagement quality" dimension to your partner scorecard — this reveals whether affiliate-sourced customers are power users or ghost accounts.
- **Stripe / Chargebee:** Export MRR cohort data filtered by affiliate source tag to track actual revenue retention curves — the most definitive measure of affiliate customer quality beyond the 30-day churn snapshot.
- **Slack / Notion:** Paste the 200-word executive summary from each monthly run into your affiliate program Notion page and share to the #marketing-ops Slack channel to keep stakeholders current without requiring them to read the full report.
- **Zapier / Make.com:** Automate monthly PartnerStack data export → Google Sheets → Slack notification with the affiliate manager as a recurring workflow triggered on the 1st of each month.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My affiliate platform data and CRM conversion data don't match — the numbers are different for the same period.**
Solution: This is attribution window and deduplication mismatch, not data corruption. Your affiliate platform counts conversions based on cookie click date; your CRM counts by deal close date. Align on a single date field before analysis — deal close date is the gold standard. Also check whether your CRM deduplicates contacts (a trial that converts to paid is one record; your affiliate platform may count both the trial and the paid conversion separately). Always normalize to "new ARR added" as the single conversion metric for revenue analysis.

**Problem: I have too many affiliates with zero or near-zero conversions — I can't tell who's genuinely dormant vs. who's still building audience for a future promo.**
Solution: Segment dormant partners into three buckets: (1) Never converted — recruited but never drove a trial: send reactivation sequence with new creative assets and 60-day timer; (2) Previously converted but inactive 90+ days — reach out for a quarterly check-in and ask specifically what would help them promote again (usually it's a new hook, case study, or promotional incentive); (3) High-click, zero-conversion — these may be audience mismatches or tracking failures. Pull coupon code redemption data to check if conversions are happening but being attributed incorrectly.

**Problem: The AI recommends offboarding a partner who has a personal relationship with our founder or VP.**
Solution: Flag the relationship context in your input data using the "Notes" field (e.g., "Longtime friend of CEO — relationship has strategic value beyond program metrics"). The prompt will still give you the objective performance data, but the tier assignment should be weighed against relationship value you can quantify (access to their audience for co-marketing, brand association, etc.). If you decide to maintain the partnership despite poor metrics, note it as a "strategic partner" category outside the standard tier system and set explicit non-revenue relationship KPIs.
