## Overview

Designs and executes a comprehensive analytics and measurement system for B2B SaaS thought leader and micro-influencer partnership programs, transforming the dark social attribution challenge into a defensible pipeline attribution model that links creator-driven content directly to qualified pipeline and closed revenue. Use when your B2B SaaS company has invested $30K–$500K+ in LinkedIn creator partnerships, newsletter sponsorships, niche podcast placements, or micro-influencer collaborations and lacks a rigorous framework to prove what's driving pipeline; when your CFO demands creator marketing ROI beyond reach and impressions; or when you need to optimize your influencer partner mix by identifying which creators produce qualified ICP buyers vs. unqualified noise that clogs your demo queue.

2.1 INDIVIDUAL CREATOR SCORECARD METHODOLOGY

For each creator, calculate and track monthly:

Audience Quality Index (AQI) — 40% of overall score:
- Export LinkedIn follower data for each creator (request via creator or use Phantombuster/Taplio)

- AQI = average score across 200 sampled followers ÷ 40 × 100 (produces 0–100 index)
- Minimum threshold before allocating >$5K/month to creator: AQI ≥ 65

Pipeline Conversion Rate (PCR) — 40% of overall score:
- PCR = (Demo requests attributed to creator via all 3 methods) ÷ (Estimated ICP audience reach of creator content about Company) × 100
- Estimate ICP reach: Total impressions on creator content × creator's AQI score
- Benchmark: 0.1%–0.8% PCR is typical range for B2B creator content; 0.5%+ is strong

Content Performance Score (CPS) — 20% of overall score:
- For each creator content piece about Company, track: saves-to-impressions ratio (target: 1%+), comment quality score (manually rate 10 comments: are they from ICPs asking specific questions vs. generic "great post!" engagement), share-to-like ratio (shares signal content people want their network to see)
- CPS = saves rate × 50% + comment quality score × 30% + share rate × 20%, normalized to 0–100

Overall Creator Score = AQI × 0.40 + PCR-score × 0.40 + CPS × 0.20

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Self-reported attribution survey response rate is below 30%, making creator attribution data statistically unreliable.
**Solution:** Move survey timing from post-booking email to beginning of the discovery call itself. Have the SDR/AE ask verbally: "Before we dive in — how did you first hear about us? I want to prep the right context." This approach consistently achieves 75–80% response rates because it frames the question as research for the prospect's benefit, not marketing data collection. Train reps to manually enter responses in CRM during the call via a HubSpot sidebar field or Salesforce Lightning component.

**Problem:** CFO or VP Finance challenges the dark social correction factors as "made-up numbers" and questions whether creator-attributed pipeline is real.
**Solution:** Run a 90-day manual validation study: Personally call 50 random "unknown source" inbound leads from the past quarter and ask how they found you (no survey, direct call). Compare the actual distribution to your model predictions. If your model predicted 22% creator-influenced and manual verification shows 19%, your model is within acceptable range and you can present the validation as a documented methodology audit. Also prepare the negative control argument: show CFO what the alternative is — ignoring $14K/month in spend with zero measurement — vs. a probabilistic attribution model with documented validation methodology.

**Problem:** A creator has high reach and strong engagement metrics but generates zero qualified demo requests despite 6 months of partnership.
**Solution:** This is almost always an audience mismatch issue, not a content quality issue. Immediately run an emergency AQI audit: sample 50 people who liked or commented on the creator's posts about your product and check their LinkedIn profiles against your ICP definition. If AQI is below 50 (fewer than half the audience matches your ICP), pause the partnership regardless of vanity metrics. Redirect budget to creators in narrow trade publications your ICP actually reads — a creator with 3,000 highly-targeted subscribers in your exact ICP vertical will outperform a creator with 300,000 followers in a tangentially related niche every time.
