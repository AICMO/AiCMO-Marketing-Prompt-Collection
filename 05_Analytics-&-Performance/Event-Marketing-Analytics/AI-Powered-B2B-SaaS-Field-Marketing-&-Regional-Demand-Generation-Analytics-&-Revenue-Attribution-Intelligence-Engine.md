## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CRM pipeline attribution is inconsistent across territories — some AEs tag field events, others don't, making territory comparisons unreliable.**
Solution: Use a two-stage attribution recovery method before running this analysis. First, pull raw attendee lists from your event platform and match against CRM contacts by email (direct match) and company domain (fuzzy match). Second, filter CRM opportunities created or advanced within 90 days where the primary contact appears on any attendee list — this surfaces pipeline that bad AE tagging missed. Separately, run an AE survey asking them to flag deals where a field event played a significant role; this catches verbal conversations that weren't logged. Document the data quality gap in each territory's analysis section and use recovered figures as the analysis baseline. Fix CRM tagging protocol before next quarter by requiring campaign source field completion as a mandatory Salesforce field for all event-sourced leads.

**Problem: The analysis recommends cutting a flagship event that the regional VP of Sales considers a critical relationship-building touchpoint, even though pipeline ROI is poor.**
Solution: Separate pipeline ROI from relationship equity in your output. Explicitly model two scenarios: (A) current sponsorship format at full cost with its observed pipeline output, and (B) a relationship-only format (cocktail hour, speaking slot, or hospitality event co-located with the conference at 25–35% of the cost) that maintains executive relationship value without the lead generation overhead. Most field executives will accept the hybrid model when they see they can preserve relationships at a fraction of the cost while reallocating the rest to higher-ROI formats. Present the reallocation story — not just what you're cutting, but specifically what $X goes to and what pipeline it generates instead.

**Problem: Territory pipeline coverage is critically low but field budget is already fully allocated — no room to add programs.**
Solution: Rather than adding budget, recommend a format shift within the same budget. High-cost, low-ICP-density events (large conference sponsorships, broad audience roadshows) consume 2–4x the budget per SQL compared to intimate, invitation-only formats. Identify the highest-cost, lowest-ROI-score program in the affected territory and model what the same budget produces in the highest-ROI format (typically executive dinners or targeted roundtables). The math usually shows that replacing one underperforming $40K program with four $10K executive dinners would generate 3–5x the pipeline in the same territory at identical spend. Frame this as a program mix optimization, not a budget cut.
