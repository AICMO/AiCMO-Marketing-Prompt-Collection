**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25 min | **Tags:** trade-show, event-analytics, lead-attribution, exhibitor-roi, b2b, pipeline-intelligence

## Overview
Transforms raw trade show lead data — badge scans, booth conversations, demo requests — into a fully attributed revenue intelligence system that tells you exactly which shows drive pipeline, which leads convert, and whether your $50K+ booth investment is worth repeating. Use this whenever you exhibit at a third-party industry conference and need to calculate actual ROI, not just lead count.

DELIVERABLE 6: ROI CALCULATION & GO/NO-GO FRAMEWORK

A. SHOW ROI CALCULATION FORMULA

**Cost Efficiency Metrics:**
- Cost Per Lead (CPL) = Total Show Investment ÷ Total Leads Captured
- Cost Per Qualified Lead (CPQL) = Total Show Investment ÷ Hot + Warm Leads
- Cost Per Meeting = Total Show Investment ÷ Completed Meetings
- Cost Per Pipeline Dollar = Total Show Investment ÷ Pipeline Sourced from Show (at Day 90)

**Pipeline ROI:**
- Expected Revenue from Show = (Hot Leads × Historical Win Rate × ACV) + (Warm Leads × (Historical Win Rate × 0.4) × ACV)
- Projected Show ROI = (Expected Revenue − Show Investment) ÷ Show Investment × 100
- Break-Even Leads Needed = Show Investment ÷ (ACV × Win Rate)

**Efficiency Benchmarks (B2B SaaS industry standards):**
- CPL: < $200 (small shows), < $500 (mid-size), < $1,000 (large enterprise shows like AWS re:Invent, Dreamforce)
- Cost per Meeting: < $1,500 for mid-market deals; < $5,000 for enterprise
- Pipeline Multiple: Target 5-10x show investment in pipeline within 90 days
- Show-to-pipeline conversion: Target 8-15% of total leads converting to qualified opportunities

B. GO/NO-GO SCORING MODEL FOR REPEAT EXHIBITING
Score the show across 6 dimensions (1-5 scale each, 30 points max):
1. ICP fit of attendees (% of scans matching exact ICP): Poor (1) → Excellent (5)
2. Pipeline multiple achieved (pipeline ÷ investment): <2x (1) → >8x (5)
3. Competitive presence (best shows have weak competitor coverage): Heavy (1) → Light (5)
4. Deal quality (ACV of show-sourced deals vs. company average): Lower (1) → Higher (5)
5. Account progression rate (% of show contacts that advance in pipeline): <5% (1) → >20% (5)
6. Brand awareness signal (social mentions, booth traffic, press engagement): Low (1) → High (5)

**Decision thresholds:**
- Score 24-30: Increase booth size and investment next year
- Score 18-23: Repeat at same investment level with tactical improvements
- Score 12-17: Pilot a smaller presence (sponsor vs. exhibit) next year
- Score <12: Do not exhibit; consider attending only (no booth)

## Integration Tips

- **Badge scan apps (Cvent LeadCapture, Validar, CompuSystems):** Export lead data as CSV within 2 hours of each show day closing. Map fields to your CRM contact schema before import. Most enterprise shows provide API access — use this to push leads directly to HubSpot or Salesforce in real time rather than waiting for the post-show CSV.
- **Salesforce:** Create a dedicated Campaign for the show (type: Trade Show). Use Campaign Member Status field with custom values: Scanned / Demo / Meeting / Opportunity / Closed Won. This enables native Salesforce attribution reporting without custom objects.
- **HubSpot:** Use the Events integration or a custom property "Event Source" on the Contact record. Build a workflow triggered by this property to auto-enroll in the correct post-show sequence and assign to the right SDR by territory.
- **Clay.com:** Build a Clay table post-show to enrich all leads in bulk — pull LinkedIn data, Apollo firmographics, and funding signals in one pass. Export enriched data back to CRM. Can process 300 leads in under 30 minutes.
- **Apollo.io / ZoomInfo:** Use bulk enrichment API post-show to fill firmographic gaps. Set up an automatic "enrichment on import" rule so every new contact from a show source gets enriched immediately.
- **LinkedIn Matched Audiences:** Upload your show lead list to LinkedIn as a matched audience within 48 hours. Retarget with customer case studies and ROI-focused ads for 30 days post-show. This provides a warm digital touchpoint between SDR calls.
- **Chili Piper:** Embed smart meeting links in all Hot lead follow-up emails. Chili Piper auto-routes to the correct AE based on territory/account ownership, eliminating the friction of back-and-forth scheduling.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Badge scan data quality is poor — many leads have only an email and company name, missing title and firmographic data.**
Solution: Build an enrichment-first workflow before scoring. Push all badge scans through Apollo.io or Clay within 2 hours post-show. Set a minimum enrichment threshold: if title, company size, and industry cannot be enriched from any source, flag as "unqualified — data incomplete" and skip active sequences. Re-engage these contacts at 90 days with a simple "let me make sure I have the right information" personalization request. For future shows, add two qualifying questions to your badge scan app: "What's your primary role?" and "What's your biggest challenge with [category] right now?" — these 30 seconds dramatically improve lead quality.

**Problem: Sales team doesn't follow up on Hot leads within the required timeframe — show leads go cold.**
Solution: Build urgency and accountability into the process before the show starts. Get sales leadership to commit to a 4-hour SLA for Hot leads before the event. Set up a real-time Slack channel (#show-name-leads) where every Hot lead badge scan triggers an automatic notification to the assigned SDR with the lead's score, company, and conversation notes. At 4 hours, if no follow-up logged, escalate to SDR manager via Slack. After the show, include "Hot lead response rate" in the weekly sales performance dashboard for 30 days. Visibility creates accountability faster than process alone.

**Problem: It's impossible to attribute revenue to the show because deals that started at the event close 6-12 months later and the show connection gets lost.**
Solution: Set the show as "Campaign Influenced" at opportunity creation — not just first touch. In Salesforce, add the show's Campaign to the Opportunity's Campaign influence record. In HubSpot, use the "Campaigns" attribution object. Train your SDR/AE team to always note "met at [Show Name]" in the opportunity description field. Build a saved Salesforce report: All opportunities where Contact's first activity was within 14 days of the show, OR where the Contact is a Campaign Member of the show's Campaign. Run this report quarterly. You'll recover attribution for long-cycle deals that would otherwise appear as organic pipeline.
