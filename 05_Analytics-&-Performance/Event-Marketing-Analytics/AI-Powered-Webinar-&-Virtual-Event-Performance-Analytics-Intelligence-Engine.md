### Analysis Framework

**Section 1: Full-Funnel Conversion Analysis**
Map every conversion stage against benchmarks. Use traffic-light scoring (Green = at/above benchmark, Yellow = 10-20% below, Red = >20% below). Calculate:
- Invite-to-open rate vs. program average
- Landing page conversion rate vs. industry (20-35% is strong for webinar reg pages)
- Registration-to-attendance rate vs. program average and industry benchmark
- Attendance duration score (% who stayed 80%+ of the event)
- Engagement rate (% of attendees who took any action: poll, chat, Q&A, CTA)
- Attendee-to-MQL conversion rate
- MQL-to-SQL conversion rate
- Cost per MQL, cost per SQL, cost per opportunity, projected cost per closed-won

Identify the single biggest conversion leak in the funnel and quantify the revenue impact of fixing it.

**Section 2: Audience Quality Assessment**
Score the audience on four dimensions:
- *ICP Fit Score* (0-100): What % of registrants and attendees match ideal customer profile? Break down by title, company size, industry.
- *Buying Stage Mix*: Estimated distribution across Awareness / Consideration / Decision stages based on engagement behaviors
- *Existing Relationship Quality*: What % are cold net-new, warm (known contact, no opportunity), active prospect, or customer?
- *Engagement Depth Score*: Weighted score combining attendance duration (40%), active engagement (30%), CTA behavior (30%)

Flag audience quality problems: if >40% of registrants are students, competitors, job seekers, or non-ICP personas, identify the source and recommend list hygiene measures.

**Section 3: Content Performance Diagnosis**
Identify specific content effectiveness signals:
- At which minute did the sharpest drop-off occur? What was happening in the content at that point?
- Which poll results reveal audience knowledge level or buying stage?
- What Q&A questions reveal objections, confusion, or high-intent interest?
- Did engagement accelerate or decelerate during product demos?
- How did this topic/format/speaker combination perform vs. historical program data?

Produce a Content Quality Score (0-100) using: audience resonance (poll/chat engagement), information density calibration (drop-off timing), CTA response rate, and post-webinar replay demand.

**Section 4: Revenue Attribution & Projection**
Calculate:
- *Direct pipeline*: opportunities opened within 30 days from attendee list
- *Influenced pipeline*: existing open opportunities in audience — estimate deal velocity acceleration (industry benchmark: webinar-influenced deals close 20-35% faster)
- *On-demand pipeline tail*: project 90-day on-demand view volume using 30-day trend, apply attendee-to-MQL rate with 50% discount for on-demand vs. live
- *Total program contribution*: sum all three, calculate ROI (pipeline created ÷ total cost)

**Section 5: Optimization Roadmap**
Generate 5 prioritized test recommendations. For each:
- Hypothesis: "If we change [X], we expect [Y metric] to improve by [Z%] because [reason]"
- Test method: A/B test title, change time slot, modify format, adjust CTA placement, revise follow-up sequence timing
- Effort level: Low / Medium / High
- Expected impact: Low / Medium / High
- Metric to track: Specific KPI that confirms the test worked

**Section 6: Series Health Signal**
If this is a recurring series:
- Registrant growth rate trend (MoM): [Healthy: +5% / Warning: flat / Critical: declining]
- Audience overlap across webinars: [% of registrants who attended a previous webinar in the series]
- Engagement trend: Is average attendance duration and engagement rate improving, stable, or declining?
- Topic fatigue indicators: Are high-performing topic categories becoming saturated?
- Recommendation: Maintain cadence / Reduce cadence / Pivot topics / Refresh format / Sunset series

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Pull webinar registration and attendance data via Zoom Webinars or ON24 native integration; create a webinar enrollment workflow that stamps contact records with engagement score (duration tier + actions taken)
- Build a Smart List of "Webinar Engaged" contacts (attended 60%+ AND took ≥1 action) for prioritized sales enrollment sequences
- Use HubSpot's "Activities" timeline to surface Q&A questions and poll responses directly on contact records for sales context

**Salesforce + Marketo:**
- Create a Marketo Program for each webinar; sync engagement scores as a custom field to Salesforce Contact
- Build a SFDC Campaign hierarchy: Webinar Series (parent) → Individual Webinar (child) → Member statuses: Registered / Attended / No-Show / Engaged / MQL
- Use Marketo's Revenue Cycle Analytics to track webinar program's contribution to pipeline stage-by-stage

**Goldcast / ON24:**
- Export the engagement analytics CSV (attention score, poll responses, resource clicks, CTA interactions) immediately post-event
- Use ON24's Intelligence module or Goldcast's Insights to auto-generate attendee heat maps before running this prompt — feed that data directly into Section 3

**Google Sheets / Looker Studio:**
- Create a Webinar Program Dashboard tracking: registrants, attendance rate, MQL rate, cost per MQL, pipeline contribution — rolling 12 months
- Build a Looker Studio connector pulling from HubSpot or Salesforce to auto-populate the funnel conversion scorecard weekly

**Zapier Automation:**
- Trigger: Webinar ends in Zoom → Zap exports attendee list to Google Sheets → HubSpot workflow enrolled → Sales Slack alert for hot leads (engagement score ≥ 75)
- Follow-up automation: No-show registrants → 3-email on-demand sequence starting 2 hours post-event

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Attendance rate is consistently below 30%, dragging down MQL numbers even when registrant count is healthy.
**Fix:** Three primary causes — wrong time slot (test Tuesday 11am ET and Thursday 10am PT as highest B2B attendance windows), lead time too long (4+ weeks between registration and event increases no-show; ideal is 7-10 days), or topic mismatch between registration hook and webinar delivery (registrants feel misled). Add a "mark your calendar" reminder 48 hours before and a "1 hour to go" text/email reminder — these alone recover 8-12% attendance rate.

**Problem:** Engagement data (polls, Q&A) is available but pipeline/MQL data is missing or delayed, making ROI analysis impossible.
**Fix:** Run the analysis in two passes. Pass 1 (same day): Score each attendee on engagement behavior alone using the Engagement Depth Score formula in Section 2. Pass 2 (14 days post-event): Layer in MQL and opportunity data from CRM once follow-up sequences have run. Most webinar-sourced MQLs surface within 7-14 days of the event; waiting for 30-day data before analyzing loses the optimization window for your next webinar.

**Problem:** The prompt produces generic recommendations ("improve your title") instead of specific test hypotheses tied to your data.
**Fix:** The richer your input data, the more specific the output. Minimum required for specific recommendations: registration page CVR, attendance rate, average duration %, engagement rate (at least one signal), and at least 1 outcome metric (MQLs, demos, or pipeline). If you don't have CRM-linked outcomes yet, substitute the "# of hot leads surfaced to sales" and "sales follow-up rate" as proxy pipeline indicators.
