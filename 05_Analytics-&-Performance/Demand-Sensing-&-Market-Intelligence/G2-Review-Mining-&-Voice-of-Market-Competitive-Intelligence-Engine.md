ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:

**Step 1 — JTBD Extraction**
For each competitor, identify:
- Functional JTBD: What job customers hired this product to do
- Emotional JTBD: What identity/status outcome buyers expected
- Social JTBD: How it was supposed to make them look to their teams/leadership
- Where the product fails to deliver on each JTBD dimension

**Step 2 — Sentiment Stratification**
Segment reviews into:
- Promoters (4-5 stars): What's working, which segments are thriving
- Passives (3 stars): What keeps them subscribed despite issues
- Detractors (1-2 stars): Root causes, churn triggers, competitive alternatives mentioned

**Step 3 — Switching Moment Analysis**
Using Clay Christensen's disruption theory, identify:
- "Hired for" moments: What triggered the original purchase decision
- "Fired" moments: The specific event that caused churn or consideration of alternatives
- Non-consumption segments: Who gave up and did nothing because no product served them

**Step 4 — Pain Point Frequency Mapping**

- Tier 1 (>30% of reviews mention): Existential product weaknesses
- Tier 2 (10-30% mention): Recurring friction points
- Tier 3 (<10% mention): Edge case issues
- Pain severity score (1-10) based on emotional intensity of language

**Step 5 — Competitive Displacement Playbook**

- Draft a 2-sentence positioning statement my team can use
- Identify the exact review language to mirror in outreach (language matching)
- Specify which ICP segment is most affected and most displaceable

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem:** You only have 10-15 reviews per competitor, making patterns unreliable.
  **Solution:** Supplement with Reddit threads (search "[Competitor Name] problems" or "[Competitor Name] vs" on Reddit), LinkedIn posts from users complaining about the tool, and Twitter/X mentions. Clearly label these as qualitative signals with lower confidence weighting. Even 5 Reddit threads can surface Tier 1 pain points that G2 reviewers are too polished to say directly.

- **Problem:** Reviews are mostly 4-5 stars and don't surface real pain points.
  **Solution:** Filter specifically for 3-star reviews — these are the most analytically valuable because reviewers are ambivalent enough to be honest about both positives and negatives without the emotional bias of 1-star rage or 5-star superfan reviews. Also search for "[Competitor] alternatives" or "switched from [Competitor]" on Reddit and LinkedIn.

- **Problem:** The output is too long and sales won't read it.
  **Solution:** Ask the AI to generate a separate "Sales Quick Reference Card" — a single-page version with only Section 5 (battle card snippets, discovery questions, objection scripts) formatted for fast consumption. Keep the full report for marketing and product leadership.
