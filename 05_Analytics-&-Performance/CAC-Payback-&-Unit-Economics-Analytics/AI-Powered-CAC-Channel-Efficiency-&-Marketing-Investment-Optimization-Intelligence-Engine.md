## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't have channel-level CAC data — I only know total spend and total customers."**
Fix: Start with blended CAC analysis using Section 1 only. For channel breakdown, use UTM parameter reports in Google Analytics 4 (Acquisition → Traffic Acquisition) to estimate customer volume by source, then allocate spend proportionally from your budget tracker. Even rough estimates will reveal the highest-priority channels to instrument properly.

**Problem: "The AI is giving me generic advice like 'invest in high-performing channels' without specifics."**
Fix: This happens when input data is incomplete. Ensure you provide: (1) specific spend amounts per channel, (2) specific new customers per channel, and (3) ACV or deal values by channel. The more specific the input, the more specific the output. If ACV varies by channel, this is critical data — channels that bring in larger deals can justify higher CAC.

**Problem: "My LTV:CAC ratios look off — some channels show 50x+ which seems unrealistic."**
Fix: Check your LTV calculation. If using ACV × gross margin ÷ churn rate, ensure your churn rate is monthly (not annual) and that gross margin is correctly applied. Also confirm you're using channel-specific retention rates, not blended — high-ACV enterprise customers acquired via outbound often have better retention than SMB customers from paid social, which dramatically changes LTV per channel.
