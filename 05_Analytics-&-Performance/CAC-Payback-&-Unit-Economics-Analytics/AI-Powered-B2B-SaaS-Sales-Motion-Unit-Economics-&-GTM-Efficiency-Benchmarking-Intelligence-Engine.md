## Overview
Compares the full unit economics profile (CAC, LTV, payback, NRR, contribution margin) of every sales motion your company runs — PLG/self-serve, Inside Sales/velocity, Mid-Market, Enterprise/field, and Channel Partner — then generates a data-driven recommendation for which motions to invest in, which to restructure, and which ACV thresholds make each motion economically rational. Use this when you're debating whether to add an enterprise motion, kill your SMB self-serve, rationalize your channel program, or justify your GTM investment mix to a board skeptical about growth efficiency.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't track sales motions as separate categories — all deals look the same in my CRM."**
Fix: Retroactively segment your last 12 months of closed-won deals by these proxy signals: (1) Lead source = product sign-up/freemium → PLG; (2) Lead source = outbound/SDR sequence → Inside Sales; (3) ACV > $50K + sales cycle > 60 days → Enterprise; (4) Lead source = partner referral → Channel. Use this segmentation to estimate per-motion metrics before investing in proper motion tagging in your CRM. Your CFO will want clean data within 90 days — set up the GTM Motion field now.

**Problem: "The AI recommends shutting down our PLG motion, but our product requires a free trial to convert. What do I do?"**
Fix: The prompt is measuring PLG as a standalone revenue motion. If PLG's primary value is as a top-of-funnel awareness and trial channel feeding your Inside Sales or Enterprise motion, re-run Section 2 treating PLG customers who convert to Sales-Assisted as the relevant cohort — and attribute PLG CAC only to those conversions. PLG's NRR (94%) is the real diagnostic: a PLG motion that creates low-NRR customers is not compounding your ARR efficiently, even if it generates trial volume.

**Problem: "My enterprise motion has great unit economics (6x+ LTV:CAC) but we're only closing 6 deals a quarter — it doesn't feel scalable."**
Fix: This is a capacity constraint, not a unit economics problem. Run Section 6 (Reallocation Plan) to calculate how many additional enterprise AEs you can hire before marginal CAC degrades (typically as win rates drop with AE ramp time). The key question is: at what headcount does your enterprise AE win rate start declining? For most B2B SaaS companies, enterprise capacity scales efficiently up to 3–5 AEs per market segment before needing a dedicated SDR team and field marketing support. Model that investment sequence before concluding enterprise is unscalable.
