## Overview
Calculates the full suite of SaaS growth efficiency metrics — Burn Multiple, Magic Number, Rule of 40, GTM Productivity, and Revenue per FTE — then generates a board-ready efficiency narrative and a prioritized roadmap to hit investor-grade benchmarks. Use this when preparing for a fundraise, a board meeting, or when diagnosing why growth is burning more cash than it should.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** "I don't have clean quarterly S&M data — my expenses are blended across teams."
*Solution:* Ask the prompt to estimate using total OpEx and known headcount ratios. Provide: total OpEx, total headcount by function (Sales, Marketing, R&D, G&A), and average fully-loaded cost per employee. The prompt will allocate spend by headcount ratio and flag it as an [ASSUMPTION] — accurate enough for directional benchmarking, but note that a proper calculation requires clean departmental P&L.

**Problem:** "My Rule of 40 score is very negative and I'm worried this makes us look bad."
*Solution:* Ask the prompt for a "growth-stage adjusted Rule of 40" interpretation. Sub-$15M ARR companies are typically evaluated on a modified basis where investor emphasis shifts toward growth rate trajectory rather than the blended score. Include the Section 3 bear case output in your prep and have a ready response for the specific objection — the prompt will draft it for you.

**Problem:** "The Magic Number calculation gives very different results quarter-over-quarter due to seasonality."
*Solution:* Use a trailing-4-quarter average for both inputs rather than a single quarter. Ask the prompt to calculate both the single-quarter Magic Number and the T4Q average, and use the T4Q average as the primary benchmark figure for investor conversations. Seasonal spikes in Q4 enterprise closings or Q1 reset effects can distort single-quarter reads significantly.
