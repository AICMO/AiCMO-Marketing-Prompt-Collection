## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Attribution data conflicts — TikTok Shop and my Shopify dashboard show different GMV numbers.**
Solution: TikTok Shop reports GMV at order placement time; Shopify reports at fulfillment. Export both reports for the same date range, then reconcile using order IDs. Add a column for "cancel/refund adjustments" — net GMV is what matters for profitability analysis. Going forward, use Shopify's UTM tagging + TikTok Pixel to create a single source of truth.

**Problem: I have 80+ creator affiliates but can't identify which ones are actually driving incremental sales vs. capturing customers who would have bought anyway.**
Solution: Run a simple holdout test — for 30 days, pause your 10 lowest-GMV affiliates and track whether brand organic GMV increases (suggesting their link traffic was converting customers already in your funnel). Simultaneously, use your post-purchase survey (add the question "How did you hear about us?") to identify which creators are genuinely introducing new customers. Self-reported attribution from surveys combined with creator link click-to-purchase lag time (>3 days = likely incremental, <24 hours = potentially brand-aware) gives you a workable incrementality proxy without a formal holdout infrastructure.

**Problem: The prompt output is too generic — it doesn't reflect my category's benchmarks.**
Solution: Add this to the beginning of either prompt version: "My industry category is [beauty/fashion/home goods/etc.]. Industry benchmarks I want you to reference: TikTok Shop average CVR for [category] is [X%]; average creator commission rate is [X%]; average social commerce ROAS is [X]. Adjust all recommendations and flags relative to these category norms, not general e-commerce benchmarks." If you don't know your category benchmarks, run the Competitive Intelligence Engine first.
