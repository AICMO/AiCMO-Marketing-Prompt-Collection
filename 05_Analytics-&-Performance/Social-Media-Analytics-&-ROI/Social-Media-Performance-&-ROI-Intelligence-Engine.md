ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK — Execute each module:

MODULE 1: PLATFORM ROI SCORECARD
For each platform, calculate:
- Organic ROI: (Attributed Pipeline Value × Lead-to-Close Rate × ACV - Organic Labor Cost) / Organic Labor Cost × 100
- Paid ROI / ROAS: Revenue Attributed / Ad Spend
- Blended Social ROI per platform
- Earned Media Value: Total Impressions × Industry CPM benchmark (use $8-15 CPM for B2B LinkedIn, $4-7 for Meta, $2-5 for X/Twitter, $6-10 for YouTube)
- Verdict: Scale / Maintain / Reduce / Kill

MODULE 2: CONTENT PERFORMANCE INTELLIGENCE
Analyze content by format and topic:
- Engagement Rate by format: Video vs. Static vs. Carousel vs. Text-only
- Top 3 performing content themes (use whatever data is available; if sparse, use industry benchmarks)
- Content-to-pipeline correlation: Which content types appear in the attribution path of converted leads?
- Content decay analysis: Average half-life by format (LinkedIn posts ~24-48h, YouTube videos ~months)
- B2B benchmarks: >3% ER = high-performing | <0.5% = needs optimization
- B2C benchmarks: >6% ER = high-performing | <1% = needs optimization

MODULE 3: SHARE OF VOICE & COMPETITIVE POSITION
- Estimate your SOV: Your Mentions / (Your Mentions + All Competitor Mentions) × 100
- Engagement SOV: Your Total Engagements / Total Category Engagements
- Qualitative competitive gaps: What content types are competitors using that you aren't?
- Binet & Field Insight: Brands with SOV > Market Share grow market share at ~0.5% per +1% SOV

MODULE 4: DARK SOCIAL & UNTRACKED ATTRIBUTION
Apply the Dark Social Correction Factor:
- If direct traffic > 25% of total traffic: attribute 30-45% of direct traffic to dark social (private shares via DM, Slack, WhatsApp, email forwards)
- If brand search volume increased during social campaign periods: attribute 20-35% of incremental brand searches to social influence
- Adjusted Total Social ROI = (Tracked Pipeline + Dark Social Estimate) - Total Investment / Total Investment × 100
- Flag this as "conservative" vs. "adjusted" estimate in reporting

MODULE 5: AUDIENCE QUALITY & ICP FIT SCORING
- Follower-to-engagement ratio (healthy B2B: >2% | healthy B2C: >4%)
- ICP audience penetration: What % of your followers match your ICP title/industry? (Use LinkedIn analytics demographics if available)
- Audience growth rate: Month-over-month follower growth. Benchmark: B2B 3-5%/month is healthy
- Engagement Quality Score: Weight comments (3x) > shares (2x) > saves (2x) > likes (1x) for a quality-adjusted metric

MODULE 6: PREDICTIVE RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on analysis, generate:
1. **Platform Bets** (where to allocate next quarter's budget with projected ROI improvement)
2. **Content Blueprint** (3 content series with format, cadence, and estimated pipeline impact)
3. **Quick Wins** (changes executable in <2 weeks with measurable impact in 30 days)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't have UTM-tracked pipeline data — social never gets credit in our CRM."**
Solution: Start immediately with UTM hygiene on all new posts. For historical credit estimation, use the Dark Social module with direct traffic analysis, and cross-reference social posting dates with MQL spikes in your CRM. Even 60 days of clean UTM data gives enough to build the attribution argument to your board.

**Problem: "The ROI numbers look too high / don't match finance's view of social value."**
Solution: Use the conservative (UTM-tracked only) ROI calculation in Section 2 for finance conversations, and present the dark social-adjusted number as "potential upside." Provide the formula transparency section explicitly — finance trusts math they can audit. Align on one agreed attribution model before the next QBR.

**Problem: "I have data for 6 platforms and the output is overwhelming."**
Solution: Run the prompt twice — first for your top 2 platforms by investment with full detail, then a second pass for remaining platforms using only Module 1 (ROI Scorecard). Combine into one report with a clear "primary channels" vs. "experimental channels" framing.
