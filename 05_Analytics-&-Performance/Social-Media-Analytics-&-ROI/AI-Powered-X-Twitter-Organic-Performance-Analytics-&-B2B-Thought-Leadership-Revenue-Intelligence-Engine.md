## Integration Tips

- **X Analytics:** Export monthly CSV from analytics.twitter.com — feed directly into the Quick Copy-Paste version. Focus on the "Top Tweets" tab sorted by profile clicks (not impressions) for pipeline-quality assessment.
- **Sprout Social / Hootsuite:** Use competitive benchmarking reports to pull share-of-voice data vs. competitors. Schedule posts and track link performance through built-in UTM builder.
- **HubSpot:** Create a custom contact property "Follows X/Twitter Accounts" (Yes/No) and an enrollment workflow: when any contact converts whose company domain matches a follower you've imported via X API, flag for sales with "X/Twitter Influenced" tag.
- **Salesforce:** Add "X/Twitter Influence" opportunity field. Train SDRs and AEs to ask "Did you follow us or our CEO on X/Twitter before reaching out?" as a standard discovery question. Log in notes; build a report.
- **Taplio / Typefully:** Use for CEO/executive account content scheduling. Both have built-in analytics that track thread completion rates and follower growth per post — far more granular than native X Analytics for thought leadership optimization.
- **Brandwatch / Mention.com:** Set up ICP-keyword monitoring to find conversations your buyers are having that you should join. Search for: product category keywords + job titles + pain point terms (e.g., "e-commerce attribution" + "VP" + "frustrated").
- **Google Search Console:** Cross-reference X/Twitter viral post dates against branded search volume spikes in GSC. This is your most reliable halo-effect proxy metric.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** X Analytics shows high impressions but almost no link clicks or pipeline conversion.
*Solution:* High impressions with low clicks typically indicate you're reaching the wrong audience (practitioners, peers, or bots) rather than active buyers. Run an audience quality audit: manually review the last 50 new followers for ICP match. If match rate is below 30%, your content is optimized for viral reach, not buyer resonance. Shift content mix from broad-appeal topics (marketing tactics, productivity tips) to category-specific, pain-point-forward content only buyers with an active problem would engage with (e.g., "Why your [specific vendor] data is wrong" or "The hidden cost of [specific workflow] that nobody talks about").

**Problem:** Executive account is growing but company account is flat or declining in engagement.
*Solution:* This is normal — B2B buyers follow people, not brands. Restructure company account strategy: use it exclusively for (1) amplifying executive content with company context, (2) customer proof and case studies, (3) product news with genuine value (not marketing fluff). Stop competing with executive accounts on thought leadership. The company account's primary job is converting trust built by the executive account into pipeline. Every company post should have a clear CTA; every executive post should have one subtle one.

**Problem:** Can't get reliable attribution data because X/Twitter's native analytics don't connect to CRM pipeline.
*Solution:* Implement a three-layer attribution workaround: (1) Add X/Twitter-specific UTM parameters to every link in bio, pinned post, and thread CTAs — track in GA4 and CRM. (2) Add "How did you first hear about us?" to all demo request forms with X/Twitter as an option. (3) Once per quarter, export your follower list via X API (free tier), match against CRM contacts by email/company domain, and tag matched contacts as "X/Twitter Follower" — then run opportunity overlap analysis. This triangulation will capture 80-90% of true X/Twitter influence even without native CRM integration.
