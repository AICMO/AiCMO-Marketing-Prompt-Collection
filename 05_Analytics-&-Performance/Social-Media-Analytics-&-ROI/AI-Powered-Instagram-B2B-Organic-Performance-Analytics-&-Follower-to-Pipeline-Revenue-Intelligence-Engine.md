## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Self-reported Instagram leads far exceed UTM-tracked MQLs (ratio > 3x), making attribution unreliable.**
Solution: Audit your link-in-bio architecture. If you have a single link pointing to your homepage (vs. a dedicated UTM-tagged landing page), you're losing tracking fidelity. Switch to a dedicated `instagram.yourcompany.com` subdomain that automatically appends UTM parameters to all downstream pages. Additionally, add a mandatory "How did you hear about us?" field with "Instagram" as a picklist option on all demo and contact forms.

**Problem: High reach and engagement on Reels but near-zero pipeline attribution — content is going viral with the wrong audience.**
Solution: Run an audience quality diagnostic. Check if follower demographics match your ICP (title, industry, company size). If engagement is disproportionately from personal accounts vs. business accounts, your content hooks are optimizing for general relatability rather than professional pain-point resonance. Shift from broad hook styles ("5 things that changed my life") to ICP-specific hooks ("5 signs your HRIS is costing you top talent in 2026") — expect reach to drop 30-40% initially but watch save rate, profile visits from target companies, and DM inquiry quality improve within 60 days.

**Problem: Strong content performance metrics but sales team says Instagram leads are low quality / wrong persona.**
Solution: Align on ICP definition with sales before the next content cycle. Map each content pillar to a specific buyer persona and seniority level. Request that sales CRM-stamp the job title of all Instagram-sourced leads for 60 days so you can identify the persona mismatch. Common fix: shift content from practitioner-level education (what appeals to individual contributors) to strategic/leadership-level frameworks (what appeals to VP/C-suite buyers who have budget authority). Content that generates saves from senior leaders tends to underperform on raw engagement metrics but dramatically outperforms on pipeline quality.
