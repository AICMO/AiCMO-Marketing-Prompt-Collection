## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I have almost no CRM attribution data — our UTM tracking is broken and we don't know which deals came from social."**
Solution: Start with three foundational data points that don't require CRM integration: (1) Add "How did you hear about us?" as a required field on all forms — even 30 days of self-reported data gives you Layer 1 soft attribution. (2) Use direct traffic as your dark social proxy immediately — if direct traffic grows when you post consistently, that's your attribution signal. (3) Run a two-week posting experiment: stop posting for week 1 (baseline), post heavily in week 2 — compare branded search, direct traffic, and demo request volume between weeks. This "on/off" test is the fastest way to isolate social's causal revenue impact when tracking is broken.

**Problem: "The ROI numbers seem too high — my CFO won't believe the dark social multiplier."**
Solution: Present the conservative Layer 1 only (hard UTM attribution) as your primary number in the CFO report. Then present dark social as a "what attribution science suggests" supplementary model, fully labeled as estimated. Use this framing: "Our directly measurable return is [X]x. Attribution research from SparkToro, DemandBase, and LinkedIn themselves consistently shows dark social multiplies measured social conversions by 3-7x in B2B — our actual ROI is almost certainly between these bookmarks." Credibility comes from showing your methodology and labeling your estimates clearly, not from inflating a single number.

**Problem: "Our social metrics look good (high engagement) but we can't see any pipeline impact — how do I explain the disconnect?"**
Solution: The gap between engagement and pipeline usually has one of four causes — diagnose which applies: (1) Wrong audience: high engagement from peers/competitors, not buyers — check LinkedIn follower job titles and post engagement demographics. (2) No conversion path: posts don't have clear CTAs or the landing pages post-click have high bounce rates — audit click-through destination quality. (3) Sales cycle lag: if your ACV is >$20K and sales cycle is >60 days, social posts from this quarter won't close until next quarter — present forward pipeline instead of closed revenue. (4) Attribution gap only: the revenue IS flowing through dark social but isn't tagged — implement UTM rigorously for 60 days and rerun this analysis before drawing conclusions.
