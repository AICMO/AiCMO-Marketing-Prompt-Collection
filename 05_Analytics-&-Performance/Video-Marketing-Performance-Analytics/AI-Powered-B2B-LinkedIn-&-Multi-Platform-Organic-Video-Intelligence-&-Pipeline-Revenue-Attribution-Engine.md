# ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK — Apply All Three

## Framework 1: Hook-Hold-Payoff Attribution Model
Analyze video content through three phases:
- HOOK (0-3 seconds): Which opening styles (statistic, story, controversy, question, demonstration) correlate with the highest completion rates and lowest drop-off?
- HOLD (3-90 seconds): Which content structures, pacing, and visual approaches keep viewers engaged past the 50% watch point?
- PAYOFF (CTA moment): Which CTAs (comment with a word, DM for resource, link to demo, like if you agree) generate measurable pipeline downstream?

## Framework 2: Video Funnel Hierarchy Mapping
Map all video content to buyer journey stages and analyze pipeline conversion rate by stage:
- TOFU (Awareness): POV/thought leadership, industry trend commentary, educational explainers
- MOFU (Consideration): Customer stories, product comparisons, ROI demonstrations, objection handling
- BOFU (Conversion): Implementation success stories, demo walkthroughs, peer validation, risk reversal content

Identify which funnel stage generates the highest pipeline velocity for [COMPANY NAME]'s [X]-month sales cycle.

## Framework 3: Three-Model Revenue Attribution
Calculate pipeline contribution using all three models to identify the true range of video impact:
- First-Touch Attribution: Full credit to video when it is the first tracked interaction before pipeline creation
- Linear Attribution: Equal credit distributed across all video touchpoints in the buyer's sequence
- Time-Decay Attribution: 50% weight to most recent video interaction, 25% to prior, 12.5% to one before that

Report all three numbers and recommend the most defensible model for [COMPANY NAME]'s sales cycle length.

## Framework 4: Video Content Scoring Matrix
Score each video on four dimensions (scale 1-10) to generate a composite "Revenue Potential Score":
- Reach Score = Views × (Watch Rate / 100) × Share Rate × 10
- Engagement Score = (Comments × 3 + Reactions + Saves) / Impressions × 100
- Pipeline Score = Direct pipeline attribution ($) + Influenced pipeline ($) normalized against average deal size
- ICP Resonance Score = % of LinkedIn viewers matching target job title + seniority + company size criteria

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High video views but no measurable pipeline impact**
Solution: This attribution gap has three common causes. (1) Missing UTM parameters — add `?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic-video&utm_campaign=[video-slug]` to every link in video descriptions, first comments, and bio CTAs; without UTMs, GA4 cannot attribute demo requests to specific videos. (2) CRM contact matching failure — LinkedIn Page Analytics reports aggregate viewer demographics but not individual emails; use LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms attached to video CTAs, or match company-level data (via Clearbit/Apollo) to CRM accounts for pipeline influence scoring. (3) Wrong funnel stage content — if all your videos are TOFU educational content, pipeline attribution will be near zero; add 2 BOFU videos per month (customer outcomes, product demos) specifically designed to capture in-market buyers.

**Problem: Watch rates are consistently below 12% (below B2B benchmark floor)**
Solution: The first 3 seconds determine everything on LinkedIn's auto-play feed. Run a hook audit: watch your bottom 5 videos and identify whether they open with an intro ("Hey everyone, today I want to talk about..."), a logo animation, or a slow buildup. Replace these with one of three proven B2B hooks: (a) start with a specific, counterintuitive statistic ("78% of B2B data teams are measuring pipeline incorrectly — here's proof"); (b) open mid-story with zero context, forcing curiosity ("Six months ago we almost lost our Series B because of this..."); (c) show the payoff first, then explain how ("This is the dashboard that generated $2M in pipeline — built in one afternoon"). Also ensure captions are enabled — 85% of LinkedIn videos play silently, and videos with accurate captions see 42% higher completion rates.

**Problem: Attribution numbers conflict between LinkedIn Analytics, GA4, and CRM**
Solution: Conflicting numbers are expected and normal — each tool counts differently. Standardize on a single "source of truth" for stakeholder reporting and document the logic. Recommended hierarchy: (1) CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce) is primary for pipeline and revenue attribution; (2) GA4 is primary for website behavior and demo request attribution; (3) LinkedIn Native Analytics is primary for content performance metrics only (views, watch rate, engagement). Define your "video influenced" threshold clearly in writing: a contact must watch ≥30 seconds of organic video content within a 90-day attribution window before pipeline creation to be counted as "video influenced." Publish this definition in your Marketing Measurement Policy and enforce it consistently across all reporting.
