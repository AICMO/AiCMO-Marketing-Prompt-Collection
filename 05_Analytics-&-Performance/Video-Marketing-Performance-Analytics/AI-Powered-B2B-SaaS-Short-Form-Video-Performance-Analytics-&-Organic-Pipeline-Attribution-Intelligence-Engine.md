SECTION 1 — SHORT-FORM VIDEO HEALTH SCORING SYSTEM

1.1 Universal Short-Form Video Performance Score (0–100)
Design a composite scoring model calibrated specifically for short-form B2B video (under 90 seconds). Assign weights across five performance dimensions:

HOOK STRENGTH (25% weight):
- 3-second view rate: % of people who watch past the 3-second mark (scroll-stop rate)
  - Benchmark by platform: LinkedIn 55–75% / TikTok 45–65% / YouTube Shorts 40–60% / Instagram Reels 50–70%
  - Below threshold signals hook failure requiring immediate re-edit or re-record
- Retention curve shape at 0–15 seconds: flat = strong hook; steep drop = weak hook or wrong audience targeting
- Hook-to-body transition rate: % of 3-second viewers who continue past 15 seconds (tests whether the hook promise is delivered)
- Pattern interrupt score: qualitative classification — verbal hook ("most B2B marketers are wasting their video budget because…"), visual hook (unexpected visual, on-screen text overlay, B-roll creating curiosity), data hook (counterintuitive statistic immediately on screen), story hook ("last quarter, one of our customers made a mistake that cost them $200K…")

CONTENT ENGAGEMENT QUALITY (30% weight):
- Average watch completion %: % of video length watched on average
  - Short-form B2B benchmarks: YouTube Shorts ≥50%, LinkedIn native ≥55%, TikTok ≥45%, Instagram Reels ≥50%
- Rewatch rate: % of viewers who replay all or part of the video (LinkedIn and YouTube provide this; TikTok aggregates into average watch time — infer from avg. watch time / video length > 1.0)
- Save rate (Instagram/TikTok): % of viewers who save the video — the highest-intent signal in short-form; saved = "I want to use this"
- Shares per 1,000 views: indicates content that provides portable value or strong opinion resonance
- Comment-to-view ratio: comments / total views × 1,000 (benchmark: ≥1.0 per 1,000 views indicates active ICP response)

ICP AUDIENCE QUALITY (20% weight):
- ICP reach score: % of known video viewers/engagers who match your ICP definition (title, company size, industry)
  - Measurement: LinkedIn Creator Analytics shows follower demographics + video viewer demographics by function/seniority/industry/company size
  - TikTok Business Center: shows audience demographics by age, gender, location — proxy B2B ICP by correlating with website traffic from TikTok
  - YouTube Studio: audience data shows age/gender/location; use geographic distribution as proxy for professional audience quality
- Profile visit rate: video views → profile/company page visits (intent amplification signal — viewer wants to know more)
- Follower conversion rate: % of unique video viewers who follow/subscribe after watching (audience building efficiency)
- Connection request rate post-video (LinkedIn): elevated connection requests from ICP personas within 24–48 hours of posting signals strong ICP resonance

DOWNSTREAM CONVERSION (20% weight):
- Link-in-bio / description CTR: % of video viewers who click through to website, landing page, or gated asset
  - Note: for LinkedIn organic posts, links in comments perform 30–40% better than links in the post body due to algorithm suppression
- Website visit quality from video traffic: bounce rate, pages per session, session duration from social video referral traffic (GA4)
- Form fill rate from video-referred traffic: % of video-driven website sessions that result in a lead capture event
- Cost per ICP visit: production cost of video / estimated ICP-qualified visits to website driven by that video

PRODUCTION EFFICIENCY (5% weight):
- Cost per completed ICP view: production cost / (total views × ICP quality score × completion rate)
- Content derivative yield: # of repurposed assets produced from source video (e.g., YouTube Shorts → LinkedIn native → Instagram Reel = 1 production → 3 distributions)
- Time-to-publish: days from brief/idea to live post (production velocity benchmark)

Scoring output: normalize all metrics to 0–100. Apply weights. Flag:
- 0–35: Underperforming — diagnose and either re-edit/re-distribute or retire
- 35–60: Developing — clear optimization opportunities; prescribe 1–2 specific interventions
- 60–80: Performing — maintain cadence; identify repurposing opportunities
- 80–100: Top Performer — amplify via paid boost, repurpose across platforms, build content series around this formula

SECTION 2 — PLATFORM-BY-PLATFORM ANALYTICS FRAMEWORK

2.1 LinkedIn Native Video Analytics
LinkedIn is the highest-ICP-quality platform for B2B short-form. Measure:

REACH ANALYTICS:
- Impressions: total times video appeared in feed (not unique)
- Reach: unique accounts reached (impressions / reach ratio = average frequency — target 1.3–1.8×)
- Organic vs. paid reach ratio (if boosting posts)
- Follower vs. non-follower reach split (high non-follower reach = algorithm distributing beyond your network = high-performance signal)
- Industry distribution of viewers: % in your target industry
- Function/seniority distribution: % in your target function (e.g., "Marketing" or "Engineering") and seniority level

ENGAGEMENT ANALYTICS:
- Video views (LinkedIn counts as 3 seconds minimum — align with platform definition)
- View rate: views / impressions (benchmark: 10–20% for organic B2B content)
- Watch completion buckets: LinkedIn provides quartile data (25%, 50%, 75%, 100% completion rates)
- Reactions by type: LinkedIn reactions (Like, Celebrate, Support, Love, Insightful, Funny) — "Insightful" and "Love" from ICP personas are higher-value signals than "Like"
- Comment quality score: classify comments as (a) ICP engagement ("this resonates, we have the same problem"), (b) peer industry engagement, (c) general/spam

PERSONAL ACCOUNT vs. COMPANY PAGE:
- LinkedIn personal account video posts consistently reach 3–10× more people than company page posts for organic content
- Measure: executive/founder personal account video performance vs. company page video performance. If gap exists, shift content velocity to personal accounts with company page reposts.
- Employee advocacy amplification: measure video reach multiplication when 3+ employees repost — LinkedIn's algorithm treats multi-person engagement as a quality signal and expands distribution

2.2 TikTok B2B Analytics (Business Account)
TikTok requires different measurement logic for B2B — audience skews younger but B2B professional content (especially finance, marketing, sales, tech) has demonstrated strong ICP penetration in the 25–45 demographic.

ALGORITHM PERFORMANCE ANALYTICS:
- FYP (For You Page) reach %: % of views from "For You Page" (algorithm-distributed) vs. followers vs. profile visits
  - Target: ≥70% FYP reach for brand-new videos = strong algorithm distribution signal
  - <30% FYP reach = algorithm did not distribute the video beyond your followers = hook or completion rate failure
- Average watch time: your most important metric — TikTok's algorithm uses this above all others
  - Target: ≥6 seconds for a 15-second video, ≥12 seconds for a 30-second video (proportional to video length)
  - Below 40% completion = video is being penalized by TikTok's distribution algorithm
- Traffic source analysis in TikTok Analytics: break down views by FYP / Following / Search / Profile / Hashtag — high "Search" % indicates discoverable, evergreen content

B2B AUDIENCE QUALITY PROXY METRICS:
- Age 25–44 % of audience: proxy for working professional audience
- Geographic concentration: high % from major business metros (NYC, SF, London, Chicago, etc.) = B2B professional audience indicator
- Website click rate: % of video viewers who click on bio link (TikTok bio link only, unless running paid campaigns with in-video CTAs)
- Profile visit rate: video views → profile page visits (curiosity-to-brand signal)

CONTENT CLASSIFICATION PERFORMANCE:
- Classify all TikTok videos into: educational/tip, data/insight, opinion/contrarian, behind-the-scenes, Q&A/response, storytelling. Measure average completion rate and FYP reach rate by category. Allocate production volume toward top 2 categories by FYP reach AND completion rate combination.

2.3 YouTube Shorts Analytics
YouTube Shorts is unique: it simultaneously builds long-form channel authority and generates direct pipeline through YouTube Search.

SHORTS-SPECIFIC ANALYTICS:
- Swipe-away rate: % of viewers who swipe away in the first 3 seconds (inverse of scroll-stop rate — lower is better; benchmark: <30% swipe-away is strong for B2B Shorts)
- Shorts loop rate: YouTube Shorts automatically loops; average loops per view > 1.2 indicates strong rewatch behavior
- Subscriber conversion from Shorts: % of Shorts viewers who subscribe to channel — benchmark: 0.5–2% is typical for B2B educational content; >2% = exceptionally high subscriber conversion
- Shorts → Long-form traffic: % of Shorts viewers who click through to a long-form video (Shorts as top-of-funnel for long-form channel authority building)
- Search impression share: % of Shorts views originating from YouTube Search (evergreen content signal — high search share = the video answers a question people are actively asking)

CROSS-FORMAT CHANNEL IMPACT:
- Measure: does Shorts publishing frequency correlate with long-form video performance? Channels publishing Shorts + long-form typically see 15–30% lift in long-form recommendation reach.
- Shorts subscriber quality: compare average watch time on long-form videos from Shorts-acquired subscribers vs. other acquisition sources (lower watch time from Shorts subscribers is normal — they're awareness-stage)

2.4 Instagram Reels B2B Analytics
Reels reach is highest per post on Instagram but B2B audience quality is lower than LinkedIn unless your ICP uses Instagram for professional development (common in creative industries, marketing, DTC).

REELS PERFORMANCE METRICS:
- Reach (unique accounts): total unique accounts who saw the Reel
- Plays: total plays (loops count)
- Saves: the highest-intent engagement metric on Instagram — save rate / plays × 1,000 (benchmark: ≥5 saves per 1,000 plays is strong for B2B educational Reels)
- Shares: number of users who shared the Reel to Stories or via DM — amplification signal
- Profile visits from Reel: Reel views → profile visits (intent signal)
- Accounts reached by audience type: % existing followers vs. non-followers (high non-follower reach = algorithm distributing to new audiences)

3.2 Hook Failure Diagnostic Protocol
For videos with 3-second view rate below 35% (hook is failing):

STEP 1 — IDENTIFY DROPOUT PATTERN:
- Did the audience even receive the video? Check: impression count is normal but view rate is low = audience saw it and scrolled past = weak hook.
- OR: Did the algorithm barely distribute it? Check: impression count is abnormally low = the video didn't pass the algorithm's initial quality threshold, often due to poor production quality, low resolution, or policy concerns.

STEP 2 — DIAGNOSE HOOK FAILURE TYPE:
- Slow-start failure: video opens with a black screen, lengthy intro, or logo animation. Fix: cut to your strongest statement in the first frame — no pre-roll, no intro.
- Generic hook failure: opening line is something every competitor could also say ("Today I want to talk about marketing…"). Fix: make the first sentence specific, surprising, or provocative.
- Text hook missing failure: on mobile with sound off (60–80% of LinkedIn/Instagram viewing is muted), your hook must work visually — large on-screen text in the first 3 seconds is non-negotiable for B2B short-form.
- Mismatched thumbnail failure: the still frame shown before play promises content that the video doesn't immediately deliver.

3.3 Watch Time Curve Analysis
Export retention graphs from each platform. Classify by curve shape:

CLIFF SHAPE (sharp drop after 3–8 seconds → plateau):
- Meaning: hook worked, but first transition to body content caused abandonment
- Fix: reduce the gap between hook and first value delivery — cut any "let me explain" filler after the hook and go directly to the first insight

SKIING SLOPE SHAPE (continuous gradual decline):
- Meaning: content is good but length is slightly long for the value density
- Fix: increase information density — deliver more insight per second. Target one actionable insight per 15 seconds for B2B educational content.

STEADY CLIFF THEN FLAT (big drop early, then stable retention):
- Meaning: large initial drop (hook not strong enough to capture browsing audience) but people who stayed are highly engaged
- Fix: strengthen the hook to capture more of the initial audience. The content quality is strong — the acquisition problem is at the first frame.

SHARK FIN (strong start, big drop in middle, recovery at end):
- Meaning: hook works, middle section loses audience, and something at the end re-engages those who stayed
- Fix: move whatever re-engages at the end closer to the middle. Restructure as: hook → first insight → cliffhanger/tension → payoff → CTA

SECTION 5 — PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION MODEL FOR SHORT-FORM VIDEO

5.1 The Short-Form Video Attribution Challenge
Unlike paid media, short-form organic video attribution has a fundamental gap: most views happen on platform with no CRM-trackable click. Design a measurement architecture that captures both direct and indirect attribution:

TIER 1 — DIRECT ATTRIBUTION (trackable):
Track video → website → conversion path:
- UTM framework for all CTAs in video descriptions, bio links, and comments:
  - utm_source: platform (linkedin / tiktok / youtube / instagram)
  - utm_medium: organic-video
  - utm_campaign: [content cluster name]
  - utm_content: [video title slug]
- GA4 Events: configure GA4 to fire a custom event when social video referral traffic lands on a demo request or contact page. This creates a UTM-tagged pipeline source.
- HubSpot: create a custom contact property "Video First Touch Source" populated from UTM data on the first form submission. Create a custom deal property "Video Influenced" (yes/no) populated from contact-level video engagement data.
- Salesforce: build a Campaign hierarchy for video programs. Every UTM-tagged form fill should be associated with a Campaign that maps to the video content cluster.

TIER 2 — ASSISTED ATTRIBUTION (inferred):
Capture video assists in multi-touch paths:
- In HubSpot: if contact has "linkedin-video" or "youtube-shorts" anywhere in their page view history AND is within a deal — mark deal as "video influenced"
- Run a cohort analysis quarterly: compare pipeline velocity (days to opportunity creation) for contacts with video engagement in their history vs. contacts with no video touch. If video-influenced contacts convert faster, video is accelerating pipeline even when it's not the first or last touch.
- Sales team trigger report: configure a Salesforce report of contacts who watched ≥1 video (via LinkedIn insight tag) within 14 days of a sales conversation request. Review these conversations — are prospects referencing the video? If yes, document and use as proof of influence.

TIER 3 — DARK FUNNEL ATTRIBUTION (probabilistic):
Attribute video influence you cannot directly track:
- Monthly survey to new leads: include "How did you first hear about us?" as a form field. Track "social media video" as an answer option. Calculate % of pipeline that self-reports video as first awareness.
- Hold-out test: run a 60-day video publishing pause on one platform while maintaining cadence on others. Measure: did website traffic from that platform and inbound inquiry rate decrease? The difference is your video incrementality estimate for that platform.
- Share-of-search measurement: track branded search volume (Google Search Console, Semrush) before and after periods of high video production velocity. B2B brands with strong video programs see 10–25% branded search lift — this is a video pipeline signal even though it's indirect.

5.2 Sales-Assisted Attribution Protocol
Train your sales team to capture video influence in discovery calls:

DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT ADDITION:
"Before we dive in — how did you first come across [Company]? Was it through content you saw somewhere?"

If prospect mentions LinkedIn video, TikTok, or YouTube Shorts:
- SDR/AE logs a Salesforce/HubSpot activity: "Contact mentioned [platform] video as first touchpoint"
- Marketing is notified: this closes the attribution loop for content that drove inbound without a trackable click

Quarterly Video Attribution Report:
- Total opportunities where video was first-touch (UTM tracked)
- Total opportunities where video was assisted-touch (GA4 multi-touch path)
- Total opportunities where video was sales-call-reported as first awareness (qualitative)
- Total pipeline influenced by video ($)
- Cost per video-influenced opportunity (total video production cost / video-influenced opportunities)

SECTION 6 — CONTENT VELOCITY & ALGORITHM COMPOUNDING MODEL

6.1 The Short-Form Video Compounding Curve
Short-form video programs follow a predictable compounding pattern. Map your current position:

PHASE 1 — BUILD (weeks 1–8):
- Algorithm is learning your content quality and audience
- Expect: inconsistent reach, high variance between video performance
- Success signal: average completion rate is above platform benchmark (even if reach is low)
- Failure signal: average completion rate is below benchmark — content quality issue, not a volume issue
- Target velocity: minimum 3 videos/week per platform to give algorithm sufficient data

PHASE 2 — SIGNAL ACCUMULATION (weeks 9–16):
- Algorithm has enough data to predict which audiences will engage with your content
- Expect: more consistent reach, reduced variance between videos, first viral outliers
- Success signal: non-follower reach % starts climbing (algorithm distributing to new audiences)
- Failure signal: still dominated by follower reach → content is being consumed by existing audience but not breaking through to new ICP accounts

PHASE 3 — COMPOUNDING DISTRIBUTION (months 4–8):
- Channel authority is established: algorithm reliably distributes content to new ICP audiences
- Expect: baseline reach per video has increased 3–10× from Phase 1 baseline
- Success signal: inbound mentions from prospects who found you through video
- Each new video benefits from accumulated channel authority — the "rising tide" effect

PHASE 4 — EVERGREEN SEARCH DIVIDEND (months 6–12+):
- For YouTube Shorts and TikTok: evergreen videos that answered persistent B2B questions continue generating views months after publishing
- Success signal: 30%+ of views on a video are still arriving 3+ months post-publish
- These are your "content assets" — not just posts, but permanent demand capture vehicles

6.2 Minimum Viable Velocity Calculator
Calculate the minimum weekly video volume to maintain algorithm momentum on each platform:

- LinkedIn: minimum 2 videos/week on personal account + 1 video/week on company page. Below this: algorithm deprioritizes the account and reach recovery takes 2–3 weeks.
- TikTok: minimum 4 videos/week. TikTok is the most velocity-dependent platform — posting gap >5 days typically triggers reach reset.
- YouTube Shorts: minimum 2 Shorts/week. Unlike TikTok, YouTube Shorts performance is less velocity-sensitive and more evergreen-search-dependent.
- Instagram Reels: minimum 2 Reels/week. Instagram's algorithm is velocity-sensitive but less punishing than TikTok for posting gaps.

If you cannot maintain minimum velocity across all platforms, concentrate production on fewer platforms rather than diluting across all with insufficient frequency.

- Add text overlays (for muted viewing) to any video missing on-screen text in the first 3 seconds

MONTH 2 — CONTENT MIX REBALANCING:
- Shift production mix to your top 2 performing content clusters (double the video count)
- Reduce or pause your bottom 2 performing clusters
- Launch one recurring "content series" (consistent weekly format with consistent branding/hook structure) — series drive subscriber/follower growth 40% faster than one-off videos
- Configure HubSpot/Salesforce to track "social-video" as a lead source for the first time if not already done

MONTH 3 — ATTRIBUTION INFRASTRUCTURE:
- Implement hold-out test on 1 platform (30-day posting pause) to measure incrementality
- Launch first paid boost ($200–500) on your top 3 highest-performing organic short-form videos to test paid amplification ROI
- Present first "Short-Form Video Attribution Report" to leadership: pipeline influenced + pipeline sourced + brand reach growth

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Short-form video views are high but no website traffic or leads can be attributed to video.**
Fix: The attribution gap is almost always a missing UTM tracking step. Audit: (1) Does every video description contain a UTM-tagged link? Many creators forget to update the link after pasting from a template. (2) Is your bio link on each platform pointing to a UTM-tagged URL, or to your bare homepage? Your bio link should read: yoursite.com/[offer]?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic-video. (3) Are you using Bitly or a link shortener that strips UTM parameters? Always use full UTM URLs — not shortened links (LinkedIn and TikTok allow full URLs in descriptions). If zero attribution even with UTMs in place, check GA4: is "linkedin.com" appearing in your Referral report? If yes but the UTM channel shows (not set), your UTM structure has a syntax error.

**Problem: LinkedIn short-form videos get strong view counts but almost no comments, making it hard to identify ICP engagement.**
Fix: Comment rate for B2B LinkedIn video averages 0.3–1.0 comments per 1,000 views — it is naturally low compared to consumer platforms. Three levers: (1) End every video with a specific question directed at your ICP ("What's your biggest challenge with [topic]? Drop it below"). Specific questions outperform generic "what do you think?" prompts by 3–5×. (2) Reply to every comment within 2 hours of posting — LinkedIn's algorithm uses comment velocity as a distribution signal, amplifying reach for 24–48 hours after posting. (3) Ask 3 colleagues to comment on the video within 30 minutes of posting (not likes — comments). Early comment momentum triggers the algorithm to distribute to a wider audience.

**Problem: TikTok shows strong views but our B2B ICP seems absent — how do I know if the right people are watching?**
Fix: TikTok's native analytics does not show professional demographic data. Use three proxies: (1) Comment quality review — are comments from identifiable B2B professionals? Many professionals have job titles in their TikTok bios. (2) Cross-platform validation — run the same content topic on LinkedIn and TikTok. If LinkedIn gets strong ICP engagement (measured by viewer function/seniority in LinkedIn Creator Analytics) and TikTok shows similar engagement rate, infer a comparable ICP audience exists there for that topic. (3) Business account follower demographics — TikTok Business Center shows follower demographics. If follower age skews 18–24 despite B2B content, your content is resonating with students/early career professionals rather than buyers — adjust topic positioning toward more senior business challenges and CFO/VP-level pain points.
