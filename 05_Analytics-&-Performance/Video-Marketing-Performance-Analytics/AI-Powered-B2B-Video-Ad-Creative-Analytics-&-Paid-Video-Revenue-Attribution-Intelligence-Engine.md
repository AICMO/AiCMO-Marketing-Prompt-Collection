Use this when YouTube Ads, LinkedIn Video Ads, or Connected TV are material spend channels and you need to prove ROI beyond CPV — linking video impressions and completions to actual pipeline contribution.

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem**: YouTube view-through attribution data shows wildly inflated pipeline numbers that don't match reality.
  **Solution**: Reduce the view-through attribution window from 14 days to 7 days and apply a 0.2× weighting multiplier (not 1.0× full credit). Report view-through pipeline as "influenced" not "sourced." Validate by running a 2-week incrementality holdout test: pause YouTube in one geo and compare pipeline rates to always-on geos. This will calibrate your attribution model to real lift, not overcounting.

- **Problem**: LinkedIn video ads have strong VTR metrics but CPL is 3–5× higher than Google Search — hard to justify the budget.
  **Solution**: LinkedIn video's true value in B2B is mid-funnel account warming, not direct lead generation. Measure it differently: track the pipeline influence rate of LinkedIn-video-exposed accounts (those who later convert through ANY channel) vs. non-exposed accounts from the same target list. Typical B2B result: LinkedIn-video-exposed accounts convert at 1.4–2.2× the rate of unexposed accounts. Present this account-influence metric to the CFO rather than CPL alone.

- **Problem**: CTV video ads have zero visibility into downstream conversions — the channel is a "black box."
  **Solution**: Run a structured geo holdout test over 4 weeks: pause CTV in 2–3 mid-size DMAs (e.g., Denver, Atlanta) while maintaining spend in matched DMAs (Phoenix, Tampa). Compare pipeline generation rates between on/off markets using your CRM data. This incrementality test gives you statistically defensible proof of CTV lift without requiring deterministic cross-device attribution. If no lift is detected, redirect CTV budget to YouTube Demand Gen or LinkedIn.
