SECTION 1 — VIDEO PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT ARCHITECTURE

PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION METRICS (20% weight):

PRODUCTION EFFICIENCY METRICS (5% weight):
- Cost per view (production cost / total lifetime views)
- Cost per minute watched (production cost / total minutes watched)

SECTION 4 — AUDIENCE RETENTION ANALYSIS & CONTENT OPTIMIZATION

4.1 Video Dropout Diagnostic Framework
For each video scoring below your engagement threshold, diagnose the dropout pattern using retention curve analysis from YouTube Studio or Wistia:

HOOK FAILURE (0–10% dropout):
- Symptom: Majority of viewers drop within the first 15–30 seconds
- Root causes: title/thumbnail over-promise, weak opening statement, no clear "here's what you'll get" in first 10 seconds
- Fix protocol: Re-cut the first 30 seconds with a pattern interrupt, state the value proposition immediately, use a bold claim or counterintuitive statement as the hook

PACING FAILURE (10–30% dropout):
- Symptom: Steady drop-off after the hook but before the core content
- Root causes: slow introduction, excessive credential building, no chapter markers for navigation
- Fix protocol: Add YouTube chapters, cut any section that doesn't directly serve the viewer's stated goal, increase cut frequency to minimum 1 visual change every 4–6 seconds

RELEVANCE DROP (30–60% dropout):
- Symptom: Drop-off spike at a specific segment
- Root causes: topic drift from the promised title, segment that only appeals to a subset of the audience, competitor comparison section that loses non-comparison seekers
- Fix protocol: Identify the specific timestamp causing the drop via YouTube's "audience retention" graph. Cut or move that segment. Add a transition card warning at that point ("skip to X:XX if you're already familiar with this").

LENGTH ISSUE (60%+ dropout):
- Symptom: Gradual but consistent drop-off in the final third
- Root causes: video is longer than the topic warrants, insufficient payoff in the final section, no "just one more thing" hook to retain viewers
- Fix protocol: Cut the final third to a tight summary, add a strong CTA teaser at the 60% mark ("before you close this, the most important takeaway is…"), or repurpose the final third as a standalone short-form clip

SECTION 5 — VIDEO SEO & PLATFORM ALGORITHM OPTIMIZATION

5.1 YouTube B2B Optimization Audit
Run this audit for every YouTube video published in the past 12 months:

TITLE OPTIMIZATION:
- Is the primary keyword in the first 40 characters? (YouTube truncates at ~60 characters in search)

- Is the title clickbait-free but curiosity-driving? (avoid hollow superlatives; use specific numbers and outcomes)

DESCRIPTION OPTIMIZATION:
- Does the first 100 characters (visible before "show more") contain the primary keyword and a clear value statement?
- Are timestamps (chapters) listed in the description with keyword-rich labels?
- Are 3–5 related video links included?
- Is the primary CTA (demo link, gated asset, newsletter signup) in the first 200 characters?

THUMBNAIL CTR ANALYSIS:
- Current CTR benchmark: YouTube reports your average CTR in YouTube Studio. B2B average: 4–7%. Below 3% = thumbnail problem.
- A/B test thumbnails using YouTube's built-in test feature (minimum 1,000 impressions per variant)
- Best practices: human face + emotional expression + text overlay (max 4 words) + high contrast colors

TAG & KEYWORD STRATEGY:
- Use 5–8 tags max. First tag = exact primary keyword. Second = broader category keyword. Remaining = related LSI terms.
- Avoid keyword stuffing tags (no benefit and may signal spam to algorithm)

PLAYLIST ARCHITECTURE:
- Every video should belong to at least one playlist
- Playlist titles should be keyword-optimized (they rank in YouTube and Google search)
- Playlist watch-time compounds channel authority (viewers who watch playlist sequences boost total watch-time minutes)

5.2 LinkedIn Video Algorithm Optimization

- First comment strategy: within 60 minutes of posting, add a first comment with relevant hashtags, a question to prompt replies, and the CTA link (keeping the post itself link-free)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Video views exist in YouTube Studio but zero pipeline attribution in HubSpot/Salesforce.**
Fix: YouTube views from non-logged-in users (majority of traffic) cannot be matched to CRM contacts without a click-through event. Your attribution requires CTAs — every video must drive viewers to a trackable destination (UTM-tagged landing page, gated asset, or demo request form). Without a CTA click, YouTube views are untraceable in your CRM. Add a CTA card at 60% watch time and in the end screen on every video, and add the UTM-tagged link in the description.

**Problem: Webinar attendance rate is below 30% despite strong registration numbers.**
Fix: Three proven levers: (1) Add a 1-hour-before reminder email with a one-click calendar join link — this alone typically lifts attendance 5–8 percentage points. (2) Send a "preview clip" (2–3 minute teaser) 48 hours before the event to re-engage registrants who haven't been opening emails. (3) Reduce webinar length — B2B webinar completion rates drop significantly above 45 minutes; aim for 30–40 minutes plus Q&A.

**Problem: LinkedIn video organic reach has dropped significantly in the past 90 days.**
Fix: LinkedIn's algorithm shifted in 2024–2025 to favor "knowledge and advice" content over promotional posts. Three adjustments: (1) Remove all external links from the video post body (put links in first comment instead). (2) Increase personal account posting vs. company page — personal accounts consistently receive 3–5× the organic reach of company pages for video. (3) Increase comment response velocity in the first hour post-publish — LinkedIn's algorithm uses early engagement signals to determine distribution. Reply to every comment within 60 minutes of posting.
