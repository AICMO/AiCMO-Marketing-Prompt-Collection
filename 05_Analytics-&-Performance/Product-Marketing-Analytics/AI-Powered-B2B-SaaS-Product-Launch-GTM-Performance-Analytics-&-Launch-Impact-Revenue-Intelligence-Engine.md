## Overview
Transforms raw product launch data into a launch performance intelligence system: scores every launch on pipeline velocity, win rate lift, competitive displacement, and feature adoption, identifies which GTM motions drove the most revenue impact, surfaces execution gaps that cost pipeline, and generates a prioritized optimization roadmap for the next launch. Use this within 30–90 days of every major product launch — or quarterly to review your launch portfolio — to prove launch ROI, sharpen future launch investment decisions, and build a compounding GTM muscle.

### Analysis Framework

**Section 1: Launch Performance Scorecard**

Score the launch across five dimensions using a 0–100 scale. Weight each dimension by its importance to your current GTM stage (suggested defaults shown):

*Dimension 1 — Pipeline Creation (Weight: 30%)*
- Metric: Launch-attributed pipeline as % of 90-day pipeline target
- Benchmark bands: ≥120% of target = 90–100 | 100–119% = 80–89 | 80–99% = 65–79 | 60–79% = 50–64 | <60% = <50
- Key diagnostic: If pipeline is below target, identify the gap in the GTM waterfall — was it an audience awareness problem (too few SQLs entering the top of the launch funnel), a conversion problem (demo requests not converting), or a follow-through problem (pipeline created but stalling in mid-stage)?

*Dimension 2 — Win Rate Impact (Weight: 25%)*
- Metric: Percentage point change in win rate from pre-launch to post-launch (90-day window)
- Benchmark bands: +8pp or more = 90–100 | +5–7pp = 80–89 | +2–4pp = 65–79 | 0–1pp = 40–64 | Negative = 0–39
- Key diagnostic: Win rate impact indicates whether the launch changed how buyers perceive your differentiation. A launch with strong pipeline but flat win rate means the messaging created curiosity but not conviction. A launch with flat pipeline but improved win rate means the message works once prospects engage, but top-of-funnel awareness is insufficient.

*Dimension 3 — Customer Adoption (Weight: 20%)*
- Metric: Feature adoption rate at 60 days among eligible customers
- Benchmark bands: ≥50% = 90–100 | 35–49% = 80–89 | 20–34% = 65–79 | 10–19% = 50–64 | <10% = <50
- Key diagnostic: Low adoption despite high pipeline interest suggests the product delivers on first impressions but fails to create stickiness or solve the core job-to-be-done in practice. Interview 5 non-adopters to identify whether this is a discovery problem (they don't know the feature exists), a friction problem (too hard to set up), or a value problem (they tried it and didn't see the payoff).

*Dimension 4 — Market Awareness (Weight: 15%)*
- Metric: Share of voice on launch-related keywords vs. top competitor(s) at 30 days post-launch; analyst acknowledgment; media coverage tier
- Benchmark bands: Dominant SoV + Tier 1 media + positive analyst sentiment = 90–100 | Strong SoV + trade coverage = 80–89 | Moderate SoV + some coverage = 65–79 | Weak SoV + minimal coverage = 40–64 | Ignored by market = <40
- Key diagnostic: Low market awareness despite strong internal execution means either the category framing is not compelling (the story doesn't create curiosity), the distribution was insufficient (launch budget was too low for the category noise floor), or the launch timing was poor (overshadowed by competitor announcement or market event).

*Dimension 5 — Sales Execution (Weight: 10%)*
- Metric: % of reps who completed training within 2 weeks AND are using at least 2 launch assets in active deals within 30 days
- Benchmark bands: ≥80% on both = 90–100 | 70–79% = 80–89 | 55–69% = 65–79 | 40–54% = 50–64 | <40% = <50
- Key diagnostic: Poor sales execution scores reveal an enablement system problem, not a product problem. If the product is strong but reps aren't using launch assets or aren't completing training, diagnose: Was training too long? Were assets built for marketing to distribute rather than reps to use in conversations? Did the launch timing conflict with end-of-quarter selling pressure?

*Composite Launch Grade:*
- A (90–100): Benchmark-setting launch — document and replicate the playbook
- B (80–89): Solid execution with one or two identified gaps — optimize and capture remaining value
- C (65–79): Average performance — at least one dimension significantly underperformed; targeted recovery campaign recommended
- D (50–64): Launch underperformed on multiple dimensions; conduct launch post-mortem with full cross-functional team; identify root cause before next Tier 1 launch
- F (<50): Launch failed to achieve minimum viable impact — mandatory retrospective; evaluate whether the product hypothesis or the GTM execution was the primary failure mode

*Attribution analysis questions to answer:*
1. Which 2 GTM motions generated 80% of the pipeline? (Pareto analysis — concentrate future launch budgets here)
2. Which motions had the best pipeline ROAS? (Not always the same as highest absolute pipeline — a $20K SDR campaign that generated $800K pipeline beats a $150K paid campaign that generated $2M)
3. Which motions had the worst ROAS but produced the highest-quality deals? (Some channels produce fewer, larger deals — e.g., analyst briefing → Tier 1 enterprise account inquiry)
4. What was the timing pattern? (Which motions drove pipeline in week 1 vs. week 4–8 vs. week 8–12? Launch momentum typically peaks in weeks 1–2 and again if re-energized with case study or analyst quote)

**Section 4: Sales Execution Gap Analysis**

Identify where execution broke down in the launch-to-revenue chain:

*Gap #1: Enablement Coverage*
- Diagnosis: Of quota-carrying reps, what % completed training within the first 2 weeks? What % are using ≥2 launch assets in active deals?
- Root cause hierarchy: (a) Reps didn't know training was available → distribution problem; (b) Reps started training but didn't finish → content was too long or not role-relevant; (c) Reps completed training but aren't using assets → assets don't match the conversations they're actually having; (d) Reps are using assets but not winning → asset quality or message quality problem
- Revenue impact estimate: [# of reps who didn't complete training] × [average # of deals worked per rep per month] × [historical win rate lift from enabled vs. non-enabled reps] × [ACV] = estimated pipeline lost to enablement gaps

*Gap #2: Message Consistency*
- Diagnosis: Compare the launch message as designed by PMM vs. how reps are actually delivering it (use Gong/Chorus keyword analysis or ride-along call reviews). Score consistency on a 1–5 scale.
- Common deviation patterns: (a) Reps leading with features instead of outcomes — typical when enablement emphasized the product demo without enough context on why it matters; (b) Reps overusing the pricing narrative — typical when reps perceive the launch as a justification to raise prices rather than as a new capability; (c) Reps skipping the launch message entirely in early-stage calls — typical when launch messages are complex and reps default to their existing muscle memory
- Revenue impact estimate: If Gong data shows reps who used the core launch message won at X% but reps who didn't used a 3% lower win rate, apply that delta across all deals where the message wasn't used.

*Gap #3: Asset-Deal Alignment*
- Diagnosis: Cross-reference the assets that appear in won deals vs. lost deals. Identify: (a) assets used in won deals that are also present in most lost deals — these don't provide a win lift and may create false confidence; (b) assets used in won deals that are rarely used in lost deals — these are true win accelerators; (c) assets with high rep usage but no correlation to win rate — deprioritize or redesign
- Specific finding format: "The AI Forecasting ROI Calculator was used in 73% of won deals and only 31% of lost deals — this is a 2.4x win rate predictor; reps should be coached to introduce it at the economic buyer meeting. Conversely, the Technical Data Sheet appeared in 89% of both won and lost deals with no detectable win correlation — it's being used as filler, not as a differentiator."

*Gap #4: SDR Message Adoption*
- Diagnosis: Compare SDR reply rates on launch-specific outreach sequences vs. standard sequences. Did the launch create a cold outreach angle that breaks through vs. a message that prospects dismiss as generic vendor noise?
- Flag: If the SDR launch sequence reply rate is less than 1.5x the baseline sequence reply rate, the launch is not creating a compelling enough reason for prospects to respond. This indicates the launch angle needs to be sharpened from a feature announcement to a business problem framing.

**Section 5: 30-Day Optimization Playbook**

Generate 5 specific, high-ROI actions the PMM and demand gen team can execute in the next 30 days to capture remaining launch pipeline before momentum fully dissipates:

For each action, specify:
- The specific action (what exactly to do)
- The targeting criteria (who to reach)
- The expected pipeline impact ($ estimate based on your data)
- The owner (PMM / Demand Gen / SDR / CS / Product)
- The resource requirement (hours or budget)
- The measurement criteria (how you'll know if it worked within 30 days)

**Typical high-ROI 30-day actions include:**
1. **Customer success story activation:** If 3+ customers have seen meaningful ROI from the feature, produce a 2-minute video customer testimonial and a 1-page case study within 2 weeks; deploy immediately to all mid-stage competitive deals — customer proof at evaluation stage typically lifts close rates by 15–25%
2. **Analyst quote amplification:** If an analyst acknowledged the launch favorably, license the quote, create a branded one-pager, and inject it into all enterprise deals where procurement or IT gatekeeping is slowing decisions
3. **SDR sequence refresh:** Rebuild the cold outreach sequence using the objections and questions captured from live deals in the first 60 days — a launch outreach sequence informed by real buyer language typically outperforms the original PMM-written sequence by 40–80% on reply rate
4. **Internal champion re-engagement:** For deals that went dark in the first 60 days post-launch (appeared interested, received a demo, then stopped responding), send a one-to-one email from the AE/rep referencing a specific piece of new information (new customer proof, analyst quote, or product update) — win-back rate for ghost deals with a compelling new trigger averages 8–14% in B2B SaaS
5. **Sales velocity audit:** Identify the top 10 deals where the launch feature was discussed but the deal stalled; assign PMM directly to those accounts for a co-sell assist — PMM-assisted deals in the 60–90 day post-launch window close at an average of 1.6x the win rate of unassisted deals when the deal involved a complex new product conversation

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** You don't have clean attribution data — HubSpot and Salesforce show different pipeline numbers for the launch, and sales reps are inconsistently logging which deals are launch-related.
**Fix:** Attribution data problems are universal in B2B SaaS; the goal is to get "good enough" data, not perfect data. Build a two-layer attribution model: (1) System-attributed pipeline — any opportunity created within 90 days of the launch date where the first CRM touch was a launch campaign (email, ad, webinar, SDR launch sequence); pull this automatically from your CRM. (2) Sales-reported pipeline — send a 3-question Typeform to all AEs and SDRs asking: "In the last 90 days, did you close any deals or create any pipeline where the [Feature Name] was a factor in the prospect's interest? If yes, which opportunities and approximately how central was it?" This captures deals where the product feature was a conversation trigger but the CRM touchpoints don't reflect it. Present both numbers — system-attributed and sales-reported — and use the range as your confidence interval rather than picking one number and defending it.

**Problem:** Win rate improvement appears in the data, but sales leadership is skeptical — they say the win rate improvement is due to better rep hiring or improved discovery calls, not the product launch.
**Fix:** This is a legitimate question, and the honest answer is that attribution in win rates is always mixed. The strongest counterargument is a regression analysis: if win rate improved broadly across all deal types and time periods, it could be training or rep quality; but if win rate improvement is concentrated specifically in competitive deals against Competitor A (the competitor the launch was designed to displace), time-correlated with the launch date, and stronger for deals where the launch asset was used, then the causal link to the launch is credible even if not proven. Present the data with this framing: "We can't claim the launch caused 100% of the win rate lift, but the pattern — concentrated against our primary target competitor, time-correlated with launch, and asset-correlated — is consistent with launch impact. We'd expect this pattern to dissipate over the next 2 quarters if it were purely coincidental; let's track win rates for another quarter before reaching a final conclusion."

**Problem:** Feature adoption among existing customers is below 25% at 60 days, and the CS team says customers don't know about the feature despite the in-app announcement.
**Fix:** This is a common failure mode when in-app messaging is treated as a single notification rather than an activation campaign. The solution requires 4 steps: (1) Audit the in-app message — was it an informational banner (low conversion) or a direct prompt to take a single action, like "Connect your ERP to activate" (higher conversion)? In-app messages that prompt one specific action convert at 3–5x the rate of informational messages. (2) Check which customer segment saw the announcement — if the in-app message was shown to all users but only admins can activate the feature, you showed the announcement to the wrong people. Retarget admins specifically with a direct email from the Customer Success Manager. (3) Build a 3-email CS-led activation sequence: Email 1 — "Here's what AI Cash Flow Forecasting does for companies like yours" (social proof from a peer company); Email 2 — "Your ERP integration is 80% of the setup — here's how your team can finish it in 20 minutes" (reduces perceived effort); Email 3 — "Can we schedule a 30-minute activation session?" (direct CS involvement). (4) Identify your 10 highest-NPS customers who haven't adopted the feature and have the CSM call them personally — these customers are your fastest path to adoption, case study creation, and expansion revenue.
