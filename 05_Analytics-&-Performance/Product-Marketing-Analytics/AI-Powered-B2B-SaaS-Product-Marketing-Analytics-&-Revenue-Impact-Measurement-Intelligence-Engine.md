## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Win rates fluctuate for reasons unrelated to PMM (deal size mix, rep tenure, seasonal buying patterns), making it hard to isolate PMM message impact.
**Solution:** Always segment win rate analysis by deal size cohort (SMB/MM/Enterprise separately) and control for rep tenure. Add a "message used" field to your CRM and compare win rates within the same rep/segment cohort over rolling 90-day windows to isolate messaging signal from noise.

**Problem:** Sales team doesn't track which PMM assets were used in deals, making enablement attribution impossible.
**Solution:** Start with Gong/Chorus call tagging — configure keyword alerts for battlecard language, key message phrases, and product launch names. This gives indirect attribution without requiring rep data entry. In parallel, add a mandatory "PMM materials shared" multi-select field to your Opportunity record with a 3-week enforcement sprint.

**Problem:** Executives question whether PMM influence attribution is inflated because any deal with even a single content touchpoint gets counted.
**Solution:** Implement a threshold-based model: asset must appear in at least 2 deal touchpoints within a 30-day window to count as "influenced." Compare influenced vs. non-influenced win rates — if the gap is statistically meaningful (≥5pp), the attribution model is defensible. Document this methodology and present confidence intervals rather than precise point estimates to pre-empt skepticism.
