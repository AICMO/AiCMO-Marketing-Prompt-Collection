**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, saas, analytics, customer-health, churn-prevention, lifecycle-marketing, predictive-analytics, marketing-automation, retention, nrr

## Overview
Builds a predictive customer health scoring framework from marketing's vantage point — fusing product usage, engagement, support, and commercial signals into actionable health scores that automatically trigger segmented marketing intervention programs before churn risk materializes. Use this when your team is reacting to churn instead of preventing it, when CS and marketing are misaligned on which accounts need intervention, or when you want to turn customer health data into autonomous AI-driven retention campaigns.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Health scores are accurate in aggregate but individual account scores feel wrong to CSMs**
Solution: Add an "explainability layer" — when displaying the health score to CS, show the top 3 signals driving the score with their individual contribution. A CSM who can see "Score is 38 because: DAU/MAU = 0pts (25pt gap), Champion dark = 0pts (15pt gap), Support escalation open = 0pts (15pt gap)" will trust and act on the model. A black-box score invites skepticism. Also establish a quarterly "CSM feedback session" where CS can flag 5–10 accounts where the model score doesn't match their on-the-ground perception — use this to identify missing signals.

**Issue: Intervention email sequences are getting low engagement from at-risk accounts — they're already checked out**
Solution: At-risk accounts often have diminished email engagement as a symptom, not a cause. Shift the channel mix for At-Risk and Critical programs: (1) In-app messaging often outperforms email for disengaged users who still log in occasionally — target the few remaining active users directly in the product. (2) For executive-level stakeholders, LinkedIn connection + message from your VP CS or CMO outperforms cold marketing email by 4–8x. (3) Direct mail (Sendoso, Reachdesk) for accounts >$50K ACV creates a pattern-interrupt that email cannot — use a physical touchpoint as the re-engagement anchor. (4) If primary champion has gone dark, route the intervention through an active end-user: send them a quick win / feature tip email and ask them to share with their manager.

**Issue: Marketing and CS are fighting over which accounts triggered the rescue vs. which CS was already working on**
Solution: Establish clear "swim lane rules" documented in a shared SLA: (a) Marketing-owned interventions: any automated sequence triggered by health score drop before CS has a scheduled touchpoint in the next 14 days. (b) CS-owned accounts: any account with a CS touchpoint (call, email, QBR) scheduled in the next 14 days — marketing pauses automated sequences to avoid conflicting messages. (c) Joint ownership: Critical accounts always have a joint Marketing-CS plan, with CS leading the commercial conversation and Marketing providing the content arsenal. Resolve disputes by reviewing attribution in the CRM — add a "Rescue Sourced By" field (Marketing / CS / Joint) to track program performance separately.
