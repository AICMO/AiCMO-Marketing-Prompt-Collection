## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Pull gated content performance via the Content Analytics API → Forms Dashboard → Contact source reports. Use the Revenue Attribution report (Marketing Hub Enterprise) to map contact-to-deal attribution by first/last/multi-touch. Export as CSV and paste into the Advanced Version prompt.
- **Salesforce + Pardot/Marketing Cloud Account Engagement:** Use Connected Campaigns to link Pardot form handlers to Salesforce opportunities. Run the "Campaign Influence" report filtered by Form submission type. Export opportunity influence data and feed it into Module 5 of the Advanced Version.
- **Google Analytics 4:** Create a funnel exploration from "Landing Page" → "Form Submit" → "Thank You Page" → "CRM Sync" events. Use GA4's Conversion Paths report to map content touchpoints in winning journeys. Export to BigQuery and combine with CRM close data for the Content DNA analysis.
- **Clearbit/Clay/Apollo enrichment:** After form fills, enrich lead records with job title, seniority, company ARR, and headcount. Feed enriched data into the Persona-Content Resonance Matrix to separate ICP-fit form fills from noise — this transforms the analysis from volume-based to quality-based.
- **Notion/Airtable as Content Inventory:** Build a content registry in Notion with a "Revenue Impact Score" property that auto-calculates from HubSpot/Salesforce API pulls. Trigger a re-run of this prompt monthly via Zapier when the score drops below 40 for any asset.
- **Gong/Chorus:** Use the "Content mentioned in calls" feature to identify which gated assets reps reference in deals. Cross-reference with CRM attribution data — often assets that appear in winning call transcripts are invisible to first-touch attribution models.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** No clean pipeline attribution data — can't connect form fills to closed deals
*Solution:* Use a proxy: segment your closed-won accounts from the past 12 months in your CRM, then reverse-lookup which of those contacts submitted gated content forms (match by email domain if contact records don't exist). Even domain-level matching gives you a directional Content DNA map. Apply a 0.3x confidence adjustment to your pipeline figures and note the methodology caveat to the CFO.

**Problem:** Form fill volume is too low (< 50 fills per asset) to draw statistically valid conclusions
*Solution:* Aggregate assets by type (all calculators, all eBooks, all reports) rather than analyzing individually. Run the analysis at the asset-category level for Module 1-3, and reserve asset-level analysis for assets with 200+ fills. Use Module 4 (Gate Strategy) outputs to redesign high-potential low-volume assets — often low fill rates signal a CTA/promotion problem, not a content quality problem.

**Problem:** Multiple attribution models give wildly different results, creating internal debate
*Solution:* Use the W-shaped model as your primary attribution standard for executive reporting (it credits both the first content that created awareness and the last content that drove conversion, with a bump for the MQL-creation touch). Present first-touch separately as "awareness content investment" and last-touch as "conversion content investment" — this resolves the common sales vs. marketing attribution debate by showing both are true simultaneously.
