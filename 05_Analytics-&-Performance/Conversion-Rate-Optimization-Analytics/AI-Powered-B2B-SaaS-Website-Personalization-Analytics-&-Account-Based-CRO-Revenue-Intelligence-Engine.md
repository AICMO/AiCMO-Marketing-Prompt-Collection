## ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK TO APPLY

**Framework 1: Statistical Validity Audit (Experimentation Rigor)**
- For each experiment, calculate Minimum Detectable Effect (MDE) at 80% power and 95% confidence given sample size
- Flag experiments called too early (insufficient visitors for the observed lift percentage)
- Identify experiments running too long (novelty effect risk, seasonal contamination)
- Check for Simpson's Paradox: does aggregate lift mask a segment subgroup that is dragging results down?
- Recommend: call / extend / segment further / kill for each underperformer

**Framework 2: Causal Attribution (Incremental Lift Isolation)**
- Selection bias adjustment: personalized visitors are often higher-intent (identified by intent data), which inflates apparent lift. Apply cohort matching — compare personalized accounts to control accounts with similar intent scores, industry, and size
- Holdout group construction: for each segment, define a true holdout (never personalized) vs. the A/B control to separate test mechanics from intent signal lift
- Attribution formula: Incremental Pipeline = (Personalized Opp Rate - Matched Control Opp Rate) × Personalized Visitors × Avg Pipeline Value per Conversion
- Payback calculation: Tool Monthly Cost ÷ Monthly Incremental ARR = Payback Months

**Framework 3: Segment Opportunity Scoring (Where to Invest Next)**
Score each candidate new segment across five dimensions (1-5 scale):
1. Traffic volume: How many monthly ICP-qualified visitors match this segment?
2. Current baseline conversion rate: Lower baselines = higher ceiling for lift
3. ICP quality score: What % of segment visitors match your ideal customer profile?
4. Expected lift potential: Based on published benchmarks or adjacent experiment results (personalization typically delivers 20-40% lift for well-matched segments)
5. Revenue per account: Segment-specific ACV × win rate
Final score = (Traffic × ICP Quality × Expected Lift × Revenue per Account) ÷ Implementation Complexity

**Framework 4: Message-Market Fit Diagnosis (Failure Root Cause)**
For each underperforming experiment, apply the MAST diagnostic:
- M — Message: Does the personalized message address this segment's primary Job-to-be-Done? Is it specific enough to feel personal vs. generic?
- A — Audience signal: Is the segment identification accurate? Sample 20 personalized accounts and verify they actually belong to the target segment
- S — Stage: Is the offer (demo / trial / download) appropriate for this segment's buying stage? High-intent in-market accounts need frictionless conversion; early-stage need education
- T — Timing: Is the personalization showing on the right page at the right moment in the session?

## Integration Tips

- **Mutiny**: Export segment performance data via Mutiny's Analytics tab → Experiments view. Pull conversion data by segment using the CSV export, then cross-reference with Salesforce opportunity data using the `Account Domain` field as the join key. Mutiny's native revenue attribution integrates with Salesforce but lags 30 days — always pull a fresh SFDC export for accurate pipeline numbers.
- **Salesforce**: Create a custom field `Personalization_Segment__c` on the Account object and populate it via Mutiny's Salesforce integration or a Zapier workflow triggered on segment identification. This enables direct attribution of personalized touchpoints to opportunities without manual matching.
- **HubSpot**: Use HubSpot's Smart Content rules for basic personalization (by list membership, lifecycle stage, or geographic region). For analytics, pull Contact Timeline data filtered by page views + smart content variant served, then aggregate to company level. HubSpot's Behavioral Events API provides event-level data for more granular conversion tracking.
- **6sense**: Use 6sense's Account Reach reports to validate segment identification accuracy — compare the accounts identified by 6sense segment rules against your actual ICP list. The Qualification Score and Buying Stage filters are the most reliable signals for personalization triggers (Stage 4+ converts at 3-5x Stage 1-2).
- **Google Analytics 4 / Amplitude**: Create a custom dimension for `Personalization Segment` (pass via GTM data layer) to analyze behavioral metrics (session duration, pages per session, scroll depth) for personalized vs. control groups without touching CRM data. Use this for early-signal optimization before sufficient pipeline data accumulates.
- **Snowflake / BigQuery**: For teams with a data warehouse, join the personalization tool's experiment log (visitor ID + segment + variant served + timestamp) to the CRM deal table on email domain. This is the most accurate attribution method and removes dependence on tool-native reporting. Run cohort analysis across visitor segments with 90-day pipeline lag for full-cycle attribution.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Confidence levels are consistently low (<80%) even after weeks of running**
Solution: You are likely targeting micro-segments with insufficient traffic volume. Calculate the minimum sample size required for your target MDE (use a sample size calculator: at 5% baseline rate, 10% relative MDE, 80% power, 95% confidence requires ~5,800 visitors per variant). Either consolidate small segments into a broader segment definition, reduce the MDE threshold to 20% relative lift, or wait longer. Do not call experiments with <80% confidence — you risk optimizing for noise.

**Problem: High conversion lift in experiment data but no corresponding increase in pipeline or revenue**
Solution: This is typically a lead quality issue, not a CRO failure. Your personalization is converting visitors who were never genuine buyers — either the segment identification is too broad (pulling in low-intent traffic), the conversion goal is too low-friction (content downloads attracting researchers, not buyers), or there is a significant time lag between website conversion and pipeline creation that your reporting window is missing. Extend your attribution window to 120+ days, audit 20 converted accounts from the personalized group against your ICP criteria, and check whether the conversion event (e.g., content download) actually predicts downstream pipeline for your baseline audience.

**Problem: Personalization lift degrades significantly after the first 2-3 weeks (novelty effect)**
Solution: The novelty effect is real in personalization — returning visitors who have seen your personalized experience before lose the freshness of the message. Three fixes: (1) Add a frequency cap so accounts that have visited 3+ times see a different variant with a more direct/urgent CTA; (2) Rotate personalization creative every 4-6 weeks for long-running segments; (3) Segment your returning vs. first-time visitors and report lift separately — the first-time visitor cohort is your true experiment population and should maintain consistent lift without novelty effect contamination.
