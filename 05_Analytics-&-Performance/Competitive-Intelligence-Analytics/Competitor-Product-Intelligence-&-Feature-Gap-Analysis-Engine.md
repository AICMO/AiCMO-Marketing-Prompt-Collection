## Integration Tips

- **G2 Buyer Intent + Slack**: Set up G2 intent alerts for your top 3 competitors and pipe new review notifications into a #competitive-intel Slack channel. Run this prompt weekly on the batch of new reviews.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator**: Follow competitor company pages and set up keyword alerts for product-related posts. Export to a weekly digest and feed into the signal inputs section.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Create a "Competitive Intelligence Hub" database with one page per competitor. Link each prompt output to the relevant competitor page — maintain a rolling 90-day changelog of detected moves.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Tag deals with "competitive deal — [competitor name]" and track win/loss rates. Cross-reference with intelligence cycles to measure impact of battlecard updates on win rates.
- **Zapier / Make**: Build an automation that: (1) monitors competitor blog RSS feeds, (2) captures new posts, (3) feeds into this prompt via Claude API, (4) posts a Slack summary to #product-marketing within 2 hours of any competitor content publish.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: You don't have access to competitors' G2 reviews or changelog — their profile is minimal.
**Solution**: Job listings are the most reliable leading indicator of roadmap and require no paid access. Use LinkedIn Jobs filtered to the competitor company and export titles + requirements weekly. Supplement with customer-facing signals: search Twitter/X for "[Competitor] + feature" and Reddit communities where your ICP hangs out (e.g., r/sales, r/RevOps, r/legaltech). Analyst reports (even public summaries) often flag capability gaps. Your own sales reps are also a primary source — implement a structured competitive intel field in your CRM for every deal where a competitor is mentioned.

**Problem**: The AI analysis flags too many "High Priority" items and the team gets alert fatigue.
**Solution**: Add revenue weighting to your triage criteria. Only flag [HIGH PRIORITY] items that meet at least one of these thresholds: (a) mentioned in 3+ active deals, (b) directly counters a core differentiator, or (c) represents a pricing model change that affects >20% of pipeline. Everything else goes in a "Monitor" queue reviewed monthly.

**Problem**: Competitor intelligence reports are produced but sales reps don't use them.
**Solution**: Compress the output. Sales reps will not read a 10-page intelligence report. Take the output of this prompt and distill Section 4 (Battlecard Intelligence) into a 1-page PDF with three sections: "Why we win," "Why we lose," and "Three questions to ask." Deliver it as a 5-minute Loom video walkthrough from product marketing. Adoption increases dramatically when the battlecard fits on a phone screen during a live call.
