## Overview
This engine systematically monitors competitor pricing moves, quantifies deal-stage revenue erosion from competitive discounting pressure, and generates AI-driven value-based counter-positioning and negotiation playbooks. Use it when deals are being lost on price to specific competitors, when your average selling price (ASP) is declining quarter-over-quarter, or when you need to build proactive pricing defense into your competitive strategy program.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Reps say "we don't have reliable competitor pricing data" and refuse to use the playbooks.**
Solution: Start with 5 lost deal debriefs — pull actual CRM notes and call recordings. You need only 3-5 confirmed data points per competitor to establish a pricing range. Share the data in the next sales meeting to build credibility. Track the source of every data point so reps know it's real intel, not assumptions.

**Problem: The TCO model isn't landing with the CFO — they're still anchoring on the line-item license price.**
Solution: Restructure the presentation order: lead with the business risk of delayed time-to-value, not the cost comparison. For a bank, regulatory and security risk exposure during a 3-4 month implementation window has a calculable cost (potential fine size, audit risk). Reframe the comparison as "risk-adjusted cost" rather than "license fee comparison." Finance teams respond to risk mitigation framing when ROI framing fails.

**Problem: Sales leadership is skeptical that the discount defense playbooks will work — they want to just match competitor price.**
Solution: Run a 30-day A/B test: apply the discount defense playbook to 10 deals and match price on 10 comparable deals. Track win rate, ASP, and 90-day churn rate for each cohort. Price-matching typically produces higher win rates short-term but lower ASP and higher churn — the data will make the case.
