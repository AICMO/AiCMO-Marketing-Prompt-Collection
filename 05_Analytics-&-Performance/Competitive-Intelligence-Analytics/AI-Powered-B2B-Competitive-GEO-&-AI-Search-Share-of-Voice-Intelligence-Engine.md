## Troubleshooting

**"AI responses vary too much to track consistently"**
This is real — AI models are non-deterministic and update frequently. Standardize your testing protocol: use the same exact query wording, temperature settings where available, and test each query 3 times per model to get a consensus result. Use the median response, not a single sample. Run all queries within a 24-hour window for each monthly audit to minimize update-cycle noise.

**"We published content but AI still isn't citing us after 60 days"**
Content alone isn't enough — AI models primarily cite content when it appears on high-authority third-party domains, not just your own site. Prioritize syndication over self-publication: get your content republished or referenced on G2 blog, industry trade publications, analyst firm sites, and well-trafficked comparison sites. A single mention on a domain AI frequently cites is worth more than 10 blog posts on your own domain.

**"We have strong G2 presence but AI still recommends competitors first"**
G2 presence is necessary but not sufficient for AI citation dominance. Check cross-model consistency: if you appear in ChatGPT but not Perplexity, it's often a structured data gap (Perplexity heavily weights real-time web retrieval vs. training data). Build a programmatic SEO comparison page targeting competitor-alternative queries and submit it to Perplexity's publisher program. Also check Wikipedia — companies with Wikipedia entries get significantly more AI citations than those without.
