# Competitive Share-of-Voice & Market Position Analytics Intelligence Engine - Quantify Your Market Presence Across Every Channel

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, b2c, analytics, competitive-intelligence, share-of-voice, market-positioning, seo, paid-media, automation

## Overview
Constructs a multi-dimensional Share-of-Voice (SOV) analysis across organic search, paid search, social media, content, review sites, and earned media to reveal your true competitive market position. Use this when you need to defend or grow market share, justify brand investment to leadership, benchmark your competitive standing after a repositioning, or build a data-driven case for budget reallocation across channels.

## Integration Tips

- **SEMrush / Ahrefs:** Use Position Tracking to monitor keyword-level SOV weekly. Set up competitor tracking for all Tier 1 and Tier 2 competitors. Export the "Visibility %" metric monthly to populate your SOV matrix. Use Site Explorer Organic Research to snapshot competitor traffic trends.
- **Google Ads Auction Insights:** Export the Auction Insights report monthly for your top campaigns. This gives direct Impression Share, Overlap Rate, Outranking Share, and Position Above Rate vs. named competitors — the most accurate paid SOV data available.
- **G2 / Capterra:** Both platforms provide profile analytics showing your review count trend, rating history, and category rank over time. G2 Buyer Intent (paid) adds account-level signals showing which companies are researching you vs. competitors. Export monthly and feed into your SOV matrix.
- **Brandwatch / Mention.com / Muck Rack:** Set up keyword monitors for your brand + all competitor brand names. Pull monthly share-of-mention reports segmented by sentiment and publication tier. Use Muck Rack's "Share of Voice" feature for earned media SOV directly.
- **Looker Studio (free):** Connect SEMrush, Google Search Console, Google Ads, and a manual Google Sheet (for review site data) to build a unified SOV dashboard. Use blended data sources to create the composite SOV chart. Automate weekly email reports to the marketing leadership team.
- **HubSpot:** Enrich contact records with competitor data via Clearbit or similar enrichment. Use deal-level fields to track "Competitors in Deal" and build win/loss by competitor reports — this closes the loop between SOV gains and revenue outcomes.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate: weekly → pull SEMrush position data → update Google Sheet SOV tracker → if SOV drops >3 points in any channel → send Slack alert to #competitive-intelligence channel with context.
- **Salesforce:** Build a custom "Competitive Intelligence" object tracking competitor SOV scores by channel, updated monthly. Link to Campaign object to correlate SOV investment with pipeline generation.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't have competitor data — I can't estimate their SOV"**
Solution: You don't need paid competitive tools to start. For organic search, use free tools: Google the top 20 category keywords and note who ranks on page 1 — that's a proxy SOV count. For paid search, run Google Ads Auction Insights for your existing campaigns (free, already in your account) — this gives direct impression share vs. named competitors. For review sites, manually check G2 and Capterra category pages and note review counts (5 minutes of work). For PR, set up free Google Alerts for competitor brand names and count mentions weekly. You have enough to build a directional SOV baseline without spending a dollar on tools. The prompt is designed to work with estimated data and will flag confidence levels accordingly.

**Problem: "Our composite SOV is low in all channels — where do we start?"**
Solution: Apply the "SOV beach-head" strategy — don't try to gain SOV across all channels simultaneously. Identify the single channel where: (a) the gap to the leader is smallest, (b) your ICP relies on this channel most heavily in their buying journey, and (c) investment-to-SOV-gain ratio is highest. For most early-stage B2B SaaS companies, this is review sites (G2/Capterra) — a 90-day review generation sprint is the fastest path to credible SOV. Once you have review SOV, it compounds into paid and organic SOV via improved CTR and trust signals. Sequence your SOV investments rather than spreading thin.

**Problem: "Our SOV scores are improving but pipeline isn't growing"**
Solution: SOV and pipeline have a lagged relationship — typically 3-6 months for organic/content SOV, 30-60 days for paid SOV, and near-immediate for review site SOV. First, check whether you're gaining SOV in channels your ICP actually uses in their buying process (e.g., gaining Instagram SOV when your buyers are CFOs who don't use Instagram is irrelevant). Second, check intent stage: SOV in "what is [category]" content reaches awareness-stage buyers, while SOV in "[your brand] vs. [competitor]" content reaches decision-stage buyers. If pipeline is lagging, prioritize decision-stage SOV (comparison pages, review site ranking, branded paid defense) over awareness-stage content investment.
