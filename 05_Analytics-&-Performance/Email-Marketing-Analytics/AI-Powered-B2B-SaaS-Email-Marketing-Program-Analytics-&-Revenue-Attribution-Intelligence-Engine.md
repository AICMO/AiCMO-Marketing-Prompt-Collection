## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Use the Campaigns tool to tag all email sends to a campaign, then pull revenue attribution via the Revenue Attribution report. Enable "Email program revenue influence" in the reporting settings. Export program performance via the Marketing Email Analytics export and cross-reference with the Deals report using `createdate` and `closedate` filters.
- **Salesforce + Pardot/Marketing Cloud:** Build a custom "Email Influence" report type in Salesforce that joins Campaigns, Campaign Members, and Opportunities with a filter on `CampaignMember.HasResponded = true`. Use Salesforce's Einstein Attribution or build a custom multi-touch model using the Campaign Influence object.
- **Marketo:** Use Revenue Cycle Analytics (RCA) or the Opportunity Influence Analyzer to map email program touches to pipeline stages. Build a Smart List for "MQLs sourced from email in last 90 days" and cross-reference with Salesforce opportunity records.
- **Customer.io:** Export event data via the Data Warehouse sync to BigQuery or Snowflake, then join with CRM opportunity data using contact `external_id`. Build attribution models in your BI tool (Looker, Tableau, or dbt).
- **Zapier/Make automation:** Set up a weekly trigger that pulls email metrics from your ESP API (HubSpot, Mailchimp, etc.), formats them as a structured data table, and passes them to Claude via the Anthropic API to auto-generate your weekly email performance brief.
- **Google Sheets:** Build a "Email Revenue Tracking" sheet with tabs for: (1) Weekly sends × engagement metrics, (2) MQL source tracking, (3) Pipeline influence by program, (4) Monthly RPS trend. Feed this sheet into the prompt for automated analysis.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Can't link email engagements to closed revenue — attribution data is missing or unreliable.**
Solution: Start with a simpler proxy metric: track "email touch before demo request" using your CRM's `Lead Source` and `Last Campaign` fields. Map every demo request to the most recent email campaign the contact interacted with. This is a "last-touch" approximation, but it gives you actionable directional data within 2 weeks. Gradually layer in multi-touch by enabling campaign influence tracking in Salesforce or HubSpot's attribution settings.

**Problem: Open rates look great (40%+) but pipeline from email is low — disconnect between engagement and revenue.**
Solution: You have an audience-content mismatch — contacts are engaging with educational content but aren't being moved toward a buying decision. Audit the call-to-action in your top 10 highest-open-rate emails. If >80% of CTAs lead to blog posts, videos, or thought leadership (not demos, case studies, or calculators), you're running an education program not a demand program. Introduce "revenue-intent CTAs" (demo, pricing, case study) in at least 30% of emails in ICP Tier 1 programs.

**Problem: Deliverability is degrading — open rates dropping across all programs even though content hasn't changed.**
Solution: Pull your Google Postmaster Tools domain reputation data (free at postmaster.google.com) and check inbox placement trends. If reputation is dropping, immediately: (1) suppress all contacts who haven't opened in 90 days, (2) kill any program with >1.5% unsubscribe rate, (3) reduce total send volume by 30% for two weeks, (4) run a re-permission campaign to your engaged list only. Deliverability recovery typically takes 4-8 weeks of disciplined sends to high-engagement segments. Do NOT increase send volume to compensate for poor delivery — this accelerates the damage.
