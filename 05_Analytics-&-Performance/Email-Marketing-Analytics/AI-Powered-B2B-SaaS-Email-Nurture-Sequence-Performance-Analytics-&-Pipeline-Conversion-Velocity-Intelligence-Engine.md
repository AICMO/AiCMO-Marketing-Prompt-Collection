## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Attribution data is incomplete — many MQLs show no source sequence**
Solution: This usually means your CRM lacks first-touch campaign attribution on contact records. Fix: Add a hidden "Original Lead Source" field that captures the first campaign a contact entered. In HubSpot, enable "Original Source" property and map it to specific nurture sequences using enrollment triggers. Retroactively, use campaign membership data to reconstruct source attribution for the past 90 days.

**Problem: MQL conversion rates look artificially high because MQL threshold is too low**
Solution: Before analyzing nurture sequence performance, audit your MQL definition. A common issue: an MQL score of 40 triggers when a prospect opens 4 emails (behavioral scoring alone). This produces high "MQL rates" that never progress to SAL because the prospect never had genuine intent. Fix: Require at least one high-intent action (demo request click, pricing page visit, or bottom-funnel content download) as a minimum MQL gate. Recalculate historical MQL conversion using the corrected definition before drawing conclusions.

**Problem: Sequences have very small sample sizes (<50 enrolled), making metrics unreliable**
Solution: Do not optimize sequences with <100 enrolled contacts in the analysis period — statistical noise will send you in the wrong direction. Instead: 1) Consolidate two low-volume similar sequences into one, 2) Extend the analysis window to 6 months for low-volume programs, 3) Focus optimization effort on sequences with ≥150 enrolled contacts where A/B test significance is achievable within 4-6 weeks.
