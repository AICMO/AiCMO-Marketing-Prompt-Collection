## Troubleshooting
**Problem: Chat platform reports show high conversation volume but CRM shows almost no chat-sourced pipeline**
Solution: The most common cause is a broken CRM integration — the chat platform is not correctly associating conversations with CRM contacts/leads. Check that your chat platform's email capture field is syncing to CRM in real-time; verify UTM parameters are passing through the chat widget to your CRM lead source field. Also check if your attribution window (typically 7–30 days) is configured correctly.

**Problem: AI intent classification accuracy is below 60% despite recent training updates**
Solution: Low accuracy typically means the training data doesn't reflect actual visitor language — visitors describe their problems differently than your internal team does. Export your last 200 chat transcripts, identify the 10 most common phrasings for each intent category, and retrain your AI classifier using real visitor language. Also check if your playbook's qualifying questions are too product-centric; rewrite them to be outcome-centric (what the visitor wants to achieve, not what your product does).

**Problem: Meeting-booked rate is good (>20%) but chat-sourced pipeline is low**
Solution: High meeting rate with low pipeline suggests one of three issues: (1) meetings are being booked with non-ICP visitors — tighten AI qualification logic; (2) meetings are not being created as CRM opportunities — fix the meeting-to-opportunity workflow; or (3) attribution is broken — SDRs are logging meetings as self-sourced rather than chat-sourced. Audit 20 recent chat-booked meetings in CRM and verify the source attribution is correct.
