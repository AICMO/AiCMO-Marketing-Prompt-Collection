## Troubleshooting

**Problem: X/Twitter analytics show thousands of impressions but zero UTM-confirmed pipeline — can't justify the investment**
Solution: X/Twitter has the highest dark social leakage of any B2B channel because users routinely share content via DM, bookmark without clicking, and reference content in offline conversations without any trackable link click. Deploy a 3-part dark social measurement stack: (1) Add "How did you hear about us?" to your demo request form with X/Twitter as an explicit option — even 5-10% self-attribution materially changes the ROI story; (2) Add a standard discovery question to SDR/AE call scripts: "Did you come across any content from us on X/Twitter before reaching out?" — log answers as CRM activities; (3) Apply a 4:1 dark social multiplier to your UTM-confirmed conversions for board reporting (industry benchmark based on Dreamdata and HockeyStack research on B2B dark social ratios).

**Problem: Engagement is high (likes, reposts) but followers are mostly peers, competitors, and job seekers — not ICP buyers**
Solution: Audience quality is an algorithm feedback problem. X's algorithm shows your content to accounts similar to those who engage first. To break the peer bubble: (1) Identify your top 5 ICP customers who use X/Twitter and personally ask them to reply (not just like) on your next 3 posts — their profile signals who you want the algorithm targeting; (2) Write one post per week explicitly in the language of your economic buyer (budget language, board-level concerns, ROI framing) rather than practitioner/peer language — this attracts different engagement profiles; (3) Use X/Twitter's Topic targeting in a small paid promotion ($200-500) to amplify your best organic post to verified accounts in your ICP industry — the resulting engagement trains the algorithm and then turns off the spend.

**Problem: Can't get consistent executive or employee participation in X/Twitter advocacy — it dies after 2 weeks**
Solution: X/Twitter advocacy fails when it feels like extra work on top of a full-time job. Reduce friction to near-zero: (1) Create a shared Slack channel called #twitter-amplify where you post 3 pre-written replies to that week's top company thread — employees need only copy-paste and add their name; (2) Frame it as career benefit (personal brand building, visibility to investors and press who heavily use X/Twitter) rather than company obligation; (3) Set up a Zapier automation that pings the #twitter-amplify channel whenever a company post hits 1,000 impressions within 2 hours — this creates natural momentum and FOMO; (4) Track and celebrate individual advocate reach stats monthly ("Alex's repost reached 8,400 engineers this month") to reinforce the personal value.
