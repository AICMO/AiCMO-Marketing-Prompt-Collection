Analyzes organic social media performance across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and YouTube, attributes content to pipeline and revenue using dark social and CRM-linkage methodologies, identifies the highest-performing content formats and topics, benchmarks share of voice against competitors, and builds a compounding content strategy roadmap. Use this when your social program lacks measurable revenue attribution, when leadership questions organic social ROI, or when you need to prioritize which social investments to scale versus cut.

## Integration Tips

**Sprout Social:**
- Use Sprout's Competitor Report to benchmark your LinkedIn/Twitter posting frequency and estimated engagement rate vs. 5 competitors
- Enable Sprout's Link Tracking to auto-append UTM parameters to every social link — configure the UTM template once in Sprout Settings → Link Tracking
- Connect Sprout to Salesforce via the native integration: social profile matching maps Twitter handles and LinkedIn members to CRM contacts; social touchpoints visible in Salesforce Activity timeline
- Build a Sprout Custom Report: Sent Messages → Link Clicks → Group by Campaign Tag; export monthly to Google Sheets for pipeline correlation analysis

**HubSpot:**
- Use HubSpot Social Publishing tool with UTM auto-tagging: Settings → Marketing → Social → URL Tracking
- Build a HubSpot Report: Original Source = "Social Media" → Contacts Created → Pipeline Influenced; segment by Social Network for platform-level attribution
- Create a HubSpot List: Contacts who clicked a social UTM link within the last 90 days and have no CRM owner → route to marketing nurture
- Enable HubSpot's "Visited page" + "Original source is Social Media" enrollment trigger in workflows for social-engaged lead routing

**Salesforce:**
- Create a Custom Campaign Type = "Organic Social" in Salesforce; create sub-campaigns per content series (e.g., "LinkedIn — Manufacturing Carousel Series Q2")
- Use Salesforce's Campaign Influence model (Customizable Attribution) to weight social touchpoints: First Touch = 30%, Last Touch Before MQL = 30%, Linear remaining = 40%
- Add Social UTM Source to Lead Source picklist in Salesforce; create a Lead Source = "LinkedIn Organic" field value and configure web-to-lead UTM capture via hidden form fields

**Google Analytics 4 (Dark Social Detection):**
- Create a GA4 Custom Segment: Source = "(direct)" AND Session Default Channel Group = "Direct" AND page_location contains a high-intent page (pricing, demo, contact)
- Build a GA4 Exploration: Date range → correlate spikes in direct traffic sessions within 48-hour windows after high-impression LinkedIn posts
- Install a UTM shortener (Bitly, Short.io, or Rebrandly) with GA4 click tracking for all LinkedIn posts to capture partial dark social data
- Use GA4's Data-Driven Attribution model (vs. Last-Click) to see if "Direct" sessions have social preceding them in the path

**Zapier Automation:**
- When Sprout Social post reaches 1,000+ impressions → Zap: add to a "high-performance post" Google Sheet log with post text, format, topic tag, engagement rate
- When new demo request form submitted with "LinkedIn" self-reported source → Zap: create Salesforce Campaign Member on the "Social-Self-Reported" campaign; notify social team in Slack
- Weekly: Zap exports LinkedIn Company Page analytics (via API or CSV) → appends to Google Sheets performance tracker → triggers Slack digest to marketing team with week-over-week deltas

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn UTM links get suppressed by the algorithm, reducing post reach by 30-50%**
Fix: Use the "first comment" technique — publish the post body text without any URL, then immediately add the first comment with the UTM-tracked link and a call-to-action ("Full breakdown in the comments ↓"). LinkedIn's algorithm treats the post as text-only (high distribution), while the comment captures link clicks. Test: compare 2-week impressions of link-in-post vs. link-in-comment on identical content series — expect 25-40% reach increase with the comment technique. Track clicks via your UTM link shortener.

**Problem: Social-influenced pipeline is impossible to attribute because buyers don't fill out forms after clicking social links — they just visit the website and come back later**
Fix: This is the dark social problem affecting 60-80% of B2B social traffic. Implement a three-layer approach: (1) Add "How did you hear about us?" to all forms with explicit "LinkedIn / Social Media" options and train SDRs to ask on discovery calls — this captures self-reported social attribution for 15-30% of social-influenced deals; (2) In GA4, build a path analysis segment looking for sessions where Social was a prior channel within 30 days before a Direct session that converted — this reveals the multi-session dark social journey; (3) Deploy a LinkedIn Insight Tag (LinkedIn Pixel) to all website pages — this lets you run LinkedIn Matched Audience campaigns to retarget website visitors AND builds a data bridge to identify which LinkedIn members are visiting your site, enabling probabilistic attribution via LinkedIn's analytics dashboard.

**Problem: Employee advocacy program has stalled — advocates shared 2-3 times then stopped**
Fix: Advocacy program churn is almost always caused by one of three issues: (a) Content friction — advocates are expected to write their own captions; fix by providing pre-written, personalized caption options they can post with one click using tools like Oktopost, GaggleAMP, or even a Notion template library; (b) No feedback loop — advocates don't see any impact from their posts; fix by sending weekly Slack messages to advocates showing their top-performing post's reach and whether any CRM contacts engaged; (c) Wrong advocates — senior executives have high follower counts but low posting frequency; identify the "hidden advocates" — mid-level ICs with active LinkedIn followings in your ICP persona — and build the program around them first.
