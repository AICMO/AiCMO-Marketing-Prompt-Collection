## Overview
Designs, audits, and optimizes automated email sequences — welcome series, trial nurture, post-purchase, win-back, and churn prevention flows — by mapping each sequence to revenue outcomes and identifying gaps, dead ends, and underperforming steps. Use this when building your email automation architecture from scratch, auditing an existing program for revenue leakage, or when your automated flows are active but not converting at benchmark rates.

## Success Metrics

**Sequence-Level Metrics (measure per flow):**
- Open rate vs. benchmark: B2B SaaS automated flows should hit 35–55% open rates
- CTR vs. benchmark: 4–8% for nurture flows, 8–15% for transactional/triggered flows
- Conversion rate per sequence: trial-to-paid >12% (B2B SaaS), post-purchase repeat purchase >25% (ecommerce)
- Revenue per recipient (RPR): Total revenue attributed to sequence ÷ unique recipients

**Program-Level Health Metrics (monthly dashboard):**
- Email-attributed revenue as % of total revenue: B2B SaaS target 15–25%, ecommerce 30–45%
- List engagement rate: % of list active (opened/clicked) in last 90 days — target >40%
- Sequence coverage score: % of lifecycle stages with active, optimized automation
- Average emails per subscriber per month: B2B target 4–8, ecommerce 8–16 (without fatigue)

**90-Day Optimization Success Criteria:**
- Activation sequence: increase key action completion rate by ≥25% relative
- Trial nurture: improve trial-to-paid conversion by ≥2 percentage points
- Win-back sequence: recover ≥8% of churned/lapsed subscribers within 30 days
- Overall email-attributed revenue: increase by ≥20% without list size growth

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Use Workflow branches on contact property "Lifecycle Stage" to route contacts into the right automated sequences automatically
- Set up "Goal" conditions on each workflow to exit contacts when they convert (e.g., became customer property = true)
- Connect sequences to Deals pipeline: trigger expansion emails when deal stage advances to "Customer"
- Use HubSpot's Revenue Attribution report to measure email-attributed revenue per workflow

**Klaviyo (ecommerce):**
- Use predictive analytics (Predicted CLV, Churn Risk) as flow triggers — high-churn-risk contacts enter win-back flow automatically
- Build "Smart Send Time" into each flow for time-zone-optimized delivery
- Connect Shopify/WooCommerce order events as behavioral triggers for post-purchase and repeat-purchase flows
- Use Klaviyo's Flow Analytics to compare RPR (revenue per recipient) across all flows — sort by revenue to find underinvestment

**ActiveCampaign:**
- Use Goals as conversion events within automations — contacts who hit a goal (e.g., made a purchase) exit the current flow and enter the next lifecycle stage
- Build a "Master Lifecycle Automation" that routes contacts between all flows based on tagging events
- Connect CRM deals to trigger expansion and renewal sequences based on deal close date + subscription term length

**Notion/Google Sheets:**
- Use the Lifecycle Coverage Map table as your email program audit doc — update monthly
- Track each sequence's RPR in a Google Sheet connected to your email platform's export to build a live revenue waterfall dashboard
- Use the A/B test log template (hypothesis → result → decision) to maintain institutional knowledge about what works

**Zapier/Make:**
- Trigger email sequences from CRM events: when Salesforce opportunity stage changes → Zapier → add contact to HubSpot workflow
- Sync trial activation data from your product database → email platform to trigger behavioral sequences without engineering effort

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The model produces sequence blueprints but they feel generic, not specific to my product.**
Fix: Add specific product features, use case examples, and customer job-to-be-done statements to the context. Replace "[your product]" placeholders with actual feature names, specific customer outcomes, and the exact milestone event that defines activation for your product. The more product-specific language you include, the more tailored the output.

**Problem: Revenue estimates in the waterfall model seem unrealistic (too high or too low).**
Fix: Provide your actual conversion rates and revenue numbers in the Advanced Version's BENCHMARK DATA section. If you don't have them, explicitly state "use SaaS/ecommerce industry medians" and the model will apply published benchmarks from sources like Klaviyo's ecommerce benchmarks or ChartMogul's SaaS metrics. Always cross-check the math: (list segment size × conversion rate × AOV) should produce a believable number before acting on it.

**Problem: The sequences overlap and a subscriber might receive emails from multiple flows simultaneously.**
Fix: Add this constraint to your prompt: "Identify and resolve all sequence overlaps. For each overlap, specify the priority order (which flow wins) and the suppression logic (e.g., if contact is in trial nurture, suppress re-engagement flow). Output a conflict resolution matrix." Then implement suppression lists or exclusion conditions in your email platform's workflow logic.
