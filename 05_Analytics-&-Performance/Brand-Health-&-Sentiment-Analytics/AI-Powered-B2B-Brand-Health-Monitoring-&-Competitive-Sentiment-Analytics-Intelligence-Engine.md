## Overview
Continuously monitors brand health signals across social media, news, review sites, and community forums — scoring sentiment, tracking share of voice, detecting reputation risks, and correlating brand perception with pipeline and revenue outcomes. Use this when you need to replace manual brand tracking with an always-on, AI-orchestrated intelligence system that surfaces actionable insights rather than vanity metrics.

## Integration Tips

- **Brandwatch / Sprinklr / Mention**: Export monthly mention datasets as CSV; feed directly into the Advanced Version prompt. Schedule automated exports to a shared Google Sheet as the agent's data input source.
- **G2 / Capterra / TrustRadius**: Use their review RSS feeds or export APIs to pull new reviews weekly. Aggregate into a rolling 90-day review corpus for trend analysis.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Export call themes monthly from the competitive intelligence dashboard. Map top objection themes to the Narrative Gap Analysis section.
- **HubSpot**: Create a custom property "Brand Exposure Score" on contact records. When a contact has 3+ positive brand touchpoints (content downloads, event attendance, social engagement), flag in the brand-pipeline correlation analysis. Use workflows to notify SDRs when high-BES contacts enter ICP accounts.
- **Salesforce**: Log brand health score as a custom field on Opportunity records. Track whether deals with high brand-exposed buyers close at higher rates — this becomes your brand ROI proof for the CFO.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate the data collection pipeline — trigger a new brand health report generation on the 1st of each month by pulling from Brandwatch, G2, and Gong APIs and passing to this prompt via Claude API.
- **Slack**: Post the Executive TL;DR section to a #brand-intelligence Slack channel via webhook immediately after the monthly report runs. Tag the CMO and PR lead automatically when CRITICAL risks are flagged.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Maintain a rolling Brand Health log in your marketing wiki. Use this prompt's output as the monthly entry, with a running BHS trend chart built from monthly scores.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Brand Health Score fluctuates wildly month-to-month without clear cause.
**Solution**: Check for data volume consistency — if you had 800 mentions one month and 200 the next due to a news spike, the score is noise. Normalize by setting a minimum monthly data threshold (e.g., 500 mentions minimum before scoring). If volume is genuinely low, add more data sources (Reddit, LinkedIn comments, Slack community discussions) before running the analysis.

**Problem**: The prompt recommends actions but doesn't specify which are highest ROI given limited team bandwidth.
**Solution**: Add this constraint to the Advanced Version: "Given a 2-person marketing team with 20% bandwidth for brand work, rank all recommendations by: (1) severity of risk addressed, (2) effort to execute (Low/Medium/High), (3) time to see measurable impact. Output only the top 3 actions."

**Problem**: The brand-pipeline correlation analysis produces "insufficient data" every time.
**Solution**: You need at least 6 months of paired data (monthly BHS + monthly pipeline generation) to see correlation. Start by building a BHS tracking spreadsheet today — run this prompt monthly and log the score. After 6 months, you'll have enough data to run the correlation. In the interim, use the proxy metric: do inbound demo requests spike 2-4 weeks after positive media coverage? That's your leading indicator.
