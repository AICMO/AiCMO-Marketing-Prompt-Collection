## Overview
Quantifies brand equity as a measurable financial asset — translating brand-building investments (awareness advertising, PR, content, events) into pipeline velocity, revenue lift, and CAC reduction metrics — and produces board-ready attribution models that prove brand ROI to CFOs and investors. Use this when you need to move beyond vanity metrics (impressions, brand lift surveys) and connect brand investment to hard revenue outcomes using statistical modeling and predictive analytics.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot/Salesforce CRM**: Add a "brand exposure score" custom field to Contact records, incremented via workflow when a contact views brand content, attends brand events, or is reached by brand ad campaigns. Use this field to segment the Brand CAC Dividend analysis.
- **Google Search Console**: Export branded keyword click volume monthly → load into Google Sheets or Looker Studio → build the Brand Leading Indicator Dashboard as an auto-refreshing view.
- **Bombora/G2 Buyer Intent**: Filter intent data by "brand research" behavior (accounts researching your brand specifically vs. category broadly) — this is a high-fidelity "brand consideration" signal you can track monthly.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Use the LinkedIn Brand Lift study feature (available for campaigns >$90K) to get statistically validated brand awareness and consideration lift scores from ICP audiences — this is the most rigorous awareness measurement available for B2B.
- **Gong/Chorus**: Create a tracker for the phrase "[Your Company Name] awareness" or "I've heard of you" appearing in sales call transcripts — correlate this with inbound vs. outbound lead source to quantify brand-assisted deal progression.
- **Looker Studio / Tableau**: Build the Brand Equity Index as a live dashboard pulling from GA4 (direct traffic share), Search Console (branded keywords), CRM (win rate), and review APIs (G2/Capterra) — set a monthly automated email distribution to CFO, CMO, and board.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: CFO rejects brand attribution saying "correlation isn't causation — of course people who already know us close at a higher rate."
**Solution**: Run a hold-out controlled experiment in one vertical or geographic market: pause all brand investment for one quarter and measure pipeline velocity change vs. a matched control market where brand investment continues. Even a 90-day holdout will produce statistically significant results. Document the methodology before presenting to CFO so the rigor is established upfront.

**Problem**: Brand Equity Index scores look artificially high because branded search is inflated by existing customers searching for the login page.
**Solution**: Separate branded searches into two buckets in Search Console: "acquisition-intent branded" queries (e.g., "[brand name] pricing", "[brand name] vs [competitor]", "[brand name] demo") vs. "support/customer-intent branded" queries (e.g., "[brand name] login", "[brand name] help"). Use only acquisition-intent branded searches in your Brand Equity Index calculation.

**Problem**: Thought leadership content attribution is being double-counted with demand generation content attribution.
**Solution**: Define brand content as content designed for category-level or problem-level awareness (e.g., "State of Contract Management" report, CEO keynote video) vs. demand generation content designed for solution-level consideration (e.g., "Veridian AI vs. Ironclad comparison"). Tag all content assets in your CRM/MAP with a content tier (Brand/DemandGen) and run attribution separately for each tier — then add them for total marketing ROI while preserving the brand-specific line item.
