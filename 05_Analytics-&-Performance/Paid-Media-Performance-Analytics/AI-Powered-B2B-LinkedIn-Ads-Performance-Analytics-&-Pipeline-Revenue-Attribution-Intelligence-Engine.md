## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn-reported conversions are 3x higher than CRM-recorded MQLs**
- *Root Cause:* LinkedIn's default view-through attribution window (28 days) is inflating conversions — any user who saw a LinkedIn ad in the past 28 days and converted anywhere gets attributed to LinkedIn
- *Fix:* Narrow the view-through attribution window to 7 days in Campaign Manager settings. Cross-validate LinkedIn conversions against UTM-tagged sessions in GA4 and CRM contact source data. For most B2B campaigns, reduce reliance on LinkedIn-reported conversions and prioritize CRM-sourced attribution as the source of truth

**Problem: Lead Gen Form CPL is low ($45) but MQL rate is only 6% — deals never close**
- *Root Cause:* LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms pre-populate with profile data, making it frictionless for anyone to submit — including irrelevant job titles, wrong company sizes, and competitive researchers
- *Fix:* Add a qualifying question to your LGF (e.g., "What is your team's current annual software spend?" or "How many employees does your company have?") to introduce intentional friction. Layer in tighter audience targeting (seniority = Director+, company size = 500+) and exclude competitor company domains. Evaluate switching high-intent campaigns to landing page conversion to capture stronger purchase intent signals

**Problem: LinkedIn frequency is high (6+) but campaign can't be paused because it's the only converting campaign**
- *Root Cause:* Budget concentration in a single campaign creates both fatigue and dependency — a common trap in underfunded LinkedIn programs
- *Fix:* Don't pause — instead: (1) rotate in 2–3 new ad creatives immediately to reset frequency perception, (2) duplicate the campaign with a fresh audience segment (e.g., if original targets "Director of Operations," test "VP of Supply Chain" as a net-new audience), (3) split the existing audience with exclusion layers to reduce overlap and expand reach. Allocate 20% of the campaign budget to a Lookalike of your top-converting audience to build a scaling path
