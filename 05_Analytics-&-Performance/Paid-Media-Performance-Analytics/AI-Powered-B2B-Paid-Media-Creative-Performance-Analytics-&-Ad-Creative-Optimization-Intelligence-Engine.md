ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK — Execute in this sequence:

STEP 1 — CREATIVE PERFORMANCE RANKING
For each active creative:
- Calculate pipeline ROAS = pipeline $ sourced / spend
- Calculate cost-per-MQL = spend / MQLs
- Rank all creatives by pipeline ROAS descending
- Apply tier labels:
  - Tier 1 (Scale): Pipeline ROAS > 6x AND MQL rate > 40%
  - Tier 2 (Hold): Pipeline ROAS 3–6x OR MQL rate 25–40%
  - Tier 3 (Monitor): Pipeline ROAS 1.5–3x (watch for improvement or decline)
  - Tier 4 (Retire): Pipeline ROAS < 1.5x OR frequency > 4 with declining CTR

STEP 2 — WINNER PATTERN EXTRACTION
Using the FRESH Framework (Format, Relevance, Emotion, Social Proof, Hook):
- Format: Which ad formats (single image, video, document/carousel, text, RSA) are over-indexing on pipeline ROAS vs. cost share?
- Relevance: Which persona/industry targeting combinations produce highest MQL rates?
- Emotion: Which emotional triggers (fear of loss, aspiration, curiosity, social proof) produce above-median CTR?
- Social Proof: Do ads with customer logos, testimonials, or numbers outperform ads without? By what margin?
- Hook: Which opening hook types (question, stat, pain point, bold claim) produce highest CTR by channel?

Identify the top 3 winning creative patterns. For each pattern: name it, describe it, cite which ads exemplify it, and state the average pipeline ROAS of ads using it.

STEP 3 — CREATIVE FATIGUE DETECTION
For each creative, assess fatigue risk using the FADE Score (Frequency, Age, Decay, Efficiency):
- Frequency: LinkedIn/Meta frequency > 3 = fatigue risk
- Age: Creatives running > 45 days without refresh = stale risk
- Decay: CTR declining > 15% week-over-week for 3+ weeks = active fatigue
- Efficiency: CPL increasing > 20% from launch baseline = efficiency erosion

Assign each creative a FADE Risk: Low / Medium / High / Critical
High and Critical = retire within 7 days

STEP 4 — OFFER & CTA ANALYSIS
For each offer type in market:
- Calculate average CPL, MQL rate, and pipeline ROAS by offer
- Identify which offers attract Tier 1 ICP vs. unqualified volume (use MQL rate as proxy)
- Flag any offer generating high lead volume but < 20% MQL rate as "lead quality destroyer"
- Recommend: which offers to promote, which to sunset, which to test in new formats

STEP 5 — CHANNEL-CREATIVE FIT ANALYSIS
Some creative formats outperform on specific channels:
- LinkedIn: document ads and thought leadership static often outperform product-focused ads
- Google: RSA with pain-point-specific headlines + price objection handling outperform generic benefit ads
- Meta: human-first video and UGC-style content outperform polished brand ads for TOFU
For each channel, identify the best-performing format and recommend increasing its budget share.

STEP 6 — CREATIVE BRIEF GENERATION
Based on winning patterns from Step 2:
Write 3 new creative briefs. For each brief include:
- Brief Title: [Descriptive name]
- Hypothesis: [Why this creative will outperform based on data]
- Target Persona: [Specific job title, seniority, pain point]
- Funnel Stage: [TOFU / MOFU / BOFU]

- Success Threshold: [CTR and CPL target to declare the test a winner]

## Integration Tips

- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager + Google Sheets**: Use LinkedIn's Campaign Manager API or the native CSV export to pull creative-level data weekly. Structure the export in the input table format above. Set up a Google Sheets template with the FRESH Framework scoring built in as formulas so you can paste data and get instant FADE scores before feeding the data to this prompt.
- **Meta Ads Manager Creative Reporting**: In Meta Ads Manager, use the "Breakdown → Creative" view to see performance by individual ad. Add "Quality Ranking," "Engagement Rate Ranking," and "Conversion Rate Ranking" as columns — these Meta-native signals corroborate the FADE Score. Export at the ad level, not the ad set level.
- **HubSpot Campaign Attribution**: In HubSpot, create a custom report using "Contacts by original source" filtered to Paid Ads — segment by UTM content (map to creative name) to pull MQL rate and lifecycle stage progression per creative. This bridges the gap between ad-platform CPL data and CRM pipeline attribution.
- **Zapier + Slack Creative Alert**: Build a Zapier workflow that monitors your Google Sheets creative tracker weekly — if any creative's 7-day CTR drops > 15% from its launch baseline, auto-post a Slack alert to your paid media channel with creative name, current CTR, and a link to the tracker. This catches fatigue before it drains budget.
- **Figma + Creative Brief Template**: Convert the creative brief output format from this prompt into a Figma component library brief card. When the AI generates a new brief, paste it directly into the Figma template — your design team gets a brief that includes visual direction, hook copy, and success thresholds in one place, reducing brief-to-launch cycle time.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Pipeline ROAS data is unavailable — my ad platforms don't connect to CRM pipeline data.**
Solution: Use a proxy metric hierarchy until CRM attribution is established: (1) MQL rate × average CPL × pipeline conversion rate = estimated cost-per-opportunity. If your average opp value is $60K and you know 1 in 4 MQLs becomes an opp, a $500 CPL creative at 60% MQL rate generates an opp for $833. This is estimable without CRM integration. For step-by-step CRM attribution setup, use the Multi-Touch Attribution prompt to close this gap permanently.

**Problem: The AI identifies the same 2–3 ads as winners every time, and the creative brief output looks similar to what I already have.**
Solution: Your creative portfolio likely has a homogeneity problem — all ads share the same hook type or format. Force creative diversity by adding a "creative hypothesis type" metadata field to each ad (Pattern-Interrupt / Social Proof / Fear-of-Loss / Aspiration / Education). If all your top performers cluster into one hypothesis type, explicitly prompt the AI: "Assume we need to test 2 alternative hypothesis types not currently represented in our top performers — write briefs for those, not variations of existing winners."

**Problem: LinkedIn creative performance looks strong on CTR but pipeline attribution is unclear because the sale cycle is 90+ days.**
Solution: For long-cycle B2B, shift your 30-day creative evaluation from pipeline ROAS to leading indicator proxies: (1) MQL rate per creative — this is your best short-cycle quality signal. (2) Opportunity creation rate at 60 days — pull a cohort of leads from 60 days ago and check which creative they entered through. (3) Deal velocity by creative source — are leads from your "Forecast Accuracy" doc ad progressing to discovery faster than leads from the logo wall? Use HubSpot's time-in-stage report segmented by original campaign UTM to surface this.
