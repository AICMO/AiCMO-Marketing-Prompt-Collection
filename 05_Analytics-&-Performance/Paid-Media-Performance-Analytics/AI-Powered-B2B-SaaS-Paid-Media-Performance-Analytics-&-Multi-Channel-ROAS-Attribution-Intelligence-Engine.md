**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25 min | **Tags:** paid-media, roas, attribution, ppc, linkedin-ads, google-ads, pipeline-attribution, budget-optimization, b2b-saas, revenue-intelligence, media-mix-modeling

## Overview
Transforms raw paid media spend and performance data across Google, LinkedIn, Meta, and other channels into a revenue-focused attribution intelligence report that identifies true pipeline contribution, surfaces budget waste, and produces a prioritized reallocation plan to improve ROAS without increasing total spend. Use this when board asks "what is paid media actually returning," when CPL is rising faster than pipeline, or when you need to defend or reallocate a paid media budget with data.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CPL looks low but pipeline quality is terrible — SQL rates are below 10% for paid channels.**
Solution: Your attribution is capturing quantity but not quality. Add ICP match scoring to your CRM and tag every paid lead with an ICP fit score (1-5) at the point of lead creation. Re-run this analysis filtering only for "ICP fit 4-5" leads to get true pipeline ROAS. You'll likely find one channel is generating volume noise that masks a much higher-performing source.

**Problem: LinkedIn is reporting 8x ROAS internally but our CRM shows only 2.1x pipeline ROAS from LinkedIn-tagged leads.**
Solution: This is the LinkedIn attribution inflation problem. LinkedIn's Conversions API counts view-through and click-through against almost anyone who saw your ad, inflating their reported numbers. Trust your CRM's first-touch or multi-touch attribution over LinkedIn's platform data. Run a geo-holdout incrementality test (recommended above) to establish true causal contribution. In the interim, cap your LinkedIn ROAS expectation at 60-70% of platform-reported as a rule of thumb.

**Problem: We have a 14-week sales cycle and most pipeline from paid hasn't closed yet — how do we measure ROAS accurately?**
Solution: Use pipeline ROAS (pipeline influenced ÷ spend) rather than revenue ROAS for in-quarter decisions. To project eventual revenue ROAS, apply your historical SQL-to-close rate and ACV to the current pipeline number. For example: $2M pipeline × 19% close rate × $85K ACV average = projected $380K closed-won. Build a cohort model that tracks all paid leads by quarter and measures their 6-month and 12-month revenue contribution — this is the gold standard for long-cycle B2B paid attribution.
