## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Meta-reported conversions are 4–6x higher than CRM-recorded MQLs from Meta**
- *Root Cause:* Meta's default attribution window (7-day click + 1-day view) counts conversions from users who saw a Meta ad in the last 24 hours and converted anywhere — including organic, direct, and email-driven conversions. View-through attribution is especially inflated for B2B with long purchase cycles, as every page visitor who later converts gets credited to Meta
- *Fix:* Change attribution window in Ads Manager to 7-day click ONLY (remove view-through) under Campaign Settings > Attribution Setting. Implement UTM-based CRM attribution as your primary revenue source of truth — Meta Ads Manager data is for optimization signal, not pipeline reporting. Cross-validate by building a GA4 report showing sessions from utm_source=facebook and comparing to Meta-reported link clicks (gap indicates bot traffic or attribution inflation). For pipeline reporting to leadership, always use CRM UTM-based data

**Problem: Facebook Lead Ad CPL is low ($38) but 90% of leads are non-ICP — wrong job titles, wrong company sizes**
- *Root Cause:* Facebook Lead Ads pre-populate profile data from the user's Facebook personal account — which for B2B professionals often contains outdated job titles, personal email addresses, or consumer-adjacent profile information. Meta's interest targeting for B2B is based on content engagement and behavior signals, not verified professional data, making it easy to capture non-ICP volume at low CPL
- *Fix:* Add qualifying questions to the Lead Ad form to introduce friction: "What is your job title?" (open text, not pre-populated), "How many employees does your company have?" (dropdown: Under 100 / 100–500 / 500–2,000 / 2,000+), and "What is your primary business challenge?" Require a business email address field (disable pre-population by using a custom question instead of the native email field). Layer in tighter audience targeting — use Custom Audiences from CRM (ICP-matching contacts) and Lookalike audiences seeded from closed-won customers rather than broad interest targeting. Consider switching high-intent campaigns to landing page conversion to capture stronger purchase intent signals through form friction

**Problem: Meta campaign performance suddenly drops 40% with no creative or budget changes — CPL doubles overnight**
- *Root Cause:* This is a Meta algorithm reset event — typically caused by: (1) a significant change to campaign structure triggering the learning phase restart (audience edit, bid change, budget change > 20%), (2) audience saturation hitting a cliff (reach exhaustion), (3) a Meta platform algorithm update affecting delivery, or (4) iOS update or browser privacy change impacting Pixel event matching quality
- *Fix:* First, check Events Manager for Pixel health — look for sudden drop in event match quality score or event volume. If Pixel is healthy, check if any team member made structural campaign changes in the past 7 days (even small edits can trigger learning phase reset). If saturation is the cause (frequency > 4.5, reach > 60% of audience), duplicate the campaign with a fresh audience segment rather than editing the existing one (editing triggers re-learning; duplicating preserves the learned model). If no obvious cause, give the campaign 5–7 days to restabilize before making further changes — Meta's algorithm frequently self-corrects within a learning phase window
