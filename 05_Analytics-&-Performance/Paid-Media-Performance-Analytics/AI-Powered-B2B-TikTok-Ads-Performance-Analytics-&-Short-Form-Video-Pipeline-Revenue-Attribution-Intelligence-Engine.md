ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK — Apply all of the following:

**1. REVENUE PERFORMANCE TIER ASSESSMENT**
Classify each campaign into one of four tiers (calibrated for TikTok B2B benchmarks, which differ materially from LinkedIn/Google):
- Tier 1 (Scale): Pipeline ROAS > 3x, CPL < $120, MQL rate > 18%, VTR > 20%
- Tier 2 (Optimize): Pipeline ROAS 1.5–3x, OR CPL $120–$220, VTR 12–20%
- Tier 3 (Test & Learn): Pipeline ROAS < 1.5x, campaign < 21 days old (insufficient data on TikTok's learning phase), OR new creative batch in first 7 days
- Tier 4 (Pause): CPL > $300, zero pipeline attribution after 30 days, 6-second view rate < 15%, or VTR < 10%

Note: Apply caution with Tier 4 on pure brand/reach campaigns — pipeline ROAS may not be the right primary metric if the objective is demand creation. Flag these separately.

**2. CREATIVE FATIGUE DIAGNOSTIC (TikTok-Specific)**
TikTok creative fatigue is 2–3x faster than LinkedIn or Meta due to high scroll velocity and content novelty expectations:
- Flag any creative running > 14 days with CTR declining > 20% week-over-week
- Flag any creative with 6-second view rate < 25% (hook is failing — audience scrolling past before engaging)
- Flag any creative with VTR < 15% at the 25% mark (content loses relevance in first 15 seconds)
- Apply the TikTok 3-Hook Rotation Rule: for every ad group, maintain 3 distinct hook styles (problem agitation, social proof/data hook, curiosity/pattern interrupt) to combat fatigue and algorithm learning reset
- Identify "zombie creatives": still spending budget but generating zero pipeline-attributed conversions for > 10 days

**3. ATTRIBUTION COMPLEXITY AUDIT**
TikTok view-through attribution is the most inflation-prone of all major paid social platforms:
- Identify the attribution window mismatch: TikTok's default 7-day click / 1-day view window vs. B2B sales cycles of 30–180 days
- Calculate the "Attribution Inflation Factor": (TikTok-reported conversions − CRM-verified TikTok-touched leads) ÷ TikTok-reported conversions × 100. Flag if inflation > 40%
- Recommend the correct B2B attribution window setting: click-through 7 days, view-through OFF (or maximum 1 day for brand-heavy campaigns) for direct-response campaigns
- Identify whether TikTok Events API (server-side) is implemented — if not, estimate undercounting from iOS 14.5+ signal loss and recommend implementation priority
- Cross-reference TikTok Ads Manager conversions vs. GA4 UTM-tagged sessions vs. CRM source data to triangulate true attribution

**4. ICP AUDIENCE QUALITY SCORING**
TikTok's B2B targeting capabilities are less precise than LinkedIn — assess audience quality with rigor:
- Score each ad group's audience targeting against ICP criteria: professional role match (using behavioral/interest proxies), company type signal (B2B software interest clusters), seniority proxy (age 28–45, interests in business management/entrepreneurship), geography fit
- Calculate "ICP Proxy Match Rate": estimated % of ad group audience that could plausibly be in-ICP
- Flag campaigns where low CPL is driven by B2C consumer traffic (common TikTok failure mode — wide audiences include high volumes of irrelevant impressions that convert on curiosity, not purchase intent)
- For retargeting and customer list campaigns, calculate ICP purity rate from CRM data (% of list members meeting full ICP criteria)

**5. SPARK ADS vs. IN-FEED ADS PERFORMANCE MATRIX**
Spark Ads (boosting organic TikTok content) and standard In-Feed Ads have distinct performance profiles:
- Compare on: CPM, 6-second view rate, VTR, CTR, engagement rate, CPL, MQL rate, brand lift indicators
- Spark Ads typically outperform on engagement metrics (comments, shares, saves) because they feel native — but may underperform on direct conversion metrics if the organic content wasn't optimized for a CTA
- Recommend: Spark Ads for top-of-funnel brand and demand creation campaigns; In-Feed Ads for direct response and demo request campaigns
- Flag any Spark Ad where the parent organic post has engagement rate < 3% before boosting — low organic engagement predicts poor paid performance

**6. BID STRATEGY & BUDGET PACING ANALYSIS**
- Identify campaigns in TikTok's "learning phase" (< 50 conversions in 7 days) — flag budget constraints limiting algorithm optimization
- Flag campaigns spending < 80% of daily budget (delivery constraints: audience too narrow, bid floor too low, or low creative quality score)
- Evaluate bid strategy fit: CPM (brand awareness) vs. oCPM optimized for click/conversion vs. Minimum ROAS bid strategy
- Identify budget concentration risk: any single campaign consuming > 45% of total monthly spend
- Recommend budget reallocation with projected CPL improvement based on tier assessment

**7. COMPETITIVE BENCHMARKING (B2B TikTok Ads)**

- Average CPM: $8–$18 (B2B software and SaaS targeting)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: TikTok Ads Manager reports 3x more conversions than CRM shows as TikTok-sourced leads**
- *Root Cause:* TikTok's view-through attribution window (default: 1-day view, 7-day click) is crediting conversions from users who saw any TikTok ad — even briefly — and later converted through an entirely different channel (Google Search, email, direct). In long B2B sales cycles, this inflates TikTok contribution dramatically.
- *Fix:* Navigate to TikTok Ads Manager → Campaign → Attribution Settings and set view-through window to 0 days (off) for all direct-response campaigns. Maintain click-through at 7 days. Accept that reported conversions will drop 30–60% — this reflects true TikTok-influenced pipeline more accurately. Cross-validate using GA4 UTM data and CRM source tracking as the dual source of truth. Implement TikTok Events API to improve signal accuracy from first-party data rather than relying on pixel-based view-through.

**Problem: TikTok CPL is low ($65) but MQL rate is only 5% and deals from TikTok leads never progress past discovery call**
- *Root Cause:* TikTok's broad interest and behavioral targeting is delivering high volumes of B2C consumer traffic that superficially resembles the target audience but lacks purchasing authority or enterprise budget. The low CPL is a false economy — it's driven by irrelevant volume, not ICP-quality conversions.
- *Fix:* Layer targeting restrictions: add age bracket 25–44, restrict to the top 3–4 interest categories most proximal to your B2B ICP (e.g., "Business Management," "Entrepreneurship," "Marketing Technology," "SaaS Software"), and add household income or education-level filters where available. Switch from TikTok Lead Gen Forms (frictionless, prone to low-intent submits) to a landing page with a qualifying question (e.g., "What is your company's headcount?" or "What is your annual software budget?") to introduce friction that filters out non-buyers. Consider building a retargeting-only TikTok strategy fed by high-quality first-party audiences from HubSpot and Salesforce rather than cold interest targeting.

**Problem: TikTok campaigns exit the "learning phase" slowly and CPL is erratic in the first 30 days**
- *Root Cause:* TikTok's algorithm requires a minimum of 50 optimization events per ad group per week to exit the learning phase and stabilize delivery. B2B advertisers with low conversion volume (common for demo request or free trial events with $15K–$25K monthly budgets) can take 3–6 weeks to accumulate enough signal, causing volatile CPL and unpredictable pacing.
- *Fix:* Use a micro-conversion strategy to accelerate learning: set the TikTok optimization event to a higher-funnel action with more volume (e.g., "View Content" on a key product page, or "Lead" for a content download) rather than "Complete Registration" for a free trial. Once the campaign exits learning phase on the micro-conversion, clone the campaign and retarget micro-converters with a direct free trial or demo request ask. Allocate a minimum of $4,000–$6,000 per ad group per month to give TikTok's algorithm enough budget to accumulate 50+ events weekly and optimize effectively.
