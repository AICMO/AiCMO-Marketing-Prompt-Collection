## Overview
Analyzes paid media performance across all channels (LinkedIn, Google, Meta, YouTube, programmatic, etc.) as a unified paid portfolio — tracking ROAS, pipeline contribution, cost per opportunity, and budget efficiency at both the channel level and the blended portfolio level. Use this when you need to defend or reallocate a paid media budget to the CMO or CFO, when paid channel performance is diverging and you need to understand why, or when you're scaling paid investment and need to know which channels deserve more capital before you commit.

**2. CHANNEL EFFICIENCY MATRIX**

For each paid channel, calculate and rank:
- Channel ROAS: Revenue closed (channel-attributed) ÷ channel spend
- Channel pipeline ROI: Pipeline sourced ÷ channel spend
- Channel influence efficiency: Influenced pipeline ÷ channel spend
- Cost per MQL: Channel spend ÷ MQLs attributed
- Cost per opportunity: Channel spend ÷ opportunities created
- Cost per pipeline dollar: Channel spend ÷ pipeline sourced
- Time-to-pipeline: Average days from first paid touch to opportunity creation (by channel)
- Budget allocation efficiency: What % of total pipeline does this channel produce vs. what % of budget does it consume? (Channels where contribution % > budget % = efficient; inverse = inefficient)

Channel role classification:
- **Demand Capture** (Google Search, branded/competitor keywords): High intent, direct pipeline, higher ROAS, limited scalability — optimize for efficiency
- **Demand Creation** (LinkedIn, YouTube, display, Meta): Lower direct ROAS, builds awareness pipeline, high influence value — optimize for reach and frequency
- **Demand Acceleration** (retargeting, ABM programmatic, review site ads): Converts existing awareness, high influence ÷ sourced ratio — optimize for audience freshness and creative rotation

Misclassification risk: Flag any channel being measured by the wrong KPI (e.g., measuring LinkedIn awareness by direct ROAS instead of influenced pipeline and TAL reach — a common CMO-CFO conflict point).

**3. PIPELINE CONTRIBUTION & DARK FUNNEL ANALYSIS**

First-touch vs. influenced pipeline by channel:
- Which channels source new pipeline (first-touch) vs. accelerate existing pipeline (influence)?
- Influence multiplier per channel: Influenced pipeline ÷ sourced pipeline. Channels with multiplier >2.5x are high-leverage awareness channels — cuts to them reduce pipeline months later, not this quarter.
- Dark funnel adjustment: Add 25-40% to attributed paid pipeline as a proxy for:
  * LinkedIn view-through conversions not captured in CRM (impressions without click)
  * SDR call-to-meeting lift driven by prior paid exposure (priming effect)
  * Organic search traffic from branded queries driven by paid awareness (paid-organic synergy)
  * Word-of-mouth referrals from ICP contacts who saw paid content but weren't the direct form fill
- Dark funnel methodology: Compare ICP accounts reached by paid media that later converted through organic, direct, or SDR channels vs. ICP accounts not reached by paid media. Conversion rate delta = estimated dark funnel contribution.

Audience overlap analysis:
- What % of paid audience reach overlaps across channels (e.g., same LinkedIn users also seeing Google Search ads and Meta retargeting)?
- Overlap is positive (multi-channel reinforcement increases conversion rate) or negative (frequency fatigue wastes spend on saturated audiences)?
- Recommendation: Should you expand reach to under-reached ICP segments or deepen frequency with engaged segments?

**4. DIMINISHING RETURNS & SATURATION DIAGNOSIS**

For each channel, assess saturation signals:
- CPM trend (last 3 months): Rising >15% MoM = audience saturation or auction pressure
- CTR trend: Declining >10% MoM = creative fatigue or audience exhaustion
- Impression frequency trend: LinkedIn >8 per account per quarter, Meta >15 per user per month = fatigue threshold
- Marginal MQL cost trend: Is the cost per MQL increasing as spend increases? Calculate marginal cost at different spend levels (if historical data is available across spend increments)
- Keyword coverage ceiling (Google Search): Impression share above 80% = you've captured available demand; additional spend yields diminishing returns — pipeline ceiling is the TAM for your keyword universe

Recommended spend caps:
- For each channel, identify the inflection point where marginal ROAS drops below 1.0x (you're spending more than you're returning)
- Express as: "LinkedIn saturates at approximately $X/quarter for this ICP size; above that, frequency exceeds 8 and CTR falls below 0.2% — incremental spend beyond this threshold should go to audience expansion or creative testing rather than increasing frequency on the existing audience"

**5. ATTRIBUTION MODEL RECONCILIATION**

Compare how channel contribution shifts under different attribution models:
- Last-touch: Which channels get credit? (Typically Google Search and retargeting win)
- First-touch: Which channels get credit? (Typically LinkedIn and content syndication win)
- Linear multi-touch: Credit distributed proportionally across all paid touches
- Time-decay: Recent touches (SDR outreach, retargeting) overweighted vs. awareness channels
- Position-based (W-shaped): First paid touch + MQL conversion + opp creation each get 30%; remaining 10% distributed

Model comparison table: Show each channel's attributed pipeline under each model. The variance between models reveals which channels are systematically under- or over-credited by your current model.

True north recommendation: For B2B SaaS with 60-180 day sales cycles, the W-shaped or custom position-based model most accurately represents how paid media generates revenue. If you're using last-touch only, LinkedIn and YouTube are guaranteed to look worse than they are — frame this for CMO/CFO when defending awareness spend.

## Success Metrics

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My LinkedIn attribution is near-zero in HubSpot because LinkedIn drives impressions and brand awareness but rarely generates the last-touch conversion — CFO thinks LinkedIn is wasted spend.**
Fix: Add this instruction to the prompt: "LinkedIn attribution is systematically under-counted in our last-touch model. Provide two LinkedIn analysis tracks: (1) Last-touch attributed metrics as reported in HubSpot, and (2) LinkedIn's true contribution using the following proxy: identify all closed deals this quarter, then cross-reference the buying company against LinkedIn Campaign Manager's Company Engagement Report to identify deals where the buying account was in the LinkedIn paid audience during the quarter. The deal count and ACV from that cross-reference is the 'LinkedIn-influenced revenue' estimate." This approach gives you a concrete, evidence-based LinkedIn influence number that doesn't require changing your attribution model — it just adds a parallel lookup. Present both numbers to the CFO: "HubSpot last-touch attributes $38K revenue to LinkedIn. Our account-level cross-reference analysis shows LinkedIn reached the buying team in 8 of our 11 Q2 closed deals, representing $312K in revenue influenced. The true contribution is somewhere between these numbers."

**Problem: Google Performance Max is reporting excellent ROAS in the Google Ads dashboard but the pipeline number in Salesforce is much lower than other channels — I can't tell if PMax is actually working.**
Fix: Add this data reconciliation step to the prompt: "Performance Max ROAS from Google Ads ($X) does not match Salesforce pipeline attribution for PMax ($Y). Possible explanations: (1) PMax is counting branded search conversions that would have happened organically anyway — add a brand exclusion to PMax and compare performance before/after; (2) PMax conversion tracking counts form fills but Salesforce only counts MQLs that meet lead score threshold — audit the conversion definition mismatch; (3) PMax is reporting view-through conversions inflating the number — check if view-through is enabled and what the lookback window is. Provide analysis assuming all three scenarios are partially true and recommend a 4-week PMax diagnostic test with a $10K holdout budget isolated from branded keywords."

**Problem: We have no paid media attribution at all — marketing is using UTM parameters inconsistently and CRM campaign tagging is broken — I have impression and click data but no pipeline data per channel.**
Fix: Add this instruction: "Attribution data is unavailable at the channel level. Use the following proxy approach: (1) Apply the company's known MQL-to-Opportunity rate (X%) and Opportunity-to-Close rate (X%) to each channel's MQL volume to estimate pipeline sourced per channel. (2) Apply the company ACV ($X) to estimate revenue per channel. (3) Flag all estimates as Low Confidence and include a data infrastructure recommendation section that specifies: exact UTM taxonomy to implement, Salesforce/HubSpot campaign tagging rules, lead source field mapping, and the 90-day plan to have channel-level attribution working before next quarter's analysis." The output should include both the estimated performance metrics AND a data quality remediation plan — otherwise you'll be running the same broken analysis every quarter.
