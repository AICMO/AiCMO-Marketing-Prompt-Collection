ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK — Execute in this sequence:

STEP 1 — PIPELINE ROAS ANALYSIS
For each channel and campaign:
- Calculate pipeline ROAS = pipeline sourced / spend
- Calculate revenue ROAS = closed won / spend (where data allows)
- Rank all campaigns by pipeline ROAS descending
- Flag any campaign with pipeline ROAS below 2:1 as "under review"
- Flag any campaign with pipeline ROAS above 8:1 as "scale candidate"

STEP 2 — CPL EFFICIENCY ANALYSIS
Apply the LONA Framework (Lead Origin, Lead Quality, Lead Nurture Pathway, Attribution Accuracy):
- Lead Origin: Is CPL rising due to competition, audience exhaustion, or bid changes?
- Lead Quality: Is MQL rate degrading even if lead volume holds?
- Nurture Pathway: Are leads converting to opportunities at historical rates?
- Attribution Accuracy: Is last-click attribution masking assisted channel value?

Identify the top 3 CPL outliers (over- or under-performing vs. target by > 25%) and diagnose root cause for each.

STEP 3 — AUDIENCE SATURATION & FREQUENCY AUDIT
For LinkedIn and Meta:
- Flag any audience with frequency > 3 (awareness fatigue risk)
- Calculate audience penetration rate = reached / total audience size
- Identify audiences > 70% penetrated as "exhausted — expand or refresh"
- Recommend lookalike or intent-based audience expansions where saturation detected

For Google Ads:
- Review search impression share by campaign — flag < 50% as "under-invested"
- Review Quality Score distribution — flag ad groups with QS < 6 as "drag on CPL"
- Identify search term report anomalies (high-spend terms with zero pipeline)

STEP 4 — CREATIVE PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
Using the Hook-Bridge-CTA Framework:
- Hook: Which headlines/images are driving above-median CTR?
- Bridge: Which ad formats (document ads, video, single image, carousel) are converting to form fills at highest rates?
- CTA: Which offer types (demo, free trial, webinar, report) produce lowest CPL AND highest MQL rate?
- Identify the 2 creatives to pause and 2 creatives to scale

STEP 5 — BUDGET REALLOCATION MODEL
Build a reallocation recommendation using Diminishing Returns logic:
- Which campaigns are past the efficiency frontier (CPL rising with each additional $)?
- Which campaigns have headroom to scale without CPL degradation?
- Model: if [X dollars] move from [Campaign A] to [Campaign B], expected impact on CPL and pipeline
- Produce a specific reallocation table: From | To | Amount | Expected Pipeline Delta

STEP 6 — CROSS-CHANNEL INFLUENCE MAPPING
Using available data, identify:
- Which channel most often appears first in multi-touch journeys?
- Which channel most often appears as last touch before opportunity creation?
- Are there compounding channel effects (e.g., LinkedIn awareness + Google retargeting = lower CPL)?
- Recommended: increase investment in channel combinations showing compounding effects

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Pipeline ROAS looks wrong — the numbers don't match what Salesforce shows.**
Solution: Verify attribution model alignment. This prompt uses pipeline sourced (opportunities created with campaign influence) — not closed won. Ensure your CRM is configured with Campaign Influence enabled and that all opportunities have a "Primary Campaign Source" populated. If < 60% of opps have campaign source data, your pipeline ROAS will be understated by 2x or more. Fix CRM hygiene first using the Marketing Operations Data Quality prompt.

**Problem: LinkedIn CPL appears 4–6x higher than Google Ads CPL, making it look like a poor investment.**
Solution: CPL comparisons across channels are misleading without MQL rate normalization. A $750 LinkedIn lead that converts at 70% MQL rate produces a $1,071 cost-per-MQL. A $150 Meta lead at 12% MQL rate produces a $1,250 cost-per-MQL. Always normalize to cost-per-MQL or cost-per-opportunity before making budget allocation decisions. This prompt calculates pipeline ROAS as the primary metric — use that as your budget arbiter, not raw CPL.

**Problem: The AI is recommending budget moves but I can't tell if the projected pipeline impact is realistic.**
Solution: Instruct the AI to provide confidence ratings (High/Medium/Low) on each projection, based on how much historical data supports the recommendation. Projections based on < 30 days of data or < 20 conversions per campaign should be labeled Low Confidence. Validate projections by running a 2-week "shadow budget" test — reallocate 20% of the recommended amount first and measure actual CPL and MQL rate before committing the full reallocation.
