## Integration Tips

- **Google Ads + Looker Studio:** Export YouTube campaign data via the Google Ads API into Looker Studio; build a dedicated YouTube performance dashboard with VTR, CPV, and pipeline ROAS tracked weekly against targets
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Import Google Ads offline conversions (MQLs, SQLs, Closed-Won) back into Google Ads to enable smart bidding optimization toward actual pipeline revenue, not just form fills
- **GA4 Cross-Channel Path Analysis:** Use GA4's Advertising Snapshot and Path Exploration to identify what % of closed deals touched a YouTube ad in the 30, 60, and 90 days before converting — validates or challenges your attribution window
- **Salesforce Campaign Influence:** Tag YouTube ad-sourced leads with a YouTube UTM campaign source in HubSpot/Salesforce; use Campaign Influence reporting to measure YouTube's weighted contribution to pipeline across all stages

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: View-through conversions are massively inflating pipeline numbers, making YouTube look far more valuable than it actually is.**
Solution: Run the attribution calibration in Section 6 — compare 1-day, 7-day, and 30-day view-through windows side-by-side. For B2B sales cycles >90 days, a 7-day view-through window typically provides a more defensible pipeline attribution. Cross-reference with GA4 path analysis to validate. Present both conservative (7-day) and liberal (30-day) estimates to leadership with clear methodology notes.

**Problem: VTR is declining week-over-week but CPL is still within range — unclear whether to refresh creative.**
Solution: Check frequency per user first (target: <3 views/week for mid-funnel). If frequency is above threshold, pause the fatigued creative even if CPL hasn't degraded yet — CPL degradation typically lags fatigue by 2–3 weeks. Use the Creative Health Index to score momentum: a declining VTR trend is an early warning system, not a lagging indicator.

**Problem: YouTube generates lots of view-through conversions, but the MQL-to-SQL rate from YouTube-sourced leads is significantly below channel average.**
Solution: This signals ICP misalignment in your audience targeting — you're reaching buyers who watch but aren't true ICP fits. Run the Audience Intent Depth Scoring from Section 4: compare MQL-to-SQL rate by audience type (custom intent vs. in-market vs. topic vs. customer match). Shift budget toward segments with both acceptable CPL AND strong downstream SQL conversion — even if their raw CPL is 30–40% higher.
