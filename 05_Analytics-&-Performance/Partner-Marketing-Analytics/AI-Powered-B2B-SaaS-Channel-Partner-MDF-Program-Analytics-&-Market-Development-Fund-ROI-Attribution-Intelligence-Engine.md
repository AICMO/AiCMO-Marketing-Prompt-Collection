## Overview

DELIVERABLE 2 — PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION ARCHITECTURE

A. Partner Attribution Framework (3-Layer Model)
Layer 1: Partner-Sourced Revenue
- Opportunity created directly from an MDF-funded campaign (partner generated the first meaningful interaction)
- Attribution rule: partner tagged as Primary Source in CRM, campaign linked in opportunity record
- Metric: Partner-Sourced ARR as % of total new ARR

Layer 2: Partner-Influenced Revenue
- Partner participated in the deal but didn't source it (attended event, provided reference, co-sold on late-stage deal)
- Attribution rule: partner activity logged in CRM within 90-day lookback window before opportunity close
- Metric: Partner-Influenced ARR (total pipeline partner touched)

Layer 3: MDF-Specific Attribution
- Subset of partner-sourced/influenced revenue specifically traceable to MDF-funded campaign activity
- Attribution rule: opportunity contains UTM or campaign tracking link from MDF-approved campaign, OR partner attests in reimbursement request
- Metric: MDF-Attributed Pipeline per dollar of MDF invested (the primary ROI metric)

B. UTM & Campaign Tracking Architecture
Design a standardized campaign tracking taxonomy for all MDF-funded digital activities:
- UTM structure: utm_source=partner | utm_medium=[campaign_type] | utm_campaign=[partner_id]-[YYYYQ#]-[activity_code]
- PRM integration: how to pass UTM data from partner campaign tools back to CRM opportunity records
- Event attendance tracking: registration → attendance → follow-up meeting → opportunity creation attribution chain
- Offline campaign attribution: trade show leads → MDF partner → opportunity (phone/form match methodology)
- Self-reported attribution: when to use partner attestation vs. digital tracking (B2B enterprise sales cycle reality)

C. Sales Cycle Intelligence

- Days from MDF campaign execution to first opportunity creation
- Days from opportunity creation to close (partner-sourced vs. direct sourced comparison)
- Win rate: partner-sourced MDF deals vs. direct marketing MDF deals vs. no-MDF partner deals
- Average deal size: MDF-influenced deals vs. non-MDF partner deals
- Multi-touch: average number of partner touchpoints in deals that close
- Deal risk: MDF campaigns that generate high MQL volume but low SQL conversion (signal of poor partner ICP targeting)

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce**: Create a custom "MDF Campaign" object linked to Opportunities; use Campaign Influence to attribute partner-sourced pipeline; build a Partner ROI Dashboard using Salesforce Analytics (Einstein)
- **HubSpot**: Use Custom Properties on Deals to capture MDF campaign source; build Partner ROI reports in HubSpot Reporting via Associated Contact and Company filters
- **Impartner/PartnerStack**: Connect PRM reimbursement data via API to CRM opportunity records; automate partner ROI report delivery via PRM's built-in email workflows
- **Google Sheets/Looker Studio**: Build a weekly MDF ops tracker in Google Sheets with auto-refresh from Salesforce/HubSpot exports; visualize partner ROI scoring in Looker Studio for channel team and executive review
- **Zapier/Make.com**: Trigger automatic MDF request review notifications when new submissions arrive in PRM; auto-create CRM tasks for partner follow-up when deals stall post-MDF campaign
- **Crossbeam/Reveal**: Use partner ecosystem intelligence platforms to identify account overlap and prioritize MDF investment toward partners with strongest white-space coverage in your target accounts

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Partners report pipeline from MDF campaigns, but it doesn't appear in CRM — "pipeline ghost" syndrome
**Solution**: Implement a mandatory CRM tagging SLA: partners must submit the Salesforce Opportunity ID or HubSpot Deal link with every reimbursement request. Without it, the campaign is counted as $0 pipeline ROI. Communicate this policy change 60 days before enforcement starts and provide partners with a simple "How to log a partner-sourced deal in Salesforce" guide. For deals that genuinely require confidentiality, allow partner attestation with a signed form, but cap unverified attestation at 25% of any partner's reported pipeline.

**Problem**: Top partners resist the new ROI scoring model — "we've been loyal partners for 8 years and now you're scoring us like we're new"
**Solution**: Grandfather legacy partners with a "Partner Heritage Bonus" that adds 0.5 points to their composite score for each year of active partnership (max 2.0 bonus). This protects long-standing relationships while still incentivizing performance improvement. Frame the new model not as punishment but as "we're investing more in our best performers" — emphasize the upside for high-scoring partners (larger fund pools, co-selling support, executive access) rather than the downside for low scorers.

**Problem**: Finance refuses to approve the $800K MDF budget without clear ROI proof, but you can't prove ROI without the budget to run the campaigns
**Solution**: Run a 90-day MDF Proof of Concept with your top 5 partners and $150K of the budget. Implement rigorous UTM tracking and CRM attribution for this subset. After 90 days, present CFO with verified ROI data from the controlled cohort. Use the 90-day results to extrapolate full-program ROI and get budget approval with hard data instead of projections. Most finance teams will approve the full program when shown even one quarter of clean pipeline attribution data.
