## Overview
Diagnoses how generative AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude) are eroding your organic traffic, quantifies revenue risk per content category, and produces a prioritized protection and migration playbook. Use this when you detect unexplained traffic declines despite stable rankings, when zero-click search is accelerating, or when you need to brief your CMO and CFO on the strategic risk of AI search disruption to your organic pipeline.

## Integration Tips

- **Google Search Console**: Export Performance data (6-month comparison, filtered by page and query) as the primary data input. The Queries tab filtered by pages with declining CTR despite stable/growing impressions is your AI cannibalization detection layer.
- **GA4**: Use Landing Page reports filtered to organic source/medium to correlate session-level data with MQL events. Create a custom segment for "organic sessions → MQL conversion" to quantify pipeline impact directly.
- **Semrush/Ahrefs**: Use the "AI Overview" filter in Position Tracking to identify which of your tracked keywords are now showing AI-generated answers. Export this weekly and feed into the monitoring framework.
- **HubSpot/Salesforce**: Create a custom property for "organic content first-touch" to track which pages are in the attribution path of closed-won deals — this prevents over-reacting to session-level declines on pages that still drive pipeline through longer nurture cycles.
- **Perplexity/ChatGPT manual audit**: Run a monthly 50-query audit: paste your top revenue-driving queries into Perplexity and note which brands appear with citations. Track this in a simple Google Sheet month-over-month as your competitive AI citation share-of-voice tracker.
- **Zapier automation**: Set up a weekly GSC → Google Sheets → Slack alert workflow: when CTR for tracked keywords drops >10% week-over-week, trigger an alert to your SEO and content Slack channels with the affected pages listed.
- **Notion/Confluence**: Store the Content Category ARIA Vulnerability Matrix in your marketing wiki as a living document. Update quarterly as AI search behavior evolves (Google rolls out AI Overviews expansions roughly every quarter).

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: I don't have CRM pipeline data linked to organic traffic — I can't quantify revenue at risk.**
Solution: Use a proxy model. Take your total organic sessions, apply your known site-wide MQL rate (even if not source-segmented), and build the revenue model from there with a conservative attribution discount (e.g., 60% of organic MQLs are "organic-influenced" even if not last-touch). This gives CFOs a directional number, not a perfect one — which is sufficient for the investment case. Once the program is approved, instrument proper UTM-based organic attribution as the first 30-day deliverable.

**Problem: My impressions AND clicks are both declining — how do I know if it's AI cannibalization vs ranking drops vs seasonality?**
Solution: Cross-reference average position data. If position is stable (within 2 positions) while clicks fall, it's almost certainly AI cannibalization or intent shift. If position is declining alongside clicks, you have a ranking problem, not an AI problem — the standard SEO performance analytics prompt is the right tool for that situation. If you see seasonality patterns (same decline in prior years), factor that out before attributing to AI.

**Problem: The GEO playbook recommends original research, but we don't have budget or time to run a survey.**
Solution: Original research doesn't require a formal survey. Analyze your own product data (anonymized and aggregated) to produce benchmarks: "Based on [X,XXX] Rampify users, the average sales rep responds to inbound leads in [Y] hours..." This is proprietary, AI-unsynthesize-able data that drives citations. Alternatively, mine public data (LinkedIn, G2 reviews, job postings) and synthesize a trend report — this takes 2-3 days and costs nothing except analyst time, but produces citation-worthy content.
