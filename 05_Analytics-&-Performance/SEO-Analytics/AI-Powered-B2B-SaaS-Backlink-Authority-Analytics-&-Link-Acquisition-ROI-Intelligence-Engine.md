## Overview
Analyzes your backlink profile against competitors to quantify link acquisition ROI, identify toxic links, surface high-value link gaps, and generate a prioritized outreach pipeline ranked by domain authority impact and estimated ranking improvement. Use this when you need to justify link-building investment to finance, diagnose why a competitor is outranking you despite similar content, or identify the highest-leverage digital PR targets for next quarter.

## Integration Tips

- **Ahrefs Site Explorer:** Export the Referring Domains report (filter: dofollow, DR ≥ 20, one link per domain) for your domain and each competitor. Export as CSV and paste directly into the Advanced prompt's competitor fields. Use the "Link Intersect" tool to automatically identify domains linking to competitors but not you — paste this report as your Competitor Link Gap input.
- **SEMrush Backlink Analytics:** Use the Backlink Gap tool to compare your profile vs. 4 competitors simultaneously. Export the "Best" filter (domains linking to all competitors but not you) as your priority outreach list.
- **Google Search Console:** Cross-reference GSC's "Links" report (top linking sites) with Ahrefs data to identify which high-authority links are actually passing PageRank vs. being nofollow. GSC shows Google's view; Ahrefs shows estimated authority.
- **HubSpot/Salesforce:** Tag every organic contact with their entry keyword. When a new organic MQL converts from a keyword that recently improved in rankings, attribute that conversion to the link-building campaign that moved the ranking. Build a custom "Link ROI" field in your CRM that tracks estimated pipeline per referring domain acquired.
- **Google Disavow Tool:** Submit your disavow file via Google Search Console → Legacy Tools → Disavow Links. Submit as a plain text .txt file with one domain or URL per line. Only disavow domains where you have clear evidence of spam — over-disavowing can hurt authority.
- **Pitchbox / Respona:** Import the Competitor Link Gap table directly as a prospecting list in your outreach CRM. Tag each prospect by outreach angle (research citation / guest contribution / product review / broken link replacement) to track response rates by strategy.
- **Zapier/Make.com Automation:** Set up an Ahrefs API trigger that fires when you gain or lose a referring domain with DR ≥ 50. Connect to a Slack notification and automatically feed the domain data into this prompt to generate an instant link intelligence alert with competitive context.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "My Domain Rating hasn't moved in 6 months despite acquiring 40+ new links."**
Solution: DR is a logarithmic scale — moving from DR 50 to DR 60 requires significantly more link equity than moving from DR 20 to DR 30. Audit the quality, not just the volume: if your 40 new links are averaging DR 25–35, they're contributing minimal DR lift. Focus acquisition on domains DR 50+ (even 10 high-DR links will move the needle more than 100 low-DR links). Also check for link loss — if you're acquiring 40 but losing 35, net growth is only 5, which produces negligible DR movement. Run a Lost Links audit in Ahrefs and prioritize reclaiming recently lost high-DR links first.

**Problem: "The competitor link gap analysis shows 500+ domains I should target — the list is overwhelming."**
Solution: Apply a three-filter prioritization: (1) DR ≥ 60 only — the top 20% of domains produce 80% of the authority impact; (2) Topical relevance — domains that cover your specific industry vertical (not just general business or tech news); (3) Acquisition difficulty — eliminate domains that exclusively publish in-house content (no guest posts, no external citations) unless you have a legitimate news hook. This typically reduces a 500-domain list to 30–50 genuinely actionable targets you can work through in 90 days.

**Problem: "We received a Google manual action for 'unnatural links' — what do we do?"**
Solution: Do not panic and mass-disavow everything — that can remove legitimate authority links. First, request reconsideration and document all your link-building activities as editorial and non-manipulative. Then run a full backlink audit using this prompt's Toxic Link Audit section, focusing specifically on links acquired through paid schemes, private blog networks, or bulk link-buying services. Disavow only demonstrably manipulative links — keep all editorially earned links even if they came from low-DR sites. Submit a reconsideration request through Google Search Console with a detailed explanation of what was removed and a commitment to editorial-only link building going forward.
