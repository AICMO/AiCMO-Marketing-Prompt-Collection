## ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### STEP 1: COHORT VELOCITY DIAGNOSTIC

For each pipeline cohort (by segment, source, and persona), calculate the Velocity Index:

**Velocity Index = (Historical Median Days / Current Avg Days in Stage) × (Current Conversion Rate / Benchmark Conversion Rate)**

Scoring:
- Index > 1.0: Faster than benchmark — healthy, maintain current motion
- Index 0.7–1.0: Lagging — monitor, consider light-touch acceleration
- Index < 0.7: At-risk — intervene immediately with prescribed plays below

**Root Cause Classification for Each Underperforming Cohort:**
Classify the primary blocker using this taxonomy:
- **URGENCY**: No compelling event driving a decision; status quo comfortable
- **VALUE**: ROI and business case not quantified for economic buyer
- **CHAMPION**: Champion lost job, changed role, or lacks internal authority
- **BUDGET**: CFO/finance approval bottleneck, budget freeze, or fiscal cycle mismatch
- **COMPETITIVE**: Active competitive evaluation lengthening the cycle
- **TECHNICAL**: IT, security, or legal review creating delays
- **CONSENSUS**: Multi-stakeholder buying committee not aligned

### STEP 2: TIME-TO-REVENUE SCENARIO MODELING

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** No historical benchmark data to compare current velocity against
**Solution:** Use published B2B SaaS industry medians as a proxy baseline, then refine with your own data after 60 days. Published medians for sales-led B2B SaaS: MQL→SAL 2–3 days; SAL→SQL 5–7 days; SQL→Demo 7–10 days; Demo→Proposal 10–18 days; Proposal→Close 21–45 days for mid-market; 45–90 days for enterprise. Adjust by ±20% for your ACV (lower ACV moves faster; higher ACV slower). Flag to stakeholders that benchmarks are industry proxy until your own cohort data accumulates.

**Problem:** CRM data is unreliable — stage dates are wrong, deals stuck in wrong stages
**Solution:** Before running this analysis, conduct a 30-minute CRM hygiene sprint with RevOps: (1) export all open opportunities to a Google Sheet, (2) have each AE confirm their top 5 deals' actual stage and stage entry date in 15 minutes, (3) use "opportunity created date" as a conservative proxy for stage entry date where stage-change timestamps are missing. The analysis works directionally with imperfect data — you need approximate velocity signals, not actuals, to prescribe the right plays. Flag data quality issues in the executive brief.

**Problem:** Sales team is resistant to marketing-driven deal rescue ("don't touch my pipeline")
**Solution:** Position velocity plays as a menu the AE controls, not campaigns marketing runs unilaterally. In your Week 1 AE briefing, present the Cohort Velocity Matrix as "here is what the data shows about your deals" — let the Velocity Index speak for itself. For each stalled deal, show the AE the 3 available plays and let them choose which to activate. Frame marketing's role as "I'll execute the plays you select, on your behalf, without you lifting a finger." AEs who own quota are almost always receptive when you lead with data that shows they're at risk of missing, and you offer to do the execution work.
