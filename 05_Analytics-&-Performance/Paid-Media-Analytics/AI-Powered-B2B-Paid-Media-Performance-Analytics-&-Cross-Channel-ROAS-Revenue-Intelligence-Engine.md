## Overview
Analyzes paid media performance across all channels (Google Ads, LinkedIn, Meta, programmatic) to identify ROAS optimization opportunities, detect budget waste, attribute pipeline and revenue contribution, and generate a prioritized reallocation action plan. Use this when you need to justify paid media spend to the CFO, rebalance budget mid-quarter, or diagnose why CPL is rising.

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem:** Pipeline ROAS data is unavailable because CRM attribution isn't set up.
  **Solution:** Start with cost-per-SQL as the primary optimization metric (pull SQL counts from CRM filtered by lead source). Then run the attribution setup as a parallel workstream — use UTM parameters + HubSpot/Salesforce source fields. Even rough attribution (last-touch) is vastly better than optimizing on MQL volume alone.

- **Problem:** The AI recommends cutting LinkedIn, but the sales team says deals from LinkedIn are higher quality.
  **Solution:** The prompt is working on your data — if LinkedIn's pipeline ROAS looks weak, the CRM attribution is likely not capturing LinkedIn's influence on deals sourced elsewhere (dark funnel / multi-touch). Add the attribution analysis section and compare first-touch vs. last-touch vs. linear attribution. LinkedIn often appears in early-stage research but gets no last-touch credit. Run a 30-day holdout test on one LinkedIn audience segment to measure incrementality.

- **Problem:** Data from multiple ad platforms is hard to normalize (different metric definitions across Google, LinkedIn, Meta).
  **Solution:** Standardize on these definitions before inputting data: "Lead" = form fill or demo request; "MQL" = lead that meets ICP criteria and passes lead scoring threshold; "SQL" = lead accepted by sales and entered into active sequence. Map each platform's native metrics to these definitions in a legend before analysis.
