## Overview
Transforms the registration window (typically 2–4 weeks before a webinar goes live) into an active pipeline generation period: scores every registrant against your ICP at sign-up, tiers them by deal potential, alerts sales to high-value accounts already in the pipeline, and triggers personalized pre-event nurture sequences by segment — so your webinar generates pipeline before a single attendee joins the room.

### Analysis Framework

**Section 1: Registrant ICP Scoring System**

Score each registrant across 5 dimensions, each worth a maximum of 20 points (total 100 points):

*Dimension 1 — Role Authority (0–20 pts):*
- 20 pts: Economic buyer / primary decision-maker (e.g., CFO, VP Finance)
- 14 pts: Strong influencer / secondary decision-maker (e.g., Finance Director, Head of FP&A)
- 8 pts: Practitioner / influencer (e.g., Senior FP&A Analyst, Finance Manager)
- 0 pts: Disqualifying title (junior, admin, student, competitor)

*Dimension 2 — Company Fit (0–20 pts):*
- 20 pts: Matches both ICP company size AND qualifying industry
- 14 pts: Matches company size, industry unclear or secondary
- 8 pts: Matches industry, company size outside primary range but adjacent
- 0 pts: Clearly disqualifying (too small, wrong industry, competitor domain)

*Dimension 3 — Pipeline & CRM Status (0–20 pts):*
- 20 pts: Contact is attached to an active open opportunity (any stage)
- 16 pts: Contact is an MQL not yet in active opportunity
- 12 pts: Contact is known but cold — no prior engagement or opportunity
- 8 pts: Net-new contact from a known ABM target account
- 4 pts: Net-new contact from an unknown company that matches ICP firmographics
- 0 pts: Net-new contact, company doesn't match ICP

*Dimension 4 — Buying Intent Signals (0–20 pts):*
- 20 pts: Active 6sense/Bombora intent spike for your category in last 14 days
- 14 pts: Recent high-value website behavior (pricing page, ROI calculator, demo page — last 30 days)
- 8 pts: Content engagement in last 90 days (content download, prior webinar attendance)
- 0 pts: No intent signals detected

*Dimension 5 — Technology & Competitive Fit (0–20 pts):*
- 20 pts: Uses positive technology signal (complementary stack — indicates sophistication)
- 14 pts: No technology data available (unknown — neutral)
- 4 pts: Uses a competitive solution (currently a competitor customer — higher barrier to switch)
- 0 pts: Uses a technology that disqualifies the fit (e.g., built in-house equivalent of your product)

**ICP Tier Classification:**

| Tier | Score Range | Label | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | 80–100 | Immediate Sales Alert | Personalized SDR outreach within 24 hours of registration; AE briefed if late-stage opportunity |
| Tier 2 | 60–79 | High-Value Nurture | SDR LinkedIn + email sequence within 48 hours; invite to ask a question pre-event |
| Tier 3 | 40–59 | Standard Nurture | Automated pre-event email sequence; no SDR outreach unless they trigger a behavioral signal |
| Tier 4 | 0–39 | Awareness Audience | Confirmation + reminder emails only; minimal marketing investment; suppress from SDR queue |

**Section 4: Registration Quality Diagnosis & Mid-Campaign Optimization**

Assess the current registration cohort's pipeline potential in four dimensions:

*4a) ICP Concentration Analysis*
- Calculate: % of current registrants who are Tier 1 or Tier 2
- Benchmark: For a B2B SaaS demand-gen webinar, ≥35% Tier 1+2 is healthy; 25–35% is acceptable; <25% indicates the promotion is attracting a broad but low-conversion audience
- If ICP concentration is below benchmark: identify which registration sources are importing non-ICP contacts (by UTM source) and recommend immediate promotion reallocation — shift spend from low-ICP sources to channels with historically higher ICP match rates
- Predict: if current ICP concentration holds through the event, estimate expected MQLs, SQLs, and pipeline based on historical webinar conversion rates by tier

*4b) Account Coverage Analysis*
- Of the registrants who are Tier 1 or Tier 2, how many represent accounts not currently in pipeline?
- How many are from ABM target accounts without an active opportunity? (This is the most important metric — a Tier 1 registrant from an ABM target account is a pipeline creation trigger, not just a webinar metric)
- Recommendation: immediately create a "webinar-triggered ABM expansion" workflow for these accounts — sales alert + LinkedIn engagement + 1:1 direct mail if warranted by deal size

*4c) Pipeline Influence Potential*
- Of registrants who are in active open opportunities, calculate: (# of registrations × estimated acceleration impact)
- Benchmark: webinar attendance reduces average sales cycle length by 15–30% for deals where multiple buying committee members attend
- Recommendation: for Tier 1 contacts in late-stage opportunities (Stage 4+), alert the account AE immediately — this is a high-leverage "buying signal" moment to schedule a follow-on call immediately after the webinar

*4d) Registration Pace Assessment*
- Project final registrant count at current daily pace
- Calculate: at projected final count and current ICP concentration, what's the expected pipeline outcome?
- If behind target: recommend specific promotional actions with estimated impact on final registrant volume and ICP mix
- If ahead of target: flag any signs of audience quality dilution (high volume but low ICP match rate) and recommend targeted promotion channel tuning to improve quality before close

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot (MAP + CRM):**
- Build a Workflow triggered by webinar registration that immediately fires a webhook to an enrichment tool (Clearbit Enrichment API), waits 2 minutes for enrichment to complete, then evaluates ICP scoring properties and sets a custom "Webinar ICP Tier" Contact property (values: Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 / Tier 4)
- Create a separate HubSpot List for each tier, filtered on "Webinar ICP Tier = [Tier X] AND Registered for Webinar = [Webinar Name]" — enroll each list into its corresponding pre-event email sequence automatically

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Enrichment data is incomplete or unreliable — many registrants have inaccurate titles, missing company sizes, or wrong industries, making ICP scoring unreliable.
**Fix:** Enrich at two points, not one. First enrichment at registration (automated via Clearbit/ZoomInfo webhook). Second enrichment manually or via LinkedIn for Tier 1 contacts that the SDR will be calling anyway — SDRs should verify title and company before outreach, which also updates the CRM record. For the rest, use a "fuzzy ICP" score: if title is missing but company domain matches a known target account, classify as Tier 2 (the account signal is stronger than the missing title). Build this exception into your scoring logic with a "ABM Account Override" flag that auto-elevates to Tier 2 regardless of enrichment completeness.

**Problem:** The SDR team ignores the Tier 1 alerts — they're busy with other pipeline priorities and the webinar registrant outreach doesn't happen before the event.
**Fix:** This is a prioritization and incentive problem, not a data problem. Three changes to make: (1) Get sales leadership to add "pre-event Tier 1 contact rate" as a tracked metric in the weekly sales standup — what gets measured gets done; (2) Show reps the data: in a webinar you ran 6 months ago, Tier 1 contacts who received pre-event outreach converted to MQL at 3x the rate of Tier 1 contacts who didn't — this makes the business case concrete; (3) Make the outreach as low-friction as possible — provide pre-written LinkedIn message templates personalized to each contact so the rep's job is to review, click send, and log the activity in CRM; don't ask reps to write from scratch.

**Problem:** Registration volume is hitting target but ICP concentration is low (e.g., 20% full ICP match) because broad-reach promotion channels (Google Ads, LinkedIn broad targeting) are driving high registrant volume but low-quality audiences.
**Fix:** Shift promotion strategy from volume optimization to quality optimization with 7+ days remaining. Three specific tactics: (1) Pause or reduce spend on broad-reach channels (generic LinkedIn Sponsored Content, broad Google Display) and reallocate to intent-based channels — LinkedIn Sponsored Content with job title AND seniority filters, LinkedIn Sponsored InMail to a list of named target accounts, email to a curated list of ICP contacts who haven't registered yet; (2) Activate partner co-promotions for the remaining window — a send from a trusted industry newsletter or CFO advisory firm will generate smaller but much more ICP-concentrated registrant volume; (3) Enable an SDR-led "VIP invitation" campaign: give each SDR 10 named accounts from the ABM target list and have them personally invite the ICP contact via LinkedIn message. This generates small but very high-ICP registrant increments, and the personalized invite improves pre-event relationship with the account regardless of whether they attend.
