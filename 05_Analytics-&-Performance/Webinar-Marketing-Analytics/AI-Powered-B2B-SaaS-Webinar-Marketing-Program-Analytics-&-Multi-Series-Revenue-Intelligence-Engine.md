### Analysis Framework

**Section 1: Program Health Scorecard**
Rate the program on 8 dimensions using traffic-light scoring (🟢 At/above benchmark, 🟡 10-25% below, 🔴 >25% below benchmark). For each, show this quarter's actual, the benchmark, and the QoQ trend (▲ Improving, → Stable, ▼ Declining):

1. *Registration Volume Efficiency* — total registrants per $1,000 spent vs. channel benchmark ($200-$400/registrant for promoted webinars)
2. *Audience ICP Concentration* — % of registrants matching ICP profile; benchmark: >60% ICP-qualified for demand gen-focused webinars
3. *Attendance Rate* — registration-to-live-attendance; benchmark: 38-52% for properly nurtured registrant lists
4. *Engagement Depth* — % of live attendees who took ≥1 active engagement (poll, Q&A, chat, CTA); benchmark: 55-70%
5. *MQL Conversion Rate* — attendees who become MQLs within 7 days; benchmark: 7-14% for well-targeted programs
6. *Pipeline Efficiency* — pipeline sourced per $1 spent; benchmark: 5:1 to 12:1 ROI for mature webinar programs
7. *Database Growth Health* — net new ICP-qualified contacts added per quarter; benchmark: 15-25% of total registrant volume should be net-new ICP contacts
8. *Audience Engagement Longevity* — repeat attendance rate (% attending 2+ webinars); benchmark: 20-35% repeat for healthy series

**Section 2: Series Performance Matrix**
For each active series, calculate and rank on:
- *Pipeline Efficiency Score* (0-100): weighted blend of pipeline-per-dollar-spent (50%) and MQL-to-SQL rate (50%)
- *Audience Quality Score* (0-100): weighted blend of ICP match rate (40%), attendance rate (30%), engagement depth (30%)
- *Series Momentum Score* (0-100): weighted blend of QoQ registrant growth (40%), repeat attendance rate (30%), on-demand demand trend (30%)

Classify each series as:
- **Scale** — top quartile on ≥2 of 3 scores; increase investment 20-30%
- **Maintain** — mid-range performance; hold investment, test 1-2 format optimizations
- **Revitalize** — declining on ≥2 scores but covers high-value ICP segment; restructure format, topic, or cadence before scaling or sunsetting
- **Sunset** — bottom quartile on ≥2 scores with no evidence of audience appetite; reallocate budget within 60 days

**Section 3: Channel ROI Positioning**
Analyze where webinars sit in the demand gen channel portfolio:
- Calculate webinar cost-per-MQL indexed to 100; score each other channel relative to webinars
- Calculate pipeline-per-dollar-spent for each channel
- Identify webinar's *unique value proposition* vs. other channels: higher intent signals, longer-cycle buyer education, customer retention/expansion acceleration?
- Determine if webinar channel is *over-invested* (high cost relative to ROI), *appropriately invested*, or *under-invested* (strong ROI signal, constrained budget)
- Model what a 20% budget increase/decrease to the webinar program would produce in MQLs and pipeline, using current efficiency ratios

**Section 4: Audience Database Intelligence**
Assess database health and growth trajectory across four sub-analyses:

*a) Growth Rate Analysis*
- MoM and QoQ registrant database growth rate; plot trend
- Net new ICP-qualified contacts as % of total new additions
- Projected database size in 12 months at current acquisition pace
- Identify if organic database growth (repeat attendees + word-of-mouth) is increasing or decreasing as % of new registrants

*b) ICP Concentration Analysis*
- ICP match rate trend over last 4 quarters (is the database becoming more or less ICP-dense?)
- If ICP concentration is declining: identify which acquisition channels are importing non-ICP contacts (paid promo vs. organic vs. partner)
- Breakout: what % of the database is within your addressable ICP universe (estimated), indicating remaining TAM penetration opportunity?

*c) Engagement Cohort Analysis*
- Segment database into 4 engagement tiers: Super-fans (3+ webinars in 6 months), Regulars (2 webinars in 6 months), One-timers (1 webinar), Dormant (registered, never attended)
- Calculate pipeline contribution and MQL rate for each cohort
- Identify re-engagement opportunity: what's the pipeline value of converting 10% of Dormant contacts to One-timers?

*d) Database Decay Management*
- Quarterly decay rate (unsubscribes + bounces ÷ total database)
- At current decay rate, how many months until the database needs major replenishment efforts?
- Decay benchmarks: <2%/quarter = healthy; 2-4% = monitor; >4% = intervention required (relevance problem or email fatigue)

**Section 5: Predictive Q3 Performance Model**
Build three scenarios based on historical program performance:

*Scenario A — Maintain Investment*:
Project Q3 registrants, MQLs, and pipeline using Q2 trend line. Apply audience fatigue correction if repeat attendance rate is >35% (indicates potential saturation of the engaged database).

*Scenario B — Scale Up (20% budget increase)*:
Model the incremental registrants, MQLs, and pipeline from increased paid promotion and one additional webinar per active series. Apply diminishing return factor if database ICP penetration is already >40% (harder to acquire new ICP contacts at the same cost).

*Scenario C — Rebalance (hold budget, restructure mix)*:
Model the pipeline impact of sunsetting the lowest-performing series and reinvesting that budget into scaling the highest-performing series. Quantify the net change in MQLs and pipeline.

For each scenario, include:
- Expected Q3 registrants, live attendees, MQLs, SQLs, pipeline sourced
- Expected cost-per-MQL and pipeline ROI
- Key assumptions and risk factors
- Recommended scenario with rationale

**Section 6: Strategic Program Investment Decisions**
Generate exactly 3 high-impact decisions for the next quarter. For each:
- Decision: What specific change to make (format, budget, series, cadence, audience targeting)
- Evidence: Which data points from the quarterly analysis support this decision
- Expected impact: Specific metric improvement with range estimate (e.g., "MQL volume +15-25%, cost-per-MQL -$40-$80")
- Implementation timeline: What needs to happen in the first 30 days, 60 days, 90 days
- Success criteria: What metric combination at Q3 end confirms this decision worked

## Integration Tips

**Goldcast / ON24 / Demio (Webinar Platforms):**
- Export the Program Analytics dashboard quarterly — most platforms aggregate registrants, attendance rates, engagement scores, and on-demand views across all events in a date range; use this as the raw input for Section 4 data
- Build a custom report that tracks registrant source (where they were promoted) to populate the audience acquisition mix in Section 4a — this requires UTM parameter discipline on all registration links
- Enable attendee overlap reporting (Goldcast's "Audience Insights" or ON24's "Audience Report") to calculate repeat attendance rate without manual deduplication

**HubSpot:**
- Create a HubSpot Dashboard with three views: (1) Webinar MQL volume and velocity by series, (2) Webinar-sourced pipeline by close date and stage, (3) Webinar audience database growth and ICP match rate over time
- Use Custom Properties on Contact records to track "Webinar Engagement Tier" (Super-fan / Regular / One-timer / Dormant) — update via Workflow based on cumulative attendance count in the last 180 days
- Build a Revenue Attribution Report in HubSpot using the "Webinar" campaign type as the first-touch filter to generate the pipeline-per-webinar-dollar data needed for Section 3

**Salesforce + Marketo:**
- In Marketo, build a Program Channel hierarchy: "Webinar" > [Series Name] > [Individual Webinar]; this enables Program Channel ROI reporting at the series level without manual aggregation
- Create a Salesforce Report that pulls opportunities where a Campaign Member record exists for a webinar campaign and opportunity created date is within 60 days of the most recent webinar attendance — this is the "pipeline created" attribution formula
- Build a Marketo Analytics workspace filtered to the Webinar channel — this gives you all the Series-level metrics (total registrants, attendance rate, MQL volume, cost per MQL) automatically across any date range

**Google Sheets / Looker Studio:**
- Maintain a Webinar Program Tracker spreadsheet with one row per webinar, columns for every metric in the Program Health Scorecard, and a live formula calculating rolling 3-month averages — this becomes the source of truth for the quarterly review
- Build a Looker Studio dashboard with a Salesforce connector showing webinar-sourced pipeline by series and by quarter, refreshed weekly, shared with the Revenue leadership team
- Create a Database Health tab in the tracker that updates monthly: total registrant count, new additions, ICP match rate, unsubscribe rate — plot as a trend line to catch database decay before it impacts program results

**Zapier / Make Automation:**
- Automate quarterly data export: Trigger at the end of each quarter → pull webinar platform aggregate data → push to Google Sheets tracker → send Slack notification to demand gen team to complete the channel comparison fields manually
- Build a rolling "Series Health Alert": if any webinar in a series has attendance rate drop >15% QoQ or MQL rate drop >30% QoQ, send a Slack alert to the program owner flagging potential audience fatigue before the quarterly review

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Webinar pipeline attribution looks strong in the webinar platform but is impossible to verify in Salesforce, leading to leadership skepticism about the channel's true ROI.
**Fix:** This is a data infrastructure problem before it's an analytics problem. Implement a two-step fix: (1) Confirm that every webinar registrant is synced to Salesforce as a Campaign Member with the correct campaign name and member status (Registered / Attended / No-Show); (2) Create a Salesforce Opportunity Contact Role that requires reps to associate at least one contact (who attended a webinar) when creating new opportunities. With these two data points, the "webinar-influenced pipeline" report becomes defensible. If you have historical gaps, use the self-reported attribution method: in the post-event follow-up email, add the question "How did you first hear about [Company]?" with webinar as a response option — 30-40% of buyers will self-select it, giving you a floor estimate of webinar's pipeline contribution.

**Problem:** The Series Performance Matrix classifies a beloved internal series as "Revitalize" or "Sunset," and the content team (who owns the series) pushes back on the data.
**Fix:** Present the Series Performance Matrix with a "minimum viable benchmark" overlay rather than as an absolute ranking. Show what metrics the series would need to hit in Q3 to move from Revitalize to Maintain — typically: attendance rate +8pp, MQL rate +3pp, and one topic that outperforms the series average by 20%+ on pipeline. Frame the decision as "Q3 is this series' performance period with clear success criteria" rather than "we're sunsetting this series." Give the series owner ownership of the optimization plan rather than imposing changes from analytics. If the series still underperforms at Q3 end with no improvements, the sunset decision is now data-backed and the content team was a partner in the process.

**Problem:** Database growth has stalled — net new ICP contacts added per quarter has declined three consecutive quarters, but existing webinar content is still performing well on attendance and conversion.
**Fix:** Audience saturation is a real risk for companies with a small ICP universe (<50,000 total addressable contacts). Three actions: (1) Expand promotional channels beyond email — if 70%+ of registrants come from your own house list, you're recirculating the same contacts; add LinkedIn Sponsored Content targeting ICP job titles and company sizes you haven't yet penetrated, and activate 1-2 partner co-promotions per quarter to reach adjacent audiences; (2) Add a "bring a colleague" post-webinar CTA — attendees inviting ICP peers is the lowest-cost acquisition channel and generates high-ICP-match registrants; (3) Introduce a new series format explicitly designed for top-of-funnel ICP contacts who are too early for your existing series — this widens the top of the webinar funnel while preserving the nurture value of the existing programs for warmer contacts.
