# AI-Powered Net Revenue Retention & Expansion Revenue Intelligence Engine - The #1 SaaS Health Metric, Fully Automated

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** nrr, grr, expansion-revenue, upsell, cross-sell, saas, b2b, retention, unit-economics, revenue-intelligence, churn, automation

## Overview
Transforms raw subscription and billing data into a complete NRR diagnostic — pinpointing exactly where expansion revenue is being left on the table, which accounts are at risk of contraction, and what marketing motions drive net revenue retention above 120%. Use this when board reporting is due, when NRR is trending below 100%, or when you need to build a marketing-driven expansion revenue playbook that proves marketing's impact beyond new logo acquisition.

BUSINESS CONTEXT:

- Churned revenue (cancellations): [$X]

SEGMENTATION AVAILABLE:
- Customer segments: [e.g., SMB / Mid-Market / Enterprise OR by plan tier / industry / geography]
- Any expansion motion data: [e.g., 47 accounts upgraded, 23 added seats, 12 purchased add-ons]

PRODUCE THE FOLLOWING:

1. NRR CALCULATION & BENCHMARK
   - Calculate Gross Revenue Retention (GRR): (Starting ARR - Churn - Contractions) / Starting ARR
   - Calculate Net Revenue Retention (NRR): (Starting ARR + Expansions - Contractions - Churn) / Starting ARR
   - Benchmark against SaaS standards: SMB (<$10K ACV: 90-100% NRR is good), Mid-Market ($10K-$100K: 105-115%), Enterprise (>$100K: 115-130%)
   - Flag if NRR is below 100% (revenue is shrinking from existing base) with severity assessment

2. REVENUE WATERFALL BREAKDOWN
   - Build a revenue waterfall: Starting ARR → +New Logo → +Expansion → -Contraction → -Churn → Ending ARR
   - Calculate expansion rate: Expansion Revenue / Starting ARR
   - Calculate churn rate: Churned Revenue / Starting ARR
   - Calculate net expansion efficiency: (Expansion - Contraction) / Starting ARR
   - Identify the single biggest revenue leak (churn vs. contraction vs. low expansion)

3. EXPANSION OPPORTUNITY SIZING
   - Identify the revenue gap to reach 110% NRR target (how much expansion is needed)
   - If segment data exists: rank segments by NRR — which segments are above/below 100%?
   - Calculate theoretical max NRR if top 20% of accounts expanded at average expansion rate
   - Identify the "expansion-ready" account profile: what do accounts that expanded have in common?

4. CONTRACTION & CHURN DIAGNOSIS
   - Break down churn by segment/tier if data available
   - Identify early contraction warning pattern (which accounts contracted before churning?)
   - Calculate the "save window" — average months between first contraction signal and full churn
   - Recommend top 3 intervention triggers marketing should alert on

5. MARKETING-DRIVEN EXPANSION PLAYBOOK
   - Design 3 specific marketing motions to increase expansion revenue by 15% in 90 days:
     Motion 1: Trigger-based upsell campaign (specify trigger, message, channel, timing)
     Motion 2: Feature adoption → upgrade nurture (specify feature, segment, sequence)
     Motion 3: Usage-based expansion signal (specify threshold, outreach, offer)
   - For each motion: expected revenue impact, effort level, and owner (marketing vs. CS vs. sales)

6. NRR IMPROVEMENT ROADMAP
   - 30-day quick wins: campaigns/programs to launch immediately
   - 60-day structural improvements: process or product changes to recommend
   - 90-day strategic initiatives: new expansion revenue streams or pricing changes to evaluate
   - NRR target trajectory: Month 1, Month 3, Month 6, Month 12 targets with required expansion/retention rates

7. BOARD-READY NRR SUMMARY
   - One-paragraph investor narrative explaining current NRR, trend, and improvement plan
   - Key metrics table: Current NRR | GRR | Expansion Rate | Churn Rate | Net Expansion Efficiency
   - 3 leading indicators that predict NRR improvement (e.g., product adoption milestones, health score trends)

Format as a structured intelligence report with an executive summary, detailed sections, and a prioritized action table: Action | Revenue Impact | Effort (H/M/L) | Owner | Timeline.

## Integration Tips

- **ChartMogul / Baremetrics:** Export the MRR Movements report (new, expansion, contraction, churn) for the trailing 12 months. Paste directly into the Quick Copy-Paste prompt or use segment-level exports for the Advanced version.
- **Salesforce:** Use the "Closed Won — Existing Business" opportunity report for expansion ARR. Filter by "Renewal" or "Upsell" opportunity types. Combine with "Closed Lost — Existing Business" for churn data.
- **Gainsight / Totango:** Export account health scores and usage metrics. Map health score quartiles to the churn risk segmentation matrix in Module 3.
- **HubSpot:** Use the Revenue Analytics report to pull expansion deals. Build a custom "Expansion Revenue" pipeline stage and run the report by stage and close date.
- **Zapier / Make:** Set up automated triggers: when an account's Gainsight health score drops below 60 OR when usage hits 85% of plan limit, push data to this prompt template and send output to Slack (#expansion-alerts or #churn-risk).
- **Google Sheets:** Build a live NRR dashboard by connecting ChartMogul or Baremetrics API to Sheets using Zapier. Automate monthly prompt runs to populate the NRR narrative section for board slides.
- **Notion:** Create an "NRR Intelligence" database with a template that auto-populates from Salesforce data via Zapier. Run the advanced prompt monthly and store outputs with timestamp for trend tracking.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't have clean revenue movement data — my CRM mixes new logo and expansion revenue."**
Solution: Ask your finance or RevOps team for the ARR bridge (also called "ARR waterfall" or "ARR movements") from your billing system (Stripe, Chargebee, Recurly). If unavailable, approximate: pull all "Closed Won — Existing Customer" opportunities as expansion, and all "Closed Lost — Existing Customer" opportunities as churn or contraction. Even rough data produces directionally correct NRR insights.

**Problem: "Our NRR looks great (>120%) but we're still struggling to grow ARR."**
Solution: High NRR with slow ARR growth means your new logo engine is the bottleneck — not expansion. The prompt will still identify which customer segments are driving NRR and help you target look-alike new logo acquisition toward your highest-NRR segments. Use the "board-ready NRR summary" output to redirect investment thesis toward new logo acquisition efficiency.

**Problem: "The prompt output shows expansion opportunities but I don't have a CS team to execute."**
Solution: The Marketing-Driven Expansion Playbook (Module 4) is specifically designed for companies where marketing must own expansion without heavy CS involvement. Focus on the in-product and automated email motions — these require no CSM bandwidth. Start with the usage-threshold trigger campaign (Module 2, Program C) as it's fully automatable through your product analytics tool + email platform.
