## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Health scores are not predictive — customers are churning from Green and Yellow status, not just Red, making the churn risk quantification unreliable.**
Solution: Audit your health score inputs by running a retrospective analysis: pull the health score of every churned account from 90 days before their churn date. If Green accounts are churning, the model is missing a key signal — typically executive sponsor engagement, login frequency by decision-makers (not end users), or support ticket escalation pattern. Add these missing signals to the health score, weight them heavily, and revalidate the model against your last 6 months of churn. Even an imperfect revised model will outperform the current one.

**Problem: CS team does not have capacity to execute the 30-day interventions — they are already stretched across too many accounts for personalized outreach.**
Solution: Prioritize ruthlessly using the ARR at risk quantification. If Red accounts represent $150K ARR at 68% churn probability, that is $102K in recoverable ARR. If the CS team can only take 5 intervention actions, focus all 5 on the 5 largest Red accounts in the onboarding stage. Simultaneously, use marketing automation (email sequences, in-app guides via Pendo, or automated Gainsight plays) to serve the remaining accounts with low-touch intervention that does not require CS time.

**Problem: No defined "Aha! moment" or adoption milestones exist — the product team has not established what early product behaviors predict retention.**
Solution: Run a survival analysis using whatever usage data you have. Export a list of all customers who renewed at 12 months and all who churned. Compare the product behaviors in their first 30 and 60 days — what features did retained customers use that churned customers did not? The top 2-3 differentiating behaviors are your de facto Aha! moment and adoption milestones. Document them, socialize with product and CS leadership, and build your health score model around them. This analysis can be done in Google Sheets using pivot tables and does not require a data science team.
