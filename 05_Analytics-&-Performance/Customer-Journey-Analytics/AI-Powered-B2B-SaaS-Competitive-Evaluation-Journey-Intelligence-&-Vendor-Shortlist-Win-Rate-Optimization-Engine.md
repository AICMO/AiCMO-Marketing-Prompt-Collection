# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Competitive Evaluation Journey Intelligence & Vendor Shortlist Win-Rate Optimization Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25 min | **Tags:** b2b, competitive-intelligence, buyer-journey, vendor-evaluation, win-rate, analytics, pipeline-velocity, evaluation-stage

## Overview
Detects when prospects enter active vendor evaluation mode, maps every signal and touchpoint across the competitive consideration journey, and automatically deploys a calibrated response playbook to maximize shortlist wins. Use this when win rates are declining in late-stage pipeline, when deals are stalling after demo, or when your team keeps losing to the same 1-2 competitors without understanding why.

## Integration Tips

- **Gong / Chorus:** Set up a Gong Tracker for each competitor name and common evaluation phrases ("also evaluating," "comparing you to," "looking at alternatives," "shortlist"). Configure Gong webhooks to write a custom field to Salesforce Opportunity the moment a competitor is detected on a recorded call. This is the fastest signal you have — treat it as a fire alarm, not a weekly report.
- **6sense / Bombora / G2 Buyer Intent:** Route G2 Buyer Intent signals (competitor profile views from known accounts) directly into Salesforce via native integration. Create a Salesforce workflow: if an account in open pipeline shows 6sense "Decision" stage intent for a competitor keyword, update Opportunity field "Evaluation Signal Detected" = TRUE and trigger evaluation playbook enrollment in Marketo/HubSpot.
- **Salesforce:** Build a "Competitive Eval Dashboard" with four views: (1) Open pipeline by competitive exposure (how many deals have detected competitor signals vs. dark), (2) Deal age vs. competitive signal date (showing response gap), (3) Win rate by competitor for last 90 days, (4) Deals at risk (stalled > 14 days with competitor signal detected). Review this in weekly revenue review.
- **HubSpot / Marketo:** Create a dedicated enrollment list called "Evaluation Stage — Competitive Active" with trigger criteria: any contact at an open deal with competitive evaluation signals detected. Enroll in a branching sequence that forks by competitor (Clari branch, Revenue.io branch, Generic branch). Each branch has 6 steps over 14 days with fully personalized content. Set email sender as AE alias, not marketing alias — reply-to goes to AE inbox.
- **Slack:** Configure a #competitive-deals Slack channel. Every new competitive evaluation signal triggers an automatic post: "@AE_Name [Account] is now comparing you to [Competitor]. Signal: [G2 view / Gong mention / pricing page]. Recommended next action: [link]. Counter-asset: [link]. Act within 24 hours." Add a 24-hour reminder bot if no Salesforce activity is logged.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build a "Competitive Evaluation Playbook" wiki page for each competitor with: their top 3 claims, your counter-narrative, approved email templates, proof assets, and reference customer list (with permission). Keep it live — update after every new win or loss. Link directly from the Slack alert.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: You don't have Gong or a conversation intelligence tool, so you can't detect competitor mentions on calls — half of your evaluation signals are invisible.**
Solution: Start with the signals you do have: pricing page visits, G2 intent data (even the free G2 notification emails), and CRM fields where reps can log "competitor mentioned" manually. Create a mandatory CRM field: "Competitors In Evaluation" (multi-select) that reps fill out after every discovery call. Make it required to advance the opportunity stage. Over 60 days you'll have enough data to build the loss pattern matrix manually. Once you can show the revenue impact, the business case for Gong/Chorus writes itself.

**Problem: Marketing built the evaluation-stage playbook but sales isn't following it — AEs keep going off-script with their own competitive responses instead of using the approved content.**
Solution: The problem is distribution, not content quality. AEs ignore email attachments and static PDFs because they can't find them fast enough in the heat of a deal. Solve for speed: embed the top 3 competitive one-pagers directly in Salesforce as native files on the Opportunity record (not in a shared drive). Build a Slack bot that delivers the right asset in < 10 seconds when an AE types "/compete Clari." Measure usage at the deal level. Present win-rate data showing deals where approved content was used vs. not used — nothing changes rep behavior faster than revenue correlation.

**Problem: Your win-rate data is unreliable because CRM loss reasons are inconsistently logged — reps say "no decision" or "budget" when the real reason was a competitive loss.**
Solution: Implement a mandatory post-mortem trigger: any deal Closed-Lost where a competitor was in the evaluation field must have a completed "Loss Review" in Salesforce within 48 hours. Use a 5-question form (what did they buy instead? what was the deciding factor? what content did we send? did the prospect see our ROI model? what would have changed the outcome?). After 90 days you'll have cleaner loss data than your entire prior 2 years. Until then, trust your Gong call data and G2 intent signals more than CRM loss reasons.
