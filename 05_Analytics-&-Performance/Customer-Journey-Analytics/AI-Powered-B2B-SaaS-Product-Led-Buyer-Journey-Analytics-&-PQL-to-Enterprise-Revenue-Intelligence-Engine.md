## Overview
Maps the complete product-led buyer journey from anonymous trial signup through activation, product qualified lead (PQL) identification, sales-assist trigger, and enterprise conversion — using AI to identify which product usage patterns predict paid conversion, when sales should intervene, and which cohorts are most likely to expand into enterprise deals. Use this when self-serve trial-to-paid conversion is declining, PQL-to-sales-assist hand-off is creating revenue leakage, or when you need to justify PLG investment with a usage-to-revenue attribution model for the board.

## Integration Tips

- **Amplitude/Mixpanel:** Build a "PLG Journey Funnel" chart with custom events for each activation milestone. Set up a "Conversion Cohort" comparison: users who completed the Golden Activation Sequence vs. those who didn't, tracked 30, 60, and 90 days to paid conversion. Use behavioral cohorts to segment by acquisition source to power the cohort quality analysis.
- **Salesforce/HubSpot:** Sync product usage data from your analytics tool via Segment or a native integration. Create custom contact/account fields: "PLG Stage," "Activation Score (0-100)," "Golden Sequence Completion Count," "PQL Triggered Date," and "PQL Routing Tier (Self-Serve / SDR / AE)." Build automated workflows to change owner and trigger tasks when PQL threshold is reached.
- **Intercom/Customer.io:** Set up in-app messaging campaigns triggered by product usage milestones — specifically the "activation gap interventions" identified in Step 2 of the sequence. For accounts stalling between activation steps, trigger contextual nudges (e.g., "Here's a 2-minute walkthrough to connect your CRM") using the specific feature name that's creating the drop-off.
- **Segment:** Use Segment as the CDP layer to connect product analytics events to CRM updates and marketing automation triggers in real-time. Build a "PQL Score" computed trait that updates every 24 hours and pushes to HubSpot/Salesforce. When score crosses the PQL threshold, fire an event to Outreach/Salesloft to enroll in the appropriate sales sequence.
- **Snowflake/BigQuery:** For deeper cohort analysis, build a recurring SQL query that joins signup date, activation events, PQL trigger date, and converted date to calculate exact time-in-stage for every account. Schedule it weekly and pipe results to a Looker or Tableau dashboard tracking the PLG Journey Health Score over time.
- **Outreach/Salesloft:** Build separate sequences for "PQL → SDR" and "PQL → AE" routing outcomes from your segmentation matrix. Each sequence should reference the specific product actions the account has completed: "I noticed your team built 7 workflows and connected your CRM — wanted to share how [similar company] used that same setup to [outcome]."
- **Zapier/Make:** Build a "PQL Alert" zap: when an account's PQL score crosses the threshold in your CRM, send a Slack DM to the assigned rep with a pre-populated message showing the account name, company size, features used, and the one-line personalization hook for outreach.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Product usage data and CRM data are siloed — can't correlate product behavior with pipeline stages or revenue outcomes.**
Solution: Start with a manual data join rather than waiting for a technical integration. Export a CSV of all trial accounts from your product analytics tool (with activation events) and match against closed-won/churned accounts in CRM by email domain or account name. Even a rough 80% match rate is enough to identify the Golden Activation Sequence. Use this analysis to build the business case for a proper Segment + CRM integration — the revenue opportunity quantified in the leakage analysis is typically compelling enough to get engineering time allocated.

**Problem: PQL definition is politically contentious — sales says marketing's PQLs are "garbage leads" and refuses to work the new definition, regardless of data.**
Solution: Frame the PQL definition change as a joint sales-marketing experiment, not a marketing mandate. Run a 30-day A/B test: half of PQLs routed to sales using the old definition, half using the new higher-threshold definition. Track conversion rate and time-to-close for each cohort. Present results in the next sales-marketing QBR. Data showing the new definition converts at 58% vs. 40% is inarguable — it means every rep works 31% fewer leads to hit the same quota. Lead with rep efficiency, not marketing accuracy.

**Problem: Activation rate analysis shows wildly different results by acquisition source, making it hard to know whether activation is a product problem or a targeting problem.**
Solution: Treat source-specific activation as a distinct diagnosis. If activation rates vary by more than 15 percentage points across sources, you have a targeting problem (wrong ICP is signing up) at least as much as a product problem. Run the cohort quality analysis first — identify your highest-activating, highest-converting source. Use that cohort's company size, job title, and company type distribution as your "true ICP signal" and tighten paid targeting to match. Then address the product-level activation friction separately for each major ICP segment.
