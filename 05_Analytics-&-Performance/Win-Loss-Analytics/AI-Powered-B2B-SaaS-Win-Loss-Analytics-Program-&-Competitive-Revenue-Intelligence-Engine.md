## Overview
Deploys an AI agent to ingest CRM deal data, sales call transcripts, competitor mentions, and exit survey responses to surface statistically significant win/loss drivers — generating a prioritized action plan for product marketing, sales enablement, and demand generation teams. Use this when close rates are plateauing, competitive losses are increasing, or your team is flying blind on why deals are won or lost.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "My loss reasons field in CRM is garbage — 80% just say 'No Decision' or 'Lost to Competitor.'"**
Solution: Supplement with 3 data sources that don't require rep input: (1) Gong talk tracks — search for keywords like "going with" and "deciding to" in call transcripts, (2) Email thread summaries from the last exchange in lost deals, (3) LinkedIn job change alerts for deal contacts 60–90 days post-loss (often reveals internal org change was the real driver). Feed these as supplemental context in the prompt.

**Problem: "The output is too generic — it's not giving me specific enough insights for my niche."**
Solution: Add 3 specificity layers to the prompt: (1) Paste in 5–10 actual verbatim quotes from loss reason notes or Gong clips, (2) Name your specific ICP job titles and the words they use to describe their problem, (3) Include 2–3 example deals with details — one won, one lost to a competitor, one lost to no-decision. Specificity in → specificity out.

**Problem: "Win rate looks different depending on who you ask — Marketing, Sales, and Finance all have different numbers."**
Solution: Define a single win rate formula before running the analysis: (Closed-Won ARR in Period) ÷ (Total Opportunities Reaching Proposal Stage in Same Period). Exclude early-stage pipeline that never made it to proposal. Run the prompt with this clarified definition explicitly stated. Reconcile with Finance quarterly.
