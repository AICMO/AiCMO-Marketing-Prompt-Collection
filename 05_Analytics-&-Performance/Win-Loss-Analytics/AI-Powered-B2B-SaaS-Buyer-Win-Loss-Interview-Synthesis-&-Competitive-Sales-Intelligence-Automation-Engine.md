ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:

Apply the following analytical frameworks sequentially:

**Framework 1: Jobs-to-be-Done Extraction**
For each interview, identify:
- The functional job the buyer was trying to accomplish
- The emotional job (how they wanted to feel as a result)
- The social job (how they wanted to be perceived by peers/leadership)
- The "job executor" (who in the buying committee owned each job)
Map jobs to your product's feature set and identify coverage gaps.

**Framework 2: Decision Criteria Hierarchy**
Apply a two-axis analysis:
- Axis 1: Frequency (how often this criterion appeared across interviews)
- Axis 2: Deal weight (did this criterion determine the decision, or was it secondary?)
Output: 2×2 matrix with quadrants: (High Freq/High Weight = Must-Win criteria), (Low Freq/High Weight = Sleeper criteria), (High Freq/Low Weight = Hygiene criteria), (Low Freq/Low Weight = Noise)

**Framework 3: Competitive Perception Mapping**
For each competitor mentioned, extract buyer perception data on:
- Capability: What can they do that we cannot (or appear to)?
- Credibility: Why do buyers trust them?
- Cost: How does pricing influence the competitive dynamic?
- Chemistry: Relationship, references, and sales experience factors
Output: Per-competitor positioning map with verbatim buyer evidence for each dimension.

**Framework 4: Decision Journey Reconstruction**
Map the buyer's actual journey (not your funnel):
- What triggered the search?
- How did they build their shortlist?
- What evaluation activities did they conduct? (demos, trials, references, G2 reviews)
- Who influenced the final decision?
- What caused the final tipping point?
Identify: where your product/team gained or lost momentum at each stage.

**Framework 5: Objection Archaeology**
Classify every objection raised in interviews:
- Price objections: Is this a real budget constraint or a value communication failure?
- Risk objections: Switching cost, implementation complexity, vendor stability
- Capability objections: Missing features, integration gaps, scalability concerns
- Relationship objections: Incumbent vendor inertia, internal champion strength elsewhere
For each objection class: current handling effectiveness, recommended response with buyer language, sales enablement assets needed.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Buyers won't agree to win-loss interviews**
Fix: Reframe the interview as a "customer/prospect success review" rather than a sales retrospective. Offer a $50 donation to their chosen charity. Use a neutral third-party interviewer for lost deals — buyers are 3× more likely to be candid when not talking to the vendor directly. Automate the first touchpoint as a 5-question Typeform survey with an optional 15-minute follow-up call option.

**Problem: Interview data is too thin — fewer than 3 interviews per competitive loss reason**
Fix: Supplement with secondary sources: G2/Gartner Peer Insights reviews mentioning competitors, Gong conversation intelligence themes from evaluation-stage calls, LinkedIn comments on competitor announcement posts, Reddit/community forum discussions. Feed all sources into the Advanced prompt as a combined corpus and flag confidence levels accordingly.

**Problem: Sales team ignores the intelligence outputs — battlecards gather dust**
Fix: Move from static documents to embedded intelligence. Integrate battlecard content directly into Salesforce opportunity records as a dynamic sidebar. Create a 90-second Loom video summarizing each competitive battlecard update. Run a live 30-minute "Win-Loss Debrief" with the full sales team monthly — present top 3 findings, 1 deal story, and 1 updated play. Make win-loss intelligence part of deal review meetings, not a separate program.
