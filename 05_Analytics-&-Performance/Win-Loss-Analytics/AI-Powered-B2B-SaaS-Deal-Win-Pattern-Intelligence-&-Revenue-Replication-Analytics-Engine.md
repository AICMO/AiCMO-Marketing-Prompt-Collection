## Overview
Deploys an AI analytics engine to mine your won deals for statistically significant patterns across marketing source, content touchpoints, persona sequence, deal velocity, and competitive dynamics — then generates a closed-loop revenue replication playbook that tells marketing exactly which programs to invest in, which buyer journeys to recreate, and which signals predict a deal will close at 2x the average rate. Use this when win rates are inconsistent across reps or segments, when you can't explain why Q3 outperformed Q1, or when marketing investment decisions are still based on intuition rather than pattern intelligence.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sample size too small — fewer than 50 won deals in the analysis window.**
Solution: Extend the time window to 18–24 months, but apply time-decay weighting (more recent deals weighted 2x). Alternatively, cluster deals by similarity (same vertical + company size + channel) to reach statistically meaningful group sizes. Flag all conclusions as "directionally valid, not statistically significant" and use to form hypotheses to test rather than to make irreversible decisions.

**Problem: CRM data is inconsistent — missing lead source fields, incomplete contact roles, or irregular stage progression data.**
Solution: Before running the analysis, audit the last 100 closed-won deals manually. Fill in gaps using: email domain history (for source), LinkedIn activity (for contact roles), and call recording timestamps (for stage timing). Document data quality score per field (% populated, % accurate) and note which patterns are high-confidence vs. low-confidence based on data completeness.

**Problem: The "Golden ICP profile" from win pattern analysis is different from the ICP the company has been officially targeting.**
Solution: This is the most valuable finding — present it as a "Market Fit Discovery" rather than a discrepancy. Run a TAM analysis on the newly discovered Golden ICP: how large is the addressable market? Is this a segment you can realistically expand into, or is it too niche? Use the data to have a structured conversation with product, sales, and exec team about whether the company's ICP definition needs updating. Do not abandon the existing ICP without validating the new one through 90 days of deliberate targeting.
