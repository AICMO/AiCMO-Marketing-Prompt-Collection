## Integration Tips

- **6sense / Demandbase:** Export account engagement scores and intent stage data via API or CSV. Map account IDs to your CRM opportunities for attribution. Use the platform's built-in "influenced pipeline" report as a cross-check against your AI-generated analysis.
- **HubSpot:** Use the "Associated Companies" report to pull all deals where the associated company is on your ABM list. Filter by ABM contact property or list membership. Export to Google Sheets for the AI analysis input.
- **Salesforce:** Create a custom ABM_Tier__c field on Account object. Use Campaign Influence (Customizable Attribution) to pull multi-touch data. Run the SOQL query: `SELECT Account.ABM_Tier__c, SUM(Amount), AVG(Days_to_Close__c) FROM Opportunity WHERE ABM_Tier__c != null GROUP BY Account.ABM_Tier__c`
- **Google Sheets automation:** Connect your ABM platform export + CRM export in a Google Sheet. Use Apps Script to auto-calculate ROI multiples. Feed the structured data table directly into the prompt for fully automated quarterly reporting.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Paste the board narrative output directly into your QBR template. The 5-bullet format maps directly to most exec presentation structures.
- **Looker / Tableau:** Use the pipeline attribution waterfall data to build a live ABM funnel visualization. The stage-by-stage conversion rates make excellent dashboard tiles for weekly monitoring.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Attribution numbers don't match what finance or sales reports.**
Solution: This almost always comes down to attribution model mismatch. Your marketing attribution (multi-touch, influenced) will always be larger than finance's close-date-attributed revenue. Explicitly define which number you're citing upfront: "marketing-influenced pipeline" vs. "finance-recognized closed revenue." Run both and present them side-by-side with methodology footnotes. Never present marketing-influenced as equivalent to closed-won ARR.

**Problem: Tier 1 ROI looks worse than Tier 3 because investment per account is much higher.**
Solution: This is expected in the short term for Tier 1. Adjust your time horizon — Tier 1 deals have longer sales cycles (often 6–12 months) and higher LTV. Calculate Tier 1 ROI on a 12-month trailing basis rather than a single quarter. Also include NRR and expansion ARR in Tier 1 calculations, which typically shift the ROI picture dramatically.

**Problem: Can't separate ABM-generated from ABM-influenced pipeline because touchpoint data is incomplete.**
Solution: Implement a retroactive tagging approach — any CRM account that appears on your ABM list AND had an opportunity created within a 90-day window of any ABM campaign touchpoint gets the "ABM-influenced" tag. For future accuracy, enforce a CRM workflow rule that auto-stamps the Opportunity record whenever an ABM touchpoint occurs on the parent account within your ABM platform. Document your methodology clearly so the model is auditable.
