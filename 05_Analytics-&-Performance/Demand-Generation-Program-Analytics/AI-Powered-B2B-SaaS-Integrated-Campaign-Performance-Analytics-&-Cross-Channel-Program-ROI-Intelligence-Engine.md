# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Integrated Campaign Performance Analytics & Cross-Channel Program ROI Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25 min | **Tags:** integrated-campaigns, campaign-analytics, cross-channel-roi, b2b-saas, program-measurement, marketing-analytics, pipeline-attribution, campaign-performance, revenue-contribution, marketing-roi

## Overview
Measures the performance of integrated, theme-based marketing campaigns as unified programs across all channels simultaneously — tracking how a single campaign concept (e.g., "AI-Powered Revenue Operations" or "Zero Trust Security") performs across LinkedIn ads, email nurture, webinars, SDR outreach, content, and ABM in a single cohesive ROI view. Use this when you need to prove integrated campaign ROI to the CFO, identify which campaign programs to continue or kill, or when you're running 2-5 simultaneous campaign programs and can't determine which is driving pipeline most effectively.

**3. AUDIENCE PENETRATION & FREQUENCY ANALYSIS**

- Dark funnel gap: % of TAL that shows buying behavior (G2 views, competitor research, tech stack changes) but hasn't engaged with campaign — recommendation for re-engagement
- Frequency optimization: Identify accounts showing fatigue signals (unsubscribes, ad frequency above 8, SDR opt-outs) vs. accounts underreached (below 3 total touches)

**4. CHANNEL SYNERGY & PATH ANALYSIS**
Analyze channel combination effects:
- Single-channel conversion rate: Accounts that touched only 1 campaign channel → MQL/Opp rate
- Multi-channel conversion rate: Accounts that touched 3+ channels → MQL/Opp rate
- Identify the "priming effect": Which channels, when first, improve conversion rates of subsequent channels?

  * Test by comparing: SDR response rate for accounts with prior LinkedIn impression vs. those without
- Winning path analysis: For deals that closed, what was the most common channel sequence? Assign a "path ROI" score to the top 3 combinations
- Channel dependency mapping: Are there channels that can be removed without impacting pipeline? (Subtract each channel and model impact on total program performance)

**5. CREATIVE & CONTENT PERFORMANCE**

**6. CAMPAIGN ATTRIBUTION RECONCILIATION**

- First-touch attribution: Which channel gets credit if you use first-touch?
- Last-touch attribution: Which channel gets credit if you use last-touch?
- Linear multi-touch: How is credit distributed across all campaign touches?
- Time-decay: Which recent touches get disproportionate credit vs. awareness-stage touches?
- Position-based (W-shaped): First touch, lead conversion, and opportunity creation each get 30%; remaining 10% split across middle touches

- **Campaign pipeline ROI** ≥10x (total sourced pipeline ÷ total campaign investment) for campaigns targeting enterprise ICP
- **Goal attainment** ≥90% of primary pipeline target by campaign end date
- **TAL penetration** ≥70% of target account list reached with ≥3 touches across ≥2 channels by campaign midpoint

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce CRM Campaign Object**: The most reliable way to track integrated campaign performance is to create a parent Salesforce Campaign object (e.g., "Q2 AI Efficiency Push") with child campaigns per channel. Use Campaign Influence (Primary Campaign Source + Related Campaigns) to capture both first-touch and multi-touch attribution in a single report. Export the Campaign ROI report from Salesforce and paste it into the Channel Performance Data section of this prompt.
- **HubSpot Marketing Hub**: Use HubSpot's multi-touch revenue attribution report (available in Marketing Hub Enterprise) to pull campaign-level data across all channels. Filter by "Campaign: [campaign name]" in the attribution report to see all contacts and deals influenced. Export the CSV and map the columns to the Channel Performance Data section above.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Export campaign analytics filtered by "Campaign Group" (one Campaign Group per integrated campaign) to get unified LinkedIn reach, frequency, and engagement data. The "Company Engagement Report" shows which specific target accounts engaged — cross-reference against your TAL to calculate TAL penetration %.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Use GA4's Campaigns report filtered by utm_campaign parameter to track cross-channel campaign traffic to the campaign landing page. Export session data segmented by channel to populate clicks/engagements in the Channel Performance Data section.
- **6sense / Demandbase / Bombora**: Use the account engagement dashboard filtered by campaign date range to identify which TAL accounts engaged with campaign content even without providing contact information (dark funnel activity). This populates the "Dark funnel gap" section and adds 20-35% to your total audience penetration estimate.
- **Marketo**: Use Smart Campaigns with Program Membership to tag every touchpoint across the integrated campaign. The Program Performance Report in Marketo pulls first-touch MQLs, program cost, and success metrics per channel — export this and it maps directly to the Channel Performance Data table above.
- **Google Sheets / Looker Studio**: The Channel ROI table in the output can be pasted directly into a Google Sheet. Build a Looker Studio campaign performance dashboard by connecting Salesforce and LinkedIn Campaign Manager data sources and using the Channel Contribution Matrix as the dashboard template.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My campaign touches are not tagged consistently across channels — I can't reliably attribute MQLs or pipeline to a specific campaign.**
Fix: Add this instruction to the prompt: "Attribution data is incomplete — use available UTM data as primary source, supplement with date-range filtering (any lead created during campaign period from an ICP account in the TAL), and flag all estimates as low-confidence." Then request the AI to produce a data quality assessment alongside the performance analysis, identifying which channels have reliable attribution and which don't. The structural recommendation should include a campaign tagging governance plan (UTM taxonomy, Salesforce campaign membership rules, and lead source mapping) to fix attribution for the next campaign before it launches.

**Problem: I'm running 4-5 simultaneous campaigns targeting overlapping audiences — I can't tell which campaign is driving pipeline for overlapping accounts.**
Fix: Add a "Campaign Isolation Protocol" instruction: "Multiple campaigns are running simultaneously targeting overlapping TALs. For accounts that appear in multiple campaigns' TALs, attribute pipeline using: (1) the campaign that generated the first marketing touch, (2) the campaign that generated the most touches (plurality), or (3) the campaign most recently active before opportunity creation — specify which model to apply." The advanced version's Attribution Reconciliation section handles this by showing results under multiple attribution models — if two campaigns both claim the same opportunity, the analysis will flag the conflict and recommend a resolution rule.

**Problem: My SDR team doesn't tag their outbound activities to campaign objects in the CRM — I have no data on SDR contribution to the campaign.**
Fix: Modify the Channel Performance Data to exclude SDR from the per-channel ROI calculation and instead run a "Campaign Lift Analysis" for SDR: compare SDR meeting-to-opportunity rates for accounts in the campaign TAL that received campaign touches before the SDR call vs. accounts with no campaign exposure. If the campaign-exposed group converts at 2x+, that's the campaign's priming multiplier for SDR — quantify it as incremental pipeline influence. Add this to the integration tips: "For the next campaign, require SDRs to associate all outbound activities and booked meetings with the campaign object in Salesforce before the campaign launches.
