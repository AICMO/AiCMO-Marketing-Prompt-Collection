## Overview
Measures the full revenue contribution of your AI-powered and human SDR outbound program — from sequence performance and reply rates through to pipeline creation, deal velocity, and closed revenue. Use this when you need to justify AI-SDR investment to the CFO, diagnose why outbound pipeline is underperforming, or benchmark your outbound efficiency against industry standards for your growth stage.

**Show rate fix protocol (implement in Week 1):**
1. Day before meeting: Automated LinkedIn connection request from SDR + personal note ("Looking forward to our call tomorrow — connected in case you need to reach me")
2. Morning of meeting: SMS reminder via Salesloft (if phone number available): "Hey [First Name] — [SDR Name] from Meridian Ops. Our call is at [time]. Calendar invite below has the Zoom link. See you then."
3. 15 minutes before: Auto-email reminder with pre-read agenda (2 bullets max: "We'll cover: (1) your current RevOps data challenges, (2) 3 ways Meridian Ops customers solved them. Takes 20 mins.")
4. No-show response protocol: Within 3 minutes of missed meeting → immediate email ("Looks like now may not be the best time — can we find 15 mins next week?") + 90-second Loom video walking through the key value prop.

Tag opportunities at creation with outbound source, sequence name, and which SDR/AI-SDR was involved. This makes the AI vs. Human SDR comparison section of the prompt automatable from a CRM report export. Run a Salesforce campaign influence report filtered to outbound campaign source to populate the pipeline contribution numbers directly.
- **Outreach / Salesloft**: Export the Sequence Analytics report for each active sequence (steps, reply rates, positive reply rates, meeting booked rates) and paste directly into the Sequence Performance section of the prompt. Both platforms allow CSV export of sequence performance by step — this is the raw data for the Sequence Performance Matrix.
- **Clay**: In Clay, add a "Sequence Tier" enrichment column based on your outbound performance matrix — A/B/C tier classification can be automated using Clay formulas. This lets you automatically route new accounts to A-tier sequences and pause or exclude C-tier sequences from enrollment.
- **Apollo.io**: Use Apollo's analytics dashboard to pull Reply Rate, Positive Reply Rate, and Meetings Booked Rate by sequence. The sequence performance export maps directly to the prompt's Sequence Performance Matrix inputs. Apollo also provides cost per meeting analytics natively — use these as inputs to the AI-SDR efficiency section.
- **ZoomInfo / Clearbit / Clay enrichment**: For the ICP Penetration Analysis section, export your contact database by company size and industry and cross-reference with your CRM to calculate account coverage rate. ZoomInfo's Company Enrich API can automate this on a scheduled basis.
- **Gong / Chorus / Clari**: Use conversation intelligence to analyze outbound discovery calls — which talk tracks have the highest meeting-to-opportunity conversion rates? Export the outcome data from Gong's Deal Intelligence to populate the Discovery → Opportunity conversion rate by sequence type, giving you a quality-adjusted view of which sequences produce better-qualified opportunities, not just meetings.
- **Google Sheets / Notion**: The Sequence Performance Matrix and Program Health Scorecard are designed to export cleanly as tables into Google Sheets or Notion databases. Build a weekly Outbound Health Tracker by pasting the scorecard into a Sheet with conditional formatting — green (4+), yellow (3-4), red (<3) — and share with RevOps, SDR leadership, and CMO weekly.
- **6sense / Bombora / Demandbase**: Feed intent data into the ICP Penetration Analysis to prioritize the outbound targeting queue. Accounts in the "buying stage" in 6sense should be surfaced to human SDRs first; accounts in "awareness" or "consideration" stage are better candidates for AI-SDR sequences. This intent-to-routing integration is one of the highest-leverage investments in a hybrid outbound program.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: I can't separate AI-SDR pipeline from Human SDR pipeline in my CRM — they're all just tagged as "outbound."**
Fix: Add the constraint "Note: AI-SDR and Human SDR pipeline cannot be separated in CRM — use total outbound metrics only and compare AI-SDR activity metrics (volume, reply rate) against Human SDR activity metrics as separate inputs." The prompt will shift its AI vs. Human comparison to activity-level efficiency (cost per reply, cost per meeting) rather than pipeline-level attribution. Going forward, add a "Sequence Owner" field (AI-SDR-Tool vs. SDR-FirstName) at the opportunity creation stage in CRM — most sales engagement platforms can push this automatically via native CRM integration.

**Problem: Our reply rates look strong but meetings-to-pipeline conversion is very low (below 30%).**
Fix: Add this instruction: "My outbound meeting-to-pipeline conversion rate is [X%], which is below the 30-40% minimum benchmark. Diagnose whether this is a targeting problem (wrong ICP), a discovery call quality problem (SDR qualifies poorly), or a handoff problem (AE follow-up too slow after SDR meeting). Recommend 3 specific interventions." Root causes typically include: (1) SDRs booking meetings with contacts below VP-level who lack budget authority — fix with an ICP re-qualification checklist before meeting confirmation; (2) Meetings converting to opportunities at SDR stage without rigorous MEDDIC qualification — fix with SDR-to-AE handoff form requiring minimum qualification criteria before opportunity creation; (3) AE response time to SDR-booked meetings is >48 hours — fix with SLA enforcement and automated AE activity tracking.

**Problem: My outbound win rate (18%) is much lower than inbound win rate (32%) and I'm not sure if outbound is worth the investment.**
Fix: Add this framing: "Outbound win rate is 14 points below inbound. Diagnose whether this gap is acceptable given outbound's ability to reach net-new accounts inbound would never reach, or whether it signals a fundamental ICP-targeting or discovery quality problem." The win rate gap between outbound and inbound is normal in B2B SaaS (outbound prospects are typically less sales-ready) — but the gap should be 8-12 points, not 14+. A 14-point gap usually indicates either (1) SDRs are booking meetings with contacts who expressed polite interest but have no real problem — fix with tighter qualification; (2) The outbound ICP is too broad and includes accounts that are not your best-fit customers — fix with ICP scoring refinement and lower-funnel win rate analysis by segment.
