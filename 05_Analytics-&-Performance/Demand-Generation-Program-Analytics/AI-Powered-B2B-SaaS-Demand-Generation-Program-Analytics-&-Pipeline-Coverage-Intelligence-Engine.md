# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Demand Generation Program Analytics & Pipeline Coverage Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** demand-generation, pipeline-coverage, b2b-saas, marketing-analytics, multi-channel-attribution, revenue-operations, pipeline-forecasting, funnel-analytics, demand-gen-efficiency, marketing-performance

## Overview
Analyzes your entire demand generation program as a unified system — measuring pipeline coverage ratios, channel contribution, funnel velocity, and demand gen efficiency across all channels simultaneously. Use this when pipeline coverage is below 3x quota, when you cannot explain which demand gen investments are driving qualified pipeline, or when you need a CFO- and board-ready view of demand generation program health versus revenue targets.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: I don't have reliable channel-level pipeline attribution data — all I have is lead source.**
Fix: Use lead source as a proxy for channel contribution, but flag the limitation in your analysis. Add a constraint to the prompt: "Attribution data is incomplete — use first-touch lead source as a proxy for channel contribution and flag where multi-touch attribution would change the analysis." The prompt will produce estimates with appropriate caveats and will include a recommendation for improving attribution infrastructure as part of the structural fixes. Most importantly, the pipeline coverage and funnel velocity analysis will still be accurate regardless of attribution model.

**Problem: My company uses a non-standard funnel model (e.g., no formal MQL stage, or PLG model where product signups are the entry point).**
Fix: Replace the MQL/SAL/SQL stages with your actual funnel stages. For PLG companies, substitute: "Signup → PQL (Product Qualified Lead) → Sales-Accepted PQL → Opportunity → Closed Won." For companies without a formal MQL, use "Raw Lead → Qualified Lead → Opportunity." The conversion rate benchmarks will need adjustment — add a line in the CONTEXT section describing your funnel model and asking the AI to apply relevant benchmarks for your motion (PLG, outbound-led, inbound-led).

**Problem: The pipeline coverage ratio looks fine at the aggregate level but we're still missing revenue targets.**
Fix: The aggregate coverage ratio hides segment and timing imbalances. Add this instruction: "Decompose pipeline coverage by: (1) segment — Enterprise vs. Mid-Market vs. SMB; (2) time horizon — deals expected to close this month vs. next quarter; (3) deal stage — stage 1-2 heavy vs. stage 3-4 heavy; (4) channel source — inbound vs. outbound vs. partner." A 3.5x aggregate ratio with 70% of pipeline in Stage 1 (early discovery) and only 1.2x in Stage 3-4 (late stage) means revenue attainment is at risk despite the headline number looking adequate.
