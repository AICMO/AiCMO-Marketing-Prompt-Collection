## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My community platform doesn't integrate with my CRM, making pipeline attribution nearly impossible to track automatically.**
Solution: Use email as the universal matching key. Export your full community member list with emails from your platform weekly; upload to HubSpot/Salesforce as a static list; match against existing contacts using a VLOOKUP or CRM import deduplication. For each new opportunity created, manually check if the contact or their company has community members in the matched list. Run this process monthly to build a 90-day rolling attribution window. Even manual attribution on 20% of your pipeline is more actionable than 0% data — start there and automate incrementally as the business case proves out.

**Problem: My analysis shows high community engagement overall, but I can't tell if engaged members are actually the ones buying — it might just be existing customers who are already committed.**
Solution: Segment your community member list into three cohorts before running this analysis: (1) Prospects — companies with no CRM record or at Awareness stage, (2) Active Opportunities — contacts/accounts currently in pipeline, (3) Customers — post-close members. Run the content performance and pipeline attribution analysis separately for each cohort. Prospect engagement → opportunity creation is your demand gen signal. Active opportunity engagement → deal velocity change is your sales acceleration signal. Customer engagement → NRR expansion is your expansion revenue signal. Blending these cohorts creates attribution noise — separate them to get clean insight for each use case.

**Problem: Most of my community posts have high view counts but very few replies or saves, making it impossible to distinguish which content is actually resonating vs. just appearing in the feed.**
Solution: Reweight your engagement scoring formula to account for lurker-dominant community behavior. Shift to a passive consumption scoring model: track link clicks from community posts to external resources, downloads of attached files or templates, cross-posts to other channels/forums, and direct message spikes after specific posts. In communities with high lurker rates (>70% of members never post), passive signals like saves and resource downloads are 3-5x more predictive of pipeline conversion than reply counts. Implement UTM parameters on all links shared in community posts to track downstream web behavior in GA4 or your CDP, creating a direct community content → website behavior → conversion attribution chain without requiring member participation.
