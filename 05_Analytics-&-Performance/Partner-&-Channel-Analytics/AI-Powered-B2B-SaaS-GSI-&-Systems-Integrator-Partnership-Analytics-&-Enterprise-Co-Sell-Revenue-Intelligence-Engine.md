## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AEs are not filling in GSI influence fields — data quality is low.**
Solution: Reduce the ask to a single required field (GSI_Influence_Signals) at Stage 2 with a maximum of 30 seconds of effort. Gamify compliance by publishing AE-level attribution tagging rates in the weekly sales standup. The Alliance Manager should provide weekly "GSI intelligence briefs" to AEs — making the data-sharing relationship reciprocal (AEs give influence data, Alliance Manager gives GSI client intel useful for deal qualification) dramatically improves compliance.

**Problem: Leadership doesn't trust GSI attribution numbers and believes they are inflated.**
Solution: Build credibility by starting with the most conservative definition of influence — signal-count of ≥2 confirmed signals before attributing influence — and by conducting a retroactive attribution audit on 12 months of closed/won deals with Alliance Manager validation. Present the audit methodology, the confidence level per attribution tier, and the known limitations alongside the numbers. A number presented with honest uncertainty bounds is more persuasive to a skeptical CFO than a number presented without caveats.

**Problem: Top Deloitte practice leader just left the firm and pipeline is already declining.**
Solution: Activate the relationship change playbook immediately: (1) Within 72 hours, schedule a call with the outgoing leader to understand their successor and to request a warm introduction, (2) Use your relationship map to identify the top 2–3 Delivery Champions who are independent of the departing executive and who can provide continuity during the transition, (3) Initiate a 90-day pipeline review with each Delivery Champion to understand which active deals they are involved in and what is needed to keep those deals moving, (4) Adjust the practice health score to Red and escalate to VP Alliances level.
