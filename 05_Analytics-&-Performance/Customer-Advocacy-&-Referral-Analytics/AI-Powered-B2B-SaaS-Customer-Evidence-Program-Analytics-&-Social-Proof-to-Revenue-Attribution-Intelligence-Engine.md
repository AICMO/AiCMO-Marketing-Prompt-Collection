- **Win rate lift**: Measure win rate (deals with evidence used vs. without) quarterly; target closing the gap by 10+ points within 6 months

- **Proof content freshness score**: % of case studies under 18 months old; target >65%
- **Evidence-to-revenue ROI**: Evidence program total cost (production + time) ÷ evidence-influenced closed revenue; industry benchmark is 8-15x ROI

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce**: Add a custom "Evidence Used" multi-select field to the Opportunity object. Track which specific assets were shared per deal. Build a report comparing win rates by evidence type. Use Salesforce Flows to auto-surface the most relevant case study when a deal moves to Stage 3 (Evaluation) based on Industry and Company Size fields.
- **Highspot / Showpad / Seismic**: Use the native analytics to export "most shared" and "most viewed" content reports. Cross-reference with your CRM to build the win-rate correlation analysis. Set up content effectiveness scoring using these tools' built-in win/loss overlays.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Search for call recordings where phrases like "do you have any customers like us?" or "can you connect us with a reference?" appear. This surfaces where evidence needs are not being met in real calls. Export these moments and feed into your gap analysis.
- **HubSpot**: Use the Documents tool or Sales Hub content library. Track document opens and time-spent per prospect. Build a workflow that notifies the rep when a prospect views a case study so they can follow up immediately.
- **Notion / Confluence (for evidence database)**: Build a living evidence library with tags for: industry, company size, buyer persona, use case, outcome, competitor displaced, and age. This makes the gap matrix updatable quarterly.
- **Airtable**: Use as a lightweight evidence program management tool — track each customer story from "identified" to "published" to "in CRM," with relationship readiness score and estimated impact.
- **Slack**: Set up a #customer-evidence Slack channel with a bot that reps can query: "What case studies do we have for pharma?" This reduces friction in evidence discovery and increases adoption.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales reps say they can't find relevant evidence even though it exists.**
Solution: The issue is almost always discoverability, not content volume. Audit your sales enablement platform's taxonomy and search experience. Move the top 20 most-needed assets into a "Quick Access" or "Pinned" section. Add CRM-embedded recommendations at the deal stage where evidence is most needed. Run a 30-minute "evidence scavenger hunt" at a team meeting — if reps can't find what they need in 2 minutes, fix the navigation before creating more content.

**Problem: You don't have enough CRM data to run the win-rate-with-evidence vs. without comparison.**
Solution: Start tracking immediately by adding an "Evidence Used" field to Opportunities. For historical analysis, use conversation intelligence (Gong/Chorus) to search for evidence mentions in past calls. Alternatively, survey your top 10 AEs: "In your last 10 won deals, what customer evidence did you use and when?" to build a directional dataset while proper tracking catches up. Even 3 months of clean data will produce meaningful signal.

**Problem: The champion who was featured in your best case study has left the company.**
Solution: Implement a "champion monitoring" process — set up Google Alerts and LinkedIn Sales Navigator alerts for your top 20 advocates. When a champion leaves, immediately: (1) assess whether the story can still be used with company permission, (2) reach out to the champion at their new company as a warm reconnect opportunity, and (3) add a note in your CRM flagging the story as "champion departed" so reps know to caveat it. Build quarterly check-ins into your customer marketing calendar to verify all featured customers are still live references.
