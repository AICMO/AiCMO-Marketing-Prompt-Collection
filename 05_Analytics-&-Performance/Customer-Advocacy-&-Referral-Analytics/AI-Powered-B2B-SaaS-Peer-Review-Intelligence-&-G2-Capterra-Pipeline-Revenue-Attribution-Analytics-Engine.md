## 1. Review-to-Revenue Full Attribution Model
Construct a three-layer attribution model:

**Direct Attribution Layer:**
- Revenue from deals where review platform was the self-reported source or last-touch UTM
- Pipeline from UTM-tracked clicks converting to demo/trial
- Formula: (Review platform referral visits × conversion rate to demo) × (demo-to-pipeline rate) × (win rate) × (ACV)

**Influenced Attribution Layer:**
- Revenue from deals where reviews were referenced during the sales cycle (Gong keyword data + CRM notes)
- Win rate lift calculation: (influenced win rate − baseline win rate) × influenced pipeline = incremental revenue from review influence
- Deal velocity lift: days saved × pipeline value × time-to-revenue cost of capital

**Halo Attribution Layer (Brand Trust Premium):**
- Estimate brand lift from achieving Leader/High Performer badge status (industry benchmark: 15-25% increase in inbound branded search after G2 Leader badge)
- Category ranking improvement impact on organic discovery (profile views correlation to rank)
- Quantify "social proof tax" avoided: deals that would have churned to competitor due to review gap

**Total Review Program ROI:**
- (Direct pipeline + Influenced pipeline increment + Halo pipeline estimate) / (Review generation investment + Platform fees)
- Payback period in days
- Compare ROI against paid search, content, and events for same investment level

## Integration Tips
- **G2 Buyer Intent API**: Connect G2's buyer intent data to HubSpot/Salesforce to automatically tag inbound leads who viewed your G2 profile before converting — enables true review-to-CRM attribution without relying on self-reported data. Trigger a "G2 Profile Viewer" workflow that fast-tracks these leads to SDR outreach within 24 hours.
- **Salesforce**: Create a custom field "Peer Review Influenced" (checkbox) and "Review Platform Source" (picklist: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius) on the Opportunity object. Populate via SDR discovery call form completion. Build a Review Influence dashboard in Salesforce Reports showing influenced pipeline, win rate, and ACV by review platform source.
- **HubSpot**: Build an automated review request workflow: Contact reaches "Active Customer — 60 Days" lifecycle stage → delay 3 days → send personalized email from assigned CSM with direct G2 review link. Add conditional branch: if email opened but no review in 14 days → send Capterra follow-up. If NPS < 7, suppress from review request and route to retention workflow instead.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Set up keyword trackers for "G2," "Capterra," "what do customers say," "reviews," "TrustRadius," "peer reviews." Export monthly call count data to CRM opportunity record via Gong's Salesforce integration. Use this to auto-populate "Peer Review Referenced in Call" field — removes dependence on SDR manual logging.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate the review response and amplification workflow: New G2 review published → Slack notification to Marketing + CS → Thank-you email sent from CS team → Reviewer added to Customer Advocate HubSpot list → Top-rated reviews (4.8+) auto-queued for LinkedIn social proof posts and sales email signature snippets.
- **Tableau / Looker**: Build a Review Attribution Revenue Dashboard with four panels: (1) Review platform traffic → pipeline funnel, (2) Review-influenced vs. non-influenced pipeline comparison, (3) Category rating vs. win rate scatter plot, (4) Competitive review velocity tracker. Schedule weekly automated email delivery to CMO and VP Sales.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Create a living "Review Intelligence Brief" that auto-pulls latest G2/Capterra data weekly and feeds into competitive battlecards, website social proof sections, and email signature testimonials — one source of truth, no manual updating.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: No UTM-tracked pipeline from review platforms — impossible to prove attribution to CFO**
Solution: Implement UTM parameters on all review platform profile links immediately: `?utm_source=g2&utm_medium=peer-review&utm_campaign=review-profile`. For historical pipeline, run a keyword search in Salesforce/HubSpot opportunity notes and email threads for "G2," "Capterra," "reviews," "what are other customers saying." Add "How did you first discover us?" as a required field on demo booking forms with review platforms as explicit options. Use G2's Buyer Intent API to retroactively match company IP data against your CRM — this typically recovers 15–30% of review-influenced pipeline that was previously unattributed.

**Problem: Review volume is too low (under 50 reviews) to run meaningful attribution analysis**
Solution: With fewer than 50 reviews, focus on building measurement infrastructure before attempting attribution modeling. This week: instrument UTM tracking, add "Peer Review" as a source option in CRM, and launch a Tier 1 advocate outreach campaign targeting your 10 highest-NPS enterprise customers with a personalized ask. Use this prompt specifically for the 90-Day Review Generation Plan section to reach 50 reviews first. Rerun the full attribution analysis at 100 reviews when you have statistically meaningful data. Meanwhile, track profile visitors → demo conversions as your leading indicator.

**Problem: Sales team won't consistently log review-influenced deals in CRM — data is incomplete**
Solution: Add "Was a peer review platform mentioned by the prospect?" as a single yes/no required field on the Stage 2 (Technical Evaluation) deal progression checklist in Salesforce — takes 30 seconds and eliminates reliance on discretionary note-taking. Present win rate data showing review-influenced deals close at [X]% higher rates in your next sales team meeting — self-interest alignment is more effective than process mandates. As a backup, use Gong keyword analysis to auto-detect review mentions in calls and push a "Review Mentioned" flag to the opportunity via Gong's Salesforce integration, requiring zero sales rep action.
