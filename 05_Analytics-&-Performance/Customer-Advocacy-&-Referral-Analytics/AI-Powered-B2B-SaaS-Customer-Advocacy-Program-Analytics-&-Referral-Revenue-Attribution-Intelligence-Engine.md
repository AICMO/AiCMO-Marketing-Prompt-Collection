## ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### 1. ADVOCACY REVENUE ATTRIBUTION MODEL

Produce revenue attribution for each channel using these methodologies:

**Sourced Revenue** (advocacy was the originating touchpoint):
- Referral-sourced ARR = Total value of closed-won deals where referral was first touch
- Review-sourced ARR = Estimate based on review site analytics + CRM source attribution
- Community-sourced ARR = Pipeline generated from community-engaged prospects

**Influenced Revenue** (advocacy was a touchpoint but not first):
- Reference-influenced ARR = Win rate lift × deal count with reference × average ACV
- Case study-influenced ARR = Win rate lift × deal count where case study shared × average ACV
- Review-influenced ARR = Win rate delta for known G2 researchers × deal count

**Accelerated Revenue** (advocacy shortened the sales cycle):
- Calculate: (Average deal velocity with advocacy touchpoint - without) × deals with advocacy × average daily deal value
- Advocacy acceleration value = Days saved × (ACV / average sales cycle length)

**Total Advocacy Revenue Impact** = Sourced + Influenced + Accelerated ARR

### 2. ADVOCACY ROI CALCULATION

For each program component:

PROGRAM_ROI = (Revenue Impact - Program Cost) / Program Cost × 100

Portfolio ROI = (Total Advocacy Revenue Impact - Total Advocacy Program Cost) / Total Advocacy Program Cost × 100

B2B SaaS benchmark: Best-in-class advocacy programs generate $8-15 of influenced revenue for every $1 invested. Referral programs specifically should generate >$6:1 ROI to justify vs. paid acquisition.

### 3. ADVOCATE VALUE SCORING (AVS)

Score each advocate cohort on a 0-100 composite:
- Referral Activity (30%): Referrals submitted, quality, close rate
- Reference Activity (25%): Reference calls completed, deals won after reference
- Proof Content (20%): Reviews written, case study participation, testimonials
- Pipeline Influence (15%): Marketing activities that influenced pipeline
- Retention Signal (10%): Is their own renewal secure? Active advocates rarely churn

Use AVS to prioritize advocate investment and identify at-risk advocates (high AVS historically, now inactive).

### 4. DEAL INFLUENCE HEAT MAP

Map advocacy touchpoints to deal stages to identify where advocacy has maximum impact:

- Awareness stage: Review site discovery → sourced pipeline
- Evaluation stage: Case study shared → deal velocity acceleration
- Consideration stage: Reference call → win rate lift
- Decision stage: Peer network validation → deal rescue

Identify the combination of advocacy touchpoints that correlates with highest win rates. Most B2B SaaS companies find: "reference call + case study from same vertical + 3+ G2 reviews" is the highest-win-rate combination.

### 5. PROGRAM HEALTH SCORECARD

Score each program on 5 dimensions (1-5):
- Volume: Is enough advocacy activity happening?
- Quality: Is the advocacy from high-credibility, ICP-matched advocates?
- Coverage: Does advocacy coverage match your target market segments?
- Velocity: Is the program accelerating or plateauing?
- Health: Are advocates burning out or remaining engaged long-term?

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Can't isolate advocacy influence from other deal touchpoints — every deal has multiple touches**
Solution: Use a controlled comparison approach instead of pure attribution. Segment deals into groups: (1) had reference call, (2) had case study shared, (3) had both, (4) had neither. Compare win rates and ACV across groups. The delta between group 4 and groups 1-3 is your influence estimate. This is influence measurement, not last-touch attribution — and it's how the best customer marketing teams justify program investment.

**Issue: Advocacy program has high-NPS customers but few are taking action — low activation rate**
Solution: Advocacy asks that are too generic ("would you be willing to be a reference?") convert poorly. The highest-converting approach is a specific, low-friction ask tied to a value moment: "You just hit your 6-month ROI milestone — would you spend 10 minutes with one of our prospects in your industry who's evaluating a similar challenge?
