## Overview
Diagnoses every conversion failure and SLA breach across the MQL-to-SQL-to-Closed-Won funnel, calculates marketing's true revenue contribution by channel and segment, and produces a shared Marketing-Sales Revenue Accountability Scorecard that both teams can use to identify systematic gaps, agree on fix owners, and measure improvement week-over-week. Use this when marketing and sales are arguing about lead quality, when MQL volume is high but pipeline isn't growing, or when you need to prove marketing's revenue contribution to a skeptical CFO or CRO.

**Revenue Efficiency Ratio (RER)** = (Revenue Sourced) / (Total Investment in Channel)

- RER > 5x = highly efficient channel (scale)
- RER 2–5x = acceptable (optimize)
- RER < 2x = under-performing (cut or redesign)

**Lead Quality Index (LQI)** for each source = weighted average of:
- ICP match rate (30%): % of MQLs that are in target industry, company size, and persona
- Engagement depth (25%): Average number of content pieces consumed before MQL threshold
- Pipeline conversion rate (25%): MQL → SQL rate
- ACV index (20%): Avg deal ACV vs. company average ACV

LQI Scale: 80–100 = Premium Source | 60–79 = Standard Source | <60 = Low-Quality Source

- Audit the MQL scoring model — are you crediting form fills that indicate no buying intent?
- Check persona match — are these the right job titles or just the most common visitors?
- Review offer/CTA — gated content that attracts wrong-stage buyers inflates MQL volume without quality

4. Is the average lead score of SAL-accepted leads significantly higher than rejected leads? (If gap is <15 points, scoring is not predictive)
5. Do MQLs from content downloads have lower pipeline conversion than MQLs from product trials or demo requests? (If yes, behavioral intent should outweigh demographic scoring)

## Troubleshooting

**"Sales says MQL quality is terrible, but our MQL → SQL rates look fine in the CRM"**
This is the most common measurement trap in B2B SaaS. Check whether your SDRs are accepting MQLs as SALs without genuinely qualifying them — SAL acceptance inflation happens when SDR teams are measured on SAL volume rather than SQL creation. Pull a cohort of all SALs from last 90 days and check how many became SQLs vs. stalled in "SAL" status for >30 days. If SAL → SQL rate is below 50%, your sales team is accepting MQLs to hit SAL metrics, then letting them die. Fix: redefine SAL to require a documented 15-minute qualifying conversation with specific BANT/MEDDIC elements before SAL status is granted.

**"Marketing-sourced pipeline is growing but sales doesn't believe the attribution numbers"**
Attribution credibility requires agreement before the fact, not after. If marketing set up attribution unilaterally and is now presenting numbers sales disputes, the data will always be contested. Fix: convene a joint Marketing + RevOps + Sales Ops session to agree on attribution model rules before measurement begins. Document: which model (first-touch, multi-touch, influenced), how channel "credit" is defined, and what "marketing-sourced" vs. "marketing-influenced" means.
