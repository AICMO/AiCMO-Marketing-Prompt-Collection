## Troubleshooting

**Problem: 30-50% of opportunities have no lead source / motion attribution, making the analysis unreliable.**
Solution: Run a data recovery sprint before first analysis. For SDR-sourced deals with no campaign attribution, set `Primary_GTM_Motion__c = Outbound-SDR` based on SDR task activity in the 30 days before opportunity creation. For deals with a form-fill activity, set to Inbound. For deals with a trial sign-up activity, set to PLG. This rule-based backfill recovers 70-80% of unattributed pipeline with high accuracy. Accept that 5-10% will remain "Unknown" permanently — report it transparently rather than forcing false attribution.

**Problem: The same deal has touches from 3 different motions — how do I avoid double-counting in pipeline totals?**
Solution: Separate "sourcing motion" (the first motion touch that created the opportunity) from "influencing motions" (subsequent motions that assisted it). Sourcing motion gets full pipeline credit for the efficiency matrix. Influencing motions are tracked separately in the Motion Interaction analysis (Step 4) to reveal amplification effects. Never allow a deal to be counted more than once in pipeline totals — only the sourcing motion owns it.

**Problem: My events team insists all influenced pipeline should count as their pipeline. Marketing disagrees. Finance won't accept either number.**
Solution: Present two views explicitly — a "Direct-Source View" (first-touch attribution, no double-counting) and an "Influence View" (any motion that touched the deal within 90 days of opportunity creation). Show the board both. For budget allocation decisions, use Direct-Source View. For storytelling and team recognition, use Influence View. The key insight — if 60% of your inbound deals had an event touch in the prior 90 days, events deserve recognition in the narrative even if they don't own the pipeline number.
