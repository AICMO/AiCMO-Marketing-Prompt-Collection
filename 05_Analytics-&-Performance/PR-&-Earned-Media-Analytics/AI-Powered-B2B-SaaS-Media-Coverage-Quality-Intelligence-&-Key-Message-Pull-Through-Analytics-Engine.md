## CONSTRAINTS & METHODOLOGY NOTES

- Score what's actually in the article, not what the journalist intended
- Distinguish between "message appeared" and "message landed with impact" — a single-sentence mention buried in paragraph 8 scores lower than a full quote in the lead
- Account for article type: a roundup piece should be scored differently than a feature profile
- Never infer pull-through from length alone — a 2,000-word profile can have zero key message pull-through if none of our messages appear
- Competitive intelligence in coverage is a strategic asset — track it systematically
- Suppress your bias toward "positive" framing — an accurate, balanced article with high pull-through is better than a glowing piece that misrepresents your product

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Pull-through scores are consistently low across all journalists**
Solution: The problem is likely your Tier 1 message architecture, not your pitching. Messages that are abstract, lack specific numbers, or are not "journalist-friendly" will never pull through. Rewrite Tier 1 messages as standalone, quotable sentences with a specific data point. Test: could this sentence appear in a headline? If not, it's too abstract.

**Issue: Narrative Control Index is high but pull-through is low**
Solution: You're controlling the frame but your messages aren't memorable or quotable enough to survive the editing process. Create a "message survival kit" — for each Tier 1 message, provide journalists with a ready-to-paste quote from your CEO, a supporting customer quote, and a one-line proof point. Make it zero-effort for them to include your messages.

**Issue: Suppressed messages keep appearing in coverage**
Solution: If a suppressed message appears more than 3 times, journalists are inserting it themselves — which means it's salient enough that it survives even without you pitching it. You have two options: (1) proactively address it in every briefing with a redirect ("We know people ask about X, but here's why Y is the more important story...") or (2) accept that you cannot suppress it and work to reframe it on your terms rather than leaving it undefined.
