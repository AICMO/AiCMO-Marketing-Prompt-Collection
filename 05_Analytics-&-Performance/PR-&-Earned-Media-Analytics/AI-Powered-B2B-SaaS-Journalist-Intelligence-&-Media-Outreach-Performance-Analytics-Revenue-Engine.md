## ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### 1. JOURNALIST RELATIONSHIP SCORING MODEL

Score each journalist relationship on a 5-stage maturity scale:

**Stage 1 — Cold Prospect (Score: 1-20)**
- Never responded to any outreach
- No prior coverage of your company
- Covers your category and key competitors
- Action: Test with highly relevant, data-driven pitch angle they haven't seen before

**Stage 2 — Warming (Score: 21-40)**
- Has responded at least once OR covered you once from a pitch
- Response rate <30%
- Relationship is transactional, not strategic
- Action: Move from news-based pitching to relationship-building via expert commentary and non-commercial story angles

**Stage 3 — Active Contact (Score: 41-60)**
- Response rate ≥30%, ≥2 coverage pieces from your pitches
- Will occasionally cover your stories but doesn't proactively reach out
- No exclusives offered yet
- Action: Deepen relationship through non-pitch value exchange (sharing their articles, providing independent research, connecting them with credible sources)

**Stage 4 — Trusted Source (Score: 61-80)**
- Proactively contacts you for expert commentary or quotes on industry stories
- ≥4 coverage pieces in last 12 months
- Has accepted at least one exclusive briefing
- Relationship exists independent of specific news events
- Action: Maintain cadence, provide first-look access to major announcements, offer exclusive research

**Stage 5 — Strategic Media Asset (Score: 81-100)**
- Goes to you first for category-defining story angles
- Covers you proactively without pitching required
- Amplifies your coverage and advocates for your positioning
- Provides competitive intelligence (mentions what competitors are pitching)
- Action: Protect this relationship with extreme care; assign senior team member as primary point of contact; offer exclusives, early access to data, and C-suite access

**COMPOSITE RELATIONSHIP SCORE FORMULA:**
- Coverage frequency (12 months): 30 points
- Response rate to pitches: 25 points
- Proactive outreach from journalist: 20 points
- Exclusive opportunities granted: 15 points
- Sentiment quality in coverage: 10 points

### 2. PUBLICATION ROI RANKING

For each publication your coverage appeared in, calculate a Publication Business Value (PBV) score:

**PBV = (Traffic Impact × Demo Conversion Lift × Deal Mention Rate × Authority Score) / Pitch Effort**

Inputs:
- Traffic Impact: Direct referral traffic from publication + % lift in branded search within 72 hours of coverage
- Demo Conversion Lift: % increase in demo request rate in 48 hours post-coverage vs. baseline
- Deal Mention Rate: % of deals in pipeline where this publication's coverage was mentioned (from CRM notes + win/loss calls)
- Authority Score: Publication DA + journalist social reach + analyst/industry citation frequency
- Pitch Effort: Number of pitches required to generate one coverage piece (lower = more efficient)

Rank all publications from highest to lowest PBV. Reallocate pitching effort toward top-quartile publications.

### 3. PITCH ANGLE PERFORMANCE MATRIX

Build a 2×2 matrix for each pitch angle you use:

**X-axis**: Pitch-to-coverage conversion rate (Low <5% / High >10%)
**Y-axis**: Business impact per coverage piece (Low / High)

Quadrant interpretation:
- High conversion + High impact = SCALE (invest more here)
- High conversion + Low impact = OPTIMIZE (getting coverage but wrong publications/journalists)
- Low conversion + High impact = REFINE (great story, wrong approach — test different hooks, formats, or targets)
- Low conversion + Low impact = RETIRE (stop wasting cycles on this angle type)

### 4. COVERAGE GAP OPPORTUNITY SCORING

For each un-tapped journalist who covers your category, calculate:

**Gap Priority Score = (Publication Authority × Competitor Coverage Frequency × ICP Reader Overlap) / Prior Rejection Count**

Interpretation:
- High score = must-pursue relationship investment this quarter
- Medium score = include in programmatic outreach testing
- Low score = monitor but don't prioritize

### 5. PR TEAM EFFICIENCY BENCHMARKS

B2B SaaS industry benchmarks for comparison:
- Pitch-to-response rate: 12-25% (top performers: 30-40%)
- Response-to-briefing rate: 40-65%
- Briefing-to-coverage rate: 55-75%
- Overall pitch-to-coverage rate: 4-12% (top performers: 15-20% for relevant, targeted outreach)
- Average coverage pieces per full-time PR resource per month: 8-15 (top performers: 18-25)
- % of total coverage from Tier 1 publications: 15-25% for Series B/C companies
- Strategic media asset relationships (Stage 4-5): ≥5 active at any time for healthy PR program

## Integration Tips

- **Muck Rack / Cision**: Export journalist engagement data (open rates, click rates on pitches, response history) quarterly and run through this prompt to generate updated relationship scores. Set up automated alerts for when journalists who cover your category publish new stories — respond within 24 hours with a relevant expert comment to build relationship equity without a pitch.
- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM**: Create a custom "Media Influence" field on opportunity records. Train SDRs to ask "How did you first hear about us?" on all discovery calls and log "press" + specific publication name. Query this field quarterly and feed into Publication ROI rankings — the PBV score becomes more accurate every quarter as you accumulate real deal attribution data.
- **SparkToro**: Use to analyze the media consumption habits of your ICP (e.g., "where do CISOs read industry news?"). Cross-reference with your publication outreach list — any publication with high ICP readership that you're not targeting is an immediate coverage gap to investigate.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Create audience segments for visitors who arrived via press referral. Track these visitors separately through the conversion funnel to measure press-referred demo rates vs. other acquisition channels. Use this data to calculate the "Traffic Impact" component of your Publication Business Value score.
- **Slack / Notion for PR team workflow**: Build a shared Journalist Relationship Tracker with: journalist name, publication, relationship stage (1-5), last touchpoint, last coverage, next planned action, notes. Review weekly in PR team standup. The data quality of this prompt output improves dramatically when you maintain a clean, updated journalist CRM.
- **AI agent workflow**: Automate quarterly analyst runs — have an AI agent pull data from Muck Rack (coverage and outreach stats), Salesforce (media-sourced demos and deals), and GA4 (referral traffic from media). Run this prompt with fresh data each quarter to generate updated relationship scores, PBV rankings, and a prioritized outreach plan. Flag journalists whose relationship scores dropped >15 points quarter-over-quarter for urgent relationship repair actions.
- **HARO / Qwoted / Featured.com**: Track reactive inquiry response rates separately from proactive pitching. Best-practice benchmark: respond to 100% of relevant journalist queries within 2 hours during business hours. Build this into a daily PR workflow and measure response rate and coverage conversion from reactive channels quarterly.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: No data on which publications or coverage pieces actually drove demos or pipeline — can't calculate Publication Business Value without this**
Solution: Start with proxy metrics immediately while you build proper attribution. Proxy method 1: Correlate traffic spikes from coverage pieces (visible in GA4 referral reports) with demo request volume in the 72 hours post-coverage. Proxy method 2: Add a single required field to your demo intake form: "How did you hear about us?" and tag all press mentions. Proxy method 3: During discovery calls, train SDRs to ask about awareness and log responses in CRM. Within one quarter you'll have enough data to calculate rough PBV scores. Don't wait for perfect data — imperfect attribution data is infinitely more useful than no data for prioritizing PR investment.

**Issue: Journalist response rates are very low (<10%) and coverage output is minimal — unclear whether this is a story problem or a relationship problem**
Solution: Diagnose with a controlled test before assuming either cause. Run two weeks of purely reactive outreach (respond to journalist queries only, no proactive pitches) to isolate whether journalists find your perspective credible and interesting when you're not selling a story. If reactive response rates are high but proactive response rates are low, you have a pitch relevance problem (your story angles aren't compelling or timely). If both are low, you have a credibility or targeting problem — you're pitching journalists who don't cover your space or whose ICP doesn't overlap with yours. Fix targeting before fixing the pitch.

**Issue: Have strong relationships with Tier 2-3 journalists but can't break into Tier 1 publications despite effort**
Solution: Tier 1 reporters typically won't cover companies they don't already have context on. The most reliable Tier 1 access pathway is analyst coverage → Tier 1 citation. When a Gartner or Forrester analyst recognizes your company in a report, Tier 1 journalists use it as a credibility shortcut.
