# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Competitive PR Share of Voice Intelligence & LLM Citation Earned Media Revenue Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** pr-analytics, earned-media, share-of-voice, b2b-saas, brand-intelligence, llm-citations, revenue-attribution, competitive-intelligence

## Overview

An AI agent that transforms raw PR coverage data into competitive share of voice intelligence, media quality scores, and pipeline attribution — including the emerging metric of LLM citation presence (how often your brand appears in AI-generated answers). Use this when you need to prove earned media ROI to your CFO, understand how your PR footprint compares to competitors, or identify which publications and story angles are actually driving pipeline.

ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORKS TO APPLY:

1. WEIGHTED SHARE OF VOICE CALCULATION

   Methodology:
   a) Raw mention SOV: Your mentions / Total category mentions (your brand + all competitors)

   b) Quality-weighted SOV (primary recommended metric):
      - Tier 1 mention = 10 points × sentiment multiplier
      - Tier 2 mention = 3 points × sentiment multiplier
      - Tier 3 mention = 1 point × sentiment multiplier
      - Sentiment multipliers: Positive = 1.0 | Neutral = 0.5 | Negative = -0.3
      - Executive quote bonus = +2 points per qualifying mention
      - Product-specific coverage bonus = +1 point per mention
      - Backlink generated bonus = +3 points per DA40+ backlink

   c) Reach-adjusted SOV:
      - Weight each mention by publication reach (UV/circulation)
      - Normalize across competitive set for fair comparison

   d) Category share of narrative:
      - What % of total category coverage does your brand own?
      - Are you setting the narrative or reactively responding?
      - Who is leading category education: analyst coverage / press / competitor / your brand?

2. COVERAGE QUALITY INDEX (CQI)

   Score each earned media placement across 5 dimensions (1–10 each):

   a) Publication authority: Domain Authority, brand recognition in your ICP's actual reading list
   b) Audience relevance: Does this publication reach your specific buyer persona?
   c) Message penetration: Does the article communicate your key differentiation, or just name-drop you?
   d) Sales enablement value: Can a salesperson share this article with a prospect to advance a deal?
   e) Search/AI value: Is this article likely to be indexed and cited by LLMs or appear in search results for buyer queries?

   Calculate average CQI across your portfolio. Benchmark against competitors where data allows.
   Identify your top 5 highest-CQI placements (replicate the formula) and bottom 5 (for deprioritization).
   Flag any PR activities that generate Tier 3 volume at the expense of CQI — quantity without quality.

3. AI/LLM CITATION INTELLIGENCE FRAMEWORK

   Phase 1 — Citation gap analysis:
   - Map which earned media content types correlate with LLM citation appearance
   - Identify which publications are most frequently cited by LLMs in your category

   - Calculate your "AI Share of Voice": Of your 15–20 standard category queries, in what % does your brand appear vs. each competitor?

   Phase 2 — Citation root cause analysis:
   - Which story angles are most likely to generate LLM-cited content?
     * Original data/research reports with proprietary statistics (highest LLM weight)
     * Head-to-head comparison articles from high-DA publications
     * Named award/recognition coverage ("Best HR software 2025")
     * Analyst report citations (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
     * G2/Capterra category page rankings with volume of verified reviews
   - Identify 3 high-authority publications where competitor coverage appears in LLM responses but yours does not
   - Map each gap to a specific, pitchable PR story that would credibly fill it

   Phase 3 — LLM citation acceleration playbook:
   - Prioritize: one original research report to generate Tier 1 citations and LLM-indexable data
   - Wikipedia category page audit: Is your brand listed? Are competitors listed and you're not?
   - Crunchbase/G2/Capterra profile completeness audit — LLMs reference these constantly

4. PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION MODEL FOR EARNED MEDIA

   Use a multi-signal attribution approach because earned media rarely has perfect UTM tracking:

   Signal 1 — Direct attribution (hard data, High confidence):
   - UTM-tracked clicks from press coverage → demo form completions
   - Press coverage domains appearing as referring source in CRM contact records
   - Press article URLs shared by sales reps via Gong/Chorus email sequences

   Signal 2 — Correlation attribution (observational, Medium confidence):
   - Website traffic spikes on high-intent pages (pricing, demo, product) within 72 hours of major coverage
   - Branded search volume spikes (Google Search Console) in the week of major press moments
   - LinkedIn company page follower/view spikes following coverage events

   Signal 3 — Self-reported attribution (survey data, Low-Medium confidence):
   - Sales team quarterly survey: "In what % of discovery calls did prospects mention a specific article or media coverage?"
   - Closed-won customer survey: "How did you first learn about us?" — capture % selecting "press/media coverage"

   Signal 4 — Deal quality correlation (strategic, Medium confidence):
   - Compare average deal size / close rate / sales cycle length for leads with PR touchpoints vs. without
   - Hypothesis: Coverage-influenced leads close 15–30% faster due to pre-existing brand credibility

   Calculate a blended "Earned Media Pipeline Contribution Estimate" using all 4 signals:
   - Conservative estimate (direct attribution only — hard floor)

## Success Metrics

- **SOV measurement discipline:** Competitive share of voice uses verified mention data from Meltwater, Cision, or Mention — not manual Google searches. Run full competitive SOV report monthly.
- **Coverage quality improvement:** Average Coverage Quality Index (CQI) increases quarter-over-quarter. Track quality, not just raw mention volume.
- **LLM citation rate baseline and improvement:** Run the same 15–20 standard category queries monthly across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini. Track brand appearance rate (%) as a formal KPI. Target: minimum 5 percentage point improvement per quarter from a focused effort.
- **Pipeline attribution confidence:** At minimum, UTM-tracked press link conversions are being captured in CRM. Self-reported PR influence is captured in discovery call notes by sales team via a standardized survey.
- **PR ROI threshold:** Every $1 in PR budget generates at least $3 in influenced pipeline at the moderate attribution level. Below $2 signals program restructuring is needed.
- **Tier 1 coverage cadence:** At least 2 Tier 1 placements per month on a consistent basis — not relying on one mega-moment per quarter to carry the quarter.
- **Journalist relationship conversion rate:** Track pitches-to-placement rate per publication. Improvement of 5%+ quarter-over-quarter on target outlets signals relationship investment is compounding.

## Integration Tips

- **Meltwater / Cision / Mention:** Set up daily email digests and weekly full coverage exports. Use Boolean search queries to capture brand + competitor mentions simultaneously: `"Pulsewave AI" OR "Pulsewave" AND (NOT site:pulsewave.com)`. Export to CSV weekly for competitive SOV calculation in Google Sheets. Build a running quality-weighted SOV tracker using the scoring formula from section 1 above.
- **Google Search Console:** Monitor branded query volume week-over-week under "Search results → Queries" filtered to your brand name. PR coverage events appear as volume spikes within 24–72 hours. Document every spike alongside the coverage event date and publication — this becomes your correlation attribution evidence over time.
- **HubSpot:** Create a custom property "PR Influenced" (checkbox) on the Contact and Deal objects. Configure a workflow: if Original Source Domain is on your "Top 50 Media Publications" list → auto-stamp "PR Influenced = True" and note the referring publication. Build a monthly CRM report filtering for PR-influenced contacts' downstream pipeline and revenue.
- **Salesforce:** Create a Campaign record for each significant press placement (e.g., "TechCrunch Feature — April 2026"). Use Campaign Influence (multi-touch) to associate any Deal where the Contact was created within 14 days of the coverage date, or where Opportunity notes reference the article title. Pull quarterly "Campaigns by Influenced Pipeline" report, filter to PR Campaign Type.
- **Perplexity / ChatGPT API automation:** Automate monthly LLM citation audits using the OpenAI or Perplexity API. Write a simple Python script that iterates through your 15–20 standard category queries, calls the API, checks each response for presence of your brand name and each competitor's brand name, and outputs a CSV showing brand mention rate per query. Schedule via cron or GitHub Actions for monthly execution — building a time-series dataset of LLM SOV.
- **Ahrefs / Semrush:** Export all new backlinks from press coverage monthly. Sort by Domain Authority. Build a "Press Backlink Value Log" tracking: publication name, DA, anchor text, article topic, whether LLMs are citing this article (check manually). This log becomes your input for prioritizing which publication relationships generate the most compound SEO + AI citation value.
- **Google Alerts (free tier):** Set up instant email alerts for your brand name, each competitor brand name, and 5–8 high-priority category keywords. Route all alerts to a dedicated Slack channel via Zapier. Use this as your real-time PR monitoring between formal Meltwater reporting cycles — critical for catching competitive announcements that require rapid reactive PR response.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: No UTM tracking on press coverage links — can't directly attribute pipeline to earned media**
Solution: This is the most common PR analytics gap. Implement three fixes immediately: (1) Create a custom UTM parameter convention for all press-linked content you control: `utm_source=[publication-slug]&utm_medium=earned-media&utm_campaign=pr-q3-2026`. Include these links in all pitches for contributed content, press releases, and any article where you provide a URL (product screenshots, "learn more" links). (2) For inbound links from reporters/editors you don't control, configure a Zapier workflow: any form submission where referrer domain matches your media publication list → automatically create/update the HubSpot Contact with "PR Influenced = True" and note the source domain. (3) For a near-term proxy while tracking is being set up, run a 5-question survey to your sales team: "In your last 20 discovery calls, in how many did a prospect reference a specific article or media coverage about us?" — self-reported is imperfect but provides an immediately actionable baseline attribution estimate.

**Problem: LLM citation audit shows your brand is completely absent from AI-generated responses — can't determine why competitors appear and you don't**
Solution: Three root causes account for 90% of LLM citation invisibility in B2B SaaS categories. Diagnose in order: (1) Insufficient backlinks from DA60+ publications that LLMs treat as authoritative reference sources — pull your Ahrefs backlink profile and compare it to your leading competitor. The gap in DA60+ referring domains is your gap in LLM citation. Fix: Commission one original research report to earn 10–20 high-authority links simultaneously. (2) No Wikipedia presence or minimal Wikipedia category-page mention — LLMs heavily index Wikipedia as a source. Check the Wikipedia page for your product category. If competitors are listed and you're not, add your brand following Wikipedia's notability standards (you need Tier 1 press coverage as citation — another reason Tier 1 placements are critical). (3) Absence from or incomplete profiles on Crunchbase, G2, and Capterra — LLMs treat these as structured data reference points. Ensure your Crunchbase profile has an accurate, keyword-rich description; your G2 profile has 50+ reviews and a complete product category tag; and Capterra lists you in all relevant categories.

**Problem: Board or CFO dismisses earned media ROI as "squishy" and demands hard revenue numbers**
Solution: Present in three layers: (1) Lead with hard data — "We generated 23 UTM-tracked demo requests directly attributable to press coverage in Q2, representing $73K in conservative pipeline."
