# AI-Powered DTC Ecommerce Revenue Analytics & Customer Acquisition Retention Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** dtc, ecommerce, d2c, cac, ltv, roas, cohort-analysis, retention, paid-media, shopify

## Overview
Runs a full-stack DTC ecommerce analytics intelligence engine across your acquisition, conversion, and retention data to identify which channels, cohorts, and customer segments drive the highest lifetime value — then produces a prioritized roadmap for improving blended CAC, LTV:CAC ratio, and net revenue per customer. Use this when your DTC brand is scaling paid media spend but can't identify why some acquisition cohorts retain at 3x the rate of others, or when contribution margin is compressing despite rising revenue.

1. BLENDED CAC vs. PAID CAC ANALYSIS

   - For each paid channel: gross revenue attributed, ad spend, ROAS, CAC, new customer %, return rate

   - Revenue from campaigns by segment

6. RETURNS & REFUND ANALYTICS
   - Return rate by channel, product, and customer segment
   - Net revenue per order after returns and shipping
   - Identify product-channel combinations with >20% return rates (contribution margin destroyers)

7. PRIORITIZED 90-DAY ROADMAP
   - 5 highest-impact actions ranked by estimated incremental contribution margin

   - Timeline: <1 week / 2-4 weeks / Next quarter
   - One leading metric to track progress for each action

Format output as a DTC Revenue Intelligence Report with comparison tables (text-based), contribution margin waterfall, and 90-day execution roadmap organized by revenue impact.

ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORKS TO APPLY:
- **LTV:CAC cohort waterfall**: Track cumulative revenue per acquisition cohort over 3/6/12/24 months, overlaid with CAC recovery milestones (payback period curves)
- **Contribution margin cascade**: Revenue → minus COGS → minus shipping → minus returns → minus ad spend → Contribution Margin 1 (before overhead). Apply per channel and per product.
- **RFM segmentation**: Score entire customer database on Recency (days since last purchase), Frequency (number of orders), Monetary (total spend). Produce 5-tier RFM matrix.
- **LTV-adjusted ROAS (LTV-ROAS)**: Instead of 7-day or 28-day attributed ROAS, calculate projected 12-month revenue per acquired customer / CAC. Reranks channels completely from standard ROAS.
- **Post-purchase survey attribution**: Weight platform-reported attribution against self-reported survey data (Fairing/KnoCommerce) to correct for Meta's systematic over-attribution and Google's systematic under-attribution of new customers
- **Subscription survival curve**: For subscription products, plot % of subscribers still active at Month 1/2/3/6/9/12. Identify the "churn cliff" month where retention drops most sharply.
- **Incrementality-adjusted attribution**: Flag channels where attributed ROAS is likely inflated vs. true incremental lift (direct mail and branded search are common inflators; TikTok and Meta top-of-funnel are often under-credited)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "Our attribution data is all over the place — Meta says 5x ROAS, Google says 4x, but our Shopify revenue doesn't add up to both."**
Solution: Multi-touch attribution inflation is universal in DTC — every platform takes credit for the same conversion. Fix with a three-layer approach: (1) Use platform ROAS only as a relative performance indicator within each channel (not cross-channel comparison); (2) Implement a post-purchase survey (Fairing or KnoCommerce) asking "How did you hear about us?" as your ground-truth channel allocation; (3) Run geo-holdout or time-based incrementality tests on your top 2 channels to establish true incrementality. Until then, use blended new-customer ROAS (total spend / new customer revenue) as your headline metric — it's imperfect but honest.

**Problem: "We don't have 12+ months of cohort data — our brand is too new."**
Solution: With 3–6 months of data, you can still run high-value analysis. Focus on: (1) 30-day and 90-day repurchase rates as leading LTV indicators — these strongly predict 12-month cohort behavior; (2) Channel-level AOV differences, which are visible immediately; (3) RFM segmentation on your existing customer base even with small cohorts; (4) Subscription survival curve if applicable — even 3-month churn data reveals the cliff. Project 12-month LTV by multiplying 90-day LTV by your category's observed LTV multiplier (skincare: 2.8x, supplements: 2.4x, apparel: 1.9x, food/bev: 3.1x).

**Problem: "Our email list is large but open rates are below 20% and revenue per subscriber is below $0.50/month."**
Solution: List health is the root cause. Before optimizing flows, run a list reactivation and suppression sequence: (1) Send a re-permission campaign to all subscribers who haven't opened in 180+ days — suppress non-responders within 2 weeks; (2) After suppression, recalculate revenue per subscriber — it will likely jump 30–60% as denominator shrinks while revenue stays flat; (3) Fix deliverability issues first (check sender reputation in Google Postmaster Tools) — inbox placement rate below 85% means your flows aren't reaching subscribers regardless of content quality; (4) Once list is clean, optimize flow timing and segmentation using this prompt's Email/SMS module.
