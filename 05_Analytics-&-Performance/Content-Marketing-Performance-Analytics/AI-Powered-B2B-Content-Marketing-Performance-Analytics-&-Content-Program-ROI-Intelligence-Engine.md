## Overview
Deploys an AI-powered content marketing performance analytics system that goes beyond traffic metrics — measuring production velocity, engagement quality, decay risk, topic cluster health, and program-level ROI across your entire content library. Use this when you need to run content as a revenue function with metrics that hold up to CFO scrutiny, prove which formats and topics are worth the investment, and build a data-driven optimization engine your team can run every week.

SECTION 1 — CONTENT PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT ARCHITECTURE

1.1 Universal Content Health Score (0–100)
Design a scoring framework that works across all content formats. Assign weights to four metric categories to produce a composite per-piece score:

TRAFFIC & VISIBILITY METRICS (25% weight):
- Organic sessions: 30-day + 90-day trend direction (+/- %)
- Keyword rankings: # of target keywords in positions 1–3, 4–10, 11–20, 21+
- Click-through rate from SERP (Google Search Console data)
- Featured snippet or PAA ownership on target keyword
- Referring domains acquired (past 90 days; weight by donor Domain Rating ≥ 40)
- Direct traffic share (signal of brand-associated content recognition)

ENGAGEMENT QUALITY METRICS (25% weight):
- Average engaged time on page (benchmark: >2.5 min for long-form, >1.5 min for standard posts)
- Scroll depth % (benchmark: >60% scroll = high-quality read; target for pillar pages: >75%)
- Bounce rate delta vs. site average
- Internal CTA click-through rate (leading to next funnel step)
- Return visitor rate (measures content bookmarkability — strong signal for evergreen assets)
- Social shares + LinkedIn/Reddit community citations (dark social signal)

LEAD & PIPELINE CONTRIBUTION METRICS (35% weight):
- Direct leads sourced (for gated: form submissions triggered by this piece)
- Assisted leads (for ungated: piece appears in contact's journey within 30 days before conversion)
- Lead-to-MQL conversion rate for content-sourced leads
- Pipeline influenced ($): sum of open opportunities where this piece appears in contact journey
- Pipeline influence by funnel stage (ToFu signal vs. MoFu/BoFu accelerant — different value)
- Closed-won revenue assisted (trailing 12 months)

PRODUCTION EFFICIENCY METRICS (15% weight):
- Production cost per piece (writer + editor + design + promotion budget)
- Content decay index (MoM traffic velocity — negative = decaying)
- Update recency (days since last meaningful refresh)
- Evergreen classification: TRUE (stable for 12+ months) vs. TIME-SENSITIVE (event, news, version-specific)
- Repurposing yield: # of derivative assets generated from this piece

COMPOSITE SCORE INTERPRETATION:
- 80–100: THRIVING → Amplify, build internal links from new content, repurpose aggressively
- 60–79: STABLE → Maintain, minor CTA or internal linking improvement, monitor monthly
- 40–59: AT RISK → Refresh within 60 days: update data, improve structure, add FAQ schema
- 20–39: DECLINING → Full refresh required: rewrite sections, new keyword strategy, re-promote
- 0–19: DECAYING → Evaluate: aggressive rewrite OR 301 redirect to most relevant pillar page

1.2 Content Decay Algorithm
Define precise decay triggers for automated weekly monitoring:

DECAY CLASSIFICATION CRITERIA:
- THRIVING (80–100): 3-month organic session growth ≥ 0%, primary keyword position ≤ 10, engaged time >2 min
- DECLINING (40–59): 3 consecutive months of organic session decline ≥ 10% MoM, OR primary keyword dropped from ≤ 10 to 11–20, OR engaged time dropped >30% from 90-day average
- DECAYING (0–39): 3-month session loss ≥ 30%, OR primary keyword fell from ≤ 10 to >20, OR zero pipeline contribution in last 90 days despite > $1,000 production cost
- ARCHIVE CANDIDATE: No backlinks, < 50 sessions/month for 6+ months, zero pipeline contribution, search volume for primary keyword < 100/month

DECAY TRIGGER SIGNALS TO MONITOR WEEKLY:
1. Keyword falls from position ≤ 10 to > 15 in any 4-week period
2. MoM organic session loss ≥ 20% for 2 consecutive months
3. Average engaged time drops below 45 seconds (signal: content no longer satisfies intent)
4. Top competitor publishes a more comprehensive piece that now outranks you on your primary keyword
5. Primary keyword search volume decays ≥ 30% YoY (topic obsolescence signal)

TRIAGE DECISION MATRIX:
For each decaying piece, score two dimensions: (1) Revenue Recovery Potential (0–10: what traffic + pipeline could be recovered with a refresh?) × (2) Refresh Feasibility (0–10: how quickly and cheaply can it be improved?). Plot on 2x2 matrix:
- High potential + high feasibility → IMMEDIATE REFRESH (this quarter)
- High potential + low feasibility → SCHEDULED REBUILD (next quarter)
- Low potential + high feasibility → QUICK FIX or ARCHIVE (48-hour decision)
- Low potential + low feasibility → ARCHIVE AND REDIRECT (execute this week)

1.3 Topic Cluster Performance Dashboard
Map your full content library to topic clusters (e.g., "Workflow Automation," "Operations KPIs," "Manufacturing Compliance") and measure cluster-level health:

CLUSTER HEALTH METRICS:
- Total organic sessions per cluster (trailing 90 days)
- Keyword portfolio depth: # of ranking keywords per cluster × position distribution
- Pillar page authority: backlinks + Domain Rating of pillar page
- Internal linking density: average # of cluster pages that link to pillar (benchmark: every sub-page links to pillar once; pillar links to every sub-page)
- Content gap ratio: % of high-volume ICP-relevant keywords in cluster WITHOUT a published piece
- Buyer stage coverage: % of cluster content mapped to ToFu vs. MoFu vs. BoFu — flag clusters that only have one stage covered
- Pipeline influence per cluster ($): correlate cluster topic to CRM opportunity source + influence tags

CLUSTER INVESTMENT MATRIX (2x2):
- X-axis: Revenue impact score (pipeline influenced per 1,000 organic sessions × close rate for content-sourced leads)
- Y-axis: Growth potential score (sum of unfilled keyword gap volume × 1/average keyword difficulty)
- INVEST & AMPLIFY: High impact + high potential → build 4+ new sub-pages, acquire backlinks, add gated asset
- MAINTAIN & OPTIMIZE: High impact + low growth remaining → focus on refresh and protect rankings, no new production
- RETHINK: Low impact + high potential → test 1–2 new angles before committing full cluster build
- DIVEST: Low impact + low potential → stop production, redirect older pieces, reallocate budget

2.1 Full Cost Accounting Per Content Piece
Define the production cost model for each format:

COST INPUTS:
- Content creator fee: (hourly rate or per-word rate) × (hours/words produced)
- Editorial review: editor hourly rate × review hours
- Design and visuals: designer hourly rate × design hours (or stock/AI tool cost)
- SEO optimization: SEO manager time + tool cost (Clearscope license prorated per piece)
- Video/audio production: if applicable (voiceover, editing, motion graphics)
- Promotion budget: paid amplification + newsletter feature + influencer distribution cost
- Tool overhead amortized: content platform, analytics, distribution tools / # pieces published per month

REVENUE ATTRIBUTION PER PIECE (12-month trailing):
- Sourced leads × average lead value (ACV × close rate from organic × content source credit share)
- Pipeline influenced ($) × fractional credit (define credit percentage in your attribution model)
- Sales cycle acceleration: calculate average days-to-close reduction when this content is consumed; multiply by revenue per deal × monthly run rate to get acceleration value
- Backlinks earned → estimate Domain Authority improvement → calculate incremental organic traffic value ($)

2.2 Content Velocity & Production Efficiency
Measure operational efficiency across the full content production workflow:

KEY PRODUCTION METRICS:
- Brief-to-draft time (business days): target ≤ 5 days
- Draft-to-published time (business days): target ≤ 3 days including review cycles
- Revision cycles per piece: target ≤ 2 rounds
- Editorial calendar fill rate: % of planned pieces published on schedule (target: ≥ 85%)
- Content backlog health: # of pieces briefed but not started + started but not published + stuck in review
- Repurposing rate: % of new long-form pieces that generate ≥ 3 derivative assets (social posts, email snippets, short-form video scripts, sales one-pagers)
- AI augmentation rate: % of workflow steps where AI tools reduce time by ≥ 30% (e.g., brief creation, first draft, SEO optimization, metadata generation)

PRODUCTION BOTTLENECK IDENTIFICATION:
For each stage in the content production pipeline, calculate average dwell time and flag if dwell exceeds benchmark. Common bottlenecks:
- Approval step: legal, brand, or executive review adds > 3 days → fix: async review via Notion comments with 24-hour SLA
- Design queue: design requests back up 10+ days → fix: build templated Canva/Figma layouts writers can self-serve; reserve designer time only for hero visuals
- SEO brief quality: writers miss keyword targets because briefs are inadequate → fix: standardize brief template with target keyword, search intent, recommended H2 structure, and Clearscope score target
- SME interview scheduling: technical content waits weeks for subject matter experts → fix: build "expert bank" recordings (Loom library) that writers can draw from async

SECTION 4 — AI-POWERED CONTENT OPTIMIZATION ENGINE

4.1 Per-Piece Optimization Checklist
For each piece scoring below 60 (AT RISK, DECLINING, or DECAYING), generate a ranked action list:

PRIORITY ORDER FOR OPTIMIZATION ACTIONS:
1. KEYWORD STRATEGY REFRESH: Is the target keyword still relevant? Does search intent match current content structure? Identify 3 secondary keywords to add to H2s/H3s. Add FAQ section targeting 5–8 PAA (People Also Ask) queries from Google.
2. CONTENT FRESHNESS SIGNALS: Update all statistics and data points with [current year] sources. Replace outdated vendor/tool references. Update product screenshots if SaaS product has changed. Add "Updated [Month Year]" to meta title and intro sentence.
3. DEPTH & COMPREHENSIVENESS: Add comparison table, decision framework, or downloadable template. Expand thin sections (< 200 words for a sub-topic that warrants more depth). Add a credible expert quote or original data point not available elsewhere.
4. INTERNAL LINKING ARCHITECTURE: Add contextual links from 3–5 newer high-authority pieces in the same cluster. Ensure this piece links to the cluster pillar page. Add at least one intent-matched CTA linking to a relevant lead magnet or product page.
5. TECHNICAL SEO: Verify Core Web Vitals (LCP < 2.5s). Add FAQ schema markup. Fix any broken internal or external links. Compress images (target: < 100KB per image). Verify canonical tag is correct.
6. CTA OPTIMIZATION: Replace generic "Book a Demo" CTAs with context-specific CTAs matching the piece's search intent. A post targeting "how to reduce operations costs" should CTA to a cost calculator, ROI case study, or relevant playbook — not a generic homepage demo button.
7. UX & READABILITY: Improve H1 to match current search intent precisely. Add a summary box (TL;DR) at the top for fast scanners. Add table of contents for content > 2,000 words. Embed one relevant video or infographic to increase engaged time.

4.2 Content Calendar Optimization (Monthly)
Based on performance data, generate monthly editorial recommendations:
- Top 5 decay-risk pieces to refresh this month (ranked by estimated traffic and pipeline recovery potential)
- Top 5 content gaps to fill with net-new content (ranked by ICP buyer intent and pipeline opportunity)
- Top 3 top-performer repurposing opportunities (e.g., your #1 blog post → LinkedIn carousel + email sequence + webinar follow-up)
- Underperforming gated white papers that should be ungated to capture organic search leads
- Evergreen pieces that should be featured in the next 3 newsletter issues for compounding return on production investment

## Integration Tips

- **GA4 + Looker Studio:** Build a Looker Studio template pulling per-page: sessions, avg. engagement time, scroll depth events (via GA4 enhanced measurement), and conversion events (form submit, CTA click). Connect to a Google Sheet storing your content inventory with production cost and CRM attribution data to auto-calculate per-piece ROI each month.
- **HubSpot:** Use HubSpot Campaign tool to tag each content piece as an Asset. Enable "Original Source Drill-Down" in CRM properties to attribute pipeline to content. Build a custom HubSpot Report using Contact + Deal objects filtered by "Original Source = Organic Search" to surface content-sourced pipeline per piece in a ranked table.
- **Salesforce:** Use Salesforce Campaigns with Campaign Influence (Einstein Attribution) enabled. Map each blog post and gated asset as a Campaign Member touchpoint. Create a Campaign Influence Report showing pipeline influenced per content piece sorted by influenced opportunity revenue.
- **Ahrefs / SEMrush:** Export keyword rankings for your full URL inventory monthly via API. Import to Google Sheets and calculate MoM ranking velocity per piece to auto-trigger "Decay Alert" when primary keyword position drops exceed your threshold (e.g., fall of ≥ 5 positions in 30 days).
- **Clearscope / Surfer SEO:** Use content optimization scores as a quality input to your Health Score. Pieces with Clearscope scores < 70 AND declining traffic are high-priority refresh candidates — low content quality is likely contributing to ranking loss.
- **Slack Automation (via Zapier or Make):** Build a weekly automation that pulls from your Content Health Score sheet → posts a "Content Decay Digest" to your team Slack channel every Monday morning listing pieces that crossed into Declining or Decaying status. Include: piece title, score, primary keyword position change, and recommended action. Zero manual effort after setup.
- **Notion / Airtable Content Database:** Build a Content Intelligence Database where each record = one published piece, with auto-synced metrics from GA4 (via Supermetrics or Apify scraper) and manually maintained CRM attribution data. Use database filters and views to instantly pull: "Show all pieces with Health Score < 50 sorted by estimated revenue recovery potential."

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Can't connect blog post reads to pipeline influence because most readers are anonymous (not logged in or tracked in CRM).
**Solution:** Use reverse IP lookup tools (Clearbit Reveal, Albacross, 6sense) to identify company-level visits and match to CRM account records.
