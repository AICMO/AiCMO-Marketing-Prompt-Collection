## Overview
This prompt transforms your B2B podcast and owned audio program from a vanity metric exercise into a revenue-attributable demand generation channel. It generates a complete analytics framework — listener-to-pipeline journey mapping, episode-level revenue attribution, audience quality scoring, and board-ready ROI reporting — enabling you to optimize content decisions based on revenue impact, not downloads.

DELIVERABLE 2: EPISODE PERFORMANCE INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM

**2A. Episode Tier Classification**

Classify every published episode into one of four performance tiers within 30 days of publish:

Tier B — Audience Builders (high downloads, lower conversion)
- Criteria: Downloads ≥ program average BUT pipeline influence score < 40
- Action: Re-optimize CTAs, add mid-episode offer, create companion gated content
- Indicators: Broad appeal content (trends, news) — attracts audience but lacks buyer intent context

Tier C — Niche Converters (lower downloads, higher conversion)
- Criteria: Downloads < program average BUT pipeline influence score ≥ 50
- Action: Amplify to target account list via LinkedIn targeting, email directly to ICP segment
- Indicators: Highly specific topic — smaller but more qualified audience

Tier D — Underperformers (low downloads AND low conversion)
- Criteria: Downloads in bottom 25% AND pipeline influence score < 25
- Action: Analyze topic/guest/format failures; do not repeat format; consider sunlighting after 6 months
- Indicators: Misaligned topic, weak guest authority, poor production quality, no ICP relevance

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom "Podcast Listener" contact property and list; use UTM parameters (`utm_source=podcast&utm_medium=audio&utm_campaign=[episode-slug]`) to track demo requests; build automated workflow to flag podcast subscribers entering pipeline
- **Salesforce:** Add "Podcast Influence" checkbox to Opportunity object; create a report showing Closed Won deals where Contact has Podcast Listener = true; build a dashboard comparing podcast-sourced CAC vs. other inbound channels
- **Chartable:** Configure SmartLinks for CTA tracking per episode; set up listener geo/demo reports weekly; export subscriber data monthly to sync with HubSpot for ICP scoring
- **Transistor/Buzzsprout/Castos:** Enable advanced analytics for per-episode completion rates; set up email capture integrations to push subscriber data into CRM; use private podcast features for customer-only content to drive activation
- **LinkedIn:** Create a custom audience from your podcast subscriber list via CSV upload; run sponsored content to re-engage listeners who haven't converted; use Conversation Ads to offer new episode notifications to matched audiences
- **Gong/Chorus:** Add "podcast" as a tracked keyword in call recordings; review win/loss calls for organic podcast mentions to supplement attribution data; create a library clip of reps referencing podcast content in winning calls

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Download numbers are strong but pipeline attribution is nearly zero.**
Solution: This is the most common podcast analytics failure — a measurement problem, not a performance problem. Implement episode-specific UTM tracking links for every CTA. Create a dedicated "Podcast Subscriber" segment in HubSpot/Salesforce and retroactively match existing subscribers against open/closed pipeline. Add a "How did you hear about us?" field to your demo request form with "podcast" as an explicit option. Most attributed pipeline exists — it's just invisible to your current measurement stack.

**Problem: ICP listener ratio is low (high downloads but wrong audience).**
Solution: Audit the last 10 episode topics. If more than 40% are broad industry/trend topics (AI, future of work, etc.), you've optimized for downloads over audience quality. Pivot to customer success stories, specific technical implementation topics, and guests who write for or speak to your exact ICP. Simultaneously, stop promoting episodes to broad tech audiences and redirect budget to LinkedIn targeting narrowly against ICP titles and company profiles. Audience quality improves within 60-90 days of format and promotion changes.

**Problem: Cannot justify podcast spend vs. other channels to CFO.**
Solution: Build a side-by-side comparison using same-audience CPM benchmarks. The average LinkedIn CPM to reach VP Sales at B2B SaaS companies is $85-$200. If your podcast reaches the same audience at $15-$40 per ICP listener, the efficiency argument is compelling even before revenue attribution. Layer on the trust/authority advantage (a 30-minute episode creates far more brand affinity than a display ad) and the evergreen asset nature (episodes continue generating listeners for 12-24 months). Present the conservative attribution scenario as a floor, not a ceiling.
