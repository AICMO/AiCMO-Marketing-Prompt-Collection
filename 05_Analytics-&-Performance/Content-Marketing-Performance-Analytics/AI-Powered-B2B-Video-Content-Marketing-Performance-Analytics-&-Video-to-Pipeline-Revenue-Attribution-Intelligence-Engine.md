SECTION 1 — VIDEO CONTENT HEALTH SCORING SYSTEM

1.1 Video Health Score (VHS) — Composite 0–100 Metric
Design a per-video scoring framework across four weighted categories to produce a composite Video Health Score:

VIEWER ENGAGEMENT METRICS (30% weight):
- Average watch rate: % of total video duration watched per view session (benchmark: ≥ 50% for videos > 5 min; ≥ 65% for videos < 3 min)
- Completion rate: % of viewers who watch ≥ 80% of the video (benchmark: ≥ 35% for 5-min videos; ≥ 55% for 2-min videos)
- Engagement index: ratio of engaged views (watch rate ≥ 40%) to total plays (filters bots and accidental clicks)
- Rewatch rate: % of views that include rewound or replayed segments (signals high-value content sections)
- Thumbnail click-through rate from browse/search (YouTube CTR benchmark: ≥ 5% for B2B educational content)
- Drop-off profile: is drop-off gradual (normal content fatigue) or cliff-edge at a specific timestamp (structural problem)?

PIPELINE & REVENUE METRICS (40% weight):
- Viewer-to-MQL conversion rate: % of video viewers who appear as known contacts in CRM within 30 days post-view (requires CRM-connected video hosting: Wistia + HubSpot, Vidyard + Salesforce)
- Pipeline influenced ($): sum of open opportunity values where a contact on the deal viewed this video during the sales cycle
- Pipeline acceleration: compare average days-to-close for deals where buying committee members watched vs. did not watch this video (≥ 10% faster close = strong influence signal)
- Closed-won assist rate: % of closed-won deals (trailing 12 months) where at least one contact viewed this video before deal close
- Gated video lead capture rate: for videos behind email gates, % of viewers who submit the gate form vs. abandon (benchmark: ≥ 60% form completion for high-intent gated content)
- Sales-send engagement rate: % of prospects who watch when a sales rep sends the video via Vidyard / Loom / HubSpot video embed in email (benchmark: ≥ 40% watch rate for sales-sent video)

DISCOVERY & REACH METRICS (20% weight):
- Organic search views: views originating from YouTube search or Google video SERP (measures video SEO performance)
- Channel subscriber conversion rate: % of video viewers who subscribe to your YouTube channel (signals long-term audience building)
- Video share rate: % of viewers who share via social or direct link (strong signal of content quality and ICP resonance)
- Syndication reach: views generated from embeds on partner sites, PR pickups, community forums, or repurposed content (LinkedIn posts, email campaigns)
- LinkedIn native video reach: impressions × engagement rate for videos posted natively to LinkedIn (not YouTube link shares — algorithm strongly favors native upload)

PRODUCTION EFFICIENCY METRICS (10% weight):
- Cost per qualified view: production cost ÷ total engaged views (watch rate ≥ 40%)
- Cost per pipeline dollar influenced: production cost ÷ pipeline influenced ($)
- Video content decay rate: MoM view velocity (negative = declining distribution; positive = compounding audience growth)
- Repurposing yield: # of derivative assets generated from this video (short clips, GIF previews, transcript blog posts, email sequences, podcast audio extractions)
- Update recency: days since any meaningful re-edit, title/description optimization, or thumbnail refresh

COMPOSITE VHS INTERPRETATION:
- 80–100: STAR PERFORMER → Amplify distribution, create follow-up content in same topic/format, use in paid retargeting, build into sales enablement library
- 60–79: SOLID PERFORMER → Maintain, test new distribution channel, add or optimize CTA card and end screen
- 40–59: UNDERPERFORMING → Diagnose drop-off, re-edit intro (first 30 seconds are critical), update thumbnail/title, re-promote to ICP audience via LinkedIn or email
- 20–39: POOR PERFORMER → Full re-edit or retire: assess whether the topic has ICP demand; if yes, reformat (e.g., convert 10-min explainer → 2-min punchy version + 30-sec highlight reel)
- 0–19: ARCHIVE → Retire from website embed, unlist from YouTube (do not delete — preserve view history and external embeds), redirect production budget to higher-priority formats

1.2 Video Drop-Off Forensics Algorithm
For every video with an average watch rate < 45% or a cliff-edge drop-off at a specific timestamp, diagnose the root cause:

DROP-OFF CLASSIFICATION:
- INTRO DROP (≥ 30% of viewers leave in first 30 seconds): Cause: weak hook, slow opening, no value promise stated in first 10 seconds, logo animation too long. Fix: Recut to lead with the most compelling insight or proof point; state viewer benefit in first sentence.
- MID-CONTENT CLIFF (≥ 25% viewers drop at single timestamp in minutes 1–5): Cause: topic shift that loses relevance, competitor mention without proof, dense jargon without context, or visual monotony (talking head with no B-roll). Fix: Insert a reengagement hook at that timestamp ("here's the part that changes everything"), add B-roll or motion graphics, cut the irrelevant segment entirely.
- FEATURE FATIGUE (gradual decay after minute 3 in product demos): Cause: showing too many features before establishing buyer pain. Fix: Reorder the demo — lead with the pain scenario, then show 1–2 features that resolve it directly. Use Jobs-to-be-Done sequencing.
- CTA DEAD ZONE (high completion rate but zero CTA clicks): Cause: CTA placed too late, generic CTA ("Visit our website"), no urgency, or viewer is already a customer. Fix: Add interactive CTA card at peak engagement moment (use Wistia heatmap to identify), use specificity ("Download the 2026 Benchmark Report — referenced in this video").
- MOBILE DROP-OFF DIVERGENCE: Watch rate on mobile is ≥ 20% lower than desktop. Cause: captions missing (85% of mobile video is watched muted), text on screen too small to read on phone, vertical format needed. Fix: Add burned-in captions, test 9:16 vertical recut for LinkedIn/Instagram.

1.3 Video SEO Keyword Opportunity Framework
For YouTube-hosted content, run a structured keyword gap analysis:

VIDEO SEO AUDIT DIMENSIONS:
- Current keyword rankings: which videos rank in YouTube search for B2B-relevant terms? What position? What estimated monthly YouTube search volume?
- Title optimization score: does the video title include the primary keyword in the first 5 words? Is it under 60 characters (to avoid YouTube truncation)?
- Description depth: does the description contain 200+ words with secondary keywords, timestamps (chapters), and a CTA link in the first two lines (above the fold)?
- Tags and category: are video tags using a mix of broad category terms, long-tail buyer terms, and competitor brand terms (for suggested video placement)?
- Chapters: are timestamps used to create chapter markers (improves YouTube search snippet extraction and watch time for long-form content)?

KEYWORD GAP METHODOLOGY:
Identify B2B buyer-intent video topics where:
- Competitors have ranking YouTube videos (estimated views ≥ 1,000/month for a B2B topic)
- You have no video ranking in top 10 YouTube results
- The topic matches PROBLEM-AWARE or SOLUTION-AWARE buyer stages in your ICP's journey
- The keyword appears in YouTube Autocomplete (signal of active search volume)

Priority Score = (Estimated Monthly YouTube Search Volume × ICP Buyer Intent Score [1–10] × Competitor Video Quality Score [inverted: low-quality competitor = higher opportunity]) ÷ Estimated Production Cost

BUYER JOURNEY STAGE SIGNAL:
Map video consumption to CRM deal stage:
- Awareness stage viewers (first time engaging with brand): TOFU video (thought leadership, industry trend, "how-to" tutorial) → trigger top-of-funnel nurture email sequence
- Consideration stage viewers (active in CRM but no open deal): MOFU video (product comparison, customer testimonial, ROI calculator walkthrough) → trigger SDR alert for personalized outreach within 24 hours
- Decision stage viewers (open opportunity): BOFU video (product demo, security overview, implementation overview, executive sponsor brief) → trigger deal acceleration sequence: send related case study, offer a custom demo or POC call

3.2 Video Viewer Cohort Analysis

COHORT DIMENSIONS TO ANALYZE:

4.2 Video Attribution Model Architecture
Define how to credit video in your marketing attribution model:

RECOMMENDED VIDEO ATTRIBUTION APPROACH (Multi-Touch):
- Video First Touch: a video is the first known marketing interaction before a contact becomes a lead → video receives 30% attribution credit
- Video Mid-Touch: a video is consumed during active nurture or evaluation stage → video receives 20% attribution credit per video touchpoint, up to 3 touchpoints per deal
- Video Last Touch: a video is the last content interaction before a demo request or closed-won → video receives 40% attribution credit
- Video Sales Assist: a sales rep sends a video during an active opportunity and the prospect watches ≥ 40% → video receives 10% pipeline influence credit

IMPLEMENTATION IN CRMS:

- Salesforce: Use Vidyard's Salesforce AppExchange integration. Video views log as Activity records under Lead/Contact. Build an Einstein Attribution Campaign where video views appear as Campaign Member touchpoints. Create an Opportunity Influence Report: filter opportunities with video Campaign Member activity → sum influenced pipeline.

SECTION 5 — VIDEO OPTIMIZATION ENGINE

5.1 Per-Video Optimization Checklist
For every video with VHS < 60, generate a ranked action list:

PRIORITY OPTIMIZATION ORDER:
1. INTRO RE-EDIT (highest impact on watch rate): If ≥ 30% of viewers drop in the first 30 seconds, recut the opening. The first 7 seconds must include: a pattern interrupt (striking visual or unexpected statement), a clear value statement ("In the next 8 minutes, you'll see exactly how [Company X] cut their [metric] by [X%]"), and no logo animation longer than 2 seconds. Remove any boilerplate corporate intro.
2. TITLE AND THUMBNAIL REDESIGN (highest impact on click-through): If YouTube CTR is < 3.5%, test a new thumbnail and title. Thumbnail rules for B2B: human face with clear emotional expression + high-contrast text (3–5 words maximum) + branded logo mark small in corner. Title rules: lead with the buyer's problem or outcome ("How [ICP Job Title] Cut [Pain Point] by X% Using [Method]") — not your product name.
3. CTA REPOSITIONING: If completion rate is ≥ 45% but CTA clicks are < 2%, the CTA is placed too late or is too generic. Use Wistia's Turnstile or Vidyard's CTA card to inject an interactive CTA at the highest-engagement timestamp (identified by the heatmap). Test: offer a piece of content that directly extends the video's topic vs. a generic demo CTA.
4. CAPTION OPTIMIZATION: If mobile watch rate is ≥ 20% lower than desktop, add burned-in captions using Descript or Rev. Auto-generated captions are acceptable for a test; invest in human-edited captions for videos with > 500 views/month.
5. CHAPTER MARKERS AND TIMESTAMPS: For videos > 5 minutes with < 40% completion rate and gradual drop-off (not cliff-edge), add YouTube chapters. Chapter markers increase average watch rate by 15–25% on long-form content by letting viewers jump to the section they need. Timestamp format: "0:00 Intro / 1:45 The Core Problem / 3:20 Live Demo / 6:10 Results & ROI / 8:30 Next Steps."
6. REPURPOSING AUDIT: Every video with VHS ≥ 60 that has not been repurposed into at least 3 derivative assets is leaving distribution value on the table. For each qualifying video: extract a 30–60 second highlight clip (for LinkedIn and paid retargeting), generate a transcript-based blog post (SEO value), pull 3 pull quotes for social posts, and build a 5-email nurture sequence from the video's key insights.
7. REDISTRIBUTION TO NEW CHANNELS: High-quality videos (VHS ≥ 65) that were only distributed on one channel (e.g., YouTube only) should be repromoted: upload natively to LinkedIn, embed in a dedicated email campaign, run as LinkedIn video ads to ICP job titles, and add to relevant website product pages.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Video views can't be connected to pipeline because most video viewers are anonymous and never appear in the CRM.
**Solution:** Use a three-layer approach: (1) For gated videos, add a Wistia Turnstile email gate at the 20% mark (not the start — reduce abandonment) to capture email before the most valuable content. This typically captures 40–60% of engaged viewers as known contacts. (2) For ungated YouTube videos, use Google Ads remarketing audiences: create a "YouTube Viewers — 50% Watch Rate" audience in Google Ads (available in YouTube Studio → Audience Manager) and retarget them with LinkedIn Matched Audiences or Google Display ads featuring an ICP-specific offer. (3) For fully anonymous website video viewers, deploy reverse IP lookup via Clearbit Reveal or 6sense — this identifies the company of the anonymous viewer and logs it in HubSpot/Salesforce as an account-level video engagement signal, enabling ABM triggers even without a known contact.

**Problem:** Watch rates look artificially high because the video auto-plays on the website and most "views" are passive impressions where visitors immediately scroll past.
**Solution:** In Wistia or Vidyard, filter your analytics to "Engaged Plays" only — these are plays where the viewer actively clicks the play button and watches ≥ 1 second. Disable auto-play on all embedded videos (auto-play inflates view counts, reduces average watch rate metrics, and signals low intent to your video analytics platform). Set your reporting baseline to Engaged Play Rate (engaged plays ÷ page visitors with the video visible) rather than raw play count. An Engaged Play Rate ≥ 15% for a website homepage video is a strong performance benchmark for B2B SaaS.

**Problem:** The video analytics dashboard shows strong views and watch rates, but the sales team doesn't act on video engagement signals because they distrust the data or find the alerts too noisy.
**Solution:** Run a "Video Signal Calibration Sprint" for 30 days: (1) Identify the last 20 closed-won deals and retroactively check whether any contact on those deals viewed a video during the sales cycle (using Wistia/Vidyard contact history). If ≥ 60% of deals had a video touchpoint, you have proof of correlation to show the sales team. (2) Reduce alert noise by only triggering sales alerts when a prospect watches ≥ 60% of a BOFU video (product demo, comparison video, testimonial) and has been inactive in email for > 14 days (signals video is the active re-engagement signal). (3) Build a 30-day pilot: assign 5 SDRs to follow up within 4 hours of every qualifying video alert. Compare connect rate, meeting booked rate, and pipeline generated vs. the control group of SDRs not using video alerts. Present the A/B result to sales leadership as proof that video signals predict buying intent.
